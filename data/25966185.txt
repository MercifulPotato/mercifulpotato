Item(by='Kranar', descendants=None, kids=[25966204, 25967481, 25966423], score=None, time=1611981998, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25966029, text='No you really can&#x27;t because of what is known as free riding.<p>Consider a scenario where you have a cash account with say 100 dollars and you buy 1 share of stock X for 100 dollars. That&#x27;s it, you can no longer trade for two days. Because trades take two days to complete, if you have a cash account you need to wait that full two days before you technically own the shares and can sell it. Until that two day elapses, your account has 0 dollars in it.<p>This is known as free riding:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Free_riding_(stock_market)" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Free_riding_(stock_market)</a><p>Now if you have 100 dollars in your account and you buy stock A for 30 dollars, then your account will have 70 dollars left that you can use to trade with, but this is an incredibly inefficient and impractical way of trading.')
