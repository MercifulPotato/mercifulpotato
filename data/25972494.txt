Item(by='simias', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1612037312, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25971646, text='I do think that in many situations dynamic linking doesn&#x27;t make a whole lot of sense these days (like, say, a JPEG decoding library for instance). But having a central point where you can configure, test and administer networking makes a lot of sense to me.<p>The idea of every application coming up with its own DNS implementation for instance annoys me greatly. I can already anticipate the amount of time I&#x27;m going to lose hunting down the way application X handles DNS because it won&#x27;t apply my system-wide settings.<p>I also firmly believe that SSL&#x2F;TLS should&#x27;ve been handled at the OS level instead of everybody shipping their own custom OpenSSL implementations (complete with various vulnerabilities). I wish I could just use some setsockopt() syscalls to tell the OS I want a secure socket and let it figure out the rest.<p>Moving stuff to higher layers is, IMO, mainly because Google wants to become the new Microsoft&#x2F;Apple and moving that stuff up is a way to take the control away from Windows&#x2F;Mac OS. The browser is the OS. You use web apps instead of apps.<p>You turn every device out there into a glorified ChromeBook.')
