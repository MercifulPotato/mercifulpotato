Item(by='rayiner', descendants=None, kids=[24683975, 24683615], score=None, time=1601852359, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24682772, text='What makes you think that &quot;educational funding&quot; is a problem, or that other countries have &quot;centralized control and funding of schools?&quot;<p>In all but a few states, poorer school districts receive similar funding to rich ones: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;hechingerreport.org&#x2F;in-6-states-school-districts-with-the-neediest-students-get-less-money-than-the-wealthiest" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;hechingerreport.org&#x2F;in-6-states-school-districts-wit...</a>. In several, poor school districts receive <i>more funding</i>.<p>Also, the U.S. is more comparable to the EU than to any individual EU country. Obviously, EU countries make their own funding decisions. But many EU countries push funding decisions even further down to individual localities. Germany and Sweden, for example, have highly decentralized education systems. The same is true for Canada. Indeed, in Canada, an even smaller percentage of school funding comes from federal sources (just 2%) compared to the US (about 5-10%).')
