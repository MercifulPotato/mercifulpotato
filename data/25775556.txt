Item(by='Nextgrid', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610627203, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25775251, text='According to what the reporter is saying it seems to be an off-the-shelf bug with a microphone and SIM card (though I didn&#x27;t see the SIM card myself - either it&#x27;s on the underside of it or I didn&#x27;t pay enough attention) and is powered off wires soldered to the batteries.<p>The reporter mentions that the circuit board touches the display and that it must somehow be able to monitor what&#x27;s being displayed on it but I think he is mistaken given how difficult the task is (this would be a Tempest-style attack: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Tempest_(codename)" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Tempest_(codename)</a> - why bother when you could just hook into the display connector and get the data out of there?).<p>In short it seems to be a microphone bug + GPS tracker, all powered off the iPhone&#x27;s original battery (so that it&#x27;s essentially always on as long as you keep your phone charged) but it doesn&#x27;t seem to be accessing the iPhone&#x27;s data or compromising its operation in any way.')
