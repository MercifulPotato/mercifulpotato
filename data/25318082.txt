You are recapitulating the EPR argument.  The reason the Bell inequalities are a thing is that they refute the EPR argument.  It is <i>not</i> possible for QM to be completed as a local hidden-variable theory.  If it turns out that the information required to predict the result of a QM experiment actually exists, then QM is not merely incomplete, but actually <i>wrong</i>.  That is possible, of course.  But I&#x27;ll give long odds against.')
