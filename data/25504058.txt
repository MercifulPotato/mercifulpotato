Chapter 113 of title 18, United States Code, is amended by inserting after section 2319B the following:<p>2319C. Illicit digital transmission services<p>(a) DEFINITIONS.  In this section<p>(1) the terms &quot;audiovisual work&quot;, &quot;computer program&quot;, &quot;copies&quot;, &quot;copyright owner&quot;, &quot;digital transmission&quot;, &quot;financial gain&quot;, &quot;motion picture&quot;, &quot;motion picture exhibition facility&quot;, &quot;perform&quot;, &quot;phonorecords&quot;, &quot;publicly&quot; (with respect to performing a work), &quot;sound recording&quot;, and &quot;transmit&quot; have the meanings given those terms in section 101 of title 17;<p>(2) the term &quot;digital transmission service&quot; means a service that has the primary purpose of publicly performing works by digital transmission;<p>(3) the terms &quot;publicly perform&quot; and &quot;public performance&quot; refer to the exclusive rights of a copyright owner under paragraphs (4) and (6) of section 106 (relating to exclusive rights in copyrighted works) of title 17, as limited by sections 107 through 122 of title 17; and<p>(4) the term &quot;work being prepared for commercial public performance&quot; means<p>(A) a computer program, a musical work, a motion picture or other audiovisual work, or a sound recording, if, at the time of unauthorized public performance<p>(i) the copyright owner has a reasonable expectation of commercial public performance; and<p>(ii) the copies or phonorecords of the work have not been commercially publicly performed in the United States by or with the authorization of the copyright owner; or<p>(B) a motion picture, if, at the time of unauthorized public performance, the motion picture<p>(i)(I) has been made available for viewing in a motion picture exhibition facility; and (II) has not been made available in copies for sale to the general public in the United States by or with the authorization of the copyright owner in a format intended to permit viewing outside a motion picture exhibition facility; or<p>(ii) had not been commercially publicly performed in the United States by or with the authorization of the copyright owner more than 24 hours before the unauthorized public performance.<p>(b) PROHIBITED ACT.  It shall be unlawful for a person to willfully, and for purposes of commercial advantage or private financial gain, offer or provide to the public a digital transmission service that<p>(1) is primarily designed or provided for the purpose of publicly performing works protected under title 17 by means of a digital transmission without the authority of the copyright owner or the law;<p>(2) has no commercially significant purpose or use other than to publicly perform works protected under title 17 by means of a digital transmission without the authority of the copyright owner or the law; or<p>(3) is intentionally marketed by or at the direction of that person to promote its use in publicly performing works protected under title 17 by means of a digital transmission without the authority of the copyright owner or the law.<p>(c) PENALTIES.  Any person who violates subsection (b) shall be, in addition to any penalties provided for under title 17 or any other law<p>(1) fined under this title, imprisoned not more than 3 years, or both;<p>(2) fined under this title, imprisoned not more than 5 years, or both, if<p>(A) the offense was committed in connec4 tion with 1 or more works being prepared for commercial public performance; and<p>(B) the person knew or should have known that the work was being prepared for commercial public performance; and (3) fined under this title, imprisoned not more than 10 years, or both, if the offense is a second or subsequent offense under this section or section 2319(a).<p>(d) RULE OF CONSTRUCTION.  Nothing in this section shall be construed to<p>(1) affect the interpretation of any other provision of civil copyright law, including the limitations of liability set forth in section 512 of title 17, or principles of secondary liability; or<p>(2) prevent any Federal or State authority from enforcing cable theft or theft of service laws that are not subject to preemption under section 301 of title 17.<p>(b) TABLE OF SECTIONS AMENDMENT.  The table of section for chapter 113 of title 18, United States Code, is amended by inserting after the item relating to section 2319B the following: 2319C. Illicit digital transmission services.')
