Item(by='jcranmer', descendants=None, kids=[24772257], score=None, time=1602631727, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24771446, text='The logical fallacy of &quot;begging the question&quot; is essentially &quot;A because A&quot; although the two propositions are usually worded in a way that obfuscates their identity. For example &quot;opium makes you sleep because it&#x27;s soporific,&quot; where &quot;soporific&quot; is a fancy way of saying &quot;makes you sleep.&quot;<p>In colloquial speech, the phrase &quot;begging the question&quot; is often <i>not</i> used to refer to the logical fallacy but rather the more literal translation of &quot;makes you ask.&quot;')
