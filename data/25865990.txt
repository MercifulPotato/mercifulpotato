Item(by='dragontamer', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611271074, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25864863, text='Skylake has 180 registers. &quot;mov rax, [blah]&quot; performs &quot;rax = malloc()&quot; from this pool of 180-registers &#x2F; &quot;the register file&quot;. Eventually, the retirement unit &quot;garbage collects&quot; the registers that aren&#x27;t used anymore for recycling.<p>Yeah, out-of-order processors are weird. (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Tomasulo_algorithm" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Tomasulo_algorithm</a>)<p>If you have one thread that doesn&#x27;t need a lot of registers for some reason (ie: most of its dependency chains are short, and it has very few memory operations), then you can have a 2nd thread eat up the ~180 registers that it wasn&#x27;t using. &quot;Sharing&quot; the malloc pool of registers.')
