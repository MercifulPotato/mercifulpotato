Item(by='jamesdsadler', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608244126, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25459054, text='Git <i>does</i> use diffs extensively its algorithms, just not in its representation of history. Git&#x27;s &quot;domain model&quot; is blobs, trees, commits, branches and tags.<p>At a lower layer than that (the pack files) it <i>does</i> use binary diffs to store similar objects as deltas to save on disk space.<p>TL;DR diffs play an important part in git&#x27;s machinery at different levels of abstraction. They just aren&#x27;t part of how it represents its domain model.')
