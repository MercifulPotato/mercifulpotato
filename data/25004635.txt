Item(by='ben509', descendants=None, kids=[25004977, 25004768], score=None, time=1604633867, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25003182, text='The only flag I&#x27;d say I want 99% of the time is -E (same as calling egrep) because extended regular expressions are what you almost always want.<p>A sibling comment suggested -i, other useful flags are -w (find whole words) and -C (change the amount of context around matches), but I don&#x27;t think I always use those.<p>A handy function for searching a tree:<p><pre><code>   greptree() {\n       find &quot;$1&quot; -name &quot;$2&quot; -type f -exec egrep &quot;$3&quot; &#x27;{}&#x27; +\n   }\n\n   greptree . &#x27;*.java&#x27; &#x27;foo[123]&#x27;\n</code></pre>\nThat would search all java files in the current tree. One thing people often don&#x27;t know is that -exec ... {} + in modern &#x27;find&#x27; works like piping to xargs.<p>All that said... when I&#x27;m searching a tree, I can usually use &#x27;git grep&#x27;.')
