Item(by='bialpio', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602033898, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24704017, text='The problem in the US, as I understood it, is that there is shortage of workers in IT [1]. To me, it sounds like turning off the stream of potential workers, without fixing the underlying issue. With the current rules (85k H-1Bs a year), it would take 10 years for immigrants to fill all the spots. There may be a lot of untapped talent left in the US (based on under-representation of women, that&#x27;s a discussion for another time), but somehow, the vacancies are not getting filled - what gives?<p>In addition, it seems that even currently, H-1B program has rules to make sure the wages paid are fair [2] - if they are not, it sounds like a problem with enforcement, or with how prevailing wages are calculated.
