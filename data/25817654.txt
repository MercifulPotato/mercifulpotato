Item(by='userbinator', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610938891, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25813601, text='<i>Google is building an internet</i><p>That is the scariest thing about this. Google already has an effective monopoly on information discovery via its search engine, and is rapidly approaching one with what software people use to view information on the Internet. It controls the &quot;web standards&quot; and can change them rapidly with little opposition. Websites that have no business being anything but browser-agnostic, yet are usable only in the latest version of its ever-churning browser (or its not-too-different clones), are already uncomfortably common. For a lot of people, Google <i>is</i> &quot;the Internet&quot;.<p>I don&#x27;t know what the solution is. But there&#x27;s definitely a problem.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=25802366" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=25802366</a>')
