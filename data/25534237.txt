Item(by='pksebben', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608871105, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25526231, text='It&#x27;s for this very reason I&#x27;m so enamored with the concept of organizational fragmenting - gore-tex does it, and it&#x27;s the sort of de facto method of restructuring that goes on in online communities:  the idea is that when you get so big you need to create new vertical controls, you split horizontally - control is never vested in a central authority (instead, teams are responsible for their zone, and communicate with each other when necessary).<p>This has the power to make it much more difficult to achieve economic capture.  It&#x27;s way harder to buy off key figures in 8 different hierarchies than it is to just dump a load of money on a single CEO.<p>(this)[<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.managementexchange.com&#x2F;story&#x2F;innovation-democracy-wl-gores-original-management-model" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.managementexchange.com&#x2F;story&#x2F;innovation-democrac...</a>] is one of the better write-ups on such a structure that I could find.')
