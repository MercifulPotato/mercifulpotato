Item(by='hobofan', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604914976, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25032305, text='There is a difference between one-off bounties and continuous sponsorhips.<p>- Stable income vs. unplannable amount of bounties<p>- Bounties generally don&#x27;t cover general maintenance work for a project (e.g. updating dependencies)<p>- Bounties (as they are implemented today) generally only pay the contributor, and not the maintainers, which can also have significant cost in reviewing a feature<p>- Bounties traditionally have been so disproportionally small compared to the work required that they don&#x27;t come close to provide a reasonable hourly rate for contributors<p>(I think you&#x27;ll find enough articles that go into more details on the difference between the two.)<p>Bounty platforms have been around for ages, and I don&#x27;t know a single project that is able to finance itself from that. Even for the OBS example you mentioned, it the bounty was a good way to get the ball rolling on a specific issue, but the overall maintenance is still financed by monthly sponsoships.<p>One-off donations might be a nice supplement, but they don&#x27;t form a solid foundation.')
