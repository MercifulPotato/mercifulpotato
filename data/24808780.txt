Including previously unknown practices of self-ornamentation, used ochre, pigmented artifacts, and portable art, these findings advance our knowledge of the cultural repertoires of modern humans in Pleistocene Wallacea, including the nonparietal artworks and symbolic material culture of some of the world’s earliest known “cave artists.”<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.pnas.org&#x2F;content&#x2F;114&#x2F;16&#x2F;4105" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.pnas.org&#x2F;content&#x2F;114&#x2F;16&#x2F;4105</a>')
