Item(by='jariel', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609294317, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25576976, text='I think the bifurcation might be more clearly described as &#x27;problem space&#x27; and &#x27;solution space&#x27; complexity.<p>The &#x27;complexity of the problem&#x27; is inherent, by definition.<p>Unless accountants want to literally change the requirements.<p>The complexity of the solution space is something else entirely and it may be hard to derive theoretically ...<p>But with sufficient tooling, such as AI, maybe it becomes trivial.<p>If we have &#x27;AI Apis&#x27; that can immediately handle complex problems and dissolve them into something really simple, then many apparently complex domains could be reduced to something simple.<p>As long as we&#x27;re allowed to use &#x27;other software&#x27; and not have it &#x27;count&#x27; as part of the complexity we are measuring, well, the then complex of the &#x27;app&#x27; tends towards 0.<p>You can already see this a little bit with CRUD apps, which often feel like just gluing some forms together.')
