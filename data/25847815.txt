Item(by='stabbles', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611157797, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25843218, text='Oh come one now, don&#x27;t be silly.<p>Think about vectorization and loop unrolling. It _always_ does a memory load with an offset as a single CPU instruction.<p>As an example a 4 times unrolled sum of doubles on AVX looks like this:<p><pre><code>    L64:\n     vaddpd ymm0, ymm0, ymmword ptr [rcx + 8*rsi]\n     vaddpd ymm1, ymm1, ymmword ptr [rcx + 8*rsi + 32]\n     vaddpd ymm2, ymm2, ymmword ptr [rcx + 8*rsi + 64]\n     vaddpd ymm3, ymm3, ymmword ptr [rcx + 8*rsi + 96]\n     add rsi, 16\n     cmp rdx, rsi\n     jne L64\n</code></pre>\nThe `rcx + 8*rsi + 32` stuff is offsets the compiler generates. Don&#x27;t even think about worrying about -1 here...')
