Item(by='jacobolus', descendants=None, kids=[24885029], score=None, time=1603608111, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24884137, text='I think this is the “virtual trackball” from this SIGGRAPH 1988 paper <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.math.utah.edu&#x2F;~palais&#x2F;Links&#x2F;Chen_Trackball_3-D_rotation.pdf" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.math.utah.edu&#x2F;~palais&#x2F;Links&#x2F;Chen_Trackball_3-D_ro...</a> which I think was also the method described in <i>Graphics Gems</i> circa 1990.<p>A mouse is in general a horrible input device for inputting arbitrary 3d rotations, because it doesn’t have enough degrees of freedom.<p>By far the best tool is a 3-DOF trackball set up so that arbitrary rotations of the ball are directly converted into rotations in the computer.<p>Next best is some other physical device with gyro&#x2F;accelerometer so that (slightly less accurate but still) direct manipulation can be input.<p>Next best after that is something with multitouch or perhaps one of those 6 DOF “space mouse” devices.')
