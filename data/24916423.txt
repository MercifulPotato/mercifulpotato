Item(by='jlokier', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603872698, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24907077, text='In practice, it doesn&#x27;t work like that.<p>There are two major networks, but many vendors of credit cards with very difficult qualities from each other.<p>If you have any problems, it&#x27;s the vendors you will deal with and who decide things like your credit limit, how often that&#x27;s revised (up or down), whether payments are blocked and how to unblock them, how you are notified about transactions, whether paying off the card turns into credit instantly or takes a few days, your interest rates, whether the interest is applied immediately or not, whether you have balance transfer and money transfer, whether you have 0% transfers, cashbacks and other deals, and the kind of customer service you get (whether it&#x27;s on the phone or forced to be via chat, whether they answer quickly and are helpful or take forever and are not), and whether their website and app are down &quot;for maintenance&quot; regularly.<p>Basically almost everything you&#x27;d care about is up to the vendor of which there are many, not the network.')
