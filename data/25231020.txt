I&#x27;m talking about computer numbers, specifically IEEE-754.<p>NaN is represented by a maximal (all ones) exponent, and at least one non-zero in the mantissa.  Infinity has the same exponent but an all-zero mantissa.  So with a naive comparison, NaN compares greater than infinity.<p>If you only believe output from programming languages, javascript claims that the type of infinity is &quot;number&quot;.<p><pre><code>    &gt;&gt;&gt; typeof Number.POSITIVE_INFINITY\n    &quot;number&quot;</code></pre>')
