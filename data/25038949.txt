Giving up U.S. citizenship comes with an expatriation tax [0] on all assets including foreign assets. The purpose of this tax is to deter people from renouncing citizenship and then selling off assets while avoiding paying capital gains on them.<p>So expatriation can protect you from paying taxes on capital gains from future assets, but it doesn&#x27;t do much to protect you from capital gains on assets you currently own.<p>[0] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.irs.gov&#x2F;individuals&#x2F;international-taxpayers&#x2F;expatriation-tax" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.irs.gov&#x2F;individuals&#x2F;international-taxpayers&#x2F;expa...</a>')
