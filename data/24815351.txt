Item(by='repiret', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602992156, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24813376, text='I&#x27;ve done work on a proprietary embedded RTOS that has had high level versions of those barriers at least a decade before the C atomics model was standardized (and compiles them to the closest barrier supported by the target architecture).<p>I suspect that the OS and Architecture communities have known about one-way barriers for a very long time, and they were only recently added to the Arm architecture because people only recently started making Arm CPUs that benefit from them.  And that seems like a more likely explanation than them having been plucked from the C standard.<p>Moreover, one-way barriers are useful regardless of what language you&#x27;re using.')
