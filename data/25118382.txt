Item(by='floatingatoll', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605568959, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25117352, text='I assume that Apple&#x27;s reasoning is &quot;Userspace malware shouldn&#x27;t be able to trivially intercept anti-malware checksum updates&quot;.<p>Trivially is defined as &#x27;click an OK button in a dialog&#x27; as the CF permissions process works in Big Sur today.<p>Malware could still block anti-malware checksum updates with PF, but that requires sudo&#x2F;root which is &#x27;less easy&#x27; than the one-click CF approval process, and PF blocks break so many things on the system that malware can&#x27;t get away with it without triggering a call to technical support.<p>(I personally think that much of the issue here is &#x27;macOS background updates can cause $$$$ cellular data drain over wifi-tethered cellular&#x27;, and that the absence of Low Bandwidth mode in Control Center is <i>one</i> thing that drives users towards these third-party content filters, when they might otherwise not need a third-party product at all.)')
