Item(by='billiam', descendants=None, kids=[25361660], score=None, time=1607535181, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25358504, text='I could be wrong, but despite the astute analysis by Pareto that explains the persistence of elites through &quot;revolutions,&quot; he doesn&#x27;t have too many ideas about how the perfect social mobility that would make societies efficient (governed by the most qualified) can actually be achieved. As I found when covering the revolutions of 1989 in Eastern Europe, the former Communist elites just transformed into the new post-Communist elites, and in many places (Hungary, Poland, Slovenia, etc) they are literally the same people. Or as the great moral philosopher Peter Townsend once said, &quot;meet the new boss, same as the old boss.&quot;')
