You can read more about them below.<p>&gt;&gt; <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;workercenters.com&#x2F;labors-loophole&#x2F;what-is-a-minority-union&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;workercenters.com&#x2F;labors-loophole&#x2F;what-is-a-minority...</a><p>The article posted points this out, but unlike most unions this one wasn&#x27;t formed due to economic reasons. It was formed for social issues that Google isn&#x27;t addressing or is purporting.<p>The current HN discussion seems to all veer towards collective bargaining rights, but that&#x27;s too early to discuss. Don&#x27;t get me wrong that this is a first step to collective bargaining; however, that will only ever be if current membership gets big enough and they get certified by the NLRB. That isn&#x27;t an easy feat in the US and will probably take years to happen!<p>Right now, it would be more interesting to discuss if this type of union will allow employees to have a a real say in Google&#x27;s direction as a business on what projects they will work on and which clients they will accept.<p>Google can&#x27;t do anything about this union because it&#x27;s considered protected concerted activity.<p>&gt;&gt; <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.m.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Protected_concerted_activity" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.m.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Protected_concerted_activity</a>')
