Item(by='DennisP', descendants=None, kids=[25715568, 25715572, 25717316], score=None, time=1610292228, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25715003, text='The article actually talks about their record:<p>&gt;  in late 1997, as the S&amp;P 500 passed its previous 1929 peak of 21x earnings, we rapidly sold down our discretionary U.S. equity positions then watched in horror as the market went to 35x on rising earnings. We lost half our Asset Allocation book of business but in the ensuing decline we much more than made up our losses.<p>For the average investor it might be overly bold to step out of the stock market entirely in times like these. However, there&#x27;s nothing wrong with moving to a more conservative allocation that will do better in crashes, but still perform reasonably well if you hold it that way forever. A great place to start is the sample portfolios at portfoliocharts.com.<p>If you&#x27;re 25 and saving to retire at 65 then maybe don&#x27;t worry about it, but if you&#x27;re 50 that&#x27;s another matter.')
