Item(by='sneak', descendants=None, kids=[25211258], score=None, time=1606321038, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25210670, text='I think that there are a lot of people on HN and similar communities who either didn&#x27;t live through the crypto wars of the 90s or are burned out on the critical privacy and societal implications of networked software and services.<p>It&#x27;s nicer to just &quot;shut up and hack&quot;, but the last time we did that, we ended up with hollywood indirectly dictating what we can do with our own devices. This time the stakes are much higher, as it&#x27;s not just the &quot;hollywood-only&quot; devices like disc players and TVs they&#x27;re getting ready to totally ruin.<p>It&#x27;s disheartening to me to see the trend of people in technology, at least in certain circles, wishing only to enjoy the benefits of giant corporation resources (eg golang, TLS1.3, HTTP2&#x2F;3, v8&#x2F;node, Chrome, Android, iOS) without contemplating the implications of the downsides of giant corporation resources (surveillance, censorship, AMP, javascript hard dependencies, browser monoculture, et c). A system needs to be evaluated on the whole.')
