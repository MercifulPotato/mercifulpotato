Item(by='karaterobot', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603207982, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24836422, text='Goodheart&#x27;s Law says that when a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure. Generally, that&#x27;s because you start optimizing around improving the measurement rather than the conditions the measurement is supposed to depict. In other words, you start cramming for the test, rather than learning the subject.<p>Half serious, half modest proposal: would it be useful for the BLS or OECD, or whoever, to periodically and unexpectedly change how unemployment is measured and calculated, in order to minimize the tendency observed in Goodheart&#x27;s Law?')
