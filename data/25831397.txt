Item(by='stareatgoats', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611047216, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25820966, text='This seems to be an old social network analysis of a primarily Chinese web phenomenon which entails doxing people for various reasons (including corruption in government).<p>The phenomenon is interesting as one example (among many others) of the democratization and &#x27;cutting out the middle man&#x27; that the web offers: citizens taking legal and ethical matters into their own hands. It relates to things like the #metoo movement and Anonymous in the west.<p>Ultimately these &#x27;courts&#x27; tend to devolve into witch-hunts and mob rule without possibility to appeal, but it doesn&#x27;t mean that they don&#x27;t play some positive role - in nudging law enforcement to step up their game if nothing else.<p>This document doesn&#x27;t explore those aspects however.')
