Item(by='lelandbatey', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604608605, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24997882, text='PPM is especially useful for fractals because, as an optionally text-based format, the max brightness of any pixel is arbitrary. This means you can have unlimited color depth, something that&#x27;s really useful when you want to do things like brighten up _really_ dim areas of an image, areas that would be aliased to 0 if represented with only 24 bit color (like in PNG). This was really useful for a toy buddhabrot renderer I wrote a while back:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;lelandbatey&#x2F;rust_buddhabrot" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;lelandbatey&#x2F;rust_buddhabrot</a>')
