Item(by='megameter', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608770521, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25514670, text='I think the thing about the Valley is that it was forged in a context where it was the rational mode of development for a high tech urban center: car-centric office parks and factories, big-box stores and malls, vast housing subdivisions with some toxic waste buried here and there. Although the players in SV were all building &quot;next big things&quot;, everything still ultimately ran on physical presence - real venues that you could go to and make connections in, and the landscape was eminently suited to that between ~1970-2000.<p>It was with the opening of the commercial Internet in the 90&#x27;s that it all started changing. The new companies weren&#x27;t in boxed products destined for retail: they started <i>replacing</i> retail. You didn&#x27;t go to a users&#x27; group to learn, you subscribed to Usenet or email. And so on. So even by the early 2000&#x27;s, things started to feel hollowed out in SV.')
