Item(by='whizzter', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606849244, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25267662, text='It&#x27;s really a situation of the 90&#x2F;10 rule, you&#x27;ll get the first 90% of the benefits from 10% of the work and then it&#x27;s many many incremental changes that gets progressively more expensive.<p>Also in the case of Erlang the language model will reward these first steps even more than most subsequent optimizations because much Erlang code in general don&#x27;t have much tight loops of the kind that are prominent in benchmarks were a good register allocator would provide huge wins.<p>More on the 90&#x2F;10 rule we need to remember that these expensive JIT&#x27;s are very complicated with optimization levels and interpreters combined with tons of GC options whereas here they explicitly just dumped JIT-interpreter cross-calling to simplify the design as well as a more straightforward internal memory model with less complicated edge cases.')
