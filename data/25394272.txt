Item(by='AndrewKemendo', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607736367, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25391535, text='What does &quot;Govern themselves&quot; practically mean in this context?<p>Lets say that I&#x27;m an intelligent person that firmly believes in &quot;rugged individualism&quot; to the point where I&#x27;m an anarcho-georgist [1] and do not recognize the state as an authority for real property allocation based on a well reasoned moral philosophy.<p>Given the fact that there is no unclaimed territory for which I could live without falling under the purview of a state&#x27;s thumb, and hence would not be allowed to &quot;govern myself&quot;, how would I then govern myself consistent with my political philosophy?<p>This is obviously a hyperbolic example, but it&#x27;s IMO an important exercise to explicate how a large group of people with heterogeneous values, is supposed to act&#x2F;behave when a prime mover state system is (in effect) demanding no values be higher than it.<p>[1]<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Geolibertarianism" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Geolibertarianism</a>')
