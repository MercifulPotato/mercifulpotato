Item(by='Balgair', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608311570, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25468416, text='Epstein kinda treats things as a binary: un&#x2F;kind. But I think there is a spectrum of &#x27;environmental kindness&#x27;. Troubleshooting is a good case of this. If all you do is debug a single system, then you&#x27;ll get really good at it, via practice. It starts out environmentally unkind, but ends up being environmentally kind via repeated use. While if you have to debug multiple systems and add in new systems, then you stay in an environmentally unkind situation.<p>Being a mechanic on your own car is one thing, but working in a shop with new cars and problems coming in all the time is another.')
