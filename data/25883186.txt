Item(by='sneak', descendants=None, kids=[25888943], score=None, time=1611417786, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25880912, text='We&#x27;re talking about federated&#x2F;decentralized protocols that Pidgin serves as the type of client for, in a thread about why we don&#x27;t see things like Pidgin on iOS.<p>In a centralized system where the mobile app is made by the same people as the IM server, of course the IM server can send a notification to the app (such as is done in e2e stuff like Signal, or in non-e2e such as Telegram).<p>That falls apart when you&#x27;re talking about, say, ActivityPub on an instance you run yourself, or one not operated by the vendor of the IM app.  The developer&#x27;s server can&#x27;t know when to send you a notification without having some knowledge, from your own e.g. IRC, ActivityPub, or Matrix server, that you have received a new DM.<p>The Matrix&#x2F;Element people have addressed this by running one centralized notification service for every single client of the Matrix&#x2F;Element iOS app.')
