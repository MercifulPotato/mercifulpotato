When you’re stung by people, it’s hard to see the value in continuing to remain open.<p>Regarding the accrual of knowledge and its hardening effects on the soul:<p>&gt; with much wisdom comes much sorrow; the more knowledge, the more grief.<p>&gt; The wise have eyes in their heads, while the fool walks in the darkness; but the same fate overtakes them both.<p>These quotes come from Ecclesiastes, which does not contain a much of a resolution.<p>&gt; A person can do nothing better than to eat and drink and find satisfaction in their own toil.<p>Tao Te Ching makes a similar recommendation: empty hearts and fill bellies. In short, worry less of ideas, and more of literal substances that bind us together (food, drink, and toil).')
