Item(by='spappal', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602722543, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24775706, text='The reason for magnetic field strength falling off as 1&#x2F;r^3 is interesting: the biot-savart law says that magnetic field falls off as 1&#x2F;r^2 from a magnetic source, but in reality sources tend to be better approximated by magnetic dipoles than magnetic monopoles. A &quot;north pole&quot; is always accompanied by a &quot;south pole&quot;, and at distance there are &quot;interaction effects&quot; such that a part of the field strength is &quot;canceled out&quot;.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Magnetic_dipole" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Magnetic_dipole</a><p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Magnetic_monopole" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Magnetic_monopole</a>')
