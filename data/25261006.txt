Item(by='brandmeyer', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606784177, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25260139, text='Big cache lines and big pages together.  16 kB pages combined with 128-byte lines means it can be 8-way set associative and still take advantage of a VIPT structure.<p>Larger pages mean that performance on memory-mapped small files will suffer... which is a use-case that Apple doesn&#x27;t normally care about in its client computers.<p>Larger cache lines mean that highly mulththreaded server loads could suffer from false sharing more often.  Again, its a client computer so who cares?<p>Regarding the definition of &quot;huge&quot;: A64FX uses 256B cache lines.  Granted its a numerical computing vector machine, but still.  Huge covers a lot of ground.')
