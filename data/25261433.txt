There are tons of software solutions – on the web and offline – to visualize the shape of proteins from their sequence of amino acids. How do these work then, if we don&#x27;t know how the atoms might be arranged in space?<p>For example, this[1] is the code for SARS-Cov-2&#x27;s Spike(S) protein. From what I understand of this page it&#x27;s pretty short, only ~1,757 proteins (corresponding to ~3,821 bases in RNA).<p>And here[2] is a visualization of it in 3D. You&#x27;ll likely recognize the characteristic mushroom shape that&#x27;s been portrayed in 3D models of SARS-CoV-2 in the media.
