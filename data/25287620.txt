Item(by='tremon', descendants=None, kids=[25288492], score=None, time=1606992092, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25286873, text='<i>Studies show people with ADHD lack a specialized area of their brains for handling working memory, unlike non-ADHD people. This means that for me, my brain handles working memory tasks analogously to how blind people can learn to &quot;see&quot; with their tongues -- I have to marshal unrelated brain areas to try to make up the difference.</i><p>I don&#x27;t qualify as ADHD, but I&#x27;ve found that I do the same, substituting my auditory memory for working memory. Apparently it&#x27;s much easier for me to keep a train of thought on the rails if I use my language processor for additional short-term storage. I didn&#x27;t even realize I did this, until people started telling me that I shouldn&#x27;t talk out loud during written exams.<p>This may be miles off from your experience, it just lit a spark of recognition.')
