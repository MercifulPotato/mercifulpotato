Item(by='valuearb', descendants=None, kids=[25585942, 25584795, 25584728, 25585044], score=None, time=1609354128, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25584118, text='Manufacturing requires capital. We’ve had some of the highest effective tax rates on capital in the world for a while, and the Trump tax cuts didn’t help that much.<p>A California corporation has to pay roughly 30% of its profits to the state and federal government to start. Then it’s shareholders have to pay 25-30% of what’s left to their state and federal government. In practice that’s over a 50% rate, even if the shareholder is a low income retiree. There is little progressivity in taxing investment profits.<p>No wonder multinational US companies rarely bring profits from foreign subsidiaries back to the US.')
