On that composition: I have no ear for Western classical music, and familiarity with neither the organ as a musical instrument nor the Bible&#x27;s Book of Revelation, but even so, I can guess this project is… unique. He calls it (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cs.stanford.edu&#x2F;~knuth&#x2F;fant.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cs.stanford.edu&#x2F;~knuth&#x2F;fant.html</a>) a &quot;somewhat literal translation&quot; of the book into music. So for example he goes over each verse of the book, and (almost) each time it mentions &quot;angel&quot; he plays an 8-note arpeggio, every time it mentions &quot;blood&quot; he has &quot;coagulated&quot; (clustered) notes, and so on, setting up &quot;dictionary&quot; of 100+ motifs (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cs.stanford.edu&#x2F;~knuth&#x2F;motif-dict-utf8.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cs.stanford.edu&#x2F;~knuth&#x2F;motif-dict-utf8.html</a>). He explains it in these videos (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=-JvdanSygfg" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=-JvdanSygfg</a>, <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=f1R9eY-8jL4" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=f1R9eY-8jL4</a>, <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=e_1a6bHGQGo" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=e_1a6bHGQGo</a>). Looking through his list of musical allusions (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cs.stanford.edu&#x2F;~knuth&#x2F;rev-cite" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cs.stanford.edu&#x2F;~knuth&#x2F;rev-cite</a>) I see things like Bruce Springsteen, even a washing machine&#x27;s power on&#x2F;off cycle sound. He mentions a couple of pieces of rap music for verse Revelation 3:20; maybe the reason is the same joke as <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;xkcd.com&#x2F;133&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;xkcd.com&#x2F;133&#x2F;</a>? Overall, the project seems so crazy (is this the way anyone writes music?) that one can&#x27;t help being impressed.<p>The full performance is here: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=MjxsZa3Ylao" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=MjxsZa3Ylao</a> — haven&#x27;t listened to it, but online I&#x27;ve seen both some extremely negative and some extremely positive comments. :-)<p>I have heard that he is a skilled organ player as you said; just earlier while searching for something else I saw a blog post about Rajeev Motwani&#x27;s memorial (<a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;blog.geomblog.org&#x2F;2009&#x2F;09&#x2F;rajeev-motwani-memorial.html" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;blog.geomblog.org&#x2F;2009&#x2F;09&#x2F;rajeev-motwani-memorial.htm...</a>) which mentions “Don Knuth was the organist for the entire ceremony, and played pieces you didn&#x27;t think could be played on a church organ”.')
