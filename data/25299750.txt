Item(by='madhadron', descendants=None, kids=[25300016], score=None, time=1607064973, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25298377, text='So I recently started a job where I&#x27;m on Windows, and I&#x27;ve found myself surprisingly enamored of PowerShell. So many of the things that the shell got wrong are done right. The authors wanted to use &lt;, &gt;, etc. as their real meanings, but apparently the early users rebelled at the idea that &gt; was no longer redirect to file. But things that would be nightmares in unix shells, such as going in and adding a new element as the last child of only one of four elements in an XML document (based on its attribute value) are a few lines of code. I still have decades of muscle memory in bash, but I find myself reaching for PS more and more.<p>The problem is that you really need an interface for loading software components into your system at runtime, which .NET provides, but aside from .NET or COM I am unaware of any other equivalent system. Java&#x27;s OSGi is the closest, and ugh...')
