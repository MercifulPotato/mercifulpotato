Item(by='AnIdiotOnTheNet', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609258513, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25570952, text='&gt; Hobbies where you can learn&#x2F;hone new skills are more valuable. With video games, there&#x27;s nothing to show for it.<p>Not strictly true. Games are fun because they are a learning experience. Boredom is the result of having learned all that is learnable from a thing, and frustration is the result of continually failing to learn from a thing. In this sense, all games are teaching or honing skills, just ask anyone who&#x27;s finished Getting Over it With Bennett Foddy.<p>You may argue that games teach &quot;worthless&quot; skills, but I would say the same is true of many hobbies and, moreover, not everything valuable is valuable because it can be put on a resume.')
