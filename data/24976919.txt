Item(by='eru', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604384601, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24976776, text='Pedantically enough, by formal definitions algorithms have to finish.  See eg <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Algorithm" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Algorithm</a><p>In practice, speaking informally, people talk about programs that may run forever all the time.<p>People have developed lots of theory around those as well.  Of specific interest here would be the class of programs that provably only runs for a finite time before it produces the next bit of output.<p>You need that latter class of programs to talk about producing digits of pi, or running the main loop of an OS.<p>See also <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Total_functional_programming" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Total_functional_programming</a>')
