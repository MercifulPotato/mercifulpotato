There is an argument to be made (successfully in some cases [1]) that voting power is inversely proportional to the square root of the population. And of course the house seats are distributed in a <i>different</i> which minimizes the relative differences in voters per house seat between states [2].<p>Point being, voting power is a tricky thing to determine.<p>[1]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Penrose_method" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Penrose_method</a><p>[2]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Huntington%E2%80%93Hill_method" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Huntington%E2%80%93Hill_method</a>')
