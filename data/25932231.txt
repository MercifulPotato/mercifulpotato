Item(by='MetaWhirledPeas', descendants=None, kids=[25935225, 25933327], score=None, time=1611773615, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25929060, text='The problem with totally unplugging is that you effectively put your head in the sand. You&#x27;re less informed about what is going on around you. Sure you can read books, but you&#x27;re increasing your risk of exposure to bias or falsehoods when you limit your selection like that. We may be getting pummeled with &quot;fake news&quot; right now, but at least we have the ability to discuss the matter and see more than one side.<p>The early 20th century may have seemed hunky-dory to a lot of people, but there was a lot of bad stuff going on that just never had the chance to see the light of day. I don&#x27;t want to go back to that.<p>Take it to a further extreme, the Amish. I&#x27;m sure many of them think life is great out there on the farm. But they aren&#x27;t going to be much help for solving problems on a national or global scale. Heck even their votes (do they vote?) are probably based on some community consensus or shared local interest. As you narrow your gaze you limit your ability to participate in the world.')
