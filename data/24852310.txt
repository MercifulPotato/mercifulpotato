Item(by='colechristensen', descendants=None, kids=[24852965, 24852751], score=None, time=1603315178, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24852169, text='You won’t be able to take waste heat economically from a power plant, they’re generally designed to capture more and more heat until diminishing returns take over.<p>There are a lot of other industrial processes though that don’t do any heat capturing.<p>Something left out though is that you might have lower capital costs but if you try tacking a power plant on somebody else’s factory, they’re going to want a cut.<p>Efficiency is dictated by temperature differences, you get a bigger cut for less investment when the temperature difference between heat source and environment is largest.')
