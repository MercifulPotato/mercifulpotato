Item(by='manyhats', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603075408, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24822601, text='Equities in a given sector, whether public or private, tend to be highly correlated, but external observers just don&#x27;t really get the full picture unless they dig into the specifics of the situation.<p>IME, private marks in aggregate are less volatile than equivalent public performance for a wide range of reasons, but that doesn&#x27;t mean that a private manager can liquidate a portfolio company at an optimistic mark in a downcycle any more than the manager wouldn&#x27;t be able to get a better price than the last mark in an upswing.<p>I think it&#x27;s also worth clarifying for other readers that the risk you&#x27;re talking about is volatility and has nothing to do with the actual fundamental risks of a given investment.  Private equity (broadly defined) managers look to minimize risks in their investments, but they&#x27;re talking about business and financing risks.  I don&#x27;t think I&#x27;ve ever heard a private equity manager ever talk about minimizing volatility and I&#x27;m ok with that.')
