Item(by='elmolino89', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603088512, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24820410, text='In Egypt you already have long stretches of the Indian Ocean coast without mangrove or any other vegetation.\nBut the problem with mangroves is that these grow more or less in a belt between tropics (~ &lt;= 25degs latitudes), require some rainfall and salinity not exceeding some limits.\nI am not aware of any patch of mangroves on the mediterranean coast.\nLast but not least, these trees are adopted to tides or estuaries with lower salinity.\nYou will not reproduce such conditions in an oversized saharan Dead Sea.<p>See also:\n<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.mdpi.com&#x2F;2071-1050&#x2F;10&#x2F;3&#x2F;646&#x2F;htm" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.mdpi.com&#x2F;2071-1050&#x2F;10&#x2F;3&#x2F;646&#x2F;htm</a>')
