Item(by='dalbasal', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611862382, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25946025, text='I think that &quot;after the dust settles&quot; may not be such a specific point.<p>We sit at our desks and communicate via IM &amp; email anyway. The internet has changed reality, and changed a lot of the underlying logic for the status quo. Social distancing for a whole year is one hell of a catalyst.<p>Meanwhile, there are real economic factors like large housing cost and wage disparities between (often nearby) locations. Reasons why remote isn&#x27;t always good notwithstanding, underlying economics exerts has a ratchet like influence. ...Not to mention globalisation.<p>So yes, I agree that the maximalist prediction is overblown. That said, I think this is a complex chain-of-events type of process that is now going to be moving faster. Companies are now more experienced&#x2F;capable of remote. Employees are more capable of it. People have an understanding of it, how to fix it, etc.<p>It&#x27;s a combination of prevailing wind and gust of wind.')
