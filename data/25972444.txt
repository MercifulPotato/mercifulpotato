Wind farm output typically peaks at night. Wind is a great match for night time battery energy vehicle charging and it has the lowest life cycle CO2 footprint of any electricity source:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Life-cycle_greenhouse_gas_emissions_of_energy_sources#2014_IPCC,_Global_warming_potential_of_selected_electricity_sources" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Life-cycle_greenhouse_gas_emis...</a><p>I do like your emphasis on <i>marginal</i> effects. As renewables and BEVs grow it will be a balancing act to pick the most <i>marginally</i> effective resources for emissions abatement. California may soon reach a point where an additional dollar invested in solar doesn&#x27;t abate as much CO2 as the same dollar invested in transmission, storage, or wind -- even if solar has the lowest instantaneous generation cost.')
