Item(by='bpodgursky', descendants=None, kids=[25115279, 25114441], score=None, time=1605547411, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25113745, text='The thing is, a 3-year life expectancy loss doesn&#x27;t usually mean &quot;you just cut off the last 3 years of your life.&quot;  In many (or most) cases, your years of healthy life adjust downward accordingly.<p>At 27, you have the same medical problems and quality-of-life degradations a healthy person gets at 30.  At 55, you&#x27;re a 58 year old.<p>It&#x27;s really easy to look at a number like that and think &quot;Yeah, I&#x27;d be happy to lose the 80-83 bracket&quot;.  But effectively it&#x27;s the 20-23 range of healthy, happy life you&#x27;ve cut out.  And that IS a big difference.')
