Item(by='brundolf', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1601922851, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24690064, text='On Slack it&#x27;s only ever been an annoyance for me, because at least they visibly tell you when the message eventually fails to send. But on services like Twitter I feel gaslit when I&#x27;ve liked a tweet and then I scroll by it again later, un-liked. It undercuts my faith in the integrity of the entire interface. Any part of it could be lying to me in a way I may never find out about.<p>Overall I strongly dislike this UX pattern. Users should have immediate feedback about actions that haven&#x27;t completed, but that feedback should only tell them the truth: that the operation has <i>begun</i>.')
