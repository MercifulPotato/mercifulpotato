Item(by='arethuza', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603574306, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24881858, text='The natural tree line in Scotland is fairly low - not many trees above 500m. However, the main cause is too many deer - wolves and other predators were killed centuries ago and in most of the Highlands land ended up being used for sporting estates that naturally wanted plenty deer to shoot.<p>Fortunately rewilding is now a big thing in Scotland - the white tailed sea eagle has been reintroduced, beavers are are increasingly common and people talk about reintroducing lynx and maybe even wolves.<p>Trees for Life have a nice video about Glen Affric:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=pyC-w67yz8A" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=pyC-w67yz8A</a><p>One thing that is a huge shame is that natural forest growth really only happens on islands in lochs or in fenced off areas where the deer are kept out.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;treesforlife.org.uk&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;treesforlife.org.uk&#x2F;</a>')
