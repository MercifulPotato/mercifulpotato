Item(by='Viliam1234', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603746129, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24896199, text='Depending on how frequent are complex equations in your code, you could make a macro for it, and write it e.g. like this:<p><pre><code>  (math 5 * f (x + 2))\n</code></pre>\nI might be tempted to do so if I frequently encountered math expressions of depth 3 or more, which in my short Lisp career I didn&#x27;t.<p>Also, I would probably rewrite the expression as:<p><pre><code>  (* 5 (f (+ x 2)))\n</code></pre>\nto make it easier to read by putting the simple operand first; or in case of two difficult operands I would use formatting:<p><pre><code>  (* (f (+ x 2))\n     (g (+ x 3)))\n</code></pre>\nNote that a good editor would check that the parentheses match the formatting, so I wouldn&#x27;t really count that 3 closing parentheses are needed in the last line; they would be inserted automatically.')
