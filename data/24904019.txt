Item(by='1vuio0pswjnm7', descendants=None, kids=[24904550, 24924330], score=None, time=1603766305, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24903775, text='&quot;This was not a DMCA 512 notice.&quot;<p>It is remarkable how few people commenting on the web are noticing that.<p>A notice under section 512 needs to identify both the material infringed and the infringing material.<p>The letter identifies some works that may be infringed, e.g., VEVO videos listed in youtube-dl&#x27;s documentation.<p>However it does not identify the location of any infringing material, e.g., an unauthorised copy of a VEVO video.<p>It cannot be a valid DMCA 512 notice.<p>The letter instead refers to section 1201 which includes a prohibition against enabling circumvention of copyright protections.<p>The argument is that youtube-dl violates that prohibition.<p>Online debate about the applicability of the DMCA in this case should focus on section 1201 not 512.<p>For example, is youtube-dl &quot;<i>primarily</i> designed for the purpose of circumventing a technological measure that effectively controls access to a work protected [by copyright]&quot;  Do most YouTube video URLs use enciphered signatures.  Not IME.  Does the rolling cipher &quot;control access&quot; to YouTube videos, or does it just control downloading.  Etc.<p>1201. Circumvention of copyright protection systems<p>(a) Violations Regarding Circumvention of Technological Measures.<p>(2) No person shall manufacture, import, offer to the public, provide, or otherwise traffic in any technology, product, service, device, component, or part thereof, that-<p>(A) is primarily designed or produced for the purpose of circumventing a technological measure that effectively controls access to a work protected under this title;<p>(B) has only limited commercially significant purpose or use other than to circumvent a technological measure that effectively controls access to a work protected under this title; or<p>(C) is marketed by that person or another acting in concert with that person with that person&#x27;s knowledge for use in circumventing a technological measure that effectively controls access to a work protected under this title.')
