View frameworks like React and Vue are a waste of CPU cycles and network bandwidth. Not only are they packaged with the application itself, but they also proxy objects (`reactive`) so they can do their hooking. The new way is to compile one&#x27;s app into an artifact that uses something along the lines of Apple&#x27;s Clang Automatic Reference Counting to insert re-rendering statements in appropriate areas of the code.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;svelte.dev&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;svelte.dev&#x2F;</a>\n[2] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Automatic_Reference_Counting" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Automatic_Reference_Counting</a>')
