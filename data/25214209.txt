Item(by='antonfire', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606337845, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25192719, text='A polynomial with a single variable is more or less what you would mean by &quot;use base-infinity digits&quot; in the first place.<p>Roughly, in base 10, the number represented by the sequence of digits d_0, d_1, ..., d_n is d_0 + d_1 * 10 + d_2 * 10^2 + ... + d_n * 10^n.<p>In &quot;base infinity&quot;, the &quot;number&quot; represented by the sequence of digits d_0, d_1, ..., d_n is the polynomial d_0 + d_1 * x + d_2 * x^2 + ... + d_n * x^n.<p>Multiplying these &quot;numbers&quot; (polynomials) is like decimal multiplication, except the digits can be arbitrarily high and you do no carrying at all.')
