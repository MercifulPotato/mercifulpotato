Existing guns were grandfathered in.<p>Guns have many interchangeable parts. The part of the gun that the government considers to be the &quot;legal definition&quot; is a part called the lower receiver. You can change almost anything about a gun as long as you keep the lower receiver and comply with any other laws.<p>So if you buy an old MAC-10 lower receiver that was manufactured before the new ATF policy, and add an adapter [1] that allows it to be combined with the non-lower parts from an AR-15, you can create a completely legal Frankenrifle with mostly new parts!<p>This isn&#x27;t even the craziest thing you can do with gun loopholes in the USA. &quot;Pistol braces&quot; [2], originally made for war veterans with amputations to shoot small rifles, have pretty much obsoleted restrictions on short-barreled rifles. &quot;Ghost guns&quot; [3], i.e. lower receivers that have no serial number and do not appear in any database, are easily made by anyone with access to CNC tools (including a few hours rental for a few hundred bucks). 3D printed pistols are becoming a thing [4], although they are not very durable.<p>1: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;aandsconversions.com&#x2F;2018&#x2F;11&#x2F;25&#x2F;the-next-big-thing-for-the-mac-style-family-of-registered-receiver-machine-guns&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;aandsconversions.com&#x2F;2018&#x2F;11&#x2F;25&#x2F;the-next-big-thing-f...</a><p>2: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.sb-tactical.com&#x2F;product-category&#x2F;brace&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.sb-tactical.com&#x2F;product-category&#x2F;brace&#x2F;</a><p>3: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Ghost_gun" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Ghost_gun</a><p>4: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.reddit.com&#x2F;r&#x2F;guns&#x2F;comments&#x2F;jzik44&#x2F;magdump_monday_3d_printed_glock_frame_emptying_a&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.reddit.com&#x2F;r&#x2F;guns&#x2F;comments&#x2F;jzik44&#x2F;magdump_monday...</a>')
