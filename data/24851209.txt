Item(by='behindsight', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603308815, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24850678, text='AGPL is compatible with the OSI&#x27;s definition of &quot;open-source&quot;.<p>Those other two licenses you listed are either fully non-commercial (polyform) or have a clause preventing cloud hosting SaaS (timescale).<p>I am not going to get into discussion about who has the right to define &quot;open-source&quot;, but having the right to commercially profit off of the software is one of the key OSI defined tenets which those two licenses lack.<p>In the end, AGPL may be able to achieve the same goal of preventing parasitic SaaS solutions that don&#x27;t contribute back into the community.')
