Item(by='collyw', descendants=None, kids=[24662478, 24662791, 24662453], score=None, time=1601645345, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24662257, text='Thomas Sowell makes an interesting point that very few people stay in the same categories of rich or poor in their lifetimes. Young people make up the majority of the poor, but after working and gaining experience the vast majority move out of that category.<p>I am pretty sure I am an example of that. Graduated in the dot com bust in 2001 and was unemployed for a year and a half. Apparently my partner and I are now in the top 10% of household earnings for our country. (<i>Household</i> earnings being another very misleading statistic - as people become paid better they move out of shared houses, making things look worse when they are actually getting better).')
