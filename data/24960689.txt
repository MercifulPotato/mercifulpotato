Item(by='radford-neal', descendants=None, kids=[24971096], score=None, time=1604249435, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24958785, text='It&#x27;s not noted in the article, but it may be important that Stentor is a ciliate (same group as paramecium).  Ciliates are very elaborate single-celled organisms, with the unusual feature of having a &quot;macronucleus&quot; in addition to a &quot;micronucleus&quot; that is similar to the single nucleus of other eukaryote cells. The macronucleus contains numerous copies of DNA from the micronucleus, which could provide a lot of scope for states that are represented in terms of gene activations, allowing behaviours such as described in the article.')
