See: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;scholarlycommons.law.hofstra.edu&#x2F;cgi&#x2F;viewcontent.cgi?article=2430&amp;context=hlr" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;scholarlycommons.law.hofstra.edu&#x2F;cgi&#x2F;viewcontent.cgi...</a><p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;startingpointsjournal.com&#x2F;what-is-the-original-meaning-of-the-establishment-clause&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;startingpointsjournal.com&#x2F;what-is-the-original-meani...</a><p>&gt; House’s final version: “Congress shall make no law establishing religion.”<p>&gt; The Senate responded with: “Congress shall make no law establishing articles of faith or a mode of worship.”<p>&gt; The Conference Committee came up with the final version, “Congress shall make no law respecting an establishment of religion.” The Committee’s addition of “respecting an” was not explained at the time but has been the subject of a great deal of recent commentary. In its own time, however, the clause generated none of the heated arguments that had accompanied church–state issues in the states.')
