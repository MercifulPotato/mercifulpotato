That&#x27;s what the &#x27;00 tech bubble was -- we&#x27;re prone to HUGE biases and it is important to have constant reminders to bring us back to reality.<p>The founders who have succeeded are known to be _irrationally_ optimistic, literally irrational. This means, statistically, it&#x27;s a poor choice to do what they did. It means, in some sense of the word, they got lucky. Many founders publicly acknowledge this -- luck was a huge factor.<p>If you want money, being a founder _might_ get you there, but odds are, it won&#x27;t.<p>Being a founder makes the most sense when you&#x27;re obsessed with solving a problem and the money is a perk, not a motivator.<p>Transparency: ex-founder of Series B company')
