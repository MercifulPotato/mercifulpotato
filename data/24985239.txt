Item(by='rsync', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604446463, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24983867, text='I am willing to stipulate that exclusionary zoning (like many other pieces of our laws and jurisprudence) has been used many times in bad faith and to ill effect.<p>As a literate person versed in current events, <i>of course</i> I am aware of this history.<p>That does not mean, however, that exclusionary zoning should not and should never have existed.<p>&quot;People who run factories aren&#x27;t going to tear down a house and try and wedge a factory there.&quot;[1]<p>When your starting point is well organized, well planned, well intentioned environments it&#x27;s very easy to underestimate the effects of loosening these restrictions that never made any sense to you and why can&#x27;t we have a cute little pub right here anyway ?<p>On the other hand, if you have <i>actually grown up</i> in a place where people had horse corrals next to light industrial with a smattering of houses in-between and oh, look, that&#x27;s an auto-shop on the back of their residential lot ... you would know that the bullshit people pull in absence of these rules is <i>incredible and fascinating</i>.<p>When they started enforcing some rules there[2] it had nothing to do with race and <i>everything to do</i> with the fact that one quarter of the town was running unpermitted septic systems because, by god, they were not going to pay for city sewer.  Also, llamas.<p>[1] A family member of mine did exactly this.<p>[2] Canon City, Colorado')
