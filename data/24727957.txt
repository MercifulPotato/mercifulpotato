Item(by='veddox', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602232315, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24716635, text='You have to remember that HN users aren&#x27;t a representative subset of software developers world-wide.<p>Part of that may be the continuing influence of PG: I both learnt Lisp and found HN because of his essays, and I suspect I&#x27;m not the only one.<p>But also, the HN community generally has an academic fondness for new and niche languages, so a language&#x27;s popularity on HN is a poor proxy for its popularity in the business world.<p>(A last point: I think the diminished importance of Lisp in the last decades is less due to an inherent flaw of the language and more to what you might call &quot;accidents of history&quot; - particularly its close association with early attempts at AI.)')
