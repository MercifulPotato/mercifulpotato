The journal in question[2] claims to have (or have had) an impact factor[3] of 0.593 (2017-18); &quot;the ratio between the number of citations received in that year [...] and the total number of &quot;citable items&quot; published in that journal[...]&quot;. That&#x27;s not a very good score. Nature has an impact factor of ~42 (2019), or to pick another example, because I recently referred to it here on HN, Eurosurveillance has an impact factor of ~6.<p>I don&#x27;t think blindly following these metrics is a good idea, but it&#x27;s not as a bad first approximation.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Journal_ranking" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Journal_ranking</a><p>[2] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;juniperpublishers.com&#x2F;ofoaj&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;juniperpublishers.com&#x2F;ofoaj&#x2F;</a><p>[3] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Impact_factor" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Impact_factor</a>')
