Item(by='makecheck', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605991311, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25171532, text='<i>Where it works</i>, SwiftUI is indisputably the most compact and maintainable form of data bindings <i>and</i> auto-layout that you have.  It <i>should</i> be used immediately for those working cases.<p>It is therefore critical to also learn the bridges (e.g. how to get a platform-specific view or controller out of SwiftUI) because you will <i>also</i> want those <i>somewhere</i>.<p>Note, I have seen Herculean attempts on blogs, etc. to shoehorn ungodly amounts of hacks into SwiftUI objects to get certain behaviors that don’t exist yet; That Is Wrong.  Do <i>not</i> fight this framework; either use a native element with full functionality and a bridge, or a succinct SwiftUI description.  It is clear that “someday” everything will have a nice SwiftUI version and it isn’t worth faking it for now wherever it doesn’t work yet.')
