Item(by='kazinator', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1601999484, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24697965, text='Alan Perlis epigram #32: <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cs.yale.edu&#x2F;homes&#x2F;perlis-alan&#x2F;quotes.html" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cs.yale.edu&#x2F;homes&#x2F;perlis-alan&#x2F;quotes.html</a><p>&quot;Programmers are not to be measured by their ingenuity and their logic but by the completeness of their case analysis.&quot;<p>Well-engineered programs cover all the cases: they handle what they must handle, and have an answer for the rest which is better than &quot;oh shoot, didn&#x27;t think of that&quot;.<p>The main programmer skill is remembering to ask yourself, &quot;under what conditions won&#x27;t this work?&quot; or &quot;what inputs or situations break this?&quot; and being able to come up with excellent answers.<p>Also, asking this question: &quot;though this appears to work, where are all the requirements (language, library, platform, ...) coming from which make it work, and are any missing?&quot;')
