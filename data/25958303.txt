Item(by='xpe', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611934622, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25957724, text='&gt; Furthermore, the line between amateur and professional blurs quickly when the amateurs start selling their products. Once someone starts a Kickstarter or opens a store to sell their amateur board game, they are effectively professionals. They tend to start behaving as such.<p>Yes, in the sense that they have to factor in &quot;professional&quot; responsibilities, such as paying attention to costs; e.g. production and marketing.<p>However, I think when many people say &quot;professional game designer&quot; I think they may (rightly or wrongly) include &quot;seasoned&quot; (i.e. with a track record).<p>Of course, both amateurs and professionals may be seasoned. And both may have passion and dedication to their work.')
