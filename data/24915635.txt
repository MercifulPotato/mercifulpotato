Though I am not an expert on the topic, I can recommend a few things. First, there are three directions the industry is heading with &quot;zero trust&quot; thing.<p>(1) Zero trust access (like BeyondCorp, protects application and services when a user, user credentials, user devices are compromised)<p>(2) Network micro-segmentation (contain impact when one network segment is compromised, dynamic network assignment)<p>(3) Zero trust browsing (protection for users from getting infected with malicious contents served by trusted but compromised websites)<p>Honestly, I am only more familiar with zero trust access, and for this, I can recommend you first read -&gt; BeyondCorp A New Approach to Enterprise Security [0] by Google. The trend was kickstarted from that paper<p>0: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;research.google&#x2F;pubs&#x2F;pub43231&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;research.google&#x2F;pubs&#x2F;pub43231&#x2F;</a>')
