Item(by='cbkeller', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602577546, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24763237, text='This is fairly far from my field, but as I understand it, that would be a <i>hidden variable</i> interpretation of QM [1], and specifically in that analogy a <i>local</i> hidden variable theory. That&#x27;s what Einstein himself wanted.<p>There is a famous test, Bell&#x27;s inequality [2], that specifically rules out <i>local</i> hidden variable interpretations of QM.<p>Nonlocal hidden variable interpretations, such as  De Broglie - Bohm theory [3], are potentially still on the table, however.<p>It is somewhat ironic that Bell&#x27;s theorem is sometimes presented in popular media as a general disproof of <i>all</i> hidden variable theories, in a context where locality is taken for granted --  because Bell himself seems to have been partial to <i>nonlocal</i> hidden variable theories. An article by the same Mermin mentioned in the OP is worth a read, on this subject [4].<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Hidden_variable_theory" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Hidden_variable_theory</a><p>[2] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Bell%27s_theorem" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Bell%27s_theorem</a><p>[3] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;De_Broglie%E2%80%93Bohm_theory" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;De_Broglie%E2%80%93Bohm_theory</a><p>[4] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cqi.inf.usi.ch&#x2F;qic&#x2F;Mermin1993.pdf" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cqi.inf.usi.ch&#x2F;qic&#x2F;Mermin1993.pdf</a>')
