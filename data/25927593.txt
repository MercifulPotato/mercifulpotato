Item(by='eru', descendants=None, kids=[25928616], score=None, time=1611751914, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25927224, text='At some basic level, banks do lend out their savers deposits.<p>The multiplier effect comes about because after the loan the bank&#x27;s debtors obviously have money, but also the depositors treat their (loaned out!) deposits as if they were money.  (And the depositors usually don&#x27;t take out the loans as cash, but as deposits with some bank or another.)<p>There&#x27;s some complications with regulation, central bank reserves etc.  But fundamentally, banks lend out deposits.')
