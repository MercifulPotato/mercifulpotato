Their goal is usually to ensure people have the code to change how their software and hardware works and are able to actually make those changes. The original GPL tried to address the first concern. GPL 3 was an update made in light of companies like Apple, Amazon, and Google (and Tivo) making billions off open source software and not contributing much if anything back to the original project while not letting people touch the operating system--usually based substantially on open source code--on their hardware.')
