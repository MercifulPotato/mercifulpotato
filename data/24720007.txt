Item(by='saalweachter', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602170855, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24719920, text='This is, actually, an anti-feature for some people.<p>People don&#x27;t like things they don&#x27;t like to be effective, because they&#x27;d like to be able to argue on that basis as well.<p>&quot;We shouldn&#x27;t torture people because it is cruel and a violation of their human rights&quot; is a purely moral argument; &quot;We shouldn&#x27;t torture people because it is cruel and a violation of their human rights, and it isn&#x27;t even effective at extracting useful information or true confessions, study after study has shown blah blah blah&quot; is a moral argument with an effectiveness chaser thrown in.  Even if you don&#x27;t share the same moral framework as me, I can still possibly persuade you to act in a way I see as moral because acting immorally doesn&#x27;t gain you anything but wanton cruelty.<p>People love to do this for all sorts of arguments, from taxes to environmental or fiscal policies to their monopoly house rules.<p>Granted, most of the time people also just pull their effectiveness claims out of their ass, but it is a very effective rhetorical technique.')
