Item(by='gnfargbl', descendants=None, kids=[24958749], score=None, time=1604228222, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24958599, text='There are two different mechanisms of interest here: IP fragmentation (IP packets get broken up into smaller chunks, because intermediate networks have an MTU which is too large to allow them to pass) and TCP segmentation (the TCP stack breaks up payloads into segments no bigger than the requested MSS).<p>The author here presents two variants of the attack. The UDP variant does indeed rely on IP fragmentation, but the TCP version does not. To mitigate the TCP version, the ALG needs to be aware of the state of the TCP connection. Simple IP fragment reassembly won&#x27;t be enough.')
