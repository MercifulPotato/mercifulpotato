Item(by='lkrubner', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607329791, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25328809, text='Suppose a professor has a Very Great reputation, and another professor has an Ordinary Great reputation.<p>You take a class with each.<p>From the Very Great professor you get several maxims that don’t seem to actually help you, nor can you figure out how to apply them to your work.<p>From the Ordinary Great professor you get a bunch of advice that you can easily apply to your own work, and you feel that you’ve immediately improved in the subject.<p>Is it jealousy to then say the Very Great professor seems to have a reputation that exceeds his actual skill?<p>That’s how I read the comparison of Graham to Hickey.<p>It’s an important issue, especially if Very Great has a prominent position in the industry and is using that prominence in harmful ways.')
