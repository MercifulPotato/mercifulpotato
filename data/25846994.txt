Item(by='DonaldFisk', descendants=None, kids=[25847801], score=None, time=1611153932, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25833557, text='Pascal, contrary to what the article states, allows any subrange of integer as an array index.   For example,<p><pre><code>    kernel: array[-3..3,-3..3] of real;\n</code></pre>\nis a 7x7 array.   It has negative indices, e.g.<p><pre><code>   x := kernel[-3,-3];\n</code></pre>\nSee <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.tutorialspoint.com&#x2F;pascal&#x2F;pascal_arrays.htm" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.tutorialspoint.com&#x2F;pascal&#x2F;pascal_arrays.htm</a><p>Sometimes you want negative indices, e.g. when performing a convolution on an image with a kernel.')
