Here&#x27;s [2] an article showing that such removed ballots sometimes get a larger share than a candidate, and that the rate is larger than the close state elections the US just had. Here&#x27;s [3] some UK elections where 2.9% of all ballots were discarded because they were filled out wrong.<p>So I don&#x27;t think the UK evidence shows what you claim it does. The smallest UK spoilt ballot error I can find is larger than the winning margins in many US places.<p>Finally, here&#x27;s [4] one analysis of voting tech versus ballot spoilage rates that seems to reach similar conclusions of all the papers I scanned over.
