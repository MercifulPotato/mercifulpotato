Item(by='gnfargbl', descendants=None, kids=[25275830], score=None, time=1606912302, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25275532, text='&gt; Looks like you can&#x27;t be arrested for accidentally using an unsecured Wi-Fi network.<p>You probably haven&#x27;t committed a crime if you <i>accidentially</i> use an unsecured Wi-Fi network, but if you <i>deliberately</i> use one then the situation is less clear. Section 1 of the CMA (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.legislation.gov.uk&#x2F;ukpga&#x2F;1990&#x2F;18&#x2F;section&#x2F;1" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.legislation.gov.uk&#x2F;ukpga&#x2F;1990&#x2F;18&#x2F;section&#x2F;1</a>) requires only that you &quot;<i>[cause] a computer to perform any function with intent to secure access to any program or data held in any computer, or to enable any such access to be secured</i>,&quot; that &quot;<i>the access [you intend] to secure, or to enable to be secured, is unauthorised</i>&quot; and that you &quot;<i>[know] at the time [you cause] the computer to perform the function that that is the case.</i>&quot;<p>How does a law so broadly worded translate to technical reality? Many of us in the industry don&#x27;t really know, and as a result feel that the UK&#x27;s computer misuse laws really are crying out for reform. See <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cyberupcampaign.com&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cyberupcampaign.com&#x2F;</a> for more.')
