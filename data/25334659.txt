Item(by='nradov', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607363207, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25334352, text='New England had something close to (or even superior to) Silicon Valley. Most of the leading minicomputer companies were headquartered in the Boston area, and benefitted from the world-class universities there. But that industry collectively missed disruptive changes from minicomputers to microcomputers, and then the Internet.')
