Item(by='rcoveson', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611514988, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25894512, text='I wouldn&#x27;t say &quot;no problem whatsoever.&quot; Lots of things encode Unix time as a 32 bit signed integer, which can&#x27;t date back further than 1901.<p>Any fixed-with type is going to have a lower- and upper-bound; whether it has a sign or not doesn&#x27;t change that.<p>If git was trying to choose a bound for a 32 bit timestamp, I think 1970 is a reasonable starting point; doubling your future space is more important than covering space in the past. If it&#x27;s 64-bit, though, then it&#x27;s kind of silly to not just envelop the entirety of human history with all the room you&#x27;ve got (as a 64 bit signed integer with 0=year 1970 does).')
