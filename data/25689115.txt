Item(by='blunte', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610134283, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25683727, text='I would trust Apple over Facebook or Google any day for the simple reason that Apple makes its money by selling hardware (and being a monopolistic gatekeeper of many of the apps that run on that hardware).  WRT the gatekeeper aspect, which I don&#x27;t like, at least it is very clear what the business model is.<p>Suffice to say, they aren&#x27;t depending on our data for marketing purposes to earn money.  If Apple opens an advertising division, then I will start to distrust them as much or more than the other two.<p>Sames goes for Microsoft.  I would not be surprised if MS used my data to try to sell me Office or Windows, but I would be very surprised if they were selling my data to outside parties.<p>In summary, if the company proving the service you use primarily provides &quot;free&quot; services, then they should be suspect.  Find out where their revenue comes from.  Chances are, it comes from mining your data.')
