Item(by='xalava', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603077549, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24822801, text='There is a key difference in nature, with some practical consequences.<p>You don&#x27;t have money in your bank account, the bank has a debt toward you. When you wire money or pay with a credit card, it triggers a chain of intermediaries to move that debt. It is costly in part because of the counterparty risks. In most countries, an individual however is protected from a bank default up to a certain amount.<p>Cash is directly minted by the central bank. In a trustless manner, if you exchange goods and services for cash, the settlement is instantaneous. Legally, this cash can be used anywhere in your country.<p>For everyday use, it might not change habits overnight, but it will have a long term impact on the financial infrastructure.')
