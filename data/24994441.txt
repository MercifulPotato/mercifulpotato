Item(by='cwkoss', descendants=None, kids=[24999363], score=None, time=1604535152, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24993928, text='The research and anecdotal reports coming out about microdosing is pretty exciting for the field of depression treatment.<p>Though some take greater dosages, many people are finding relief from sub-perceptual doses: indicating that the typical hallucinations and disorientation associated with recreational psilocybe use is likely not necessary for achieving anti-depressive effect.<p>I&#x27;ve also read of some interesting work being done creating &quot;anti-abuse&quot; formulations, where the active ingredients are compounded with Niacin or other drugs that cause unpleasant physical side effects at high doses to discourage overdose&#x2F;abuse&#x2F;recreational use. &quot;Stamet&#x27;s stack&quot; seems to be the most common of these.<p>Definitely needs much more research into these compounds, but seems a very promising frontier for antidepressant pharmacology.')
