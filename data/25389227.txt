Item(by='dragonwriter', descendants=None, kids=[25389436], score=None, time=1607712762, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25387094, text='&gt; All the articles on the shortage of COBOL programmers is actually companies whining that they can’t find cheap labor.<p>The word “COBOL” isn’t necessary in that sentence.<p>And, for that matter, “programmers” could be replaced with any skilled work field for which such complaints exist.<p>An actual shortage of labor (where the demand curve is above the supply curve at all price points) is theoretically possible, but in the real world nearly 100% of complaints about labor shortages are really complaints that the intersection of the supply an demand curves are at a point that results in purchasers of labor making less profit from whatever they are buying that labor for than they’d like, and laborers making more than those employers would like.')
