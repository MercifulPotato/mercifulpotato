Item(by='lostcolony', descendants=None, kids=[25259278, 25258198], score=None, time=1606762092, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25255854, text='So having written production Erlang, I have literally never had an issue with large amounts of problematic mails in the mailbox. It&#x27;s like, yeah, technically it&#x27;s possible, since there&#x27;s no forced constraints around what kinds of messages a given process can be sent (which is intentional; after all, the process may enter a later receive statement that knows how to handle those kinds of messages), but it&#x27;s really easy to do correctly. And you can always program defensively if you want to limit the kinds of messages you respond to by having a wildcard pattern match at the end of a receive.')
