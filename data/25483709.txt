Item(by='joe_the_user', descendants=None, kids=[25484685, 25484308, 25483851], score=None, time=1608436038, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25483525, text='I&#x27;ve done a variety of meditation techniques and certainly appreciate the benefits they may offer.<p>One thing I&#x27;d say is that it&#x27;s not always the techniques that feel the hardest that actually offer the most benefits. I believe Buddha&#x27;s term &quot;middle way&quot; referred to a way that was in the middle between extreme privation&#x2F;altered consciousness and the ordinary world. I think those interested in meditation techniques should consider this.<p>And in terms of long term silent retreats, I had an old friend who&#x27;s schizophrenic symptoms were intensified by retreats of that sort and meditation in general isn&#x27;t a thing that&#x27;s riskless - I recall the possibility of serious reactions to meditation and intensive meditation in particular has been documented in a number of articles.<p>Which relates to claims about &quot;All problems&quot;')
