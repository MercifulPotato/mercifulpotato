The people who have these jobs have created them; they weren&#x27;t handed these jobs by their employers.<p>This often requires going above and beyond the pay grade, doing not only your work but whatever needs to be done. i.e: it is a momentary expenditure of a scarce ressource to reach escape velocity.<p>This is something I believe the majority of people will shy away from for fear of being a sucker  or because they&#x27;d work &quot;more than they&#x27;re compensated for&quot;. I understand where they&#x27;re coming from and that people have different circumstances and that there are abusive employers who&#x27;ll take advantage of you, but you won&#x27;t know if the envelope is set by yourself or your employer unless you expand it. If you do what most people do, you get what most people get.<p>There <i>is</i> a risk, but if you&#x27;re in a good organization, there is payoff, and if you are in a bad organization, you&#x27;ll get to know that quicker than if you had worked at a leisurely pace.<p>What I mean by going above and beyond: being good at the job and tasks you have been given is table steaks. That&#x27;s your official job. Your mandate, though, is to make your team and organization win. You can go ahead and be helpful and you&#x27;ll be dealing with a lot of problems that will knock boredom out of you.')
