Item(by='mooreds', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609181098, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25558813, text='I&#x27;m not sure what level of permissions is available with &quot;sign in with google&quot;. In my experience, that is handleded within the SaaS. Here&#x27;s what&#x27;s happened (at least in the companies I&#x27;ve worked in, which tend to be a bit smaller).<p>1. New employee hired\n2. They get a gsuite account. This gives them access to google drive, gmail, gcal, etc. (This is the provisioning I think you are referring to.)\n3. They can now login to asana, zapier, &lt;other saas tool&gt; with their gsuite account, using &quot;login with google&quot;\n4. Their permissions within these saas tools are managed by the admins in those tools (not from gsuite).\n5. When an employee departs, you disable their gsuite account. Then they can&#x27;t log in to any of the SaaS tools, since their google account is disabled.<p>If you want a user to have centralized RBAC or ABAC, you need to use a real IdP, not gsuite in the way I outline above.<p>If you are using SAML for gsuite, you can use SCIM, I believe, to provision, but that&#x27;s a different flow than I have outlined above. <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;support.google.com&#x2F;a&#x2F;topic&#x2F;6400789" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;support.google.com&#x2F;a&#x2F;topic&#x2F;6400789</a> has more on that.')
