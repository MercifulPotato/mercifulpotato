Item(by='ChrisMarshallNY', descendants=None, kids=[25606396], score=None, time=1609529871, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25605211, text='I am sorry to hear this.<p>I was a manager for 25 years, at a renowned corporation, that wasn’t fun, but didn’t have too much of this kind of thing.<p>When I became a manager, my personal vow was to “be the manager I always wanted.”<p>Contrary to popular mythology, this was <i>not</i> “Become a doormat.” As anyone that worked for me can tell you, I could be quite firm.<p>But that was the exception; not the rule.<p>Kindness and empathy pay <i>huge</i> dividends, as a manager —as long as they are “for real.” In my experience, artificial empathy gets detected quite quickly, by the types of folks we manage (smart ones).<p>But this should not be considered just an “Apple” problem. It happens <i>everywhere</i>, and, unlike in the movies, where the villain always gets their comeuppance, the perpetrators often thrive, establishing ugly corporate environments.<p>Sadly, their bosses are often quite aware of how bad they are, but they “get results,” and results are the bottom line.')
