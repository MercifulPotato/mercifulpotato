If the search criteria matches 100 rows at the time of the request, you may want to page through <i>those</i> matches, even if, by the time the client (human or machine) gets to page three, the query matches 80 or 110 rows - or worse, if the query still matches 100 rows, but not all of them are the same as the original 100!<p>You would normally capture such state by using cursors.')
