Item(by='stereolambda', descendants=None, kids=[25773235], score=None, time=1610575477, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25764562, text='I see all this as a heartwarming story where a company was forced, with a &quot;trap&quot; set by GPL and its philosophy, to offer people for once a <i>square deal</i>: good hardware, fairly priced, you are free to do with it what you want. All this serves human needs better and the manufacturer could in fact turn a profit.<p>There is a faint, faint glimmer of hope that this is a peek of the far future of our techno-political-economic system. Of course with very different laws around intellectual property, company governance, customer protection, terms of participating in the market etc. We might be as far from it as the Enlightenment in 1750 (in a world built on overt serfdom and not even fully developed colonialism) was from the year 2000, but still. Makes me feel a teensy bit better about doing the right thing today, just because.')
