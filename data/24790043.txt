Item(by='omarhaneef', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602777721, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24789445, text='The article quotes Ben Thompson (yes, from Stratechery) to explain why unprofitable stocks are performing well. It is basically what you might expect (they are re-investing profits in growth).<p>For those who wonder how you tell the good unprofitable companies from the bad unprofitable companies: typically you look at gross margins -- or unit economics. This is a little complicated to estimate for any sticky subscription company -- like a SaaS company -- you need to estimate the LTV and discount down to get the true gross margins.<p>Thompson&#x27;s point is that a sticky subscription company can have enormous LTV.')
