Item(by='dredmorbius', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1601945621, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24693444, text='Fair points.<p>My friend Woozle noted a few years ago an epistemic paradox:<p>&gt; Our present epistemic systems are undergoing kind of the same shock that the online community underwent when transitioning from BBSs and Usenet to the commercial web to social media.<p>&gt; We were used to a very high content-to-BS ratio because it took a certain amount of intelligence and intense domain-interest for people to be there in the first place -- and we&#x27;ve now transitioned to a situation where many people are there more or less accidentally and (the worst part), <i>because of a high percentage of the population being present, there is now substantial power to be had by influencing the discussions that take place.</i><p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;old.reddit.com&#x2F;r&#x2F;dredmorbius&#x2F;comments&#x2F;5wg0hp&#x2F;when_epistemic_systems_gain_social_and_political&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;old.reddit.com&#x2F;r&#x2F;dredmorbius&#x2F;comments&#x2F;5wg0hp&#x2F;when_ep...</a><p>Shoshana Zuboff put this more succinctly, in the 1980s, in her Thid Law:<p>&gt; Every digital application that can be used for surveillance and control will be used for surveillance and control.<p>And you can find far earlier examples, say, Sun Tzu&#x27;s &quot;On the Use of Spies&quot; (book 13), and of course &quot;All warfare is based on deception.&quot;<p>So yes, the practive is <i>not</i> merely modern, and not a passing fad.<p>&quot;Build it and they will come&quot; turns out to be a curse.<p>Non-dominant cultures <i>may</i> advance by out-surviving adversaries.  The Angles were subordinate to the Romans, the Song Dynasty and Poland to the Mongolian Empire, Egypt and Persia to Macedonia.  Direct combat is expensive and risky.<p>But an utter failure at self-preservation (or in securing suitable patronage) tends to end badly.  To invoke another mid-century classic, Huxley&#x27;s <i>Island</i>.  &quot;Here and now, boys!&quot;')
