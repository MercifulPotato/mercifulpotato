BPG (and H.265 it uses) may be technically excellent, but H.265 is a commercial patent-encumbered codec. Patents cover any implementation, even free and open-source ones, so in countries that allow software patents BPG is illegal to use without a patent license for H.265.<p>&quot;Hope you&#x27;re not worth suing&quot; may be an OK strategy for many people using BPG on their websites, but it&#x27;s a blocker for wide adoption of the format. The licensing situation is so bad, that the industry went all behind the AV1 alternative, and the AVIF format rides that wave.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;aomedia.org&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;aomedia.org&#x2F;</a>')
