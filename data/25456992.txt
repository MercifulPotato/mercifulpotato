Item(by='sounds', descendants=None, kids=[25458571], score=None, time=1608222258, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25456829, text='In essence, the freedom &quot;free as in freedom&quot; means you are free to pay nothing, or pay it to the off-brand hosting company.<p>As you say, &quot;I&#x27;m not a douchebag&quot; implies there&#x27;s some kind of karma involved; when MySQL &quot;went Oracle,&quot; the free-as-in-freedom choice of the developers was to start MariaDB and many in the community interpreted this as the developers&#x27; way of saying &quot;I&#x27;m not a douchebag.&quot;<p>Oracle kind of has a reputation.<p>So in essence, if someone pays me when they were free to pay the off-brand hosting company, then it is a strong positive signal. Companies that Oracle-lock their customers are trading long-term profitability for revenue this quarter, as their savvy customers will immediately begin looking elsewhere for a competitor.<p>Basically if I grant you, the user, the freedom not to pay me, I can trust the signal that those who do pay me want more of my product.<p>Economies are all about signalling.')
