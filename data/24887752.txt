Item(by='bloak', descendants=None, kids=[24888264, 24887984, 24888718, 24888004], score=None, time=1603645211, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24887268, text='I&#x27;m no expert, but I&#x27;ve read a bit about Vespula vulgaris (the most common sort of wasp in the UK) and tackled a few nests in the past.<p>My impression is that if you put a vacuum cleaner next to the entrance you may, if you&#x27;re lucky, catch nearly all the wasps that leave the nest, but the queen never leaves the nest, and the eggs and larvae don&#x27;t leave the nest, and I think there may also be some workers that stay in the nest looking after the larvae, so things will be quieter after you&#x27;ve finished vacuuming, but the colony will probably regenerate itself in a week or so.')
