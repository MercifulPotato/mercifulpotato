Item(by='pjmlp', descendants=None, kids=[25930007], score=None, time=1611762830, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25929378, text='C only got there, because UNIX just like the Browser did to JavaScript, has placed it everywhere.<p>This happened only because Bell Labs&#x2F;AT&amp;T were forbidden to sell UNIX, hence its source code spread across the industry, with a symbolic price that made it by comparison with other OS vendors feel almost as quite cheap beer.<p>During the 80&#x27;s and early 90&#x27;s, C only mattered in universities and companies that had access to some kind of UNIX flavour.<p>Everywhere else we had Assembly, Modula-2, Pascal and Basic flavours, BLISS and some PL&#x2F;I variants like PL&#x2F;M on CP&#x2F;M.<p>So when you happen to have UNIX, and a C compiler around, trying to bring people to use something else, it is the same as trying to replace JavaScript on the browser.<p>So we come up with our CoffeScript and Typescript likes for systems programming, but C (even in C++, Objective-C) is always there.')
