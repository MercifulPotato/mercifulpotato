Item(by='frankus', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605839710, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25156509, text='There definitely are some subtleties of building science involved. Insulation doesn’t so much keep your house warm (that’s what the furnace does) as keep the outside of your walls cold (at which point they radiate&#x2F;convect less heat into the air). If the air reaches its dew point at an inopportune place in the wall assembly you can easily get moisture damage and&#x2F;or mold.')
