Item(by='bmitc', descendants=None, kids=[25972576, 25975033], score=None, time=1612037619, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25970285, text='I get it to a degree, but it&#x27;s a very dangerous line of thinking to write off systems level thinking as conspiracies. Stafford Beer points out, as an analogy in <i>Designing Freedom</i>, that the idea of an all encompassing force that affects everyone on Earth is hard to fathom, and yet gravity is an accepted idea today, where it wasn&#x27;t at the start. Making connections between wide ranging events, people, and ideas doesn&#x27;t make a conspiracy.<p>Adam Curtis&#x27; documentaries warn us against systemic effects, and they are warnings that what we&#x27;re presented with isn&#x27;t always the truth. It&#x27;s basically what Chomsky has warned about for most of his career.')
