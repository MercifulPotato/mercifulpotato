The hard part is finding how to change <i>while moving existing ecosystem</i> and preserving all the use cases people depend on.<p>I expect there is a reason Arch Linux symlinks to &#x2F;usr&#x2F;bin and not to &#x2F;bin:<p><pre><code>    $ ls -l &#x2F;\n    bin -&gt; usr&#x2F;bin\n    lib -&gt; usr&#x2F;lib\n    lib64 -&gt; usr&#x2F;lib\n    sbin -&gt; usr&#x2F;bin\n</code></pre>\n$PATH is configurable and allows several directories. One needs something like Plan 9 namespaces and union mount to replicate, for example, rbenv:<p><pre><code>    ; cd foo\n    ; bind -a &#x2F;ruby2.6&#x2F;lib &#x2F;lib\n    ; bind -a &#x2F;ruby2.6&#x2F;bin &#x2F;bin\n</code></pre>\nReal world analogy: we are not perfect as species, but let&#x27;s spend our time where and how we can improve instead of speaking of &quot;garbage&quot; in our DNA and how it would be great without it.')
