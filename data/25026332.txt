Item(by='axiosgunnar', descendants=None, kids=[25026394, 25026354, 25026754, 25026438, 25026373, 25026370, 25026410], score=None, time=1604849479, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25026095, text='You can think of these NPM micropackages as Github Gist snippets that you can programmatically link to your code.<p>The first advantage to just copy-pasting is that the snippet author might update it (e.g. if he finds a faster&#x2F;safer way to do the one thing his package does), and you don&#x27;t have to do anything.<p>Imagine finding a solution on Stackoverflow and just being able to import foo from stackoverflow&#x2F;a&#x2F;12345, and when the community finds a better way to do it, your software is automatically using the new version!<p>The second advantage is that it&#x27;s basically built-in refactoring: Code from these micropackages will not litter your code, there is a clean separation between what is your business logic vs where you just need some generic functionality.')
