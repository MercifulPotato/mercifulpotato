Item(by='lcuff', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602003312, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24697965, text='The essential skill needed to be an &lt;anything&gt; is to be interested in it and enjoy it.  Paul Graham writes (somewhere in his essays, couldn&#x27;t find it with a quick scan) that the notion of &#x27;aptitude&#x27; is highly suspicious. We get better at things by doing them, and after doing them for a long time we  <i>look</i> like we have an aptitude.<p>A caveat: Just as being interested in and liking to play a sport won&#x27;t get you to play at the top levels without talent as well, there are lots of programmer&#x27;s jobs that will be out of reach, but others WILL be in reach.')
