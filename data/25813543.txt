Item(by='antonvs', descendants=None, kids=[25815022, 25813661], score=None, time=1610909094, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25812849, text='You&#x27;re understandably thinking of Google&#x27;s track record with many of its other products that were discontinued. However, most of those products were &quot;free&quot; to the user, and as such Google had no real obligation to its customers.<p>GCP is a very different kind of product. Customers pay for it directly, and often their business depends on it. It&#x27;s covered by all sorts of contractual agreements, including service level agreements. It&#x27;s backed by a great deal of physical hardware around the world that Google wouldn&#x27;t otherwise need. Its revenue is growing fast, currently over $12 billion&#x2F;year. That&#x27;s revenue from customers paying it directly. In Q2 2020 it had 43% growth, even though Alphabet had its first quarterly revenue drop.<p>It&#x27;s not the kind of thing they&#x27;re going to dump on a whim, and if they did decide to exit that space, it would most likely be by letting another company acquire it, since it would be hugely expensive to just drop it.')
