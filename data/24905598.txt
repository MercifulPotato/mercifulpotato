Item(by='mullsork', descendants=None, kids=[24905644], score=None, time=1603789409, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24886659, text='My experience outside the Nordics says somewhat different.<p>&gt; An appreciation of and desire to be in nature is part of almost every society on the planet<p>I would rephrase that as &quot;An appreciation of and desire to be outside in great weather with friends is part of almost every society on the planet.&quot; I&#x27;m sure there are plenty of exceptions, but I&#x27;ve met few people who are willing to, for no purpose other than to be outside, go outside in hard wind or light rain. The idea of &quot;go get some fresh air, you&#x27;ll feel better&quot; doesn&#x27;t seem to resonate well.<p>&gt; fika<p>What makes the fika culture unique (we have it in Finland too) is not that you decide to go grab a coffee during the work day. For me, it&#x27;s that _everyone_ takes a break at the same time to have coffee. I only did an internship in tech back home, but even there we would all take a break at 9:30 to sit down, have a coffee, and talk about whatever we came up with.<p>When I hear non-Nordics talk about Nordic culture I get the feeling that they&#x27;re very focused on what individuals do, whereas the whole point is what people collectively do.')
