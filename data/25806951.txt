Item(by='dcolkitt', descendants=None, kids=[25807222], score=None, time=1610843772, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25805635, text='The easiest way to mitigate this is to require two-phase commits on any transaction above X% of the pool.<p>A flash loan requires that the funds be returned before the end of a transaction. If removing funds is a two-step process, then by definition you can’t use attack it with a flash loan.<p>Of course there’s always the possibility of someone with that much capital to stake attacking. But the number of participants with $50 million in funds lying around is a lot less than the number with $0. It drastically shrinks the attack surface.')
