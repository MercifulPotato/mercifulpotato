Item(by='strenholme', descendants=None, kids=[25515711], score=None, time=1608711133, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25505212, text='I’m just glad we’re no longer in the IE6 days where getting something as simple as a stable three-column pure CSS layout was a nay-to-impossible challenge.<p>IE9 was probably the first version of IE with decent CSS (read ”display: table” which wasn’t broken); all of the other browsers (which didn’t have IE9’s market share at the time) had already added “display: table” support.<p>Before “display: table” was widely supported, people would either mix CSS with a &lt;table&gt; layout, or have a fixed width (usually) single column design.')
