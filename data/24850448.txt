Item(by='allemagne', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603303659, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24845410, text='The fact that large corporations are certainly top-down, planned, and successful in a meaningful sense is certainly a rhetorical blow to anyone who believes in a simplistic way that &quot;markets&quot; and &quot;decentralization&quot; are literally the only way to be large and successful.<p>But if large corporations prove the efficacy of planning at scale, then they surely prove the drawbacks as well. What are the society-wide central planning equivalents of layoffs and maximizing shareholder value? Does a centrally planned corporation outsourcing their supply chain or HR department represent a fundamental failure of central planning?')
