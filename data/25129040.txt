You don&#x27;t need to convince literally everyone. There are still some people who believe the Earth is flat and probably always will be, but it is not super-relevant to collective actions because they are a tiny minority<p>2. People do in fact change their minds on things. It usually just happens on larger time scales so while one article isn&#x27;t moving the needle, the accretion of many articles and arguments can change population-wide beliefs.<p>3. The population of people with an opinion on any given topic is not static. Even if any individual never changes their mind, the composition of the population can change if new people are forming opinions in one way more often. Science progresses one funeral at a time and all that....')
