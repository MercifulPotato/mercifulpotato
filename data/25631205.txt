Item(by='gsnedders', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609767945, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25631013, text='&gt; The interpretation of US law by a GB judge seems extraneous.<p>For the extradition treaty to apply, it must be alleged that the defendant has committed actions which amount to a criminal offence in both countries. If his alleged actions are not a criminal offence in both countries, then the extradition treaty does not apply.<p>Therefore, you can challenge extradition on dual criminality by arguing that the act is not a crime in one or both countries.')
