Item(by='PragmaticPulp', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608997593, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25543320, text='Frankly, focusing on the absolute security of the communication medium isn&#x27;t a real issue for 99% of business purposes.<p>Phone calls are secure enough for most purposes. At the upper levels of business, e-mail is used for quick notes and corrections, but the heavy lifting is going to happen in phone calls and other real-time communications.<p>Techies some times put too much emphasis on things like cryptographic security of the communication channel or strength of encryption, when in reality it doesn&#x27;t matter for phishing attacks like these. You could go to great lengths to get your customers set up on Signal or Telegram, but it doesn&#x27;t matter the second they get an e-mail phishing attack that says &quot;Hey, I got a new phone, locked out of my account, can you just attach the document here?&quot;')
