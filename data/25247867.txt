Item(by='heavyset_go', descendants=None, kids=[25247980, 25248239, 25247950, 25248543, 25249881], score=None, time=1606682262, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25247338, text='If I thought there was a good chance of having the work upstreamed so that a mainline Linux kernel would eventually work on these computers, maybe.<p>In my experience, there are not many ARM SoCs that have mainline support, even some of the Raspberry Pi boards don&#x27;t, and they&#x27;re some of the most popular ARM SoCs used for Linux.<p>A lot of these projects get to the stage where a forked kernel will run on the SoC, but then support and attention peters out when it comes upgrading the fork or getting the work into mainline Linux because a new and better ARM SoC will be released, and then that requires the same amount of work on a new Linux fork.
