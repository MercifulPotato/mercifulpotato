Data I&#x27;ve seen (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.hofstra.edu&#x2F;pdf&#x2F;academics&#x2F;colleges&#x2F;hclas&#x2F;cld&#x2F;cld-rlr-fall12-incomeinequality-waldman.pdf" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.hofstra.edu&#x2F;pdf&#x2F;academics&#x2F;colleges&#x2F;hclas&#x2F;cld&#x2F;cld...</a> is the clearest presentation I can find off-hand) indicates that complete political disengagement - never discussing or following politics - is much more common in lower income brackets.<p>I&#x27;d argue that this is what we should expect from an abstract perspective too. If someone has to struggle just to put food on their table, it&#x27;s going to be pretty hard to convince them that the goings-on of some political candidate are worth spending their time on.')
