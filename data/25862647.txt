Item(by='CyberRabbi', descendants=None, kids=[25863475], score=None, time=1611255665, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25862503, text='I think in general not causing death in the target host is a naturally selected trait in viruses. The least lethal viruses are the ones that spread the most.<p>This has been observed in herpes, which has evolved to infect nervous system cells but explicitly not nerve cells in the brain (which would cause death).<p>So considering that, perhaps future strains of COVID will reduce their lethality while increasing their ability to infect.')
