Tiers with more capability and support are available for $800, and $1300.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.redhat.com&#x2F;en&#x2F;store&#x2F;red-hat-enterprise-linux-server" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.redhat.com&#x2F;en&#x2F;store&#x2F;red-hat-enterprise-linux-ser...</a><p>Oracle has a larger range of tiers. The &quot;no support&quot; license is $120&#x2F;year. Tiers with more capability and support are available for $500, $1200, $1400, and $2300.<p>Oracle Linux can be used in production without a paid license of any kind; Red Hat Linux cannot be used in this way for large deployments (excepting the new 16-seat license for a developer account).<p>Red Hat is aggressive with software audits; I have seen one.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.oracle.com&#x2F;linux&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.oracle.com&#x2F;linux&#x2F;</a><p>Both Oracle and Red Hat now have complete toolsets to convert support between an installed CentOS&#x2F;RedHat&#x2F;Oracle OS.<p>Red Hat can now convert an installed CentOS or Oracle Linux to RHEL; previously a wipe and reinstall was required (&quot;have fun reinstalling your system&quot; is still on Oracle&#x27;s CentOS site). The description looks much more thorough in replacing all possible packages with Red Hat versions:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;access.redhat.com&#x2F;articles&#x2F;2360841" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;access.redhat.com&#x2F;articles&#x2F;2360841</a><p>Oracle does not replace CentOS or RedHat RPMs in their conversion (AFAIK), so it is much less violent on the platform changes.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;oracle&#x2F;centos2ol" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;oracle&#x2F;centos2ol</a><p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;linux.oracle.com&#x2F;switch&#x2F;centos&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;linux.oracle.com&#x2F;switch&#x2F;centos&#x2F;</a><p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;blogs.oracle.com&#x2F;linux&#x2F;reasons-for-switching-centos-to-oracle-linux" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;blogs.oracle.com&#x2F;linux&#x2F;reasons-for-switching-centos-...</a><p>Red Hat has terminated two major platforms over the last two decades - the original Red Hat Linux, which was terminated at v9, and now CentOS.<p>Oracle has &quot;somewhat&quot; terminated a platform, Oracle Linux for SPARC, which was only supported for two minor releases. The Linux for ARM64 and AMD64&#x2F;x86 seem reasonably healthy.<p>There is other baggage in and between these corporations, but the track record on Linux OS platforms between these two is demonstrably different.')
