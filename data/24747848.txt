No one would cross the sea or the desert if there wasn&#x27;t a prize, so I take for granted that there is an inbalance.<p>&quot;Bottleneck&quot; seems to suggest that commerce is like a natural process. It isn&#x27;t. There are trade cultures like Phoenicians or Renaissance Venice, but often the powerful don&#x27;t like commerce. Commerce implies some values that subvert authoritarian regimes, maybe just because different ways of understanding life make contact.<p>So when I hear bottleneck, I think of trade restrictions, not flow. You mean bottleneck as a funnel where the flow is more intense and thus more susceptible to capture wealth. It makes sense, but it&#x27;s somehow a twisted metaphor, because it&#x27;s the inbalance (gravity, or demand + the cheaper production elsewhere) that creates the flow, the bottleneck just make it easier to exploit. But a cascade can be wide or narrow, tall or short. In any case, there&#x27;s water falling...')
