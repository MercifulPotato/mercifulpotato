If you need this today, look at Ricochet or Vuvuzela etc.<p>&gt; Protecting metadata is incompatible with bridging.<p>&gt; However, in future peer-to-peer home servers could run clientside, tunnelling traffic over Tor and using anonymous store-and-forward servers (a la Pond).<p>&gt; But for now this is sci-fi.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;matrix-org&#x2F;synapse&#x2F;issues&#x2F;2188" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;matrix-org&#x2F;synapse&#x2F;issues&#x2F;2188</a><p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;matrix-org&#x2F;synapse&#x2F;issues&#x2F;4565" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;matrix-org&#x2F;synapse&#x2F;issues&#x2F;4565</a><p>Signal, in contrast, put a lot of effort into metadata reduction--critical as they are a single giant hosted relay service--and in the process (I am very sure) even fixed the issue I used to complain about wherein their server was technically keeping around a temporary-ish in-memory metadata log for rate limiting.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;signal.org&#x2F;blog&#x2F;sealed-sender&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;signal.org&#x2F;blog&#x2F;sealed-sender&#x2F;</a><p>If you are going to switch to something, switch to Signal (...though I sadly can&#x27;t in good faith ever really recommend anyone do that, due to how Signal has crippled the ability to do chat backups; more info on this in the other thread going on today re Signal&#x2F;WhatsApp).<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=25686475" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=25686475</a>')
