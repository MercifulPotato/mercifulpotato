Item(by='bbarnett', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608341909, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25474135, text='I just realised that you may not get the other side of the scenario.<p>On a 5xx series response, you bounce.  Where does the bounce go?  Back to the original sender.<p>This is the &#x27;closing of the loop&#x27;.  Original sender sees the message was not sent successfully.  They can now retry, resend, make a phone call, whatever.<p>&quot;Hey Bob, what was your email address again?  I got a bounce.  Ohhh, damn, I typed bod@ instead of bob@.  I&#x27;ll fix and resend.<p>That&#x27;s how it works.<p>That&#x27;s how it works for:<p>- a typo in the email address<p>- when an account is deleted (and maybe your friend has a new email address)<p>- the remote mail admin did something horribly, horribly wrong, and it bounced as a result<p>(Horribly, horribly wrong being -- they told their own mail servers that accounts did not exist, who helpfully passed that info on.)')
