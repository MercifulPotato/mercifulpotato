Item(by='pmiller2', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611733151, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25924584, text='I would say that depends more on the culture of the institution than anything.  &quot;Professor&quot; is acceptable at some places, first name is the norm at others, and &quot;Mr.&#x2F;Mrs.&#x2F;Ms.&quot; at still others.<p>When I was a grad student, I made a point when introducing myself on day 1 that I was <i>not</i> a professor, so please don&#x27;t call me &quot;Professor Pmiller2&quot; Then, I said something about &quot;if you call me &quot;Mr. Pmiller2,&quot; I&#x27;m going to wonder if my dad is standing behind me, so, please just call me &quot;$FIRSTNAME.&quot; That was pretty much the standard where I was among people in my department (grad students = first names).')
