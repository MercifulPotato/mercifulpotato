Item(by='jlgaddis', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609633969, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25617842, text='&gt; <i>... why are CVEs that are just packages of software being accepted to the CVE database anyways?</i><p>Ultimately, because there are now a few hundred [0] CNAs [1] which are &quot;authorized to assign CVE IDs&quot; and, AFAICT, there is nothing in the &quot;CNA rules&quot; [2] that requires them to (attempt to) verify the (alleged) vulnerabilities -- although, in at least <i>some</i> instances, I assume it simply wouldn&#x27;t be possible for them to do so.<p>--<p>&gt; <i>7.1 What Is a Vulnerability?</i><p>&gt; <i>The CVE Program does not adhere to a strict definition of a vulnerability. For the most part, CNAs are left to their own discretion to determine whether something is a vulnerability.</i> [3]<p>Officially, a &quot;vulnerability&quot; is:<p>&gt; <i>A flaw in a software, firmware, hardware, or service component resulting from a weakness that can be exploited, causing a negative impact to the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of an impacted component or components.</i><p>Fortunately, there is a &quot;Process to Correct Assignment Issues or Update CVE Entries&quot; [5]. In instances of multiple, &quot;duplicate&quot; or &quot;invalid&quot; CVEs, I can see how this might be both frustrating and time-consuming for software developers, though.<p>--<p>[0]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cve.mitre.org&#x2F;cve&#x2F;request_id.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cve.mitre.org&#x2F;cve&#x2F;request_id.html</a><p>[1]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cve.mitre.org&#x2F;cve&#x2F;cna.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cve.mitre.org&#x2F;cve&#x2F;cna.html</a><p>[2]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cve.mitre.org&#x2F;cve&#x2F;cna&#x2F;rules.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cve.mitre.org&#x2F;cve&#x2F;cna&#x2F;rules.html</a><p>[3]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cve.mitre.org&#x2F;cve&#x2F;cna&#x2F;rules.html#section_7-1_what_is_a_vulnerability" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cve.mitre.org&#x2F;cve&#x2F;cna&#x2F;rules.html#section_7-1_what_is...</a><p>[4]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cve.mitre.org&#x2F;about&#x2F;terminology.html#vulnerability" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cve.mitre.org&#x2F;about&#x2F;terminology.html#vulnerability</a><p>[5]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cve.mitre.org&#x2F;cve&#x2F;cna&#x2F;rules.html#appendix_c_process_to_correct_assignment_issues_update_cve_entries" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cve.mitre.org&#x2F;cve&#x2F;cna&#x2F;rules.html#appendix_c_process_...</a>')
