He&#x27;s not suggesting that you speak in order to attain some predicted Google Translate output, but instead it&#x27;s helpful to use precise language that doesn&#x27;t rely on a shared contextual understanding.<p>For example, you&#x27;d use the phrase &quot;upload it to the server&quot; rather than &quot;put it on the server&quot; - the verb &quot;upload&quot; is unambiguous and lets both the recipient and the translation software know that &quot;server&quot; is referring to a computer system.')
