Item(by='pjmlp', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610920571, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25813933, text='Back when I did my degree in the mid-90&#x27;s, our labs were running a mix of LC III, Windows 3.11 for Workgroups, the recent Windows 95 and terminals for the DG&#x2F;UX server.<p>The only classes using UNIX were the ones specific about UNIX content.<p>A the end of the 5 years, the DG&#x2F;UX server had been replaced by a Red-Hat one and most terminals, were dual booting between the university own distribution and Win95&#x2F;95 OSR, some of us also had access to the freshly released Windows NT.<p>The large majority of students was still only booting into GNU&#x2F;Linux for the UNIX related labs.<p>Anything related to graphics programming, Smalltalk, databases, AI, engineering tools (UML, Boochs, CASE), digital circuits, was done on Windows&#x2F;Mac OS (System).')
