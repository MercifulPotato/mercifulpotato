Item(by='legerdemain', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604091496, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24937370, text='When a problem comes along, some people run to their &quot;government&quot; to solve it for them. That&#x27;s called Learned Helplessness.[1] Problems don&#x27;t get solved because of &quot;rules,&quot; they get solved when individuals get involved in community action.<p>To pick two recent examples, it&#x27;s useless to wait for people in suits to &quot;conserve&quot; the gray wolf or enact fuel economy &quot;standards.&quot; I vote with my wallet to pressure companies constantly to produce more fuel-efficient cars, and I promote wolves and wolf appreciation in my local community.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Learned_helplessness" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Learned_helplessness</a>')
