Item(by='tw25610275', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609615560, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25612966, text='&gt; Humanity has literally only one way to gain knowledge about the world: Making a testable prediction and then testing it (The Scientific Method).<p>I think Popperian science occupies too large a place in your epistemology. &quot;Science&quot; has come to mean two distinct things: any procedure for arriving at highly reliable knowledge, and one specific procedure for arriving at highly reliable knowledge. The latter is constantly threatening to crowd out the former.<p>The scientific method itself, for example, would be valid even if no science had ever been done. It is not at all the product of empirical analysis, but of <i>deductive reasoning</i>.')
