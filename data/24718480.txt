Item(by='murgindrag', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602160057, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24714373, text='Early Google was incredibly arrogant and aggressive around only hiring people they thought were brilliant. The model was small teams of super-elite people. They took some flack for hiring for high caliber people into even relatively menial positions (e.g. random office admins). Even for things like the cafeteria, they wanted a world-class chef.<p>On the whole, it actually worked very well. Everyone wanted to work there because you were surrounded by top people.')
