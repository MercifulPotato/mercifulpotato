Item(by='dahart', descendants=None, kids=[24674506, 24674540, 24676600], score=None, time=1601754365, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24673749, text='The end of Moore’s law has been broadly reported for the last decade and is widely understood. Nobody is likely to get huge efficiency improvements in general processors ever again, not like what we saw in the past, and it isn’t news anymore.<p>Algorithmic improvements, custom domain specific ASICs, and maybe quantum computing or other physical processes in the future are where large efficiency deltas might come, but for now small improvements are here to stay for all chip makers.')
