Other answers are below.<p>--\nRe: CI --\nWe dogfood GitLab CI via gitlab.com -- so no need for integrations.<p>GitLab non-engineering teams use CI all the time because we constantly update the handbook to document all of our work.<p>I would love to see this practice more often in OSS orgs, and other companies for that matter. Having a handbook-first approach (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;about.gitlab.com&#x2F;company&#x2F;culture&#x2F;all-remote&#x2F;handbook-first-documentation&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;about.gitlab.com&#x2F;company&#x2F;culture&#x2F;all-remote&#x2F;handbook...</a>) really helps enable remote team collaboration and makes it easier for newcomers to jump in. I think OSS orgs have done a good job of recognizing the importance of documentation for development projects, but there&#x27;s an opportunity to increase documentation around workflows and community operations.<p>--\nRe: tracking conversations on Diffs --\nAdmittedly, I don&#x27;t have a lot of experience with this outside of GitLab. But maybe that&#x27;s the point.
