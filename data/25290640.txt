Item(by='walleeee', descendants=None, kids=[25290708], score=None, time=1607013160, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25290512, text='&gt; Currently, afaik, this problem is primarily driven by Asia, so I think the effort that would have the most impact is figuring out how to convince Asian countries to stop throwing plastic in to rivers.<p>The west could start by not exporting a huge portion of its plastic waste to said Asian countries.<p>&gt; I think this is overblown. The earth is already &#x27;full&#x27; of... toxic things.<p>The earth was certainly not &quot;full&quot; of macro-, micro-, and nano-plastics 50 years ago.<p>Americans ingest and inhale tens to hundreds of thousands of microplastic particles per year[0]. Microplastics likely impair cognition in hermit crabs[1]. Nanoplastics accumulate in plants[2].  It&#x27;s not just waterways.<p>Nobody really understands how this might affect human health. We&#x27;re all participants in a planet-sized experiment to find out.<p>[0]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;doi.org&#x2F;10.1098&#x2F;rsbl.2020.0030" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;doi.org&#x2F;10.1098&#x2F;rsbl.2020.0030</a><p>[1]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;doi.org&#x2F;10.1021&#x2F;acs.est.9b01517" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;doi.org&#x2F;10.1021&#x2F;acs.est.9b01517</a><p>[2]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;doi.org&#x2F;10.1038&#x2F;s41565-020-0707-4" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;doi.org&#x2F;10.1038&#x2F;s41565-020-0707-4</a>')
