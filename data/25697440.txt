Item(by='dragonwriter', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610169456, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25689672, text='&gt; A judge providing an opinion is not adjudication.<p>If it is a tangential opinion (not supporting one of the decisive grounds for an action), sure. If it is a opinion that is part of the basis for a grounds of dismissal that inherently asks whether, even before considering how the other side might challenge it, the evidence could <i>ever</i> support the plaintiff winning, it absolutely is adjudication.<p>The difference is important because the last can be (and has been in some of the cases at issue) part of the analysis when dismissing for failure to state a claim.')
