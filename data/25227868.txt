Item(by='TeMPOraL', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606477678, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25227805, text='I don&#x27;t know how they solved it in KSP, I was just presenting a general approach for when you need to have things happen in multiple places around a large simulated universe.<p>I can imagine KSP going with what you described, because in that game, things you aren&#x27;t actively controlling and that aren&#x27;t in &quot;physics range&quot; (2.5 kilometers IIRC) are either treated as stationary or on-rails (i.e. they&#x27;re in orbit, and their positions are computed with a closed-form equation); anything that&#x27;s neither stationary nor in orbit will be deleted once it leaves the physics range. This way, there is no physics simulation to be done on things you don&#x27;t control or aren&#x27;t withing few kilometers of, so centering the coordinate system at your active vessel would be a very good approach.')
