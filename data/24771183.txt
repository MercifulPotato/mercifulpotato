Item(by='danans', descendants=None, kids=[24784591], score=None, time=1602629210, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24770764, text='They are undoubtedly referring to the highly controversial Nostratic language family hypothesis, which postulates a language superfamily that includes most of the large language families of Eurasia (i.e. Indo European, Semitic, Dravidian, Uralic) and also the proposed &quot;Altaic&quot; family which (also controversially) is comprised Korean, Japanese, and Turkic.<p>Nostratic excludes the Sino-Tibetan family (i.e. Chinese languages), most languages of Southeast Asia, and all the African languages.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Nostratic_languages" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Nostratic_languages</a><p>Again, it&#x27;s so broad a grouping, projected so far back in time and based on such weakly detected feature similarities, that it&#x27;s by no means broadly accepted as a language family in the way that Indo-European, Semitic, Uralic, etc are.')
