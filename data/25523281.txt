Item(by='gen220', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608764409, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25521858, text='Big plus one. The Nth Edition of Fannie Farmer&#x27;s Cookbook (or equivalent) is a great entry point for self-starters.<p>Playing with the basic recipes in there helps develop an instinct for ingredients and the cooking process, with relatively low-risk ingredients and processes.<p>Once you&#x27;ve mastered those fundamentals, you develop an ability to simulate whether a recipe, ingredient substitution, or bizarre process &quot;makes sense&quot; or not, which is invaluable in deciding whether to risk a dinner on some crazy recipe recommended to you by the internet.<p>The internet is a great place to deepen a knowledge of cooking, but it&#x27;s hard to dive in without the fundamentals. And for the time and money, these great cookbooks are the easiest way to acquire the norms and fundamentals.')
