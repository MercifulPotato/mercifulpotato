Item(by='sfusato', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607374065, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25336042, text='Regarding attention spans, from &quot;Avoid News. Towards a Healthy News Diet&quot; By Rolf Dobelli [0] (top-notch article, btw):<p>&quot;In  a 2001  study [1] two scholars in Canada showed that comprehension declines as the number of hyperlinks in a document increase. Why? Because whenever a link appears, your brain has to at least make the choice not  to click, which in itself is distracting.&quot;<p>This had me thinking if a dead simple button at the end of an article &quot;Enable links&quot; would have a positive effect on link heavy articles (Wikipedia type) or not. Something like a conscious action of &quot;okay, now I&#x27;m ready to explore this further&quot;. Or, better yet, a browser add-on that will detect just the content of an article and disable its links by default. Obviously, with an enable button close by.<p>---<p>[0] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.gwern.net&#x2F;docs&#x2F;culture&#x2F;2010-dobelli.pdf" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.gwern.net&#x2F;docs&#x2F;culture&#x2F;2010-dobelli.pdf</a><p>[1] Nicholas Carr: The Web Shatters Focus, Rewires Brains, Wired, May 2010')
