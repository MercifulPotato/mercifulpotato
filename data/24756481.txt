Item(by='ArchOversight', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602521646, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24755889, text='Comcast ran out of RFC1918 addressing to manage all the devices on their network (think cable modems&#x2F;set top boxes&#x2F;routers&#x2F;everything) and had a massively segmented network setup.<p>They rolled out IPv6 because it flattened that network again, and instead of worrying about being in the right segment for reachability everything now gets a globally unique IP address.
