This may be specific to B2B markets, but my customers place a high degree of trust in my company&#x27;s products. If our products fail, large customers will fire teams, small customers will go out of business.<p>In order to be trusted,  my customers have to be confident that I understand their technical and business needs. In some cases, even personal needs as relates to their career.<p>I cannot build enough trust without a healthy dose of listening and answering questions. And yes, even if I don&#x27;t know the answer to a question, saying I don&#x27;t know, but I will get the answer by a specific date, and then doing so, is the most effective approach to building trust that I&#x27;ve found in 30 years.')
