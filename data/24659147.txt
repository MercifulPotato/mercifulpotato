In abstract algebra it&#x27;s common to surround an operator in a circle to denote a &quot;generic&quot; or &quot;lifted&quot; operator. So &lt;$&gt; denotes a &quot;lifted application&quot;, i.e.<p><pre><code>  ($)   ::              (a → b) → a   → b\n  (&lt;$&gt;) :: Functor f =&gt; (a → b) → f a → f b</code></pre>')
