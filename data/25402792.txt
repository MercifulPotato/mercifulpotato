You&#x27;re making a screen recording of your content to demo or educate others (Youtube, etc.).<p>I very much prefer using light themes but I also record screencasts (400+ of them at this point).<p>Every time I run a poll asking folks if they prefer light or dark there&#x27;s been a majority requesting dark mode in the recordings.<p>Personally I find the colorization of most dark themes having more contrast between the colors. A lot of light themes will make certain keywords dark colors which kind of looks the same on compressed video. I&#x27;m not sold on that being the reason most folks prefer dark themes, but I&#x27;m also not convinced light mode is better for video recorded content. I think with the right themes, maybe both are equal.<p>I&#x27;ve gone as far as writing a shell script to easily toggle dark and light mode for my terminal, tmux, Vim theme, FZF and other tools: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;nickjanetakis.com&#x2F;blog&#x2F;live-coding-a-shell-script-to-toggle-dark-mode-for-terminal-tools" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;nickjanetakis.com&#x2F;blog&#x2F;live-coding-a-shell-script-to...</a><p>All I end up doing is running `toggle-dark-mode` to seamlessly switch between the 2.')
