Item(by='627467', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605192316, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25068594, text='I&#x27;m torn. Obviously sovereign societies are free to enact any rights and restrictions (within constrains of treaties they sign up to) they see fit. But I see people&#x27;s freedom of movement as a natural balancing right in a globalized world were capital moves easily across borders.<p>In EU freedom of movement is normally only talked about as a right due to shared european values but another pragmatic view is: if you allow for such drastic unequal neighbors to be living under similar legal&#x2F;economical frameworks it&#x27;s only fair (and economically sound) to let people move with capital.')
