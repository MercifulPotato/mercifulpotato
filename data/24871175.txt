Money, perhaps more than anything else in human society, exhibits network effects that incline it toward winner-takes-all dynamics. Every money implicitly competes against every other money.<p>I recommend taking a look at something like <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;bitcointreasuries.org&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;bitcointreasuries.org&#x2F;</a><p>And don&#x27;t just glance at it - try asking some probing questions, like &quot;What does this mean?&quot; and &quot;What might I be missing concerning the underlying dynamic at play?&quot;<p>You can also try reading The Bitcoin Standard for a good analysis of the dynamics and history of competition between different forms of money - the central thesis being: History has shown that you can&#x27;t protect yourself from someone else holding money that&#x27;s harder (better) than yours. Bitcoin is better money than anything else we have or have ever had.')
