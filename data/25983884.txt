Item(by='jka', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1612137054, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25982860, text='Something this doesn&#x27;t seem to mention is the organizational size of the software supplier, which I think is an important factor.<p>If a company provides a free and open source software product and they are composed of a small number of employees, it can be easier for the community to trust, observe and hold that entity accountable for their changes and roadmap.<p>There&#x27;s a balance to strike, of course; extremely small teams may be more limited in what they can achieve.<p>But I think that can be overcome by identifying the bottlenecks and spinning off smaller free and open source software enterprises to fill the gaps, fostering a positive feedback loop.  To a certain extent this mirrors the Unix philosophy regarding command-line tools.')
