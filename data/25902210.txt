Item(by='mr_gibbins', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611576073, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25902027, text='This sounds like a great advancement, however an implementation in RDBMS products may be some way away yet - MSSQL uses 8KB pages by default, and I believe (without checking) that most other RDBMSes use at least 4KB. B+ tree index implementations on RDBMS products may be here to stay for a while yet unless these performance issues can be minimised, or unless there is a paradigm shift to use smaller pages - which would have a knock-on effect to query performance unrelated to indexes, such as number of page lookups, increased I&#x2F;O for non-contiguous page reads...')
