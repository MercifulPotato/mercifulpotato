The Japanese word for real estate, 不動産, actually came from French [1], &#x27;immobilier&#x27; (real estate, by opposition to what is movable or &#x27;mobile&#x27; such as &#x27;meubles&#x27; or &#x27;mobilier&#x27;, i.e.  furniture). And of course the French word comes from Latin...<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;ja.wiktionary.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;%E4%B8%8D%E5%8B%95%E7%94%A3" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;ja.wiktionary.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;%E4%B8%8D%E5%8B%95%E7%94%A3</a><p>The Japanese would treat the whole combination as a single word (in my experience) and wouldn&#x27;t analyse the components either (although &#x27;they make sense&#x27;).  \nThe etymology is often more apparent in  Japanese and Chinese  thanks to the characters, whereas it&#x27;s not always easy to see it in other language (unless you speak Latin or Greek..). But this is a borrowed word and in this particular case it&#x27;s very intuitive to French speakers as immobilier &#x2F; mobilier &#x2F; mobile are all common words.')
