Bechtel managed to acquire control over water rights in Cochabamba, Bolivia in 1999. They started charging a licensing fee just to collect rainwater. Within a year there were hundreds of thousands out on the streets demonstrating, as the speculation didn&#x27;t actually fix the water scarcity in the city. It just made it worse, with over half the population lacking access to fresh water.<p>Water is not onions.
