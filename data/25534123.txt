Item(by='cl42', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608869553, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25530559, text='Not a PhD but I hire many data scientists, with and without PhDs. Unless you are working in a research setting that requires publishing papers, you probably don&#x27;t need to worry about having a PhD. You can work on your skills in a number of other ways.<p>This also depends on your long-term career goals. If you want to eventually manage teams or products, the years you put into a PhD might actually take away from experience around product design, strategy, management, and communication, all of which are critical to moving into leadership roles.<p>Some hiring managers even look down on PhDs as they can sometimes be too pedantic, nuanced, and miss the &quot;bigger picture&quot; around decision making. I wouldn&#x27;t personally go this far but just be aware of the tradeoffs and biases.<p>Of course, if your only goal is to do research then you should probably get a PhD...<p>I&#x27;ve run a few webinars with people who have moved from academia to industrial data science, and here are two talks you might appreciate: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;phaseai.com&#x2F;resources&#x2F;from-phd-to-applied-ml" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;phaseai.com&#x2F;resources&#x2F;from-phd-to-applied-ml</a> and <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;phaseai.com&#x2F;resources&#x2F;career-data-product-management" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;phaseai.com&#x2F;resources&#x2F;career-data-product-management</a>')
