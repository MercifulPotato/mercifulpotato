Item(by='wruza', descendants=None, kids=[24895549], score=None, time=1603695115, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24891275, text='NVMe throttling from 2GB&#x2F;s to 0.5GB&#x2F;s is usually not <i>thermal</i>, it is an SLC cache exhaustion that brings a drive back to MLC&#x2F;TLC packing mode. One can get samsung pro or a similar ssd and it will retain 2GB&#x2F;s indefinitely. On cheaper drives, having a lot of free space (i.e. slc cache) may help. Not sure though what process can generate such sustained write bandwidth beyond few things like copying or video transcoding.')
