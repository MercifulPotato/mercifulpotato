Item(by='feanaro', descendants=None, kids=[24919458], score=None, time=1603896489, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24919221, text='&gt; Reproducing more efficiently is a win for natural selection and will normally result in a dominant strain: whether that strain is more deadly or not is going to be random.<p>The conventional wisdom is such because a virus that is swiftly fatal and&#x2F;or has more dramatic health consequences would have less chance to propagate to other hosts. For instance, because the original host will be unable to move or will look threatening to others so they will know not to reside close to them. This should make it less than random.<p>Unless of course some unforeseen factor makes this reasoning untrue.')
