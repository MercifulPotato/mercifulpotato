Item(by='timr', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602962827, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24811787, text='<i>&quot;No regular influenza could do that.&quot;</i><p>Sure it could. The whole point of the IFR calculation is that it gives you an average perspective on how fatal a disease is, relative to other diseases. It&#x27;s not an absolute <i>maximum</i> fatality rate, for every circumstance.<p>Put a strain of &quot;normal&quot; flu in a vulnerable population with no pre-existing immunity, and it would do a lot of damage. But if you don&#x27;t count <i>all the other people</i> who had it without symptoms, then you get a misleading picture.<p>Also, of course, you have to realize that the population of &quot;Lombardy&quot; (~10M) is a bit larger than the population of New York City (~8M), where we see 100-300 deaths per day as a baseline mortality rate:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.baruch.cuny.edu&#x2F;nycdata&#x2F;population-geography&#x2F;pop-characteristics.htm" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.baruch.cuny.edu&#x2F;nycdata&#x2F;population-geography&#x2F;pop...</a>')
