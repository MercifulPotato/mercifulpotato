Item(by='lxgr', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604582081, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24997381, text='Yes, firewalls are very much required for IPv6 home networks.<p>Default global reachability is definitely not advisable, but having the option for individual devices and&#x2F;or ports is just great, and doubly so with not having to resort to using non-standard ports.<p>I&#x27;ve always despised the pattern of having device 1&#x27;s SSH exposed on port 22001, device 2 on 22002 etc., especially for protocols&#x2F;applications not supporting SRV records.<p>And for many client to client use cases these days, port forwarding isn&#x27;t even necessary: UDP hole punching is just a breeze with IPv6 and works consistently and reliably without any STUN&#x2F;ICE guesswork, as long as UDP is not outright blocked.')
