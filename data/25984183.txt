Item(by='oddity', descendants=None, kids=[25984259], score=None, time=1612139395, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25983609, text='I&#x27;m strongly sympathetic to the Alan Kay vision of browsers-as-operating systems (see an earlier comment on a similar topic [0]) and yet I still miss the days where I could read what someone had to say without loading yet another JS webapp.  The dumb document viewer model for the web was probably doomed from the start.  There are just too many unique ways to consume media, so instead, we as an industry repurposed the document layer (http) as a portable distribution platform for all the new web browsers that could just be dumb document viewers accessible to a mass audience of creators (facebook, twitter, youtube, etc...).<p>I think the Electron&#x2F;ChromeOS&#x2F;FirefoxOS&#x2F;WebOS&#x2F;Early iOS model is us finally recognizing that we&#x27;ve made some levels redundant and are now collapsing the stack (browser&#x2F;native OS).  It&#x27;s slow, it&#x27;s ugly, but we&#x27;re getting there.<p>[0] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=25855029" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=25855029</a>')
