Item(by='natmaka', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603604489, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24879899, text='The Economist graph shows &quot;covid-19 excess deaths&quot;.<p>The article is about &quot;September 2020 least deadly month ((...)) 2020 third least deadly year ever so far&quot;.<p>AFAIK there is no normalized way to count COVID-19-induced deaths.<p>If so there is no way to compare official &quot;COVID-19-induced deaths&quot;, because a given case may be counted as such in a country and not in another one, or even in the same nation by a given doctor and not by another one.<p>The total amount of deaths seems more objective.<p>Moreover the pandemics is running, therefore any comparison offers only a peek on a fraction of the challenge.  Isn&#x27;t this &quot;herd immunity&quot; approach a sort of &quot;let&#x27;s endure more short-term deaths, then durably much less afterwards&quot; bet?  In such a case any accounting may be moot for years.')
