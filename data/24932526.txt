Item(by='ocdtrekkie', descendants=None, kids=[24936444], score=None, time=1603990437, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24932471, text='youtube-dl -U would be challenging without a download server, but, the issue here is collaboration. Many people have copies of the youtube-dl repo, and many people can <i>build</i> the youtube-dl binary if provided the up-to-date changes. But to <i>develop</i> youtube-dl, they need to be able to continue to collaborate, which is currently at risk due to the DMCA takedown.<p>If they can continue to share patches over distributed mediums like a mailing list, they can continue to collaborate, and share improvements to the youtube-dl code that many already have copies of.<p>Possibly not a fantastic long-term solution, but certainly worth attempting whilst fighting it out with the RIAA.<p>Another important point is that many projects use GitHub as their general communication about issues, support, roadmap, etc. When GitHub suspends a repo, <i>all</i> of it goes away. Whereas Sourcehut is selling the point that your collaboration tools would survive even if they were required to remove your code.')
