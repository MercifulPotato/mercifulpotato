A governing body could make changes to the land or its use if it cared.<p>Not really. Often there was simply no entity that exercised executive function over the land or its use, which is a large part of why even plainly dysfunctional arrangements persisted.<p>The idea that there must be an owner to incentivise is modern and ahistorical. As traditionally conceived, the tragedy occurs not because &quot;the owner&quot; has the wrong incentives but because there simply isn&#x27;t an owner.
