Item(by='ggm', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610506580, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25735348, text='&quot;the&quot; alphabet being a western construct. Alphabets sui generis, were invented in lots of places to fill a need. I don&#x27;t think the precolombian American or Asiatic cultures were waiting for ISO-LATIN1 to come down the pipe.<p>There is a very odd theory about speech and writing and intelligence which I can&#x27;t quite capture, but its something around the idea that pre-literate people cannot be proven to have had any abstract concept of communication.<p>It gets shot down in flames pretty rapidly by burial remains which clearly indicate an organising principle (to bury) and belief in the afterlife: belief pre-supposes communication to shared belief. Neanderthal buried people with significant artifacts. Even the artifacts presuppose communication, you&#x27;d have to be able to communicate a desire for pretty-necklace-of-teeth-on-hair to somebody, for it to become widespread.<p>Or the Venus of Wuttenburg. It presupposes communication.')
