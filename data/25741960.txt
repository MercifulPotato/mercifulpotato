Item(by='carlineng', descendants=None, kids=[25742050], score=None, time=1610422760, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25739247, text='The CEO of Chartio recently gave a talk advocating for wide tables [1], but I don&#x27;t fully agree.<p>Having a wide table can have some performance benefits, but I think the primary benefit of dimensional modelling isn&#x27;t related to performance, but having standardized definitions, or &quot;conformed&quot; dimensions. For example, having a &quot;calendar&quot; dimension with attributes you can pull into your analysis such as fiscal quarter or reporting week.<p>In a data model with wide tables, if you need to change the definition of a dimension, it&#x27;s going to be painful to track down all the different places where that old dimension is used.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=3OcS2TMXELU" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=3OcS2TMXELU</a>')
