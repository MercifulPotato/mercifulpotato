Item(by='johncearls', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607890912, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25408681, text='I wouldn&#x27;t call Heinlein nuts. I think he was deeply interested in the idea of how social mores are a reflection of the times and conditions they exist in. It&#x27;s one of the things that set him above his peers.  The technological advances and space cowboy stuff was just a backdrop to discuss philosophy and morality. He did so in a generally entertaining way. I don&#x27;t know that he was advocating any particular philosophy or moralism. He was just exploring them. The difference in points of view between Stranger in a strange land, starship troopers, and the moon is a harsh mistress demonstrate that.')
