Item(by='onecommentman', descendants=None, kids=[25242667], score=None, time=1606618364, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25242481, text='The song, quite popular in the 1960s among some, is titled “Little Boxes”<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Little_Boxes" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Little_Boxes</a><p>From which I learned that no less than Tom Lehrer, someone else youngsters should know about, thought it was “the most sanctimonious song ever written”.  And he was right.<p>On the plus side, great children’s tune and the simplicity of the lyrics match the melody and provide a nice counterpoint to the message.<p>It perfectly captures the sour, smug, almost elitist cultural tone set by US 60s coastal urban intellectuals that hasn’t aged well over the years. I suppose you had to have been there.<p>Surprised no one has written a parody lampooning the cultural elites of the era with the same song and lyrical structure.')
