There is almost no evidence for &quot;long covid&quot;. The vast majority of the evidence is anecdotal, self-reported clusters of symptoms that resemble other common illnesses:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;sebastianrushworth.com&#x2F;2020&#x2F;11&#x2F;17&#x2F;what-is-long-covid&#x2F;" this one, from the CDC, which defined &quot;long-term&quot; as &quot;symptoms lasting longer than two weeks&quot;, where &quot;symptoms&quot; mostly resemble the aftermath of <i>any</i> mild respiratory illness -- fatigue, cough and headache were the most common:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cdc.gov&#x2F;mmwr&#x2F;volumes&#x2F;69&#x2F;wr&#x2F;mm6930e1.htm"
