See <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.companyformationswitzerland.com&#x2F;register-swiss-company" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.companyformationswitzerland.com&#x2F;register-swiss-c...</a> and elsewhere<p>2) Registering a Swiss company wouldn&#x27;t by itself allow them to escape US DoJ enfoncement actions.  see <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.justice.gov&#x2F;opa&#x2F;pr&#x2F;ubs-enters-deferred-prosecution-agreement" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.justice.gov&#x2F;opa&#x2F;pr&#x2F;ubs-enters-deferred-prosecuti...</a> for example<p>3) Registering a Swiss company wouldn&#x27;t exempt them from KYC requirements <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;bankersacademy.com&#x2F;pdf-aml&#x2F;amlswitzerland-law.pdf" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;bankersacademy.com&#x2F;pdf-aml&#x2F;amlswitzerland-law.pdf</a> for example says that the swiss AML legislation includes non-bank financial institutions (such as exchanges)<p>Edit to add: It&#x27;s also non-trivial to get a Swiss work visa if you&#x27;re not a national.  I had one for a while and it required a Swiss company to use up one of their finite central allocation and show that the work wasn&#x27;t replacing the job of a Swiss national and was being paid the average wage or more (this to prevent exploitation of migrant labour apparently).')
