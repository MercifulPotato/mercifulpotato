It&#x27;s due to changes in income <i>distribution</i>.<p>While it&#x27;s true that labor productivity growth has slowed over the last few decades, GDP per capita has continued to grow (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.macrotrends.net&#x2F;countries&#x2F;USA&#x2F;united-states&#x2F;gdp-per-capita" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.macrotrends.net&#x2F;countries&#x2F;USA&#x2F;united-states&#x2F;gdp-...</a>). What&#x27;s changed is that those gains have been overwhelmingly distributed to the rich.<p>The middle and lower classes have compensated for this by, as you note, increasing workforce participation, increasing reliance on consumer debt, and increasing personal bankruptcy (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;files.stlouisfed.org&#x2F;files&#x2F;htdocs&#x2F;publications&#x2F;review&#x2F;07&#x2F;01&#x2F;Garrett.pdf" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;files.stlouisfed.org&#x2F;files&#x2F;htdocs&#x2F;publications&#x2F;revie...</a>).')
