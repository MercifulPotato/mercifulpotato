Item(by='qayxc', descendants=None, kids=[25740958], score=None, time=1610411657, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25739255, text='It depends entirely on personal preferences.<p>You might find it baffling because <i>some</i> authors were early adopters of word processors and PCs in general, but others were not and some prefer to not use computers to this day.<p>I don&#x27;t argue that there is no use for word processors in writing, what I&#x27;m (obviously very poorly) trying to communicate is that productivity (measured by what exactly in this context?) doesn&#x27;t depend on using these tools.<p>Quentin Tarantino once told Reuters that he prefers to use pens and a notebook (the paper kind). Similarly with Joyce Carol Oates:<p>&gt; I always sketch out material “by hand.” Why is this so unusual? Every writer has written “by hand” until relatively recent times. Writing is a consequence of thinking, planning, dreaming — this is the process that results in “writing,” rather than the way in which the writing is recorded. [1]<p>Creativity doesn&#x27;t seem to suffer when not using a PC.<p>Neil Gaiman shares this sentiment [2], so even quality sci-fi doesn&#x27;t require much tech.<p>Danielle Steel managed to write 179 books without using a computer [3] - quantity isn&#x27;t it either.<p>George Clooney apparently has his writing partner do the typing as he himself prefers to write everything out by hand as well:<p>&gt; I&#x27;m probably the least computer literate writer there is... Literally when I cut and paste, I cut pages and tape them together. [4]<p>So the superiority of word processing software simply doesn&#x27;t materialise for everyone who&#x27;s in the business of writing.<p>Writing is, after all, first and foremost a creative process and everyone has a different approach to get the most out of their creativity. The tools used are the least important part in determining whether that productive (e.g. successful) or not.<p>For some (including me, most of the time) technology is a welcome helper and improvement, while others do just fine without.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.salon.com&#x2F;2012&#x2F;09&#x2F;13&#x2F;joyce_carol_oates_romneys_ghastly&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.salon.com&#x2F;2012&#x2F;09&#x2F;13&#x2F;joyce_carol_oates_romneys_g...</a><p>[2] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.verbaltovisual.com&#x2F;neil-gaiman-on-writing&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.verbaltovisual.com&#x2F;neil-gaiman-on-writing&#x2F;</a><p>[3] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.glamour.com&#x2F;story&#x2F;danielle-steel-books-interview" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.glamour.com&#x2F;story&#x2F;danielle-steel-books-interview</a><p>[4] <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.reddit.com&#x2F;r&#x2F;IAmA&#x2F;comments&#x2F;1wdzwq&#x2F;hello_reddit_george_clooney_here_amaa" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.reddit.com&#x2F;r&#x2F;IAmA&#x2F;comments&#x2F;1wdzwq&#x2F;hello_reddit_ge...</a>')
