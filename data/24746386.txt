The solution I came to involves putting the hash of the most recent Bitcoin block in your document, and then signing the hash of your document into the Bitcoin blockchain.<p>This cryptographically proves that the document was created&#x2F;published within those two time bounds.<p>This of course doesn&#x27;t help in all cases, i.e. you could edit an old document to make it look newer, but I&#x27;m not aware of any way to properly solve that particular problem without a trusted third-party.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.jamieweb.net&#x2F;blog&#x2F;proof-of-timestamp&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.jamieweb.net&#x2F;blog&#x2F;proof-of-timestamp&#x2F;</a>')
