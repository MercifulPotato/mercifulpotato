Item(by='tzs', descendants=None, kids=[24932253, 24932228, 24933978, 24932325], score=None, time=1603988560, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24930952, text='I never really thought of this before, but now that I have I&#x27;m confused about backups. In particular, why do we back up some types things but not others?<p>I&#x27;ve never made backups of my books.<p>I&#x27;ve never made backups of my vinyl records or my cassette tapes.<p>I have made backups of all my CDs.<p>If the backup is to protect against loss of or damage to the media, I should be backing up books and records and tapes, and not backing up CDs. CDs live in a protective case, only coming out for short trips to a CD player, where they are played entirely enclosed, and playback does not cause any wear on the play surface.<p>Records and tapes are degrade slightly each time you play them. Books also suffer wear each time they are read, and are usually read in environments where they can suffer accidents.')
