Item(by='spyspy', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610806773, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25800842, text='I’ve built an api gateway in go at my last company and my current one, so I’m actually a fan of the pattern. Usually the decision revolves around complex auth and permissioning rules that already exist in go, and would be a pain to rewrite. It also allows for custom multiplexing of requests to hit multiple downstream APIs. It also makes monitoring simpler, since you can have a view of your entire platform by just monitoring a single service. A reverse proxy in go is dead simple to write and maintain, especially if it matches the rest of your stack.')
