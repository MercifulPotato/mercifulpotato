Item(by='billllll', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610345156, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25724380, text='I think there is certainly a middle ground between &quot;engineers do all the decision making&quot; and &quot;engineers do none of the decision making.&quot; I&#x27;m not sure the author was advocating the former. Rather, I think the author was saying that the possibility to influence the decision making at a fundamental level is what makes the SV-like companies great.<p>This follows my experience. Even at a FAANG, product managers aren&#x27;t going to get every detail and edge cases are going to pop-up, so requirements are going to come from a back-and-forth between product and eng throughout the process. That back-and-forth is the key, not expecting engineers to figure out all the requirements, nor from product managers either.')
