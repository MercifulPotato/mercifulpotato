Individual words in Japanese can be represented in three ways:<p>1) Pure hiragana\n2) Pure Kanji\n3) A mixture of Kanji + hiragana<p>#3 comes into picture especially when conjugating verbs. As the base verb is written in Kanji but the various forms are tacked on in hiragana.<p>The important bit : The kanji symbol essentially just compresses the hiragana into a symbol, but all words that contain kanji can be uncompressed back to hiragana.<p>Japanese is definitely hard to learn, but it is not hard to pronounce. The hardness comes from the fact that the Kanji can have multiple &#x27;readings&#x27; depending on the context.<p>Source: Studying&#x2F;struggling with Japanese for the last 5 years.')
