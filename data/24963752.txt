Item(by='pmiller2', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604276293, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24962482, text='I actually think this is a fairly simple thing to teach at work.  If I&#x27;m being asked a question by a junior, I will frequently go do a few minutes&#x27; research, then come back and say &quot;this is the answer, found on these [wiki pages|Slack threads|JIRA tickets|Google docs].  I found it by searching these terms in [appropriate place].&quot;  If I&#x27;m the one asking, I frequently say &quot;Okay, I can accept that as an answer, but can you show me how you arrived at that?&quot;<p>Both of these things, when done in good faith, are moves that help the ignorant asker to learn.  I&#x27;ve never been put off by a sincere request by someone to show them my work, so to speak.  I don&#x27;t think anyone should be put off by asking something like this sincerely, either.')
