Item(by='ydant', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610588816, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25760066, text='I just recently started trying to get my note-taking together and build my own personal knowledge base (or at least stop trying to remember <i>everything</i> and instead store it somewhere to refer back to).<p>After trying a handful of the usually mentioned tools, I&#x27;ve been very happy with Trilium Notes [1], which seems to share some of the same concepts as this project, but providing rich-text (including embedded images).<p>As much as I like the flexibility that flat files, markdown, or Org-mode provide, I also really like the extra built-in functionality that comes along with Trilium.<p>I&#x27;m not wild about having the data tied into a single program, but the data is at least accessible and documented (if primarily through the open source code), so I know my data&#x27;s not entirely locked up.<p>Trilium says macOS isn&#x27;t supported as the author doesn&#x27;t have a Mac, but it works 95% like you&#x27;d expect with a few small quirks.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;zadam&#x2F;trilium" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;zadam&#x2F;trilium</a>')
