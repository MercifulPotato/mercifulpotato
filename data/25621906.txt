Item(by='grumbel', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609683673, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25621398, text='&gt; The &quot;Uncanny Valley&quot; only exists in two dimensions.<p>The original Uncanny Valley hypothesis came out of robotics back in 1970, its application to computer graphics came decades later.<p>I don&#x27;t even think 2D vs 3D plays much of a role, a realistic Luke Skywalker wax figure will look just as uncanny as CGI Luke in Mandalorian.<p>I find deepfakes interesting in this regard, as while they look much more realistic than most CGI, they never go uncanny. They can go a bit glitchy, blurry and might not look like a perfect replica of the faked person, but they still end up looking like person, not like an uncanny CGI copy.')
