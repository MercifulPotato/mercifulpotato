I come from a heavy-drinking culture that is now promoting “Mediterranean-style” drinking habits as being a “healthy alternative” to binge-drinking.<p>Maybe this is a good public health tactic, to push people to a “lesser evil”, but according to meta-analyses like <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov&#x2F;17478320&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov&#x2F;17478320&#x2F;</a>, there really is no safe level for alcohol. And even a “lesser evil” approach can be applied while being honest — know that when you’re drinking, it should always be pure indulgence, not under some false pretense of getting resveratrol or some other nonsense. <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov&#x2F;25670722&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov&#x2F;25670722&#x2F;</a> provides something of an overview.<p>Apropos of nothing, sci dash hub dot se.')
