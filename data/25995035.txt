Item(by='magic_quotes', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1612218779, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25994444, text='&gt; Closed source GPU drivers are not ordinary drivers. They have a lot of quirks and workarounds for programs, especially games.<p>The quirks part is a bit of a meme. Mesa contains a few application-specific overrides here and there: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;mesa3d&#x2F;mesa&#x2F;blob&#x2F;c62996796cc46f1a1406f365cbb26f67e5d07c42&#x2F;src&#x2F;util&#x2F;00-mesa-defaults.conf" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;mesa3d&#x2F;mesa&#x2F;blob&#x2F;c62996796cc46f1a1406f365...</a>, <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;mesa3d&#x2F;mesa&#x2F;blob&#x2F;a678ec9b8c057311ed7e96977c067e5469ca9825&#x2F;src&#x2F;amd&#x2F;vulkan&#x2F;radv_device.c#L608-L667" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;mesa3d&#x2F;mesa&#x2F;blob&#x2F;a678ec9b8c057311ed7e9697...</a>. The reason Linux drivers, <i>including</i> the one from Nvidia, have fewer quirks is simply that Linux has fewer 3d applications overall. Those quirks also reside in userspace driver parts and should not affect system stability.<p>&gt; When you try to port these drivers as-is to another platforms, things get awry.<p>Again, quirks are a property of a relatively high-level userspace part of the driver. There is nothing hazardous about porting them.')
