Item(by='GordonS', descendants=None, kids=[25142003, 25140980], score=None, time=1605725067, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25140031, text='There is a newer type of test, only very briefly mentioned in the article, and strangely not even by name: NIPT (Non-Invasive Prenatal Testing).<p>It works by testing tiny amounts of the baby&#x27;s DNA that can be found in the mother&#x27;s blood. It is close to 100% effective for detecting Down&#x27;s Syndrome.<p>It needs only a blood sample, so carries no meaningful risk for mother or fetus, but it&#x27;s more expensive than the older amnio or sampling methods, so is usually only available privately (at least in the UK).')
