Item(by='tedsanders', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606812261, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25262259, text='I hesitate to speculate, but one possibility is that for large reactors you have so much heat that you need heat exchange by liquids rather than solids or gases or non-atomic particles. My understanding is that solids are bad because electrons and phonons cannot carry as much energy as atoms. Gases are bad because they don&#x27;t have enough atoms. This leaves liquids as best, which is why we so often see liquid cooling in animals and machinery.')
