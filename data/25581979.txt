Item(by='gpderetta', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609338669, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25581842, text='These days GCC and clang can inline function pointers in many scenarios even through function calls, but it wasn&#x27;t the case even a few years ago, and they still fail to inline in many important cases.<p>Inlining pointers requires doing constant propagation and the compiler can give up if it has to track too much stuff, while in the lambda case, the type of the comparator is always available.<p>Also lambdas can be removed in the early inliner stage exposing them to more optimization opportunities, while function pointers have to be inlined after constant propagation.<p>As usual, it is a trade off, so there is no substitute to  measuring in your specific scenario.')
