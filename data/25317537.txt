Item(by='antognini', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607196630, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25317388, text='In addition to that, while Kepler was lecturing on the Great Conjunction he was drawing the trigon [1] which shows how the locations of Great Conjunctions change over time.  This drawing (consisting of a triangle inscribed inside a circle that gets shifted over time), inspired him to pursue the idea that the distances to the planets were determined by polygons of different shapes inscribed in circles.  This didn&#x27;t work, so he moved on to Platonic solids, which worked better, but still not perfectly.  Eventually this pursuit lead him to the idea of describing the planetary orbits by ellipses.<p>[1]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Great_conjunction#&#x2F;media&#x2F;File:Keplers_trigon.jpg" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Great_conjunction#&#x2F;media&#x2F;File:...</a>')
