Item(by='scatters', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607346497, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25330863, text='Back-of-the-envelope calculation: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.climate-lab-book.ac.uk&#x2F;2015&#x2F;back-of-the-envelope-attribution&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.climate-lab-book.ac.uk&#x2F;2015&#x2F;back-of-the-envelope...</a><p>The TCR needs some explanation, but can be estimated from the Stefan-Boltzmann law; the temperature differential to produce a radiative output of 3.7 W&#x2F;sq m is 3.7 &#x2F; (4 * sigma * T0^3) which at a T0 of 287 K is 0.69 K. Clearly the Earth is not a perfect black-body so this lower bounds the TCR.<p>This also allows us to lower bound the anthrogenic climate contribution due to GHG radiative forcing 1850-2020 at 0.48 K.')
