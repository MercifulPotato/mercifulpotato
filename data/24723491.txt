Item(by='tptacek', descendants=None, kids=[24725371], score=None, time=1602188569, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24723028, text='I like Result&lt;T, E&gt; and would welcome its addition to Go, but the most surprising thing to me about switching from Go to Rust is that error handling is basically identical in practice; a little less typing in Rust, though you weigh that against Go&#x27;s (weak but convenient) universal error interface.<p>Rust gives a hard shove where Go gives something closer to a nudge, but in practice, Go&#x27;s nudge (the requirement to do something with an error or else bear the shame of _&#x27;ing it) gets me 99.999% of the way to Result.')
