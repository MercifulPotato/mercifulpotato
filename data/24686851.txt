Item(by='goto11', descendants=None, kids=[24686949, 24689065, 24687624], score=None, time=1601899108, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24686211, text='&gt; Multiple times I&#x27;ve designed systems where I&#x27;ve reduced the representable states to the minimum, and when some requirements change I realize I have to re-design the full system.<p>Yes, if requirements change, you change the design and code to support the new requirements.<p>Compromising the consistency and maintainability of the current design to accommodate a hypothetical future requirement change is a bad trade-off IMHO, since you can&#x27;t predict the future. A requirement change may happen in a completely different direction than the one you anticipated, and then you have the worst of both worlds.<p>It is better to make code <i>maintainable</i> than making it <i>flexible</i>.')
