Item(by='webmaven', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603808545, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24903706, text='<i>&gt; You can&#x27;t kill them but you sure as hell can kidnap and torture them for eternity.</i><p>For many would-be Stalins, the main point isn&#x27;t the torture, but the kidnapping and incommunicado imprisonment (eg. in gulags).<p>The utility of torture comes in when you want to ensure the prisoner&#x27;s compliance when reintroduced to society.<p>So: please note that immortality by itself doesn&#x27;t preclude either sequestration nor adversarial behavior modification.<p>Technical solutions to both of those (integrity checks to detect coercion, restoring from distributed backups, etc.), start to run into the same sort of arms-races we have with internet filtering, botnets, and malware. Except that so far as a totalitarian regime is concerned, the uploaded dissident <i>is</i> the malware, as is any sufficiently advanced freedom-preserving tech.')
