Item(by='ziftface', descendants=None, kids=[25939448], score=None, time=1611817067, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25938826, text='I don&#x27;t think it&#x27;s this simple. That interpretation of freedom of speech seems a bit literal. The point is that people should have the ability to voice their opinions, and have their voice heard if anyone wants to hear it. Since today that&#x27;s done primarily through social media sites, blogs, and videos, preventing people from using those mediums is limiting their freedom of speech.<p>And of course I&#x27;m not saying there aren&#x27;t limits to freedom of speech. There are legitimate limits. But a social media company deciding they don&#x27;t want you on their platform anymore without any due process seems wrong.<p>I just don&#x27;t buy the overly literal &quot;this is private property and I get to decide what you&#x27;re allowed to say here&quot;, when that private property is our primary medium for communication.')
