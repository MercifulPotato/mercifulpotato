Item(by='inopinatus', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604518167, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24982651, text='That isn&#x27;t how email confirmation works, because it&#x27;s a) enormously prone to false negatives, b) subject to ruinous delays, c) occurring after the transaction i.e. too late, and d) building you a reputation as a bad sender.<p>This is why all email address confirmation today is asking you to click a link or enter a code.<p>Also note that the latter strategy is rising in popularity; it is because clickable links, whilst seeming so convenient, are themselves prone to both false positives and negatives, and also increase the likelihood of ending up in junk mail.')
