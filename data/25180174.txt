Item(by='clairity', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606072745, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25179696, text='i make the same argument for hiring. once you&#x27;ve done some basic filtering, you&#x27;re likely better off hiring at random than trying to &quot;pick the best&quot;, as alluring as that is, because your biases will select against intellectual and creative diversity.<p>economies of scale work to your advantage here as well. when hiring 1 person, you feel immense pressure to hire correctly. but when hiring for 10 similar roles, you can, for instance, hire 11 people at random from a qualified pool and find the best fits along the way (often even for the &#x27;extra&#x27; hire, who might fit in an entirely different role).')
