The coolest-sounding technologies are the ones where you can describe to a layman how it works, but they&#x27;ve still rarely or never seen something that works that way.<p>3d-printing is perfect tech for this: the principle is graspable by anyone, it makes for cool stop motion videos, yet it&#x27;s still rare in practice. Injection moulding is too commonplace: we&#x27;ve cast metal in moulds for thousands of years.<p>A cool name helps as well, of course. 3D printing is definitely sexier than &quot;additive manufacturing&quot;.')
