Item(by='mcpherrinm', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603139891, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24830647, text='Just because something&#x27;s closed source doesn&#x27;t mean we can&#x27;t learn about it.  The field of reverse engineering has been around for a long time, and the iPhone and Mac are two of the most studied devices around.<p>Ultimately you do have to trust your platform if you&#x27;re going to use a platform authenticator to some degree.<p>But the alternative in the FIDO2&#x2F;webauthn space is something like a Yubikey which has no biometrics; it just takes a simple tap.  And can be easily removed from your computer.  So in that comparison, the fingerprint is purely additive security.  Even if it&#x27;s adding nothing to a serious adversary, it&#x27;s still dramatically reducing risk to a less-skilled local attacker.')
