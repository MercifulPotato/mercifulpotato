Item(by='Wowfunhappy', descendants=None, kids=[25641376], score=None, time=1609811463, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25640894, text='I think there might be psychological issues at play here, sadly. If you click onto a news website from a search engine, and it ends up being for the wrong thing, people feel cheated and will want their money back.<p>This is probably silly, because if you&#x27;re paying a couple cents per view, you should be concentrating on the value you receive in the aggregate... but people aren&#x27;t rational, and personally, I don&#x27;t think I&#x27;d be immune to feeling a sense of regret when I spent money on the wrong link.<p>I&#x27;m not going to remember the episode, but I&#x27;m pretty sure Planet Money said this is what happened when micropayments were tried in the 90s.<p>I subscribe to Scroll.com, and I think they&#x27;ve probably got a better model down. Make agreements with <i>lots</i> of sites from different companies, and then offer a bulk subscription which removes ads from all of them. It doesn&#x27;t let you through paywalls, mind you, but you could imagine a variation on the concept which did.')
