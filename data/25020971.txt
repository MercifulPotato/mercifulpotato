Item(by='analog31', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604792475, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25020841, text='A dark secret of power amp design is that the power supply is half the battle. Note that this design uses a conventional line frequency transformer rather than a switchmode power supply. Making an all-switchmode amp is actually a bit of a challenge. I&#x27;ve been there. My home stereo is a cheap switchmode power amp board, but the power supply is line frequency, as a concession after I discovered that powering it from a switchmode power supply makes it put out all sort of weird noises.<p>An extremely nice product are the &quot;IcePower&quot; modules made by Bang and Olufsen, which incorporate both power supply and power amp into a single board. So they&#x27;ve worked out the bugs of making the parts play nicely together. These are now the jellybean power amps in smaller musical instrument amplifiers, of which I own a couple.')
