There are practical and political reasons for not allowing phone use, but meaningful risk to the safety of flight is not one of them.<p>Instruments are fine, what could suffer problems are RF receivers (radio, radar, GNSS) but outside of interference during the event and the potential need to reboot problematic equipment, the critical instruments (pitot-static, accelerometers and gyros) aren&#x27;t particularly exposed. In terms of effects to the aircraft systems directly, all that article mentions are upset events, and those are already a design consideration.<p>The 737-Max story came about specifically because it was <i>not</i> a FBW aircraft and not designed as such with sufficiently redundant systems. The safety case was that crews could disable the system if it misbehaved, rather than it being essential for flight like a real FBW system would be.')
