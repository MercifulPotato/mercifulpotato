Item(by='sillyquiet', descendants=None, kids=[25780751], score=None, time=1610650691, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25780278, text='I think part of it is the corporate nature of the owners of D&amp;D now, and part of it is that the D&amp;D 1st and 2nd editions and to a lesser extent the 3rd edition,  were designed to appeal to the sensibilities and tastes of the Dave Arnesons and Gary Gygaxes of the world, i.e., middle-class western European or North American, mostly white and mostly male nerds.<p>The later editions have much more general audiences from much different generations than those early ones, and so the game changes - and that&#x27;s not a <i>bad</i> thing.\nBuuuut, combine that with the impetus of a corporation to make their product sanitary and palatable to that wide range of tastes, you end up with something bland and inoffensive.<p>edit: none of that is to say that I think 5ed is a bad game, it&#x27;s not, it&#x27;s just different in kind and in philosophy to the earlier editions.')
