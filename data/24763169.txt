The difference between a cluster and a standalone setup is just whether you mount local instances of a service, or remote instances.<p>For a cluster, you&#x27;ll usually have a cwfs&#x2F;hjfs (persistent storage) server, a faktotum (auth) server, and one or more CPU servers, and possibly a terminal machine.<p>You can then freely use or connect to CPU servers. As everything is shared, and the GUI is also file-based, everything will feel local.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;9p.io&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;plan9&#x2F;Expanding_your_Grid&#x2F;index.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;9p.io&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;plan9&#x2F;Expanding_your_Grid&#x2F;index.html</a>')
