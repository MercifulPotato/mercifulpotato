Item(by='teraflop', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609779567, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25632714, text='&gt; If 1% of users can&#x27;t send messages, then that should count as a full-blown outage and should start counting against whatever SLA they advertise.<p>Google published a paper last year describing this approach to measuring uptime: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;blog.acolyer.org&#x2F;2020&#x2F;02&#x2F;26&#x2F;meaningful-availability&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;blog.acolyer.org&#x2F;2020&#x2F;02&#x2F;26&#x2F;meaningful-availability&#x2F;</a><p>The idea is to define availability as &quot;the probability that the site &#x27;appeared&#x27; to be down for a random user, averaged over a time window of size w&quot;. You can choose a particular value of w and look at trends over time, or you can plot availability as a <i>function</i> of w to understand patterns of downtime.')
