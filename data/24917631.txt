Item(by='Macha', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603885547, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24916434, text='My understanding is the LUKS header block contains a key actually used to decrypt your data, but that key is itself decrypted by a key derived from your password or other auth data. grub is using the first key to obtain the second key, the kernel already has the second key (or you&#x27;d have to enter your password again).<p>When you set up the encrypted drive, it deliberately picks settings for this that will be as slow as possible to resist brute force attacks while also aiming not to take over 30s on your current CPU. You&#x27;d have to manually specify a lower iteration count to cryptsetup to get a faster unlock.')
