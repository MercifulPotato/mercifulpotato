Item(by='libraryofbabel', descendants=None, kids=[24831772, 24831551, 24831739, 24832672, 24831536, 24831997], score=None, time=1603144109, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24821398, text='Good writing matters. One of the most undervalued skills in engineering, especially at the Senior+ levels. Being able to write a clear, concise, well-argued design doc will be useful to you long after the hot framework of the moment has become obsolete. And yet, few people take the time to hone the skill.<p>Strunk and White is a classic, sure, which this article helps to put in context. But it’s 75 years old, and it has its problems (as the article discusses). I wouldn’t really recommend it anymore. Instead, have a look at:<p>Williams and Bizup, <i>Style: Lessons in Clarity and Grace</i><p>And, for everything you ever wanted to know about standard American English <i>usage</i> (and how it’s shifting over time!), there is <i>Garner’s Modern English Usage</i>, which sounds dry but is actually fascinating (and inspired this brilliantly controversial review by David Foster Wallace: <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;harpers.org&#x2F;wp-content&#x2F;uploads&#x2F;HarpersMagazine-2001-04-0070913.pdf" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;harpers.org&#x2F;wp-content&#x2F;uploads&#x2F;HarpersMagazine-2001-0...</a>).')
