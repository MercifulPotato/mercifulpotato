Item(by='Jun8', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607729560, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25382501, text='These slim volumes are a <i>joy</i> to read precisely for the reasons given in the article: minimal textual flourish, old-fashioned translations, green and red colors, etc. as well their enormous breadth. I still remember the joy when my wife gifted me Procopius&#x27;s <i>On Buildings</i> (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.loebclassics.com&#x2F;view&#x2F;LCL343&#x2F;1940&#x2F;volume.xml" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.loebclassics.com&#x2F;view&#x2F;LCL343&#x2F;1940&#x2F;volume.xml</a> many years ago (and the guilty delight in reading his <i>Anecdota</i> later on: talk about an un-bowdlerized translation!). When you hold one, you feel you are a <i>classicist</i>, a feeling that cannot be easily described.<p>The only bad thing about the Loeb volumes are their price, they are generally $25-$30, which is steep for such small books. If I had a ton of money I would commission similar volumes subsidized to $10 max. For the starving student currently the only alternative is the Web, e.g. Textkit (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.textkit.com&#x2F;greek-latin-forum&#x2F;viewtopic.php?t=70592" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.textkit.com&#x2F;greek-latin-forum&#x2F;viewtopic.php?t=70...</a>) and Perseus (<a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.perseus.tufts.edu&#x2F;hopper&#x2F;collection?collection=Perseus:collection:Greco-Roman&amp;redirect=true" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.perseus.tufts.edu&#x2F;hopper&#x2F;collection?collection=Pe...</a>)')
