Item(by='quacked', descendants=None, kids=[24806544, 24805044], score=None, time=1602876552, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24804026, text='It definitely isn&#x27;t helping, haha.<p>I also play piano, guitar, and compose, and I used to struggle with memorizing, but I&#x27;ve been reading a lot of piano practicing theory and have drastically upgraded my finger memory, mostly by using these resources:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.pianostreet.com&#x2F;smf&#x2F;index.php?topic=12590.0" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.pianostreet.com&#x2F;smf&#x2F;index.php?topic=12590.0</a> (look for Bernhard&#x27;s post)<p><a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.pianopractice.org&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.pianopractice.org&#x2F;</a><p>Loosely, stop using sheet music except when you&#x27;re initially memorizing the piece, learn music in much smaller chunks than initially expected, and play entirely from memory whenever possible, even when you&#x27;re practicing the repetition of something.<p>The fact that you can remember music easily in your head means that if you spend 1-3 years really banging out the hand-brain connection, you might be able to rocket your playing forward. Raw improvisation where you think of the melody before you play it will help, too, if you don&#x27;t already do that.')
