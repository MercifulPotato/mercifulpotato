Item(by='naringas', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603150501, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24831699, text='I think this has something to do with the sense of ownership behind the software.<p>An open source software is owned (literally and figuratively) by the people writing it, hence they will take care of it as they take care of their own.<p>On the other hand, propitary software is owned by some company (and its stockholders); it is written by employees payed to do so.<p>The employees will come and go, only the company will remain; naturally this reduces the sense of ownership any programmer may have over the code and with it, the quality of the software.<p>this is, of course, a very broad generalization riddled with exceptions and special cases. Not all software is alike.')
