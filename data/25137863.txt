Item(by='aazaa', descendants=None, kids=[25140919, 25140966, 25143068], score=None, time=1605713583, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25135752, text='From the author&#x27;s description of the One Item Store:<p>&gt; Don&#x27;t get me wrong, I&#x27;m happy with the final product, I think its actually really cool. But building it.. it was just a bit boring. There were lots of bugs, and I started to get a bit burnt out thinking about the endless list of things required for a good online store builder.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;tinyprojects.dev&#x2F;projects&#x2F;one_item_store" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;tinyprojects.dev&#x2F;projects&#x2F;one_item_store</a><p>This is a fascinating observation. In a couple of weeks he&#x27;s both built something from scratch and figured out that the work of doing it wasn&#x27;t enjoyable. It takes some people years to get to that point.<p>To figure out what you want to do, you often have to sift through a lot of fool&#x27;s gold - junk that looks appealing as an idea but isn&#x27;t appealing as a part of your life. The tiny projects idea offers a way to accelerate that process.<p>But the key here is to <i>finish</i>. To have an end point in mind and then reach it. 20% completion may get you 80% of the functionality. But by god, getting that last 20% could be the hardest, grinding work you will ever do. It&#x27;s going to be very difficult to finish unless it&#x27;s aligned in some fundamental way with your life.')
