Item(by='nostromo', descendants=None, kids=[24840595, 24844560], score=None, time=1603216575, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24840216, text='The monopolized market in this case would be the market for default search engines on mobile.<p>If the only two players (Google and Apple) both use Google Search by default, then Google has effectively captured 100% of the mobile ad market.  (And since Google is paying Apple et al for that default state, it is indeed a market.)<p>The court could require device makers (including Google itself) to prompt users to select their search engine provider, or potentially ban Google from buying search engine defaults from other companies (Apple, Mozilla, etc.) as an anticompetitive practice.')
