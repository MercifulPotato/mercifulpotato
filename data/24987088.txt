Item(by='aidenn0', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604475578, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24985905, text='If DSL counts as broadband, then it&#x27;s about 1&#x2F;4 the population, if DSL does not count as broadband then it&#x27;s about 80% of the population.<p>Note that the FCC numbers linked elsewhere are optimistic because of their coarse granularity; if there is a single household with two options in a census region, then all of the population is counted as having it.<p>There&#x27;s obviously the issues with suburban and rural housing, where density is insufficient for two last-mile solutions (and all local-loop unbundling regulations were so successful in the late 90s that the telecom companies lobbied them out of existence).<p>Something less talked about are the urban apartments where you can only get broadband from the company that contracts with your landlord.  If someone that doesn&#x27;t use the broadband can pick which broadband you use from several options, does that count as competition?')
