Item(by='ska', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608595312, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25501135, text='That&#x27;s not a disclaimer.  That&#x27;s (part of) the indications for use, which is a term of art.  These are inherently narrowing statements, and they are in place to constrain what the company can <i>market</i> a device as, and gives the FDA a stick to beat you with if you aren&#x27;t careful about this.<p>If you follow the submission you&#x27;ll also see they were granted approval to market as a de-novo filing as a class II device, so it&#x27;s not as much work as a PMA but the regulatory work is not peanuts.  Since then they have self-predicated for a 510(k) on a newer version.<p>Even for a smallish software only project like this, it means they had to set up all the basic infrastructure and have it audited.  They also wrote a ton of formal engineering process documentation, a full design history file, etc. These skills at least are pretty transferable.')
