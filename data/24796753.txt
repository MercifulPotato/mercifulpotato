Item(by='dognotdog', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602814837, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24793578, text='I do use a D800 with a 50&#x2F;F1.4 lens for video conf calls.<p>A lot of the current generation DSLRs do provide XGA, 1024x768 sized preview images when tethered, as, surprise surprise, that is the resolution of their viewfinder&#x2F;screen. Older cameras, like the D800, only do 640x480, though IMO that is still a vastly superior image for video conferencing.<p>I&#x27;ve put together what I&#x27;ve found out so far at <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;dognotdog&#x2F;ptpwebcam&#x2F;blob&#x2F;master&#x2F;CAMERAS.md" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;dognotdog&#x2F;ptpwebcam&#x2F;blob&#x2F;master&#x2F;CAMERAS.m...</a><p>Sony ILSM cameras seem to all do 1024x, DSLRs mostly do 640x480 unless they&#x27;re from the last 2 or so generation of cameras. I have most complete information for Nikon, as they actually publish specs for the cameras PTP interfaces, unlike Canon or Sony, which have to be reverse engineered.')
