Item(by='DHowett', descendants=None, kids=[25526149], score=None, time=1608774487, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25517084, text='Haiku has a clever solution to the problems presented by vector images at smaller sizes: Haiku Vector Icon Format (HVIF)¹².<p>HVIF supports only basic shapes with integer coordinates (by default) and detail reduction. Each shape is tagged with a minimum and maximum scale³--computed from a base icon size of 64x64--at which it should be visible. Rounding and hinting bring those shapes within tossing distance of custom-drawn icons at small scales.<p>¹ Leah Hanson&#x27;s post is quite illuminating, though it focuses more on the small on-disk size of HVIF data; <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;blog.leahhanson.us&#x2F;post&#x2F;recursecenter2016&#x2F;haiku_icons.html" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;blog.leahhanson.us&#x2F;post&#x2F;recursecenter2016&#x2F;haiku_icons...</a><p>² <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;web.archive.org&#x2F;web&#x2F;20070111153750&#x2F;http:&#x2F;&#x2F;haiku-os.org&#x2F;node&#x2F;280" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;web.archive.org&#x2F;web&#x2F;20070111153750&#x2F;http:&#x2F;&#x2F;haiku-os.o...</a><p>³ &quot;stippi&quot;&#x27;s original announcement: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.freelists.org&#x2F;post&#x2F;haiku&#x2F;Icon-Artwork" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.freelists.org&#x2F;post&#x2F;haiku&#x2F;Icon-Artwork</a>')
