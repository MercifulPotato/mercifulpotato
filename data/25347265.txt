Item(by='IfOnlyYouKnew', descendants=None, kids=[25347773, 25347783], score=None, time=1607444359, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25345902, text='Yes, the audiophile market is uniquely stupid, in that they tend to make specific claims that are objectively wrong. The ethernet cables come to mind, or any (digital) cable that&#x27;s supposed to be better at conducting a signal than any generic spec-compliant alternative.<p>Wine, for example, just doesn&#x27;t usually come with specific claims, except it&#x27;s &quot;good&quot;, or &quot;fruity&quot;, or &quot;created in the rich terroir of the L&#x27;Alpesian Valley&quot;. That region may not actually be better than any other, but it <i>is</i> different. Beyond that, everyone knows it&#x27;s a matter of taste, with no objective measures of &quot;good&quot; or &quot;bad&quot;, although there are obviously (imprecise) trends that many people tend to agree with (i. e. wine is better than vinegar).')
