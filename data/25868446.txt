Item(by='pdonis', descendants=None, kids=[25868510], score=None, time=1611291169, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25868337, text='<i>&gt; It has never been the case that journalists have honored a right to public pseudonymity.</i><p>Even if this is true, that doesn&#x27;t make it right.<p><i>&gt; ordinary life, where the things people say in different places are profoundly relevant to their reputation and credibility</i><p>That depends on <i>why</i> you are interested in their reputation and credibility. I&#x27;m not saying there are never cases where it&#x27;s justified to reveal things the person would rather not reveal. But &quot;we&#x27;re writing a story and we have a policy&quot; is not enough justification (even if the policy is consistently understood by all the newspaper&#x27;s employees, which it doesn&#x27;t seem like it was in this case). There needs to be an actual <i>reason</i> why people have a right to know details about a person that that person would rather keep private, and it has to be a strong enough reason to outweigh that person&#x27;s choices.<p>In this case, we&#x27;re talking about someone&#x27;s <i>blog</i>, and the whole <i>point</i> of a blog is that it stands or falls by what is written there and what is referenced there, and personal details about the writer that aren&#x27;t included are irrelevant. So it seems like this is an <i>obvious</i> case where a choice to remain anonymous should be respected.')
