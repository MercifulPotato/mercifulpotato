Item(by='iLemming', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602286953, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24718478, text='My favorite documentation&#x2F;comments feature of Clojure is the ability to attach examples to the metadata, e.g:<p><pre><code>     (defn prime?\n      &quot;Returns true if given number is prime.&quot;\n      {:example\n       &#x27;((prime? 97)   &quot;=&gt; true&quot;\n         (prime? 74)   &quot;=&gt; false&quot;)}\n      [n]\n      ,,,\n      ;; implementation\n      )\n\n      \n     (-&gt; #&#x27;user&#x2F;prime? meta :example) \n     ;; =&gt; ((prime? 97) &quot;=&gt; true&quot; (prime? 74) &quot;=&gt; false&quot;)\n     </code></pre>\nYou can even incorporate assertions and have tests that would make sure the examples are correct, something like:<p><pre><code>    (defn prime?\n      &quot;Returns true if given number is prime.&quot;\n      {:examples\n      &#x27;((-&gt; (prime? 74) false? assert)\n        (-&gt; (prime? 97) true? assert))}\n      [n]\n      ,,,\n      ;; implementation\n      )\n\n    (doseq [f (-&gt;&gt; #&#x27;user&#x2F;prime? meta :example)] (eval f))</code></pre>')
