Whatever you are trying to learn, take a stab at learning it<p>2. Write everything down, in as simple a way as possible, as if you were preparing a lecture for an inquisitive child<p>3. Ask questions as if you were a child to identify gaps in your understanding<p>4. Repeat step 3 until the questioning adds no incremental value<p>&quot;We have to learn that these are the kinds of disciplines in the field of science that you have to learn – to know when you know, and when you don’t know, and what it is you know, and what it is you don&#x27;t know.<p>You’ve got to be very careful not to confuse yourself.&quot;<p>-Richard Feynman')
