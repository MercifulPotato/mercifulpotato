Item(by='johndoe0815', descendants=None, kids=[25779312, 25773360], score=None, time=1610608541, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25772792, text='&quot;Accessing the internet&quot; is a fairly broad term. In general, all machines supporting IPv4 and some sort of interface (UART for SLIP&#x2F;PPP or Ethernet) can access the internet. However, older machines will not be able to use more modern services like ssh or the web due to compute power or memory limitations and you will obviously not be able to connect to IPv6-only hosts.<p>So, for example a PDP11 running 2.9BSD (e.g. a 11&#x2F;70 from 1975) should suffice, but there are other machines of similar vintage that support IPv4. You can also try putting a virtual PDP11 on the net using the simh emulator (<a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;simh.trailing-edge.com" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;simh.trailing-edge.com</a>) and 2.9BSD from TUHS (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.tuhs.org&#x2F;Archive&#x2F;Distributions&#x2F;UCB&#x2F;2.9BSD&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.tuhs.org&#x2F;Archive&#x2F;Distributions&#x2F;UCB&#x2F;2.9BSD&#x2F;</a>).')
