Item(by='StillBored', descendants=None, kids=[24793244], score=None, time=1602789165, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24792298, text='Lack of &#x27;break&#x27; is a very useful bit of syntax I use somewhat frequently for categorization. AKA you have a handful of case values that fall into a smaller subset of catagories. Rather than an unreadable set of if&#x2F;else&#x27;s that all have multiple if \n&quot;a||b||c||d..&quot; conditions, vs a big state machine, or match structure.<p>Then if one enforces a clearer formatting style than is common on most open source projects the lack of &quot;break&quot; statements stick out visually and it becomes much harder to miss a break statement when the intentionally missing ones have lines like<p>&quot;&#x2F;&#x2F; Intentional fall-through&quot;<p>Or you use a &quot;nobreak&quot; macro and enforce the use of break&#x2F;nobreak in the automated style&#x2F;linter.')
