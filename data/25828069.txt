Item(by='bluescrn', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611012954, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25827655, text='In the old days of the Internet, you carefully selected the newsgroups and websites you wanted to read. This worked pretty well.<p>There were places where heated arguments about politics and religion took place, but it wasn&#x27;t forced into your face as soon as you logged on. You had to choose to look for it. But if you just wanted to read about coding or sci-fi, discussions generally stayed on-topic.<p>We weren&#x27;t in this crazy world of &#x27;everyone&#x27;s an activist&#x27;. We were in a world of &#x27;Don&#x27;t feed the trolls&#x27;, and that was wise advice. Now you&#x27;re essentially encouraged to bait the trolls to score &#x27;likes&#x27; and &#x27;karma points&#x27;, bonus points for getting an opponent banned.<p>We can&#x27;t uninvent social media, but maybe we can learn some lessons from the rather-less-destructive Internet of the past?')
