Money is the general equivalent in modern societies, so its hard to believe you really can&#x27;t see why it&#x27;s so special. No philosopher has argued for equality of looks, or even equality of resources. This seems like a straw man you&#x27;ve set up just to attack the idea of taxation.<p>&gt;I work with my body using my brain. It&#x27;s my body, my choice. Just like no one deserves to be raped, you cannot force people to give away gains of their work.<p>This argument makes several philosophical assumptions that may not be justified. See the Stanford Encyclopaedia of Philosophy on this: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;plato.stanford.edu&#x2F;entries&#x2F;justice-distributive&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;plato.stanford.edu&#x2F;entries&#x2F;justice-distributive&#x2F;</a><p>The same moral reasoning that dictates the state should not interfere in matters of speech extends to other spheres of life to give other outcomes e.g. prosperity, rather than only freedom.')
