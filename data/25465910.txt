Item(by='berkes', descendants=None, kids=[25470887], score=None, time=1608289121, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25464871, text='Layout for GUIs are hard.<p>And HTML nor CSS weren&#x27;t developed with layout in mind. The original idea was to <i>mark up</i> (the M in HTML) meaning and semantics and leave the rendering and display to the client entirely.<p>If you consider that systems that were designed entirely around layout (Like QT layout  <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;doc.qt.io&#x2F;qt-5&#x2F;layout.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;doc.qt.io&#x2F;qt-5&#x2F;layout.html</a>), are complex and hard, you can imagine what happens when you try to do layout with a system that was never intended to handle this, must be backwards compatible, is a standard (not a lib or framework) and can be freely interpreted by renderers by definition.')
