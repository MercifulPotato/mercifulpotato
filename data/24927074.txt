Item(by='jml7c5', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603944280, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24926585, text='I don&#x27;t think it&#x27;s quite that simple. There are all sorts of small things people can miss during document forging: leaking of metadata (not stripping a PDF), leaking of IPs, bad forging (a notable example is the &quot;Killian documents&quot;, purportedly from 1973 but which were probably made with Microsoft Word[0]).<p>Distribution&#x2F;guiding matters too, especially on the internet: what&#x27;s the best way to place it so it will spread? What techniques will be caught? Should you run a botnet? How difficult is it to evade detection? Light touch or heavy handed? Do you need a base of fake accounts years in advance, or can you create them at the time?<p>Red team&#x2F;blue team exercises are valuable for defense. If you &quot;practice&quot; only by examining real-world propaganda, you can&#x27;t debrief the opposition at the end. And if you can&#x27;t debrief the attacker, you can&#x27;t find out what you missed and what would have been effective.<p>[0]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Killian_documents_controversy" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Killian_documents_controversy</a>')
