Item(by='PaulDavisThe1st', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602732080, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24784517, text='Ironically, at least half of the things that make Haiku awesome are buried deep inside its kernel rather than being anything directly to do with the desktop.<p>Also, the contrast between Haiku and Linux in my mind just points to the more fundamental difference between a design that says &quot;you <i>will</i> do things this way, because this is better&quot; (Haiku) and one that says &quot;we&#x27;ll give you a dozen ways to approach every problem because we&#x27;re not into dictating optimum solutions, which may change over time anyway&quot; (Linux). It&#x27;s not that Linux can&#x27;t replicate almost everything that Haiku gets right, it&#x27;s more that it provides everything necessary to do 12 related things, on the same desktop.')
