Ford at the time was actually a subsidiary of Standard Oil (Henry Ford and Rockefeller were also personal friends) which resulted in the gasoline powered Ford Model-T, the most successful mass produced car of the era. Smaller car manufactures, seeing the success of Ford and possibly to not be targeted by Standard Oil themselves, fell in line and abandoned electric vehicles and alcohol based fuels. Then Standard Oil formed an alliance with General Motors and Firestone.
