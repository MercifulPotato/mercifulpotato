Item(by='mopsi', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602773126, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24783610, text='&gt; <i>The power to imprison and seize property is the core free speech issue.</i><p>There are more ways to control participation in public discourse than imprisonment and seizures of printing presses. Times have moved on. Instead of imprisonment, we have banning and filtering, with no independent oversight or appeal process. Twitter, Facebook, and a few other companies can do more to restrict the spread of certain information than most governments in the world have ever been able, so why shouldn&#x27;t at least the same standard apply to them?<p>Their virtual town squares are nowadays larger than any square ever under government control, and they have absolute control over who can and cannot participate. They are the absolute monarchies of 21st century, more powerful than ever.')
