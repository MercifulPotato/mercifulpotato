Item(by='pdonis', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607808652, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25401457, text='<i>&gt; brightness is not continuous, at a certain point you only have one photon left</i><p>If you are going to use a &quot;photon&quot; interpretation, you are using quantum mechanics, and in QM &quot;brightness&quot; involves the <i>probability</i> of detecting a photon and does not require there to be an exact integral &quot;number&quot; of photons. So brightness <i>is</i> still continuous in QM; the probability of detecting a photon can keep getting smaller and smaller indefinitely, without ever having to discontinously jump to zero.')
