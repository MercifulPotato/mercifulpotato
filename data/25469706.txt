Item(by='bsanr2', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608313487, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25468234, text='It&#x27;s been well known for a while among those who are honest, with themselves and with the evidence, that degree requirements are mostly a cultural gatekeeping exercise for entry-level work. That isn&#x27;t to say that training and expertise aren&#x27;t critical, particularly the more responsibility a worker takes on; it&#x27;s more that one rarely requires 4 years of preparation for a placement that involves work a supervisor that&#x27;s known you for a month, at best, will trust you with. And the old axiom holds: &quot;The best way to learn how to do something is to do it.&quot;<p>A degree <i>does</i> show that you&#x27;re committed and willing to jump through hoops (even if only because you don&#x27;t know any better).')
