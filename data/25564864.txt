Item(by='dwcnnnghm', descendants=None, kids=[25567316], score=None, time=1609199848, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25564478, text='Plan 9 troff might work! It works with utf8 out of the box[0], and while I haven&#x27;t used it for complex math typesetting, there is a command (eqn [1]) that was developed for it. I&#x27;d recommend Ali Rudi&#x27;s port (neatroff [2][3]) for a minimalist implementation. There&#x27;s also Heirloom Documentation Tools [4] which is an implementation of *roff-and-friends that uses Knuth&#x27;s paragraph-at-once algorithm (instead of the original line-wise one) for typesetting, plus some other interesting features.<p>The authors of eqn wrote a paper about it: &quot;Typesetting Mathematics&quot; by Brian Kernighan and Lorinda Cherry. Kernighan also wrote two manuals (one in 1976 with a revision in 1992, and one in 2007 with updates for the Plan 9 version). [5].<p>[0] utf8 was developed by Ken Thompson and Rob Pike during the creation of Plan9. The entire OS is compatible. Story here: <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;doc.cat-v.org&#x2F;bell_labs&#x2F;utf-8_history" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;doc.cat-v.org&#x2F;bell_labs&#x2F;utf-8_history</a><p>[1] <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;man.cat-v.org&#x2F;plan_9&#x2F;1&#x2F;eqn" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;man.cat-v.org&#x2F;plan_9&#x2F;1&#x2F;eqn</a><p>[2] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;aligrudi&#x2F;neatroff" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;aligrudi&#x2F;neatroff</a><p>[3] PDF manual for neatroff: <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;litcave.rudi.ir&#x2F;neatroff.pdf" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;litcave.rudi.ir&#x2F;neatroff.pdf</a><p>[4] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;n-t-roff.github.io&#x2F;heirloom&#x2F;doctools.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;n-t-roff.github.io&#x2F;heirloom&#x2F;doctools.html</a><p>[5] These (and more) can be found here: <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.kohala.com&#x2F;start&#x2F;troff&#x2F;troff.html" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.kohala.com&#x2F;start&#x2F;troff&#x2F;troff.html</a>')
