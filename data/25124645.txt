Item(by='ProfHewitt', descendants=None, kids=[25125693, 25141594, 25141088, 25128257], score=None, time=1605626162, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25116881, text='Dear xyzzy,<p>Unfortunately, you missed that the author&#x27;s proof does <i>not</i>  actually prove inferential undecidability (sometimes called &quot;inferential incompleteness&quot;) of Russell&#x27;s Principia Mathematica for the following reason:<p><pre><code>     The [Gödel 1931] proposition *I&#x27;mUnprovable* does **not** exist in Principia Mathematica because it violates restrictions on orders of propositions that are necessary to avoid paradoxes (such as Russell&#x27;s Paradox). Gödel numbers (and in the author&#x27;s case Lisp expressions) leave out the order of a proposition with the consequence that the Diagonal Lemma **cannot** be used to construct the proposition *I&#x27;mUnprovable*.</code></pre>\nFurthermore, existence of the proposition <i>I&#x27;mUnprovable</i> contradicts the following fundamental theorem of provability that goes all the way back to Euclid:<p><pre><code>     A theorem can be used in other proofs.\n</code></pre>\nSee the following article for further details:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;papers.ssrn.com&#x2F;abstract=3603021" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;papers.ssrn.com&#x2F;abstract=3603021</a>')
