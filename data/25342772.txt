Item(by='gamblor956', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607412132, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25333108, text='Also, a just-published database of gaming performance on the M1s confirms that the M1 is not the revolutionary chip that Apple claims it to be once you run it in uncontrolled (aka real-world) use cases.<p>The M1 struggles to run 4-year old games that modern AMD and Intel chips breeze through. While the M1 appears to have good burst capabilities, its ability to handle extended processing loads for anything that isn&#x27;t hardcoded into the chip (aka, anything other than video processing) is woefully deficient. Whether that&#x27;s a function of the CPU design, or poor cooling, or both, is not yet clear.')
