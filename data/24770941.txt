Item(by='cs702', descendants=None, kids=[24771663], score=None, time=1602627322, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24770607, text='Well... not really:<p>* The Fed buys treasury notes and bonds from third parties, because it cannot buy them directly from the US Treasury at issuance.<p>* The Fed can earn and book profits from treasury securities <i>only to the extent the US Treasury continues to make interest payments and repay principal</i>.<p>* The US Treasury can pay continue to pay interest and repay principal only with money that is (a) borrowed from third parties by issuing new treasury securities, or (b) collected via taxation.<p>Things are this way <i>by design</i>, for many reasons, including the explicit goal of limiting short-term political influence over monetary policy.')
