Item(by='vkou', descendants=None, kids=[25188254], score=None, time=1606121218, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25184141, text='&gt; There are steel mills down the road from my manufacturing business that were shuttered for decades and reopened when the tariffs came into effect.<p>This is a beautiful, heart-warming story that is not really supported by the data.<p>Actually, steel employment in the US declined after the tarrifs were introduced [1].<p>Net steel production in the US went sideways over the past decade. It rose around the time of the tariffs, but it also fell and rose at other times [2].<p>The tariffs did, however, cause the cost of US steel to skyrocket [3]. Which is not fantastic for the ~330,910,000 Americans who aren&#x27;t steel workers (who didn&#x27;t actually win anything, since employment declined), or owners of steel plants (who I assume did win something, since they reaped the lion&#x27;s share of the benefit of higher margins).<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.ibisworld.com&#x2F;industry-statistics&#x2F;employment&#x2F;iron-steel-manufacturing-united-states&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.ibisworld.com&#x2F;industry-statistics&#x2F;employment&#x2F;iro...</a><p>[2] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;tradingeconomics.com&#x2F;united-states&#x2F;steel-production" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;tradingeconomics.com&#x2F;united-states&#x2F;steel-production</a><p>[3] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;tradingeconomics.com&#x2F;commodity&#x2F;steel" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;tradingeconomics.com&#x2F;commodity&#x2F;steel</a>')
