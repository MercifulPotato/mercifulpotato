Item(by='EForEndeavour', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611083205, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25837022, text='All else being equal, you&#x27;re probably better off taking public health advice from a public health employee over a software developer.<p>However, if the software developer raises an admittedly good point based on specific, verifiable information (in this case sourced from covid19-projections.com, which sources covidtracking.com, which in turn aggregates data from state public health authorities) which questions the pre-COVID practice of observing a vaccine recipient for 15 minutes, their occupation should not make their point any less valid. This wouldn&#x27;t be the first time that a well-established best practice turned out to be exactly the wrong thing to do given new circumstances.<p>The process of evaluating an argument should balance the informative value of the argument-maker&#x27;s credentials without veering too far into gatekeeping or argument from authority (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Argument_from_authority" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Argument_from_authority</a>).')
