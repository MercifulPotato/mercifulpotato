A look at the release schedule[0] suggests that major versions are supported for 2 years. In contrast, major releases of CentOS historically were supported for a stunning 10 years (until yesterdays&#x27; announcement, which moved the support window for CentOS 8 from 2029 to 2021). I&#x27;ve worked with companies that had been running legacy CentOS 6 boxes for over half a decade, and from my perspective the longevity was incredibly valuable for that kind of business.<p>[0] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;wiki.alpinelinux.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Alpine_Linux:Releases" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;wiki.alpinelinux.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Alpine_Linux:Releases</a>')
