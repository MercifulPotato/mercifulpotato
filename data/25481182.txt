Item(by='Smaug123', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608411837, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25479777, text='Re &quot;there is no evidence&quot;: this is due to a mismatch between the medical profession&#x27;s use of the words &quot;no evidence&quot; and everyone else&#x27;s.<p>To a standard human, &quot;no evidence&quot; means &quot;there is no evidence&quot;.<p>To a medical professional, &quot;no evidence&quot; means &quot;there is insufficient evidence for us to be certain&quot;.<p>The clash of terminology is very unfortunate, and it prevents one from discussing <i>probabilities</i> at all. A statement from the WHO is either that something is &quot;certain&quot; or that there is &quot;insufficient evidence at this time&quot;, even if there is sufficient evidence for one to conclude something with 80% probability.')
