Item(by='joe_the_user', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611522447, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25895168, text='The problem with Twitter&#x2F;&quot;microblogging&quot; isn&#x27;t just post length.<p>The problem with Twitter&#x2F;&quot;microblogging&quot; is also lack of hierarchical structure. A BBS has a series of subboards, those subboard have posts and the posts have comments. Sure, things can degenerate to just a series of &quot;mega-threads&quot; but if it&#x27;s done, everyone the topic and the subtopic and so you can good contextual discussion. Facebook has a three level structure; post, comment and reply. Some of the oldest boards had full tree-structure as does HN, here. Multilevel comment and reply can be confusing but can be really useful to drill down to the detail of discussion wants.')
