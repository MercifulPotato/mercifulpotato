Item(by='KenoFischer', descendants=None, kids=[24901529], score=None, time=1603743493, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24900688, text='The subtyping system is fairly complicated, but in practice users only need to really think about situations where the LHS is a concrete type, since that&#x27;s primarily what determines runtime semantics (not to say that there aren&#x27;t situations where specificity really matters, but it&#x27;s quite rare). That simplifies the mental model considerably and our users tend to find it relatively intuitive. For a formal discussion, the group at Northeastern has been doing great work in phrasing some of the Julia internals in more traditional PL language, including a formal subtyping model. That paper is at <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;janvitek.org&#x2F;pubs&#x2F;oopsla18a.pdf" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;janvitek.org&#x2F;pubs&#x2F;oopsla18a.pdf</a>')
