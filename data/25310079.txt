Because value and market price are separate things in marginalism.<p>Using the rational model somebody will only purchase something when it&#x27;s price is below how much the person values the object.<p>It&#x27;s related to consumer surplus. To make it measurable we use money as an approximate judge to work out much somebody values it. But the market price and how much somebody values something are different things in marginalism at the core.')
