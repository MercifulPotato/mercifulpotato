I can&#x27;t find it now, but long ago I came across a description of a British captain and his crew landed on an island, and was confronted with the natives.  The natives on had rocks, which they threw at them.  They had the usual muskets and swords.<p>He described the rocks as huge, accurately thrown, and a never ending deluge.  While you were ducking one, another hit you from another direction.  He reported they broke arms and skulls.  Had he been able to put more distance between himself and the rock throwers, so a musket could reach but a rock couldn&#x27;t the outcome might have been different I guess, but they had just landed on a beach.<p>Rocks were the first distance effect weapons.  The answer to &quot;why doesn&#x27;t anybody do it any more&quot; is simply we have even better distance effect weapons now.
