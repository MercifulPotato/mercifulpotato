Item(by='stubish', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610423171, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25733085, text='&gt; I&#x27;m struggling to try to understand what this means for the risks of running a business in the cloud going forward.<p>This is nothing new, and the risks of running a business are the same. If your suppliers drop your business, you have to find a new supplier or you are dead. And you get to decide if you want to chase compensation in the courts (breach of contract, persecution based on protected classes such as politics or race) or not.<p>The suppliers who are not allowed to drop you are regulated and called utilities. AWS dropping the ban hammer here is interesting, as the end result might end up with cloud services getting regulated and defined as a utility.')
