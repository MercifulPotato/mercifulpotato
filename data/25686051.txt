Item(by='potench', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610122052, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25684114, text='I didn’t see it mentioned but a common use case for 3rd party cookies is correlating session data across your own multiple domains (if your company owns multiple domains). Publishing companies typically own multiple domains&#x2F;verticals and can increase ad revenue &#x2F; seo &#x2F; traffic quality by linking properties together. It’s important to be niche as a site (verticalization) but also broad as a publishing operation (own many verticals and shift your marketing spend daily).<p>Anyways, google has been writing a spec for “first party sets” to help replace the use of a 3rd party cookie to connect domains you own. <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;privacycg&#x2F;first-party-sets" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;privacycg&#x2F;first-party-sets</a><p>I believe this will need to be implemented before Chrome moves aggressively against 3rd party cookies.')
