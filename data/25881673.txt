Item(by='exDM69', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611403805, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25881575, text='The lunar orbit is inclined so that the moon moves between about +&#x2F;- 28 degrees north&#x2F;south latitude with reference to the Earth equatorial plane.<p>That&#x27;s Earth&#x27;s axial tilt of ~23 deg + the inclination of the lunar orbit w.r.t. the ecliptic of ~5 deg.<p>This means that when the moon is at its northest (above southern Florida), it has a pretty good view over the north pole (but it&#x27;s at quite an oblique angle of ~60 deg), and the south pole is not visible. About 14 days later it&#x27;s the opposite.<p>But yeah, I agree with the conclusion that the Moon is not a good place for a Earth observation for a lot of reasons.')
