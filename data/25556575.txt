Item(by='0xbadcafebee', descendants=None, kids=[25556914], score=None, time=1609128274, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25553095, text='&gt; Root cause analysis is harmful because it artificially limits your investigation to a predetermined &quot;single&quot; error or fault.<p>The Five Whys specifically does not stop at a single root cause or fault, that&#x27;s why it&#x27;s the <i>five</i> why&#x27;s and not the <i>one</i>.<p>Moreover, robustness cannot emerge from a single event no matter how much you analyze it. It comes from continuous improvement processes, quality assurance, war games, service level analysis, etc. It&#x27;s a multi-headed hydra of efforts to improve a system. One of those things is RCA of service-impacting events, or manufacturing defects, or customer complaints.<p>This is like those &quot;considered harmful&quot; blog posts so fashionable a few years ago. So it turns out that if you blindly and inaccurately follow a generic process, it might not work out great. Who knew? It&#x27;s almost like there might be subtlety involved.')
