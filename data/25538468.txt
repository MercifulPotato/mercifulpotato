Item(by='LorenPechtel', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608924632, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25536672, text='Repair mechanisms cost energy even when they&#x27;re not being used.  Immortality requires much better repair mechanisms, that makes the owner less competitive.  Thus the immortal loses the evolutionary race against the mortal.<p>In practice immortality in a complex organism requires many steps, those will never be done because they up the energy budget and put the creature at a disadvantage.<p>The fact that it&#x27;s an evolutionary disadvantage not to be replaced with superior offspring is irrelevant, that implies intent which evolution does not have.')
