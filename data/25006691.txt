Item(by='srg0', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604660230, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25004118, text='One of the simplest and most common approximations is to assume a Poisson process for arrivals, which corresponds to exponential distribution of the inter-arrival times.<p>It&#x27;s tempting to model work time with an exponential distribution too, and there&#x27;s a lot of theory for this case. Unfortunately, exponential distribution has a mode = 0, so it&#x27;s not a good choice for real life process times. It&#x27;s more common to see distributions like lognormal, gamma, Weibull, loglogistic, beta, or others with a mode &gt; 0.')
