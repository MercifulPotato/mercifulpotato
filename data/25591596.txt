Item(by='xondono', descendants=None, kids=[25592157], score=None, time=1609412507, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25591249, text='You mean to say me that there’s no such thing as “superior programming practices”.&#x2F;s<p>I’d take a heavily used and tested system over one programmed “with superior practices”, like obsoleting rarely used code, any day of the week.<p>Yes, heartbleed was bad, but those type of bugs are <i>rare</i>, and once made public patches come fast. You don’t make your system more secure by exchanging the rare occasional general bug with obscure and unknown bugs (with unknown reach).<p>A lot of people thinking in security tend to fall for the availability bias, they want to protect themselves from the bugs that make news, while ignoring the ones that are removed day by day in a changelog of some minor utility they are using.')
