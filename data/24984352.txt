Item(by='nobody9999', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604439885, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24982552, text='&gt;at the same time I don&#x27;t want a single government ISP controlling everything controlled by bureaucracy.<p>Firstly, <i>most</i> municipal broadband networks, either built or proposed, absolutely <i>do not</i> include ISP services.<p>Rather, they provide last-mile&#x2F;Fiber-to-the-premises infrastructure that comes back to an interconnection point where <i>multiple ISPs</i> can provide Internet access services to customers.<p>This actually <i>increases</i> competition, because ISPs don&#x27;t need to pull cable to <i>every single customer</i> in order to provide service.  They just need the appropriate back-haul capacity to service those customers who sign on with them.<p>This allows for more competition on price, features and customer service, not a &quot;single government ISP.&quot;')
