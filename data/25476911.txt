Item(by='the_mitsuhiko', descendants=None, kids=[25476933], score=None, time=1608379499, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25476855, text='There is a big difference between running &quot;third party native code&quot; that you have some level of trust of (eg: image magick) vs having customers upload code to your infrastructure.<p>In the latter case I&#x27;m not aware of a single service that uses seccomp to do this.  The closest to a non WASM setup is either things like Firecracker or to enforce some sense of sandboxed language like JavaScript.  Seccomp quite often plays a role in keeping those sandboxes safe, but it&#x27;s generally not used to have people execute arbitrary binary blobs on SaaS.<p>Having people compile code to WASM to execute it in a sandbox is becoming quite widespread.  Shopify is not the first company to do that.')
