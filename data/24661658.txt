Item(by='mkl', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1601639539, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24660286, text='It is not dangerous, and does not perform the same operation (it is actually incorrect and incomplete: &quot;:&quot; and &quot;#&quot; swapped, and missing substitution words).  I guess it was downvoted because it didn&#x27;t explain?  The bash manual does, though, under &quot;HISTORY EXPANSION&quot;.  Try this:<p>Type a command, but don&#x27;t press enter, e.g.<p><pre><code>  echo &#x27;hello&#x27;;\n</code></pre>\nType<p><pre><code>  !#:s&#x2F;hello&#x2F;world&#x2F;\n</code></pre>\nPress enter.  !# means the whole current command so far, and the s&#x2F;a&#x2F;b&#x2F; modifier replaces the first instance of &quot;a&quot; with &quot;b&quot;.<p>Press up to get the command that ran:<p><pre><code>  echo &#x27;hello&#x27;; echo &#x27;world&#x27;;\n</code></pre>\nTo see such history expansion things before they are run, press M-^ (probably Alt-Shift-6).')
