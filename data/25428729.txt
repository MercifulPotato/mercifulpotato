Item(by='harryf', descendants=None, kids=[25428768, 25430475, 25428779, 25429127], score=None, time=1608026714, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25428649, text='You think GDPR didn&#x27;t accomplish anything? Companies around the world had to implement it to be able to do business with the EU, as one of the largest trading blocks by GDP.<p>That means anyone storing any kind of consumer data had to consider a) how to report on what they were storing if asked and b) how to delete data if asked. Banks and others spent massive amounts of cash on implementing GDPR. It was a major breakthrough in getting corporations to take privacy seriously.<p>Has it solved surveillance capitalism? No. And for most of us dumb consumers we&#x27;re just &quot;ugggh.... annoying modals about privacy on every website&quot;.<p>But it&#x27;s the first step towards having some kind of protection for consumers and it shows, despite lobbyists or otherwise, governments can have a real impact. And now the EU is building on that success.')
