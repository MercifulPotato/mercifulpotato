Item(by='coddle-hark', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607795482, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25398563, text='I think it’s a general truth that all software projects tend to end up at the maximum possible complexity. Projects that aren’t inherently complex tend to achieve this by complicated tooling, deploys, and a slew of “special cases” that inevitably slow things down. It’s a special case of Parkinson’s law (work expands so as to fill the time available for its completion); all these developers need to spend their time on <i>something</i> after all, no-one’s going to code them self out of a job.')
