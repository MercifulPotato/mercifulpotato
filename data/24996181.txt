Item(by='xapata', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604559235, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24996156, text='&quot;When you&#x27;re rich, they think you really know.&quot; -- Fiddler on the Roof<p>People have a hard time understanding randomness, so of course they can&#x27;t distinguish luck from skill.  If you ask the average person to put dots on a page randomly, they&#x27;ll generally create some kind of pattern in order to cover the page thoroughly.  If you show someone a random distribution of dots, they&#x27;ll be convinced there&#x27;s a pattern.  Faces in clouds.<p>I used to play poker semi-professionally.  I wondered why the same people came, day after day, to give me money.  Sure, sometimes they&#x27;d win.  It&#x27;s the randomness of it that confused them.  They&#x27;d see skill where there was luck, and luck where there was skill.')
