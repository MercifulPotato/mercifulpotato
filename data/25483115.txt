The first step is to persuade people that work, like aging, is bad [0], and that abolishing work is a comparatively neglected social cause relative to the harms caused.<p>We might have to live with work for a long time now, because scarcity abounds; but there&#x27;s no point pretending that&#x27;s good, or lionizing work for its own sake. It sucks to work at &quot;a job that slowly kills you&quot; [1], just like it sucks that we get old and die.<p>[0] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.nickbostrom.com&#x2F;fable&#x2F;dragon.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.nickbostrom.com&#x2F;fable&#x2F;dragon.html</a> (discussed previously here: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=24770566" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=24770566</a>)<p>[1] Radiohead, No Surprises: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=u5CVsCnxyXg" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=u5CVsCnxyXg</a>')
