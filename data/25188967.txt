Item(by='oftenwrong', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606151530, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25187809, text='It&#x27;s not a matter of design. We can look downwards and work in many positions, yes. However, is it healthy to work this way on a regular basis? I doubt it, but I am open to seeing evidence. I know that for me, it is not a good position; it will cause me back, neck and shoulder pain, and headaches if I work that way.<p>Anecdotally, I know many avid readers that have developed forward head posture and various pains from their habit.<p>I&#x27;m not sure people have been reading books flat on a desk or in their lap for thousands of years. After all, literacy was not widespread until relatively recent times. Furthermore, many scholar of the past would read and write on an erect, angled surface when possible: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.khanacademy.org&#x2F;humanities&#x2F;medieval-world&#x2F;medieval-book&#x2F;using-medieval-book&#x2F;a&#x2F;the-medieval-desktop" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.khanacademy.org&#x2F;humanities&#x2F;medieval-world&#x2F;mediev...</a> . Personally, I prefer to do the same when reading for any extended period of time.')
