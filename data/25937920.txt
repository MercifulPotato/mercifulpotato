Item(by='Scoundreller', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611805820, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25937730, text='It&#x27;s sort of the open market.<p>The history is that Canada used to have &quot;compulsory licensing&quot; for patented drugs. Anyone could manufacture a patented drug in Canada and pay a ?6?% or whatever royalty to the patent owner and they just had to deal with it.<p>Over the years, first 4, then 10 years of exclusivity were provided to the patent owner.<p>Then the WTO came along, and presently, everyone has agreed to ~20y exclusivity and charge&#x2F;pay the median price of OECD countries before it&#x27;s a royalty-free free-for-all.<p><a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;publications.gc.ca&#x2F;Collection-R&#x2F;LoPBdP&#x2F;BP&#x2F;prb9946-e.htm" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;publications.gc.ca&#x2F;Collection-R&#x2F;LoPBdP&#x2F;BP&#x2F;prb9946-e.h...</a><p>Canadian generic drugs today aren&#x27;t usually cheaper than US generics, except where patent exclusivity mismatches.')
