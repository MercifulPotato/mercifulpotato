Item(by='olliej', descendants=None, kids=[24812332], score=None, time=1602961068, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24812138, text='Correct, the overhead is minimal - it basically just makes the float-&gt;int conversion use a fixed set of rounding and clamping modes, irrespective of what the current mode flags are set to.<p>The problem is JS&#x27;s double-&gt;int conversion was effectively defined as &quot;what wintel does by default&quot;, so on arm, ppc, etc you need a follow on branch that checks for the clamping requirements and corrects the result value to what x86 does.<p>Honestly it would not surprise me if the perf gains are due to removing the branch rather than the instruction itself.')
