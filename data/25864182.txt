Item(by='dragonwriter', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611262873, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25863940, text='&gt; Such executive agreements are not legally binding on the U.S.<p>They are binding <i>on</i> the US (they are binding under international law), they just aren’t always binding <i>within</i> the US on their own.<p>A Congressional-Executive Agreement is binding under the terms of the relevant statutory authority, and typically also is (and authorized by Congress as) the basis for binding regulation by the executive.<p>A sole executive agreement isn’t binding under domestic law on its own, but typically is implemented by being used as a policy guideline for executive action under other authority, or as a basis for the executive seeking supporting legislation (the latter of which may transform it, after the fact, into a Congressional-executive agrement.)')
