So I would argue that you <i>do</i> see more innovation from poor families than rich families. After all, rich families have no need to innovate, they just throw money at problems until they go away. Poor people are financially constrained and have to think outside the box to solve their problems. But you also see more companies started by rich people. Not necessarily <i>innovative</i> companies, mind you, just companies.')
