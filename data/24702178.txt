Item(by='gen220', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602017239, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24698882, text='This is a great take.<p>&gt; What is the end state if we let this continue?<p>One of the pillars of capitalism is the idea of &quot;creative destruction&quot; (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Whale_fall" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Whale_fall</a>). That is, when a moderately-sized company falls apart, it&#x27;s actually a long-term positive for the economy as a whole. It&#x27;s decomposed, sold off, and its former pieces are repurposed for the good of all.<p>One purpose of antitrust law is to keep the cycle of whale falls going. If antitrust law is unenforced, the whales never fall, which has a negative impact on the fragility of our economy: if and when these megacorps become anemic, they threaten to take down the whole economy&#x2F;whale-decomposition process with them.')
