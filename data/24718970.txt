Item(by='jlokier', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602164210, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24714600, text='There&#x27;s a neural theory of tinnitus, that says you hear it because your brain is compensating for lack of signal at some frequencies.<p>Like the amplifiers are turned up to max at some frequencies, and the neural net maybe even adds an interpolated perception.<p>If that theory has anything to it, it makes sense that it might be possible to train your brain to reduce the effect.<p>That&#x27;s not just &quot;accepting&quot;, but it might be difficult to perceive a difference if &quot;accepting&quot; results in &quot;not noticing as much&quot;, and &quot;not noticing as much&quot; is indistinguishable from &quot;turning down my brain&#x27;s perception gain for these signals&quot;.')
