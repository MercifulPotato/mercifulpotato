Replay the last five years twice, with different circumstances: Once without the shelf stocker, and once without the programmer. In one case, the increased productivity still appears, in the other, it does not.<p>It seems to me that the person who is required for the addition of the increased productivity <i>caused</i> the increased productivity, while the person who is not required for it did not.')
