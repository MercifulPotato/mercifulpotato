Item(by='SpicyLemonZest', descendants=None, kids=[24733876], score=None, time=1602270537, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24733099, text='The fundamental problem is, if you need a factory with 100 workers in order to effectively manufacture anything, then the ability to innovate in manufacturing is limited to groups who have sufficient capital to direct the efforts of 100 people.<p>There are of course different ways to organize that capital. Capital can be owned by rich people, by corporate firms, by the government, by worker collectives. But it&#x27;s still a finite resource. As a matter of mathematical inequality, there&#x27;s no way to give <i>everyone</i> the power to direct a large factory.')
