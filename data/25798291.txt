They are both designed for the same general profile that Lua has—a scripting language, safe to use, embeddable, with a small VM (low code size).<p>- <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;marcobambini&#x2F;gravity" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;marcobambini&#x2F;gravity</a><p>- <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;wren.io&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;wren.io&#x2F;</a><p>The language design choices are nice and familiar to people who are used to other existing languages. Lua is a bit radical.<p>Two other options are AngelScript and Squirrel, which are both a bit older and more mature than Gravity and Wren.<p>- <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.angelcode.com&#x2F;angelscript&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.angelcode.com&#x2F;angelscript&#x2F;</a><p>- <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;squirrel-lang.org&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;squirrel-lang.org&#x2F;</a><p>Finally, it’s much more feasible these days to embed something like Mono, and Guile has gotten a lot better.')
