Item(by='corty', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610907871, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25812258, text='In GCP you can configure budgets for projects (groups of resources). Budgets can issue alerts when reaching certain percentages and reconfigure or shutdown resources when exceeded.<p>The budget howto docs have an example to stop everything that incurs a cost on budget overrun: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cloud.google.com&#x2F;billing&#x2F;docs&#x2F;how-to&#x2F;budgets" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cloud.google.com&#x2F;billing&#x2F;docs&#x2F;how-to&#x2F;budgets</a><p>But as the warning says, that might delete data in storage or other resources you may want to keep paying for. For that case, you can execute a program, that shuts down everything you can get rid of, but the cost for storage and everything you forgot will continue to be billed.')
