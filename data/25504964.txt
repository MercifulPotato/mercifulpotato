Item(by='bullen', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608634112, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25498079, text='I think most microservice implementations get it wrong. You&#x27;re not supposed to run one service per machine or even process (that leads to orchestration hell and fragmentation cost at the loss of redundancy): The way you get all benefits of a microservice architecture is if you run all services on all machines: by VM sandboxing them all on many distributed machines f.ex. like this does: <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;host.rupy.se" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;host.rupy.se</a><p>This requires your entire solution to be async.-to-async. end-to-end though. But performance, scalability and strength is unmatched: it becomes anti-fragile!')
