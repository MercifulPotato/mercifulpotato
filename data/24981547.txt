Item(by='dasil003', descendants=None, kids=[24984236], score=None, time=1604423460, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24980964, text='The discussion is around financial and market dynamics for different types of companies.  Quibbling over the definition of zero vs near-zero is completely side-stepping the argument.<p>The point is, for Google search variable costs are immaterial.  This is not the case for any business that needs deals in physical inventory or human-provided services (which was the majority of businesses pre-Internet).<p>Does Google incur some marginal infra costs from increased traffic or growth of the web?  Yes.  If you project out infinite growth does it become a &quot;major cost&quot;?  No, the limit of profit over variable cost approaches zero.')
