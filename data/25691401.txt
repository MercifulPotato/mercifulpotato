Item(by='mkeeter', descendants=None, kids=[25692495, 25697633, 25691567, 25691610], score=None, time=1610145204, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25690189, text='Isosurface extraction is a <i>fascinating</i> rabbit hole to go down (speaking as someone that&#x27;s deep underground).<p>There&#x27;s a long list of properties that you want your algorithm to have:<p>- Meshes should be watertight<p>- Meshes should be manifold<p>- There should be no self-intersections<p>- Sharp features (edges and corners) should be reproduced accurately, rather than blurred or bevelled<p>- Thin features should be preserved (which makes it tricky to sample on a regular grid!)<p>- The mesh should be adaptive, i.e. having fewer triangles in flat areas<p>It&#x27;s relatively easy to get ~3 of these properties, and (last time I checked) nigh impossible to get 5 or 6 in the general case.<p>If you&#x27;d like to read more, Doug Moen has a comprehensive overview of the literature:\n<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;curv3d&#x2F;curv&#x2F;blob&#x2F;master&#x2F;ideas&#x2F;v-rep&#x2F;To_Mesh.rst" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;curv3d&#x2F;curv&#x2F;blob&#x2F;master&#x2F;ideas&#x2F;v-rep&#x2F;To_Me...</a><p>I&#x27;ve also written up a 2D study of Marching Cubes (Squares) vs Dual Contouring:\n<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.mattkeeter.com&#x2F;projects&#x2F;contours&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.mattkeeter.com&#x2F;projects&#x2F;contours&#x2F;</a><p>And a deep dive into the math that lets you precisely position vertexes in dual contouring:\n<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.mattkeeter.com&#x2F;projects&#x2F;qef" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.mattkeeter.com&#x2F;projects&#x2F;qef</a>')
