Item(by='sylvain_kerkour', descendants=None, kids=[25594657, 25594449, 25594090, 25595199, 25597471, 25594060], score=None, time=1609426427, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25592325, text='After a lot of time spent investigating the different kind of (U)UIDs, I&#x27;ve come to the conclusion that ULIDs[0] are the best kind of (U)UIDs you can use for your application today:<p>* they are sortable so pagination is fast and easy.<p>* they can be represented as UUID in database (at least in Postgres).<p>* they are kind of serial, so insertion into indexes is rather good, as opposed to completely random UUIDs.<p>* 48 bits timestamp gives enough space for the next 9000 years.<p>[0] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;ulid&#x2F;spec" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;ulid&#x2F;spec</a>')
