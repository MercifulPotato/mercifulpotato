Item(by='learnstats2', descendants=None, kids=[24853777, 24854291], score=None, time=1603315142, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24851399, text='I don&#x27;t think this has to be adversary.<p>Journals choose to set a standard for publication, which is generally peer-review.<p>If journals instead set a standard which was that the work must be reproducible (in other words: there must be instruction enough for an independent team to reasonably be able to follow and repeat what you did), then that sets a higher standard for science.<p>Note that the peers in peer review are likely to mutually ignore that type of requirement - rather than face that requirement themselves.')
