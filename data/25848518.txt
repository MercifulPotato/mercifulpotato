Item(by='dodobirdlord', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611160878, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25847225, text='I once had the surprising experience of having to help a postdoc with a PhD in mechanical engineering wrap his head around a block of Python code he was reading that looked like:<p><pre><code>    x = f0()\n    x = f1(x)\n</code></pre>\nHe was just mentally blocked on how all of these things could be true at the same time, and&#x2F;or on how a variable could be used in setting its own value, since assigning a new value to x would change the value that had been passed to f1! His intuition was that this meant some kind of recursion-to-stability was at play.<p>All he needed was to be informed that this could be equivalently represented as<p><pre><code>    x = f0()\n    y = f1(x)\n</code></pre>\nor<p><pre><code>    x = f1(f0())</code></pre>')
