But what&#x27;s frustrating with Wordpress is that there are many plugins to do caching, and each caching plugin has a million options in it. How they handle images, dynamic content, cache headers, ETags, etc, are often buried deep in submenus.<p>On top of that, testing caching is challenging - replicating between local, staging, and prod is ultimately a very manual and error prone process, so there&#x27;s no real way to figure out how to test and if what you&#x27;re doing is the right thing. Since caching is not an immediate thing (it can take time for a CDN to pick up an asset, for example), it can be unclear if what you&#x27;ve done works, or if you need to wait five minutes and try again.<p>I wrote a blogpost a few months ago about this and other issues.
