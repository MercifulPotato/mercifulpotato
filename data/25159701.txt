Item(by='curt15', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605874332, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25158747, text='Other advantages:<p>-Unlike btrfs subvolumes, zfs datasets can be mounted with different zfs-specific mount options, such as compression algorithms or recordsizes.<p>-zfs can take atomic recursive snapshots of nested datasets, whereas btrfs snapshots of a subvolume do not include nested subvolumes.<p>Overall, zfs treats datasets as first-class citizens, whereas the only purpose of btrfs subvolumes seems to be to exclude folders from snapshots.')
