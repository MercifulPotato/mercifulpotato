Item(by='MrDOS', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604076131, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24941726, text='At my last employer, I built a filter program, creatively called CSVTools[0], to do something like this. One piece of the project parses CSVs and replaces the commas&#x2F;newlines (in an escaping- and multiline-aware manner, of course) with ASCII record&#x2F;unit separator characters[1] (0x1E and 0x1F); the other piece converts that format back into well-formed CSV files. I usually used this with GNU awk, and reconfigured RS[2] and FS[3] appropriately. Or you can just set the input separators (IRS&#x2F;IFS) and produce plaintext output from AWK.<p>[0]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;bitbucket.org&#x2F;rbr&#x2F;csvtools" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;bitbucket.org&#x2F;rbr&#x2F;csvtools</a><p>[1]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Delimiter#ASCII_delimited_text" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Delimiter#ASCII_delimited_text</a><p>[2]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.gnu.org&#x2F;software&#x2F;gawk&#x2F;manual&#x2F;html_node&#x2F;awk-split-records.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.gnu.org&#x2F;software&#x2F;gawk&#x2F;manual&#x2F;html_node&#x2F;awk-split...</a><p>[3]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.gnu.org&#x2F;software&#x2F;gawk&#x2F;manual&#x2F;html_node&#x2F;Field-Separators.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.gnu.org&#x2F;software&#x2F;gawk&#x2F;manual&#x2F;html_node&#x2F;Field-Sep...</a>')
