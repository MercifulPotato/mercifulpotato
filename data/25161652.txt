Item(by='jfengel', descendants=None, kids=[25161793, 25162036, 25161984, 25163582], score=None, time=1605888064, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25136406, text='For me the big win is that it feels familiar to users. It&#x27;s not a native app, but it looks like their native browser.<p>Users are acutely sensitive to apps that feel right. It&#x27;s something Java learned the hard way, or not at all: there&#x27;s an uncanny valley if you get it wrong, and users hate that. Native apps will always be better than a web app, but a web app will be better than an app that doesn&#x27;t really have a home on any platform.<p>It would have been great if users were willing to say, &quot;Oh, I know this, it&#x27;s a Swing app, just like the one I used on my Mac&#x2F;PC&#x2F;Linux&#x2F;etc.&quot; But Swing never really succeeded, and web browsers did.')
