Item(by='rswail', descendants=None, kids=[25179146], score=None, time=1606025585, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25173950, text='In safety critical environment, you&#x27;d better have done your FMECA and designed the system to be &quot;fail-safe&quot;. The problem with the 737-Max is that it wasn&#x27;t.<p>Fail-safe doesn&#x27;t mean &quot;no failures&quot; or &quot;no breakages&quot;. It means that when they happen (and they will), the system fails in a way that is safe.<p>QA &quot;gates&quot; in terms of the &quot;throw it over the wall to QA and they will test it in all possible scenarios to ensure quality&quot; is different to QA &quot;gates&quot; that are &quot;throw all of the failure modes that have been identified as dangerous and ensure that the system fails in a safe manner.&quot;<p>That can be part of a CI&#x2F;CD pipeline in exactly the same way as in the web&#x2F;cloud world, including generating all of the necessary documentation and evidence for ISO etc standards.')
