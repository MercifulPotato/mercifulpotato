Item(by='mrfredward', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602257610, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24730132, text='The distinction that makes ai compression creepy is that it can use prior knowledge of what a face is.<p>Traditional compression has no high level knowledge of what a video call looks like, the algorithms are about patterns in how pixels change in time and space, so the artifacts from the algorithm are pixel-based effects (blockiness, blurriness, etc).<p>A neural net that has been trained on a million faces and is setup to draw a face may draw a perfectly clear image of a face on low bandwidth...but when it doesn&#x27;t have bandwidth to be accurate, it doesn&#x27;t blur things, it fills in details learned from looking at strangers who aren&#x27;t on the call.')
