Item(by='gkbdibfj', descendants=None, kids=[24661202], score=None, time=1601602507, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24657762, text='Googling the term will confirm that I didn&#x27;t just invent the notion of bijective compression.<p>That &quot;the domain and range are the same set&quot; is a given for compression programs which operate on strings or files. It&#x27;s the bijective property which is added to that here.<p>So it&#x27;s not that we&#x27;re dealing with alternative meanings of &quot;bijective&quot;, rather that it&#x27;s understood in the work on bijective compression that compression&#x2F;decompression are fundamentally functions from strings to strings.<p>(So compression is <i>not</i> conceptualized as a function from arbitrary strings to some more restricted set of &quot;well-formed&quot; or syntactically valid compressed strings.)<p>See for example:\n<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;arxiv.org&#x2F;abs&#x2F;1201.3077" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;arxiv.org&#x2F;abs&#x2F;1201.3077</a><p>&quot;[T]he transform we present is bijective, in that the inverse transformation exists for all strings.&quot;')
