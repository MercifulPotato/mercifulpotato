Item(by='dodobirdlord', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605001323, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25042806, text='The longbow armies of Wales and England were made up of yeomen, who were certainly well into the upper end of medieval society. There certainly weren&#x27;t very many of them. There were only 5000-6000 at the Battle of Agincourt, and the loss of ~2500 of them 14 years later at the Battle of Patay crippled the ability of the English to field a substantial longbow army through to the end of the Hundred Years War.')
