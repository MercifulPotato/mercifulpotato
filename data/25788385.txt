Item(by='taneq', descendants=None, kids=[25788479, 25788817], score=None, time=1610699526, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25788052, text='When I was first learning to ride motorcycles I came across &quot;A Twist of the Wrist&quot; by Keith Code, which uses a similar analogy for attention.<p>In this analogy, you have $10 worth of attention to spend. Maybe as a novice rider you spend $5 just coordinating steering, clutch, gears and brakes to keep the bike upright. You then spend $3 on navigating, which leaves you with only $2 left to pay attention to the traffic around you.<p>A more experienced rider might only have to spend $2 on physically riding the bike, leaving them with much more attention to pay to traffic and road conditions.')
