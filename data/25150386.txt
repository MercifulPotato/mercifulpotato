Item(by='torginus', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605799356, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25149297, text='I&#x27;m not super familiar with the history of aviation, but by the end of the 90s, 3D accelerators became widespread.<p>This meant that 3D became available to the masses, but the only way you could render graphics was the DirectX&#x2F;OpenGL way, with triangles,Z-Buffers,vertex&#x2F;pixel shaders.<p>Unfortunately this meant that other techniques, like voxel raycasting, spline surfaces and other weird techniques couldn&#x27;t be accelerated, and became much less prominent.<p>So yeah, the analogy stands that 3D transitioned from a period of whimsical experimentation to a &quot;this-is-how-we-do-things&quot; mindset in the timeframe, while a number of otherwise interesting solutions fell by the wayside.')
