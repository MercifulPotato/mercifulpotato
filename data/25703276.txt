Item(by='barnacled', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610218389, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25701312, text='One thing I&#x27;ve noticed in my career is that those who possess a certain kind of &#x27;wow&#x27; factor are the ones who are categorised as the &#x27;stars&#x27; and thus progress quickly through the technical ranks.<p>I&#x27;ve tended to find that these people don&#x27;t write particularly good code (not usually bad, just mediocre) and tend to lack ability in software engineering - writing deeply unrobust code that falls far short of their perceived &#x27;rockstar&#x27; qualities.<p>I can&#x27;t tell you the number of conversations I&#x27;ve had with managers or even colleagues where their untouchable genius is extolled to the heavens.<p>Combine this with the fact that those who raise issues and foresee problems are often viewed poorly (possibly even MORE so if those problems actually end up happening).<p>Ultimately you end up with a situation where those who advocate for solid engineering are, while respected, never really raised above the low levels of the career ladder and those who embrace woo rise up.')
