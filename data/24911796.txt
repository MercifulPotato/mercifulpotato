Item(by='Someone', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603832303, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24911302, text='Thirty, or, probably, even twenty years ago, nobody could build a electric car that could compete with ICE engines. So, why waste money making them if, instead, you can make money selling ICE cars?<p>That could have changed if society had (through regulations, subsidies or taxes) changed the rules, but it didn’t until fairly recently.<p>Even today, I haven’t seen numbers (but I haven’t looked hard, either) of _any_ manufacturer that shows they make a profit making and selling electric cars.<p>Tesla’s profits, for example, as far as I know, always were smaller than the money they get from selling regulatory credits (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cnbc.com&#x2F;2020&#x2F;07&#x2F;23&#x2F;teslas-sale-of-environmental-credits-help-drive-to-profitability.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cnbc.com&#x2F;2020&#x2F;07&#x2F;23&#x2F;teslas-sale-of-environmental...</a>).<p>That’s society finally saying ICE cars must go out.<p>Government mills mill slowly, but I think many people underestimate how much they can accomplish in the long run.')
