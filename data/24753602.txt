Apparently, writing lexicographical ordering on tuples is surprisingly unintuitive:<p><pre><code>    bool operator&lt;=(version lhs, version rhs) {\n        return (lhs.Major &lt;= rhs.Major) || (lhs.Minor &lt;= rhs.Minor) || (lhs.Patch &lt;= rhs.Patch);\n    }\n</code></pre>\nlooks correct for anyone who have never wrote such comparison before, but of course it&#x27;s wrong. The correct one is<p><pre><code>    bool operator&lt;=(version lhs, version rhs) {\n        return (lhs.Major &lt; rhs.Major)\n               || ((lhs.Major == rhs.Major) &amp;&amp; ((lhs.Minor &lt; rhs.Minor)\n                   || ((lhs.Minor == rhs.Minor) &amp;&amp; (lhs.Patch &lt;= rhs.Patch))));\n    }\n</code></pre>\n[0] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;devblogs.microsoft.com&#x2F;oldnewthing&#x2F;20040213-00&#x2F;?p=40633" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;devblogs.microsoft.com&#x2F;oldnewthing&#x2F;20040213-00&#x2F;?p=40...</a>')
