Item(by='danielheath', descendants=None, kids=[24766993], score=None, time=1602561542, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24762056, text='Your body constantly sheds skin flakes (dust).<p>Tiny mites find those flakes, cover them in enzymes that break down skin, then wait for them to break down enough to eat.<p>When a puff of air lifts the dust into your lungs, those digestive enzymes attack your lungs.<p>Your body produces extra mucus in response, to trap dust on the way in, and inflames the tissue surrounding any damage to prevent infection taking hold. Neither response is pleasant.')
