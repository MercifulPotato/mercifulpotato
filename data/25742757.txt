Item(by='IdoRA', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610429956, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25742480, text='I am familiar with Heller.<p>The temporary surrender under 5207(b) is quite limited both in scope and duration, more than you suggest:<p>&gt; Nothing in this section shall be construed to prohibit any person in subsection (a) from requiring the temporary surrender of a firearm as a condition for entry into any mode of transportation used for rescue or evacuation during a major disaster or emergency, provided that such temporarily surrendered firearm is returned at the completion of such rescue or evacuation.<p>The reference you make to “otherwise authorized to carry firearms…” (I assume) in 5207(a)(4) is also more limited in scope than you state:<p>&gt; prohibit the carrying of firearms by any person otherwise authorized to carry firearms under Federal, State, or local law, solely because such person is operating under the direction, control, or supervision of a Federal agency in support of relief from the major disaster or emergency.<p>I do know of states that <i>relax</i> bearing of arms during an emergency (Florida) and several that largely mirror the federal code. You’ve made a very broad claim that “Legally, you&#x27;re allowed to use emergency powers to suspend open&#x2F;concealed carry.” that I don’t think holds across the entire US, and I am skeptical would hold even across a majority of the US. Some concrete examples would be helpful.')
