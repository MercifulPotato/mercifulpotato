Item(by='fabrica', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607832050, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25399394, text='I believe it&#x27;s possible to be very interested in business and money and still end up with an ethically credible result.<p>The problem is that we(in the broader sense of the working world) don&#x27;t treat those concepts as an &quot;artistic medium&quot; to which additional principles and ideas are needed to structure and shape the outcomes, rather, we just use them as scorekeeping for a nebulous measure of life success, which results in founders laboring hard over the business equivalent of a fart joke, because they&#x27;re only aiming to min-max response for effort.<p>The measure of what&#x27;s an &quot;impactful&quot; business is socially constructed within the arbitrage of what we could potentially strive for against what societal conditions currently allow. Entrepreneurs, as a group, are always testing the boundaries of that arbitrage. That&#x27;s not a bad thing in and of itself, but it&#x27;s hard to find balance in the arrangement because they aren&#x27;t able to do their study in private: part of the deal is that they demand access to significant resources to make it happen, and that occasional violations get overlooked in the name of progress. In a time like now when the population is mostly asking for relief measures, it looks indulgent, and sometimes it is.')
