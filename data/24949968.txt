Item(by='TheOtherHobbes', descendants=None, kids=[24952016, 24951989], score=None, time=1604134725, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24949801, text='Nothing in physics has infinite precision. Math is an abstraction - which is to say a stylised representation - and while you can <i>imagine</i> processes and representations with infinite precision, that level of precision is metaphysical.<p>This confuses Platonists who seem to believe that differential equations <i>really exist</i> - somewhere.<p>But that&#x27;s like arguing that you will clock up air miles by zooming around Google Maps.<p>The set of operations you can perform on representations of reality is in no sense identical to the set of operations you can perform on reality itself.<p>If this still isn&#x27;t obvious, try to differentiate a rock.')
