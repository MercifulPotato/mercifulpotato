Item(by='bobthechef', descendants=None, kids=[24900136, 24904448], score=None, time=1603738492, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24896650, text='Maybe people are failing to understand that work is often uninteresting. The expectation that it will be and must be can itself be a cause of despair.<p>From the economic perspective, no one cares whether you find your work interesting. You don&#x27;t either when you buy a product or a service. Someone is paying you for some product or service, not to make your life fun. That&#x27;s your problem. You think everyone else is having lot of fun at work? You want to go tell them how uniquely awful your life is? How you thought work was supposed to be a 24&#x2F;7 orgasm? How your jobs is about _you_ and _your_ jollies and not the client or the user?<p>Perhaps a maximum 40hr work week would be a better industry benefit than free snacks and the other goofy gimmicks that companies like Google like to ply people with. That way, you can spend the extra time on your hobbies.')
