However, I almost feel it is more Pythonic to use a dictionary instead, collapsing the function into:<p>def http_error(status):<p><pre><code>  try:\n    return HTTP_STATUS_CODES[status]\n  except KeyError:\n    return &quot;Something else&quot;\n</code></pre>\nAlso, method polymorphism always seemed more powerful and intuitive to me than switch statements when the operation is something like:<p><pre><code>  multiply(scalar, scalar)\n  multiply(vector, scalar)\n  multiply(matrix, scalar)</code></pre>')
