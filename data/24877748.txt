Item(by='xondono', descendants=None, kids=[24878212], score=None, time=1603527676, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24877673, text='The trick is that ESP* chips implement a lot of the stack in firmware. This saves a lot of IP in dedicated hardware that allows for price reduction, but has it’s drawbacks, mostly in terms of energy consumption.<p>There’s also a good amount of problems involved in having a non-auditable (non certified) piece of firmware running in a coprocessor that handles communications, and being closed source and Chinese doesn’t help.<p>The difference is generally that the maker space doesn’t need to be production ready.<p>By the way, Murata has modules on the ~6$ range (depending on what features you want)')
