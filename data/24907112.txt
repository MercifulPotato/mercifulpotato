Item(by='abrowne', descendants=None, kids=[24907210], score=None, time=1603804871, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24906362, text='Not a solution for most people, but if you didn&#x27;t know, it&#x27;s very easy to replace the new tab page with a custom one using a minimal, local extension. In your manifest.json include<p><pre><code>    &quot;chrome_url_overrides&quot;: { &quot;newtab&quot;: &quot;ntp.html&quot; }\n</code></pre>\nand if you want it to work in incognito tabs<p><pre><code>    &quot;incognito&quot;: &quot;split&quot;\n</code></pre>\n(you will also need to go to your extensions&#x27;s detail page and flip on &quot;Allow in incognito&quot;).<p>Then create your ntp.html page (can be named whatever, just change the json to match), with CSS file(s) and image(s) as needed. I made a simple flexbox &quot;speed dial&quot; page with sites I specify rather than changing based on some kind of &quot;frecency&quot;.<p>In chrome:&#x2F;&#x2F;extensions&#x2F;, flip the &quot;Developer mode&quot; switch and then click &quot;Load unpacked&quot; and choose your extension&#x27;s folder.')
