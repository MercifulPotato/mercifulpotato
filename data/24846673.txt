Item(by='thu2111', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603279283, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24843312, text='I&#x27;ll add a few more I&#x27;ve noticed over time.<p>Certifications are a strong negative predictor. Someone who turns up with a lot of corporate certifications is unlikely to do well when asked difficult questions.<p>People who have only ever worked in low grade or low tier banks tend to do poorly. There are a few exceptions. GS has good technologists. Most big investment banks have a few programmers (older ones usually) who have solid experience - but who are unlikely to put any creative effort in and may have a &quot;here&#x27;s why it can&#x27;t be done&quot; attitude. May not matter for some roles.<p>People who list a very large number of short roles tend to do poorly.<p>People who give a list of projects in their resume experience for which they only assert they were part of the group that delivered it, without concrete claims of tech leadership, often do poorly - generally it indicates a &quot;hiding amongst the crowd&quot; problem in which someone is a poor performer and attempts to hide lack of any actual achievement by reference to group projects.<p>People who list obscure programming languages as a skill do relatively well, people who list obscure products as a skill do relatively poorly.')
