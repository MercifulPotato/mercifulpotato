Item(by='ineedasername', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607990242, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25424644, text='&quot;always&quot; is too much of an absolute. It really depends on a variety of factors, not least if which are other medications you might be on and underlying conditions. For example, ibuprofen doesn&#x27;t play nicely with prednisone. Or if you have any sort of existing liver problems then acetomenophen is really not a great idea.<p>If you have a regular doctor, it&#x27;s probably a good idea to ask which of a few different options are safe for you for occasional use, and probably check in if you find yourself needing something for longer than a few days. (And if you don&#x27;t have a regular doctor, find one... If you lack insurance, it&#x27;s still probably worth the money to pay once a year for a physical, and also have a resource that knows your health history that you can consult with. For basic questions, like &quot;does my medical history rule out acetomenophen&quot;, most doctor&#x27;s offices will take a message for the doctor and then call you back with an answer)')
