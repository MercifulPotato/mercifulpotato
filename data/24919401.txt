Item(by='F00Fbug', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603896751, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24918691, text='In the case of an engine failure, a helicopter trades its potential energy (altitude) to kinetic energy by allowing the passing air (as it &#x27;falls&#x27;) to spin the rotors.  This is the helicopter&#x27;s equivalent of gliding, more or less.  The condition is called auto-rotation.  Gyros are always in a state of auto-rotation, so if the engine quits they simply begin trading potential energy for kinetic energy, which they&#x27;re really good at.')
