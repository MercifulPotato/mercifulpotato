Item(by='waffletower', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608051217, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25431505, text='The uncertainty principle is is the key concept that I think is relevant here:  <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Uncertainty_principle#Harmonic_analysis" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Uncertainty_principle#Harmonic...</a><p>A Discrete Fourier Transform (DFT&#x2F;FFT) balances frequency vs time resolution based upon its parameterization.  In this application, I think that frequency differentiation is less important than time differentiation for events.  To get an advantage from utilizing spectral analysis, I think that the STFT can be used with very small overlapping analysis windows. (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Short-time_Fourier_transform" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Short-time_Fourier_transform</a>)  However, due to the importance of transients I would consider use of wavelet transforms instead (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Wavelet_transform" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Wavelet_transform</a>) since they can resolve temporal characteristics better than a DFT.')
