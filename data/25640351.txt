On the other hand, bookstores, libraries, and other distributors of material are liable for republication only if they know, or had reason to know, that the statement is defamatory.&quot; \n <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.encyclopedia.com&#x2F;social-sciences-and-law&#x2F;law&#x2F;law&#x2F;libel-and-slander" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.encyclopedia.com&#x2F;social-sciences-and-law&#x2F;law&#x2F;law...</a><p>In other words: they aren&#x27;t expected to have actually read every word of every book they sell. Publishers, newspapers, etc, are. But if a book defames me on page 154, the law doesn&#x27;t hold the bookstore responsible unless it knew. Once it does know, you could probably sue. But that&#x27;s not the same as treating it like a publisher- it&#x27;s a different standard.')
