Item(by='wtallis', descendants=None, kids=[25657137], score=None, time=1609931108, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25656519, text='The radius at which your WiFi AP causes interference with other networks is larger than the useful radius at which your client devices can maintain a decently fast connection speed.<p>And for many APs with poor management of airtime, having a client device on the fringe of your coverage area can cause your router and that client device to both waste a disproportionate amount of airtime shouting at full TX power and a low data rate, maximizing the amount of interference you&#x27;re causing to anyone else trying to make use of that channel.')
