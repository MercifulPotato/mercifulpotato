Item(by='MaxBarraclough', descendants=None, kids=[25166680, 25162828, 25168357, 25162623], score=None, time=1605892105, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25161907, text='Seems to me the word has a clear sense of permanence built into it.<p>The term arose because a brick cannot be transformed into functioning electronic equipment. A device is said to be <i>bricked</i> when it is no more useful than a brick. If you drop your smartphone into water, and it shows no signs of life even after a couple of weeks of drying, you&#x27;ve bricked it.<p>The average user is not competent at repair, as that&#x27;s a specialized skill. They might not know what to do with a laptop if you blanked its SSD, but we wouldn&#x27;t call that laptop <i>bricked</i>.')
