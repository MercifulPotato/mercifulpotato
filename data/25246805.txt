Item(by='ayewo', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606673615, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25245678, text='Models are tools to help us reason clearly about the world. They help us deal with complexity in manageable chunks by focusing on salient parts of a problem, rather than the whole.<p>From my course notes, a nearly comprehensive summary of how the social sciences use models was presented:<p><i>16 uses of modeling outside prediction include from Epstein, Joshua M. (2008). Why Model?. Journal of Artificial Societies and Social Simulation:</i><p>1. Explain (very distinct from predict)<p>2. Guide data collection<p>3. Illuminate core dynamics<p>4. Suggest dynamical analogies<p>5. Discover new questions<p>6. Promote a scientific habit of mind<p>7. Bound (bracket) outcomes to plausible ranges<p>8. Illuminate core uncertainties<p>9. Offer crisis options in near-real time<p>10. Demonstrate tradeoffs &#x2F; suggest efficiencies<p>11. Challenge the robustness of prevailing theory through perturbations<p>12. Expose prevailing wisdom as incompatible with available data<p>13. Train practitioners<p>14. Discipline the policy dialogue<p>15. Educate the general public<p>16. Reveal the apparently simple (complex) to be complex (simple)')
