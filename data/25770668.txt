It was about whether a union would. Those are different. I don&#x27;t know whether there is any such possibility of legal recognition of a &quot;union&quot; of customers having any meaningful right to come to any kind of binding bargaining agreement with these companies, which is why the distinction matters.<p>And being &quot;below its peak&quot; is completely irrelevant to the practicality of this endeavor when we&#x27;re talking about a drop from, say, 99% to 94%.<p>The <i>overwhelming</i> majority of people are not only not annoyed enough by big tech to take action, but would <i>immediately</i> retreat from any such action the moment they realized what it would cost them.<p>A <i>miniscule</i> fraction of the people who use these services, or even pay for these services, care enough about their misdeeds to stop using them personally, much less to join some sort of advocacy group about it.')
