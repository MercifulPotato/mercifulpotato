Item(by='shawnz', descendants=None, kids=[25150423], score=None, time=1605798813, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25150273, text='I do not think that it is fair to say that changing a default is equivalent to taking a choice away. They are only moving the choice from the network operator&#x27;s hands directly into the user&#x27;s hands, which is where I think it belongs (and where it really was in the first place). In the hands of the user whose device is actually establishing the connection.<p>You could block your guest&#x27;s VPN connections, but you don&#x27;t do that.. why not? Aren&#x27;t they subverting your protection by using VPNs?<p>&gt; nor would I subject them to centralized DNS. Both are equally dangerous in my view.<p>I would argue that large providers like Cloudflare are the only ones who realistically have the resources to stand up to threats like government intervention (where it is possible). They have strong legal and financial incentives to maintain their privacy guarantees which smaller providers don&#x27;t have.<p>Also you have the advantage of your data &quot;hiding in plain sight&quot; due to the high volume they deal with.<p>I certainly wish there were more providers available than just Cloudflare and Google though.')
