Item(by='user5994461', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603815026, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24905513, text='&gt;&gt;&gt; I&#x27;m wondering a bit about that as many frameworks&#x2F;libraries that use Postgres already implement an internal connection pool.<p>That&#x27;s precisely the problem.<p>The usual misconfigured python&#x2F;ruby application will throw 16 connections at the database.<p>The model for these languages is to deploy one process per core, so there will be 8 concurrent processes running. 8 cores * 16 pool = 256 connections total.<p>Multiply by N parallel servers and that&#x27;s even more connections.<p>There are issues with large amount of connections and postgresql, hence people decide to put pgbouncer in front of the database to mitigate.')
