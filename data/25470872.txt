I recall seeing multiple human interest stories about people who were eager to volunteer for challenge trials. I believe challenge trials are how the original vaccines were tested in the earlier 20th century prior to contemporary developments in medical ethics.<p>And you&#x27;ll get volunteers. It&#x27;s a rational decision for effective altruism. Say you face a 1% chance of death but a 50% chance of substantially contributing to saving a million lives. If that&#x27;s technically possible, highly informed people should have the opportunity to make that choice.<p>I believe some of the earliest challenge testers were the developers of the drug themselves.')
