Item(by='pizza234', descendants=None, kids=[24991027, 24987545], score=None, time=1604479652, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24986864, text='&gt; I couldn&#x27;t get my head around why people like untyped languages<p>What do you mean exactly with &quot;liking&quot; untyped languages?<p>I personally program mainly in both a (&quot;cognitively demanding&quot;) typed and an untyped languages, and they have different use cases.<p>For relatively small programs, untyped languages are much faster to develop. A program I wrote yesterday in a few hours would have taken 2 to 4 times as much in a typed language.<p>On large libraries (I consider gaming engines as so), common wisdom suggests that typed languages make the project easier to manage (modify and so on).<p>There&#x27;s obviously a wide range of use cases in between.')
