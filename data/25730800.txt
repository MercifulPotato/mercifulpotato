Item(by='thesz', descendants=None, kids=[25737249, 25732683], score=None, time=1610381340, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25727482, text='C++ cannot have non-local type inference due to, you know, object-oriented part of it.<p>This means that you cannot say something like this:<p><pre><code>  auto sepulka;\n  auto bubuka = zagizuka(sepulka);\n</code></pre>\nBecause if zagizuka&#x27;s parameter is a structure or a class, you have a selection of parents. On a contrary, you have a selection of descendants of the result type of zagizuka() for bubuka, each having their own copy or assignment constructor.<p>[1] <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;lucacardelli.name&#x2F;Talks&#x2F;2007-08-02%20An%20Accidental%20Simula%20User%20%28ECOOP%20Prize%29.pdf" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;lucacardelli.name&#x2F;Talks&#x2F;2007-08-02%20An%20Accidental%...</a><p>[2] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Intuitionistic_type_theory#Martin-L%C3%B6f_type_theories" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Intuitionistic_type_theory#Mar...</a><p>[1] shows how hard it is to make right type system with inheritance. I believe these slides mention 25 years of collaborative effort. Compare that to [2] where it took 8 years to design intuitionistic type theory (1971-1979) by mostly single person.')
