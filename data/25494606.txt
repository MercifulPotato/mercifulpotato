Item(by='yorwba', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608556574, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25494094, text='That article seems to suggest that the resemblance is related to the Austronesian migration out of what is today China. But that happened thousands of years ago, so it&#x27;s unlikely that any word remained exactly the same over such a long time.<p>Partially, the similarity is just orthographic: the pronunciation of Mandarin huì is written [xweɪ̯⁵¹] in the international phonetic alphabet, while the IPA transcription of Māori hui is apparently just [hui].<p>Additionally, both words derive from forms that were even less similar: the Mandarin word is reconstructed to come from Old Chinese &#x2F;* m-kˤop-s&#x2F; or &#x2F;* ɡoːbs&#x2F; <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wiktionary.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;%E6%9C%83#Pronunciation_1" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wiktionary.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;%E6%9C%83#Pronunciation_1</a> while the Austronesian Comparative Dictionary relates the Māori term to Proto-Oceanic * pui, referring to a cluster e.g. of fruit: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.trussel2.com&#x2F;acd&#x2F;acd-s_p.htm#4023" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.trussel2.com&#x2F;acd&#x2F;acd-s_p.htm#4023</a>')
