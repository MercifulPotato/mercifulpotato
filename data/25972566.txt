Item(by='tptacek', descendants=None, kids=[25973133], score=None, time=1612037880, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25972271, text='For example: HFT-backed trading systems enable companies like Citadel to quote better spreads to retail traders than to hedge and mutual funds, which is why PFOF arrangements are structured in terms of <i>how much better</i> their prices will be than the actual exchange (which they are required to at least match, by regulation).<p>It&#x27;s probably the case that no one person needs to make a microsecond-scale trade. But, obviously, there are many people trading, not just one, and making things very fast is one way you make things scale. In reality, though, extremely high performance is probably more important as a vector for competition, which is ultimately what brought spreads down.')
