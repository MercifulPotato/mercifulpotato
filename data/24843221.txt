Item(by='ivanbakel', descendants=None, kids=[24844189], score=None, time=1603235299, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24843046, text='As well as this being rather closed-minded, it&#x27;s also not true. The contents of the 0000-FFFE codepoints are public knowledge, and the biggest users of space are:<p><pre><code>  1. the private use area\n  2. the general &quot;CJK&quot; area\n</code></pre>\nThe second of which has a truly mind-boggling number of characters, including every possible composite Hangul glyph used in modern Korean, despite them being constructable from the basic Hangul codepoints.<p>Emojis and other symbols which aren&#x27;t used for language appear relatively rarely. Certainly there is no reason to believe that UCS-2 would be sufficient for writing if they were removed. The number of scripts included in Unicode would exhaust even the private use area, and UTF-16 would have been invented regardless.')
