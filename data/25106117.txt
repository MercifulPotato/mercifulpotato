Item(by='pembrook', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605483797, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25104578, text='This is the classic problem all technical people starting companies face.<p>Technical people both enjoy, and know how to build digital products. But they don&#x27;t understand that a product is not a business.<p>A product solves a problem.<p>A business has a <i>steady stream</i> of customers actively searching to fix that problem (a market) and a set of <i>reliable</i> channels for converting these leads into customers (distribution).<p>If you&#x27;re not in a reliable market and have no reliable distribution within that market...you have a product, not a business.<p>A good way to figure out if you&#x27;ll enjoy being an entrepreneur is to start building an audience first (namely an email list built through social and blog content). It&#x27;s all of the vital marketing&#x2F;sales work and talking to customers you&#x27;ll need, without burning all your savings on 6 months of product development that might be wasted.<p>If you don&#x27;t enjoy building the audience of customers, selling to them, and talking to them, you won&#x27;t enjoy being an entrepreneur.')
