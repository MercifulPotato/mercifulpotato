Item(by='themodelplumber', descendants=None, kids=[25160264], score=None, time=1605877598, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25159808, text='You can always choose to just say those things, but if it&#x27;s worth doing, the language and approach should make it more likely that the general community response will be better for you than saying nothing at all. To me that means being gentle and professional about wording and boundaries, (which is easier provided one is not absolutely shattered by their work experience). Examples:<p>- We are currently backlogged and may be unable to accept contributions or support requests.<p>- Need support? We may be able to help. Contact us for bespoke personal or commercial support and development rates. (Packages start at...)<p>- We are evaluating license revocations for those who violate our clearly-written and frequently reviewed community standards, which you can review at this link.<p>Support abuse situations are often best dealt with situation-by-situation, though, and unfortunately I didn&#x27;t see a specific, real-world example in the article. Sometimes just the possibility of someone taking advantage is a turn-off for a good project owner, so I would add that at the very least it&#x27;s a good idea to have a general communication plan &amp; principles in place before that happens.')
