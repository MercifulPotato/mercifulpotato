I think busting monopolies is better in general, but sometimes (like for existing utilities) it makes more sense to regulate a &quot;natural&quot; monopoly.<p>Facebook is not really an &quot;magazine&quot;. In this analogy, it&#x27;s more like a print shop or a point of sale, except that 3 giant corporations own all the printing equipment&#x2F;all the newspaper stands, and they decided to stop printing&#x2F;selling NYT because some opinion piece in there used bad language.<p>There are degrees of regulation; people on HN tend to like &quot;net neutrality&quot;, and I don&#x27;t think many people are against anti-discrimination laws for businesses, so why not &quot;hosting neutrality&quot; where cloud platforms, oligopolic in particular, don&#x27;t get to kick people off their hosting just because they supposedly disagree with them?')
