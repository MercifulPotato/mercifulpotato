Item(by='jariel', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609726830, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25624768, text='The answer is not complicated, and the logic on this board is strained by people I think that don&#x27;t work in marketing.<p>1) Advertising works.<p>2) Coke, a sugary drink, is largely kept alive by smart marketing, that involves mostly advertising in the end.<p>3) Much of advertising is quite wasteful, because it is a fuzzy instrument - but on the whole, good marketing works.<p>4) There is a lot of self-awareness in the industry on this, and no doubt, a lot of borderline fraudulent actions by participants willingly spending money they know doesn&#x27;t work.<p>5) Ad networks will happily sell you ads that don&#x27;t work, and look the other way when there are shenanigans.<p>The problem here in the thread, is that people are having a hard time grasping how all of these things can be true at the same time, but it&#x27;s not that hard really.<p>That some ad spending turned out to be &#x27;not useful&#x27; is &#x27;not news&#x27; when everyone knows that easily 50% of ad spending is probably wasted, we usually just don&#x27;t know which 50%.')
