Item(by='rrss', descendants=None, kids=[24877847], score=None, time=1603456074, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24867819, text='This is an inaccurate take.<p>The default for an approved CA ballot initiative is that it cannot be amended or repealed by the state legislature. Normally changing a ballot initiative requires another ballot proposition, so an initiative permitting the legislature to make changes with a seven-eighths vote is actually more amenable to changes than normal, not less.<p>The California Constitution has a clause that enables initiatives to determine whether they can be changed by the state legislature.<p>You may argue that this is weird, but it&#x27;s how ballot initiatives work in California, so this one isn&#x27;t rewriting the democratic process.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;ballotpedia.org&#x2F;Legislative_alteration" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;ballotpedia.org&#x2F;Legislative_alteration</a>')
