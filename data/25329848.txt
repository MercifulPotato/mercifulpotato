Item(by='aasasd', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607322547, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25317829, text='Not quite patterns, but I think writings of Jakob Nielsen and his company Nielsen Norman Group may be considered as applying to ‘dense’ interfaces by modern standards—as he&#x27;s mostly known for his work from the 90s and 2000s. Their priority for the past twenty years is web, but not just casual-user sites, and IMO the principles are universal. Also he and the company have an ongoing series of criticism specialized on corporate intranets. BTW, a persistent theme is that interfaces should be tested on users soon and often—even if just with a few users and a cheap methodology, that&#x27;s better than no testing.<p>This is the main collection of articles: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.nngroup.com&#x2F;articles&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.nngroup.com&#x2F;articles&#x2F;</a><p>And there&#x27;s a tag for ‘design patterns’: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.nngroup.com&#x2F;topic&#x2F;design-patterns&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.nngroup.com&#x2F;topic&#x2F;design-patterns&#x2F;</a><p>I&#x27;ve been meaning to re-read Nielsen&#x27;s classic ‘Designing Web Usability: The Practice of Simplicity’, which I have in print from mid-2000s, translated. It&#x27;s likely to be a rather intense return to the past, considering how both the design space and my understanding changed over the years.')
