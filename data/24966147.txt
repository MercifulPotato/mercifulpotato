Item(by='SiVal', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604309247, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24965859, text='It&#x27;s not victim blaming to recommend that people who know how ought to switch to more self-managed solutions, but I agree with you that doing so is not an option for the vast majority of people. I&#x27;ve always promoted the idea that anti-monopoly laws should be upgraded to be especially sensitive to network effects, now that so much of the economy is becoming networked. The bigger a tech company gets, the less power they should have to decide whose account gets suspended, what you can say, what videos you can show, etc.<p>&quot;We&#x27;re a private company&quot; is no defense against traditional anti-monopoly enforcement. New powers that make Big Tech so big should be explicitly added to the dangers anti-monopoly regulation was created to defend against.<p>If Google wants to lure you in, they should end up being as stuck with you as you are with them.')
