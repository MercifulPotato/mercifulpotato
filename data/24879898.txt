Item(by='susam', descendants=None, kids=[24880685, 24880678], score=None, time=1603554432, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24879062, text='Any alphanumeric string with a length that is a multiple of 4 is valid Base64 string.<p><pre><code>  $ printf AAAA | base64 --decode | od -tx1\n  0000000    00  00  00\n  0000003\n\n  $ printf AAAAAAAA | base64 --decode | od -tx1\n  0000000    00  00  00  00  00  00\n  0000006\n\n  $ printf AQEB | base64 --decode | od -tx1\n  0000000    01  01  01\n  0000003\n\n  $ printf AQID | base64 --decode | od -tx1\n  0000000    01  02  03\n  0000003\n\n  $ printf main | base64 --decode | od -tx1\n  0000000    99  a8  a7\n  0000003\n\n  $ printf scrabble | base64 --decode | od -tx1\n  0000000    b1  ca  da  6d  b9  5e\n  0000006\n\n  $ printf 12345678 | base64 --decode | od -tx1\n  0000000    d7  6d  f8  e7  ae  fc\n  0000006\n</code></pre>\nSince &#x27;+&#x27; and &#x27;&#x2F;&#x27; are also used as symbols in Base64 encoding (for binary 111110 and 111111, respectively), we also have:<p><pre><code>  $ printf 1+2+3+4+5&#x2F;11 | base64 --decode | od -tx1\n  0000000    d7  ed  be  df  ee  3e  e7  fd  75\n  0000011\n\n  $ printf &quot;\\xd7\\xed\\xbe\\xdf\\xee\\x3e\\xe7\\xfd\\x75&quot; | base64\n  1+2+3+4+5&#x2F;11</code></pre>')
