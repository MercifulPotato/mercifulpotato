Item(by='Nextgrid', descendants=None, kids=[25838308], score=None, time=1611086783, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25838079, text='I&#x27;m not talking about moderation here. I&#x27;m talking about the fact that Facebook promotes certain content in user&#x27;s feeds (including content that these users have no direct relationship to - they&#x27;re not friends with the author nor follow him) in order to generate &quot;engagement&quot;. This should stop.<p>Regarding moderation, it&#x27;s true that you can&#x27;t moderate billions of people with 100% accuracy, but you can discourage them from posting undesirable content in the first place by associating <i>real</i> consequences such a a permanent ban (or a monetary loss, by charging an entry fee to create an account) or make them earn the <i>privilege</i> of posting content (for example, not being able to post links until your account has certain reputation of good behavior).<p>Discouraging people from posting bad content, and not amplifying the reach of bad content for engagement&#x27;s sake should go a long way.')
