Inner city may offer quicker&#x2F;easier access to favored activities. When I had a car, I found that a ten-mile drive in from the burbs (and back) can become a barrier. Mass transit, much moreso.<p>Socializing, there can be a substantial difference between interaction and meaningful interaction. Both can require time away from favored&#x2F;necessary activities; both can have multiple consequences (desirable or not). A perceived need for constant contact may be unhealthy.<p>Tech is a crappy substitute for the real thing (as E. M. Forster famously noted), so has some destructive potential.')
