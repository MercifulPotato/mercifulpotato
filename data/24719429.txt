Item(by='yokaze', descendants=None, kids=[24719614], score=None, time=1602167803, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24719031, text='The point is, at a certain scale you _are_ unable to secure your perimeter. Are you surprised that a handful of likely thousands external facing application can be hacked?<p>Especially, if most of your colleagues never have to bother with security, because they think, they are safe behind the perimeter, how can you expect a secure perimeter? With so many applications, there is bound to be one to have a hole.<p>The argument is more on the meta-level. Most of the shown ones are implementation issues. Hundreds of people have their hands in here.\nBut being able to gain more privileges because you have managed to compromise a service, that is one of design. And here, only few should have a say in.<p>Expect failure, limit the impact.')
