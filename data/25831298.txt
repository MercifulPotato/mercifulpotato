Item(by='konjin', descendants=None, kids=[25834174], score=None, time=1611046250, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25831206, text='&gt;If you are a software guy, drilling a 12mm horizontal hole 30mm deep into the edge of a 20mm x 400mm plate is surprisingly difficult.<p>Punch to center the first hole, then a center drill to start, then pilot holes at 3mm, 6mm and 12mm, use duck tape on the drill bit to keep the depth right.<p>If you really need the holes to be dead straight use a handheld router those are a great thing to have anyway and pretty much all of them have 100mm of or so of travel.<p>If you&#x27;re drilling into something very hard skip the 3mm.<p>A good video on how to tap so you break fewer of them: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=33wfSMsUbuc" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=33wfSMsUbuc</a>')
