Thus, the example makes just as good an argument that making better predictions is still what makes a model better.<p>The missing question is &quot;what are you using the model for?&quot;<p>Are you trying to learn chess? Understand human psychology? Predict the outcomes of chess games? The same model isn&#x27;t going to be the best for all of them. Like, the super GM level stockfish is going to suck at predicting beginner chess games<p>Whether one model is better than another is all about the purpose it&#x27;s trying to serve, and how well it does that.')
