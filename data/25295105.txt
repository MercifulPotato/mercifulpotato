Item(by='reader_mode', descendants=None, kids=[25295216], score=None, time=1607030262, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25294707, text='&gt;It may well be that the demand for physical presence H1Bs drops significantly in tech firms.<p>Even if you&#x27;re not required to be physically present there&#x27;s still value in being under the same legal system - good luck enforcing NDA&#x2F;IP ownership on an employee you never met from a random country across the world with a different legal system and different employment laws.<p>Also employee retention is even harder in that scenario (the opposite of H1B scenario where you basically capture the employee).<p>I say this as someone who&#x27;s freelancing for US clients from Eastern Europe - the only model I saw working for non-trivial stuff is opening offshore offices with a dedicated team&#x2F;staff&#x2F;etc. - but then you&#x27;re dealing with plain old offshoring C19 probably makes this harder because travel is harder and you probably want to fly key people to set things up properly.<p>There are full remote companies like GitLab but most of their stuff is OSS anyway and their core product is not very innovation based (something that needs IP&#x2F;NDA protection), this kind of work was getting outsourced&#x2F;offshored full speed even before C19.')
