Item(by='_Microft', descendants=None, kids=[25560517], score=None, time=1609169529, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25558458, text='The short(er)-term carbon cycle has a certain amount of carbon in circulation but using <i>fossil</i> sources <i>increases</i> this amount directly (by burning coal, oil, natural gas) or indirectly (plastics made from fossil carbon sources that is eventually burned).<p>Growing trees sequesters carbon and burning wood chips releases it again. This does not change the total amount in circulation and is much less of a problem.')
