Item(by='thamer', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604503738, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24985560, text='World War I was significantly different from previous wars in many ways due to advances in science and technology.<p>One of the earliest inventions that completely changed warfare at the start of World War I was the machine gun, which saw its first large-scale use in the 1914 German campaign against Belgian forts. Machine guns had been used in the Russo-Japanese War, but not to this extent and not with such terrible efficiency. Dan Carlin covers this topic in great detail in his fascinating 23h+ podcast series &quot;Blueprint for Armageddon&quot;.')
