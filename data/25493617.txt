Item(by='baybal2', descendants=None, kids=[25493639], score=None, time=1608546273, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25493556, text='<i>&gt; Israel is the only country in the world where the judiciary appoints itself</i><p>No.<p><i>Judges who serve on the Supreme Court, as well as the district and magistrate courts, are appointed by the Judicial Selection Committee, which consists of nine members: the Minister of Justice, another cabinet member, two Knesset members (in practice one is from the coalition and the other is from the opposition), two members of the Israel Bar Association, and the President of the Supreme Court and two other Supreme Court justices. The committee is chaired by the Minister of Justice. It can appoint judges to the magistrate and district courts by a majority vote, but appointing a Supreme Court judge requires a majority of at least 7 to 9 or two less than the number present at the meeting.</i>')
