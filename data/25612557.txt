On x86, which is little endian, compilers are smart enough to convert it to a mov.<p>&gt; These functions will either swap, or not, depending on the endianness of the target machine<p>So will the above. Here&#x27;s the MIPS little endian version:<p><pre><code>    read_int32(unsigned char*):\n        lwl     $2,3($4)\n        j       $31\n        lwr     $2,0($4)\n</code></pre>\nThis doesn&#x27;t swap. The MIPS big endian version:<p><pre><code>    read_int32(unsigned char*):\n        lbu     $3,1($4)\n        lbu     $2,2($4)\n        sll     $5,$3,8\n        lbu     $6,0($4)\n        sll     $3,$2,16\n        lbu     $2,3($4)\n        or      $3,$5,$3\n        or      $3,$3,$6\n        sll     $2,$2,24\n        j       $31\n        or      $2,$3,$2\n</code></pre>\nThis swaps and ors. What part of this do you not consider portable between different-endian machines?')
