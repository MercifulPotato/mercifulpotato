Item(by='comex', descendants=None, kids=[25967372], score=None, time=1611957723, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25963086, text='The processor itself supports configuring some aspects of floating point behavior; on x86 this is the MXCSR register.  The most important one is rounding mode: you can choose round-to-nearest, round-down, round-up, or round-towards-zero.  Less common options include whether to raise an exception on various out-of-range conditions, and whether to treat denormal numbers as zero (faster but less accurate and not IEEE 754 compliant).<p>The C standard library has functions to do this configuration; search for fesetenv.  _fpreset is not a standard function but, at least in Wine&#x27;s implementation [1], just resets the relevant configuration registers to an initial state.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;wine-mirror&#x2F;wine&#x2F;blob&#x2F;e909986e6ea5ecd49b2b847f321ad89b2ae4f6f1&#x2F;dlls&#x2F;msvcrt&#x2F;math.c#L2139" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;wine-mirror&#x2F;wine&#x2F;blob&#x2F;e909986e6ea5ecd49b2...</a>')
