Item(by='Slartie', descendants=None, kids=[25269694, 25269425], score=None, time=1606852621, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25268525, text='If you hadn&#x27;t tethered your software to proprietary AWS services in the first place, you wouldn&#x27;t have the problem to &quot;replicate&quot; all of that on your own infrastructure. You would simply &quot;have&quot; that stuff - or better said: you&#x27;d have the parts that you actually need (having a giant grab bag of ready-to-use cloud services at your disposal tends to entice developers to use them, regardless of actual need, especially in &quot;enterprisey&quot; environments where just another service doesn&#x27;t really ring a bell with anyone, corporate pays for everything anyway and every developer is constantly engaged in putting more tech buzz words on their CVs).<p>Queueing and messaging systems, databases, key-value stores, backup solutions etc. have all been invented pre-AWS, and there are battle-tested solutions for all of that out there, actively being used by companies which did not choose to depend on AWS.')
