The former is certainly the right thing to do and it also offers hope of redemption because the wronged individual can accept the apology and forgive (or not).</i>&quot;<p>Note that those to whom you apologize may communicate that apology to &quot;the mob&quot;, with the result that they have proof of your guilt as well as proof of your lack of forthrightness.<p>In the ultimate case, if you are following your own advice, Machiavelli and my bitter cynicism suggest that not leaving live enemies behind you is the best strategy.')
