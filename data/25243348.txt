Item(by='ijidak', descendants=None, kids=[25243359, 25243466, 25244090], score=None, time=1606631382, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25243037, text='I&#x27;d recommend buying rental properties in areas with low housing costs relative to rent.<p>There are parts of Florida where 3 bedroom existing homes (i.e. not new) are $100,000, but monthly rent for that same house is $1,000+ per month.<p>Based on discounted cash flow, you can determine the value of any revenue stream. In some parts of the country, you&#x27;ll find homes are undervalued compared to rents.<p>Stocks are good, but they&#x27;re not good for steady withdrawals. For example, during a bear market your withdrawals are very costly.<p>To retire, you need predictable income streams, and rental property is one of the best.<p>Plus, it&#x27;s easy for a regular person to leverage money for real estate. For example, with $1 million, you can buy $3 million worth of property with 33% down -- not counting transaction fees, though).<p>33% down should give you immediate profits from most of your properties.<p>$3 million could get you as many as 30 condos&#x2F;homes in the right areas.<p>If monthly profit on each of those 30 condos&#x2F;homes can average $200 per month, that&#x27;s $6,000 per month right there.<p>You might still want to continue working for a few years to give you capital to deal with hiccups and allow some time for those mortgages to pay down further.<p>In a nutshell, rental property is one of the faster ways to generate income without working.<p>The other, simpler way is to move to a country like the Philippines.')
