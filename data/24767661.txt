It is, in addition to the stated goal, thought to be the ultimate linguistic geekery by the King because one of the first books printed in Hunminjeongeum was Dongguk Jeongun [1], which set out to standardize the &quot;orthodox&quot; Chinese pronunciation in Korea. As always that didn&#x27;t go well; the book does tell a lot about the Middle Korean despite of its failure.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Dongguk_Jeongun" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Dongguk_Jeongun</a>')
