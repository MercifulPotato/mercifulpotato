Item(by='curiousllama', descendants=None, kids=[25995813], score=None, time=1612223061, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25993519, text='I&#x27;ve done a fair amount of work in the area, and one of the biggest lessons is &quot;even if you can diagnose, you shouldn&#x27;t&quot;<p>We likely won&#x27;t have to deal with the legal dilemmas for a while for a simple reason: doctors _hate_ when non-doctors diagnose, and you can&#x27;t build a diagnostic tool without doctors.  The tools simply won&#x27;t get built to any real quality standard.<p>There&#x27;s an easier path, interestingly. It&#x27;s much more effective to just give docs the info they need.  A system that could do the following would be the holy grail: &quot;Patient entered ER with [chest pain, shortness of breath], and has a history of [hypertension, diabetes].  They are on [X, Y medication], but have not filled their prescription in [Z weeks].&quot; No diagnosis, just the right info at the right time.')
