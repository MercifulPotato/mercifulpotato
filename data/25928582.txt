Item(by='marvin', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611757463, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25928305, text='When you own shares, you own a fraction of the company. The assets of the company partly belong to you, and if you have the voting power, you can decide to pay out some of it.<p>Of course, there&#x27;s a philosophical argument to be made if the ownership structure is such that a dividend decision like that can never be made.<p>I suppose everyone is confident that if the leadership of such a company <i>actually</i> tried to assert that &quot;no, your class ZZ share is actually just monopoly money, and your ownership share does not entitle you to any rights&quot;, such a claim would be struck down in court. Or cause the stock price to crash to such a degree that the class A shareholders would amend the agreement.')
