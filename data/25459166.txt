Item(by='wpietri', descendants=None, kids=[25459613, 25459412], score=None, time=1608232616, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25458923, text='I suspect the same will be true about artificial light. Our sleep cycles changed dramatically with the introduction of electric light.<p>A few years back I built an automated lighting system [1] for my apartment that was basically a scaled-up version of a light-based alarm clock [2]. I got the expected benefit, getting up more easily in the morning. But it turned out the bigger benefit was going to sleep at a more regular hour thanks to lights that dimmed in the evening. It turned out that previously I just wasn&#x27;t a responsible lightswitch user: if the lights were on and I was doing something interesting, I&#x27;d blow my sleep schedule out. Now, though, that almost never happens. I think it&#x27;s had significant mood and health benefits.<p>For those interested, the history podcast BackStory had a great set of stories on how time and sleep have changed in the industrial era.
