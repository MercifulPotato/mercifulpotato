Item(by='fairity', descendants=None, kids=[25153764], score=None, time=1605815687, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25153327, text='To get a better idea of why zooming out 20 years isn&#x27;t reasonable, take a look at the past 120 years in the US (Sweden&#x27;s numbers likely follow the same trajectory): <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cdc.gov&#x2F;nchs&#x2F;data-visualization&#x2F;mortality-trends&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cdc.gov&#x2F;nchs&#x2F;data-visualization&#x2F;mortality-trends...</a><p>Directly comparing 2000 to 2020 makes little sense given the consistent downward trend and magnitude of difference between 2001-2005 and 2015-2019.<p>Given the downward trend in adjusted deaths per million over time, a more reasonable approach is to average the last 5 years and compare it to 2020.  Doing so, you will find that 2020 has ~750 more deaths per million in Sweden than prior periods.  (Keep in mind this approach likely understates the affect of COVID on mortality rate due to the aforementioned trend).')
