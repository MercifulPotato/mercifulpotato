Item(by='nine_k', descendants=None, kids=[25342644, 25342690], score=None, time=1607409927, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25342218, text='If you need microservice architecture, it&#x27;s likely because either the size or the rate of change of your data prevent you from using a single RDBMS with synchronous transactions. So your microservices talk to something like Cassandra.<p>You might want some of the advantages of microservices without yet reaching this scale. Then you likely have most microservices stateless, and can admit you have a single database where you can do joins (but not updates) across microservice  borders.')
