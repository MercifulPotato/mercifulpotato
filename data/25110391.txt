Item(by='austincheney', descendants=None, kids=[25111544], score=None, time=1605525142, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25108835, text='So many of the thought exercises present in your comment are missing rather obvious validations.<p>Why do we not ask this of other professions?  Because other professions have metrics and regulatory bodies.<p>Why are no skyscrapers falling?  Because they are well regulated and required a huge infusion of cash to build.  A software company with such a large cash infusion is also likely to not fail.<p>Software engineers are best analyzed on a bell curve and once identified according to such plotting relative to other developers the criteria becomes clear.  The problem, for many people, is that selective bias prevents people from seeing the objective distinctions.  If not for those blinders the question of what makes an excellent software engineer is largely self answered.')
