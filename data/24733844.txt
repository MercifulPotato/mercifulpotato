Item(by='hintymad', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602273840, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24729679, text='That&#x27;s partly because high-paying salaries can be a stepwise function with respect to one&#x27;s time. If you make $500K a year, it does not mean you can make $300K a year by working three days a week. I&#x27;m sure many people are capable of pulling that off, but I guess more can&#x27;t. Software engineering work in corporate requires certain level of continuity. You may be called upon for a meeting. You may need to jump into a conference call for a system outage. You may need to do an in-depth research to debunk your co-worker&#x27;s design. You may be able to pace yourself, but your teams won&#x27;t be able to wait, which disproportionally diminish your value. Again, we can argue how to practice essentialism, but the reality is that many people can&#x27;t or are not capable of doing so. Otherwise, we wouldn&#x27;t have books about essentialism.')
