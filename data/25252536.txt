Item(by='fouronnes3', descendants=None, kids=[25252812, 25252624], score=None, time=1606733678, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25249563, text='I recently came accross a math theorem that in my opinion perfectly illustrates how mathematical notation can sometimes reach harmful levels of abuse.<p>E( E(X|Y) ) = E(X)<p>This is known as &quot;the law of total expectation&quot;, and as a programmer this notation is so weakly typed it makes no sense. The more correct notation is<p>E_Y(E_X(X|Y))<p>If you can see that the outer E is summing over Y and the inner one over X, then the theorem is immediately clearer and very intuitive.')
