Item(by='akiselev', descendants=None, kids=[24880508], score=None, time=1603559189, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24880187, text='That &quot;epidemic of chronic disease&quot; isn&#x27;t an American phenomenon, it&#x27;s a global one. The percentage of people suffering from chronic conditions is surprisingly stable from country to country. It&#x27;s the prevalence of acute diseases that changes due to quality of healthcare and (in the case of COVID) transient government competence.<p>The each step in the shift from subsistence agriculture =&gt; industrial labor =&gt; sedentary lifestyles are characterized by their own maladies. It starts with chronic malnutrition, then moves to chronic diseases cause by pollution, then to chronic obesity and heart disease and is compounded by climate. Each country has its own unique mix of the four but the overall rates don&#x27;t change much.')
