Item(by='lmkg', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608173907, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25451497, text='Entirely setting aside the current debate on whether police have enough accountability for the unique powers they wield...<p>Public sector employees are subject to extra scrutiny that private-sector employees are not. That&#x27;s simply part and parcel of being employed by a democratic government. Your employer represents the general public, and therefore you are accountable to the general public.<p>For example, government employee salaries are public record. And not just in-theory records, this data is often easily-searched online databases. If you&#x27;re employed in the private sector, having your compensation disclosed is considered a breach of privacy; it&#x27;s considered <i>mandatory</i> in the public sector.<p>So yeah, I think it&#x27;s reasonable to expect a certain level of workplace surveillance in the public sector that would be unacceptable for a private company.')
