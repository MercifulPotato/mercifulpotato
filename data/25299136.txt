However I&#x27;m not sure about using him as a model for how activists should behave. What that&#x27;s essentially requiring is that activists should patiently reach out until the people oppressing them learn enough to stop oppressing them.<p>In some cases this is possible. The Klu Klux Klan is comprised of broken men who dance in costumes at night. What about the racists who do not do that? Who enact their racism as government policy? Or as housing discrimination? Or through their art?<p>How long will it take to reach them? To quote James Baldwin^[1]:<p>&gt; You always told me it takes time. It&#x27;s taken my father&#x27;s time, my mother&#x27;s time, my uncle&#x27;s time, my brothers&#x27; and my sisters&#x27; time, my nieces&#x27; and my nephews&#x27; time. How much time do you want.
