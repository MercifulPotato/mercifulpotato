Item(by='umanwizard', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604164056, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24953035, text='For anyone who is interested: this type of name is called a patronymic, and besides Tamil Nadu exists in at least Iceland and Russia.<p>In both of those countries, a particular grammatical ending is added to the name, whereas in Tamil culture the bare name is used.<p>In Russia, people <i>also</i> have family surnames, in addition to patronymics. For example: “Dmitry Anatolyevich Medvedev”, whose father was presumably named “Anatoliy [something] Medvedev”.<p>Or: “Katrín Jakobsdóttir”, whose father was presumably named “Jakob [something]”.')
