Not only <i>can</i> the corporation fire me for putting such a label on the food, they <i>must</i>, if such a law exists - if they don&#x27;t, they&#x27;re liable.<p>So, let&#x27;s say I&#x27;m a baker selling, I dunno, kosher cookies. I can sell them to my friends and say, &quot;By the way, these are kosher!&quot; They&#x27;re popular with my friends, and so I decide to make a business out of it. As soon as I do that, the business is never given the right of free speech, and the business is now given an <i>obligation</i> to prevent me-the-individual from saying certain things in the course of my job duties. That is, if I continue saying the same thing I said yesterday, the government can shut down my business.<p>You&#x27;d say that this is constitutional?')
