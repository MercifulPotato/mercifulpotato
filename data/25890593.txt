Item(by='tsimionescu', descendants=None, kids=[25890725], score=None, time=1611480229, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25890165, text='For many non-native speakers, &#x27;man&#x27; tends to be used as &#x27;person&#x27; instead of &#x27;male&#x27;, probably because the translation of many common idioms involving &#x27;man&#x27; uses a neutral word in their language.<p>For example, when translating between English and Romanian, &#x27;man&#x27; often gets translated to &#x27;om&#x27;, which doesn&#x27;t imply a gender in modern Romanian.<p>Even in English, &#x27;man&#x27; is sometimes used without a gendered connotation. For example, if I say &#x27;man is evil&#x27;, I am unlikely to be referring to males, but rather people. Similarly, &#x27;hey, man!&#x27; is not reserved for males.')
