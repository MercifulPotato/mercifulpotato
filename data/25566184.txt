Item(by='lhl', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609210021, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25565913, text='Bitcoin: A Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System - “The cost of mediation increases transaction costs, limiting the minimum practical transaction size and cutting off the possibility for small casual transactions, and there is a broader cost in the loss of ability to make non-reversible payments for non-reversible services.” <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;bitcoin.org&#x2F;bitcoin.pdf" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;bitcoin.org&#x2F;bitcoin.pdf</a><p>Average transaction fees are frequently $5-10 these days, which completely invalidates the original proposed use case for a system that would enable “small casual transactions.” <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;bitinfocharts.com&#x2F;comparison&#x2F;bitcoin-transactionfees.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;bitinfocharts.com&#x2F;comparison&#x2F;bitcoin-transactionfees...</a><p>Since transactions will only increase in price as demand grows (and the tx limit has been effectively capped for years), I don’t see Bitcoin ever improving in this regard. Bitcoin may be successful as a speculative asset and for other purposes, but as  “peer-to-peer electronic cash” it’s a failure.')
