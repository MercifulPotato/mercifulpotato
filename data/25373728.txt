Item(by='erinaceousjones', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607608707, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25373418, text='JMAP does solve the same problems, and then some -- looking at the specs, it&#x27;s more of a 1:1 mapping between IMAP+SMTP. I&#x27;d say that if you&#x27;re looking at implementing _all the functionality of a modern email client_ you might want to look at that. If your use-case is just wanting to fetch emails from a bunch of inbox folders in real-time, potentially with some querying&#x2F;filtering, I&#x27;d say JMAP is a bit overwhelming. Sure, it has some clients&#x2F;libraries available, but only a few.<p>imapapi, in my opinion, being a RESTful API, appears to have a much simpler surface, seems easier to play with using curl, and if you&#x27;re developing a bunch of RESTful services that use Swagger, it fits better with your stuff. So, less dev-time to use :-)')
