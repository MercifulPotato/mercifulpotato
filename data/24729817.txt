Item(by='kalenx', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602251017, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24729345, text='That&#x27;s the kind of argument that could make sense in a vacuum, but does not apply at all in practice.<p>Sure, _in theory_, I would be glad to know enough, to have enough time and to have enough money to verify every scientific theory since the dawn of times. In practice, it&#x27;s been totally not scalable for a single person for about 500 years. _Careful trust_, with verification systems in place, is then the second best choice we can make.<p>The difference with religion lies in the fact that you do _not_ have to blindly accept everything. If you doubt a specific scientific theory, then by all means, put it to the test! But no, you won&#x27;t be able to do that for science as a whole, not because &quot;we&#x27;re whining children who like conformting stories&quot; but simply because of the blunt fact that our time here is limited.')
