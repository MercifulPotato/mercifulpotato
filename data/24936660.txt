Item(by='floatrock', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604009209, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24935722, text='To expand on the coral example: before fish get large enough to catch and eat, they start off as little hatchlings. Coral reefs are fish nurseries that offer protected environments before setting off for the deep blue seas, where the fishing boats find them.<p>Something like 40% of humans worldwide rely on fish as their primary source of protein. What happens when there&#x27;s no habitat to support that food source? Mass disruptions and migrations.<p>So yes, the earth was warmer before, but that warming happened over geologic timeframes that allowed ecosystems to adapt and change. 100 years is long on human timescales, but it&#x27;s an instantaneous disruption on geologic timescales.<p>And despite the convenience of your neighborhood supermarket, we are not that detached from our ecosystems. The empty shelves at the start of covid should have been a wakeup call that our supply systems are still interconnected.')
