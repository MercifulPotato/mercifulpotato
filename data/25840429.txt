I have used it to grow both tomatoes and pot. It does not replace the need to use fertilizer (or compost, etc.) so at least for the home grower, there is no cost savings. At scale it could allow you to use less expensive types of fertilizer or something, I am not sure.<p>Theoretically it helps to break down organic matter into forms more readily bio-avialible to plants. In my experience I&#x27;m not sure I&#x27;ve noticed much difference, although I did see some mushrooms popping out of the side of one of my fabric pots once.<p>Products like this should be looked at with the same skepticism as &quot;Audiophile grade capacitors&quot; and such.
