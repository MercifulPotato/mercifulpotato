Item(by='colejohnson66', descendants=None, kids=[24975868, 24975611], score=None, time=1604368031, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24975285, text='In C++, type punning through pointers and unions is undefined behavior. Even `reinterpret_cast&lt;T&gt;` isn’t allowed because of aliasing (IIRC). The only “defined” way to do type punning is a memcpy. A compiler targeting something like x86 would optimize out the memcpy.<p>For more information, see the C++20 final draft[0§7.6.1.9]<p>[0]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;isocpp.org&#x2F;files&#x2F;papers&#x2F;N4860.pdf" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;isocpp.org&#x2F;files&#x2F;papers&#x2F;N4860.pdf</a>')
