Item(by='SubzeroCarnage', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605395530, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25094104, text='Android has two ways of encryption.\nFBE = File Based Encryption, newer.\nFDE = Full Disk Encryption, older.<p>FBE is <i>only</i> secure when the system partitions have dm-verity enforced and verity is only enforced when the bootloader is locked.<p>FDE is a bit more resilient with an unlocked bootloader  since there is less data left unencrypted.<p>However in either scenario without a locked bootloader and verity it is trivial for an attacker to insert malicious code that can then run once your device is unlocked and send off your files.')
