After puberty, boys were more represented.<p>The theory I heard was societal pressure in the US was higher on girls to be &quot;liked&quot; and accepted, and that girls felt that doing well in school was counter to that. Girls would stop focusing on performance and ultimately move towards the center of the curve.<p>Pressure to be liked, and antithetical attitudes towards academic performance also applied to boys as well, but to a lesser degree.')
