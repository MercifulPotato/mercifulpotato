Berkeley looked at this for the simplest case of LEA [1]:<p><pre><code>  &#x2F;&#x2F; &amp;(array[offset])\n  slli rd, rs1, {1,2,3}\n  add  rd, rd,  rs2\n</code></pre>\nThe sequence in the article uses what Intel calls the fast case but it still wouldn&#x27;t qualify for Berkeley&#x27;s two instruction fusion. Dunno if anyone does three instruction fusion.<p>As an aside, LEA is never getting added to the base RISC-V nor should it be.
