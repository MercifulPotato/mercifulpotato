Item(by='Closi', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609442192, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25595569, text='It doesn&#x27;t sound like Mann is disagreeing with the core mechanics of GTD in those posts, it sounds like he is saying that there are diminishing returns with applying &#x27;personal productivity&#x27; methodologies, and you can get obsessed with tweaking your personal workflow to an extent where you no longer get payback.<p>This is a trap I have fallen into before - where you are effectively procrastinating by trying to optimise and system-tweaking your personal GTD system rather than just actually-getting-stuff-done.<p>Having said all that, GTD helps me personally massively. It&#x27;s not for everyone, but for lots of people in senior jobs that gets so much &#x27;stuff&#x27; in that they struggle to keep up, it&#x27;s really important to have a systematic way to process it all and not drop anything. I find people who don&#x27;t have a system like GTD or similar will tend to work from their inbox and end up dropping stuff.')
