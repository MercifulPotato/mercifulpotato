Floating point `+=` and `-=` are not exactly reversible. This is the only approximation that we have made in NiLang. To compile NiLang to a reversible device (rigorously reversible), we need to overhaul current number systems, i.e. using fixed point numbers and logarithmic numbers instead. Fixed point numbers are exactly reversible under `+=` and `-=`, logarithmic numbers are exactly reversible under `*=` and `&#x2F;=`. Here is an example of implementing Bessel function with two number systems: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;giggleliu.github.io&#x2F;NiLang.jl&#x2F;dev&#x2F;examples&#x2F;besselj&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;giggleliu.github.io&#x2F;NiLang.jl&#x2F;dev&#x2F;examples&#x2F;besselj&#x2F;</a>')
