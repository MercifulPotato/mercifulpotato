Item(by='Jochim', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605026923, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25047704, text='A counterexample I&#x27;d offer is that upon being treated charitably those employees could then be incentivised to use that windfall to pursue their own ideas, seeing the success of their founder benefactor and being empowered with a financial cushion they would not have otherwise had.<p>They might then go on to create something of massive value which the world never would have had if they simply did not have the financial means to do so.<p>Remembering the &quot;charity&quot; they received in helping build the business that enabled them to succeed as founders they then pass that same &quot;charity&quot; on to their own employees upon a successful exit, kickstarting a cycle of innovation that spreads much farther.<p>There&#x27;s an argument that &quot;that&#x27;s not the way the world is&quot; but the world is as compassionate or dispassionate as we make it. Personally I&#x27;d like to see it move towards a point where taking everything just because you can isn&#x27;t viewed as acceptable.')
