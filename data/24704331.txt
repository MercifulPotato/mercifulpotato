Item(by='mceachen', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602033448, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24703171, text='I think it doesn&#x27;t help you (as an entrepreneur) or the customer if you aren&#x27;t forthright, <i>especially</i> in your initial communications, which are setting up their first impression of your product and your company.<p>When I was working on AdGrok, which did Google AdWords campaign management, the <i>very first thing</i> that would save <i>thousands</i> per month is to add the negative keyword &quot;free&quot; to their ad campaign: People searching for &quot;free $whateverYourProductDoes&quot; are not going to be easy to convert to paid, and are likely skewed towards subsequently toxic customers.<p>There are many &quot;freemium&quot; products that hide the limitations of their free offering, which may result in higher initial conversions, but no one feels good when they&#x27;re &quot;tricked&quot; into using a product.<p>Clearly enumerate what&#x27;s the value-add of paying for the thing, and make sure there&#x27;s actual value to that value-add.<p>Also: it&#x27;s (possibly) PR-worthy when you make previously-paid features be free. It&#x27;s flame-worthy when you push previously-free features behind a paywall. Consider being conservative with your free tier due to this imbalance.<p>(I&#x27;m wrestling with what&#x27;s going to be in the &quot;free&quot; versus &quot;plus&quot; tier for PhotoStructure right now!).')
