Item(by='hedora', descendants=None, kids=[25179900], score=None, time=1606068568, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25178661, text='Credit card pins aren’t a thing in the US (debit cards require pins, however).<p>In theory, the merchant is supposed to look at the signature on the back of the card and compare it with the signature you write on the receipt.   You can even write ID REQUIRED and then they’re supposed to check for ID.<p>In practice, I didn’t bother signing any of my cards for about 15 years.  After two cashiers expressed annoyance at my blatant disregard for the rules (a few years apart) I started signing the cards.')
