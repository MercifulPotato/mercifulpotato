Item(by='auganov', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602835682, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24796507, text='There are much more fundamental issues with doing any research on drugs like this.<p>First there&#x27;s a huge selection issue, many won&#x27;t ever want to take it.<p>Since the drug has a strong effect you cannot easily design a placebo study - people would still know if they got a sugar pill or the real deal [0].<p>Anything that&#x27;s a highly unusual experience is likely to be rated as significant.<p>Very hard to design a good study.<p>I believe the reported efficacy would greatly go down with increased availability. If alcohol was a mostly illegal schedule 1 drug in most of the world I could see similar narratives forming around it.<p>[0] btw I think that&#x27;s a very underappreciated factor across all kinds of research. Even with regular medical drugs. Don&#x27;t see it brought up too much.')
