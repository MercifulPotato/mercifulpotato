There are a few things that help us really get the most out of hardware and network IO.<p>1. Async everywhere - We use AIO and io_uring to make sure that your inter-core communications are non-blocking.<p>2. Shard-per-core - It also helps if specific data is pinned to a specific CPU, so we partition on a per-core basis. Avoids cross-CPU traffic and, again, less blocking.<p>3. Schedulers - Yes, we have our own IO scheduler and CPU scheduler. We try to get every cycle out of a CPU. Java is very &quot;slushy&quot; and though you can tune a JVM it is never going to be as &quot;tight&quot; performance-wise.<p>4. Direct-attached NVMe &gt; networked-attached block storage.
