Item(by='TheOtherHobbes', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605746814, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25144751, text='There is a legal principle that contractual benefits can be assigned, but not contractual obligations.<p>But it doesn&#x27;t work like this. It works on a much simpler basis - e.g. if I&#x27;m a landlord and receive rental income and I assign that income to a third party, the third party does not <i>automatically</i> become responsible for fixing the roof.<p>It <i>does not</i> mean that I can assign the income to a third party and all responsibilities for the upkeep of the property are magically wiped out, and everyone involved - except the tenant - can use this as an excuse for a party.')
