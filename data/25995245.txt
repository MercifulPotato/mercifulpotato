Item(by='drej', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1612220010, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25992464, text='I have seen several deployments of Databricks (including first hand experience) and... most use cases could be better served by Postgres (or Redshift, Athena, Snowflake for larger scale). It has honestly been such an overkill for so many workloads, it was quite astonishing. I&#x27;ve seen people move from Excel to Spark... to handle the same volume of data. That&#x27;s obviously not Databricks&#x27; fault, but their PR is pretty much &quot;please do all your data work in our product, it&#x27;s well suited for it&quot;.<p>Yes, it&#x27;s very good if you don&#x27;t like setting up clusters (few do&#x2F;can) and the UI is rather useful for getting up and running (not so much for writing code though). But you need to really understand the platform before adopting it. Please, please don&#x27;t just adopt it because it&#x27;s popular.')
