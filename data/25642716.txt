Funding drugs based on profit potential is hugely distorting. E.g., Oxycontin. Or the whole pharmaceutical sales and promotion apparatus. Or the way it&#x27;s much easier to fund something that mitigates a problem instead of curing it.<p>We could publicly fund pharmaceutical research. Indeed, I suspect we&#x27;d be better off publicly funding it on cost terms alone. Global pharma research was $186 billion in 2019. [1] The US spends over $100 billion more on drugs compared with other countries&#x27; per-capita spending. [2] The current opioid crisis costs nearly $80 billion&#x2F;year, [3] and was kicked off by a prescription opioid boom [4] driven by the promotion of Oxycontin.
