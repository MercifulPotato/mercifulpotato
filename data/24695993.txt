Item(by='orbifold', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1601976036, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24695083, text='They don&#x27;t address the fundamental issue of BPTT in an RNN model. To my mind this is a much larger block to biological plausibility than how to track the error locally. Besides this has been addressed in various forms in earlier work:<p>- Hochreiter and Schmidhuber present a truncated version of BPTT in the original LSTM paper that tracks part of the error locally and forward in time (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.bioinf.jku.at&#x2F;publications&#x2F;older&#x2F;2604.pdf" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.bioinf.jku.at&#x2F;publications&#x2F;older&#x2F;2604.pdf</a>)<p>- Bellec et al. <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.nature.com&#x2F;articles&#x2F;s41467-020-17236-y" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.nature.com&#x2F;articles&#x2F;s41467-020-17236-y</a> present a similar algorithm that tracks the error with something they call eligibility traces. Despite the title of the paper the method is also applicable to non-spiking RNNs.<p>- Bohnstigl et al. present another solution: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;arxiv.org&#x2F;pdf&#x2F;2007.12723.pdf" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;arxiv.org&#x2F;pdf&#x2F;2007.12723.pdf</a>')
