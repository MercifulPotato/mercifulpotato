Item(by='Blikkentrekker', descendants=None, kids=[25779040], score=None, time=1610644084, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25778707, text='&gt; <i>What type of societal structure would be likely to merge the words for &quot;human&quot; and &quot;male&quot; while leaving the word for &quot;female&quot; separate?</i><p>The origin of the secondarily developed semantic meaning of “man” as a “male adult human&quot; is simply because when a man be anything other than an adult male, speakers are statistically more likely to emphatically note it in some way.<p>Exactly what you did by asking “what of the women?” when it is quite clear from context to anyone what “man” means in this context. “woman” is an overtly marked phrase compared to “man&quot; that arises from a need felt by some speakers to explicitly mark it when a person be female, but not when it be a male.<p>A similar situation is that white U.S.A. residents are often called “Americans” but for other colors overtly marked phrases such as “African-American” or “Asian-American” are common in parlance, which, due to this behavior, eventually gives further currency to the term “American&quot; as developing a secondary meaning of “specifically white American”.')
