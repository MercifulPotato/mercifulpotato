Item(by='qchris', descendants=None, kids=[25318574], score=None, time=1607203413, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25318217, text='Actually, I found watching The Good Place to be really enjoyable in part because of how well it acted as an introduction to philosophy and ethics.<p>There&#x27;s some great scenes explicitly addressing concepts like utilitarianism and it&#x27;s ups and downs, moral desserts, classic examples like the Trolley Problem, and relativism that, at least for me, made those concepts much more accessible when I was doing more reading later on because I already had some sort of concrete idea of how those concepts addressed certain (fantastical) situations.<p>It&#x27;s also just a wonderful show, and I think it&#x27;s worth recommending on its own merits, but there&#x27;s definitely some actual value there as an accessible intro to several different aspects of philosophy.')
