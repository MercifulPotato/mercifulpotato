Item(by='hedora', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611087976, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25837934, text='The penalties in these cases have historically been pretty disproportionate:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Copyright_misuse" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Copyright_misuse</a><p>In Lasercomb v. Reynolds, and Practice Management Information v. American Medical Association the existence of an abusive licensing agreement allowed third parties to simply violate the copyright.<p>So, assuming misuse of copyright was found, precedent suggests the copyrights that were being misused would be rendered unenforceable.<p>A case finding that bad faith DMCA take downs amounted to misuse of copyright would be setting a new legal precedent, but, based on the facts in multiple recent DMCA takedowns, such a finding wouldn&#x27;t be a huge logical leap.')
