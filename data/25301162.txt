Item(by='Veen', descendants=None, kids=[25302407, 25301287, 25302149, 25301469, 25301478, 25302233, 25301418, 25301354, 25301828], score=None, time=1607081000, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25300547, text='Your comment reminds me of something Andy Matuschak wrote:<p>&gt; But most people who write about note-taking don’t seem particularly accomplished in their own fields, whatever those may be. In fact, most such writers aren’t applying their notes to some exogenous creative problem: their primary creative work is writing about productivity. These writers offer advice on note-taking to help scientists and executives with the challenges of their work, but the advice was developed in a context disconnected from those external realities.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;notes.andymatuschak.org&#x2F;zUMFE66dxeweppDvgbNAb5hukXzXQu8ErVNv" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;notes.andymatuschak.org&#x2F;zUMFE66dxeweppDvgbNAb5hukXzX...</a><p>Essentially, be wary of taking advice about reading, research, writing, or anything else from people whose primary contribution to the world is telling people how to read, research, and write. Instead, listen to people who have used these techniques to make a substantial contribution to a field of knowledge.<p>The problem is that those people are far more interested in doing research and writing than teaching other people how to do it.')
