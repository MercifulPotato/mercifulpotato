Item(by='toomuchtodo', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607452163, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25349366, text='&gt; Veolia Environnement SA agreed to recycle General Electric Co. wind-turbine blades into a raw material that can be used to make cement.<p>&gt; The multiyear agreement involves most of the massive blades GE’s renewable-energy unit removes when replacing its onshore turbines in the U.S., according to a statement Tuesday.
