Item(by='jazzyjackson', descendants=None, kids=[25175203, 25175328], score=None, time=1606022618, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25174925, text='The fact that a D major is a different hand-shape than a C major lets me recognize <i>where</i> on the keys I should have my hand to play them -- like the ridges on the F&#x2F;J keys on a qwerty give you the home row, the uneven placement of black keys makes the middle C obvious by sight and by feel.<p>And this next part is just conjecture but I think the uniqueness of each chord aides in muscle memory, that is, it&#x27;s a feature not a bug that a b minor feels different in my hand than an a minor, makes it easier to recall that specific hand arrangement.<p>Makes it easier to look over one&#x27;s shoulder and know exactly what chord they&#x27;re playing too!')
