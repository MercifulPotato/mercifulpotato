Item(by='stakkur', descendants=None, kids=[24704413], score=None, time=1602016922, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24684499, text='&#x27;The Vikings&#x27; weren&#x27;t a people; it&#x27;s a term. The people who went a-viking were from several countries (Norway, Sweden, Denmark, Frisia, some others), and were (of course) somewhat varied in their genetic makeup. And, they often took wives from other lands, and had children with them. It&#x27;s a familiar story throughout history--peoples intermingling, defying easy modern classification.<p>The stereotype of &#x27;blonde Vikings with horned helmets&#x27; wasn&#x27;t perpetuated by historians or scientists, it was perpetuated largely by 19th century artists and others. Yet pop media seems to write false antagonisms like this:<p>&quot;They also found considerable genetic variety in the ancient remains, indicating migration of Southern Europeans, before the Viking Age, to the area of Denmark, <i>which undermines any idea of a single Nordic genetic identity</i>.&quot;')
