Item(by='gumby', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605374290, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25085987, text='The funding rounds are not a good way to describe the embryology of a company.  Different kinds of companies, like different species, develop in different ways.<p>Look at a company like si5 that&#x27;s making hardware: it had several rounds before getting into revenue, each one reducing risk for new (and existing) investors.<p>When I worked in pharma I thought of company stages from a tech perspective.  I once characterised them as &quot;pre revenue -&gt; pre profit -&gt; profit&quot;.  Our chief scientist asked, &quot;what&#x27;s pre profit?&quot;  In her experience you went from pre revenue either straight to profit or dead.  No ramp up in the tech sense.')
