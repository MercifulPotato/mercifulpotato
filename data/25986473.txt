Item(by='sooheon', descendants=None, kids=[25990700, 25990817], score=None, time=1612165158, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25985951, text='&gt; I&#x27;d rather take a small hit to the expected value of my returns in order to reduce risk of being on the wrong side of that average.<p>This is a common fallacy. That is not actually the tradeoff you are making, in fact you are taking a hit to the mean value, and also greatly increasing the chances of being on the wrong side of the average[1]. Dollar cost averaging increases variance and decreases the expected return in proportion to your averaging period. You earn less <i>and</i> have a much lower floor for almost no increase in the ceiling.<p>There are nice visualizations here: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;ofdollarsanddata.com&#x2F;lump-sum-investing&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;ofdollarsanddata.com&#x2F;lump-sum-investing&#x2F;</a><p>[1] at least according to history. Who knows what the future holds.')
