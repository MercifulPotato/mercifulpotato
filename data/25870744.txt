Item(by='throwaway0a5e', descendants=None, kids=[25871228, 25872163], score=None, time=1611315387, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25869224, text='Do we live in the same reality? Massive situations that constrains available resources cause innovation, prioritization of key goals and efficiency.  Abundance of resources results in the resources being wasted on various forms of fluff.  This is not unique to government, you see it in just about every type of organization.<p>I&#x27;m not saying &quot;defund all the things&quot; is the right answer (mostly because I don&#x27;t think it&#x27;s that simple or it would have worked where tried and we&#x27;d have done it everywhere) but there&#x27;s a very clear correlation between available wealth to allocate to government and bloated government.  Not having government be a gravy train also seems to help select for people who genuinely want to be civil servants because the people who are just in it for the paycheck don&#x27;t stick around in challenging environments like that.<p>It seems like all the innovation in government (e.g. novel program to keep the parks clean or whatever) comes out of places like Detroits and Buffalos of the world who actually have a pressing need to figure out how to stretch their resources whereas the NYCs and the SFs of the world just increase their intake.')
