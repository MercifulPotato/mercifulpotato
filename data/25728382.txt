Item(by='Twisol', descendants=None, kids=[25729423], score=None, time=1610370528, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25728279, text='You don&#x27;t get any information from `IO _` functions, but that&#x27;s the case with <i>every</i> mainstream language, where you have an implicit `IO` on every single function.<p>The benefit is not in `IO` -- its that its <i>absence</i> on a function tells you that it is heavily restricted in what it can do.<p>If you want to go the <i>other</i> way, you can concretely model the kinds of effects you need parts of your programs to do, and use that model instead of `IO`. Even if you end up implementing it using `IO`, you know it can&#x27;t use more than what your model exposes.<p>Both of these possibilities, while not truly <i>unique</i> (see Agda, etc.), are absolutely rare-to-nonexistent in mainstream languages. Effect modeling is a real shift in perspective.')
