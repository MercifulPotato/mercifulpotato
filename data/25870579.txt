Item(by='ad404b8a372f2b9', descendants=None, kids=[25871360], score=None, time=1611313482, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25868936, text='That&#x27;s why I setup my Linux install to work like a live-CD, with a two layer filesystem: a read-only base, and a read-write overlay that lives in the RAM.  \nThe files that I know I want to keep are bound from a read-write partition on the disk to the RAM filesystem, and all the rest gets deleted every time I shutdown my PC.<p>A lot of pieces of software non-maliciously keep records of everything you do with them through logs or caches that aren&#x27;t straightforward to delete and it&#x27;s the only way I found to have control over it.')
