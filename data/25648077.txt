Item(by='cesarb', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609867867, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25647872, text='These #[...] and #![...] are not comments; they&#x27;re attributes (somewhat similar to Java&#x27;s @Something annotations). Comments in Rust start with &#x2F;&#x2F; or &#x2F;&lt;star&gt; like in C or C++. There&#x27;s only the special case that &quot;doc comments&quot; (starting with &#x2F;&#x2F;&#x2F; or &#x2F;&lt;star&gt;&lt;star&gt; or &#x2F;&#x2F;! or &#x2F;&lt;star&gt;!) are automatically converted into #[doc=&quot;...&quot;] or #![doc=&quot;...&quot;] attributes.<p>The difference is that attributes are always meaningful to the compiler (and can only be used in specific places); other than doc comments (which turn into attributes), comments never are meaningful to the compiler.<p>(edit: HN&#x27;s comment markup eats the * character, I replaced them with &lt;star&gt; above)')
