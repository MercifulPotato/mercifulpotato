Item(by='acjohnson55', descendants=None, kids=[25334972], score=None, time=1607363674, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25334591, text='I can relate. I actually used to work at Artsy (the company who published this article), and before I worked there, I felt much the same way. What I learned after being immersed in the art world is that occasionally, art will &quot;speak&quot; to me immediately. But more often, it grows on me as I understand the context of the art and who made it. This is definitely the case for me with Basquiat, whose work can look like childish scribbles at first glance.<p>In my farewell email to my Artsy coworkers, I wrote that as I really began to engage with the art world in my time there, I realized I had gained a whole new layer of perception. I was seeing new references and callbacks everywhere in the world around me.<p>So what I would say is that if you feel like it&#x27;s going over your head and want to invest the time, it can be extremely rewarding to go down the rabbit hole. You can do this by reading articles, watching documentaries, visiting exhibitions, and finding spaces inhabited by enthusiasts.<p>Some things to check out:<p>- The book The $12 Million Stuffed Shark<p>- The film Exit Through The Gift Shop')
