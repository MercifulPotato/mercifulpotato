Item(by='Blikkentrekker', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610288109, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25714623, text='I&#x27;ve found most of them to have absurd black and white plots that lack any intrigue.<p>In particular, <i>Charles and Elbegast</i> stood out for me how none of it made any sense and how the final conflict of deciding whether Elbegast or Eggeric was truthful was decided by having them fight to the death, with the winner declared veracious, not to mention being gifted the loser&#x27;s spouse, of course.<p>I was told it was one of the apices of Middle Dutch literature but found it rather wanting, and so asked my teacher if he knew of any Middle Dutch literature that was not considered an “apex” — he could not provide me with such and I still can&#x27;t find much myself so I&#x27;m left to conclude that anything that survived the ages is automatically called an “apex”.<p>I wasn&#x27;t impressed and suspect that it is not valued despite being old, but singularly due to that it&#x27;s old. Had it been written yesterday, it would certainly not be so well received.')
