Item(by='dragontamer', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606858130, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25269277, text='Tftp is commonly used for PXEBoot scenarios.<p>You have a centralized PXEBoot server (basically a DNS + TFTP Box). All servers you manage auto-boot to PXEBoot, then download their image from the central server, then automatically provision themselves through Puppet or Kubernetes or whatever the heck you&#x27;re doing.<p>Throw down a few network power-adapters (to turn on&#x2F;off in times of emergency), IPMI for network remote KVM, a VPN to provide security, and you&#x27;re set to run your own small cluster.<p>-------<p>If you need more servers, you buy them up, give them a PXEBoot thumb-drive, and add them to your network.')
