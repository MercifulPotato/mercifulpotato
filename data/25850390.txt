Item(by='alexhutcheson', descendants=None, kids=[25855041, 25852662], score=None, time=1611169019, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25850028, text='Those are probably the users that RedHat is trying to nudge onto RHEL. &quot;Enterprise&quot; software is normally quite expensive, so the marginal cost of some RHEL Server licenses is probably a small fraction of the total cost of deploying the software in most cases.<p>More generally, orgs that are running &quot;Enterprise&quot; software are already set up to pay for software. Going from spending $X to spending $X+Y is often not a big deal (&quot;Ok, I&#x27;ll run that through the same process I used to get approval for buying FooSoftWorks&quot;), while going from $0 to $Y can be very hard (&quot;I don&#x27;t even know who I&#x27;d need to ask to get purchase approval for that&quot;).')
