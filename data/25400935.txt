Item(by='JNRowe', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607802135, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25400457, text='Like many things in vim it depends on whether you wish to write it ;)<p>You can define your own popup(:h bexpr) however you feel, including per-filetype or buffer.  Instead of simply operating on the variables it defines to you can also work upon the selection by querying the register(:h quotestar) instead.<p>As an example, I like displaying wordnet¹ popups when writing prose.  Occasionally, I&#x27;ll also dump diction² output in to a popup too.<p>However, I do think that using messages(:h :messages) might make more sense for selection info in general.  That way you can select a text, call a map to display information, and still easily recall that data when you&#x27;re in another place via :messages at a later time.  Depends entirely on your use case, and once again how interested in writing the functionality you are.<p>¹ <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;wordnet.princeton.edu&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;wordnet.princeton.edu&#x2F;</a><p>² <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.gnu.org&#x2F;software&#x2F;diction&#x2F;diction.html" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.gnu.org&#x2F;software&#x2F;diction&#x2F;diction.html</a>')
