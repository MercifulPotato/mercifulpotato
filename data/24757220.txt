Item(by='shadowgovt', descendants=None, kids=[24757604], score=None, time=1602525124, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24757041, text='The rule of law doesn&#x27;t exclude an employer deciding an employee is more trouble than they&#x27;re worth outside of some very specific situations (class protection, etc.).<p>Within the constraint of the rule of law, a group of people can organize to petition an employer to remove an employee for behavior A, and the employer can evaluate A and decide whether the group&#x27;s claims are untrue, A should be removed for the behavior, or it&#x27;s not worth the time to decide and A should be removed to quiet the group.<p>Good employers won&#x27;t do 3, and we should probably shame the employers who do, but I think it opens a Pandora&#x27;s box to constrain their choices with further law on this topic.')
