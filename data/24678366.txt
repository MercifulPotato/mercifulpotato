MIPS Risc&#x2F;OS, when MIPS made pizzaboxen .. sufficiently productive to be able to afford my own home lab gear .. and in at work comes a brand new 386 system instead of the X Workstation I wanted, and which apparently &#x27;solved the problems of the x86 platform&#x27; .. yeah, no.  I found some respite in a Quarterdesk&#x2F;Desqview configuration, multitasking DOS terminal sessions, but it was .. severely .. lacking in what I wanted.<p>So, at home, I got a copy of a similar 386 box set up, and did what everyone did at the time: got on USENET to find out what else could be done.  GNU&#x2F;Hurd was &#x27;gonna happen any day now&#x27;, and I&#x27;d already switched to using a bunch of gnu tools, but nothing was really working out .. which lead me to minix-list.<p>Which is where I read Linus&#x27; post about his little kernel project.<p>And, in the months and years to follow from that point, I became an avid Linux user.  Not long after, I brought a bootable Yggdrasil CD into work, and turned my cruddy DOS &#x27;workstation&#x27; into a viable X host, albeit after about 5 days of compiling ..')
