Item(by='MertsA', descendants=None, kids=[25494134], score=None, time=1608539845, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25492356, text='Honestly all joking aside systemd timer units have a lot of nice aspects. The only drag is that crontab entries are so much more terse and quick to add. Just one line instead of two unit files plus enabling&#x2F;starting the timer. You can actually get the best of both with systemd-crontab-generator (not from the systemd project, just an unaffiliated third party).<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;manpages.debian.org&#x2F;buster&#x2F;systemd-cron&#x2F;systemd-crontab-generator.8.en.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;manpages.debian.org&#x2F;buster&#x2F;systemd-cron&#x2F;systemd-cron...</a><p>It creates transient unit files based off of a standard crontab on boot and after any file modifications. If you want to extend one of your cron jobs with something that you can&#x27;t express in a crontab you can just mv it from &#x2F;run to &#x2F;etc and modify it however you&#x27;d like.')
