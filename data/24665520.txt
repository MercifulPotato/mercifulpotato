Item(by='masklinn', descendants=None, kids=[24665725], score=None, time=1601663350, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24663687, text='&gt; You can bind references to what you are matching with @name.<p>Given what they&#x27;re saying I assume they&#x27;re trying to match both the entire value and the sub-values e.g.<p><pre><code>    xs@(_:xs&#x27;)\n</code></pre>\nin Haskell. Stable Rust currently lets you use at-patterns and perform <i>assertions</i> on the sub-pattern, but not match there.<p>&gt; You also don&#x27;t need to take ownership of a value. You can match on a reference to it with &amp;.<p>`ref` is what you&#x27;d use in a pattern though, `&amp;` would deref&#x27; a reference so usually it&#x27;d be to Copy a Copy type which is provided behind a ref&#x27;.')
