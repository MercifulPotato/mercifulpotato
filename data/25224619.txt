There are tons of Zigbee and Z-Wave devices which physically don&#x27;t have a WiFi chip.<p>Of course, you should build your own bridge to connect them to (rather than using the bridge of a big tech company).<p>For Zigbee you can use the Conbee [0] and for Z-Wave you can use the Z-Stick [1] to build your own bridge.<p>Furthermore, some of the cheaper WiFi devices that are based on the ESP8266 chip can be flashed with a custom firmware called Tasmota [2] to make them cloud-free.<p>[0]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.phoscon.de&#x2F;en&#x2F;conbee2" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.phoscon.de&#x2F;en&#x2F;conbee2</a><p>[1]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;aeotec.com&#x2F;z-wave-usb-stick&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;aeotec.com&#x2F;z-wave-usb-stick&#x2F;</a><p>[2]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;arendst&#x2F;tasmota&#x2F;#readme" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;arendst&#x2F;tasmota&#x2F;#readme</a>')
