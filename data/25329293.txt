Item(by='zozbot234', descendants=None, kids=[25329503], score=None, time=1607314831, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25329155, text='Indian classical music can be understood (admittedly in <i>very</i> broad terms) as being based on melodic elaboration of what in Western music would be the bass line (aka <i>basso continuo</i> or <i>thoroughbass</i>).  The way this elaboration works is very similar to what one finds in the Western musical tradition (as clarified e.g. by Schenker), it&#x27;s just applied in a <i>very</i> different way in terms of structure (indeed there&#x27;s no equivalent to the &#x27;lead&#x27; melody, to which diminution would be applied in Western music!), and obviously with a strong focus on a single monophonic &#x27;line&#x27;.')
