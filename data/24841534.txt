Item(by='ineedasername', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603222666, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24840171, text='No, the fact that Linux is not currently a practical option for most users isn&#x27;t proof that it can&#x27;t be or never will be.<p>I am saying the lack of desktop adoption is indicative of the difficulties of doing so. There is an level-of-effort barrier and technical-knowledge barrier to it. 20 years of progress have lowered those barriers a lot, but even if something like Ubuntu will often be fully functional with a standard install, most users never have to install an OS. They can&#x27;t walk into Best Buy and come out with a computer that runs desktop Linux.<p>I think the success of Chrome Books show that people would be receptive to alternative operating systems, but we don&#x27;t have a retail or post-purchase support environment in place to facilitate it, and I don&#x27;t see that coming on the horizon.')
