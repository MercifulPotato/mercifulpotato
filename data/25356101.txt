Item(by='est31', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607497657, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25355261, text='Email seems simple to you, but in fact it&#x27;s an incredibly complicated protocol, so software treating it is likely to have many (exploitable) bugs. Also, there are attachments. Those are the classical way to hack many places.<p>Even if you got all of the issues caused by e-mails figured out, somehow you have to transfer them from one network to another. You&#x27;ll either have to poke holes into your firewall or use USB sticks.<p>My point is: even in airgapped networks you usually want to exchange <i>some</i> data. The moment you want to exchange data, you have a path where a virus can be smuggled in.')
