Item(by='pdmccormick', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603950162, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24927333, text='The Linux kernel actually does allow executing non-ELF executables, via the binfmt_misc (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Binfmt_misc" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Binfmt_misc</a>) mechanism.<p>My favourite use for this feature is via `qemu-user-static`, which allows you to transparently &quot;run&quot; executables compiled for other architectures thanks to QEMU emulation.
