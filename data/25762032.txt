The prep is just unpleasant because you spend so much time on the toilet.<p>For myself and many others, it&#x27;s not really about the pain (supposedly there is little to none), it&#x27;s just the unsettling thought of that scope so far up one&#x27;s...<p>I was sedated with Propofol and that seemed to be routine in the facility I went to. It was a white fluid that came in through an IV. I remember saying to the nurse as I saw the propfol going in &quot;that tingles...&quot; and she said &quot;that&#x27;s normal&quot;, but it seemed to me that she said her answer before I finished saying &quot;tingles&quot;, then a herringbone pattern visual from my tightly shut eyes, then nothing, and I woke as I was being wheeled out to the recovery area and felt no grogginess at all, seemed that only a couple seconds had elapsed from the time I saw the propfol (it was 20 minutes). Powerful drug.')
