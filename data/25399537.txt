Item(by='horsawlarway', descendants=None, kids=[25400114, 25400121], score=None, time=1607791889, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25399352, text='I feel like your understanding of a debugger is <i>very</i> limited.<p>To me - A debugger <i>is</i> the instrumentation you&#x27;re talking about, but created by the language&#x2F;runtime developers instead of having to be remade by you, poorly, for each new project.<p>A good watch statement is a hell of a lot more useful than an assert - even though they both try to do the same thing.<p>Not even touching the places where a debugger solves a problem you simply can&#x27;t touch with instrumentation after the fact (crash dumps immediately come to mind...).<p>And neither of them are (in any sense) a replacement for writing a test after you solve the problem, or adding comments so the next developer has context on the <i>why</i> of that piece of code.')
