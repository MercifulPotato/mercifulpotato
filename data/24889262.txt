Item(by='Animats', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603654904, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24884963, text='Lloyds, in its insurance period, was really a co-working space. That approach to insurance required multiple people to assume part of the risk. Each insurer would sign their name on a contract, below other names. This is where the term &quot;underwriting&quot; comes from. They had to get together somewhere to do this.<p>It&#x27;s probably not authentic, but you can see the Hollywood version of Lloyds Coffee House in &quot;Lloyds of London&quot; (1936)[1], around 25:30.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;archive.org&#x2F;details&#x2F;1936LloydsOfLondon" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;archive.org&#x2F;details&#x2F;1936LloydsOfLondon</a>')
