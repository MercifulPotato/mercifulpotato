Item(by='TeMPOraL', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606818119, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25263607, text='There&#x27;s a common category error I find grating in discussions about technology and tech companies, in which both engineers and business people are called &quot;tech people&quot;, and the label &quot;tech&quot; is extended to cover <i>business</i> of companies that sell technology products and services.<p>The consequence of that is a worryingly common rhetoric of pointing out various negative impacts of &quot;technology&quot; on society, which in fact are just age-old problems of businesses abusing people - just in this case, the businesses sell technology products and services. But I guess it&#x27;s more refreshing to say that &quot;technology is ruining society&quot; and unload it on <i>engineers</i>, instead of talking about the real problem - the same usual failure modes of capitalism that have been identified 100+ years ago.')
