Item(by='cakoose', descendants=None, kids=[25121692], score=None, time=1605600072, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25117385, text='First, &quot;innocent people can be framed&quot; is not that simple.<p>Without non-repudiation, you don&#x27;t automatically get to frame someone for whatever.  You need to provide the usual (non-DKIM) evidence of whatever you&#x27;re claiming.<p>And even with non-repudiation, you can still try and frame someone.  Not having the DKIM signature might be suspicious in some circumstances, but it doesn&#x27;t eliminate the possibility.<p>Second, &quot;innocent&quot; is not that simple.<p>I don&#x27;t want my private communication to become public, or publicly verifiable.  That doesn&#x27;t mean I&#x27;m not &quot;innocent&quot;.  This is not a fringe concept: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Nothing_to_hide_argument" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Nothing_to_hide_argument</a><p>&quot;Give me six lines written by the most honest man in the world, and I will find enough in them to hang him.&quot; - Cardinal Richelieu')
