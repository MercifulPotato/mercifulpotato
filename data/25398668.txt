Item(by='gryson', descendants=None, kids=[25398894], score=None, time=1607785916, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25398443, text='Sure, it&#x27;s somewhat rare but not that uncommon.<p>The Swedish researchers Kenneth Hyltenstam and Niclas Abrahamsson have a series of studies looking at immigrants in Sweden who began learning Swedish as adults and who are judged to be native speakers by true native speakers who heard their speech.<p>In one study, they gave a very thorough battery of linguistic tests to these adult learners, and for some of them, the only test they failed to perform equal to native speakers was on their knowledge of proverbs and idioms.<p>The place you&#x27;re most likely to find such near-native speakers is in immigrant communities.')
