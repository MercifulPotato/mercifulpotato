Item(by='askthereception', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607407812, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25335175, text='For those who are getting confused about the S and K combinators, another definition is implied by the notion of a &#x27;partial combinatory algebra&#x27; [1]. It is a set A together with a partial map A x A -&gt; A, viewed as &#x27;application&#x27;. You then require the S and K combinators to exist somewhere in A (they are non-unique) and you can prove things like the recursion theorem.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;ncatlab.org&#x2F;nlab&#x2F;show&#x2F;partial+combinatory+algebra" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;ncatlab.org&#x2F;nlab&#x2F;show&#x2F;partial+combinatory+algebra</a>')
