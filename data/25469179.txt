Item(by='MrBuddyCasino', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608311244, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25468008, text='&gt; Interesting; my strategy has been almost the polar opposite of his (&#x27;learn widely&#x27;, not &#x27;deeply&#x27;), and it served me well so far.<p>This whole kind of advice is not very useful in practise. Copying a recipe for success from a person that is wildly different in personality than you will not lead to the same outcomes.<p>If you&#x27;ve read Masters of Doom, it is evident that Carmack is not neuro-typical. Few people have the ability to concentrate like him. There is no point trying to become like him, this will only make you miserable. Don&#x27;t fall for post-hoc rationalisation: he naturally gravitated towards it and became successful. 200 years ago he might have been a scholarly monk, or a watchmaker tinkering.<p>You have to choose your <i>own</i> path. Doesn&#x27;t mean you cannot learn from the mistakes of others, but this will probably be about <i>how</i> and less about <i>what</i>.')
