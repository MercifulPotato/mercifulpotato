Item(by='jsmith99', descendants=None, kids=[24855984], score=None, time=1603355111, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24854411, text='I have ulcerative colitis so have had many many colonoscopies. General anaesthetic is never used in UK but twilight sedation is normal. I refuse this as (after an unpleasant gastroscopy) I can&#x27;t stand having tubes stuck up my orifices while I&#x27;m unable to fully intellectualise what&#x27;s happening. They&#x27;re usually happy to give a bit of fentanyl instead.<p>My experience is that GI doctors enjoy chatting during the procedure but specialist nurses (who often perform minor endoscopies) are usually not very communicative.')
