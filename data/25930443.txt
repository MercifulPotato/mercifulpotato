In the long term, the market is a weighing machine.<p>If you look at the option chains, it is clear that traders value GameStop in the long run far below the current trading price.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.nasdaq.com&#x2F;market-activity&#x2F;stocks&#x2F;gme&#x2F;option-chain" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.nasdaq.com&#x2F;market-activity&#x2F;stocks&#x2F;gme&#x2F;option-cha...</a><p>A November 2021 &quot;put&quot; at the present market price of ~$360, the right to sell GameStop stock in November at $360,  is selling for about $300.<p>For comparison, a January 2022 put at Microsoft&#x27;s present market price of ~$235 is selling for ~$30.<p>GameStop is presently a cafeteria food-fight. It will end, a lot of people will be sad, everyone will remember the story, and GameStop stock will eventually better-correlate with business performance.<p><i>Edit: Indeed, the fact that one can buy puts for so cheap is interesting, as GameStop is probably &quot;worth&quot; $30 or less at present.
