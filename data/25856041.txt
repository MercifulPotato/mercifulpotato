It&#x27;s not necessary, for instance, that the person being referred to by the kid is &quot;known&quot;. It&#x27;s quite common, for example, for kids to address an unknown stranger on the street using &quot;uncle&quot; or &quot;aunty&quot; - it&#x27;s merely a respectful way of addressing an older person.<p>2. When used by an adult, it can take on one of two tones:<p>a) The genuinely respectful tone, similar to (1)<p>b) A derogatory way to taunt a slightly older person on account of their age, using outdated practices, etc.<p>The way it&#x27;s used in THIS article, borders on 2(b) - taunting an older generation for sending good morning messages, etc.')
