Item(by='beerandt', descendants=None, kids=[25695739], score=None, time=1610157256, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25692680, text='It&#x27;s the equal protection amendment.<p>The meaning of it has been &quot;stretched&quot; a bit (at least from the pov of a textualist&#x2F;originalist) to constitutionally justify civil rights laws applied to individuals&#x2F;businesses, not just state governments.<p>So it&#x27;s &quot;true meaning&quot; is often debated, but as I understand it:<p>If civil rights laws can be&#x2F; are interpreted to apply to individuals and businesses via the 14th (which they are), it&#x27;s hard to also square that diversity of political opinion is not also protected, given that it&#x27;s the ultimate civil right and we&#x27;re a democratic-republic.<p>From wiki: &quot;nor shall any State ... deny to any person within its jurisdiction the equal protection of the laws&quot;. It mandates that individuals in similar situations be treated equally by the law.')
