Item(by='diggan', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605275154, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25081973, text='I think the point is if you charge upfront for storing peoples data under a certain price plan, you&#x27;re less likely to change anything regarding the deal because you&#x27;re in the business of storing peoples data. If you do bad, people will stop using the service, and you lose your business. You&#x27;re more aligned with your users.<p>Compare it to Google, where they can operate under a loss for a particular service (like Google Photos) in order to increase profits in a different market (like AI recognition tools). Once they are done with whatever they wanted to do, they can close down the service without much consequence, the business lives on.')
