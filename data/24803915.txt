Item(by='moron4hire', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602874865, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24802168, text='While it is true that English is mutable across time and non-standardized across cultures, for an individual learning English as their first language, they are learning a snapshot of it that is--from their perspective--immutable and standardized.<p>The article&#x27;s argument is the lack of <i>consistency</i>, not standardization. Unless you are arguing that children--in whom dyslexia first manifests--are being exposed to the vast diversity of English across time and space, then there can be no doubt that mutability and lack of unified culture contributes nothing to dyslexia.')
