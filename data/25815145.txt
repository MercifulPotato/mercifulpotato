Item(by='IfOnlyYouKnew', descendants=None, kids=[25818832], score=None, time=1610918944, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25815044, text='No, office cannot have cameras (in Germany).<p>The legal framework is rather easy (and hasn&#x27;t changed with GDPR, as far as I can tell): video surveillance is generally illegal. There are specific circumstances where it is legal, and those tend to require two things:<p>- there needs to be some &quot;risk&quot; that is higher than usual, and where video surveillance can make a difference.<p>- it&#x27;s <i>extremely</i> hard to justify surveillance of spaces where people regularly spend long amounts of time, such as their workplace.<p>Generic offices fail both tests: what, exactly, are you looking for? Stapler thefts? And, at the same time, the intrusion is rather is far more significant than, say, a public transit station that you pass through.')
