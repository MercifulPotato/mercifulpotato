<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;schema.org&#x2F;Book" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;schema.org&#x2F;Book</a> (edition)<p>Here&#x27;s works&#x2F;editions in Wikidata: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.wikidata.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Q47461344" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.wikidata.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Q47461344</a> (work) ; <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.wikidata.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Q3331189" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.wikidata.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Q3331189</a> (edition) (e.g.: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.wikidata.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Q19320911" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.wikidata.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Q19320911</a> )<p>All of these are loosely based on the FRBR model in librarian science, where a work is a work, and an edition is called an &quot;Expression&quot;: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Functional_Requirements_for_Bibliographic_Records" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Functional_Requirements_for_Bi...</a><p>And yeah, there&#x27;s no isbn-analog for series; each site has its own identifier.<p>Also as a side-note, ISBNs are very inadequate as a unique identifier for an edition; there are frequent cases where an ISBN will be re-used for books with different covers (Dover classics does this a bunch); some modern books published in odd ways are not given an ISBN (e.g. all of these books: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;sorted.club&#x2F;books&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;sorted.club&#x2F;books&#x2F;</a> ); and, most importantly, books published before ISBNs went into effect (~1970) have _no way_ of being accessible when using ISBNs as a unique key.')
