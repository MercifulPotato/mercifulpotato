Item(by='ignotos', descendants=None, kids=[25548515], score=None, time=1609025467, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25535845, text='He&#x27;s not really talking about abstraction or level of detail.<p>He&#x27;s talking about how the &quot;magical&quot;, naive, imagination-based ways we understand things when we are young colour the way we perceive them we we get older.<p>Even if we know, rationally, that the way we used to think about something was incorrect, and conflicts with what we know to be true as adults, we don&#x27;t discard those old ideas entirely, and they remain internalised to some extent:<p><i>The point is that all the earlier versions, even those as patently false as the one with the stork, are not discarded completely. Something of them remains in us; they mesh with succeeding versions and somehow continue to exist</i><p>I actually think it&#x27;s at least a little more profound than you give it credit for.')
