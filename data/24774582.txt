Item(by='Reelin', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602665806, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24771214, text='No? Both terms are interpreted literally.<p>If you offer something that you never intended to deliver is that not false advertising? It really doesn&#x27;t matter how you go about denying the delivery. It doesn&#x27;t matter that any <i>given</i> termination is legally in the clear. All that matters (IMO) is that you offered something when you knew up front that you were never going to deliver it.<p>And yes, the above would mean that marketing almost any service as &quot;unlimited&quot; would be false advertising. I do actually think that practice should be actively disallowed by regulators. I really don&#x27;t think that having slick marketing terms is more important that having offerings that are truthfully and accurately advertised. A functional marketplace absolutely depends on the ability of participants to readily understand the services being offered.')
