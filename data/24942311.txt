Item(by='gattr', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604066856, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24940893, text='To add some detail: a common minimum quality criterion for optical surfaces is λ&#x2F;4 (divergence from an ideal surface), where λ is the wavelength used. For visible light this gives about 100 nm (though large professional telescopes are &quot;figured&quot; to more like λ&#x2F;20). Any alleged glass creep visible in window panes over a few hundred years would quickly ruin any astronomical mirror (which definitely does not happen).')
