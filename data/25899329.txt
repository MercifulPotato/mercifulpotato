Item(by='bobby_bob', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611550179, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25898989, text='The &quot;generally understood&quot; purpose of prison is for:<p>retribution, incapacitation, deterrence and rehabilitation<p>The US skews a more towards the retributive (i.e. &quot;just punishment&quot;) than other jurisdictions. For example, the federal sentencing guidelines explicitly prohibit reduced sentences in order to facilitate rehabilitation (except drug and alcohol rehabilitation programs).<p>It&#x27;s easy enough to imagine a society where prison has a significantly less retribution focused, instead focusing on how a criminal can be rehabilitated (education opportunities, community corrections orders e.t.c). Popular example being some Nordic prisons.')
