Item(by='emmanueloga_', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604098348, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24944496, text='I feel like the number one reason to use XML is the mixed content. This is also why XML and derived formats (the xml-y parts of HTML) are so good as markup languages.<p>So this:<p><pre><code>    p { \n      &quot;mixed&quot;\n      img href=&quot;test.png&quot;\n      &quot;content&quot;\n    }\n</code></pre>\nis worse than:<p><pre><code>    &lt;p&gt;mixed&lt;img href=&quot;test&quot; &#x2F;&gt;content&lt;&#x2F;p&gt;\n</code></pre>\n... at least as markup, since having to wrap all text in quotes is tedious.<p>The other language I know of that provides &quot;natural&quot; mixed content is scribble [1], but I think the only implementation that I know of is the racket one, since it is so tied to racket semantics.<p>If you take mixed content away from XML, then you may as well use one of the myriad of existing IDL languages, no?<p>1: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;docs.racket-lang.org&#x2F;scribble&#x2F;getting-started.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;docs.racket-lang.org&#x2F;scribble&#x2F;getting-started.html</a>')
