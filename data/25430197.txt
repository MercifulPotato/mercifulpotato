Item(by='HatchedLake721', descendants=None, kids=[25430406], score=None, time=1608039937, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25429948, text='Sorry, did you read Mozilla&#x27;s blog post or aware of what IDFA is and it&#x27;s history?<p>Apple released IDFA to improve consumer privacy, to avoid advertisers being able to fingerprint iOS devices using Unique Device ID (UDID).<p>Access to UDID was deprecated in iOS 5 (that&#x27;s 2011).<p>It has also given control to consumers since then to opt-out of providing IDFA by default.<p>In 2019, Mozilla asked Apple to take rotate the IDFA every month.<p>But Apple has taken it even further, which Mozilla publicly applauded <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;foundation.mozilla.org&#x2F;en&#x2F;blog&#x2F;applause-for-apples-idfa-decision&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;foundation.mozilla.org&#x2F;en&#x2F;blog&#x2F;applause-for-apples-i...</a><p>Apple is now making IDFA disabled by default and requiring users to enable it if they want to.<p>&gt; Apple went even further than what Mozilla supporters had asked for when it announced that it will give consumers the option to opt-out of tracking in each app, essentially turning off IDFA and giving millions of consumers more privacy online.')
