Item(by='gen220', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608270503, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25463991, text='I think 300 miles is around the inflection point where, if the transportation systems are mature and trafficked enough, it’s hard to nail down an ideal form of travel.<p>It’s far too long for a taxi, too short for the plane to be obvious, too short for a drive to be overly boring, and too short for a train to be mind-numbing.<p>Any longer (500+), I think the plane gets the obvious advantage. Any shorter and you wouldn’t consider a plane.<p>I guess my point is that, at this point in my life, I’d only want to fly to avoid a reliably 6+hour drive (NYC to Augusta ME, for an example).\nEven then it’s a toss up, depending on how much stuff I’m bringing with me.')
