Item(by='pbourke', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609006510, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25544225, text='&gt; In the realm of software engineering I don&#x27;t know that we&#x27;ve had a catastrophic failure where the infrastructure costs of repair were in the billions or there was tangible loss of life.<p>The multi-day 2003 blackout in the Northeast US states and Ontario, Canada qualifies in terms of impact (many billions of $). It was a systemic flaw with a software bug as a proximate cause. I think most major failures with high impact in a software-intensive system will sort of look similar: a software fault that cascades to the real world.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.m.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Northeast_blackout_of_2003" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.m.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Northeast_blackout_of_2003</a><p>Other examples might be the various outages in airline reservation systems.<p>If you’re interested in this kind of thing, the archives of the Usenet newsgroup comp.risks are interesting (not sure if it’s still active).')
