Item(by='gamblor956', descendants=None, kids=[25366613, 25366600, 25368055], score=None, time=1607552282, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25365505, text='Walmart paid for the products on their shelves. Amazon does not pay for most of the products you can buy on Amazon.<p>The difference, in case it&#x27;s not clear: Walmart is the manufacturers&#x27; primary customer, so it&#x27;s irrelevant from their point of view what products Walmart chooses to sell in its stores.<p>The third-party sellers are the manufacturer&#x27;s customers. The manufacturers still get paid either way. But we&#x27;re not concerned about the manufacturers; the antitrust issue is with the <i>third party sellers</i>. Since Amazon uses their data to come up with competing products that are sold in the same marketplace, it matters a great deal that Amazon competes with them, and how.')
