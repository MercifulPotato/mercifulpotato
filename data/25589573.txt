Item(by='halfmatthalfcat', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609388631, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25589467, text='Becoming a SME in, let’s say Typescript (or for any statically typed language), for example easily takes 2-3 years unless you really excelled in type theory in college. Knowing the ins and outs of advanced types, generics, conditionals, etc and also having implemented these things to a degree where you can do it in your sleep.<p>No way a junior would be able to do that. Seniors have deep knowledge of the systems&#x2F;technologies that they work in and are able to articulate that to more senior management on why using that particular thing is the way to go.<p>Obviously you want seniors to have more than just one (for both their sake and the organization) but it’s a mark that they’ve attained at least one technology that they’ve “leveled up” so to speak.')
