They are a hard bargainer (in the sense that they are attempting to push the needle as much to their side as they can, rather than find something equitable). They are the person coming in to the yard sale, seeing the sign that says &quot;$5&quot;, and saying &quot;I&#x27;ll give you 30 cents for it&quot;.<p>You don&#x27;t move the needle back by being kind and compassionate with those sorts of people. You move it back by using whatever leverage you have. We don&#x27;t know what leverage the OP has, but his only defense is to use it to either trigger a real buyout, to get the other investor on his side, to risk tanking the company, to say &quot;no&quot; in the belief the co-founder won&#x27;t tank the company in response, or some other option we&#x27;re unaware of.<p>In the same way you won&#x27;t make headway trying to explain to that lowballer at the yard sale why $5 is more than fair, you won&#x27;t make any headway trying to explain to someone who has come out of the gate with something so starkly unfair as the OP presents why their proposal is unfair. They know.')
