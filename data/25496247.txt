Item(by='Balgair', descendants=None, kids=[25498370], score=None, time=1608567515, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25494710, text='Aside: Consulting and mentoring share some things in common. One thing that is gold for a consultant is to be sitting in a meeting and have the client repeat your ideas, word for word, back to the execs. and claim the idea as their own. To outright &#x27;steal&#x27; the idea and get all the credit. It means that the client actually listens to you and thinks your ideas are good [0]. The same mechanism is true with mentoring. If your mentee nearly parrots your ideas, you&#x27;re gold. If they continue to do bull-headed things, you&#x27;re sunk; you must how you mentor as it&#x27;s clearly not working [1].<p>[0] This means you can raise your rates without fear, btw.<p>[1] To be clear, mentoring is a <i>lot</i> more than just this. Whole books have been written about mentoring, read them.')
