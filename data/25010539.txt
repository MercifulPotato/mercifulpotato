Item(by='brlewis', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604692211, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25009396, text='&gt; Who doesn&#x27;t have an auto increment Id field on every table?<p>In large organizations with a lot of teams, natural primary keys make interoperability easier across databases, as opposed to a forest of artificial keys that are all different. SQL experts who advocate natural keys point to ON UPDATE CASCADE as the solution to natural keys that occasionally change.<p>So the absence of an auto increment Id field might be a dumb error, or might be super smart, or might be both. Both means you&#x27;re a SQL expert but haven&#x27;t made sure everybody else working on the project always will be.')
