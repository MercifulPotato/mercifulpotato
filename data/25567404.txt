Item(by='dredmorbius', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609224482, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25562640, text='Launch costs are falling, though (and despite being proved wrong several times by Musk) $10&#x2F;kg Lunar sounds delusional.  Consider that typical bulk freight costs on Earth are in the range of $0.40&#x2F;tonne-day (180,000 DWT Capesize vessel at $48,000&#x2F;day <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.easyship.com&#x2F;blog&#x2F;bulk-shipping-for-delivery" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.easyship.com&#x2F;blog&#x2F;bulk-shipping-for-delivery</a>), or roughly $5&#x2F;tonne for a 14 day transoceanic voyage (8,000 mi, 12,000 km, very roughly).<p>Only twice that to Lunar surface is ... dubious.<p><i>In situ</i> construction is an option, and while asteroids <i>can</i> be metal-rich, useful in fabricating condenser and evaporator coils, they tend to be refrigerant poor.  Substances which have properties of good refrigerants (boiling and volatility at low pressures and temperatures) are poorly retained by microgravity bodies in vacuum.')
