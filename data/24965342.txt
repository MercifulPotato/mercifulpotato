Item(by='tgamblin', descendants=None, kids=[24965459], score=None, time=1604299784, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24965104, text='It&#x27;s there because on some systems all the args are passed as one.  -S splits them out.  You can try this on Linux and env even warns you about it:<p><pre><code>    # cat test.py\n    #!&#x2F;usr&#x2F;bin&#x2F;python3\n    import sys\n    print(sys.argv)\n\n    $ cat test\n    #!&#x2F;usr&#x2F;bin&#x2F;env test.py foo bar baz\n\n    $ PATH=. .&#x2F;test   \n    &#x2F;usr&#x2F;bin&#x2F;env: &#x27;test.py foo bar baz&#x27;: No such file or directory\n    &#x2F;usr&#x2F;bin&#x2F;env: use -[v]S to pass options in shebang lines\n</code></pre>\nwith -S, env does the right thing, and you get this:<p><pre><code>    $ cat test \n    #!&#x2F;usr&#x2F;bin&#x2F;env -S test.py foo bar baz\n\n    $ PATH=. .&#x2F;test\n    [&#x27;.&#x2F;test.py&#x27;, &#x27;foo&#x27;, &#x27;bar&#x27;, &#x27;baz&#x27;, &#x27;.&#x2F;test&#x27;]</code></pre>')
