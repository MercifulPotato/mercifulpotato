Item(by='room500', descendants=None, kids=[24725029], score=None, time=1602194568, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24724187, text='To be fair, this can apply to a lot of young companies.<p>Fundamentally, the job to work in a startup is different from the job to work at a large company. The people that grew a company when it was small <i>might</i> not be the best people to grow a company when it becomes bigger.<p>I saw this at a previous company where the OGs were convinced they were untouchable because they created the entire technical solution with a handful of engineers. To their credit, it was an amazing accomplishment. Unfortunately, as the company grew, their solution ran into scalability and reliability concerns that couldn&#x27;t be easily fixed. In every architecture meeting, there was always the disagreements between team &quot;ten years ago, this product would have already been shipped with half the engineers&quot; and  team &quot;but that product wouldn&#x27;t work with the scale we need - it is an exponentially harder problem now&quot;')
