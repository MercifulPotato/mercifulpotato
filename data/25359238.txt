From this paper, here&#x27;s the poem in Ancient Greek and three translations:<p><pre><code>    ἐδυκε μὲν ἀ σελάννα\n    καὶ πληΐαδες μέσαι δε \n    νύκτες, πα ὰ δ‘ ἔ χετ‘ ὤ α, \n    ἔγω δὲ μόνα κατεύδω\n\n    The Moon hath left the sky; \n    Lost is the Pleiads‘ light;\n    It is midnight\n    And time slips by;\n    But on my couch alone I lie. \n    (Symonds, 1873–1876).\n\n    The moon has set, and the Pleiades;\n    it is midnight,\n    the time is going by, \n    and I sleep alone. \n    (Wharton, 1887: 68).\n\n    The silver moon is set;\n    The Pleiades are gone;\n    Half the long night is spent, and yet \n    I lie alone.\n    (Merivale, 1838: 226).\n</code></pre>\nThere has been criticism on the methodology used, e.g. (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;rogueclassicism.com&#x2F;2016&#x2F;05&#x2F;16&#x2F;problems-with-the-scientific-dating-of-sapphos-midnight-poem&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;rogueclassicism.com&#x2F;2016&#x2F;05&#x2F;16&#x2F;problems-with-the-sci...</a>).
