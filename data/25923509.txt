Item(by='mceachen', descendants=None, kids=[25923543], score=None, time=1611714025, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25923459, text='Great question: it&#x27;s for expandability.<p>If you run RAID-1 in a 2-slot NAS, and you run out of space, you&#x27;ve got two options:<p>1) buy another NAS<p>2) move off of RAID, and have 2 distinct volumes. You&#x27;ll have twice the storage, but no spindle redundancy.<p>If you&#x27;ve got 4 or 5 slots, you can throw in 2 larger disks, and use the prior disks either for backup, or use the new disks for incremental additional storage. You&#x27;ve just got a ton more flexibility.')
