See for example OPAQUE:<p>&gt; <i>Currently, the most widely deployed (PKI-free) aPAKE is SRP [RFC2945], which is vulnerable to pre-computation attacks, lacks a proof of security, and is less efficient relative to OPAQUE. Moreover, SRP requires a ring as it mixes addition and multiplication operations, and thus does not work over plain elliptic curves. OPAQUE is therefore a suitable replacement for applications that use SRP.</i><p>&gt; <i>This draft complies with the requirements for PAKE protocols set forth in [RFC8125].</i><p>* <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;tools.ietf.org&#x2F;html&#x2F;draft-irtf-cfrg-opaque" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;tools.ietf.org&#x2F;html&#x2F;draft-irtf-cfrg-opaque</a><p>More on PAKE generally:<p>* <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;blog.cryptographyengineering.com&#x2F;2018&#x2F;10&#x2F;19&#x2F;lets-talk-about-pake&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;blog.cryptographyengineering.com&#x2F;2018&#x2F;10&#x2F;19&#x2F;lets-tal...</a><p>* <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=18259393" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=18259393</a><p>If you&#x27;re looking for a challenge-response system for your application, then it&#x27;s hard to go wrong with SCRAM (which Postgres went with a while ago):<p>* <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Salted_Challenge_Response_Authentication_Mechanism" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Salted_Challenge_Response_Auth...</a>')
