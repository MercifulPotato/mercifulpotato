Item(by='s1artibartfast', descendants=None, kids=[25798161], score=None, time=1610752816, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25796559, text='In most union workplaces, one of the first contract demands is exclusivity, where the employer can only hire members. This is referred to as a &quot; closed shop&quot; [1] When a closed shop forms, works have a choice to join or leave. Some places allow non-union workers, but they must pay part of their salary to the union. This is called and &quot;agency shop&quot; [2]<p>For example, if you become a police officer and do not choose to join the union, you must still pay fees which are spent by the union on political lobbying.<p>I am generally pro union but think that all unions should be optional to the workers.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Closed_shop" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Closed_shop</a><p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Agency_shop" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Agency_shop</a><p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.nolo.com&#x2F;legal-encyclopedia&#x2F;free-books&#x2F;employee-rights-book&#x2F;chapter15-5.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.nolo.com&#x2F;legal-encyclopedia&#x2F;free-books&#x2F;employee-...</a>')
