Item(by='MaxBarraclough', descendants=None, kids=[25804620], score=None, time=1610809099, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25802187, text='I tried out Svelte recently. I really like the idea of a &#x27;compiler first&#x27; framework which offers useful features that compile away to plain old lightweight JavaScript. I got on ok with the bare-bones Svelte compiler, but I found the surrounding ecosystem to be lacking.<p>There are two different material design libraries for Svelte. [0][1] I wasn&#x27;t able to get either of them to behave. It looked like they weren&#x27;t being kept up to date with Svelte, or else the documentation was inadequate.<p>I then gave Angular a go, and encountered no such issues. Angular has the added bonus that its material design library [2] is very mature and well polished.<p>[0] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;smeltejs.com&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;smeltejs.com&#x2F;</a><p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;sveltematerialui.com&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;sveltematerialui.com&#x2F;</a><p>[2] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;material.angular.io&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;material.angular.io&#x2F;</a>')
