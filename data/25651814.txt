Item(by='WorldMaker', descendants=None, kids=[25652318], score=None, time=1609884277, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25650168, text='The US spends 2&#x2F;3rds of a year in DST rather than Standard Time, so that would possibly imply there is no health difference or the health difference is actually weighted towards DST at this point.<p>Also, a problem with hour-wide timezones is that DST is closer to what &quot;standard time&quot; is supposed to be (when solar noon and observed noon most closely match) naturally on the western edges of the timezones. So any arguments over whether DST or Standard is &quot;healthier&quot; also likely have to take into account how much the population is weighted to one edge or the other.')
