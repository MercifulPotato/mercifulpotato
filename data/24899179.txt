Item(by='rektide', descendants=None, kids=[24899231], score=None, time=1603735868, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24896003, text='For the last half decade, almost all apps have been deployed with webpack&#x2F;browserify&#x2F;other bundles. All the assets get smashed together into a big custom bundle that doesn&#x27;t get cached across sites.<p>This has been really sad &amp; a big loss for the web, in my opinion. And it&#x27;s one that we were about to emerge from[1], it seems like.<p>Alas, if we do go back to a more old-school CDN-based style of web scripting&#x2F;javascripting, powered by our new ES Modules (&amp; hopefully Import Maps) this new sharding-by-origin change will mean that we will never ever see the CDN hit-rate benefits we once saw.<p>It seems like it is a necessary change, to protect the user from being tracked, but it still hurts my heart so much, that we are so near to getting back to sharing resources on the web, only to have all that sharing snatched away. Whatever metrics you are looking at today, know that they represent a very sad state of affairs, that brought great pain &amp; suffering to the hearts of many webdevs who aspired for much much much higher hit rates.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.bryanbraun.com&#x2F;2020&#x2F;10&#x2F;23&#x2F;es-modules-in-production-my-experience-so-far&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.bryanbraun.com&#x2F;2020&#x2F;10&#x2F;23&#x2F;es-modules-in-producti...</a>')
