It seems somewhat naive to say that every person with a billion in their bank account has built something and always is the best allocator of capital.<p>Oil and Gas billionaires leverage the fact that society hasn&#x27;t figured out how to factor in negative environmental externalities.  Consumer Staples billionaires leverage their supply chain monopolies and labor monopolies.<p>The original tech billionaires leveraged their early knowledge of computers&#x2F;internet.  Web 2.0&#x2F;3.0 billionaires leverage the fact that society hasn&#x27;t figured out how to appropriately regulate stuff that&#x27;s similar but slightly different from their old school regulated counterparts (i.e. Uber, Airbnb).<p>In general, leveraging technology capabilities seems less exploitive than leveraging human&#x2F;environmental resources&#x2F;lack of regulation.')
