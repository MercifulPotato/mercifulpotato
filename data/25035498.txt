Item(by='elorant', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604934409, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25034717, text='I live in Greece and only buy seasonal produce because everything out of its season is choke full of pesticides, plus they don&#x27;t taste the same. For example tomatoes in September are sour, while if you buy them during February they&#x27;re sweeter. You can also tell by their shape, produce grown in a greenhouse looks in a far better shape than the one grown in the field, because the latter is exposed to weather condition and it takes a beat on its surface&#x2F;appearance. As a general rule of thumb, the more uniform a vegetable&#x2F;fruit appears the less tasty it is.<p>Other than that, there are plenty of guides on the web regarding what to buy when. Just make a search for &quot;seasonal vegetables and fruits&quot; and you&#x27;ll find a lot of infographics.')
