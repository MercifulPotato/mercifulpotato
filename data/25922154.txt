Item(by='megous', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611702797, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25920620, text='Not sarcastic. SUID root binaries are a prime target for hackers, and even I&#x27;ve used them to gain local privilege escalation in  the past. For example authors of VEGA5000 payment terminal made pppd used for GPRS internet connection have a suid root permissions, and pppd can be used to execute other programs just based on the command line arguments. So that was that.<p>sudo looks quite complicated, just by looking at what it has to parse and validate (sudoers file). I&#x27;d rather not have it on the server, and just use custom purpose made static built suid binaries for necessary minimal purposes of privilege granting.<p>It&#x27;s not even installed by default on some popular Linux distros. Of course I realize that it&#x27;s very ubiquitous, regardless of this. And it&#x27;s probably fine to use on your workstation. But leaving it unattended on some server feels a bit dreadful.')
