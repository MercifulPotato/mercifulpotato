Item(by='ska', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603476624, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24871882, text='It does in my experience.<p>This may be biased by my academic background where a sabbatical year is typically one where you would have no teaching duties so were free to travel to another university to work with a new group, explore a new field, finish a manuscript, etc.  Expectation is that you are working, but not your routine work.<p>I&#x27;ve heard it used similarly outside academic contexts and when it was not something you were contractually guaranteed  - e.g. &quot;I took a year off my job to finish my book&quot; , but never in the sense of &quot;I took a year off my job to go surfing&quot;.<p>So i guess I think of it as a formalized break from your work routine where you do something else productive with the expectation of going back to that work afterwards.<p>Of course &quot;productive&quot; here is a bit slippery.')
