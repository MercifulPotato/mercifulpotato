Item(by='InvisibleUp', descendants=None, kids=[25567365, 25565289, 25566368, 25565451], score=None, time=1609199039, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25564404, text='I think a large part of this (in the USA, anyways) is due to how modern cities are structured.<p>It used to be, 100 or 200 years ago, that your workplace, your home, and your usual hang-out place were all within reasonable walking distance of each other. It wasn&#x27;t a huge deal to go out and meet new people, as you&#x27;d naturally bump into them every day.<p>Nowadays we have a large portion of people in bedroom communities, isolated in a sea of houses. They drive in isolated cars to their workplaces, swing by to get some Starbucks on the way in, and that&#x27;s the extent of their social interaction. Apart from libraries and parks, there&#x27;s not many places that you can regularly go and just &quot;hang out&quot; without spending money just to exist in that space. It&#x27;s <i>incredibly</i> easy to go for weeks or months without any substantial social contact outside of work or your few neighbors.<p>I feel like this is a reason why internet organizations and social media have exploded in popularity, for that matter. It&#x27;s the new &quot;default place&quot; to hang out and get social interaction, for better or for worse.<p>(I expanded on this in an article I wrote, <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;invisibleup.com&#x2F;articles&#x2F;31&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;invisibleup.com&#x2F;articles&#x2F;31&#x2F;</a>, if you want to check that out.)')
