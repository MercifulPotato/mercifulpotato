Item(by='ryanianian', descendants=None, kids=[25625610, 25625538], score=None, time=1609708569, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25625166, text='Sure you can hard-code intcmp into qsort but then it would only work for arrays of ints.<p>You could do some macro magic instead of templates e.g. `DEFINE_QSORT(int, intcmp)` which could stamp out `qsort_int` but that&#x27;s not a part of the stdlib.<p>C++ arguably gets this right since sort&lt;int&gt; and sort&lt;string&gt; will be separate functions, although templates are of course a footgun. And of course duping the logic for std::sort&lt;T&gt; for a bunch of different T impls increases the binary size.')
