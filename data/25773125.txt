The importance of these goods might be as important as the goods produced by people living in large cities (services for instance, being produced by many people).<p>If we start considering the people living in an area as the likely maintainers and experts of their field, I could imagine that we might want to assign the importance of votes based on the total importance of the &quot;produced goods&quot; in that area. That way these &quot;maintainers&quot; have a say in politics relative to their actual importance in society. I guess?')
