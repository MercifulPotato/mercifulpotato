You couldn&#x27;t give placebo MDMA to a group of 50 people without the majority of them calling your bluff.<p>The problem then becomes the &quot;nocebo&quot; effect, wherein the patients who know they haven&#x27;t been dosed with a powerful psychoactive substance assume that their treatment will be ineffective, and therefore stop giving it the required effort.<p>With therapy, it&#x27;s important that the patient be onboard with and believe in the therapy. Giving people obvious placebos is a reliable way to lower expectations and instill doubt.')
