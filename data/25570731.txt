Item(by='jtsiskin', descendants=None, kids=[25572445, 25571838], score=None, time=1609256312, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25570551, text='Here’s the bullet points:<p>Eat these:<p>• Vegetables of all types—dark green; red and orange; beans, peas, and lentils; starchy; and other vegetables<p>• Fruits, especially whole fruit<p>• Grains, at least half of which are whole grain<p>• Dairy, including fat-free or low-fat milk, yogurt, and cheese, and&#x2F;or lactose-free versions and fortified soy beverages and yogurt as alternatives<p>• Protein foods, including lean meats, poultry, and eggs; seafood; beans, peas, and lentils; and nuts, seeds, and soy products<p>• Oils, including vegetable oils and oils in food, such as seafood and nuts<p>Don’t eat these:<p>• Added sugars—Less than 10 percent of calories per day starting at age 2. Avoid foods and beverages with added sugars for those younger than age 2.<p>• Saturated fat—Less than 10 percent of calories per day starting at age 2.<p>• Sodium—Less than 2,300 milligrams per day—and even less for children younger than age 14.<p>• Alcoholic beverages—Adults of legal drinking age can choose not to drink, or to drink in moderation by limiting intake to 2 drinks or less in a day for men and 1 drink or less in a day for women, when alcohol is consumed. Drinking less is better for health than drinking more. There are some adults who should not drink alcohol, such as women who are pregnant.')
