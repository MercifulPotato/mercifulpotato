Item(by='webmaven', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608224858, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25453221, text='<i>&gt; it just seems so easy to find counterexamples to racist ideas. hate black people? surely i can introduce you to one who is pretty cool. hate whites? they aren&#x27;t all bad.</i><p>The problem here is the presentation of a counterexample in this way is casting it as an <i>exception</i>, and implicitly confirming the validity of the negative stereotype as the default assumption:<p>This is also the underlying problem with certain compliments, eg. &quot;Wow, you&#x27;re a really good developer (unlike most women)!&quot;, &quot;You&#x27;re so well spoken (unlike most Black people)!&quot;, and so on.<p>Given the human ability to rationalize, even a never-ending flood of in-your-face counterexamples is more likely to provoke reactions like &quot;Well, women are just less talented developers on average, of course there are exceptions&quot;, and so on.')
