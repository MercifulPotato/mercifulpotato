Item(by='muneeb', descendants=None, kids=[25787663], score=None, time=1610684635, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25782367, text='I&#x27;d argue the inverse.<p>It&#x27;s easier to build more complex things on top of simpler primitives than vice versa. So a simpler base layer (say TCP&#x2F;IP for the internet) opens the doors to more complex things on top (say http or JavaScript).<p>You don&#x27;t want a Solidity bug (and there are many) to mess with the supply or ownership of Bitcoin. The limited&#x2F;simple scripting language is there for a reason. It&#x27;s easy to add complexity and more functionality at Bitcoin base layer: the decision to not do it is the hard (but in my view the right) choice.')
