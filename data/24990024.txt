Item(by='raesene9', descendants=None, kids=[24990932], score=None, time=1604504669, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24988227, text='I&#x27;m always slightly dubious of full automated penetration testing tools, particulary ones that state things like<p>&quot;It is non-intrusive, with no impact on your network.&quot; (from <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.guardicore.com&#x2F;infectionmonkey&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.guardicore.com&#x2F;infectionmonkey&#x2F;</a>)<p>Having a quick look at the site some of the modules it can use are brute-force password guessers which could  cause account lockouts quite easily, depending on how they&#x27;re used.<p>Also in my moderately long experience of pentesting (~15 years) even quite innocuous seeming actions can negatively impact systems under review (I&#x27;ve knocked over whole networks with standard port scanning, taken out a website with a &#x27; character in a login box, seen another website taken out by being spidered etc)<p>This isn&#x27;t to knock this tool in particular, just expressing a general wariness of tooling which claims to be able to fully automate a process and do so with zero risk.')
