Item(by='jszymborski', descendants=None, kids=[25481643], score=None, time=1608403369, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25479694, text='I&#x27;m a big fan of trying new search engines for academic research; I hopefully am more likely to break out of whatever search bubble I&#x27;m unaware I&#x27;m in.<p>This one in particular had some very nice features, some of which are present in Semantic Scholar (my current favourite) but some which are certainly not.<p>Recommending papers based on citation graphs is a good way to very quickly get up to speed with fields I&#x27;m not to familiar with, but I&#x27;m always wary that I&#x27;ll end up back in the feedback loop of very few popular papers rising to the top while perfectly good papers go unseen because they weren&#x27;t well cited in the year they were written.<p>So I&#x27;ll certainly keep an eye on this and give it a try, but I&#x27;m certainly still in the market for a &quot;serendipity&quot; slider on such recommendation engines.')
