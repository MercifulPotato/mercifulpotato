Item(by='msla', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603396408, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24859469, text='Ideally, running on a hypervisor is absolutely identical to running on bare metal, and any difference the guest can detect is a bug in the hypervisor and&#x2F;or the hardware. That was the value proposition of CP-40 back in the 1960s, right down to being able to run newer versions of CP (later called VM) as a guest on older versions, in order to develop and debug the next generation of hypervisor in a known-good environment which is, again, utterly indistinguishable from running on bare metal.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;IBM_CP-40" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;IBM_CP-40</a><p>I wonder how long it&#x27;ll take the rest of the world to achieve that.')
