Item(by='ZephyrBlu', descendants=None, kids=[24672262, 24672573, 24672456, 24672723, 24672579, 24672673], score=None, time=1601734459, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24671655, text='Accepting and learning from mistakes means you must first admit you did something wrong.<p>It&#x27;s hard to admit you did something wrong because of what it implies.<p>Am I stupid? Am I not as skilled as I think I am? Did I miss something? Perhaps all of the above?<p>I think this is hard for smart&#x2F;perfectionist people because we think we should get things right, and this is likely to be tied to our ego&#x2F;identity.<p>But I&#x27;ve come to the conclusion that humans are just fundamentally stupid, because even extremely intelligent people fuck things up all the time.<p>Not sure if it&#x27;s the right thing to conclude from my observations, but it does make failure easier to deal with since I can remind myself that yep, I&#x27;m still just a dumb ape.')
