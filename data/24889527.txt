Item(by='throwanem', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603656959, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24888464, text='There are a lot of large insects that bear a superficial resemblance to V. mandarinia, especially in the absence of familiarity with hymenopterans. From WSDA&#x27;s map of citizen sightings, it looks like the most commonly mistaken animals are large sawflies, which are very distant relatives of wasps and hornets and actually serve a significant ecological role by parasitizing the larvae of various tree-boring beetles.')
