Item(by='quesera', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607023133, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25291435, text='I&#x27;m thinking more from the parser side.<p>Being liberal in what you accept is fine, but being strict in what you emit requires extra mental overhead.<p>It&#x27;s nothing cataclysmic (and &quot;on the developer&quot; is a often a reasonable place to add the extra overhead) but in this case it seems like selecting 0 instead of O or o, and 1 instead of I or l, would have been more straightforward.<p>Selecting &quot;1&quot; (ASCII 49), and &quot;o&quot; (ASCII 111) is inconsistent and feels odd.<p>EDIT: OK, I can think of one possible justification: with zero disallowed, a leading zero can never be lost. In a short Base58 string, it&#x27;s not unusual for all characters to be numeric, and some overzealous readers will interpret the whole value as an integer. See also: hex strings, US ZIP codes, ABA routing numbers, etc, vs Microsoft Excel. :)')
