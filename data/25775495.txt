Item(by='BlackFly', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610626573, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25774758, text='There are directly legal and historical bases for forcing companies to give someone a &quot;platform&quot; to speak, as I have already stated: telephones, mail, and public forums.<p>The debate is about how much social media resembles those communication media and how much of the philosophy of treatment in one situation is transferable from one context to another.<p>You cannot disentangle the right to speak from the right to listen, and that is why so many people are frustrated: their ability to listen to their chosen speaker was taken away from them. Which is my only point (having not given my opinion on where I stand): accusing opposition as demanding to be heard and have their voice amplified is uncharitable and misunderstands the extent of the debate.')
