One pain point about Rust is, due to its linear nature, you cannot use `as` patterns, such as:<p>match e with\n   x as (y, z) -&gt; (x[0], x[1]) == (y, z)<p>This is because the matcher itself must take ownership of the expression, and destructuring is an ownership transfer.')
