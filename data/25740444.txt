Item(by='jiggawatts', descendants=None, kids=[25741281, 25740509, 25740499], score=None, time=1610410991, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25740168, text='Yes, that&#x27;s the &quot;particles in a box&quot; model, such as the famous black-body experiment that involved light bouncing around inside a container with a tiny hole in the side for taking measurements of the glow inside.<p>Similarly, atoms can be thought of as a &quot;spherical box&quot;, the boundary in this case is a full revolution around the potential well.<p>The thing is: Not everything is a box, hence the abstract model that uses photons as a shorthand for the behaviour of the EM field in a box isn&#x27;t fully general, and simply doesn&#x27;t apply outside of those scenarios.<p>That doesn&#x27;t stop the vast majority of people talking about photons (and bosonic particles in general) as if they&#x27;re individually countable things zipping around in free space.<p>Neutrons can be counted like this. Photons can&#x27;t.')
