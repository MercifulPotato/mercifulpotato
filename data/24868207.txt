If the terms were a limited license rather than an infinite guarantee to provide activation servers, then it’s not likely legally unreasonable. And it’s not at all uncommon to be the former. I’ve never seen a court rule a license like this to be unreasonable. Have you?<p>Here’s [1] the CS3 license. It states a 90 day warranty, which is certainly legally a standard warranty. Section 14.8.1 states Adobe can disable the activation server.<p>So yep, looks like this was covered in the license. I doubt a court is going to rule Adobe must provide a server indefinitely.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;labs.adobe.com&#x2F;technologies&#x2F;eula&#x2F;photoshopcs3.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;labs.adobe.com&#x2F;technologies&#x2F;eula&#x2F;photoshopcs3.html</a>')
