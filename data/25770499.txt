Item(by='tialaramex', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610587220, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25769361, text='The experience in the Web PKI has been that you can pay lawyers to explain to a judge that they should order the thing they <i>actually</i> want done, rather than accept contorted arguments from other lawyers that a different order will achieve the goals despite not ordering what is actually desired.<p>For example the EFF spent effort helping US judges understand that if they want to order that stealmovies.example must go away they need to order the example registry to remove this name, not try to force Let&#x27;s Encrypt to revoke certificates for it as Hollywood lawyers were advocating.<p>That won&#x27;t magically stop judges from making orders you disagree with but it does force them to be clear eyed about what they&#x27;re about, and that means you&#x27;re more likely to prevail with simple just rationales either in court or subsequently in popular opinion.')
