Item(by='dwohnitmok', descendants=None, kids=[25284990], score=None, time=1606960381, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25284224, text='Here&#x27;s an example of a series of phono-semantic characters that&#x27;s fairly easy to pronounce for English speakers.<p>成, 誠, 城 all are pronounced cheng, so we say that 成 is the phonetic compound that the other two characters use to denote their own pronunciation.<p>言 and 土 on the other two characters then are semantic compounds that hint at the meaning of the character.<p>言 indicates speech&#x2F;language and 誠 can be interpreted as &quot;true to one&#x27;s word,&quot; i.e. honest&#x2F;straightforward&#x2F;earnest&#x2F;sincere.<p>土 indicates earth and by extension that which is built and 城 is in fact a kind of structure, namely &quot;city walls&quot; (later also just &quot;city&quot;).<p>There&#x27;s some subtlety here where the pronunciation rule doesn&#x27;t always hold exactly, e.g. 盛 is pronounced sheng, and sometimes not at all, e.g. 兌, 說, and 悅 are all pronounced <i>very</i> differently from one another. This is due to a variety of hysterical raisins.')
