Item(by='AnthonyMouse', descendants=None, kids=[25831025], score=None, time=1611037890, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25830372, text='We already know the answer to a lot of this. Algorithms that increase &quot;engagement&quot; by promoting controversy promote conspiracy theories, because conspiracy theories are controversial and get everybody arguing.<p>The first generation of response to this was to tweak the algorithm to favor &quot;trusted&quot; sources, but still promote controversy. This seems to be going <i>extremely</i> poorly, because it&#x27;s encouraging the &quot;trusted&quot; sources to become radicalized in an effort to get promoted by the algorithms.<p>The actual solution probably involves not optimizing for engagement anymore, but that&#x27;s a business model change.')
