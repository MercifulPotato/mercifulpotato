Item(by='freddie_mercury', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609914321, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25654562, text='Vietnam isn&#x27;t even in the Top 30 for traffic accidents. It isn&#x27;t &quot;among the highest rate&quot;.<p>Vietnam&#x27;s traffic fatality rate is 26 per 100,000. About the same was America&#x27;s traffic fatality rate in early 1970s before Ralph Nader convinced people to care about safety. But Americans had no problem driving on roads in 1970s. Virtually no one thought it was too dangerous to drive in America at those fatality rates.<p>(Today fatality rates are down ~50% from those numbers thanks to Ralph Nader.)<p>And the Vietnam numbers continue to decrease as the country develops. The WHO numbers are based on 2016 numbers. There were a reported 8,417 fatalities in 2016 but the WHO assumes that&#x27;s a lie and adjusts upwards to 24,970 fatalities. In any case, fatalities have been dropping by about 5% a year. In 2019 there were 7,624 fatalities, for instance.')
