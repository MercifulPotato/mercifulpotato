Item(by='onecommentman', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606977319, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25286048, text='“As a journalist, he knew the value of concise speech. Every word counted, as each one had to be paid for when telegraphed to the news desk. Every decorative, non-informative adjective should be axed.”  If you want proof, read newspaper columns from before the advent of the telegraph, and from just after.  Before, the writing was expansive, a bit rambling, almost poetic.  After, they are barely longer than a tweet in some cases.  There are online examples of these, but the real paper copies cost less (per unit in quantity) than an expensive coffee drink.')
