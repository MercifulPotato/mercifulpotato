I wonder if any shells that support $((..)) do not support ((..) though.<p>The update 2017 standard mentions ((..)) but not in the obvious place.  It&#x27;s under &#x27;compound commands&#x2F;grouping&#x27; ...<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;pubs.opengroup.org&#x2F;onlinepubs&#x2F;9699919799&#x2F;utilities&#x2F;V3_chap02.html#tag_18_09_04" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;pubs.opengroup.org&#x2F;onlinepubs&#x2F;9699919799&#x2F;utilities&#x2F;V...</a><p>&gt; <i>If a character sequence beginning with &quot;((&quot; would be parsed by the shell as an arithmetic expansion if preceded by a &#x27;$&#x27;, shells which implement an extension whereby &quot;((expression))&quot; is evaluated as an arithmetic expression may treat the &quot;((&quot; as introducing as an arithmetic evaluation instead of a grouping command. A conforming application shall ensure that it separates the two leading &#x27;(&#x27; characters with white space to prevent the shell from performing an arithmetic evaluation.</i>')
