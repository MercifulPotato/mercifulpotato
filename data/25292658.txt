[...] [Y]ou are investing in a business by owning a part of it which naturally means that you will and do get the returns on it[.]<p>That is how Ethereum works. Validators get returns from Ethereum by holding shares, i.e. ETH.  Validators,  after EIP-1559, will be paid with newly minted ETH and anything over the base burn fee.  (Currently there is no mandatory burning of fees.)<p>To stake in ETH 2.0 a stand alone validator needs 32 ETH (~$20,000).  It reminds me of Outback&#x27;s business model of requiring managers to buy in as stake holders (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;hbr.org&#x2F;2005&#x2F;09&#x2F;a-stake-in-the-business" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;hbr.org&#x2F;2005&#x2F;09&#x2F;a-stake-in-the-business</a>).<p>This is before all the crazy features that can be built on top of eth-as-programmatic-money, such as lending.  <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;defipulse.com&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;defipulse.com&#x2F;</a>')
