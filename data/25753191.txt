Item(by='vkou', descendants=None, kids=[25754250, 25753798], score=None, time=1610483755, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25752969, text='Only if implemented in an inconsistent fashion - where travel from some areas with high levels of COVID is permitted, while travel from other areas with much lower levels of COVID is not.<p>Which is currently the case. For example, travel from Russia to the US is currently allowed, despite Russia having &gt;3 million COVID cases, but travel from Finland (&gt;37,000 COVID cases) and China (&gt;97,000 COVID cases) is prohibited.<p>It&#x27;s not even a quid-pro-quo, because Russia has banned travel <i>from</i> the United States (&gt;22,700,000 COVID cases, FYI.)<p>If you want to stop travelers spreading COVID in the US, the first thing you should do is ban domestic travel, and the second thing you should do is to update the travel ban to include infection data from any point past ~April 2020.<p>If you&#x27;re not willing to require someone flying from New York to Nashville to have a negative COVID test before getting on an airplane, you probably shouldn&#x27;t spend too much time wringing your hands over someone flying from Hanoi to LA without one.')
