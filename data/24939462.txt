Item(by='jjav', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604035911, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24934188, text='&gt; I literally have a non-existent network of people who will help me find work.<p>A suggestion is to just build that network of past people on linkedin, today. There&#x27;s no rule saying you can only connect with people you worked with recently.<p>I&#x27;ve been on linkedin a long time (2004) and for many many years I did the introvert approach of only connecting with those I worked very closely with and considered friends. So my network was tiny.<p>Somewhere along the line I realized this isn&#x27;t my list of friends, it&#x27;s just a network of people I&#x27;ve worked with who I&#x27;d be willing to forward their resume to HR if they asked. That set is a couple order of magnitude larger than my list of friends. So I started sending out invites to everyone I&#x27;ve worked with since the 90s who was adequately competent or better. The vast majority of people accept.')
