Item(by='whatshisface', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610382114, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25731002, text='&gt;<i>If somebody can go and hold a gun to your head and thereby persuade you to give them your goods for free, that&#x27;s not a free market.</i><p>It&#x27;s a free market if you can hire private security to stop them. The ban on the violent trade (often called the &quot;monopoly on violence&quot;) is an example of an area in which modern liberal democracies do not permit complete freedom in the markets they host. I guess the slogan could be, &quot;not every policy liberal democracies like is a free-market policy.&quot;')
