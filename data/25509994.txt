Item(by='theboat', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608664663, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25505418, text='I once thought community-based mesh networking could disrupt last-mile internet providers, beyond its obvious value in remote areas and disaster zones. If we could get decent bandwidth with a freemium&#x2F;pay-as-you-go model, it would be way more consumer friendly and affordable than paying a big telco to string a wire into your house.<p>However with the advent of 5G and now satellite internet, it seems like high-speed wireless internet will be ubiquitous in relatively short order without the need for mesh networks. So that dream is probably dead.')
