Item(by='Stratoscope', descendants=None, kids=[25258154, 25259330, 25258692, 25259228, 25258809], score=None, time=1606765438, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25257162, text='I learned something the other day that blew my mind. We are now able to take pictures of the <i>far side of the Sun</i>.<p>How? We could put a telescope in Earth orbit 180 degrees opposite from us. But we don&#x27;t have one there yet.<p>We use <i>sound waves</i>.<p>Sound? In the Sun? Don&#x27;t you need air for that?<p>No, any gas will do (solids or liquids too). The Sun is a big ball of very dense gas, perfect for propagating sound waves.<p>So we image the near side and analyze distortions caused by the sound waves traveling through the Sun. The resulting images of the far side are low resolution, but they are good enough to &quot;see&quot; things like coronal mass ejections and the giant sunspot that rotated into view last week.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.google.com&#x2F;search?q=solar+sound+waves" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.google.com&#x2F;search?q=solar+sound+waves</a><p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cora.nwra.com&#x2F;~werne&#x2F;eos&#x2F;text&#x2F;solar_waves_main.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cora.nwra.com&#x2F;~werne&#x2F;eos&#x2F;text&#x2F;solar_waves_main.h...</a><p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cora.nwra.com&#x2F;~werne&#x2F;eos&#x2F;text&#x2F;farside.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cora.nwra.com&#x2F;~werne&#x2F;eos&#x2F;text&#x2F;farside.html</a><p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.space.com&#x2F;thanksgiving-sun-sunspot-treat-2020" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.space.com&#x2F;thanksgiving-sun-sunspot-treat-2020</a>')
