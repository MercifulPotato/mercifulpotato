Item(by='kgwgk', descendants=None, kids=[25988758], score=None, time=1612186734, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25988443, text='&gt; As long as a creditworthy institution guarantees the same stream of dividends, they are identical in value from the investors perspective.<p>No, the stream of dividends doesn&#x27;t have identical value.<p>There are other relevant corporate actions.<p>Shares can vote, your stream of dividends cannot vote.<p>If the company spins-off a segment it may distribute shares of the new company to shareholders while the owners of that third-party stream of dividends won&#x27;t get anything.<p>If someone wants to acquire the company they will make an offer to buy outstanding shares, they won&#x27;t care at all about those syntethic streams of dividends.<p>In general, if you want to sell a delta-one derivative that&#x27;s fine. But you cannot sell that to someone who wants to buy a stock!<p>Would <i>you</i> want to be delivered a derivative when you bought a share in the market?<p>That wouldn&#x27;t be shorting the stock, it would be selling something else. Related but far from identical.')
