Item(by='dasil003', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607439347, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25344135, text='Designing something to be “scalable” before it’s actually established is a recipe for premature optimization at best and a poorly baked SOA with service boundaries that make change incredibly difficult at worst.<p>It’s important to keep mind that SOA is about scaling teams first, code second and  not really about throughout per se.  A share-nothing web tier plus a couple judiciously applied databases and background job queues can effectively scale a huge proportion of applications without the overhead of a full SOA.')
