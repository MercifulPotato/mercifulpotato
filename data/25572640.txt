Item(by='hinkley', descendants=None, kids=[25573906, 25573120], score=None, time=1609264838, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25569572, text='One of the &#x27;atmospheric&#x27; descriptions in The Expanse that I have loved is the use of antispalling coatings on the interiors of ships.<p>Micrometeoroids are a fact of space travel, and in this universe, so are kinetic weapons. so the authors imagined&#x2F;researched a material meant to resist the tendency of the ship&#x27;s materials to become secondary projectiles ricocheting through the ship turning everyone and everything into swiss cheese.<p>I think they may be defunct now, but there was a company called Tethers, Inc who had in their toolbox making &#x27;cables&#x27; that look more like fishing nets. The idea being to reduce the likelihood of a single strike damaging multiple structural members at once.<p>I don&#x27;t know what or if we can do for satellites that is in this direction. You can make the satellites tiny but you still need solar collectors.')
