Item(by='Minor49er', descendants=None, kids=[25961075], score=None, time=1611941778, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25959319, text='Array unpacking is going to help make a lot of coding situations more concise. There isn&#x27;t a direct comparison in the article, but from what I understand, something like this:<p><pre><code>  $array = array_merge([&quot;a&quot; =&gt; 0], $array1, $array2);\n</code></pre>\ncould be replaced with this:<p><pre><code>  $array = [&quot;a&quot; =&gt; 0, ...$array1, ...$array2];\n</code></pre>\nIt seems small here, but anything that stops devs from having to nest a bunch of array*() calls in PHP is an excellent change. I don&#x27;t know how many times I&#x27;ve come across code that does something like this:<p><pre><code>  $array = array_values(array_unique(array_filter(array_merge($one, $two, $three))));</code></pre>')
