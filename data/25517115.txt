See:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Treasure_Act_1996" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Treasure_Act_1996</a><p>Finders have to offer the treasure for sale to a museum&#x2F;similar at a price determined by an independent panel.<p>The UK also has broader, older rules governing &quot;treasure trove&quot;, with slight regional variations. See:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Treasure_trove" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Treasure_trove</a><p>The bottom line isn&#x27;t &quot;finders keepers&quot;, but rewards are fair and often handsome. The rules try to strike a balance between public interest&#x2F;education, common good, and providing an incentive against individual greed.')
