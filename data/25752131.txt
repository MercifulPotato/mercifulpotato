Item(by='adrian_b', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610479663, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25750021, text='The so-called 5 Gb&#x2F;s PCIe 2.0 lanes are in fact 4 Gb&#x2F;s lanes.<p>Ethernet speeds are given in data bits per second, while USB 3.0, PCIe 1.0 and PCIe 2.0 add in their speed values the extra bits used for encoding.<p>On the other hand, 10 Gb&#x2F;s USB and 8 Gb&#x2F;s PCIe 3.0 have really those speeds. Complicated :-(<p>One PCIe 2.0 lane is more than enough for 2.5 Gb&#x2F;s Ethernet, but not enough for 5 Gb&#x2F;s Ethernet or 10 Gb&#x2F;s Ethernet.')
