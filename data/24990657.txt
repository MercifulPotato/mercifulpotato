Item(by='cesarb', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604508485, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24989393, text='If you look closely at that image, and at the image of the other side of the board (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.jeffgeerling.com&#x2F;sites&#x2F;default&#x2F;files&#x2F;images&#x2F;3-pi400-in-shell.jpeg" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.jeffgeerling.com&#x2F;sites&#x2F;default&#x2F;files&#x2F;images&#x2F;3-pi...</a>), it&#x27;s fascinating. The keyboard is USB 2 (as mentioned in the article, &quot;one of the USB2.0 ports is dedicated to the keyboard&quot;) with its connector is on the right side, and the designer did find a good path from one side of the board to the other, using some crazy routing. It goes <i>between</i> the two USB 3 ports, and then <i>below</i> all the other ports until it reaches the right side, where it passes right next to the antenna and below the wireless chip to reach the keyboard connector. It&#x27;s a tight fit, and there&#x27;s no space for another pair.')
