Item(by='dheera', descendants=None, kids=[25857307], score=None, time=1611219845, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25856695, text='Ehh there are lots of cases where I&#x27;d rather have a microcontroller than a full OS.<p>The biggest thing going for microcontrollers is that they aren&#x27;t vulnerable to file system corruption if you yank the plug on them, making them extremely useful for IoT devices like smart locks &#x2F; lighting &#x2F; air quality monitoring &#x2F; appliances &#x2F; electronics designed for use in the field where you don&#x27;t want to sit around figuring out how to ssh in and type &quot;sudo shutdown -h now&quot; instead of just yanking the plug and get on with your day, which you can do safely with a microcontroller.<p>(Strictly speaking yes you could design an OS for the Pi Zero W that mounts in read-only mode and avoids this problem but it&#x27;s a headache)<p>That said the Pi Pico doesn&#x27;t look attractive to me; I usually use ESP32s for those projects these days.')
