Item(by='JumpCrisscross', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604164711, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24952846, text='&gt; <i>As a saver, I don&#x27;t care about the basis, I care that the money doesn&#x27;t get too discounted with time, and I care about the risk</i><p>Basis links the asset to other assets. That makes it less likely your asset will singularly get discounted relative to the broader economy, <i>i.e.</i> lose purchasing power. It is still exposed to systemic risk, but so is everything.<p>Baseless assets, like diamonds and paintings and Bitcoin, derive their value <i>solely</i> from perceptions. That can be somewhat stable over the short term (up to 20 to 30 years). But it tends to fall apart between generations or through culture-changing crises.<p>Put another way, investment assets are tied to productive uses in some way. Baseless assets are not.')
