Item(by='jefftk', descendants=None, kids=[25431400], score=None, time=1608043174, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25430122, text='One of the most common cases of targeting ads is remarketing: someone comes to your site, they start the process of buying a tablesaw but then leave for whatever reason.  You pay to show ads of the saw so they can come back and finish their purchase if they want to.<p>Traditionally, this has been implemented with third party cookies.  The retailer drops a cookie on the users browser, and then buys ads to be shown to anyone matching that cookie.  I don&#x27;t know if I would call this data mining, but it is certainly not private.<p>It is possible to build a system that supports remarketing in a private way, however, with new browser APIs: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;WICG&#x2F;turtledove" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;WICG&#x2F;turtledove</a><p>(Disclosure: I work on ads at Google, speaking only for myself)')
