Because if you want to have non-renegable contracts then any digital signature will do, and you can sign a commonly agreed timestamp into it like &quot;I will provide service X as of 2020-10-24T21:57:14+0200 &lt;insert signature&gt;&quot;. The only thing you can&#x27;t prove is when it really was signed, sent, published, that sort of thing, and for that a timestamping service can be used (which other comments already mentioned).')
