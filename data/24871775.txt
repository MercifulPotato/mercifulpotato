Item(by='cambalache', descendants=None, kids=[24874625], score=None, time=1603473874, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24870552, text='The &quot;I refute it thus&quot; is one of the funniest episodes in the history of philosophy (next to Diogenes plucked chicken)<p>For the unaware:<p>&gt; “After we came out of the church, we stood talking for some time together of Bishop Berkeley’s ingenious sophistry to prove the non-existence of matter, and that every thing in the universe is merely ideal. I observed, that though we are satisfied his doctrine is not true, it is impossible to refute it. I never shall forget the alacrity with which Johnson answered, striking his foot with mighty force against a large stone, till he rebounded from it, ‘I refute it thus.&#x27;”(Boswell’s Life of Samuel Johnson, quoted from Wikipedia.)')
