Item(by='wittyreference', descendants=None, kids=[25520936], score=None, time=1608748907, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25520487, text='Challenge away, it&#x27;s the internet!<p>I think that it&#x27;s possible but unlikely that loperamide is having a unique effect, because (a) they&#x27;ve shown that it&#x27;s operating via the ATF4 mechanism, which is the same mechanism we see in other opioids (and multiple other drugs), and (b) loperamide&#x27;s activity has generally been pretty well characterized and it doesn&#x27;t really stand out (it&#x27;s by no means a new drug - I remember taking it as a generic twenty years ago.)<p>I&#x27;ll admit it&#x27;s possible in the broad sense because ATF4 isn&#x27;t some super-specific pathway. It&#x27;s a stress response signal. So while I think (b) above makes a unique mechanism unlikely, (a) by itself isn&#x27;t a slam-dunk - there are a lot of stress signals that induce cell death when they get amped up enough, and can inhibit cell death if blocked off.')
