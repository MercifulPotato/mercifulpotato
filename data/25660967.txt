Item(by='wrsh07', descendants=None, kids=[25661412, 25661107], score=None, time=1609959898, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25659615, text='Ok, summary:<p>This article lays out three scenarios: 1) accessing two random elements<p>2) accessing 3 random elements<p>3) accessing two pairs of adjacent elements (same as (1) but also the elements after each random element)<p>It then does some trivial math to use the loaded data.<p>A naive model might only consider memory accesses and might assume accessing an adjacent element is free.<p>On the Mac m1 core, this is not the case. While the naive model might expect cases 1 &amp; 3 to cost the same and case 2 to cost 50% more, instead cases 2 &amp; 3 are nearly the same (3 slightly faster) and case 2 is about 50% more expensive than 1.')
