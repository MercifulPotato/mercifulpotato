Item(by='dragonwriter', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606355434, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25212514, text='&gt; Nursing is one of very few licensed professions you can do with an associates degree.<p>There are lots of licensed professions you can do with a associates degree or less. (In California, the list includes Barber, Commercial Fisherman, Cosmetologist, Dental Hygienist, Drinking Water Plant Operator, Electrician, EMT, Funeral Service Director, General Contractor, ...and that&#x27;s just through the “Gs” of positions covered by the National Occupational Licensing Database. [0]<p>[0] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.ncsl.org&#x2F;research&#x2F;labor-and-employment&#x2F;occupational-licensing-statute-database.aspx" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.ncsl.org&#x2F;research&#x2F;labor-and-employment&#x2F;occupatio...</a><p>Nursing is actually a set of separately licensed professions, the lowest couple levels (CNA, LPN&#x2F;LVN) typically require a non-degree certification, though the latter may also be offered in associates degree form in some institutions. RN requires either an associate&#x27;s or bachelor&#x27;s (ADN or BSN), and the levels beyond RN require additional education; NP requires at least a BSN, Nurse midwives and nurse anesthetists require at least a Masters.')
