Item(by='adrian_b', descendants=None, kids=[25006128], score=None, time=1604652560, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25006032, text=' I prefer to use rsync over SSH as a replacement for scp:<p>alias scp=&#x27;&#x2F;usr&#x2F;bin&#x2F;rsync --archive --xattrs --acls --progress --rsh=&quot;ssh&quot;&#x27;<p>I have completely deprecated for myself the use of both scp and sftp many years ago and I disable the sftp server (in sshd.conf) on all my servers.<p>While scp and sftp are also slower, usually being unable to reach link speed on fast links, the main reason is that I have discovered that when copying files with them they sometimes were losing file metadata (e.g. parts of the timestamps or extended attributes) without any warnings or errors.<p>rsync does not have such problems, it can make exact copies even when copying between different operating systems or different file systems.<p>Because I have stopped using both scp and sftp many years ago, I do not know whether meanwhile there was any effort to remove the bugs from scp and sftp, but I doubt it.')
