Item(by='na85', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608876223, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25534373, text='It basically boils down to the fact that machmeters hadn&#x27;t been invented yet, because the physics of supersonic flight wasn&#x27;t sufficiently understood back then.<p>Shock waves develop inside the pitot tube under certain conditions, and that causes a spike (or sometimes a drop) in pressure which is interpreted by the instrument as a corresponding rise or drop in airspeed.<p>Edit: as for the 262 claim itself it was pretty handily debunked in a post-war NACA technical note.  If you dig around on the NTRS you can find it.')
