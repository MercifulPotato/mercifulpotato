A resource that isn&#x27;t inherently tied to value that seems to have been &quot;chosen&quot; by centuries of evolution towards a scarce material that could be centrally controlled. What&#x27;s more, it has become less scarce as better technologies have emerged for extracting it. And just like printing excess fiat currency, early gold rushes showed the same type of rapid inflation in local economies, with money made by those selling mining tools &amp; services to miners, and those purchasing the mined gold to arbitrage it out to other geographical regions.
