NAS and clouds are different things, a NAS is just a USB drive over the network, a cloud gives you more functionalities (versioning, backup, sync, collaborative folder, etc...).<p>What is exposed to the outside world (port forwarding) depends on your configuration. If you use SFTP, you will need to forward 22 towards your &quot;local&quot; cloud. If you store your cloud on the router, it will more likely be FTPS, and there will be no need for port forwarding.<p>You will need login&#x2F;password&#x2F;ip of the storage, and in the future when we implement E2EE, the password of your cloud.')
