Item(by='scythe', descendants=None, kids=[25194453, 25201393], score=None, time=1606180551, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25190770, text='Suppose <i>f</i> and <i>g</i> are normalized distributions and consider the function <i>f</i>(<i>x</i>)·<i>g</i>(<i>y</i>). If we &quot;collapse&quot; (integrate) in the y-dimension we recover <i>f</i>(<i>x</i>). If we collapse in the x-dimension we recover <i>g</i>(<i>y</i>).<p>But if we collapse along the lines <i>x</i>+<i>y</i> = <i>v</i>, we obtain the convolution <i>f</i>⋆<i>g</i>(<i>v</i>).<p>This picture is a little more advanced, but it makes clear two key properties of convolution: symmetry (commutativity) and the fact that the total integral of the convolution is the total integral of <i>f</i>(<i>x</i>)·<i>g</i>(<i>y</i>).')
