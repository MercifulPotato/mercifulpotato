Item(by='ratww', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610626830, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25771790, text='I think it <i>really</i> depends on the size and type of the app.<p>As someone who worked on large jQuery apps before React, I think most people would jump back into React or Vue in the first chance after seeing a non-trivial jQuery app. Not to mention there was a lot of &quot;bloat&quot; in those apps, too: Backbone, Bootstrap, Moment.js...<p>jQuery was amazing for small things, for landing pages, for some Bootstrap components here and there, and for sprinkling the page with progressive-enhanced features. And it still is probably the best choice.<p>But it was not a great fit for moderate-to-large apps. And it was a terrible fit for moderate-to-large apps that only consumed data from an API.<p>Not saying React and Redux are good, but I think at least the parts of React, Angular and Vue that replaced jQuery were definitely a step in the right direction.<p>I&#x27;m also not saying that &quot;big apps&quot; are the way to go. If I could I would go back to server-side rendered HTML and modern CSS, but it has become hard to find developers that are into it.')
