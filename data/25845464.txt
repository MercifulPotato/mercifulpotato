Item(by='pwdisswordfish5', descendants=None, kids=[25848558], score=None, time=1611144603, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25844849, text='But zero <i>is</i> an <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Ordinal_number_(mathematics)" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Ordinal_number_(mathematics)</a> .<p>The unintuitive step is apparently identifying the index with the cardinality of the collection from <i>before</i> the item arrives, not <i>after</i> it arrives – i.e. ‘the <i>n</i>-th element is the one that arrived after I had <i>n</i> elements’, not ‘the <i>n</i>-th element is the one such that I had <i>n</i> elements after it arrived’. The former identification results in 0-based ordinals, the latter leads to 1-based ordinals – and to some misconceptions about infinity, such as imagining an element ‘at index infinity’ in an infinite list, where no such need to exist.')
