Item(by='yaantc', descendants=None, kids=[24958538], score=None, time=1604222449, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24958135, text='In first order logic one can only quantify over objects of the domain: in &quot;for all x such that ...&quot; the variable &quot;x&quot; represent an object.<p>In second order logic, one can quantify over relations (and functions, sets...): &quot;for all P such that ...&quot;.<p>The wikipaedia page [1] has more details and is quite readable. Be careful about falling into a rabbit hole ;)<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Second-order_logic" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Second-order_logic</a>')
