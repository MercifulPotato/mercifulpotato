Item(by='mceachen', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603836138, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24911577, text='Faster CPUs certainly help everything go faster, but <i>what you&#x27;re asking of the CPU</i> may matter much more than how fast the CPU is.<p>RAW processing is actually a really interesting application, and rendering time depends heavily on what you do with the bits from the sensor.<p>If you use non-linear interpolation, 2-50x the time to build.<p>If you do highlight recovery, 2-5x the time to build.<p>If you only need half the native resolution, build time may be reduced 2-4x.<p>Basically &quot;viewing a RAW image&quot; can take 100 milliseconds or 15 seconds (on the same hardware!), depending on how you&#x27;re interpreting the sensor data.<p>(Source: playing with dcraw and libraw to rasterize raw images for PhotoStructure)')
