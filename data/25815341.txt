Item(by='bsder', descendants=None, kids=[25816239], score=None, time=1610920077, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25813406, text='It&#x27;s not about the hardware.  In spite of the fact that they can nominally be broken into, most modern USB microcontrollers can easily implement being a security key.<p>The expense is that the security world hasn&#x27;t converged on anything remotely approaching a single standard.  So, a key has to support ... PGP, OpenSSH, FIDO, FIDO2, U2F ...<p>For example, you couldn&#x27;t use some of the older YubiKeys with AWS because AWS only supported time-based TOTP.<p>That&#x27;s a <i>LOT</i> of software work, and you can&#x27;t be &quot;Startup Sloppy&quot; with your code or someone will break it.')
