I suspect only one of their devs could answer this affirmatively.<p>There are different approaches. As a sibling comment mentioned, you can use any login protocol that responds with a different message for users vs. non-users.<p>Off the top of my head, you have POP, IMAP, SMTP, OAuth, Gmail&#x27;s undocumented HTTP API, Gmail&#x27;s documented HTTP API, and web scraping.<p>You probably can&#x27;t do any of those at scale.<p>To solve the scale issue, you could buy databases of verified email addresses, which are not expensive. For any address found in those databases, you know it&#x27;s registered.<p>If it&#x27;s not in your database, you can do a live check.<p>Alternatively, you could make a business arrangement with Google, which is also possible for a company like Experian.')
