Item(by='crocodiletears', descendants=None, kids=[25181530], score=None, time=1606072449, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25180003, text='Who can afford to fly anywhere more than once every few years (assuming no surprise expenses), or not to drive 20k miles a year across urban sprawl to their jobs?<p>The first restriction is so alien to much of the population as to be meaningless, the second is a threat to their livelihoods. An accompanying promise of public transit rings hollow for many Americans, who think of their local governments as only marginally competent enough to fill potholes in the road sometimes.<p>Naturally people will bristle at the abstract of many climate restrictions. Not because the fun will stop, but because they aren&#x27;t having any fun in the first place, and they just don&#x27;t want things to get less fun than that.')
