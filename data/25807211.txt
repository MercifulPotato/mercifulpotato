In the absence of market regulation and information disclosure standards, the commercial results were often failures and dysfunction, with unbalanced networks, local monopolies, and death spiral competition on oversupplied lines.<p>In his ascent to power, uber banker JP Morgan focused on railroads, America&#x27;s largest business enterprises. He wrested control of the Albany and Susquehanna Railroad from Jay Gould and Jim Fisk in 1869; led the syndicate that broke the government-financing privileges of Jay Cooke; and developed and financed a railroad empire by reorganization and consolidation in all parts of the United States.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;J._P._Morgan#Railroads" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;J._P._Morgan#Railroads</a>')
