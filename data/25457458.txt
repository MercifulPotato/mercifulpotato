Item(by='allenz', descendants=None, kids=[25457577, 25459754, 25458899, 25458889], score=None, time=1608224554, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25455624, text='To add a bit of context: brokerages like Robinhood send buy&#x2F;sell orders to national exchanges and to private trading firms e.g. high-frequency traders. Private firms provide price improvement: orders that execute at prices better than the national exchange. All brokerages have a duty of best execution, including a duty of price improvement. Brokerages can also receive payment for order flow from private firms, as long as it does not interfere with best execution. However, &quot;Robinhood explicitly offered to accept less price improvement for its customers... in exchange for receiving a higher payment for order flow,&quot; which is illegal.<p>Full order: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.sec.gov&#x2F;litigation&#x2F;admin&#x2F;2020&#x2F;33-10906.pdf" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.sec.gov&#x2F;litigation&#x2F;admin&#x2F;2020&#x2F;33-10906.pdf</a>')
