Item(by='tristor', descendants=None, kids=[25675828], score=None, time=1610042260, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25672443, text='I think many people over-focus on GPU and CPU capabilities and the raw horsepower of console hardware as the basis for determining console hardware superiority.  As an avid gamer on PC and console, I&#x27;ve never thought of it that way, a PC will pretty much always be better on horsepower than a console.  What consoles excel at is hardware that enhances the user experience or moves forward the technology focused on the act of gaming.<p>I am incredibly happy that Nintendo said &quot;No.&quot; to Microsoft here, because the Nintendo Switch is the single greatest console device ever made.  The way it handles docking&#x2F;undocking, game switching, updates, sleep, the controller design of the removable joycons, it&#x27;s an unprecedentedly good user experience in a way that&#x27;s going to be incredibly difficult to top.  I have been interminably sad that Sega stopped making consoles because prior to the Switch, the Dreamcast pushed the envelope in every possible way for what it meant to be a gaming console. The Xbox One did something great by improving controller design significantly, but realistically the majority of consoles released in the last few decades have not been pushing the envelope in the way that the format allows.')
