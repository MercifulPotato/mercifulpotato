Item(by='valar_m', descendants=None, kids=[24783500], score=None, time=1602721768, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24783152, text='&gt;The standard argument is that freedom of speech only applies to governments, but that&#x27;s based on an outdated assumption.<p>&gt;There&#x27;s nothing special about governments with regard to freedom of speech except that it has always been assumed governments are the only entities with enough individual power to significantly restrict free speech.<p>The difference is that Twitter can&#x27;t put you in prison and it can&#x27;t seize your property. The government can. It&#x27;s an extraordinary power wielded exclusively by the government.<p>Consider this: If you think Twitter should be restrained from censoring their platform because the extent of their power is on par with the government, who will enforce any violations of that restraint?')
