Item(by='mattlondon', descendants=None, kids=[25128939, 25125645, 25124741], score=None, time=1605623898, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25120506, text='I am starting to gestate a line of thought that is pondering <i>what people think open source is for</i>?<p>E.g. from the article:<p>&gt; Once the first version exists and is found usable, the next step is to make it production ready.<p>Why? Why does open source ever have to get to this stage at all? What are people&#x27;s expectations? Is there a - potentially unconscious - expectation that all open source projects <i>need</i> to end up at the scale of linux or kubernetes or firefox or whatever?<p>Why can&#x27;t open source just be some twiddly little bit of fun that captures your imagination for a week or two, ends up on github and is then abandoned?<p>Don&#x27;t be so hard on yourselves. Create things for the joy of it.')
