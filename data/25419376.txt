Item(by='elicash', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607963193, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25414341, text='The real question is what we do in the face of uncertainty.<p>I&#x27;ll use two comparisons, one positive and one negative for fairness sake. <i>Before</i> we knew that there wasn&#x27;t much COVID spread in schools, did it make sense to close them just based on the uncertainty involved? Here&#x27;s an example from the other end: Before we knew for sure leaded gasoline was a bad idea, should it have been banned? The impact of all the lead, particularly on children, was incredibly damaging and had lifelong effects that continued after the science was in.<p>Obviously, you want to take action based on data. But before we&#x27;ve got enough information to know, what do you do? I&#x27;d imagine the risk analysis has something to do with the rate at which micro plastics are being introduced and how far we are from an answer.<p>It doesn&#x27;t have to be all or nothing. For example, it could be that items that cause the most issues are taxed a bit more to slow the growth of this potential issue. (Not suggesting that specifically; I don&#x27;t know enough to say if action should be weaker or more severe.)')
