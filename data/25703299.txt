The exceptions are virus with chronic infections because they can mutate much more easily, and some viruses like Influenza that can use antigenic shifts.<p>It&#x27;s really not typical. Even in all of the human cold coronaviruses, only one seems to be evolving its spike protein, and it does so quite slowly.<p>If it was typical, we wouldn&#x27;t see the amazing efficacy of vaccines against endemic diseases.')
