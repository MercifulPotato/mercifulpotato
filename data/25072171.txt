Item(by='thomascgalvin', descendants=None, kids=[25073155], score=None, time=1605202126, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25071676, text='This study had three groups:<p>- Control: followed the current Norwegian guidelines, which recommend 30 minutes of moderate level physical activity almost every day<p>- High Intensity Interval Training (HIIT): Generally followed the same program as control, but two of the five sessions were instead a 10 minute warmup, followed by four four-minute intervals, working at 90% max heart rate<p>- Moderate Intensity Continuous Training (MICT): Generally followed the same program as control, but two of the five sessions were instead a half hour of steady-state exercise, at about 70% max heart rate<p>Conclusion: There was little to no difference in mortality between the control, HIIT, and MICT groups.<p>I don&#x27;t love this study design, because it isn&#x27;t really testing HIIT (or MICT); it&#x27;s testing what happens when 40% of your exercise is HIIT (or MICT).<p>That being said, I think the most important takeaway is that exercise is good for you, and what that exercise looks like is almost completely unimportant. Do what you enjoy and can sustain. If you have ten minutes in the morning, do HIIT. If you have an hour after work and want to get out of the house, go jogging or ride a bike. Anything is better than nothing.')
