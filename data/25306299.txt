Item(by='Const-me', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607108680, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25294208, text='They are different caches.<p>The database engines you’re talking about do that for pages not found in RAM, which need to be read from disk.<p>This article is about accessing data that’s already present in system memory. The caches they are talking about are on-chip ones, i.e. L1D and L2 caches inside CPU cores, and L3 cache shared across cores but still located on the same CPU die.<p>Some rare CPUs like in my old laptop with Core i3-6157U even have L4 on-chip cache made of DRAM.')
