Item(by='chmod775', descendants=None, kids=[24674460], score=None, time=1601742893, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24661653, text='Since the HN crowd will be mostly running HTTP(S) servers, the answer is that you probably should just be using the kernel&#x27;s TCP stack, because however slow it is, it&#x27;ll only use a tiny fraction of the CPU time your HTTP(S) server will be using, especially if you&#x27;re doing SSL termination on the same machine.<p>Rolling a custom TCP stack is only interesting when the amount of work per TCP connection is minimal and you&#x27;re in the territory of handling half a million or more concurrent connections.
