Even in the conservative Portugal of the 1960s&#x2F;70s a small wave of &quot;modern math&quot; (as it was called here) washed over the high-schools.<p>I believe it was more of an experimental thing, with partial adoption - i.e. only one of the math teachers would use the new syllabus on their students, with classes six days a week (yep, back then there was Saturday morning school).<p>All I remember now is a bit of bafflement at the set theory paraphernalia, rings and groups etc.')
