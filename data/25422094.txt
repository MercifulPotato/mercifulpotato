With their software most any floppy disk could be copied with a standard PC drive; and with a bit of hacking you could do things like read Victor 9000 floppies.<p>There was only one floppy I could never get, a licensed Scrabble game that insisted on writing scores to its game disc. My mom loved that game and we had to buy it twice. It was humiliating, I had this special hardware and I never did figure that one out.<p>* found one: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.biocomp.net&#x2F;o62799.htm" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.biocomp.net&#x2F;o62799.htm</a>')
