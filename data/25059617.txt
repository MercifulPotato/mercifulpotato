Item(by='lotsofpulp', descendants=None, kids=[25060041], score=None, time=1605111700, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25059606, text='Until just a couple years ago, there was a tax penalty for married couples who earned similar amounts.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Marriage_penalty" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Marriage_penalty</a><p>&gt; If the incomes of the two persons are similar, then at the lower end of the tax schedule there is no difference between filing as singles and filing as a married couple (ignoring the question of deductions, see below). But at the higher end of the tax schedule, there is a penalty for a married couple whose incomes are similar, compared to what they would pay as singles.')
