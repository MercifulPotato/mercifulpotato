Item(by='TylerE', descendants=None, kids=[24984961, 24985328, 24984889, 24984796, 24988755, 24985770], score=None, time=1604441524, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24983918, text='Drones are usually copters so they can do vertical takeoffs and hover.<p>They&#x27;re quadcopters rather than single mainrotor + tailrotor because it greatly simplifies the mechanics.<p>Single rotors need a cyclic mechanism to vary the blade pitch depending on where the blade is in the rotation and the control inputs. This is VERY complicated with a lot of moving parts that have to maintained and adjusted. (See <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;upload.wikimedia.org&#x2F;wikipedia&#x2F;commons&#x2F;thumb&#x2F;4&#x2F;4f&#x2F;Sikorsky_S-92_rotor_P1230176.jpg&#x2F;1024px-Sikorsky_S-92_rotor_P1230176.jpg" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;upload.wikimedia.org&#x2F;wikipedia&#x2F;commons&#x2F;thumb&#x2F;4&#x2F;4f&#x2F;Si...</a> for one example)<p>Quads (and hexes, etc) can have full control authority via RPM only, using a simple injection molded constant pitch prop.')
