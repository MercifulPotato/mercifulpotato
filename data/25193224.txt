Item(by='etangent', descendants=None, kids=[25194137], score=None, time=1606174331, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25192571, text='&gt; Stated another way, you&#x27;re saying the people who want change are troublemakers who should know their place.<p>First, that&#x27;s only the case if you assume that by &quot;political&quot; I mean bad. But &quot;political&quot; isn&#x27;t necessarily bad.<p>Secondly, and more importantly, to make something political is often to imagine some unjust status quo to be overturned. But imagination has power of reifying fantasies to actual events. In other words, to make something political is often to exercise power <i>at the expense of others</i> and to justify it by portraying your action as serving to overturn a status quo. Even status quo can use this to further their power! Freedom isn&#x27;t always the net outcome of politics.')
