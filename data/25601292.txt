Item(by='ohazi', descendants=None, kids=[25603453, 25601891, 25601532], score=None, time=1609481521, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25601084, text='That&#x27;s not what aliasing means.<p>&quot;Lollypop&quot; sampling vs. real DAC output: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;mk0soundguyshosprmrt.kinstacdn.com&#x2F;wp-content&#x2F;uploads&#x2F;2019&#x2F;07&#x2F;Quantization-Stairs-vs-Stems.jpg" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;mk0soundguyshosprmrt.kinstacdn.com&#x2F;wp-content&#x2F;upload...</a><p>As you&#x27;ve observed, the output of a real digital circuit includes the flat parts, unlike the infinitely thin &quot;lollypops&quot; that you see in DSP textbooks.<p>But the jaggies themselves <i>are not aliasing</i>, they&#x27;re actually the result of <i>anti</i>-aliasing. The sample-and-hold reconstruction filter that practically every DAC uses <i>is</i> an anti-aliasing filter, albeit a crude one, but if you&#x27;ve chosen your sampling frequency correctly, the harmonics from the jaggies exist <i>entirely outside of your passband</i>. A simple RC filter removes them almost perfectly.<p>This is aliasing: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;upload.wikimedia.org&#x2F;wikipedia&#x2F;commons&#x2F;thumb&#x2F;2&#x2F;28&#x2F;AliasingSines.svg&#x2F;1920px-AliasingSines.svg.png" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;upload.wikimedia.org&#x2F;wikipedia&#x2F;commons&#x2F;thumb&#x2F;2&#x2F;28&#x2F;Al...</a><p>Digitally sampling an analog <i>input</i> signal is usually where you need to actually put some design effort into the AA filter, because you don&#x27;t necessarily know whether the analog signal at your input has a lot of energy just outside your passband. Such an input <i>can</i> cause you to see an alias that&#x27;s comparable to or louder than your signal of interest, and there&#x27;s no way to get around that with direct sampling except for arbitrarily sharp AA filters.<p>But with a DAC, you know exactly what you&#x27;re putting into your AA filter (your sampled signal convolved with a box), and you can easily arrange things so that the AA filter both works perfectly (sampling &gt; nyquist) and is easy to design (sampling &gt;&gt; nyquist).')
