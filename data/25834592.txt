Item(by='Emma_Goldman', descendants=None, kids=[25834715], score=None, time=1611071370, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25834511, text='The substitution might work better, you&#x27;re right, but that&#x27;s also effectively admitting that most jobs aren&#x27;t creative. There is a qualitative difference between working close to the limit of one&#x27;s abilities in a flow state, and having one&#x27;s own ideas, following through the implications, and executing it using one&#x27;s own wits. <i>That&#x27;s</i> creative, and in my experience a lot more gratifying than just being engrossed in work.<p>In any case I think it&#x27;s a mistake to become too transfixed by what features of work are most conducive to happiness. Yes, happiness is important. Perhaps it&#x27;s the single most important criteria for choosing a line of work. But there are other, larger objectives - e.g., helping humankind, producing great things, being authentic to oneself, and leading a good life.')
