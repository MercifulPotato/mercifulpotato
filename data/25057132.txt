Item(by='notahacker', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605089770, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25055617, text='There&#x27;s a difference between the <i>appearance</i> of randomness across multiple companies from employers and employees having different priorities (and levels of need, and mistakes, and luck) and actual randomness as a selection heuristic.\n Actual randomness as a policy gives the company the worst of both worlds: the roll of the dice is likely to often give a good recruit an offer they won&#x27;t possibly accept but also ensures they can follow it up with a significantly higher offer to a worse employee.\nLots of companies sacrifice more productive employees to keep salaries to a budget <i>or</i> consciously pay above market to improve their staff pool, but not many firms aim to both keep many promising recruits&#x27; salaries below an arbitrary figure <i>and</i> are willing to offer well above that figure to some obviously weaker candidates within the same time frame.')
