Being a monopoly is not illegal. Being an uncompetitive monopoly is. [1] But it is also illegal to engage in anti competitive behavior even if you don’t have a literal monopoly. [2]<p>“Courts do not require a literal monopoly before applying rules for single firm conduct; that term is used as shorthand for a firm with significant and durable market power — that is, the long term ability to raise price or exclude competitors. That is how that term is used here: a &quot;monopolist&quot; is a firm with significant and durable market power.” [1]<p>1. <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.ftc.gov&#x2F;tips-advice&#x2F;competition-guidance&#x2F;guide-antitrust-laws&#x2F;single-firm-conduct&#x2F;monopolization-defined" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.ftc.gov&#x2F;tips-advice&#x2F;competition-guidance&#x2F;guide-a...</a><p>2. <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.ftc.gov&#x2F;enforcement&#x2F;anticompetitive-practices" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.ftc.gov&#x2F;enforcement&#x2F;anticompetitive-practices</a>')
