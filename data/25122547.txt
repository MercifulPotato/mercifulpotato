Item(by='sascha_sl', descendants=None, kids=[25122609], score=None, time=1605612955, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25121416, text='Git is hard if you don&#x27;t take the time to learn&#x2F;teach it.<p>As a checkmark on a resume, knowing git doesn&#x27;t mean much. Most developers know how to branch, commit, push and pull. Maybe they know a little bit of reset to get themselves out of bad situations.<p>We had one highly-skilled developer with a background in contributing to very unrelated FOSS join a frontend team once, adding a rule about avoiding merge commits in PRs. Within a month or so they all learned how to rebase and cherry-pick.<p>Git as a skill needs to be taken more seriously, not something you learn on the way to doing actual work.')
