Item(by='dralley', descendants=None, kids=[25265234], score=None, time=1606785083, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25256615, text='What the likely mean, is that newer versions of pip support newer platforms underpinning the binary packages.<p>Python binary packages are a bit of a kludge, they take a binary built on a base platform like CentOS 5, and use tools like patchelf to take all the libraries that they dynamically link against and re-wrap them in a new package.<p>Until about a year ago, CentOS 5 was the newest base platform available. So if your library needed a glibc feature or some other library feature from the past decade you were SoL.')
