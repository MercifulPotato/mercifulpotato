Item(by='jaclaz', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605867693, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25156839, text='Yes, if you want a good insulation you need a mechanical ventilation (with air heat exchanger) otherwise - besides mold - you will be living in a non-healty environment.<p>Even on non particuarly insulated houses our grandmothers and mothers did - at least once a day - change the air, opening windows for some fifteen minutes, there were reasons for that, and they remain valid.<p>Nowadays that tradition is largely lost (for whatever reason) and some way to change air needs to be provided (and it comes with a cost and complications attached).<p>I have seen too many houses built in the early days of energy saving (that began here roughly 10-12 years ago) with serious mold or however humidity issues, and even nowadays, when the issue is (partially) re-known, I see freshly built houses in class B or C (in EU they are classified with the best being A+ and the worst being G) without any mechanical ventilation issues (houses in classes A&#x2F;1&#x2F;2&#x2F;3&#x2F;4 now largely have them).')
