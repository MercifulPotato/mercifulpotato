Item(by='simias', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608117064, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25440792, text='Most tiling WMs are highly configurable, so the idea is that you script them to your tastes and then they do the work for you.<p>I use a rather heavily customized version of dwm myself, and I have things like custom placement rule: I want my editor to be in a specific &quot;workspace&quot; (or tag, in dwm parlance), I have keybindings to focus my editor, my terminal or my web browser in one single keystroke regardless of where I am (I always cringe a bit when I watch colleagues feverishly alt-tabbing to find the window they&#x27;re looking for), I have added custom &quot;sticky&quot; windows that appear in all workspaces etc...<p>It takes a while to set up at first but I think it&#x27;s well worth it in the long run. When I have to use a normal desktop environment on a different computer everything feels clunky and more complicated than it should be.')
