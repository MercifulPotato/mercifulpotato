Item(by='hinkley', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609355472, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25580099, text='It’s been said before, about ROM chips for sure, but I don’t know if history necessarily agrees regarding other chips, that there’s a point at which the opportunity cost of “just enough” silicon is too high and so you end up with a choice between way more than you need for simple tasks, or doing without (eg, embedding that responsibility in some other chip).<p>I think a lot of projects will be targeting ARM CPUs in the next era of computing, and I hope one day we will see entire processes moving off the cpu and onto peripherals.<p>Give me a RAID controller that can run Postgres directly on it, or an SSD that can run SQLite.  Give me a network card that runs eBPF, or even nginx.')
