Item(by='bronson', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607471228, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25353016, text='Lambdas get pricey quick.<p>PAAS tends to lock you in. You don&#x27;t notice it happening as you add features, then you discover that escaping the platform requires significant re-engineering. (When freelancing, one of my more lucrative gigs was helping a company de-Herokuize their app after the original developers moved on).<p>Raw EC2 instances usually end up with wizards SSHing in to deploy &amp; tweak. It&#x27;s sooo tempting.<p>Having participated in all of these scenarios in the past, I&#x27;d recommend any sized company go right to containers. It&#x27;s the straightest road from zero through MVP to scale, and the best for onboarding new developers.<p>That said, don&#x27;t get sidetracked! Pay a company to manage the orchestration. And don&#x27;t burn time on accessories like CI pipelines or blue-green deploys until the important stuff works.')
