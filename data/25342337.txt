Our cultural heroes often feature a sense of &quot;bugger them&quot; if they&#x27;re not inventing something.<p>* Hone Heke, kept cutting down a flagpole to mess with the British<p>* The Southern Man popularised by a beer company, who prefers the company of his horse.<p>* The Good Keen Man popularised by Barry Crump, who prefers the company of the hills and his rifle.<p>* James MacKenzie, a sheep rustler who ended up with a sizable part of the South Island named after him (the MacKenzie Country)<p>* Edmund Hillary travelling to the South Pole by tractor &quot;accidentally on purpose&quot; when he was supposed to be laying out supplies for a British Lord&#x27;s expedition.<p>However, what we have in our culture that the US seems to lack (from my _very_ distant POV) is a slight tendency towards collectivism over individualism.<p>Maybe it&#x27;s from our long gone years as a &quot;cradle to the grave&quot; social democracy, maybe it&#x27;s from the emphasis on mateship.<p>Maybe it&#x27;s because we don&#x27;t really like interpersonal conflict. (How do you know a Kiwi didn&#x27;t like their meal in your restaurant? We haven&#x27;t figured that out yet).')
