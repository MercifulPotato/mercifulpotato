(Also, that&#x27;s a flax story, not a jax story. A bit confusing, but think of jax like numpy as flax like pytorch -- jax is the low level, flax is the high level.)<p>I haven&#x27;t found a way that I like. The closest I&#x27;ve come is tftorch: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;twitter.com&#x2F;theshawwn&#x2F;status&#x2F;1311925180126511104" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;twitter.com&#x2F;theshawwn&#x2F;status&#x2F;1311925180126511104</a><p>I think it&#x27;s important to have global scope names. &quot;biggan.discriminator.3.conv1.kernel.b&quot; is perfectly sensible: it&#x27;s the bias value of the kernel for the convolution of the third block of your biggan discriminator.<p>Everyone tries to treat model variables as interchangeable nameless parts. I hate it. Every variable has a global name, conceptually.<p>A global name also has other benefits. It becomes far easier to create EMA weights, for example, since you can filter the variables by name.')
