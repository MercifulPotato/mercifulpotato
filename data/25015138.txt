Item(by='golergka', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604758001, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25015105, text='It can be a very bad thing or an acceptable payment for developer velocity, based on what&#x27;s your business priorities with the project.<p>I have a couple of issues that I worked on that started exactly like this: front-end developer implemented a feature based on GraphQL, but when we deployed this feature, we quickly disabled it after receiving alerts from production database, and re-enabled it back only after backend developer (me) went and worked on database indexing.<p>But for my team, at the current point of the project&#x27;s development, this scenario is a net positive overall, because we&#x27;re exactly in &quot;move fast and break things&quot; mentality, which suits our product and our place in the market. If our project would require a lot of 9s, or worked with very confidential data, or had 10s of millions of daily users, we would have completely different internal procedures and development practices: much safer and much slower.')
