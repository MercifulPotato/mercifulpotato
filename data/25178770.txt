I can imagine &quot;Kindly do the needful, old chap&quot; scrawled in the margin of an official missive, as it languidly flitted from one administrative table to another.<p>Many such anachronisms exist; thankfully, they&#x27;re dying out one by one. A few decades ago (in the last century, to be precise), it wasn&#x27;t very uncommon for official letters to be signed off with &quot;I remain, Sir, your obedient servant&quot;. Later, it was shortened to &quot;yours obediently&quot;; it vanished altogether, thankfully.')
