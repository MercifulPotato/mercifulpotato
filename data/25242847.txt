Item(by='EbTech', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606622716, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25237744, text='I don&#x27;t think frequentism is &quot;bad&quot;; just insufficient as a gold standard interpretation of probabilistic claims. I liked an analogy from the reference by Rathmanner &amp; Hutter: the most &quot;correct&quot; chess-playing program involves a complete search along the tree of possible games. In practice, we try to approximate this ideal.<p>In the case of Kolmogorov complexity, a reasonable takeaway might be to use the shortest program that we&#x27;re able to find, even if it&#x27;s not the shortest overall.')
