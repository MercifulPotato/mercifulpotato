Item(by='anticristi', descendants=None, kids=[25889110, 25888426, 25887171, 25887866, 25886794], score=None, time=1611436994, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25882722, text='I can&#x27;t help feeling that the question is ill-posed. It&#x27;s not &quot;how much&quot;, but &quot;why&quot; that matters.<p>Think about it: Would you borrow $10,000 to a friend who opens a dentist with a solid business plan? Probably yes.<p>Would you borrow $1000 to a friend to cover an existing debt? Probably not, or, if you are very generous, you would gift them the money.<p>If a government can demonstrate that it borrows money to invest in the economy and increase future taxes -- e.g., by building infrastructure, educating the population, researching top-notch tech -- by all means, borrow more!<p>However, if all the government does is increase social welfare that it already struggles to afford or satisfy the luxury of a dictator, then stop borrowing now!')
