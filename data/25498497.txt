Item(by='fentonc', descendants=None, kids=[25502798, 25500379], score=None, time=1608579264, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25498355, text='All of the early Cray mainframes (even the Cray-1 discussed here, although maybe only the somewhat later Cray-1&#x2F;S) supported a &quot;Solid State Disk&quot; peripheral, which was basically a refrigerator full of RAM that was accessible via DMA channel. The intent was to be used to swap in and out large data sets from memory - I think even the early ones went up to a gigabyte in size.')
