Item(by='staticfloat', descendants=None, kids=[24840911], score=None, time=1603218852, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24840670, text='Julia&#x27;s SIMD programming model is still very much a work in progress; I think we have a way to go in providing the kind of flexibility and control that languages such as ISPC, Halide, TVM, etc... provide.<p>That being said, packages such as SIMD.jl [0], and LoopVectorization.jl [1] are making fantastic progress, to the point that LoopVectorization forms the basis of a legitimate BLAS contender, in pure Julia [2].  It&#x27;s not totally there yet, but it&#x27;s close enough that real work is being done in LV at OpenBLAS-like speeds.<p>As an aside, I find it incredible that these kinds of extensions can be built in packages thanks to the fact that Julia&#x27;s compiler is extensible enough to allow for direct manipulation of the LLVM intrinsics being emitted by user code.<p>[0] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;eschnett&#x2F;SIMD.jl" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;eschnett&#x2F;SIMD.jl</a>\n[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;chriselrod&#x2F;LoopVectorization.jl" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;chriselrod&#x2F;LoopVectorization.jl</a>\n[2] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;MasonProtter&#x2F;Gaius.jl" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;MasonProtter&#x2F;Gaius.jl</a>')
