God? But then if you look at every religion that claims it speaks for God, you&#x27;ll find something in the proposed moral standards you&#x27;ll disagree with on moral grounds. &quot;Structure of the universe&quot;? That&#x27;s meaningless unless you point out what aspect of that structure determines morality, and I&#x27;m willing to bet it&#x27;ll turn out that that aspect can be fully captured in the workings of the brain. What else is there to ground on?<p>As far as I know, all moral questions under all moral philosophies, no matter how convoluted, are ultimately verified by testing the conclusions against examples simple enough that you can get a gut-feeling answer for them. Every criticism of a moral philosophy I&#x27;ve ever seen boils down to finding an example where applying some high-level rule leads to conclusions that cannot be reconciled with the gut.')
