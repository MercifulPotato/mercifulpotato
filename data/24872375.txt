Item(by='sk2020', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603477743, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24870677, text='If Mozilla shut its doors for good and Firefox became abandonware, would not all the donations and volunteer labor look for another browser project, perhaps one with a more principled stance on user privacy? Would that not be a good thing? There are some small alternative projects out there. I don’t think a political analogy is a good one. Software is not a winner-take-all market (albeit it often is anti-competitive). You can install Nyxt even though Chrome is more popular.<p>The (small) gopher renaissance and Gemini are good examples of how excited some people are to change what the web IS (and consequently, make hobby-browsers an option). If you can’t view documents online without millions in software development, then maybe THAT is the real problem.')
