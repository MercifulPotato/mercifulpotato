Item(by='NovemberWhiskey', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602271452, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24733100, text='Aren&#x27;t the usual reasons we buy a service rather than doing something ourselves chosen from a list that goes something like:<p>* X is available more cheaply from a third party supplier (who presumably has achieved some kinds of economies of scale) than our fully-loaded cost to deliver the same<p>* X is a commodity product where we do not feel we can differentiate ourselves by doing it in-house<p>* X is ancillary, but important, to our core business and we lack the expertise to do it well, so we will buy in rather than build that expertise<p>Not many hotels are bothering to drill their own wells these days, bearing in mind that water is available from utility suppliers - why would room cleaning service be immune from the same forces?')
