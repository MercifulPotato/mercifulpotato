Item(by='btilly', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605805703, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25148276, text='No, they can&#x27;t.<p>Programmers have to deal with the outside world.  The outside world has timezones defined as offsets from UTC.  So when UTC changes, the whole world changes their clocks, and programmers have to cope.  If your program doesn&#x27;t agree with that world about what time it is, your program will get blamed.<p>If it was a choice that I could individually make, I would tell astronomers to go pound sand.  But it isn&#x27;t.  By historical happenstance, they have been put in charge of time, and it is truly a question of the tail wagging the dog when they care about a 1 second deviation between UTC and the position of the sun at a particular longitude.')
