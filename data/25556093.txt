Item(by='plasticxme', descendants=None, kids=[25556109], score=None, time=1609121702, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25555823, text='The reason is because object storage is slow and not meant for high performance, which is usually important for large databases.<p>For your S3 example and ignoring IOPS, you are comparing 13ms of latency on local spinning disk versus 10s-100s of ms latency from S3. SSD is faster with only 1ms of latency or less on average.<p>Adding IOPS to the equation, you’re likely going to slam your object store if you have a high volume of traffic, where your block storage likely wouldn’t even break a sweat.')
