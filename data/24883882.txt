Item(by='setr', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603600818, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24881271, text='It is my understanding that the only functional difference between a database, and a blockchain, is the elimination of a trusted third party who would have to administer the database (and could, maliciously, alter that DB)<p>AFAICT, this is not such a problem where a third party cannot be trusted (the only incentive would be if they contacted you and tried to bribe the modification, which doesn&#x27;t seem worth it).<p>Otherwise, if the DBA can be trusted, it&#x27;s not clear to me that the blockchain offers any additional verification, encryption, audit-ability, etc -- and it doesn&#x27;t seem to me that the &quot;DBA cannot be trusted&quot; is a sufficiently possible risk scenario to try to eliminate from the equation')
