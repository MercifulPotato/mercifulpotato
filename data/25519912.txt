Item(by='cgriswald', descendants=None, kids=[25521945], score=None, time=1608744694, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25517868, text='I see a lot of comments here (and some in the YouTube recommendation thread currently on the front page) suggesting sometimes a Rube Goldberg-esque series of behaviors to trick the algorithm into performing properly. I see this mentioned as &quot;easy&quot; and &quot;trivial&quot;. I consider it &quot;abrasive&quot; and &quot;tedious.&quot; The algorithms should work <i>through normal use of the app</i>. I shouldn&#x27;t have to tell an app &quot;Ignore this one-off piece of content I reached through an external link that is different from all the other content I&#x27;ve enjoyed and especially different than what I&#x27;ve searched.&quot; If I get to the point where I have to curate at that level of detail, what&#x27;s the point?<p>It&#x27;s less work to do what I&#x27;ve been doing all along: get recommendations from friends, family, articles, collabs, biographies, <i>etc.</i> (And it&#x27;s a lot more fun.)')
