Sometimes programs take a single argument that contains the whole command. @Q allows to safely escape the list of arguments. This is a synthetic example:<p><pre><code>    args=(&quot;my&quot; &quot;arg&quot; &quot;with space&quot;)\n    bash -c &quot;rm ${args[*]@Q}&quot;\n</code></pre>\nThis makes sure that the script will `rm` &quot;my&quot; &quot;arg&quot; and &quot;with space&quot;, and not &quot;with&quot; and &quot;space&quot; :)')
