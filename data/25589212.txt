Item(by='studius', descendants=None, kids=[25589349], score=None, time=1609384618, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25589168, text='We have safety statistics on real cars with a minority of autonomous vehicles on the road.<p>What we don&#x27;t have are adequate safety statistics on autonomous cars on the road with each other and human drivers at various ratios of autonomous cars to human-driven cars.<p>If a study were to be able to propose safety numbers based on the various ratios and various autonomous cars and systems, then that would be adequate, given the theories behind those numbers are well-founded.<p>If you were to say, &quot;This is how it is: given that the ratio of autonomous cars to human-driven cars isn&#x27;t changing and we expect it to stay the same, we can see that the autonomous cars are 4-5x safer,&quot; then I&#x27;d not call it a lie.<p>But 4-5x safer without qualifications shows a gross misunderstanding of the importance of the effect of the ratio of autonomous drivers to human drivers; the variation in environment and expected behavior could influence greater or decreased safety, leading false assumption.')
