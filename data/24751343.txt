Item(by='wyclif', descendants=None, kids=[24751620], score=None, time=1602476298, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24743984, text='<i>The Consolation of Philosophy</i> was required reading for my undergrad Chaucer classes and other Medieval Studies courses, because <i>Boece</i> is Chaucer&#x27;s translation into Middle English of Boethius&#x27; Consolation. For me it was an ancillary study but I ended up going pretty far down the rabbit hole into other ancient philosophical works from there.<p>Why read Boethius today? The answer, as the author says, is because Boethius is a still a guide to those who want to &quot;seek answers about how to live in relation to our mortality.&quot;')
