Item(by='mumblemumble', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611598018, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25905667, text='It almost goes both ways. Yes, being on an embedded device with no Internet access reduces your external attack surface. But it also limits your ability to use lots of newer language features designed to improve safety, because those features often cost CPU cycles or memory.<p>My (completely uninformed) guess is that most of any net benefit from being on an embedded system comes from the simple fact that &quot;embedded&quot; implies &quot;constrained&quot;, which implies &quot;less code,&quot; which, in turn, implies &quot;fewer opportunities to create bugs in the first place.&quot;')
