Item(by='carbonguy', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608941999, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25540093, text='The sibling comment from jeffbee is essentially correct, depending on which part of the &quot;petroleum infrastructure&quot; you&#x27;re referring to in your comment: the price of extracting and burning fossil hydrocarbon fuels does not reflect the cost of their externalities, primarily climate change driven by CO2 emissions.<p>However, you are right in the sense that taking advantage of our current hydrocarbon distribution infrastructure would save capital costs versus switching to, e.g., an all-hydrogen or all-electric energy infrastructure.
