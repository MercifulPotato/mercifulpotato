It inevitably ends up much better documented and tested code than the usual company developed tooling. And you get to keep it when you leave.<p>No need to spend time on it at home if you don’t want to.<p>And the company benefits as well as it now has much better internal codebase, people might consider maintaining it even after they’ve left the company, and it acts like a pr&#x2F;recruitment hook.')
