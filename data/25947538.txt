Item(by='Geminidog', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611861893, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25946625, text='When you divvy up shares in an investment to meaningless portions like 0.00001% of a company there&#x27;s no utility in the investment period.<p>It then becomes the same thing as buying and selling useless paper. In order to make the system truly a market you have to restrict the size of the portions. Something like 1% is the smallest cut someone can own.<p>But then that would stop the huge amount of cash companies tend to receive during an IPO thereby restricting the growth of the actual business.')
