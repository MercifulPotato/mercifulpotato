Item(by='twblalock', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609453320, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25596101, text='Part of the problem is selling front and rear derailleur gearing to cyclists for whom it does not make sense.<p>Internal gear hubs make a lot more sense for casual cyclists -- they are easier to learn, they can be shifted when the bicycle is not moving, and they are far less likely to drop a chain during shifting than derailleur gearing. They are also better protected from weather and impact.<p>Almost all of the utility bicycles I&#x27;ve seen in Japan and Europe had internal gear hubs. I didn&#x27;t see many people commuting on the kind of bicycles that American cycling advocates seem to prefer.')
