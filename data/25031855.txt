Item(by='LukeShu', descendants=None, kids=[25033650], score=None, time=1604901874, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25031208, text='Yes, Linux&#x2F;m68k mostly lived out-of-tree for a while (with bits and pieces of it landing upstream); from its inception in 1993 until it was (iirc) fully merged in 1996.<p>Being the first non-x86 port, a lot of things originated there.  The virtual console everyone is familiar with originated in Linux&#x2F;m68k; the i386 Linux used the hardware&#x27;s built-in text-mode, but the Amiga only had a bitmap display, so in order to get a shell they had to implement a virtual text display that rasterized the text in software.<p>Maddog had the Alpha sent to Linus in 1994, but Linus didn&#x27;t begin work until January 1995.<p>Here&#x27;s the June 1993 announcement of Linux&#x2F;m68k 0.05, which I understand to be the first release that was publicly distributed <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.ibiblio.org&#x2F;pub&#x2F;historic-linux&#x2F;ftp-archives&#x2F;tsx-11.mit.edu&#x2F;Oct-07-1996&#x2F;680x0&#x2F;old&#x2F;ANNOUNCE-0.05.tar.gz" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.ibiblio.org&#x2F;pub&#x2F;historic-linux&#x2F;ftp-archives&#x2F;tsx-1...</a> I haven&#x27;t actually been able to track down that release tarball, but the 0.05p1 patch release is at <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.oldlinux.org&#x2F;Linux.old&#x2F;ftp-archives&#x2F;tsx-11.mit.edu&#x2F;1993-12-07&#x2F;680x0&#x2F;src&#x2F;linux-005.1.tar.gz" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.oldlinux.org&#x2F;Linux.old&#x2F;ftp-archives&#x2F;tsx-11.mit.ed...</a> More links at <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.reddit.com&#x2F;r&#x2F;archlinux&#x2F;comments&#x2F;ejkp1x&#x2F;what_is_the_name_of_this_font&#x2F;fczfy60&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.reddit.com&#x2F;r&#x2F;archlinux&#x2F;comments&#x2F;ejkp1x&#x2F;what_is_t...</a>')
