Item(by='TheOtherHobbes', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608507471, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25489147, text='And in the music business - and probably also the movie business, and to some extent the writing business - there&#x27;s been a strong subculture of payola and industrial promotion. \nThe major labels hire pluggers - as they&#x27;re known - who used to deal with radio DJs and now deal with playlist curators.<p>Sometimes money changes hands and marketable but otherwise not very interesting music suddenly appears on hundreds of playlists - in the hope that it will break through into mass appeal.<p>You can only push this so far because ultimately listeners still decide what&#x27;s hot and what isn&#x27;t. But they&#x27;re forced to make their choices from a limited and crafted pool of suspected high-performing product, and not from a much wider pool of more varied styles and less homogenous artists.')
