Item(by='gvhst', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607545922, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25346456, text='MDMA Increases Cooperation and Recruitment of Social Brain Areas When Playing Trustworthy Players in an Iterated Prisoner&#x27;s Dilemma<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.jneurosci.org&#x2F;content&#x2F;39&#x2F;2&#x2F;307" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.jneurosci.org&#x2F;content&#x2F;39&#x2F;2&#x2F;307</a><p>Abstract ran through a text optimizer:<p>We administered 100 mg MDMA or placebo to 20 male participants in a double-blind, placebo-controlled, crossover study.<p>Cooperation with trustworthy, but not untrustworthy, opponents was enhanced following MDMA but not placebo.<p>Specifically, MDMA enhanced recovery from, but not the impact of, breaches in cooperation.<p>During trial outcome, MDMA increased activation of four clusters incorporating precentral and supramarginal, gyri, superior temporal cortex, central operculum&#x2F;posterior insula, and supplementary motor area.<p>MDMA increased cooperative behavior when playing trustworthy opponents.<p>Our findings highlight the context-specific nature of MDMA&#x27;s effect on social decision-making.<p>While breaches of trustworthy behavior have a similar impact following administration of MDMA compared with placebo, MDMA facilitates a greater recovery from these breaches of trust.')
