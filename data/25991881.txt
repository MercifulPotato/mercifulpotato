Germany is getting gas from Azerbaijan via AP (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.oedigital.com&#x2F;news&#x2F;484249-azerbaijan-starts-gas-exports-to-europe" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.oedigital.com&#x2F;news&#x2F;484249-azerbaijan-starts-gas-...</a>, but TAP is only able to deliver 20b cm^3 (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.dw.com&#x2F;en&#x2F;southern-gas-corridor-project-about-to-come-on-stream&#x2F;a-55555077" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.dw.com&#x2F;en&#x2F;southern-gas-corridor-project-about-to...</a>), and not all of that goes to Europe. And Turkey has a choke hold on the gas transports, much like Ukraine has on the Yamal pipeline.<p>I am not an expert in natural gas markets, but it seems obvious that Germany needs to replace the Dutch shortfall either:<p>- From Russia<p>- From Norway, but they might want to get out of fossil fuels (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.newscientist.com&#x2F;article&#x2F;2196024-norway-is-starting-the-worlds-biggest-divestment-in-oil-and-gas&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.newscientist.com&#x2F;article&#x2F;2196024-norway-is-start...</a>) and their gas is also needed to replace coal consumption in Germany<p>- From the UK, but reserves are dwindling<p>- From the USA, but fracking gas is expensive, has a bad reputation in Germany and carrier transports might be less reliable than pipelines<p>- From Azerbaijan which is an autocratic country with a lot of potential for violence, which also just started a war.<p>=&gt; None of the options are pretty.')
