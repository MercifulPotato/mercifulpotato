Item(by='TeMPOraL', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602840975, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24792339, text='But you still don&#x27;t own the tools. Can&#x27;t run Photoshop if Adobe decides they can&#x27;t, or don&#x27;t want to, serve you anymore.<p>There are some other comments that highlight why this matters, but I want to add to this: a year ago, the US government decided that they very much don&#x27;t like Venezuelan government anymore, and issued sanctions. Adobe promptly announced that they&#x27;ll cancel all subscriptions in Venezuela, no refunds - essentially shutting down the entire creative industry in the country. Now to their benefit, they&#x27;ve managed to get an exemption from the White House just before deadline and eventually did not cancel Venezuelan subscriptions - but was the US government a bit more stubborn, this could have played out in entirely different way.<p>Point being, a hard dependency on a non-commodity cloud service introduces a new class of problems that locally owned software doesn&#x27;t have.')
