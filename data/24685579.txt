The one that gets me in Philippines is the idiomatic usage &quot;until now.&quot; Example phrase &quot;The bus didn&#x27;t arrive until now.&quot;<p>&quot;Standard&quot; English: The bus arrived as I was speaking<p>PH English: The bus has not arrived yet<p>I believe this is a literal translation from Filipino &quot;hanggang ngayon&quot; where &quot;ngayon&quot; does double duty as both &quot;now&quot; and &quot;currently &#x2F; presently&quot;.')
