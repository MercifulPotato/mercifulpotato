Item(by='hinkley', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1612199463, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25989926, text='You want the type of person who helps someone help themselves.<p>Part of that, often overlooked, is doing a summary at the end explaining what were the concrete steps taken to get the result (if you have to poke around a bit to find the right spot, that can be disorienting). It&#x27;s also giving people tools with guard rails built in so they can trust doing the task themselves.<p>You don&#x27;t want people who get off on owning a workflow, and are super-helpful but involved in perpetuity. There&#x27;s a fine line between &#x27;let Tom and Harry do it because they seem to enjoy it while I don&#x27;t,&#x27; and &#x27;everyone thinks Tom and Harry&#x27;s solution is bullshit and I want nothing to do with it.&#x27; The latter leads to codependency, and these people are toxic to productivity, accidentally or intentionally.')
