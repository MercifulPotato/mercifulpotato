Item(by='tyingq', descendants=None, kids=[25092307], score=None, time=1605360986, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25090760, text='I always wondered why Unix didn&#x27;t come with a standard record based file in addition to the stream based file. Mainframes and other OSs, like VMS, for example, have both fixed width and variable width record based files.<p>So, if you&#x27;re writing a database, there&#x27;s already a bullet proof and fast backend to store things that comes with the OS and is maintained.  No need to evaluate various layers on top of a stream (RocksDB, LMDB, etc).')
