Item(by='manish_gill', descendants=None, kids=[25984418, 25984734], score=None, time=1612135883, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25983540, text='The general trend in these posts (and tweet storms) is the same - pick any technology and then preach the wisdom of not using it because &quot;You&#x27;re not Google&quot;, or &quot;You won&#x27;t need it&quot;. It&#x27;s a great tactic as it puts the speaker in a position of judgement, and they can freely share their opinions on some architecture, without getting any pushback from the creators of said architecture. Quite a one-sided affair that makes you look like you know everything. It&#x27;s, frankly, elitist.<p>This entire article can be surmised by &quot;You don&#x27;t need Kafka because the throughput seems to be low&quot;. To which the retort can be many: they might be building for the future - to integrate more systems, they might have fan-out use cases, they might want to have a log to replay in case of disasters - so on and so forth. All well documented and very valid use cases of Kafka.<p>But of course, &quot;You don&#x27;t need Kafka&quot; because &lt;reasons&gt;.')
