Item(by='_Microft', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604481659, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24987423, text='Direct democracy sounds great but I doubt large enough fractions of the voters can be informed well enough (in a reasonable amount of time, not in general) to be actually aware of the consequences of their decisions on most topics.<p>If you want to read about a textbook example of under-informed voters getting more than they bargained for, look no further than [0] (or well, the &quot;Brexit&quot;).\nA national-conservative party wanted to limit immigration to Switzerland and put that up for decision. The Swiss voted in support of it but the issue is ... immigration is part of a treaty with the EU that is protected against such &quot;cherry-picking&quot; by a so-called &quot;guillotine clause&quot; [1]. Cancelling or failing to renew any part of the treaty voids it as a whole[2].
