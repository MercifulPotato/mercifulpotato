Item(by='mlyle', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606441962, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25223640, text='&gt; Digital twins are already used today. 3D Scanning adoption is growing quickly across a whole range of industries.<p>You might be considering a lot broader things to be spatial computing that are short of the sense being considered here.<p>Yes, representing objects and annotating trivial scenes is interesting and is starting to happen today.<p>But free-form automated representation of the environment in ways that the spatial shortcuts we rely upon (&quot;inside&quot;, &quot;on top of&quot;, etc) can be used, (non-graph) paths of different types can be solved, and actuator kinematics can find and interact with things, etc: it&#x27;s really hard.<p>AR and VR and being able to interact with object meshes and geometry is a start.  But computational geometry and encoding world constraints in a useful way is really hard.  We&#x27;ll snatch some incremental parts from AR and vision research, but we&#x27;ll need some breakthroughs in the space, too, to do the really cool stuff.')
