Item(by='mensetmanusman', descendants=None, kids=[25022438], score=None, time=1604755097, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25013279, text='Not directly, but you can do really clever things with nonlinearity, look at this recent development:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.eurekalert.org&#x2F;pub_releases&#x2F;2020-11&#x2F;ou-1ei110320.php" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.eurekalert.org&#x2F;pub_releases&#x2F;2020-11&#x2F;ou-1ei110320...</a><p>Essentially, if you trap a few photons in little resonator cavities that rely on mostly all of the electrons making up the cavity, the next set of photons can pass through the cavity without interaction.<p>This means the light interacts with engineered electrons in a specific way to create an optical switch. This is a critical component of photonics on a chip, and was just demonstrated with Si (which is a requirement for something in the mid term).')
