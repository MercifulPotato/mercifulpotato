Item(by='InitialLastName', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611173101, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25850777, text='If you make it 18 year terms and stagger the appointments (as grandfathered-in justices die or retire) to every 2 years, you can make it so that each presidential term appoints two justices, which at least removes some of the potential for luck to dramatically shift the balance of the court.<p>If that were the case, and justices&#x27; terms started in January of even years (to avoid the election cycle), we would currently have 2 Trump appointees, 4 Obama appointees, and 3 Bush appointees, roughly matching what the balance of the court would be next month if Ginsburg had died 3 months later.')
