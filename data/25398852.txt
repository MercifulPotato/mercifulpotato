Yes, becoming indistinguishable from a native speaker is a pretty high bar, though having a fairly non-descript accent I think is achievable.<p>For cases of non-native speakers mastering an accent, one anecdote I heard of was Hugh Laurie&#x27;s audition tape for the role of Dr House. The story goes that the producers of the show considered it extremely important to have the character be played such that he was quintessentially American, and were very happy with the audition tape from Hugh Laurie, who convinced them on this front - only to meet him later and be shocked at his native RP accent.')
