Item(by='wocram', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608225157, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25457455, text='Retail orders generally trade with against market makers off-exchange, between the quoted market price. If the quote is Buy @ $1.00 x Sell @ $1.01, a retail order would be able to buy at a price like $1.0099.<p>That $.0001 savings is known as &quot;price improvement&quot;. Separately, the market maker pays a negotiated ahead of time rate to Robinhood for sourcing the order, aka &quot;payment for order flow&quot;.')
