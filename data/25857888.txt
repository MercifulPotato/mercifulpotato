Where I come from (southern Germany), the word &quot;Apfelsine&quot; is not used at all. If you used it here, people would most likely immediately ask if you mean an orange. As I understand it, it&#x27;s only used north of the &quot;Speyer Line&quot;:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;de.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Speyerer_Linie_(Isoglosse)" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;de.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Speyerer_Linie_(Isoglosse)</a><p>One explanation would be that southern Germany has more direct contact to France, and therefore uses the French &quot;Orange&quot;, whereas northern Germany uses the low German &quot;Apel de Sina&quot; (basically &quot;China-Apple&quot;, &quot;sina&quot; as in &quot;Sinology&quot;).
