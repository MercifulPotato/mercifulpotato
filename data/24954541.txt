Item(by='dnautics', descendants=None, kids=[24955232], score=None, time=1604173319, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24952777, text='that&#x27;s not why gold is valuable, historically.  Historically gold is valuable because it&#x27;s rivalrous, fungible, and most importantly, easily verifiable.<p>You can assess approximate purity of the metal with very low-level technology (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Touchstone_(assaying_tool)" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Touchstone_(assaying_tool)</a>).  Transactions thus have a lowered barrier of trust.  Before gold and money, you could only transact in debt to labor (see debt: 5000 years) or socialized obligations, and to be sure the person would conduct that labor, you had to trust them, or trust society to honor the obligation.<p>More interestingly in particular, once gold came into being, two people could reliable transact over a very long social distances, (without necessarily knowing each other&#x27;s reputation).  You just had to trust an intermediary party to transport the gold.  Nonetheless, that opened markets up considerably.')
