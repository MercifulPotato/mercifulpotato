(E.g. high quality comment by a not famous person)<p>Twitter seems to value the individual, but devalue the content. (E.g. low quality posts by a famous person)<p>The former is more meritorious, inasmuch as the vote base can accurately judge a comment. The latter is more consistent, in that popular people stay popular (and admittedly, are popular for a reason).<p>Personally, I prefer the HN-style model, but I also believe it only works as long as the ratio of HN-encultured users to bad &#x2F; average actors stays above a certain threshold. From a <i>technical</i> and <i>vote system</i> perspective, HN isn&#x27;t that different than Reddit: what makes it HN is the culture and community.')
