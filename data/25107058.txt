Item(by='Jedd', descendants=None, kids=[25107186], score=None, time=1605491364, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25097388, text='You didn&#x27;t mention where you are.<p>In any case, regardless of geographical location, voltage doesn&#x27;t kill you -- current does.<p>In Australia most household circuits are ~230V AC at 50Hz at 10 Amp, though 15 Amp circuits are not uncommon (the earth pin - we have three pins in a frowny-face configuration, earth being the lower &#x2F; vertical one) on those are much larger, so you can&#x27;t accidentally put that into a standard 10A (female) socket.<p>We have three-phase options too, which is ~ 415V AC - not something that&#x27;s commonly used in residential environments.<p>In any case, 230V @ 10A is less dangerous than 110V at 15A.')
