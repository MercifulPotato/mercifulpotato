Item(by='lrvick', descendants=None, kids=[25875983, 25873623], score=None, time=1611334682, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25873113, text='I don&#x27;t buy the &quot;you can&#x27;t blame anyone except the person that pulls the trigger&quot; argument.<p>That type of whataboutisim is at the heart of every major engineering effort of silicon valley and surveillance capitalisim that is used abusively on the masses.<p>We can say no. We -must- say no.<p>Making alcohol should be legal. Specifically selling it to a kid is not legal, because then you are specifically helping them commit a crime.<p>Likewise I don&#x27;t blame steel mills for producing steel that is later used in guns wielded by dictators on their own people.<p>I sure do blame the people that sell the guns to said dictators with full knowledge of their likely use case though.<p>We as engineers have an ethical responsibility if we make a nuke with full knowledge the buyer probably intends to drop it on a city.<p>I once built a company that produced a very effective social data aggregator, classifier, and sentiment analysis engine with an easy search interface. It quickly identified sentiment trends across a dozen networks and could of been amazing for brand managers or stock traders. My investors decided they wanted to repurpose it for political propaganda and market it to a specific party. I could of said &quot;well this is really cool tech, and I am making good money... Not my problem what they do with it&quot;. I thought that for a bit. I even helped build an MVP for political candidate tracking.<p>It felt wrong, so I quit. I didn&#x27;t help them replace me. The company ended and all my years of work and research with it. So be it.<p>I don&#x27;t always get it right, but looking back, -not- continuing that project remains one of the proudest choices of my life.')
