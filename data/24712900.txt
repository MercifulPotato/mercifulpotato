Item(by='crdrost', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602105826, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24712084, text='Note that the zero-energy universe is highly speculative; in particular there is no good way to define the total energy confined to a volume at large scales and it&#x27;s hard to say that &quot;energy is conserved&quot; in any way that&#x27;s nontrivial. (A ready example is the apparent loss of energy in the redshifting of light due to Hubble expansion.)')
