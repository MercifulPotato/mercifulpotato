Item(by='akimball', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610985348, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25822013, text='The differential equations are themselves in many cases an abstraction over statistical numbers of discrete events.  Arguably, in those cases, they are the wrong abstraction - a thesis which can only be proven by demonstrating the superiority of a different abstraction.  Has one been demonstrated?  Not yet, for most purposes.  Is this a promising avenue of study?  IMO, yes.')
