Item(by='dagmx', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609343928, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25581094, text='I&#x27;ve learned the best way to get ideas going in a corporate settings are:<p>- pitch the idea to as many folks as possible. Not just to the ones who are going to okay it. Even if only a small portion of each idea resonates with each person, you now have them all slightly on board, which is all you need to get a foot in the door. The more people on board, the more you can back yourself up with to get the okay<p>- do proof of concepts yourself. I always carve out some time to implement a MVP of my ideas, and then use that to hook people.<p>- make a design doc. I hate doing this personally, but a lot of people respond well to the idea of one, even if most won&#x27;t read it. It&#x27;s just the idea that it&#x27;s well thought out, even if the design doc is mostly fluff and will change dramatically. People love procedural work.')
