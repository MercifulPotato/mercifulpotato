Item(by='jamesblonde', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606887907, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25271791, text='Listing and update consistency is great, but the ACID SQL-on-S3 frameworks (Apache Hudi, Dela Lake) also require atomic rename to support transactions.<p>HopsFS-S3 solves the ACID SQL-on-S3 problem discussed here on HN last week ( <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=25149154" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=25149154</a> ) by adding a metadata layer over S3 and providing a POSIX-like HDFS API to clients. Operations like rename, mv, chown, chmod are metadata operations - rename is not copy and delete.<p>Disclosure: I work on HopsFS')
