Item(by='__blockcipher__', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611264409, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25864317, text='And more importantly, what utility does that have for the network?<p>The whole idea in their heads is that nodes will &quot;validate&quot; transactions. Which doesn&#x27;t make any sense because it&#x27;s the person engaging in a transaction (on either end) that cares about the state of the blockchain, not some random neutral third party. Your full node can detect an invalid transaction all day but without a way to tell the guy who&#x27;s about to treat that invalid transaction as valid, the utility isn&#x27;t there.<p>No, the real threat to the Bitcoin network is and has always been that a hostile actor would get 51% hashpower (or almost 50% but not quite and roll the dice until they won a few blocks in a row) and issue double-spend attacks.')
