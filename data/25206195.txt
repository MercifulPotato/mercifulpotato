Item(by='01100011', descendants=None, kids=[25206286], score=None, time=1606280822, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25203323, text='It really depends.  Like many &quot;software&quot; discussions on HN, it depends what type of software you work on.  It depends on the companies you work for.  It depends on your reputation and the types of jobs you take.  You can have folks swearing that their 20 year consulting&#x2F;contracting career was a breeze arguing with people who complain about bad customers and unpaid invoices.<p>I&#x27;ve had customers that want every minute accounted for.  I&#x27;ve been pressed to cut my rate for time spent in meetings.  I&#x27;ve also had years where the invoices were paid and no questions were asked.  I often developed good, personal relationships with clients, even the ones asking me to cut my rate for meetings.<p>It depends what the company is looking for.  It depends on if you oversold yourself, or failed to manage expectations.  It&#x27;s easy to promise too much to get the job.  It helps to work with people who are current&#x2F;former engineers because they understand realities better.  That&#x27;s not always the case, but generally.')
