Item(by='Reason077', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610493102, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25753872, text='Rapid tests (aka: Antigen tests, or lateral flow tests) are <i>high specificity, low sensitivity</i> tests.<p>That means they’re biased towards the <i>negative</i> side: they’re unlikely to return false positives, but quite likely to give false negatives.<p>Such tests are useful for epidemiological studies, or for answering the “do I have Covid antibodies?” question.<p>But they’re not a reliable way to actually test for Covid. You need a PCR test for that!')
