Item(by='redvenom', descendants=None, kids=[25062424, 25061573], score=None, time=1605120346, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25060960, text='Totally agree with this post...however, to be extremely pedantic (and thus not suitable for the article in question), a tuple in the foundations of mathematics is typically defined as a set. That is, (x,y) := {x,{x,y}}, where the latter is the set containing the element x and the set {x,y}. That is how one goes from axiomatic set theory to define tuples of numbers.')
