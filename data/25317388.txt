A triple conjunction is a conjunction of Jupiter and Saturn at or near their opposition to the Sun. In this scenario, Jupiter and Saturn will occupy the same position in right ascension (or ecliptic longitude) on three occasions (due to apparent retrograde motion) over a period of a few months.<p>Presumably the significance of a triple conjunction is that it explains the story of the Magi seeing a &quot;star in the East&quot;, which then &quot;troubled&quot; Jerusalem when they arrived there, until it &quot;went before them&quot;, and then finally &quot;stood over where the young Child was&quot;.[1]<p>[0] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Great_conjunction#%E2%88%926" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Great_conjunction#%E2%88%926</a><p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.biblegateway.com&#x2F;passage&#x2F;?search=Matthew+2%3A1-12&amp;version=NKJV" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.biblegateway.com&#x2F;passage&#x2F;?search=Matthew+2%3A1-1...</a>')
