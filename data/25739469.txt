Item(by='dfox', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610407180, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25733276, text='Scheduling fixed pauses between runs is often better (for jobs where fixed frequency is not critical, which is most of backround jobs) because you then don&#x27;t have to care whether it will complete within the period.<p>It is certainly simpler and less fragile than various solutions involving cron-like scheduler and locking (by the way implementation of cron itself is somewhat more complex than it might look like because of exactly this issue).<p>One thing that somewhat surprised me about typical industrial automation is that running the logic in some variation of do_work(); sleep()&#x2F;yield(); loop is pretty common (typical modern PLC works that way) and nobody seems to much care about the resulting latency jitter which is from theoretical standpoint totally horrible but in practice insignificant.')
