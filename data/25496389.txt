Item(by='PragmaticPulp', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608568149, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25495164, text='IPOs are rare. Most exits come in the form of private acquisitions. You don’t hear about the latter group in the news because they’re usually much smaller companies.<p>Many IPO-track companies are growing fast and attracting serious investment, so they pay engineers top dollar. They want to maximize chances of getting to IPO so they don’t benefit from cutting corners on a few engineers.<p>If you have The opportunity to join an actual IPO-track company then go for it. The catch is that many startups will claim to be headed for IPO, so you have to look at actual growth and TAM and make your own judgments.')
