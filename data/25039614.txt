Item(by='vkou', descendants=None, kids=[25039883], score=None, time=1604954468, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25038308, text='&gt; The problem with municipal ISPs is that they are very likely to end up providing worse service and being more expensive than private options, but hiding the true cost by using tax money to subsidize the operation.<p>The private sector has pretty conclusively proven that in the absence of municipal competition, their offerings are expensive, and provide terrible service.<p>In fact, it has proven it so conclusively that people are clamoring for a public alternative.<p>There is zero reason to oppose unsubsidized, zero-margin municipal broadband.<p>There are some reasons to oppose subsidized, negative-margin municipal broadband, but if &gt;50% of the electorate supports it, that&#x27;s a pretty strong signal that its better than the private alternatives.')
