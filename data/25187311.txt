Item(by='Nbox9', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606143402, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25184925, text='It seems to me to be too large of a correction against ‘highly-optimized supply chains’ to assume that at any time we’ll suddenly need a 100x supply of any medicine in the WHO List of Essential Medicines and that we’ll need this emergency supply of medicine while the world supply chain is disrupted.  This is essentially the ask of the article, when it references the sudden need for Hydroxychloroquine.<p>Should we have emergency stockpiles of food, water, medical equipment, energy, and shelters?  Absolutely.  Let’s remember that any inefficiencies in our manufacturing base serve to make us less competitive internationally and raise the prices that consumers spend.  There’s only so much emergency preparedness we can reasonably expect.')
