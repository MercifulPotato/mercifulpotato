Item(by='singron', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607893419, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25409989, text='Particularly at Google, programming languages, code styles, common libraries, and tools are incredibly similar between teams. There are really low barriers to jumping into someone else&#x27;s codebase. It&#x27;s not uncommon to review changes to your team&#x27;s codebase from people you have never met (on average, maybe weekly?).<p>Anecdotally I also think a lot of code was generally really well documented. There is some effort to document things for a general Googler audience (i.e. you should understand most of the documentation on any given page without having to read all the other docs).<p>All in all, once you have onboarded into the Google ecosystem, it&#x27;s fairly painless to jump around the codebase.')
