Item(by='dragonwriter', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602101511, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24711679, text='Functional idioms are often more expressive when you want to do something with a result other than an immediate side effect like printing. Sure, you can use imperative loops to build up a data structure to work with, but functional idioms (or comprehensions, which are generally an imperative-looking wrapper over functional idioms) can be quite concise and expressive for that purpose.<p><pre><code>  for (owner &lt;- company.staff)\n    for (pet &lt;- staff.pets if pet.type==&quot;cat&quot;)\n      yield (owner.name, pet.name)\n</code></pre>\nbuilds a datastructure with the same information yours prints, with no extra boilerplate around building the datastructure.')
