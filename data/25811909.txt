Item(by='maccard', descendants=None, kids=[25813666], score=None, time=1610900194, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25811791, text='I don&#x27;t think that&#x27;s necessarily true. There are plenty of cases where frugality would be reasonable with unit costs. A team running a k8s cluster on eks with redundancy while they&#x27;re working on an MVP is likely spending 5x what they need compared to running a single fargate container&#x2F;DO droplet. I would definitely consider the reverse of that being &quot;frugal&quot;.<p>There was a post on HN recently about a SAAS that listed out all of the SAAS products they were using, and for a team of 5 they were purchasing g suite, an email productivity &quot;enhancer&quot; to avoid conversations moving to slack, and a meeting scheduling tool, for 10% of their revenue (something like $150&#x2F;month). Being frugal would likely have cost them less than percentage points of productivity, and increased their runway.')
