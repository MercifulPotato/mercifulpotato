Item(by='Ericson2314', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609764925, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25630432, text='There is a great line of research that part of labor&#x27;s downfall in the postwar era was due to becoming to legalized&#x2F;instituionalized, creating a hysteresis trap were the unions official power (laws and norms) lagged behind the underlying conditions that give it real power (labor scarcity + worker radicalization). Workers got complacent and depoliticized starting with the red scare, and edges along by shitty union leadership, and the whole Regan era turnabout was less a right-wing conspiracy and more the hysteresis delay coming to an an end.<p>Members-only unions and whatnot that forgo the NRLB are &quot;riskier&quot; in some sense, but that vary precarity &#x2F; forgoing of intertia can avoid the lag and help keep the union vigilant.<p>See a popular exposition in <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.jacobinmag.com&#x2F;2016&#x2F;06&#x2F;uaw-academic-workers-colleges-union-walter-reuther-treaty-detroit" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.jacobinmag.com&#x2F;2016&#x2F;06&#x2F;uaw-academic-workers-coll...</a>, which is a better piece than much Jacobin stuff I might add.')
