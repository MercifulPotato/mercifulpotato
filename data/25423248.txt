Item(by='gumby', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607979713, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25420455, text='Sleeper trains were <i>de rigeur</i> for our travel when I was a kid in the 60s and 70s: Paris-Simplon, The Flying Scotsman and regular trips between Melbourne and Adelaide.  All are now gone, or recreated as shadows of their former selves.<p>A 1980s trip on the trans Siberian express: I remember it as being &quot;third class&quot;, though perhaps it was simply not first class; the idea of classes of service in a soviet train amused me, though the realities of the spartan accomodation made it only technically a &quot;sleeper&quot;.  But the friendly fellow travelers made up for it.<p>About 15 years ago we took sleeper service from Paris to Berlin, in a bid to convince my wife that it was a fun way to travel.  It was not.')
