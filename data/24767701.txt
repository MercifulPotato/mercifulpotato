Item(by='_8091149529', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602610103, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24763237, text='What seems to be missing from the replies posted so far is the notion of coherence, and the choice of measurement basis.<p>What separates a coherent &quot;quantum&quot; superposition, say, |0&gt; + |1&gt;, from a probabilistic &quot;non-quantum&quot; 50:50 mixture is that I can choose a measurement basis in which the coherent state always yields a definite result, say &quot;1&quot;, whereas measuring the mixed state always yields a 50:50 mixture of &quot;0&quot;s and &quot;1&quot;s.<p>A continuous sweep of the angle of the measurement basis generally results in an interference pattern, the amplitude of which can be used to assess the fidelity of the quantum state.<p>(I get paid to work on quantum communication and related experiments.)')
