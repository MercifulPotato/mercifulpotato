Item(by='nickff', descendants=None, kids=[24911055, 24910942], score=None, time=1603827066, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24910862, text='You&#x27;re comparing two completely different paradigms.<p>FPGAs are not an assembly line at all; the assembly line analogy applies much more closely to a processor&#x27;s pipeline.<p>FPGAs are just a massive set of very simple logic units which can be interconnected in many different ways. FPGAs are best used in situations where you want to perform a series of simple operations on a massive incoming dataset, in parallel, especially in real-time situations. Performing domain transforms on data coming in from sensor arrays is one very good application for FPGAs.')
