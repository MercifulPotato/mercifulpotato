Item(by='ChrisMarshallNY', descendants=None, kids=[25218899], score=None, time=1606388031, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25218380, text='When I worked for a Japanese company, this became the norm. It made translations a lot easier.<p>I still believe in short paragraphs; though not necessarily single-sentence ones. Paragraphs are meant to collect ideas, and it’s often a good practice to have a fairly “granular” approach, with “atomic,” self-contained “modules.”<p>The idea is to allow reading to proceed in a “piecemeal” fashion. This is due to the way people consume prose, these days, with sidebars and interruptions. It also lends itself well to reference reading.<p>In any case, a “wall of text” approach is disastrous in digital media. It works well, for justified paperbacks, but not so well on a digital device.')
