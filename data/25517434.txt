Item(by='mulcyber', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608730311, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25516930, text='&gt; How could something so mundane as a metal detector be banned?<p>Obviously it&#x27;s not...<p>The actual law says:\n&quot;Nul ne peut utiliser du matériel permettant la détection d&#x27;objets métalliques, à l&#x27;effet de recherches de monuments et d&#x27;objets pouvant intéresser la préhistoire, l&#x27;histoire, l&#x27;art ou l&#x27;archéologie, sans avoir, au préalable, obtenu une autorisation administrative délivrée en fonction de la qualification du demandeur ainsi que de la nature et des modalités de la recherche.&quot; [Code du patrimoine Article L542-1]<p>&quot;No one can use material allowing detection of metal objects to look for monuments and objects which can interest prehistory, history, art or archeology, without having, beforehand, obtained an administrative authorization delivered depending the qualifications of the applicant and the nature and modalities of the search.&quot;<p>So basically you cannot use a metal detector to do illegal archeological search. This law is weird though, since it actually condemn the intent and not the act. I&#x27;m no lawyer but I&#x27;m guessing it&#x27;s pretty hard to condemn  anyone with this law since anyone thoughtful enough can hide his intent (or at least prevent producing any proof of such intent).<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.legifrance.gouv.fr&#x2F;codes&#x2F;article_lc&#x2F;LEGIARTI000006845775&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.legifrance.gouv.fr&#x2F;codes&#x2F;article_lc&#x2F;LEGIARTI0000...</a>')
