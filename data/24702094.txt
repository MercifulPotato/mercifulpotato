In math lingo, you can only have a bijective function for domains with equal cardinality.<p>In other words, you couldn&#x27;t have a bijective function between an integer and a boolean, because there are fewer possible boolean values than integer values.<p>Demanding bijective functions is probably more restrictive than you want to be for building real-world systems. We discuss this a bit in the paper -- I think look for &quot;convert&quot;.')
