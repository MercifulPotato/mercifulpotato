Item(by='simonblack', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604620279, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24996923, text='There&#x27;s definitely an elitist undercurrent. But that is just the endpoint of working with a system that <i>actually is better</i>.<p>I discovered UNIX back in 1990. By 1991 I was working with a full-featured GUI. Around 1993 I thought I should take a look at this new-fangled &#x27;Windows&#x27; that everybody on the Internet was raving about. I couldn&#x27;t believe how horribly <i>primitive</i> it was. Windows didn&#x27;t become usable, IMHO, until Windows 95, so by then it was a full four years at least behind the half-dozen or so UNIX desktops that were based on X11. (And even then, it wasn&#x27;t a true pre-emptive multi-tasking OS.)<p>Windows in 2020 still hasn&#x27;t caught up with the network-based X11 that can easily run a program on a machine in Britain and have it launched, controlled and displayed on a desktop in New Zealand.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;dilbert.com&#x2F;strip&#x2F;1995-06-24" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;dilbert.com&#x2F;strip&#x2F;1995-06-24</a>')
