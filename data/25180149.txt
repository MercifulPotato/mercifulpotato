Item(by='geofft', descendants=None, kids=[25180393], score=None, time=1606072546, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25179337, text='Guitar Center seems to have the Barnes &amp; Noble problem, that is, they provide a valuable <i>service</i> in allowing you to browse, compare, and try out products, but they attempt to make their money from the <i>product</i> itself, which you can buy online from a non-brick-and-mortar store for cheaper. I&#x27;ve been to Guitar Center much more often than I&#x27;ve bought anything from there.<p>And both of these companies, being national chains, have the capital to out-compete local independent book &#x2F; music stores, but because they&#x27;re a chain, they won&#x27;t inspire loyalty. I might feel like I should pay a little more to buy a book from Greenlight Bookstore instead of Amazon; I don&#x27;t really feel like I should extend this courtesy to Barnes &amp; Noble.<p>How do we solve this problem in a sustainable way?')
