And not only that: the timer interrupts - normally connected to a regular per-CPU interrupt on ARM - are instead routed to the FIQ, an abstruse architectural feature, seen more frequently in the old 32-bit ARM days. Naturally, Linux kernel did not support delivering any interrupts via the FIQ path, so we had to add that.<p>and many others<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;corellium.com&#x2F;blog&#x2F;linux-m1" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;corellium.com&#x2F;blog&#x2F;linux-m1</a><p>It is on top of HN now <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=25859907" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=25859907</a>')
