It bridged to the Internet, as many networks at the time did (and many others did not).<p>The World Wide Web is a specific set of protocols (mostly:  HTML and HTTPS, most would now include CSS and JS a well as other stsndards), which can run across the Internet <i>or other networks</i>, e.g., a disconected LAN.<p>AOL&#x27;s joining the Internet had the largest immediate impact on Usenet, another protocol facility which <i>can</i> oerate over the Internet, but also transacted over phone lines (via UUCP) and avian carriers --- 747s carrying tapes between the US and Australia.<p>John Waterman&#x27;s <i>The Matrix</i> (1990) is an excellent overview of early networking systems.  That edition (there were several revisions) predates even the Web.')
