Item(by='jariel', descendants=None, kids=[24711546], score=None, time=1602097018, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24710644, text='If the laws were perfectly clear and the agency were in clear violation, that would be one thing.<p>But often it&#x27;s not, and like anything, the way we do it is figure it out in court. Which is crazy but that&#x27;s it.<p>Our system &#x27;incentivizes rule testing&#x27; because it&#x27;s the only way to find out where the boundaries are.<p>It&#x27;s ridiculous: the best lawyers in the world enact laws that are summarily overthrown by other lawyers who are Judges. That shouldn&#x27;t be possible.<p>It would be cool if lawmakers and the judiciary had a way of finding the limits without having to test them and wait years in court.<p>We have to spend 10 years for the courts to decide what the hell we meant when we wrote copyright law? It&#x27;s just dumb.<p>At very least, for national security we could have a Judicial oversight team who can maybe make rulings and then clear actions before they are taken.')
