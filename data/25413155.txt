Item(by='NZGumboot', descendants=None, kids=[25415615], score=None, time=1607915898, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25412252, text='I totally agree that lockdowns are super expensive, and should only be used as a last resort, but I think you&#x27;re missing the purpose of lockdowns. The purpose is not to save the lives of &lt;however many people are dying right now&gt;. The purpose is to bring the R number &lt;<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.bbc.com&#x2F;news&#x2F;health-52473523" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.bbc.com&#x2F;news&#x2F;health-52473523</a>&gt; below one, so that the number of sick people doesn&#x27;t increase to the point where the health care system is overwhelmed (which would lead to a substantially increased death rate).<p>Taiwan is an example of a country that managed to eliminate covid-19 without lockdowns, so it is possible, but part of their success is likely due to the fact that the authorities acted very early to impose strict quarantine rules and contact tracing (when there were fewer than 10 new cases per day!) AFAIK every other country that has managed to eliminate community transmission has done so via lockdowns.')
