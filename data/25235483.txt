Item(by='greggyb', descendants=None, kids=[25242749], score=None, time=1606540730, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25230350, text='Infinitesimals tautologically exist in any finite representation of numbers. For floats, it&#x27;s the smallest representable positive number. For integers of any type, it&#x27;s 1.<p>As for how common they are, we learn about them in any introductory calculus course when defining derivatives. You come across the idea whenever discussing limits, if somewhat obliquely.<p>If I learned about it in high school math, and again in &quot;real&quot; math courses at my university, I&#x27;d say it&#x27;s pretty standard.')
