Item(by='didibus', descendants=None, kids=[24946008, 24944921], score=None, time=1604078521, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24943312, text='The thing is Rust isn&#x27;t functional, in that the coding patterns rely a lot on mutation actually, but the compiler can assert that your mutation will be race free, and that you won&#x27;t segfault and most likely won&#x27;t leak memory.<p>So you can say it appeals to functional programmers in the sense that they tend to be wary of mutation, so does Rust in a way. Except where a functional language will turn to immutability, Rust turns to an advanced compiler and linear types to safeguard you against the dangers of mutation.<p>The real benefits are performance, which has always been the achilles heel of functional programmers.')
