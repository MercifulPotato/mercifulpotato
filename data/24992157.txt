Item(by='mmastrac', descendants=None, kids=[24992223, 24992366, 24992358], score=None, time=1604517743, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24991848, text='Yeah, the problem with jumping into Rust headfirst is that you assume that the most complex parts of the language are what you need to use to solve your problem.<p>It turns out that the vast majority of Rust code is just fine using Arc and letting the reference counts handle your memory management.<p>The lifetimes are a really cool feature for library&#x2F;data structure authors, but if you&#x27;re writing &quot;web server&quot; style code just use Arc, call clone, and be done with it, unless you&#x27;ve got a specific perf issue.')
