Item(by='dafoex', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1601746301, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24669334, text='I believe most countries allow making archival copies - either digital or physical, and sometimes both - of a whole publication of any sort.  The fair use aspect of archival is the preservation of the contained information under the assumption that it won&#x27;t see the light of day, except for if the person or party making the archive needs to reference it in some way (such as for writing a paper), or once the publication in question enters the public domain.<p>I&#x27;m not a lawyer, so check your local laws, but I know that such a thing has happened for the BBC&#x27;s Doomsday programme in order to save it from laser disc rot, so one can only assume that this logic applies to other things.')
