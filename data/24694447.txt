Stack images like <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;battle.shawwn.com&#x2F;sdb&#x2F;visualizations&#x2F;2020-09-16-117m-gpt-revnet&#x2F;index.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;battle.shawwn.com&#x2F;sdb&#x2F;visualizations&#x2F;2020-09-16-117m...</a> in 3D space, like a cube. Put them in order of layers. Ditto for activations.<p>The spatial structure is important. Right now there&#x27;s nothing, not even tensorboard, that preserves spatial structure. For example, look at these two images:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;battle.shawwn.com&#x2F;sdb&#x2F;visualizations&#x2F;2020-09-16-117m-gpt-revnet&#x2F;model_g_h6_attn_c_attn_w.png" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;battle.shawwn.com&#x2F;sdb&#x2F;visualizations&#x2F;2020-09-16-117m...</a><p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;battle.shawwn.com&#x2F;sdb&#x2F;visualizations&#x2F;2020-09-16-117m-gpt-revnet&#x2F;model_g_h6_attn_c_attn_b.png" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;battle.shawwn.com&#x2F;sdb&#x2F;visualizations&#x2F;2020-09-16-117m...</a><p>You&#x27;re looking at weights, followed by bias. You can see visually that the bias lines up with the weights. If you tried visualizing that with tensorboard, it&#x27;d shrink the images to squares with padding. Which would be fine if you could rearrange squares so that one is above the other, but you can&#x27;t. Meaning you lose the ability to correlate visually what&#x27;s going on.<p>Someone please make an ML doom blaster so that I can at least reset layers of my ML model by shooting them.')
