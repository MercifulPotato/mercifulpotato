Item(by='seszett', descendants=None, kids=[25462423], score=None, time=1608241802, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25460390, text='Chalant is the present participle of the (disused) verb &quot;chaloir&quot;, which means &quot;to matter to someone&quot;, &quot;to be important (to someone)&quot;.<p>It&#x27;s only used anymore in the words &quot;nonchalant&quot; (someone to which things don&#x27;t matter much) and the expression &quot;peu me chaut&quot; (it matters little to me).<p>But although &quot;nonchalant&quot; has a clear meaning, the meaning of &quot;chalant&quot; wouldn&#x27;t be as obvious, since while it&#x27;s easy to not care about things generally, &quot;generally caring about things&quot; is just the normal state of a person. It&#x27;s the same reason you don&#x27;t often hear about a stoppable force or a wieldy tool.')
