Item(by='jacobolus', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607019652, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25288273, text='“Euler’s identity” is a flashy way of stating that a half-turn rotation in the plane is equivalent to a reflection across the center of rotation. This is a fact which can be easily understood by young children.<p>The part that makes the expression flashy is that the rotation is written via its logarithm, which can be represented as the length of a circular arc (“angle measure”).<p>It’s a useful fact to know, but is vastly overrated.')
