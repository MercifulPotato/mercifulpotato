Item(by='gwright', descendants=None, kids=[25758164, 25758197, 25758151], score=None, time=1610511670, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25757987, text='While I understand the value of anonymous public communication, I think it comes with a huge &quot;bad-actor&quot; problem that we haven&#x27;t figured out how to offset.<p>Anonymity is a problem when it is used to commit crimes and law enforcement seems inadequately staffed to police cyber crimes even when the perpetrator isn&#x27;t even anonymous or pseudo anonymous.<p>Do we need something like an Internet &quot;drivers&quot; license for anyone who wants to post content to the public? Analogous to needing a license to drive on public roads?  Maybe that idea can be tied into Section 230 concerns?  I&#x27;m sure there are obvious difficulties, just spitballing. Balancing liberty and safety isn&#x27;t easy.')
