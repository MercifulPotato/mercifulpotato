But the point is there&#x27;s nothing unusual about America&#x27;s high spending given its very high income levels. In particular look at this chart from the source.[1]<p>Let&#x27;s use France as a comparison point since you mentioned it. Household disposable income in the US is about 36% higher than France. That&#x27;s about equivalent to the wealth gap between France and Slovenia. The US spends about 70% more per hospital stay than France, and very similarly that&#x27;s almost the exact same spending gap between France and Slovenia.<p>The point being it&#x27;s easy to ask &quot;why does the US spend more than Western Europe?&quot; But, analogously you should also ask &quot;why does Western Europe spend more than Southern and Eastern Europe&quot;? And the most clear answer is because wealthier countries tend to spend a higher percent of their income on healthcare.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;i0.wp.com&#x2F;randomcriticalanalysis.com&#x2F;wp-content&#x2F;uploads&#x2F;2020&#x2F;01&#x2F;rcafdm_america_average_discharge_cost_comparison_with_elasticity_2010.png?resize=720%2C496&amp;ssl=1" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;i0.wp.com&#x2F;randomcriticalanalysis.com&#x2F;wp-content&#x2F;uplo...</a>')
