Item(by='glangdale', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606784945, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25252307, text='I think elementary tactics are a little more important to chess than a single random bit-twiddling hack is to programming. Not having a basic grasp of tactics and missing simple forks, pins, etc. is really only going to give you a game that&#x27;s slightly beyond &quot;not hanging pieces&quot;.<p>Anyone good enough to light you up tactically probably <i>also</i> knows at least the Cliff&#x27;s Notes version of positional play and is not going to develop knights to a3, block their bishops behind an idiotic wall of pawns, or set up a nice doubled isolated pawns for you to murder.<p>A balanced approach from the start is reasonable.')
