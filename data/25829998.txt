Formats were not open then.  You did not select archive tools based on the on-disk format it used; you selected archive tools based on a combination of software cost, compression performance, and network effects.  Having multiple implementations of software that could work on the same archive format was a novelty which often resulted in legal battles, as the formats themselves were often considered proprietary information.')
