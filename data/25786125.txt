And he should be impeached for both that and his inaction in response to that.<p>And an impeachment trial is <i>not</i> an Article III court.  Nor is it intended to be.  It is inherently a <i>political</i> proceeding and not a criminal trial.  As such, ethics and morals are certainly at issue and the rules of Article III courts are not relevant.<p>Nor did the founders intend[0][1] them to be criminal trials.  Which is why someone who is impeached and convicted is <i>not</i> immune from criminal prosecution in an Article III court.<p>[0] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;avalon.law.yale.edu&#x2F;18th_century&#x2F;fed65.asp" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;avalon.law.yale.edu&#x2F;18th_century&#x2F;fed65.asp</a><p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;avalon.law.yale.edu&#x2F;18th_century&#x2F;fed66.asp" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;avalon.law.yale.edu&#x2F;18th_century&#x2F;fed66.asp</a>')
