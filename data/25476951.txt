Item(by='jka', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608380046, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25468887, text='It&#x27;s a brief article, and worth a read.<p>If you use software to guide and support you in any environment and it&#x27;s not free software (free as in speech, and as in the definition provided by the FSF), then even though that non-free software may provide you with short-term benefits (that may be worth it), you&#x27;ve given up some of your autonomy to another party.<p>People will downplay that aspect of software all the time - &quot;proprietary software is good for business thanks to support contracts&quot;, &quot;people don&#x27;t care as long as the software works&quot;, &quot;free software has poor usability&quot;.  That&#x27;s understandable and those are beliefs that those people usually genuinely hold, but it doesn&#x27;t change the underlying principle of the situation.<p>(this generally applies to hardware and computing platforms too)<p>[1] - <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.fsf.org&#x2F;about&#x2F;what-is-free-software" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.fsf.org&#x2F;about&#x2F;what-is-free-software</a>')
