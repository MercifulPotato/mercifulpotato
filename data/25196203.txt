Item(by='kevsim', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606203607, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25195487, text='Knowing when not to talk is particularly important for people in leadership positions. A manager coming into a meeting and blurting out what they think about everything is a sure fire way to stifle creative debate in their team. While some people will step up and disagree with &quot;the boss&quot; many will not.<p>I wrote a bit of a thing that touched on this a while back [0] and, based on the feedback I got, I think a lot of managers do this without realizing it. Many managers feel the need to break the silence if the team isn&#x27;t engaging. If you feel this need, do so with a question instead.<p>0: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;medium.com&#x2F;kitemaker-blog&#x2F;you-need-to-get-your-team-talking-21c6a6803b1c" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;medium.com&#x2F;kitemaker-blog&#x2F;you-need-to-get-your-team-...</a>')
