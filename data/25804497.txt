Item(by='aweinstock', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610823950, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25804109, text='The contrast between Gauss-Jordan[0] and the Bareiss algorithm[1] is a good example of explicitly handling the length of the numbers in bits as part of the runtime.<p>Gauss-Jordan is O(n^3) if you consider addition and multiplication to be constant-time, but if it operates on arbitrary-precision integers, it can get exponentially slow[2].<p>The Bareiss algorithm avoids this worst-case behavior by choosing the pivots in a slightly more involved manner, which avoids building up large intermediate values.<p>[0]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Gaussian_elimination#Computational_efficiency" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Gaussian_elimination#Computati...</a>\n[1]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Bareiss_algorithm" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Bareiss_algorithm</a>\n[2]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;staff.itee.uq.edu.au&#x2F;havas&#x2F;fh97.pdf" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;staff.itee.uq.edu.au&#x2F;havas&#x2F;fh97.pdf</a>, Section 3.1&#x27;s &quot;Construction A&quot; is a specific matrix that exhibits the worst-case behavior.')
