Item(by='fairity', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605738330, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25141891, text='Totally agree that viewing negative feedback as a tool for self-improvement can be a useful and resilient perspective.<p>My main concerns over the advice in the original comment are that:<p>- It suggests that people only give feedback to you if they care about you or have to work with you again, which isn&#x27;t true.<p>- It implies that you should value whether people like you (or care about you).  You&#x27;re obviously free to choose whatever values you want, but external validation is a poor value imo because it&#x27;s largely outside of your control.  A more resilient mindset is to derive self-worth from intrinsic values e.g. hard work, self-improvement, kindness.')
