Item(by='mattkrause', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611598843, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25906370, text='There&#x27;s a huge chunk of neuroscience devoted to questions like this.<p>Several groups have shown that they can &quot;decode&quot; a remembered image from brain activity. This is comparatively easy when the images are simple and there are only a few possibilities, but can generalize to larger sets of images and even (sort of) never-before-seen ones. Sensory and motor information is relatively accessible; I don&#x27;t know that anyone&#x27;s making great progress decoding thoughts like &quot;I should be home by 8pm&quot;.<p>If this is up your alley, you may want to check out work done by Frank Tong&#x27;s lab at Princeton (e.g. <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov&#x2F;pmc&#x2F;articles&#x2F;PMC1808230&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov&#x2F;pmc&#x2F;articles&#x2F;PMC1808230&#x2F;</a>) or Jack Gallant&#x27;s group at UC Berkley (e.g, <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.sciencedirect.com&#x2F;science&#x2F;article&#x2F;pii&#x2F;S1053811914008428" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.sciencedirect.com&#x2F;science&#x2F;article&#x2F;pii&#x2F;S105381191...</a>), among others.')
