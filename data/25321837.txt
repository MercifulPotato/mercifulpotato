Item(by='LukeShu', descendants=None, kids=[25332373], score=None, time=1607239900, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25321623, text='Not just letters!  &quot;^X&quot; indicates &quot;X, but with the control-bit flipped&quot;, where the control-bit is the left-most of ASCII&#x27;s 7 bits.<p><pre><code>    1001000 ASCII &#x27;H&#x27;\n    1000000 &quot;ctrl bit&quot;\n    0001000 ASCII backspace\n</code></pre>\nBut like I said, it&#x27;s not just letters, if you use `cat -v` to show all non-printable characters in this notation, you&#x27;ll also see things like &quot;^@&quot; for the 0x00 null byte and &quot;^[&quot; for the ESC.  All of the terminal color-code control sequences start with ESC, so if you pipe colorized output to `cat -v`, you&#x27;ll see a lot of &quot;^[&quot;.')
