Item(by='mellosouls', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603679532, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24891718, text='I think that is an optimistic view.<p>Consider that an overwhelmingly partisan ownership and stewardship of the majority of media, (whether left or right - currently, as the example in the article it&#x27;s liberal left) has a more insidious power to control the narrative and push counterpoints to the margins until they die naturally, partly because there is in that situation a <i>de facto</i> monopoly.<p>Government-censored material never actually dies, and has a habit of being leaked (or released on change of government) and causing scandals; Silicon Valley etc-suppressed material has a habit of being sidelined into the anaemic and eccentric irrelevance of the cancelled and the crackpots, and by the time we realise some of them are actually worth listening to, the damage is done.<p>Especially in the context of a society where &quot;speech is free&quot;, and egregious censorship is assumed not to exist, this is arguably even more damaging - the <i>idea that suppression exists</i> is itself dismissed by the mainstream and chattering classes; that isn&#x27;t necessarily the case in a country under authoritarian, censoring governance.')
