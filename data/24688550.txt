Item(by='HarryHirsch', descendants=None, kids=[24690480], score=None, time=1601911446, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24684727, text='The anti-sports stance is deliberate. Pursuing athletics at a high level takes time and money, both of which would better be directed elsewhere in a world of limited resources. It&#x27;s also sending a message: this is a school, not a sports club; if you want sports, join the sports club, there you get sports.<p>There are studies that school districts that dropped sports have better academic outcomes: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.theatlantic.com&#x2F;magazine&#x2F;archive&#x2F;2013&#x2F;10&#x2F;the-case-against-high-school-sports&#x2F;309447&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.theatlantic.com&#x2F;magazine&#x2F;archive&#x2F;2013&#x2F;10&#x2F;the-cas...</a><p>Especially, what&#x27;s with the focus on competitive sports? At school you absolutely want to reach the unfit kids because school sports is the only time they get any kind of exercise. You have to be inclusionary, the purpose is public health, individual fitness, not competition, but competitive sports is exclusionary.<p>Also, what&#x27;s with the focus on team sports? American schoolkids have a staggering need for conformity, and team sports seem to feed that need.')
