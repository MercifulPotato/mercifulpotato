Item(by='lapcatsoftware', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604192136, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24954549, text='I wrote an article about Developer ID certificate revocation:\n<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;lapcatsoftware.com&#x2F;articles&#x2F;revocation.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;lapcatsoftware.com&#x2F;articles&#x2F;revocation.html</a><p>Some notes:\n1) Developer ID does not use a Certificate Revocation List (CRL), only Online Certificate Status Protocol (OCSP).\n2) The only way for a developer to revoke their Developer ID cert is to email Apple Product Security.\n3) macOS checks OCSP status on launching an app. OCSP responses are cached for a time, so the check may hit the cache instead of the network.\n4) OCSP and notarization are different and separate.')
