Item(by='eru', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605598666, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25114048, text='&gt; So the author&#x27;s central thesis essentially seems to boil down to that leaked emails were able to be cryptographically verified, because of DKIM and so we should prevent that so people can&#x27;t use email to blackmail politicians? Ultimately I prefer the more information that we can get on politicians available.<p>No matter what you think about politicians, it is a failure of cryptography, or perhaps our common application of it, that the signatures we use to assure our conversation partner of our identity can also be used for our conversation partner (or divers third parties) to prove what we said.<p>Compare <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Off-the-Record_Messaging" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Off-the-Record_Messaging</a> which solved this problem quite a few years ago.  Off-the-Record Messaging allows your conversation partner to know that they are talking to the real you, but does not empower them to prove that to anyone else.')
