Item(by='speedgoose', descendants=None, kids=[25809897], score=None, time=1610881614, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25809001, text='The current trend in user interfaces is not to design for desktop but for mobile first, because that&#x27;s where the large majority of users are.<p>Of course you have exceptions, when a company develops an application only for desktop for example or when a company can afford developing and maintaining many versions of one user interface.<p>Already today it&#x27;s very fine to use a laptop with a touchscreen on most websites. And actually most of desktop apps are also possible to use while not always super nice. The screens are quite large so it&#x27;s not that bad to hit a specific area.<p>I agree the mouse is a lot more precise when you are good with a mouse, but a mouse also needs more training than a touch screen to be used fast and accurately.<p>It will always be a market for old fashioned desktop apps, maybe Apple is going for it, perhaps some GNU&#x2F;Linux folks too, but I think the industry is not targeting devices without touch interfaces.')
