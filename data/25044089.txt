Item(by='hansvm', descendants=None, kids=[25046771], score=None, time=1605000132, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25041721, text='I&#x27;ve never heard that definition of &quot;tragedy of the commons.&quot; Rather, I&#x27;ve always seen it used to describe a situation where everyone would benefit if everyone took one action, but individually the incentive structure is to do the opposite -- effectively you&#x27;re able to get the things you want for minimal personal cost and with large external costs.<p>It very explicitly applies to shared resources like fisheries, and it crops up in much more mundane scenarios too. E.g., when water costs are split between multiple apartments you can pay peanuts for nearly unrestricted personal water use by offloading most of the costs to everyone else.')
