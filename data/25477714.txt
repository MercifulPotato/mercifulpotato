By spending time in other places, you see different ways of working, thinking, organization, approach to problems, focus etc.<p>It looks like as college has become standard that everyone &quot;needs&quot; or even &quot;deserves&quot;, we are getting to the point where academic positions should also be handed out on a basis of some social distributive program.<p>Yes it&#x27;s hard to do and involves sacrifices. But not everyone needs to do it. It&#x27;s not immediately obvious that we gain a lot by opening the gates wide and accommodating any life choices to be compatible with any prestigious careers. Artists and scientists used to do this kind of traveling around and being under the patronage of various rich people or institutions. It isn&#x27;t clear that making it into a steady stable job with orders of magnitude more people chipping away at it incrementally has brought proportional results at all.<p>If it&#x27;s not for you, why not find something that is?')
