Item(by='thisisbrians', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602006520, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24697965, text='To be able to write code, you must first be able to read code. This means you must be able to &#x27;simulate&#x27; what the computer is doing when it executes a program — or at least the bit of it you&#x27;re working on — in your own head before you can write it. Tools can and do help with this, but they cannot think for you.<p>Competence in engineering and architecture come from expanding the capabilities of your mind to simulate more complex programs and to think in abstractions.')
