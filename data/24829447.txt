Item(by='Someone', descendants=None, kids=[24829905], score=None, time=1603132772, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24828181, text='You’re skipping the idea that there is such a thing as <i>the</i> eigenfunction of the derivative.<p>Because taking the derivative is a linear function, <i>a × e^x</i> is its own derivative for any constant <i>a</i>.<p>That means, for example, that <i>e^(x+1)</i> or <i>e^(x+π)</i> are their own derivatives, too, as those are different ways to write <i>e × e^x</i>, respectively <i>e^π × e^x</i>.<p><i>e^x</i> just is the ‘simplest’ of those functions (ignoring the most simple one: <i>f(x) = 0</i>, but that one isn’t representative for all the other ones)')
