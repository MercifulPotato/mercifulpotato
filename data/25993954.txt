Item(by='Jtsummers', descendants=None, kids=[25996793], score=None, time=1612213574, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25992572, text='I&#x27;ve also had employers try to copy much larger companies&#x27; methods without real understanding. You should know why you&#x27;re doing it, not just why you&#x27;re not.<p>Is SOA appropriate to <i>every</i> org? What does it solve or permit? Is it appropriate at the scale of a system with 10 developer? Or does it become effective at some scale between 10 and Amazon?<p>Much of industry treats git as the de facto version control system. Why <i>not</i> another system? What makes git better overall or is it?<p>While I&#x27;m a massive proponent of unit testing, there are systems where they&#x27;re actually <i>very</i> impractical. Should we contort our software and environment to support a popular, best practice testing method when neither support it in their existing or natural states?<p>Should everyone <i>really</i> be contorting HTTP to be the base protocol for every application? Does it make sense as a messaging system protocol? As your email protocol? Would more dedicated protocols be better?')
