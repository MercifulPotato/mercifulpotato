Item(by='myrandomcomment', descendants=None, kids=[25259452], score=None, time=1606772240, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25258463, text='This reminds me of the Amiga which had FastRAM and ChipRAM. It was all main memory, but the ChipRAM could be directly addressed by all the co-processor HW in the Amiga and the FastRAM could not.<p>It would be sort of interesting for Intel&#x2F;AMD to do something like this where they have 16GB on the CPU and the OS has the knowledge to see it differently from external RAM.<p>Apple is going to have to do this for their real &quot;Pro&quot; side devices as getting 1TB on the Mx will be a non-starter. I would expect to see the M2 (or whatever) with a large amount of the basic on chip RAM and then an external RAM bus also.')
