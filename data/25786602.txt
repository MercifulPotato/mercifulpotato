My comment addresses the vast majority of instances a human learns about a problem <i>where that isn&#x27;t the case</i>.<p>Your sphere of concern and your sphere of influence are two different things at the end of the day. If you&#x27;ve decided some social problem <i>is</i> within your sphere of influence, then by all means, take whatever helpful action you can - just do not pretend that this encompasses all problems, or for that matter that the number of problems is not infinite.<p>Down that path lay depression and burnout.')
