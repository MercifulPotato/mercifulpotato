Item(by='zajio1am', descendants=None, kids=[24926569], score=None, time=1603936597, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24924789, text='&gt; On the contrary, Wayland is very much in the spirit of Unix &amp; Linux philosophy, while X11 isn&#x27;t.<p>I have an opposite view. X11 has two key aspects separated to two independent entities: mechanism (in Xserver) and policy (in window manager). This has and advantage as neutral mechanism can be shared by everybody (Xorg), while everyone has different opinion about policies (many WMs).<p>Wayland assumes integration of compositor (mechanism) and WM (policy) to one entity.')
