It is due to the convention of how to process undefined tags and attribute:<p><i>User agents must treat elements and attributes that they do not understand as semantically neutral; leaving them in the DOM (for DOM processors), and styling them according to CSS (for CSS processors), but not inferring any meaning from them. </i> <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;html.spec.whatwg.org&#x2F;multipage&#x2F;infrastructure.html#extensibility-2" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;html.spec.whatwg.org&#x2F;multipage&#x2F;infrastructure.html#e...</a><p>This is unrelated to parsing - for example a XHTML parser will be strict and draconian in its parsing, but still process undefined tags and attributes as above.')
