Item(by='gregmac', descendants=None, kids=[25595400], score=None, time=1609438185, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25594400, text='There are about 100 ways to do any given thing when building web apps, and it&#x27;s a constantly shifting landscape where &quot;the way&quot; you do a bunch of things is different  every time you start a new app (at least it feels that way to me).<p>Being really good at front-end (to the point you know when to use a native control or not, which &quot;standard&quot; features you can rely on, how to think about accessibility+responsive+dark mode+ad blockers+low bandwidth+seo etc) is an entire discipline that takes a <i>lot</i> of effort to stay current in.<p>If you use pure, standard html with default styling you still end up with a page using bad colors and Times New Roman that looks straight out of the 90s. If that&#x27;s not the look you&#x27;re going for, you need to do <i>some</i> customization (via CSS) and the difference between &quot;some&quot; and &quot;to the nines&quot; is not exactly a clear line -- even to people that have specialized their careers in front-end dev.')
