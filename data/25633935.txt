Item(by='013a', descendants=None, kids=[25634132, 25639822, 25637527, 25640470, 25636205], score=None, time=1609779087, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25632537, text='Its especially strange when you think about how unoriginal Slack&#x27;s product domain is, and how comparable, and in some cases small, their userbase is.<p>* iMessage, which likely handles something in the range of 750M-1B monthly actives.<p>* WhatsApp, 2B users [1], though no clarity on &quot;active&quot; users.<p>* Telegram, 400M monthly actives [2]<p>* Discord, 100M monthly actives [3]<p>* Slack, 12M daily actives [4]<p>* Teams, which is certainly more popular than Slack, but I shudder to list it because its stability may actually be worse.<p>The old piece of wisdom that &quot;real-time chat is hard&quot; is something I&#x27;ve always taken at face-value as being true, because it <i>is</i> hard, but some of the most stable, highest scale services I&#x27;ve ever interfaced with are chat services. iMessage NEVER goes down. I have to conclude that Slack&#x27;s unacceptable instability, even relative to more static services like Jira, is less the product of the difficulty of their product domain, and moreso something far deeper and more unfixable.<p>I would not assume that this will improve after they are fully integrated with Salesforce.
