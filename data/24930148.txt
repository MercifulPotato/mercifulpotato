Item(by='simonh', descendants=None, kids=[24936203, 24930356], score=None, time=1603977508, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24930032, text='It&#x27;s a real market, it&#x27;s just heavily supply constrained.<p>Conventional market theory assumes that supply will rise to meet demand, but between green belt laws, NIMBY movements and the difficulty of crating new land where people want to live, that&#x27;s often a big problem in property markets.<p>There&#x27;s also the fact that we all need somewhere to live, and once we have a family we&#x27;ll devote a very large fraction of our resources to get the best place to bring up our children in.<p>So we have a limited resource we all (many of us) want. The result is people, us, compete with each other to bid up the price. We do it to each other.<p>Yes there&#x27;s definitely an impact from buy-to-let purchasers bidding up prices. Here in the UK the government recently revised property taxes to make it less advantageous to own and rent out property, which is a good start.<p>How do homeless people maintain wealthy people?')
