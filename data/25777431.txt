Item(by='1_player', descendants=None, kids=[25779717], score=None, time=1610638022, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25777070, text='You probably have seen it already, but recent Phoenix docs have a section on contexts and what is their intended use case: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;hexdocs.pm&#x2F;phoenix&#x2F;contexts.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;hexdocs.pm&#x2F;phoenix&#x2F;contexts.html</a><p>The way I see it, context are just the public API to access your data model. Your Ecto schema is a piece of data, which in a functional language has no behaviour attached. The (business) logic to deal with this piece of data lives in the context. That&#x27;s it.<p>It&#x27;s nothing particular and specific about Ecto or Phoenix, it&#x27;s just a good design practice that&#x27;s applicable and recommended in any language.<p>Gary Bernhardt at <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.destroyallsoftware.com" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.destroyallsoftware.com</a> has a few screencasts and talks about this very topic.')
