Item(by='zaroth', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606846849, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25265353, text='Tritium can be stored as a solid metal tritide. You can use several different metals but titanium seems to offer the best cost&#x2F;benefit.<p>A 7&quot; diameter cylinder 16&quot; high weighing 35 pounds can store 300 grams of tritium safely for 5 years (without releasing any decay helium). It&#x27;s totally stable at room temperature, stable in water, and needs to be heated to ~600C to add&#x2F;remove tritium. The decay heat output of 300g of tritium is ~100 watts, so the vessel should probably have some heatsinks and be ventilated.<p>About 300g of tritium will be required per day to produce 800 MW of power.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;inis.iaea.org&#x2F;collection&#x2F;NCLCollectionStore&#x2F;_Public&#x2F;26&#x2F;045&#x2F;26045388.pdf" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;inis.iaea.org&#x2F;collection&#x2F;NCLCollectionStore&#x2F;_Public&#x2F;...</a>')
