Item(by='leetcrew', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610734316, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25793795, text='I&#x27;m disagreeing that &quot;the average american has a six figure debt load&quot; is a problem to begin with. if you take the total outstanding consumer debt in the US and divide by the number of households, you get about $110k for the mean, which is pretty close to the median household net worth. unless the distributions of debt and net worth look quite different, this implies that a typical household has a debt-to-assets ratio of &lt;0.5. this doesn&#x27;t strike me as a problem.<p>this is independent of whether I think UBI is a good idea in general (I do, actually).')
