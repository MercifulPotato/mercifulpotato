Item(by='p1mrx', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602460151, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24749536, text='Buying an LCD monitor with no digital input was always a bad way to cut costs, but you can buy active DVI-to-VGA or DisplayPort-to-VGA adapters to keep these devices working with new hardware.<p>Moving rarely-used DAC hardware to an external dongle seems like a good design decision, though it makes more sense for driving a CRT, where the conversion is D-&gt;A rather than D-&gt;A-&gt;D-&gt;A.  A CRT can be better than a modern LCD in some ways, whereas VGA-input LCDs are simply obsolete.')
