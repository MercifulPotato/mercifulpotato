Item(by='CuriouslyC', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604671862, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25007182, text='The truth is that regardless of how well put together a firm is, break-out success is a  crap shoot.  A VC strategy that maximizes potential revenue (by heavy due diligence) is almost always going to end up with lower actual revenue than a VC strategy that attempts to maximize actual revenue, by having a  larger, diversified basket.  If the terms of the initial funding give the VC first crack&#x2F;options for later rounds, all the better.')
