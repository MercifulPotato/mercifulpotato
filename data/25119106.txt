Item(by='jariel', descendants=None, kids=[25119192, 25119225], score=None, time=1605574213, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25118751, text='It&#x27;s mostly regulatory arbitrage, same as Uber.<p>If cities and townships were to have looser regulations on hotels - which evidently they should, given the vast demand and &#x27;mostly&#x27; it&#x27;s not excessively problematic for communities - then AirBnB wouldn&#x27;t exist because hotel prices and types would have much more variety and competitiveness, and so would listings.<p>If anyone could just get in their car and tell the local dispatcher &#x27;they want to drive tonight&#x27; - probably Uber would have never been able to come to fruition in the first place.<p>I&#x27;m not saying that either company is good or bad, or that the regulations are good or bad, rather, that the underlying business models depended on a kind of civil regulatory asymmetry.<p>Personally, I wish cities would adjust their regulations to be more appropriate, I don&#x27;t think there needs to be an Uber or AirBnB.')
