Item(by='mc10', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609276526, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25572134, text='I think this assumes a fine distinction between &quot;domain&quot; and &quot;non-domain&quot; knowledge that often doesn&#x27;t exist.<p>If you&#x27;re writing a trading strategy, the technical details of <i>how</i> the code&#x27;s executed often dictate <i>what kind</i> of trading you&#x27;re planning on doing. e.g. How do I balance speed vs how sophisticated the algorithm is?<p>Also, if your &quot;domain&quot; is technical in nature (e.g. building a new database), it&#x27;s not clear at all what parts are &quot;essential&quot; and what parts are &quot;accidental&quot;. Is the file system accidental? Is the underlying data store (SSDs, spinning disks) accidental? Often the purpose of the project is intrinsically tied to the technical capabilities of the platform it&#x27;s built on top of.')
