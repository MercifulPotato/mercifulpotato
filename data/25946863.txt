Item(by='majormajor', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611859715, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25944357, text='&gt; Our founding fathers knew power corrupts, but today&#x27;s 20-somethings seem quite unaware.<p>The government has to be able to actively respond to the problems of the moment, and those problems will constantly change. A handcuffed government only benefits the already-powerful.<p>The dirty secrets of the &quot;but the founding fathers!&quot; argument  are that (a) they knew they weren&#x27;t creating a perfect set of rules in the Constitution, and planned for us to be modifying it as we learned new things and the world changes, and (b) it&#x27;s failed anyway. Abstract principles listed on a page don&#x27;t &quot;protect freedom,&quot; bad actors can find ways to sneak things through (sometimes in plain sight, like that whole slavery thing that took a century to get figured out, or the followup forms of discrimination that are still with us).')
