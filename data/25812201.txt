Item(by='ohthehugemanate', descendants=None, kids=[25818684], score=None, time=1610902030, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25811304, text='Yes, lowest cost is a value. But there are probably 5 or 6 cereals at the lowest price point at your local grocery store. Which one do you pick, and why? Whatever the answer is, reveals your values. (Even &quot;the one at eye level&quot; reveals the value of minimum energy expenditure)<p>&gt; If I can afford it (free is better!) and it does the job, I use it.<p>OK so one value is clear. But there are literally tens of software options that fit into those requirements. So why do you pick gdocs over libreoffice, or your workplace MS365 subscription?<p>Every choice you make is determined by your values, whether you&#x27;re conscious of it or not. Very often, people think they hold one set of values (eg &quot;i&#x27;m a vegetarian because I value animal life&quot;) but that is belied by their actions (eg wearing leather soled shoes). You can guess which one is a better indicator of their real values.')
