Item(by='janmo', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607987141, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25422762, text='My point here is not to deny the achievement of creating your own chip design, but rather demystify how the M1 achieves its performance, and I believe it has mainly to do with TSMC&#x27;s 5nm technology and the huge number of transistors.\nIf you look up here <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Transistor_count" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Transistor_count</a>\nYou&#x27;ll see that the M1 is the first CPU&#x2F;GPU (for computers) to use 5nm and one of the biggest by transistor count.\nConsidering those facts I think it is very unfair to claim that the M1 is faster only because of some Apple magic.\nThe Ryzen used in this test is also made by TSMC but using a 7nm process.\nAccording to TSMC moving from 7nm to 5nm means 84% more transistors for the same chip size with a 15% speed improvement or a 30% lower power consumption.')
