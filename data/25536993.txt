Item(by='api', descendants=None, kids=[25537150, 25537622], score=None, time=1608910306, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25535324, text='Interstellar travel is possible with 1960s technology. Search for Project Orion. Thermonuclear Orion could reach as much as 10% the speed of light, which is fast enough to reach the Centauri system in a human life span.<p>Then there is the potential of things like hibernation, or AI that can just turn itself off. The latter would be able to use a less costly but slower propulsion system.<p>I really don’t think travel implausibility works as a Fermi paradox answer. It’s hard but not hard enough to explain the absence of visitors in a galaxy full of planets in habitable zones.')
