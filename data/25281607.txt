Item(by='frereubu', descendants=None, kids=[25291974], score=None, time=1606943044, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25281359, text='The crucial data that&#x27;s missing from those figures (although they&#x27;re probably buried in the references somewhere) is what the initial risk of cancer is.<p>If the risk of cancer of the larynx in non-drinkers is 0.0001%, then a 1.4-fold increase means that a moderate drinker has a risk of 0.00014%, an absolute increase of 0.00004%. Instead if it&#x27;s 20%, then a 1.4-fold increase is 28%, an absolute increase of 8%.<p>You could potentially extrapolate back from the 3.5% of cancer deaths being &quot;alcohol related&quot;, but that gives no context around the related risks of light &#x2F; moderate &#x2F; heavy drinking.')
