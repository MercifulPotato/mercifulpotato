Item(by='uryga', descendants=None, kids=[24988702], score=None, time=1604491649, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24987665, text='as a counterpoint, genericness can actually serve as a form of documentation. you can often infer a lot from just a signature, e.g:<p><pre><code>  any :: (Functor f, Foldable f) =&gt; (a -&gt; Bool) -&gt; f a -&gt; Bool\n</code></pre>\ntells me that `any` has to work across the whole collection `f a` (list&#x2F;tree&#x2F;whatever), because that&#x27;s \nhow folding works, and that it will get the answer by calling the function on the collection&#x27;s elements (the collection is a Functor, and the only thing you can do with one is `map` a function over it).<p>[of course this is assuming that `any` isn&#x27;t implemented as `any _ _ = True`]')
