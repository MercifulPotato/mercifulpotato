Item(by='cercatrova', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606424839, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25221831, text='You should read the book Range by David Epstein. He talks about how generalists can make an impact due to bringing solutions from multiple fields together, often in ways that specialists can&#x27;t see due to being in the field too long (they lack the &quot;beginner&#x27;s mind&quot;) or just don&#x27;t have such a breadth of experiences. Here&#x27;s a HN discussion about the book: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=20094242" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=20094242</a><p>The bigger thing is the convince employers of this fact, however, which most people won&#x27;t understand to be true. Instead, therefore, of making just one big resume with all of your experiences, make resumes for disparate fields: full stack resume will only contain full stack experience; same for data science, devops, and so on. This will make you seem like a specialist. Don&#x27;t lie but don&#x27;t downplay your experience as well.')
