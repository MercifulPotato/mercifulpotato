While &#x27;permanently banned&#x27; is a tautology, &#x27;permanently suspended&#x27; is an oxymoron.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wiktionary.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;suspended" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wiktionary.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;suspended</a><p>suspended = Caused to stop for a while; interrupted or delayed.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wiktionary.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;banned" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wiktionary.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;banned</a><p>banned = Forbidden; not allowed.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wiktionary.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;oxymoron" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wiktionary.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;oxymoron</a><p>oxymoron = A figure of speech in which two words or phrases with opposing meanings are used together intentionally for effect.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wiktionary.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;tautology" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wiktionary.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;tautology</a><p>tautology = Redundant use of words')
