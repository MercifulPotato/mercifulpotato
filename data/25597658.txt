Item(by='dane-pgp', descendants=None, kids=[25598052], score=None, time=1609451960, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25595788, text='&gt; Every organization, whether tiny or enormous, needs a members-only messaging service that cannot receive traffic from external or unapproved senders.<p>&gt; Organizations that need to let certain members hear from the general public, or communicate with untrusted (perhaps anonymous) customers, may establish a separate service instance for that purpose.<p>Rather than throwing away all existing email clients and servers, couldn&#x27;t this be achieved by configuring an inbound mail rule to drop all email from addresses that aren&#x27;t in your address book (and from servers that don&#x27;t support DKIM)?<p>It doesn&#x27;t seem very meaningful to say &quot;Users should only receive messages from approved senders, unless they need to receive messages from unapproved senders&quot;.  Email is useful precisely because it doesn&#x27;t have the extra step of requiring users to manually add an address to their address book before receiving a message from a new contact, such as a receipt from an online purchase.')
