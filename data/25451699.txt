Item(by='jlgaddis', descendants=None, kids=[25452207], score=None, time=1608172466, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25447805, text='&gt; <i>NOTE: Systemd has a nasty habit of delaying logins for 25 - 100 seconds while it waits for some service you never asked for to time out and fail, before it lets you have a prompt.</i><p>If you experience this when logging in to a host via SSH, the delays are almost certainly due to either missing or non-functional forward&#x2F;reverse DNS (the SSH server will perform these lookups when connections are received).<p>You&#x27;ve got at least three options (in order of preference):<p>- Add the appropriate (A&#x2F;PTR) resource records to DNS so that the queries get a valid response<p>- Add entries to &#x2F;etc&#x2F;hosts file<p>- Set &quot;UseDNS no&quot; in the &#x2F;etc&#x2F;ssh&#x2F;sshd_config file and add &quot;-u 0&quot; to the parameters passed to `sshd` when it&#x27;s started (how to do this will vary by distribution&#x2F;operating system)')
