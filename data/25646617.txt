Item(by='panarky', descendants=None, kids=[25646746], score=None, time=1609861193, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25646078, text='Monero priced in Bitcoin is down 60% in the last year and it&#x27;s down 90% since its peak in 2018.<p>If Monero&#x27;s purpose is to be anonymous and untraceable, and Bitcoin&#x27;s purpose is to be a store of value, then it appears the market prefers the store-of-value use case.<p>There&#x27;s a popular idea that crypto currencies are only good for illegal activity. But the evidence so far from a nearly trillion-dollar market says that avoiding law enforcement is not what&#x27;s driving the crypto phenomenon.<p>There&#x27;s another popular idea that central bank money printing is devaluing national currencies when priced in crypto. I&#x27;m sure that&#x27;s part of it, but inflating the money supply by 30% doesn&#x27;t really justify a 10x increase in crypto valuations.<p>It&#x27;s a small sample size, but the handful of wealthy families I&#x27;m acquainted with who are investing 0.1% to 2% of their net worth into Bitcoin (and a little into Ethereum) are more afraid of a collapse of the western political order than central bank money printing.<p>Institutions and high-net-worth individuals aren&#x27;t looking for privacy for illicit transactions so they&#x27;re not putting their wealth into Monero.<p>They&#x27;ve witnessed or researched what the collapse of the USSR did to the ruble and asset prices in the former Soviet Union, and they&#x27;re buying some insurance against a similar unraveling in the US and Europe.')
