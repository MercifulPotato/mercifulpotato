Item(by='monkeydreams', descendants=None, kids=[24695852], score=None, time=1601964742, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24684953, text='I&#x27;m not seeing anything particularly insightful in regards to Chaos Engineering with Healthcare. None of the unique challenges or strengths in the Healthcare domain are covered. Few industries have the baked-in resilience to systems failures that the Healthcare industry possess. EMR down? Move to paper for arrivals. MPI offline? Here are some pre-allocated IDs and paper forms to fill out.<p>Healthcare providers have a different measure for criticality. Merely slow, merely down - these can increase patient or financial risk, but they are generally manageable in the frame of reference of an outage. There are always manual workflows for these. Corruption of a handful of patient records due to an interpretation of an obscure standard? That is a <i>major</i> risk and will unleash real chaos.')
