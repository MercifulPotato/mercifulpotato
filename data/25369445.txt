Item(by='curiousgeorgio', descendants=None, kids=[25370137, 25370080], score=None, time=1607572213, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25369049, text='&gt; Where is the line for you when a threat of violence is equivalent to violence?<p>The line is &quot;imminent lawless action&quot; [1], with case law clarifying that &quot;advocacy of illegal action at some indefinite future time&quot; is not considered &quot;imminent&quot; (and therefore protected free speech). It&#x27;s a pretty clear line, and one that most of the censored material being discussed objectively <i>does not cross</i>.<p>Google, Twitter, Facebook, etc. are within their rights as private companies to enforce content rules as they wish, but these recent censorship actions have strong implications as to their protections under Section 230, and are alarming insofar as they represent a trend that crosses the line of free speech protections normally recognized by the government and content platforms.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Imminent_lawless_action" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Imminent_lawless_action</a>')
