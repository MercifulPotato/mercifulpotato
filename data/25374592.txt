Item(by='dcolkitt', descendants=None, kids=[25374767], score=None, time=1607613689, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25373336, text='&gt;  What other motive would a country have to be in the EU, barring ostensibly moralistic arguments, which in themselves will be made with economic motives?<p>The original purpose of the European Coal and Steel Community was to neutralize the risk of repeating World War II by tying the major continental powers into a commercial union. The ostensible purpose was economic integration, but the ultimate purpose was geopolitical harmony.<p>In contrast Thatcher envisioned the EU as a purely commercial arrangement, designed to counterbalance the UK&#x27;s loss of global economic relevance in the face of decolonization. The difference in vision was a major source of conflict between Thatcher and her continental counterparts. They only reached a compromise because Thatcher was concerned about a newly unified Germany having too much power without Britain in the union.')
