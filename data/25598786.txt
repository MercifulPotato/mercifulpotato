By non-volatile I mean that they’re aggregates over purchasing categories - for example, it’s not the simply the price of a “40inch TV” or cod fillet (which may change in ways non-representative of general purchasing practices - as TVs of a given size get cheaper, or cod has a good year) but an aggregate over the entire purchasing category (fresh fish, consumer electronics) based on typical purchasing habits.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www150.statcan.gc.ca&#x2F;n1&#x2F;pub&#x2F;62f0014m&#x2F;62f0014m2019001-eng.htm" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www150.statcan.gc.ca&#x2F;n1&#x2F;pub&#x2F;62f0014m&#x2F;62f0014m2019001...</a><p>When things are added or removed it’s because people have stopped buying that thing (DVD rentals for example).')
