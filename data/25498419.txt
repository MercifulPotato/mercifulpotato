See:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;fred.stlouisfed.org&#x2F;series&#x2F;WFRBLB50107" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;fred.stlouisfed.org&#x2F;series&#x2F;WFRBLB50107</a><p>The average net worth of the bottom 50% over the last 30 years is about the same as it was 30 years ago.<p>Compare to:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;fred.stlouisfed.org&#x2F;series&#x2F;WFRBLT01026" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;fred.stlouisfed.org&#x2F;series&#x2F;WFRBLT01026</a><p>and note that the net worth of the top 1% has been increasing more or less monotonically for the last 30 years, with only a very small dip in 2008-2012.<p>The bottom 50% take a much bigger share of the losses and a much smaller share of the gains.
