Item(by='cbkeller', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605935956, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25167864, text='This is a pet peeve of mine as well; significant figures can be handy, but fundamentally they originated as a quick and dirty method of keeping track of uncertainty that was quite handy when doing calculations by hand, but is more or less obsolete today any time you have an actual quantitative uncertainty bound.<p>Sure, it&#x27;s still convenient when writing things out in text to not continue adding digits far beyond the uncertainty of your value, but even so there is nothing &quot;conservative&quot; or in any way accurate about chopping off a bunch of digits (however imprecise) and replacing them with zeros. This is especially clear if we consider that we generally only ever consider sig figs in base 10, but in any computational context that number is definitely not stored in base 10.')
