It provides typedefs like uint32_t and the rest of them, thus the same UX.<p>&gt; DWARF support<p>Yeah, but no PDB. Ship a Windows program written in Rust, and when it crashes or hangs on end-user’s PC you’re pretty much screwed. You need WinDBG to analyze these memory dumps written by the OS, and for that you need pdb symbols as opposed to dwarf.')
