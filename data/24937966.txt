Item(by='root_axis', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604019254, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24937846, text='It&#x27;s because the user on a website is understood to be representing themselves, not the organization that created the website. The intent of section 230 was to codify this distinction in representation into the law.<p>On the other hand, a print magazine hires employees to produce a bespoke product sold to the public, so whatever they publish in the magazine is understood to be a representation of the magazine company. In fact, the owner of a website <i>would</i> indeed be liable for posting infringing content if it were understood that the site owner was representing themselves (e.g. if I posted stolen photos from your laptop onto my self-hosted blog).')
