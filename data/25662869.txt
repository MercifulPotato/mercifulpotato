You need to maintain GA residence. The full text of the relevant statute is here[0]. Note section 15b.<p>The form requires an address for contact and precinct information. People without a permanent address can use a common place of residence (where they can receive mail), such as a shelter, or if they don&#x27;t have one of those, they can register in the park they sleep in [1].<p>Ultimately, it is up to the district registrar to determine if the residency is legitimate, but this is decided not based on the existence of a conventional fixed address, but based on the demonstrated intent to reside in the state (and more specifically the municipality).<p>[0]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;law.justia.com&#x2F;codes&#x2F;georgia&#x2F;2010&#x2F;title-21&#x2F;chapter-2&#x2F;article-6&#x2F;21-2-217&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;law.justia.com&#x2F;codes&#x2F;georgia&#x2F;2010&#x2F;title-21&#x2F;chapter-2...</a><p>[1]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;faq.georgiavoter.guide&#x2F;en&#x2F;article&#x2F;how-to-register-to-vote-if-you-experience-homelessness" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;faq.georgiavoter.guide&#x2F;en&#x2F;article&#x2F;how-to-register-to...</a>')
