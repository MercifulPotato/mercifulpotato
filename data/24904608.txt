Item(by='forgotmypw17', descendants=None, kids=[24905057], score=None, time=1603774900, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24901941, text='It begins with those who have the know-how to do it today, and they are bringing their friends and family, and the acceleration is exponential.<p>Look at Digg: two-fold cause of 1) UI changes, and 2) user perceptions of centrally controlled, biased, and influenced information flow.<p>Digg was thought to have unbreakable critical mass.<p>Now, Reddit and Facebook are both going down the same road, with user-hostile UI and opaque information sourcing.<p>People aren&#x27;t dumb, and they&#x27;ve already noticed and made up their mind about it. Now they&#x27;re just looking for somewhere to jump.<p>I recently interviewed two kids no older than 20 for a user study. Not techies. One of them doesn&#x27;t use Snapchat for privacy reasons. Other one thought about it and said, &quot;I don&#x27;t know why I use it, because I know that it&#x27;s not really private.&quot; I didn&#x27;t even prompt them with any questions about privacy or control, I was just doing a cursory &quot;what apps or social networks do you use?&quot;<p>More and more people are saying, &quot;I know it&#x27;s crap, but I&#x27;m on there because everyone else is using it.&quot; It means they&#x27;re already looking for another place to go.')
