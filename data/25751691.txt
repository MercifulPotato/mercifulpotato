Item(by='samatman', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610478112, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25750052, text='With ledger, this is very easy.<p>I also run a janky Python script which imports my various .csv files, detects which is which, and turns them into ledger entries.<p>So a vacation stop at a gas station would look like this:<p><pre><code>    2021-01-01 Chevron\n    Assets:Checking   $-100.00\n    Expenses:Auto:Gas\n</code></pre>\nSo lets say I know I spent $40 of that on food, and want to put another $40 into the vacation budget, I modify like so:<p><pre><code>    2021-01-01 Chevron\n    Assets:Checking   $-100.00\n    Expenses:Food     $40\n    Expenses:Vacation    $40\n    Expenses:Auto:Gas\n</code></pre>\nAnd it puts the rest in the gas budget.<p>There are ways to report average expenditures and smooth them out, ways to report how many days between each purchase in a particular category or from a particular merchant, and so on.  Want to amortize a particular purchase? No problem, it&#x27;s a periodic transaction.<p>ledger-cli can do accounting, really if there&#x27;s a question about your money or a way you want to keep track of it, it&#x27;s covered. Several graph libraries can ingest ledger files.')
