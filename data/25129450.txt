Item(by='gopalv', descendants=None, kids=[25131216, 25129739], score=None, time=1605645352, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25126388, text='&gt; we can check other elements as potential reversible fuels<p>In theory, you want an abundant substance which takes up oxygen, produces solid waste, which is easy to clean up, raw material which is stable in outdoor conditions (if spilled) and non-toxic to your skin in either form.<p>I read through the literature after seeing a NewAtlas article a couple of weeks ago [1] &amp; [2].<p>&gt; Nitrogen? Sulfur?<p>Gases are harder to take to recycling.<p>&gt; Sodium?<p>Can&#x27;t touch it without gloves or sneeze around it (the problem is H2 gas, not the sodium itself).<p>There was a memorable sodium leak in a Kalpakkam nuclear power plant in India that I remember, where the issue was the H2 from the sodium + water lines breaking.<p>&gt; Aluminum?<p>Aluminium would be easier to mine out of the ground than Fe, but Al02 to Al is a harder conversion to do already in terms of energy efficiency.<p>And as someone who&#x27;s burned through a crucible and the floor with Thermite &amp; spent time cleaning it up, the real value of iron oxide is that it doesn&#x27;t get &quot;harder by burning&quot; (AlO2 is <i>HARD</i>) &amp; produces a fragile and flaky waste product which is easy to clean off the equipment.<p>[1] - <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;newatlas.com&#x2F;energy&#x2F;bavarian-brewery-carbon-free-renewable-iron-fuel&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;newatlas.com&#x2F;energy&#x2F;bavarian-brewery-carbon-free-ren...</a><p>[2] - <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;newatlas.com&#x2F;mcgill-metal-powder-fuel&#x2F;40869&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;newatlas.com&#x2F;mcgill-metal-powder-fuel&#x2F;40869&#x2F;</a>?')
