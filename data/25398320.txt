Item(by='MaulingMonkey', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607782453, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25396600, text='Both matter.<p>It&#x27;s easy enough to tell someone to never contact you again.  But if you see them daily as you prepare to commute because you live next door, a lifetime of social pressure to not be so rude as to ignore people is likely to get them to slip up and at least say &quot;good morning!&quot;, even if they <i>want</i> to respect your boundaries (and definitely if they don&#x27;t, and are looking for an excuse to trample all over them while perhaps pretending otherwise!)  If you share a common living space or other &quot;shared business&quot; it&#x27;s even worse, as there&#x27;s also the completely understandable pressure, if not outright <i>need</i>, to resolve problems involving that shared business.<p>Moving is an action that can help disentangle you from shared business - be it common living spaces, shared fences, shared neighborhood issues, etc. - and help dismantle habits that would undermine your boundaries.')
