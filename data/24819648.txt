Ad blocker extensions are a major security risk, and this fully eliminates the risk without breaking most of the functionality.<p>Adblocker extensions need full access to all network traffic and all it takes is a single person&#x27;s account or machine to be compromised to get access to millions of browsers. Chrome extension compromises are a somewhat common occurrence - see [1] for a recent example.<p>I <i>want</i> ad blocking without giving the extension access to my cloud accounts, bank statements or company intranet.<p>My current solution is to use the ExtensionSettings[3] Chrome policy to blacklist extensions from particularly sensitive domains like accounts.google.com, my bank and the company intranet, but it&#x27;s a clunky solution - I still want tracking and ad scripts blocked on those!<p>[1]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=24803740" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=24803740</a><p>[2]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;blog.chromium.org&#x2F;2019&#x2F;06&#x2F;web-request-and-declarative-net-request.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;blog.chromium.org&#x2F;2019&#x2F;06&#x2F;web-request-and-declarativ...</a><p>[3]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;support.google.com&#x2F;chrome&#x2F;a&#x2F;answer&#x2F;9867568?hl=en" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;support.google.com&#x2F;chrome&#x2F;a&#x2F;answer&#x2F;9867568?hl=en</a>')
