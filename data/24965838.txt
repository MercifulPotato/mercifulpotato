Item(by='geerlingguy', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604306372, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24965774, text='The BCM2711 SoC inside is a newer stepping (C0 vs B0 in Pi 4 model B), and can run faster and slightly cooler than the older revision.<p>Thus the Pi 400 has a 1.8 GHz default clock, while the Pi 4 model B has a 1.5 GHz clock.<p>Note that I just checked my Compute Module 4, and it also has the C0 revision of the chip... so it&#x27;s interesting the Pi Foundation chose to keep it at the default 1.5 GHz.<p>It might have to do with the fact that the Pi 400 has a massive  keyboard-sized heatsink attached to the SoC in the unit!')
