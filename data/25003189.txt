Item(by='cosmie', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604618342, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25000978, text='You don&#x27;t have to have a global network to have global acceptance - RuPay[1] is a domestic-only network in India, but RuPay cards are accepted internationally via the Discover network. And the Fed seems to be heavily emphasizing interoperability with existing&#x2F;external financial systems for FedNow, so there&#x27;s no reason to think that wouldn&#x27;t extend to interoperability&#x2F;integration with international partners to extend acceptance internationally.<p>And even if they don&#x27;t, siphoning off a huge chunk of domestic-only US transactions will still be a massive blow to Visa and Mastercard&#x27;s revenue stream. And the ubiquitous nature of FedNow domestically could easily disrupt market dynamics, if&#x2F;when it reaches a point of saturation where businesses can actively choose to <i>not</i> accept Visa&#x2F;Mastercard payments. Squeezing their 40-50+% profit margins on top of reducing their revenue, as they&#x27;re forced to actually be competitive against FedNow.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;RuPay" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;RuPay</a>')
