Item(by='beardyw', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609066610, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25547448, text='&quot;What If You Could Do It All Over?&quot; - well without the benefit of hindsight it would be exactly the same of course. Free will exists up to the time we make a choice, but that is just the weighing up of the situation. The choice itself is inevitable.<p>When we release a dice from our hand it could land on any face, but the face it lands on isn&#x27;t truly random, it is just too hard to establish the factors which caused it. So with our choices.<p>A sure belief that my choices right or wrong were all I could do at the time has saved me from endless doubt and speculation. The only choices I can make are today.<p>I saw the film About Time recently which is based on this whole idea of going back and doing it differently - with hindsight. Whilst it was quite enjoyable, the inconsistencies caused by the idea just shouted out throughout. Life isn&#x27;t and can&#x27;t be like that.')
