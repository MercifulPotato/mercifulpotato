Item(by='flurben', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1612105062, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25976867, text='This would mean having elected officials, top-level managers, who knew what software development was, and prioritized those capabilities over extended periods of time.<p>The top 2-5 layers of management tend to be the types who want solutions, and they don&#x27;t care how it works, and they don&#x27;t really want to be bothered with the details.<p>To the extent they expend thought at all on systems, they tend to be focused primarily on questions like, &quot;why is all this stuff so confusing?&quot; &quot;who do I blame when this thing goes wrong?&quot;<p>Where I work, they go through cycles; a higher-up will say &quot;Hey let&#x27;s build a staff of in-house developers.&quot;  And they&#x27;ll do that for 2 or 3 years, until someone reads an article in CIO magazine about how outsourcing is superior.  Then they&#x27;ll start unfairly purging and firing developers, writing code is now &quot;bad&quot;, configuration is &quot;Good.&quot;  This goes on for several years, until the cycle starts over.  So as a developer, after you survive your first purge, you begin to see that invisibility is the only way to survive.')
