Item(by='Jugurtha', descendants=None, kids=[25311515], score=None, time=1607002235, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25288133, text='&gt;<i>Slack, Outlook in Office 365, Jira, and Zendesk are the goto tools</i><p>Add long-form writing in there. One of the most important things is alignment so everyone knows what they&#x27;re doing and why they&#x27;re doing it. This helps people become independent, make their own decisions, and take actions.<p>Upstream emails that align everyone across several functions, skillsets, and abstraction levels prevent many small interactions and ambiguities downstream, effectively leveraging your time.<p>Explaining the objectives at a high level in terms everyone understands, then going incrementally deeper to tie these objectives to specific actions of the relevant people.<p>Tying revenue to scalability to spinning up Kubernetes nodes to an open issue to a line in a particular file that should be changed is a journey across abstraction levels.<p>This is our current state, this is our desired state, this is the gap between the two, and we haven&#x27;t crossed that gap because X, Y, and Z. This is what we&#x27;ll do to solve that, cross that gap, and go from our current state to our desired state.<p>Writing down these things exposes thought processes and rationale. It allows people to chime in at the abstraction level they prefer to augment, correct, poke holes, shed light on blind spots, and question assumptions in these thought processes. It also enables everyone to be on the same wavelength.<p>The above &quot;tying&quot; thread allows an advisor to comment, a CEO to comment, a designer to comment, a developer to comment, and a domain expert to comment. Each one of them has a place they can &quot;hook&quot; into.<p>How many conversations&#x2F;requests&#x2F;Slack direct messages&#x2F;phone calls does this prevent?<p>I replied yesterday to someone asking about what they can do to understand and monitor product usage and drive development[0].
