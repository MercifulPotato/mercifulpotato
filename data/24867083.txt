Item(by='open-source-ux', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603440396, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24865450, text='If you&#x27;re looking for a low-cost alternative to Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator or InDesign, take a look at the Affinity suite of apps. Although they don&#x27;t match Adobe apps feature-by-feature, they are professional grade apps with a lot of features. They are cross-platform (Mac and Windows) and they even have iPad versions of the Designer and Photo apps. The Affinity apps are not based on an online subscription model.<p>The Affinity Spotlight website showcases what people are using the apps for and the type of content they are creating:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;affinityspotlight.com&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;affinityspotlight.com&#x2F;</a><p>I have all the Affinity apps and don&#x27;t miss Adobe one bit, but the Affinity apps are a bit clunkly in their UI (but far less clunky than Adobe&#x27;s apps).<p>After Effects (AE) is the only Adobe app that has no real rival. A new motion and animation app called Cavalry recently launched but it&#x27;s far too early to see if it can rival AE. It also uses an online subscription model (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cavalry.scenegroup.co&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cavalry.scenegroup.co&#x2F;</a>).')
