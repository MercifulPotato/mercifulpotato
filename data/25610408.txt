Item(by='PragmaticPulp', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609561995, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25610192, text='There is no one, single, correct answer. People have been starting successful startups everywhere, long before COVID.<p>Being in SF makes it easier to raise venture capital, but it&#x27;s still possible to raise capital elsewhere.<p>Rabois is making a lot of noise about Miami, but it&#x27;s not going to turn into a startup hub overnight. It&#x27;s not exactly bad right now, but Austin has a better startup scene <i>at the moment</i>.<p>Personally, I&#x27;d recommend that you figure out which city makes you the happiest. It&#x27;s much easier to maintain motivation to build a startup when you&#x27;re in a place you enjoy. If you&#x27;re living in a city you hate just because you think it&#x27;s somehow optimal for business reasons, it&#x27;s going to be a drag on everything else. Optimize for happiness and fit everything else to it.')
