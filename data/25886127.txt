Item(by='danenania', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611436229, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25885898, text='Isn’t the penal system ultimately the bottleneck that stops a lot of minor crime and property crime from being properly investigated?<p>Since prisons are full, people committing these crimes rarely end up doing time even if they’re caught, so police figure why bother if they’ll just be back on the street a week later.<p>Assuming that’s true, one thing we’ve really got to do immediately is get everyone serving time for victimless crimes out of there to make space for people who are actually causing harm to society. We should also probably reduce sentence length and optimize for lowering recidivism, not punishment.<p>On the other side of the funnel, basic income might also go a long way toward removing the motivation for doing these things in the first place.')
