Wonkishness is a positive trait in discussing policy, but it means that rhetoric, no matter how openly disingenuous, <i>has</i> to be addressed; the consequences (or the inanity) have to be dissected, that&#x27;s <i>part of why they are there</i>.<p>Incidentally, this is what leads to the current state of affairs--the side that is unmoored from any pretense of reality can say <i>whatever the hell they want</i> and will put the other side into knots.<p>It isn&#x27;t clever, and it is frustrating because your advice is good, but it is real.')
