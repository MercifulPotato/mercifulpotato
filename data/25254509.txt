Item(by='pjc50', descendants=None, kids=[25254540, 25254873], score=None, time=1606749072, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25254110, text='Yes, it&#x27;s called Apple A1.<p>Once you remove a single opcode, it&#x27;s not really x86 any more, and you need emulation at OS level. But then, once you&#x27;ve done that, why not remove more instructions? Why not remove <i>all</i> of them and start again on a much more power-efficient platform? Why not remove the <i>memory model</i>?<p>One of the brilliant ideas in the A1 is a flag for whether the current process insists on the slower but more comprehensible x86 memory ordering.')
