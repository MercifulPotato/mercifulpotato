Item(by='benlumen', descendants=None, kids=[25084652, 25083969], score=None, time=1605283413, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25080084, text='I&#x27;m probably stating the obvious, but I think it&#x27;s always worth keeping in mind that these software verification systems, both on Windows and now MacOS, are <i>not for us</i>.<p>Ten years ago, I was constantly getting laptops dropped off at my house from friends and family who&#x27;d picked up some virus and needed a clean install. That doesn&#x27;t happen anymore, and it&#x27;s not because they&#x27;re no longer using laptops - they are.<p>It&#x27;s thanks to these security systems. And yes, there are privacy implications. But for most people, you&#x27;d have to compare those to the privacy implications of having some virus sweeping your hard drive.<p>If you&#x27;re the sort of person who reads Hacker News you can probably spot a fake program or dodgy link in e-mail a mile off. But if you&#x27;re not interested enough to care enough to know the difference, there&#x27;s no free lunch on the privacy issue.')
