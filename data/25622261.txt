Item(by='jokethrowaway', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609687561, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25620264, text='I tried learning go a few times years ago but got stuck with tooling issues and when I made it to work I was put off by the syntax &#x2F; verbosity, error messages and never managed to learn it.<p>I started doing a project in rust and things just made a lot of sense (with previous haskell, C&#x2F;C++, JS experience), fixing compiler errors until green was easy.\nEventually I developed an understanding for fixing lifetime related issues, which is the only complicated bit in rust (and hard to fix ones are not even that common).<p>I&#x27;m definitely more confident in the correctness of my programs written in rust than anything else - even than haskell, my previous gold standard experience, given the lack of GC and laziness.<p>I think different people will grok different languages.<p>From a maintainability point of view, from what I experienced in a few companies, most developers will not feel comfortable with either go or rust.')
