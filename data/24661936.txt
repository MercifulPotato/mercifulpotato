Item(by='Tainnor', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1601642093, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24661770, text='Why is it sloppy?\nThe question of whether you use O or Theta is just that the one gives an upper bound only, and the other one ensures a tight bound (usually many people write O, when they mean Theta, which is not wrong, but is also doesn&#x27;t tell you as much; in the end, it&#x27;s mostly clear what is intended though).<p>That doesn&#x27;t mean that you can&#x27;t use either notation to refer to a worst-case or an average-case (given a suitably defined probability distribution) performance. To say &quot;expected complexity for hash table lookup is O(1)&quot; is neither wrong, nor sloppy.')
