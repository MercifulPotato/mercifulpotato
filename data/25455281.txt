Item(by='avar', descendants=None, kids=[25455372], score=None, time=1608211127, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25454878, text='We&#x27;re really just telling the compiler to find a file called stdio.h and act as though its contents were included at that part in our program.<p>Whether the full function is there or linked later is a matter of implementation (some headers have full functions, typically trivial &quot;inline&quot; functions).<p>I can&#x27;t remember what the standard says via the stdlib, but in general a library you load later on may not have all&#x2F;some of those functions, but as long as you didn&#x27;t use them you&#x27;ll be fine (just as with a redundant forward declaration you yourself did).<p>And finally the whole &quot;linking&quot; part of C is mostly just a widespread convention, not something that the standard really dictates. See section 6.2.2 [1] for the brief words on it mainly to do with &quot;static&quot;&#x2F;&quot;extern&quot;. But the whole &quot;compile&quot; -&gt; &quot;link&quot; (to some existing <i>.so&#x2F;</i>.a) -&gt; &quot;program&quot; is all implementation defined.<p>1. <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.open-std.org&#x2F;jtc1&#x2F;sc22&#x2F;wg14&#x2F;www&#x2F;docs&#x2F;n1256.pdf" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.open-std.org&#x2F;jtc1&#x2F;sc22&#x2F;wg14&#x2F;www&#x2F;docs&#x2F;n1256.pdf</a>')
