Item(by='Bakary', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610293985, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25714760, text='There are two insights that helped me do well in that regard<p>— 80% of the work is done in the kitchen. Getting your nutrition right is far more important than anything else.<p>— Integrating physical activity in your life through low friction means. The main roadblock here is the psychological burden of exercising regularly when you don&#x27;t want to. Hence the more of that burden you offload by integrating it as a habit, the easier it will be. Most of your efforts are to be directed towards making physical activity as friction free as possible<p>These in turn have their own corollaries<p>— Thinking in terms of specific diets invites failure as opposed to thinking in terms of lifestyle and changing your relationship to food<p>— Separating exercise as this high-priority activity that you have to do is an invitation to failure as well, as it increases the psychological burden and unconscious resentment. It&#x27;s far easier to rediscover the inherent pleasure of physical activity as you sense your strength build and your muscles and heart activate.')
