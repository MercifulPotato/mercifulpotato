They like protections such as being able to chargeback if services they&#x27;ve paid for don&#x27;t get delivered; goods are defective or counterfeit and so on. Yes, this can make life hard for merchants: on the other hand, they are free not to accept credit cards. Most do, because it&#x27;s a price of doing business.</i><p>That&#x27;s the problem. You&#x27;re free not to accept credit cards, but only in the sense that you&#x27;re free not to be able to actually sell anything to a large number of people in some very important markets. It shouldn&#x27;t be a &quot;price of doing business&quot; to accept chargeback abuse, and it shouldn&#x27;t be OK for the financial firms that permit chargeback abuse using their systems to wash their hands of the resulting liability. But right now, in practice, it is.')
