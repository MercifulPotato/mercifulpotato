Item(by='Kim_Bruning', descendants=None, kids=[25712014], score=None, time=1610263965, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25710496, text='Saying that (en.)wikipedia has a US progressive-left worldview is subtly misleading.<p>Specifically en.wikipedia.org has a western (US&#x2F;UK sphere of influence) worldview.<p>This is because en.wikipedia is&#x2F;can be edited by everyone who speaks English: not just the USA, not just regular English speaking countries, but also all those countries that teach children English as a second language, (and a not insignificant smattering of people who learn English outside of school too)<p>When you compare countries around the world, The United States of America is said to  lean rather right of center.<p>Thus from a US perspective you would expect en.wikipedia to appear to indeed be a bit on the left.<p>(this is not a comprehensive answer, but it does cover a lot of the ground)')
