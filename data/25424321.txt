The chance of prescription opioid addiction is about 25%, and I wouldn&#x27;t expect heroin to be noticably more addictive as a chemical substance.  <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cdc.gov&#x2F;drugoverdose&#x2F;opioids&#x2F;prescribed.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cdc.gov&#x2F;drugoverdose&#x2F;opioids&#x2F;prescribed.html</a><p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.jrf.org.uk&#x2F;sites&#x2F;default&#x2F;files&#x2F;jrf&#x2F;migrated&#x2F;files&#x2F;1859354254.pdf" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.jrf.org.uk&#x2F;sites&#x2F;default&#x2F;files&#x2F;jrf&#x2F;migrated&#x2F;file...</a> is a very small survey of heroin users, some of whom report controlled or occasional use.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.drugabuse.gov&#x2F;publications&#x2F;research-reports&#x2F;heroin&#x2F;scope-heroin-use-in-united-states" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.drugabuse.gov&#x2F;publications&#x2F;research-reports&#x2F;hero...</a> shows that with 90K-170K new users per year from 2006 to 2016 resulted in 600K total dependent users in 2016 in the U.S., so not all first-time users become dependent and presumably the 2016 count includes people who tried heroin for the first time before 2006.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cdc.gov&#x2F;drugoverdose&#x2F;data&#x2F;heroin.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cdc.gov&#x2F;drugoverdose&#x2F;data&#x2F;heroin.html</a> confirms that the difference in new users and final dependent users wasn&#x27;t all due to deaths, either.<p>There&#x27;s still a possibility that only rehab keeps people from becoming dependent but the numbers are harder to find for that.')
