Item(by='dredmorbius', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602861540, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24798302, text='An etymological note.<p>I&#x27;d known that <i>most</i> in Slavic means &quot;bridge&quot; dating back to the destruction of the famed Stari Most (&quot;Old Bridge&quot;) in Mostar (named for the bridge, or its keepers), Bosnia and Herzegovina, in November 1993 during the Croat–Bosniak War.[1]  The bridge was rebuilt in 2004.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Stari_Most" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Stari_Most</a><p>A related English word is <i>mast</i>:<p>&gt; &quot;long pole on a ship, secured as the lower end to the keel, to support the yards, sails, and rigging in general,&quot; Old English <i>mæst</i>, from Proto-Germanic <i>masta</i> (source also of Old Norse <i>mastr</i>, Middle Dutch <i>maste</i>, Dutch, Danish <i>mast</i>, German <i>Mast</i>), from PIE <i>mazdo-</i> &quot;a pole, rod&quot; (source also of Latin <i>malus</i> &quot;mast,&quot; Old Irish <i>matan</i> &quot;club,&quot; Irish maide &quot;a stick,&quot; Old Church Slavonic <i>mostu</i> &quot;bridge&quot;).<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.etymonline.com&#x2F;word&#x2F;mast" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.etymonline.com&#x2F;word&#x2F;mast</a><p>This is also probably the source for Mosul, the city in present-day Iraq, from the Arabic <i>al-Mawsul</i>, literally &quot;the joined,&quot; refering to the bridge there.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.etymonline.com&#x2F;word&#x2F;Mosul" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.etymonline.com&#x2F;word&#x2F;Mosul</a><p><i>Bridge</i> itself comes from proto-Germanic <i>brugjo</i>, &quot;log, beam&quot;.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.etymonline.com&#x2F;word&#x2F;bridge" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.etymonline.com&#x2F;word&#x2F;bridge</a><p>The French <i>pont</i> is from Latin <i>pons</i>, bridge, earlier &quot;connecting gallery, walkway&quot;.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.etymonline.com&#x2F;word&#x2F;pons" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.etymonline.com&#x2F;word&#x2F;pons</a><p>________________________________<p>Notes:<p>1. Also called Mostar Bridge, which since the city was named for the bridge-keepers, is a double <i>and</i> self-referential toponym:  bridge-keeper-city bridge.')
