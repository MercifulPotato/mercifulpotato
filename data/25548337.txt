Item(by='Exmoor', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609036403, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25532168, text='Besides a tremendous statistical data Christmas Bird Counts gather each year, they also have a habit of discovering some truly unusual birds. One of my favorite examples occurred last week.<p>A birder doing a bird count in South Carolina was in a location that only allows access for the bird count. He photographed a small sandpiper that appeared to be Little Stint, a species native to Eurasia and <i>very</i> rare in North America. It happened to have a metal band on its legs and close inspection of photographs were able to reveal a code that traced the bird back to Ottenby Bird Observatory off the east coast of Sweden, where it had been banded in September of this year.<p>Sadly both of my CBCs were cancelled this year due to concerns about pandemic.<p>There is also a spring&#x2F;summer cousin to the CBC, the Breeding Bird Survey (BBS). \n<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.usgs.gov&#x2F;centers&#x2F;pwrc&#x2F;science&#x2F;north-american-breeding-bird-survey?qt-science_center_objects=0#qt-science_center_objects" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.usgs.gov&#x2F;centers&#x2F;pwrc&#x2F;science&#x2F;north-american-bre...</a>')
