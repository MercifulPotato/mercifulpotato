Item(by='gumby', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607235824, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25321471, text='Yes, I believe it was the first machine Lisp ran on though that was before my time.<p>But the PDP-6 was a different class of device as every machine word was (could store) a cons; the common list manipulation functions (not just car and cdr but rplaca et al) were actual machine instructions etc (even if the names were different, e.g. CAR was HLRZ, as I can still remember).<p>In other words, lisp was implemented _on_ the IBM machine while the design of the 6&#x2F;10 architecture was influenced by the needs of lisp. Both Marvin and Gordon independently confirmed this to me.')
