Item(by='zaroth', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610502153, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25748086, text='You’ve mentioned Section 2339a multiple times in various threads. I think you may fundamentally misunderstand the necessary elements of the charge.<p>Specifically I would point you to a legal analysis of this particular section [1] which includes the following;<p>&gt; <i>Under the provisions of 18 U.S.C. 2, anyone who counsels, procures, aids, or abets a violation of Section 2339A or any other federal crime is punishable as though he had committed the offense himself. It is understood that “[i]n order to aid and abet another to commit a crime it is necessary that a defendant in some sort associate himself with the venture, that he participate in it as in something that he wishes to bring about, [and] that he seek by his action to make it succeed.”</i><p>As I believe is made clear in this analysis, providing hosting services to a social network that includes discussions about storming the capital in no way implicates either the hosting provider or the social network under 18 USC 2339A.<p>[1] - <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;apps.dtic.mil&#x2F;dtic&#x2F;tr&#x2F;fulltext&#x2F;u2&#x2F;a530624.pdf" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;apps.dtic.mil&#x2F;dtic&#x2F;tr&#x2F;fulltext&#x2F;u2&#x2F;a530624.pdf</a>')
