Imagine if you published your game online as multi-player, someone could devise the randomness and predict the next moves in the game, which could allow them to cheat. For encryption, a predictable RNG can break the entire scheme.<p>In your casual sense of just a single-player game, there&#x27;s no harm, but the idea in replacing MT with something like PCG is to be more secure&#x2F;safe by default, since you never know what someone will use rand() for.')
