Item(by='userbinator', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611671608, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25914990, text='<i>It&#x27;s cheaper to build in a 32.768kHz crystal for timekeeping than try to access the grid frequency from the isolated low-voltage circuit.</i><p>Those products don&#x27;t use isolated power supplies --- normally they use a capacitive dropper. A high-value resistor to an IC pin is sufficient to drive the clock counter, and a resistor is definitely cheaper than a crystal.')
