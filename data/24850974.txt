They&#x27;re not a party to the transaction; it is the vendor selling cryptocurrency that is taking on the risk.  Paypal can reverse the transaction and the vendor has to eat the risk, same as for any PayPal transaction.<p>If I buy a sweater from some company through PayPal, and the company sends me the sweater, they&#x27;re out the sweater if they get a chargeback. They can fight it, but PayPal is just the middleman.<p>Cryptocurrency vendors would have to figure out a policy to deal with it, so it might be that nobody would be willing to sell crypto for PayPal, but there&#x27;s no reason for PayPal to block it.')
