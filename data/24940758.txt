Item(by='rendall', descendants=None, kids=[24941262], score=None, time=1604052588, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24940653, text='Interesting idea.<p>I see 2 potential problems.<p>If the approach would be content-neutral - i.e. simply relying on the <i>form</i> of the information (eg misspellings in the title, many exclamation points, and such) to distinguish that which is more likely to be fake - then there could be a race condition where the misinformation purveyors learn and subvert the algorithm followed by the misinformation <i>identifier</i> incorporating the new forms of misinformation, and so on. In the meantime, true information purveyors would also need to be aware of this algorithm so as not to be falsely labeled. Think of the race in SEO for an example<p>If the misinformation identifier uses the content of the information to label misinformation, then the identifier itself is as open to bias and opinion as anyone else')
