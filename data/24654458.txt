Item(by='shard', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1601576920, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24650043, text='Another point is that displays can never provide real color representation anyway.<p>The process flow goes from converting the full spectrum lights in nature into color filtered photons captured on a camera sensor with an artificial color profile, it&#x27;s own resolution and pixel arrangement, and its own lens specifics, then converted to another limited color palette with another artificial color profile and it&#x27;s own resolution and pixel arrangement that is the display, in order to simulate the colors of nature as detected by yet another set of filtered detectors with it&#x27;s own color profile, resolution, neuron arrangement, and lens specifics that is the human eye. There is already no such thing as objective truth when it comes to photography, every step involves heavy interpretation.')
