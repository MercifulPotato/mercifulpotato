Plenty of people parrot the founders as though they had some kind of divine insight into what makes a good state, rather than to see political systems as subsequent attempts to learn from mistakes made in the past. It is very well possible that &#x27;free speech&#x27; should be sacrosanct, but the US is doing a piss-poor job of showing that that is the case.<p>Political systems come and they go, so far the &#x27;free speech&#x27; countries are not doing remarkably better than the countries with some limitations on free speech and it would be good to recognize this and to see what can be learned from each other rather than to put dogma first.')
