Item(by='abecedarius', descendants=None, kids=[24660362, 24659254], score=None, time=1601583080, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24655140, text='Yes, real compilers do handle `((lambda (x) ...) ...)` as well as a `let`, because as a Lisp programmer you want to be able to define and use new macros freely, without having to worry too much about low-level optimizations. Kent Dybvig gave a talk on this sort of thing called the &quot;macro-writer&#x27;s Bill of Rights&quot;. It&#x27;s a similar concept to the recent talk about what optimizations need to be guaranteed for &quot;zero-cost abstraction&quot; in Rust and C++.')
