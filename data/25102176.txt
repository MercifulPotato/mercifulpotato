Item(by='whimsicalism', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605456423, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25101576, text='&gt; I think that it&#x27;s possible to minimize tribalism by enabling civil discussion that shares many perspectives, with all hostility removed (and perhaps minimal emotions), and most importantly, let the audience form their own opinions.<p>It is unsurprising that HN commentators think that the best discussion forum would be one without any emotion or hostility.<p>I think it is important, however, to guard against the hubris of porting your own model (whether it is American-style democracy or HN-style discussion forums) to every corner of the globe.<p>It might be apparent to us why this model is better, but it might be equally apparent to other, more combative internet commentators, why their model is better. When very smart people on both sides think that a different approach is best, it might be worth treading carefully.')
