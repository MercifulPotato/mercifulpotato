He learned Greek in his old age, after despising the language and its writers during the whole of his earlier life. He was a friend and patron of the poet Ennius, and brought him to Rome, though manifestly without any generous provision for his subsistence; for Ennius led in Rome as poor and straitened a life as he could have left in Sardinia, where Cato found him.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.butwhatfor.com&#x2F;on-old-age-with-cicero&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.butwhatfor.com&#x2F;on-old-age-with-cicero&#x2F;</a>')
