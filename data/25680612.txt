Item(by='tialaramex', descendants=None, kids=[25680714, 25681424, 25680705], score=None, time=1610072054, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25680494, text='While you <i>can</i> buy Ethernet service, e.g. from my ISP, I would anticipate that the set of people who have purchased Ethernet to the Internet <i>and</i> can&#x27;t do TLS is empty. It&#x27;s a niche service, it&#x27;s fast, it&#x27;s symmetric, it&#x27;s <i>expensive</i>.<p>Also you&#x27;re going to need one hell of an adaptor to connect  Ethernet from 1980 to modern Ethernet. The bit protocol is the same, but the hardware is radically different. I have never used the original Ethernet, but I have personally built 10base2 networks (still have terminators in various bit boxes) and I&#x27;ve seen some setups with 10base5 which is the earliest Ethernet that was actually commercially deployed:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;10BASE5" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;10BASE5</a>')
