The goal isn&#x27;t to get the mix sounding good on one particular listening system, but being able to hear the <i>raw, unadulterated</i> signal as well as possible with knowledge of how it will or won&#x27;t translate in other environments. Always good to have multiple sets of speakers to test those assumptions out in real-time. Interestingly, even the geometry of your specific ears will color the resulting sound that is heard, which is why referencing to commercial tracks is so helpful in the process.')
