Item(by='WorldMaker', descendants=None, kids=[25061818], score=None, time=1605042666, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25050498, text='Ngen was never self-contained however and ngen-ed binaries were system <i>and</i> runtime specific. You pretty much had to assume you couldn&#x27;t ship ngen-ed binaries between machines and always ngen in place on the end user&#x27;s machine.<p>.NET Core&#x27;s new AOT systems are much more capable than ngen ever was, including for static, self-contained binaries that you could potentially ship to all systems of a target architecture without shipping the non-AOT IL binaries as well.<p>(ETA: Which is why the vast majority of .NET software hardly ever bothered with ngen except for very specific per-machine performance needs.)')
