One of the most famous examples in History is Caesar&#x27;s cruelty act when the Gaul was almost totally conquered. He wanted to discourage the few inhabitants that may rebel against Rome&#x27;s authority, so he &quot;resolved to deter others by inflicting an exemplary punishment on these. Accordingly he cut off the hands of those who had borne arms against him.&quot; Thousand of men were mutilated and scattered across Gaul to send this terrifying message.<p>The main account about this is in the last chapter of Bellum Gallicum, probably written by one of his lieutenants.
