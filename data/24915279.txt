Item(by='stevekemp', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603859756, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24911169, text='You need to evaluate the lisp at the bottom of the file, so that `M-x new-day`, `M-x today` and the other stuff gets loaded.<p>I&#x27;ve got a bit of code in my init-files that does that automatically, that&#x27;s linked to in the readme.  But as a quick alternative you can scroll down to the end, to the line that starts:<p><pre><code>    &quot;(defun new-day&quot;\n</code></pre>\nSelect that line, and all the way down to the line that reads &quot;(require &#x27;ob-org)&quot;, and then run &quot;M-x eval-region&quot;.  That will evaluate the region and make the two new commands available.<p>Otherwise copy and paste the lisp from here into your emacs init file, and reload everything:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;skx&#x2F;dotfiles&#x2F;blob&#x2F;master&#x2F;.emacs.d&#x2F;init.md#org-mode-code-execution" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;skx&#x2F;dotfiles&#x2F;blob&#x2F;master&#x2F;.emacs.d&#x2F;init.md...</a><p>That will cause anything named `skx-startblock` to be evaluated automatically from an org-file on-load, and will also run the contents of `skx-saveblock` before saving any org-file, if present.')
