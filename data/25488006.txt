Item(by='jcrawfordor', descendants=None, kids=[25488152], score=None, time=1608486084, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25487844, text='DOCSIS authentication is based on the HFC-side MAC address of the modem (part of the DOCSIS management protocol) and TR-069 is not required for any basic functionality - it is typically only used to manage functionality that it not common in third-party modems like an integrated WiFi AP.<p>The more complicated matter is that firmware is typically delivered to the modem via the DOCSIS management protocol (lower level than TR-069) and so the ISP must have firmware available for all supported modems. This is the major factor that leads to compatibility lists. It is possible for the ISP to not manage firmware but most don&#x27;t want to take the risk as a malfunctioning modem can take down a neighborhood in some cases.')
