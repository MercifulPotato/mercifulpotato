Item(by='lumost', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1612202371, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25991085, text='A more fair comparison might be that all advertising sucks from an advertisers perspective... but we just didn&#x27;t have a good way to measure it.<p>In the world of Nielson and print the only consistently measurable ads were brand ads - and brand awareness had a loose correlation across businesses with revenue.  There was no way of rigorously testing whether individual customers propensity to purchase a good from a given business was actually tied to any of the brand awareness ads that were being purchased, or customer&#x27;s brands they had heard of for trust reasons after deciding on a purchase, or if customer&#x27;s simply didn&#x27;t care about the branding.  Bear in mind, any negative datapoint that indicates customer&#x27;s didn&#x27;t care could always be taken as a sign that the &quot;brand&quot; had to be improved.  Not that the ads were worthless.<p>Now on digital any brand exercise, direct pay per conversion, or other form of ad gets attributed to downstream revenue, and we&#x27;re quickly discovering which half of the marketing budget isn&#x27;t working.')
