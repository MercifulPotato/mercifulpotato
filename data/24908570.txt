Later there were 16-bit solutions, like a program called &quot;Can Do&quot; for the Amiga. It isn&#x27;t.  I see this all the time with do-gooder charities on TV &quot;We&#x27;re teaching kids to code, and next week they&#x27;ll all be billionaires!&quot;  The present themselves as if a week in a makeshift classroom is all it takes to compile and deploy a Swift app.<p>I&#x27;ve thought about this since the early part of this year when a middle manager in my company&#x27;s Communications department dismissively told me that she could do my job because, &quot;I know code.&quot;  What does that even mean?')
