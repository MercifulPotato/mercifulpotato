Item(by='yorwba', descendants=None, kids=[25386158], score=None, time=1607694216, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25385376, text='No, if users didn&#x27;t like <i>anything</i> they see on Facebook, they&#x27;d log off. Facebook optimizes to make money and they can&#x27;t make money if people leave, so they try to avoid that scenario.<p>But that doesn&#x27;t mean <i>all</i> content is something people want to see, because Facebook can&#x27;t make money either if users only consume content that doesn&#x27;t make Facebook money, so they try to avoid that scenario.<p>The profit-maximizing strategy is to show users a mixture of content they like (to keep them around) and money-making content, preferably all jumbled together so people can&#x27;t easily tell the difference. (The same calculation is behind Google deemphasizing the difference between paid search ads and actual results.)')
