Item(by='VikingCoder', descendants=None, kids=[25689143, 25689149], score=None, time=1610123840, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25685630, text='Georgia has a poll book.  Do you know what a poll book is, and how it works?  When a ballot is received, the name of the person is entered into a poll book.  Therefore, when you see a poll book and a pile of ballots, you can check that the counts are the same.  Entering data into the poll book is therefore one of the most critical parts of an election, and is the part that is critically observed by election watchers.<p>You cannot merely smuggle in pre-filled ballots, as the ballot counts would not match the poll book counts.<p>You would also need to explain how poll books were falsely updated.<p>If someone smuggled in fake ballots, AND somehow managed to falsify the poll book, it would be possible to use the poll book to ASK people if they voted.  You could then find people who say they did not vote, but there is a record of them voting.<p>It is therefore trivially falsifiable with current election procedures.<p>Or is there something you think I&#x27;m missing?<p>Here is the critical moment where the woman in Michigan argues with a Republican, claiming without evidence that the poll book is off, when it was literally trivial to check it.
