Because i think its pretty good for privacy.<p>Considering that the cost of getting CT was the removal of RFC 7469 HTTP-based PKP &quot;dynamic pins&quot; (aka HPKP), I am not sure if there was a net benefit to privacy - without hate or love for Google.<p>I can&#x27;t fail to notice that intent to removal was to also remove &quot;static pins&quot; (i.e. pins harcoded into the browser) after CT requirement for all certificates was implemented [0], Chrome started checking CT for new certificates [1] and  CA Forum has limited the maximum validity of certificates issued to 39 months (2016) -&gt; 825 days  (2018) -&gt; 398 days (2020) [2] (which means pretty much all certs from 2017 would have been reissued by now!), yet static pins for certain &quot;favoured&quot; organisations remain in the Chromium code [3].<p>That list of favoured orgs? Google, Tor project, Twitter, Dropbox, Facebook, Spideroak, Yahoo and Swehack.<p>CT is great for transparency and tracking rogue certs - but privacy could have used a stronger model than current blind trust in the CAs (and only acting afterwards if the trust proves misplaced).<p>[0] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;groups.google.com&#x2F;a&#x2F;chromium.org&#x2F;g&#x2F;blink-dev&#x2F;c&#x2F;he9tr7p3rZ8&#x2F;m&#x2F;eNMwKPmUBAAJ" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;groups.google.com&#x2F;a&#x2F;chromium.org&#x2F;g&#x2F;blink-dev&#x2F;c&#x2F;he9tr...</a><p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;chromium.googlesource.com&#x2F;chromium&#x2F;src&#x2F;+&#x2F;master&#x2F;net&#x2F;docs&#x2F;certificate-transparency.md#Chrome-Policies" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;chromium.googlesource.com&#x2F;chromium&#x2F;src&#x2F;+&#x2F;master&#x2F;net&#x2F;...</a><p>[2] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cabforum.org&#x2F;wp-content&#x2F;uploads&#x2F;CA-Browser-Forum-BR-1.7.3.pdf" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cabforum.org&#x2F;wp-content&#x2F;uploads&#x2F;CA-Browser-Forum-BR-...</a><p>[3] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;raw.githubusercontent.com&#x2F;chromium&#x2F;chromium&#x2F;master&#x2F;net&#x2F;http&#x2F;transport_security_state_static.json" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;raw.githubusercontent.com&#x2F;chromium&#x2F;chromium&#x2F;master&#x2F;n...</a>')
