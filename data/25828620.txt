Item(by='geofft', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611017092, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25828531, text='I agree with this analysis (and I support the Democrats in all those things you mentioned).<p>What happened was not <i>just</i> increased talk of our government being a democracy - it was also that our republican form of government, over the course of the years, actually made itself a democracy, step by step, from the Twelfth Amendment to the Seventeenth Amendment to the various judicial rulings of the 1960s that recognized the &quot;one person, one vote&quot; standard, among many other steps.<p>The difficulty with the &quot;We&#x27;re not a democracy, we&#x27;re a republic&quot; argument is the same difficulty with monarchists today who have to deal with the fact that their beloved legitimate rulers abdicated or otherwise ceded power legitimately. We certainly <i>were not</i> a democracy in 1789, given the number of people disenfranchised, but we <i>chose to become</i> one, which is an entirely intra-vires thing for a republican government to do.')
