The only general danger is mistaking fetishism of the tool with using the tool for some fully intentional purpose. Beyond that, there can also be the risk of misidentifying tasks as important when they aren&#x27;t necessarily so in the grand scheme of what a person seeks out of life. These two black holes tend to be the consequence of trying to exert control over the world while ending up simulating it instead.<p>&gt;I wish this worked, but the brain adjusts and compensates even for memento mori.<p>Wouldn&#x27;t this be a self-fulfilling prophecy? Even if you aren&#x27;t feeling the understanding of mortality in the moment, you can still draw from the memory and awareness of its existence and your relation to it. You still have choices available to you that can&#x27;t simply be ignored by neurological hand-waving. And if it turns out we don&#x27;t have such choices, then the entire thing never mattered to begin with.')
