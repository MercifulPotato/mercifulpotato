Item(by='toyg', descendants=None, kids=[25009735], score=None, time=1604680656, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25008456, text='It’s not about the time, it’s about the risk of not using the right flags, trusting the build environment, having a process, etc etc. Every time you have to hand-craft anything, from a security perspective, you’re more likely to put a foot wrong in ways that might reveal as disastrous months or years down the line. This is particularly true for such a basic building block as SSH.<p>For example, in any decent Linux distro you’d want to replace the package, not a single executable. So at a minimum it would be openssh-clients, which is a pretty important package to potentially get wrong.')
