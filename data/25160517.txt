Item(by='AnIdiotOnTheNet', descendants=None, kids=[25160801], score=None, time=1605881177, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25156327, text='Personally I can&#x27;t help but think that this is our fault, as an industry, for abandoning the idea that users should be able to create programs. In the 8-bit micro era computers booted into a programming environment, then we had things like QBasic coming standard with the OS, then things like VB, HyperCard, Delphi, and to a lesser extent Excel and Access. For a while, enabling users to create their own tools to suit their needs was considered valuable in personal computing.<p>That&#x27;s not how we do things anymore. Now everything is an appliance and the user is cattle as far as the developer is concerned.')
