Open source is actually a licensing model, not a development methodology. And the licensing implies that at least in theory anyone could get the licensed software at no cost. Given then &quot;business&quot; typically concerns money flowing from A to B, it is certainly not entirely obvious how one can build a business around a product whose theoretical cost is zero.<p>People manage to do this of course, which simply points out that real world economics doesn&#x27;t follow theory that well.')
