In this case, they said multiple times that characters &#x27;screamed with laughter&#x27;. On about the 5th time this became somewhat jarring.<p>You are right that in reported speech a specific character might repeat the same thing over and over. And that in stylised writing there may be stock phrases that are always used without problem. A good example here is the The Iliad by Homer, which uses multiple stock phrases to support oral presentation by a story-teller to an audience. Children&#x27;s literature may use similar conventions (e.g. &quot;Once upon a time...&quot;, &quot;And they all lived Happily ever-after&quot;)')
