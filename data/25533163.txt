Item(by='wegs', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608857630, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25519736, text='They don&#x27;t fall apart. You need to look at it as expected values. Most signals are not 100% noise, but e.g. 90% noise and 10% signal.<p>If you have a candidate from Harvard, it&#x27;s a mixture of ALDCs and really smart people with some bias for policies like affirmative action. You might not know what you&#x27;re getting, but there are odds of scoring that genius. Ditto with most other low-quality signals.<p>And statistically, you do get someone better than a random schmuck off the street. I mean, the processes are noisy, and perhaps 10% of your workforce might make a better CEO, but you have no way of finding those 10%.<p>(And the noisy signals problem in hiring isn&#x27;t specific to CEOs).')
