Item(by='antonvs', descendants=None, kids=[24824401, 24823441], score=None, time=1603081949, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24822166, text='An intuitive way to tell if something is a &quot;real&quot; force (as proposed to a pseudo- or fictitious force) - can someone subject to that force feel it, or equivalently, can an accelerometer measure the acceleration it produces?<p>When you accelerate or decelerate in a vehicle, you feel it. But when you jump out of a plane and accelerate towards the ground, you don&#x27;t feel a force.<p>Noticing this fact was a big part of what led Einstein to his equivalence principle and General Relativity.')
