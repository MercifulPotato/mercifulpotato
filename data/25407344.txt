Item(by='ithkuil', descendants=None, kids=[25417495], score=None, time=1607874639, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25407137, text='&quot;thee&quot; is the dative (and later accusative) inflected form of &quot;thou&quot;, the second person singular pronoun.<p>In your sentence above &quot;Read&quot; is the imperative form of the verb. The subject is elided since it&#x27;s implicitly second person (singular or plural) (as imperative is used to give orders to people you&#x27;re talking to) and if it wasn&#x27;t elided it would have been in nominative case. Middle english had an ending -(e)th to form a plural second person imperative.<p>&quot;Learn the grammar of past and present languages, else you&#x27;ll attract pedants from the intertubes&quot;')
