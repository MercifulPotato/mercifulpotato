Many exchanges send large UDP packets with the most valuable information at the front. Or the packet is structured such that you can make an informed bet based on size and first sub-message.<p>Failing the checksum was sort of common as well, but exchanges don&#x27;t like it.<p>These days most tricks have to do with avoiding as much serialisation time as possible, e.g. by sending ahead part of the TCP payload and filling in with some innocent order if there was no opportunity.')
