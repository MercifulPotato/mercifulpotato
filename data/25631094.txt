Section 230(c)(1):<p><i>No provider or user of an interactive computer service shall be treated as the publisher or speaker of any information provided by another information content provider.</i><p>So then be a publisher or content host but not both and then you don&#x27;t need section 230 for protection at all, according to the letter of law.  That said section 230 was intended to protect things like ISPs and email providers from frivolous lawsuits, but in practice primarily benefits online advertising companies and social media from valid lawsuits.  Even if section 230 were to evaporate tomorrow Hacker News remains in no greater danger of lawsuits than any other medium currently not protected by section 230.')
