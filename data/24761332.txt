Item(by='bsder', descendants=None, kids=[24762644, 24764442], score=None, time=1602551779, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24761100, text='&gt; Considering that rebasing is a crucial feature of version control<p>&quot;Rebasing&quot; is <i>NOT</i> a crucial feature.<p>&quot;Commit history&quot; being immutable is an <i>equally valid</i> axiom.<p>&quot;Rebase&quot; is an architectural feature of git that matches the organization structure of the Linux kernel (individual maintainers squash the detail of the commits as it passes up through developer-&gt;Maintainer A-&gt;Uber Maintainer B-&gt;Super Uber Maintainer C-&gt;Linus) by virtue of Conway&#x27;s Law.<p>I would posit that most development teams would be <i>much</i> better served never squashing commit messages.  Too many groups incur the complexity of rebase without having any reason for doing so.')
