Item(by='SilasX', descendants=None, kids=[24673452, 24666851, 24669980], score=None, time=1601670092, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24660967, text='Even in the average case, hashtables can&#x27;t really be constant. Simple proof: As elements are added, you either resize the table or you don&#x27;t.[1]<p>Resize the table: a bigger table requires larger keys (to have a sufficient number of distinct ones), which require logarithmically more time to compute.<p>Don&#x27;t resize the table: You have to stuff a logarithmic number of keys into each bucket and iterate over them.<p>Both increase time with input size.<p>Earlier discussion: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=9807739" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=9807739</a><p>[1] to clarify, I don’t mean resize on every addition. Just “you occasionally resize or you never do”.')
