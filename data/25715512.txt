Item(by='troydavis', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610292335, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25715438, text='In the US, if your bank, retirement account, or brokerage&#x27;s Web site or account setup process suggested that you name a beneficiary, that was probably a &quot;beneficiary designation.&quot;<p>Those beneficiaries supercede whatever is in your will (if you have one).<p>Here&#x27;s a rough summary that is not legal advice:<p>Pros of using a beneficiary designation: The account contents don&#x27;t need to go through probate before being distributed. There may be some tax benefits for the recipients.<p>Cons: Because named beneficiaries are account-specific and retained by the bank (not centrally like a will), it&#x27;s easy to forget to update them.<p>Either way, it&#x27;s worth considering (a) for a given account, am I using a beneficiary designation or a will&#x2F;probate, and (b) are the beneficiaries current?')
