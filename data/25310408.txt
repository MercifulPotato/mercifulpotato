Item(by='dcolkitt', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607129112, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25310103, text='The link you&#x27;re citing is citing <i>median</i> wages. Productivity is measured in terms of <i>mean</i> output. You&#x27;re comparing apples to oranges.<p><i>Mean</i> wages have closely tracked productivity metrics. <i>Median</i> wages have fallen relative to <i>mean</i> wages. That strongly implies that the issue isn&#x27;t decoupling between wages and productivity, but faster growth in the output of high-skilled workers relative to low-skilled workers.<p>This is confirmed by the empirical research[1], which shows that 61% of the divergence between median wages and productivity has come from income inequality between workers.
