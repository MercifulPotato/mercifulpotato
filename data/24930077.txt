Item(by='jeroenhd', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603976951, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24929700, text='GDPR compliance is no different from any other kind of compliance. If you&#x27;re a big publication then you&#x27;ve got more than enough legal people walking around the office to make sure you aren&#x27;t accidentally printing something that can be construed as libel, to ensure that you&#x27;re paying your taxes correctly, to check new employee contracts, etc.<p>Besides, most websites need to update their privacy policy to be accessed in California anyway. The Californian privacy protection rules aren&#x27;t as strict as the GDPR, but they are very similar. I don&#x27;t really buy the &quot;it&#x27;s expensive to comply&quot; argument a lot of American companies seem to use because of this.<p>The companies want to collect and trade your personal information to the highest bidder, the GDPR got in their way and now these companies are acting out.')
