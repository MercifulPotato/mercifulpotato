Item(by='hobofan', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611479523, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25889475, text='There were too many breakages (and performance regressions in the early 3.x versions) to force a quicker transition, which would likely result in no one upgrading at all.<p>One option could have been to spread the brraking changes over multiple versions, but given the bad state of version&#x2F;dependency management in Python, this would have likely been a clusterfuck too.<p>Best option would have probably been to have a longer period of RCs and only release 3.0 with the performance regressions fixed. The myths around the slowness of 3.x (especially for scientific libraries) stuck around for a very long time after they were fixed.')
