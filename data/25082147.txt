Item(by='xoa', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605275896, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25081736, text='Also FWIW, you can run the Synology OS itself (DSM) on general x86 hardware or VMs via the Xpenology [1] bootloader which is pretty awesome. The DSM is a fairly single-admin friendly network applicance OS anyway with a decent GUI and support. Of course, their NAS hardware offerings make the whole thing more appliance-like and with official support, but if the matrix doesn&#x27;t fit your needs or desires (I wanted my data on ZFS for example) DSM on your own hardware may still be worth giving a look.<p>I actually like Synology&#x27;s software in some ways more than their hardware for many use cases, kind of wish I could just officially buy a license for that (though I guess it works for them in that familiarity means I have recommended them to some small businesses).<p>----<p>1: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;xpenology.org&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;xpenology.org&#x2F;</a>')
