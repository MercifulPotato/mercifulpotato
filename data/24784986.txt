Item(by='creato', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602736637, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24784588, text='You could start by just calculating the potential energy of the volume of the tank you are considering, and the difference in elevation between the reservoirs (volume x 1 kg&#x2F;L x gravity x elevation difference, plug in the units of your choice and let google figure it out).<p>I suspect the amount of energy is far less than you are imagining... It takes a lot of water to generate a useful amount of energy. This video (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=66YRCjkxIcg" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=66YRCjkxIcg</a>) does an interesting demo and points out that a 5 gallon bucket on a ladder has the same potential energy as a watch battery.')
