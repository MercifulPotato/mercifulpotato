Item(by='ethbr0', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611315690, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25870661, text='<i>(Someone correct me if I&#x27;m wrong!)</i> I think about differential dataflow as the <i>solution</i> to &quot;I can&#x27;t batch data operations, because I don&#x27;t know when my various inputs will land.&quot;<p>If everything exists at 7am, and&#x2F;or you don&#x27;t need the freshest computed values, this is not the solution you need.<p>If data A is ready between 2-4am, data B at noon, and data C sometime between 8am-6pm, this allows you to abstract that uncertainty into code, then let the system solve it on a daily basis.<p>This is not a problem everyone has. But it is a problem most people working with inventory or events have! And it&#x27;s usually a problem people feeding things to ML have.')
