Item(by='CaptainZapp', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611126161, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25842570, text='&gt; The thing is, it&#x27;s still a lot better than the mid-century horror stories of institutions; electrotherapy, lobotomy<p>ECT[1] (Electroshock Therapy) has an extremely bad rap (One Flew over the Cukoo&#x27;s Nest didn&#x27;t help), but there&#x27;s quite an important use for it in modern psychiatry.<p>Notably severe depression, where normal meds don&#x27;t help, or where there&#x27;s an urgency to do something immediately (psychiatric meds usually take a few weeks to work and finding the right medication and dosage can be very difficult) ECT may be the last viable option for a patient.<p>Nowadays an anesthetic is applied before the treatment and (except in very rare cases) informed consent by the patient is required.<p>There&#x27;s no doubt that it was massively abused in the past (and let&#x27;s not even get started on lobotomy) and I&#x27;m pretty sure it&#x27;s still abused in countries run by totalitarian regimes.<p>But it can be really the last hope for a patient with severe depression, despite the fact that it still has a very bad image.<p>[1] \n<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Electroconvulsive_therapy" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Electroconvulsive_therapy</a><p>E: clarifications')
