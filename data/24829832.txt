Rosenberg in Nonviolent Communication.<p>Followed by:<p>&gt; Paraphrasing minimizes misunderstandings. At the end of a conversation, you and the speaker will leave with the same interpretation, which will reduce the need for a follow-up.<p>&quot;Accurately repeat&quot; vs &quot;Paraphrasing&quot; – quite the contradiction there.<p>I&#x27;m really not a fan of paraphrasing. It shifts the burden on your counterpart to understand you accurately, and it can be annoying, even destructive of a train of thought. Practice accurate quoting and non-leading questions instead! To take this to an interesting extreme, check out Clean Language [1], a style of questioning that make it as hard as possible to insert assumptions into your questions.
