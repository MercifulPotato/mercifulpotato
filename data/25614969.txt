Google tells me: &quot;The interval from HIV infection to the diagnosis of AIDS ranges from about 9 months to 20 years or longer, with a median of 12 years.&quot; (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www2.health.vic.gov.au&#x2F;public-health&#x2F;infectious-diseases&#x2F;disease-information-advice&#x2F;hiv-and-aids" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www2.health.vic.gov.au&#x2F;public-health&#x2F;infectious-dise...</a>)<p>So yes, <i>median</i> of 12 years. Not minimum.<p>Wikipedia&#x27;s entry on HIV&#x2F;AIDS denialism links to this document from a committee organized by the National Academy of Sciences in 1988 where the committee says they find the evidence of a causal link between HIV and AIDS conclusive (page 33): <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.nap.edu&#x2F;read&#x2F;771&#x2F;chapter&#x2F;4" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.nap.edu&#x2F;read&#x2F;771&#x2F;chapter&#x2F;4</a> The rest of that section explains the evidence they have: extremely high correlates for HIV infection and subsequent AIDS prevalence in at-risk communities, no cases in populations that were not at risk, near 100% detection rate of HIV in the blood of AIDS patients, and the tracing of HIV infected blood donors to transfusion patients acquiring AIDS.')
