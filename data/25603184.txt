Item(by='olliemath', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609508568, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25593193, text='We use pypy pretty extensively at our firm for analytics&#x2F;olap work.<p>We&#x27;ve actually tried using some of the more traditional libs (Pandas et al) with CPython, but there&#x27;s always a pure-python bottleneck (e.g. SQLAlchemy).<p>Performance is important to our clients and trying to keep everything performance critical in C extensions &#x2F; NumPy would be kind of risky for us when adding new functionality, so pypy&#x27;s guarantee of more speed pretty much across the board is awesome.<p>There are downsides of course - higher memory usage, longer boot times, some more obscure libraries being unsupported - but on the whole, it&#x27;s a good choice for us')
