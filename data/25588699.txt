Item(by='jariel', descendants=None, kids=[25588801], score=None, time=1609379677, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25588029, text='This statement trivializes far too much the mechanics of code and software.<p>The &#x27;process requirements&#x27; like RFCs, tech specs and requirements, are important, but they&#x27;re not generally that hard.<p>It takes 5 years of being surrounded by &#x27;good devs&#x27; to understand the more material aspects of software - and the very long list of more mundane things.<p>It&#x27;s like an apprenticeship.<p>There&#x27;s just &#x27;a lot to know&#x27;.<p>Basic style, approaches to solving common problems, getting comfortable with the toolchains etc.<p>On mobile, just knowing when to do something in &#x27;native&#x27; on Android vs. not.<p>Just getting comfortable with git.<p>Every tech domain has a ton of &#x27;know how&#x27;, in the above example, it takes 6 months to really be comfortable with the JNI java bridge for example.<p>Good code review etiquette.<p>Being able to compile massive code bases and overcome the invariable problem on the different platforms.<p>Knowing how how to properly approach a bug fix, how much to test.<p>etc.<p>During this time, there&#x27;s no way a &#x27;young dev&#x27; can &#x27;lead&#x27; a much more experienced dev.<p>A young person could certainly &#x27;lead a project&#x27; but they&#x27;ll have to depend pretty heavily on the professionalism of the senior devs.<p>FYI - if you&#x27;re building basic CRUD apps on relatively established frameworks, and never need to go beyond that at all, a lot of this can be compressed.')
