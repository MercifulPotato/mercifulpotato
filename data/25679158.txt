Item(by='chubot', descendants=None, kids=[25680472], score=None, time=1610062337, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25676628, text='Yeah it&#x27;s definitely a problem that you have this &quot;elsewhere&quot; thing that rots.  Having some kind of online editor is one way to solve it, although it also tends to rot (who hosts that?  Are you limited to PHP?  Are you upgrading PHP?  Did that company get sold?) [1]<p>I think a simpler way to solve it is with the now standard &quot;infrastructure as code&quot; pattern.  For example, use Github Actions and similar platforms to build your blog.<p>Basically check in a spec for a container (Dockerfile) into VCS.  It&#x27;s done off of your machine, and  it will &quot;never&quot; rot.  You still have the cloud portability problem, and the &quot;shell scripts and YAML are gross&quot; problem.  But those are solvable, especially for the relatively simple case of building a blog.  I&#x27;m working on that:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=25343716" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=25343716</a><p>[1] I think it&#x27;s funny that VC&#x27;s use non-startup platforms for their own blogs, because they know that blog platforms do not last.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;blog.samaltman.com&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;blog.samaltman.com&#x2F;</a><p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;posthaven.com&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;posthaven.com&#x2F;</a>')
