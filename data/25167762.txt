Item(by='howlgarnish', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605931057, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25167243, text='It&#x27;s complicated, but perhaps a good comparison is newspaper headlines in the West: headings and phrases etc are compact without ever being slangy.<p>For example, a random ad on the Yahoo Japan homepage proclaims 予約来場いただくと進呈QUOカード５０００円分, which means &quot;get a 5000 yen gift card if you come visit&quot;, but uses literary words like 来場 (arrival&#x2F;visit) and 進呈 (gift) that would rarely be used in spoken Japanese, and even the polite いただく (to humbly receive) is in the short &quot;dictionary&quot; form instead of being conjugated out the way it would be in speech.')
