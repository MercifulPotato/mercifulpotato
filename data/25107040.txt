Item(by='zepto', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605491123, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25106459, text='The presentation video was released <i>after</i> the update was submitted to the store with the contents hidden and activated later.<p>As for whether the update was malicious for the end user, we could say we trust epic to operate a payment method, and therefore the update was not malicious.<p>But there are many actors who would use this exact same methodology, and the update <i>is</i> malicious.  \nSuch Trojans exist on Android.<p>Security policies always prevent behaviors that <i>could</i> be used for non-malicious purposes.<p>If the argument is that the end users should be the ones to decide, it’s really just another way of saying that Apple shouldn’t be allowed to enforce <i>any</i> security policy.<p>Of course there are those who believe that Apple shouldn’t be able to enforce security policies, but there is no overreach here.')
