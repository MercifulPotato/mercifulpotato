Item(by='redis_mlc', descendants=None, kids=[24814244, 24814283, 24814192, 24814731], score=None, time=1602975972, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24814005, text='If you&#x27;re a developer, a database is just a database.<p>If you&#x27;re a corporate DBA, they are each worlds apart.<p>MySQL is a perfect match for most SaaS applications that deal with compliance. Postgres is simply not.<p>The main issues with managing Postgres today are grant inheritance, and also the lack of COPY FROM&#x2F;TO grants. I shouldn&#x27;t have to update grants for non-admin or readonly users after adding new objects, and there needs to be the equivalent of MySQL&#x27;s FILE grant for Postgres&#x27; COPY FROM&#x2F;TO.<p>OTOH, it is easy to reason about MySQL grants, and almost pain-free to update them in production.<p>If anybody can send me a link on how to make Postgres work in a corporate and&#x2F;or compliance environment, let me know.<p>Source: DBA.')
