Item(by='hcnews', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608172308, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25450636, text='Playing small number of openings is a general advice by IM&#x2F;GM teachers for beginner players. This makes sense since the focus is on endgames and middlegames in the initial learning curve.<p>There&#x27;s nothing stopping you from practicing tactics even if you play d4 openings as white. I have been a mainly d4 player as white so far (London, BDG, QG) and have had quick ratings growth (Start -&gt; 1650 chess.com rapid in 5 months).<p>Also, an opening is more like 7-10 moves (not 2-3 moves) where all the minor pieces have been developed and the player has castled.')
