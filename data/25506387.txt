Item(by='almog', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608646176, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25502703, text='While not being a bad idea, I think that any advantage of this method and double&#x2F;triple locking your password manager (by an additional encryption layer) falls into the category of warm-fuzzy-feeling-of-security.<p>Also, while unlikely to pose a real threat, since this method literally breaks the 3rd rule introduced in the article (&quot;Have a significantly different password for each account&quot;) and not only that, it does so by _appending_ a constant string to all your passwords, it introduces the potential risk in case a vulnerability is found in the cipher that&#x27;s used to encrypt your passwords database.')
