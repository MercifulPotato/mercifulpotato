Item(by='ur-whale', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608132902, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25443420, text='Much like a real world contract between - say - you and a bank when they accept your deposit, a smart contract should contain code provisions to revert the funds back to their owner if a certain set of circumstances happens in the environment (i.e. in the blockchain).<p>For example there could be, as part of the contract, much like there are exceptional provisions in real world contract, a M-of-N sig allowing the funds to be reverted back to the sender if enough (M) people agree that it&#x27;s the correct thing to do.<p>This particular contract didn&#x27;t seem to have any such provisions, which made it very unsafe.<p>Current smart contracts aren&#x27;t very sophisticated yet, this is still the stone age for programmable money.<p>Things will improve, but in the meantime ... be safe out there.')
