For me it was a motivational thing, seeing my trends in activity, getting nudges, doing competitions with friends, seeing my Vo2max go up over-time, are all great, but unnecessary for my brother.<p>&gt; the ostensible ease of use just isn’t worth the hassle of wearing a watch. I’m ok just pulling out my phone.<p>I&#x27;d say there&#x27;s a mix-up here. The watch isn&#x27;t easy to use versus the phone, which is the primary reason that it allows freedom from a digital device that demands constant attention. Secondly, if you&#x27;re okay pulling out your phone, then you&#x27;re really saying you don&#x27;t have any wish to be free from your phone, so there&#x27;s an audience mismatch here.<p>The freedom from the phone first requires you to actually agree that you need it, for many of us that&#x27;s the case. If you do, the second part is being able to leave your phone at home when you go for a walk, go to the gym, go see friends, go clubbing etc. But for many that&#x27;s a step too far, without any alternative offering functionality like payments, music, maps, messages, calls. The watch offers that, but in a way that&#x27;s not super easy to use, but will get the job done. This is why the watch will never be a place where you scroll through social media for hours, or refresh your 5 news apps for the news junkies among us, but the easy-to-use phone, will be.')
