Item(by='Arcorann', descendants=None, kids=[24694373], score=None, time=1601946202, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24693548, text='The 1&#x2F;8 dollar was a feature of the old Spanish dollar (hence the term &quot;pieces of eight&quot;, the eight pieces being known as &quot;bits&quot; -- &quot;two bits&quot; is then a quarter), which is where the Americans got it in the first place. In fact, up to 2001 American stock exchanges expressed prices in 1&#x2F;8 and 1&#x2F;16 dollar increments (apparently 1&#x2F;16 was only introduced at the NYSE in 1997).')
