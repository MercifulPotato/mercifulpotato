The dominant view on &quot;intelligence&quot; these days is something along these lines.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.simplypsychology.org&#x2F;multiple-intelligences.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.simplypsychology.org&#x2F;multiple-intelligences.html</a><p>Those eight categories are perhaps a bit insufficient and arbitrary, but surely the overall notion rings true -- we have all known &quot;smart&quot; people who excelled in some of those areas while failing at others.<p>Sifting through the available information (and disinformation) and reaching a rational conclusion on something like COVID involves multiple axes of intelligence.<p>It requires a (somewhat rare? apparently?) combination of a base level of scientific literacy, <i>and</i> perhaps some interpersonal skills because sifting through the misinformation requires an understanding of various parties&#x27; motivations. As well as perhaps a few others.')
