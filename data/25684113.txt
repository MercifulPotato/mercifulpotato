Item(by='DarkWiiPlayer', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610110087, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25682865, text='Correct me if I&#x27;m saying something stupid, but:<p>Shouldn&#x27;t TCE be trivial with callee cleanup?<p>When I (the function) get called, I get X bytes of stack in arguments, then start pushing variables and whatnot. When I return, I simply put my return value at the start of my stack space, pop everything else, then jump back.<p>In a tail call, all I need to do is put the next functions arguments at the start of my stack space, pop everything else, then jump to its address.<p>Obviously in IRL CPUs some of this data would be kept in registers instead of the stack, but the mechanism should be the same.')
