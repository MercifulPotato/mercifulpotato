Item(by='Retric', descendants=None, kids=[25369136], score=None, time=1607567205, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25368696, text='That’s a slightly different argument, if you accept that the courts are final arbiters then clearly they get to determine what’s allowed not the legislature.<p>It’s often argued by public officials that exceptions are allowed in extreme situations, though what those are is of course up to the courts.  Perhaps best summed up as &quot;The Constitution is not a suicide pact&quot;. <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;The_Constitution_is_not_a_suicide_pact" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;The_Constitution_is_not_a_suic...</a>  Which of course isn’t a blank check to do anything for any reason, but it is considered by the court system in various cases.  There are many examples of this argument being considered acceptable, and perhaps more frequent examples of it being rejected.<p>As such simply saying the legislature was bypassed <i>on it’s own</i> is not considered a sufficient legal argument.')
