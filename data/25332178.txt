Item(by='DonHopkins', descendants=None, kids=[25335970, 25332345], score=None, time=1607350013, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25326552, text='Back in the 80&#x27;s, I learned Pascal, and learned about its dynamically allocated records, then I went on to learn C, and got used to its pointers and arrays.<p>Then I went back to Pascal, and designed a program in my head with some dynamically allocated linked list data structures, and another data structure that had a member that pointed to the head of the linked list.<p>Then I started typing in the Pascal code, and hit a wall, because Pascal has ^ which is like C&#x27;s * operator to dereference a pointer, but doesn&#x27;t have anything like C&#x27;s &amp; operator to make a pointer to an arbitrary field in memory, so you can&#x27;t actually make a pointer to anything except the beginning of a record that you dynamically allocated!<p>That was when I gave up on Pascal.<p>Programming Pascal is like riding a bicycle with only one leg.')
