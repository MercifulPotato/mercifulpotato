Item(by='_rs', descendants=None, kids=[25356734], score=None, time=1607490329, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25349961, text='Ah ok - sounds like a double NAT then, not a bridge.<p>Edit: to go into more detail, their router is acting as a NAT for your public IP, giving you your first subnet, and then your router is getting a single IP on that subnet and creating a NAT where your devices all get IPs. In a bridge there would only be 1 IP space behind a single NAT. In your case with a double NAT a lot of consumer things might not work (like UPnP) and port forwarding would require you to add rules to both routers.')
