Show me your tables, and I won’t usually need your flowcharts; they’ll be obvious. -- Fred Brooks, The Mythical Man Month (1975)<p>I don&#x27;t think they&#x27;re inherently contradictory. You can have plain data objects representing the domain, and functions that act on these objects representing the behaviors&#x2F;actions in the domain. You could include these functions as part of the &quot;class&quot; for these objects, and have them return new instances of the class to maintain immutability.')
