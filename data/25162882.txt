Item(by='omgwtfbyobbq', descendants=None, kids=[25163405], score=None, time=1605894435, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25161738, text='I feel like large multi-year construction costs have a tendency to increase significantly (outside of the normal increases in year-of-expenditure costs), and I wonder if this is because construction firms know something needs to be built and will behave accordingly.<p>This is in contrast to smaller projects, especially during recessions, where construction firms may be more likely to need the work (lower bids) and also don&#x27;t have the expectation that the project will have to be completed (they can wait it out to extract more money from a contract).')
