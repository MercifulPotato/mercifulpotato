Item(by='thesteamboat', descendants=None, kids=[25974688], score=None, time=1612054633, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25972929, text='From a previous discussion of this paper I took away the following clarifying insight:<p>The Drake equation is usually turned into the Fermi paradox via some estimations that show the average (mean) number of alien civilizations we expect to see is large (&gt;1).<p>However, just because the mean is large does not imply that seeing 0 is low probability.<p>More concretely, suppose there were 10 possible &#x27;parallel&#x27; universes. In 9 of them humanity is alone and in the tenth there are tens of millions of alien civilizations. The probability of appearing to be alone is 90% even though the  number of alien civilizations we&#x27;d expect to see is in the millions.')
