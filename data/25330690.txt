Item(by='bmitc', descendants=None, kids=[25330995, 25330853], score=None, time=1607334405, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25322741, text='Newton was a nut, in nearly every positive and negative, sense of the word.<p>I highly recommend James Gleick&#x27;s <i>Isaac Newton</i>: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.amazon.com&#x2F;Isaac-Newton-James-Gleick&#x2F;dp&#x2F;1400032954" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.amazon.com&#x2F;Isaac-Newton-James-Gleick&#x2F;dp&#x2F;14000329...</a><p>It is an engrossing and not long read on Newton. It covers a fair amount of his heretical thoughts and feuds with people and paints a rather complete picture of the man. It&#x27;s basically common knowledge now, but Newton&#x27;s writings on alchemy and religion are in vast excess of his writings on physics and mathematics. It seems that intriguing questions that he couldn&#x27;t find answers for almost made him rabid, in a sense.')
