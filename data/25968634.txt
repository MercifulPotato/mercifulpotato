Item(by='simonh', descendants=None, kids=[25968854], score=None, time=1612010851, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25968488, text='I think appeal to authority gets shaky when you appeal to an authority who&#x27;s domain of expertise, or specific knowledge and experience might not extend to the issue at hand. It&#x27;s a matter of trusting the process of scientific inquiry, challenge and verification, not just the person.<p>So for example I defer to Roger Penrose on anything to do with mathematics, and will absolutely he has earned a voice on astrophysics#, but quantum consciousness microtubules? Nobody is an authority on that, so who knows?<p># And to be clear part of his credibility there is in making it clear what he believes with a high degree of certainty and what is more speculative.')
