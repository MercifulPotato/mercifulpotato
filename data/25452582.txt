Item(by='tialaramex', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608180498, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25451234, text='The thing about a &quot;brownout&quot; is that service goes away, and then it comes back - for a while.<p>The idea is that even though you wrote a blog post, maybe sent out formal notices to users, even in some cases put up billboards or did PSAs on TV - some people won&#x27;t know it applies to them, or may even mistakenly believe it doesn&#x27;t apply even though it does.<p>The brownout is an opportunity for those people to notice. &quot;Hey, why doesn&#x27;t the Foozle work?&quot; &quot;I dunno. Ask on Reddit?&quot; &quot;Huh. Reddit says we need to use tokens, but I&#x27;m sure we... oh... did you actually update the Foozle script?&quot; &quot;I thought you were doing that&quot; &quot;D&#x27;oh&quot;.<p>Users who can&#x27;t react immediately when things go bang will get a reprieve - for a while - when it starts working again the next day.<p>So that&#x27;s why &quot;blockout&quot; isn&#x27;t an appropriate description.<p>There&#x27;s some degree of art and some science to planning these so that they&#x27;re disruptive enough that humans notice something went wrong, while not being disruptive enough that they&#x27;re worse than just a one shot change.')
