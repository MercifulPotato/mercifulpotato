Item(by='thristian', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606724350, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25251117, text='It&#x27;s absolutely still possible, but note that screen resolution has increased much faster than CPU speed - if you want software-rendered 320x240 like Quake II, or even 1024x768, you&#x27;ll probably be OK, but if you want to go to full HD even modern CPUs will struggle, and 4K or higher is right out.<p>A common solution is to make a low-resolution texture (like 800x480), draw onto it with a software renderer, then use hardware rendering to scale it up to full-screen. You can even add filtering like faux scanlines or a convex CRT effect.')
