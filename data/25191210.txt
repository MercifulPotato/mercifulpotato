Item(by='rmason', descendants=None, kids=[25192633, 25192249, 25192715], score=None, time=1606162601, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25190298, text='I think a lot of this is false economy.  If you like doing mechanical work then make that your line of work.<p>Comparing old to new:<p>1. New tractors get much better fuel mileage<p>2. New tractors have technology that can go a long way to making the payments on them<p>3. New tractors have a lot more operator comfort and if you&#x27;re putting in eighteen hour days that&#x27;s important.<p>Lets say you&#x27;re a writer.  Why not keep an IBM XT around to do your work?  Granted finding those 5.25 inch floppies are a chore;&lt;). But that XT can still write and print your work.  You can even use Microsoft Word version 1.<p>The smartest play that I&#x27;ve seen farmers use is to buy gently used equipment.  A three year old tractor has depreciated 30-40% and as long as you&#x27;re able to make repairs is a steal over new.  You get most of the benefits of new at a lower price.')
