Item(by='lelandbatey', descendants=None, kids=[25280997, 25280520], score=None, time=1606937016, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25279264, text='Indeed, the ability to very accurately infer lots from very small amount of data is something I&#x27;ve long though of as the &quot;Sherlock Holmes&quot; problem. If there&#x27;s just a few people with the ability to deduce lots of things, then they can be amusing and offer some limited utility, like Sherlock Holmes.<p>Computers are powerful enough though, and people clever enough, that everyone can now have a Sherlock Holmes in their pocket. And large corporations can have smarts far beyond Sherlock Holmes. So what once was a &quot;eh, there&#x27;s no rule against it, if you have the information the smart guy should be able to use it&quot;, is now a case of &quot;oh dang, hmmm maybe civilization is built on the idea that not everyone is Sherlock Holmes.&quot;<p>Maybe this should have been my own blog post somewhere, but anyway, it&#x27;s one of the many new conundrums of our time.')
