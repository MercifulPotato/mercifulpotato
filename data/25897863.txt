In the area I grew up in, each suburban village&#x2F;town had its own school district and at least one building each for elementary, middle, and high school, and we were right on the edge of rural (the house I grew up in, within one of the aforementioned suburban school districts, was literally a 150-year-old farmhouse, surrounded by farm fields).<p>It&#x27;s entirely possible—though quite unusual—that the specific suburbs you moved to had no schools to call their own, but it&#x27;s absolutely untrue that &quot;there&#x27;s no schools in the suburbs&quot;.')
