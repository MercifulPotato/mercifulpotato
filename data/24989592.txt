Item(by='onetom', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604501963, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24970130, text='When I was around 7 years old, our family was manufacturing ZX81, ZX Spectrum and Enterprise foil-keyboards, because the factory ones were just shit and died within a year or two.<p>they were all the same design. 3 layers of plastic foil. 1 for horizontal wires, 1 for vertical wires to create a matrix and a 3rd sheet of foil between them with holes, so the horizontal and vertical wires can contact when pressed.<p>before that, when I was around 6 years old, I remember my father was sticking round aluminum foil stickers on his finger and type directly on a keyboard made out of a PCB with a key matrix.<p>The keys were the shape of 2 combs facing each other and you had to short them by touching them with the alu stickers on his finger.<p>It was an AirComp II computer, iirc. I can&#x27;t find any references to it online now...')
