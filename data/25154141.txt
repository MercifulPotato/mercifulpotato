Item(by='vorpalhex', descendants=None, kids=[25154718, 25154611, 25154695, 25154276], score=None, time=1605818778, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25153716, text='I&#x27;m really conflicted over this article because it makes some really good points but is missing the forest through the trees.<p>Information overload is very real, and people are very prone to making mistakes when digesting information from social media.<p>The answer isn&#x27;t to kill social media or force users to solve CAPTCHAS before posting. It&#x27;s to improve those critical rationality skills and the ability to analyze information.<p>Social media algorithms are harmful, but they&#x27;re ultimately dumb likelihood solutions that do naive grouping - it just turns out that more partisan&#x2F;outrageous content tends to be more exciting and elicit more key value actions.<p>The fix isn&#x27;t to try and play legal games with social media providers, but to teach users how the mousetrap works and what are the levers they can control (and the ones they can&#x27;t).')
