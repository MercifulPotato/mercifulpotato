Item(by='gpderetta', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610475036, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25729866, text='Store sinking for example, which is unsafe unless the loop is guaranteed to terminate.<p>C does not have this guarantee (at least not in all cases). Also rust is compiled with the llvm backend, so my understanding is that in practice rust assumes that loops terminate. See:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;rust-lang&#x2F;rust&#x2F;issues&#x2F;28728" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;rust-lang&#x2F;rust&#x2F;issues&#x2F;28728</a><p>There are llvm directives that can be added to prevent the optimization, but they are rejected by the rust maintainers exactly because they would cause performance regressions.')
