Item(by='ggm', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610178733, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25697736, text='I saw an air pressure analog computer in Leeds, in the early 1980s. Programs were stored on giant plug boards the size of a wall map, about six inches thick, and it looked amazing. They used it for modelling air-conditioning and other things in an architecture school, fluid dynamics stuff.<p>I&#x27;ve also seen the liquid model of the economy.<p>I have a copy of Svoboda on 3 bar linkage computing in the MIT radiation labs series, published in the post war period. All the examples look to be flight and ballistics related, I guess these kinds of things were used for bomb and gun aiming, and radar.')
