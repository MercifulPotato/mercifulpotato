Item(by='znpy', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603645015, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24886194, text='it took me about 10-15 minutes to turn my eyes towards SELinux, after some initial debugging.<p>i turned off selinux temporarily and activated the connection successfully, and determined that it was indeed SELinux that was preventing NetworkManager from doing its job.<p>then i re-enabled SELinux went to look at &#x2F;var&#x2F;log&#x2F;audit&#x2F;audit.log to see what it had to complain about and indeed some files created by NetworkManager in &#x2F;root&#x2F;.cert had bad contexts.<p>I set the proper contexts (semanage fcontext -a -t &lt;context&gt; &lt;pathregex&gt;), applied them (restorecon -Rv &#x2F;root) and all was well.<p>SELinux was initially scary but:<p>- The &quot;SELinux for mere mortals&quot; talks are very informative introductory video (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=_WOKRaM-HI4" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=_WOKRaM-HI4</a>)<p>- The SELinux User&#x27;s and Administrator&#x27;s Guide from Red Hat was a deeper explaination (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;access.redhat.com&#x2F;documentation&#x2F;en-us&#x2F;red_hat_enterprise_linux&#x2F;7&#x2F;html&#x2F;selinux_users_and_administrators_guide&#x2F;index" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;access.redhat.com&#x2F;documentation&#x2F;en-us&#x2F;red_hat_enterp...</a> -- linking to rhel 7 because that&#x27;s what i read at the time)<p>I had to study this stuff in order to get Red Hat certified (RHCSA, passed with 300&#x2F;300).<p>Getting certified is absolutely worth it. Getting certified is the difference between &quot;10-15 minutes to get a diagnosis&quot; and &quot;I gave up on SELinux about 20 years ago&quot;.')
