Clarke</i><p>That is precisely the premise of the &quot;science &amp; sorcery&quot; genre or hyper-sci-fi as I like to call it, where relatively primitive people regularly stumble upon ancient technology that they can&#x27;t hope to comprehend but use it for its incidental effects.<p>For example a smartphone may be good as a compact light source but not much else, or a hard drive for its shiny sharp discs and magnets.<p>See the Numenera RPG, set a billion (!) years into Earth&#x27;s future after several civilizations have risen and collapsed: <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;numenera.com" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;numenera.com</a>')
