Item(by='PaulDavisThe1st', descendants=None, kids=[25119054], score=None, time=1605572417, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25117961, text='Depends a lot on the instrument.<p>For a keyboard instrument, played conventionally, MIDI does a fairly good job of capturing everything that a composer could tell a performer (and vice-versa).<p>Slightly less true for percussion, but still somewhat true.<p>Rather untrue for any instrument where technique can be (ab)used to alter timbre significantly (e.g. most reeds, most strings)<p>Very untrue for any instrument capable of continuous pitch generation (e.g. unfretted strings).<p>Obviously &quot;arbitrary instructions&quot; are out, but then they are also not formally part of &quot;sheet music&quot;, but more &quot;additional written material from the composer&quot;, which can accompany MIDI in various ways too.')
