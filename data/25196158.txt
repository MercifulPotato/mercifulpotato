If you convolve â‰¡ integrate this Dirac delta function (located at some value x) against any function g(t), by construction the integral is zero everywhere except at t=x, so the result is an infinitely thin slice of g at x, exactly g(x) (the &#x27;sifting property,&#x27; <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;math.stackexchange.com&#x2F;questions&#x2F;1015498&#x2F;convolution-with-delta-function" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;math.stackexchange.com&#x2F;questions&#x2F;1015498&#x2F;convolution...</a>). \nNow imagine you begin to thicken&#x2F;fuzz the spike; now you begin to accumulate the behavior of g(t) not just exactly at x, but also at points nearby, getting a schmeared representation of g.
