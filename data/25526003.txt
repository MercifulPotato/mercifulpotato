Item(by='jacobolus', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608799864, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25525887, text='In pre-WWII USA, Irish, Italians, Poles, etc. were not considered part of the dominant &quot;white&quot; &quot;American&quot; group. Likewise Jews. (Not to mention Latinos, Chinese, Blacks, ...) A very significant proportion of the US military in WWII (maybe a third? I don&#x27;t know the precise numbers) came from socially marginalized minority groups.<p>The war substantially changed the society: Common experience in the armed forces, people moving around the country after being uprooted by the war, subsidized college via the GI bill, etc.<p>But even so, many people were scandalized at the idea of a Catholic president even in the 1960s.')
