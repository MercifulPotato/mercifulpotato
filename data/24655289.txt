Item(by='abeppu', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1601580931, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24649770, text='I think we&#x27;re also jaded now, in that all expectation that a photo is a totally truthful artifact produced by a physical process has long since been eroded. Phone camera software tries to create the &#x27;best&#x27; image. When the Bay Area had orange skies, everyone noted how their phone cameras were auto-balancing out the orange. Zoom touches up your face. Yesterday&#x27;s Pixel announcements emphasized changing lighting after a photo is taken. You kind of have to go out of your way to see a recent photo which _isn&#x27;t_ manipulated in some content-informed way.<p>So when we see an upscaled, colorized image ... maybe it&#x27;s not an accurate reflection of the past. But we&#x27;re no longer used to seeing an accurate reflection of the present, so of course we don&#x27;t balk at the plausible-but-perhaps-mistaken color choices or interpolations.')
