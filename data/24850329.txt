Item(by='bobthepanda', descendants=None, kids=[24850825], score=None, time=1603302906, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24850267, text='States are expressly empowered to choose how they run elections, although the current FPTP (at least for the House) is actually a law of Congress.<p>That being said, the major issue with relying on states to do this, is that the first states to do this will probably lose out. In a parallel universe with orange and green parties, if, say, the orange state decides to move to proportional electoral college but the green state does not, then the green party will benefit because they get both split votes from the yellow state and the total votes for the green state.')
