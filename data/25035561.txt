Item(by='bzbarsky', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604934763, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25034022, text='My reason is simple: I got an environment with the following features (as of the mid-2000s):<p>* Unix shell<p>* X<p>* Virtual desktop support<p>* Runs my editor of choice<p>* Runs on a laptop<p>* WiFi just works<p>* Audio just works<p>* Updates don&#x27;t break nearly as much (e.g. I don&#x27;t have to mess with X configuration after every major OS update).<p>I had pretty much stopped trying to do advanced configuration on Linux by that point, because every single major-version OS update would break nearly all of it.<p>There are some tradeoffs, sure, but the amount of time I no longer had to spend to keep up basic functionality was worth it to me.  Others may make other tradeoffs.<p>Note that this is for macOS.  I&#x27;m not sure how I feel about iOS yet.')
