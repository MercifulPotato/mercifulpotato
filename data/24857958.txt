Item(by='phil21', descendants=None, kids=[24858003], score=None, time=1603374584, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24857908, text='It started when vendor lock-in began, which was the late 90&#x27;s&#x2F;early 2000&#x27;s when dial-up died and broadband took over.<p>Before that the phone company was the one with the poor customer service, but nothing at this level that I recall.<p>Dial-up days were the golden years for the ISP business.  When every marginal NFL city had 30+ ISPs to choose from, and switching was simply changing a dial-in number you actually had decent competition.  You had any choice you could think of - want a high quality ISP catering to highly technical users?  Done.  Want an uber-cheap ISP catering to the masses?  Got you.<p>This all went away and service went greatly downhill once you had 1 or 2 options to choose from in the DSL&#x2F;cable&#x2F;fiber era.')
