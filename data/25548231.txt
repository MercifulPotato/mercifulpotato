Item(by='wtallis', descendants=None, kids=[25548388], score=None, time=1609035147, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25547703, text='You don&#x27;t need an electron microscope to discern large-scale repeated structures like CPU and GPU cores, or to tell the difference between random logic and highly regular memory arrays (usually cache, but sometimes you can see microcode or register files if you look at a small enough area). An electron microscope is only useful if you want to fully reverse-engineer the entire circuit schematic, but that&#x27;s overkill for basically any purpose except manufacturing your own clone.')
