Item(by='pjc50', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603728517, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24896796, text='The <i>only</i> distinction between people with legal status and those without is paperwork. Which may have errors in it.<p>A coworker of mine had to spend six months living in someone else&#x27;s flat and legally barred from working while he sued the Home Office; he&#x27;d entered legally, they found a paperwork error (making him &quot;illegal&quot;), and he won (making him &quot;legal&quot;) once more.<p>Brexit provides lots of examples of how people who entered and lived in the UK legally but never got (and indeed weren&#x27;t eligible for!) ILR suddenly can become &quot;illegal&quot; if they don&#x27;t get &quot;settled status&quot;.<p>Be very clear about this: if you immigrate to the UK by what you think is a legal route, and the Home Office makes a mistake, you can become an illegal immigrant very quickly.')
