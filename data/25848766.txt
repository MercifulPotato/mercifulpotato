Item(by='jolmg', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611161949, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25848167, text='&gt; He was also recently steered away from pardoning himself<p>Is pardoning oneself legally possible?<p>EDIT: Seems possible but never tested.<p>&gt; The Constitution provides little guidance on the issue of a potential presidential selfpardon. Only one sentence is dedicated to pardons: “The President . . . shall have Power to grant Reprieves and Pardons for Offences against the United States, except in Cases of Impeachment.” Looking to historical precedent and case law likewise provides no dispositive answer. No president has ever issued a self-pardon, and very few Supreme Court cases have addressed any aspect of the president’s pardon power.<p>-- <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;papers.ssrn.com&#x2F;sol3&#x2F;papers.cfm?abstract_id=3587921" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;papers.ssrn.com&#x2F;sol3&#x2F;papers.cfm?abstract_id=3587921</a>')
