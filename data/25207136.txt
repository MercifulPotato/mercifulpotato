Item(by='leobakerhytch', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606293520, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25202665, text='Except I think there’s convincing argument to make that engagement will go down over time, if the algorithm makes no attempt to prioritize or suggest novel content.<p>The rare occasions I discover a new channel, it’s almost always from some source other than the algorithm: a referral from a friend, this site, another YouTuber, etc. My viewership of the same repetitive roster of videos absolutely tails off until I find something new from elsewhere.<p>For example, in months of being subscribed to <i>my mechanics</i> [0] (who does incredibly engrossing and relaxing restorations of mechanical stuff), not once was I suggested a video from <i>Baumgartner Restoration</i> [1], an art conservator who produces videos with a similar attention to detail and high production value.<p>Thematically this should be an easy recommendation for YouTube to make, but evidently the content is just different enough that it scores as a false-negative. After finding the latter channel independently, my viewing time absolutely rose for a while.<p>In theory, YouTube ought to be able to detect and learn from this signal of non-algorithmic discovery of new content. Yet, here we are.<p>[0]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;c&#x2F;mymechanics" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;c&#x2F;mymechanics</a><p>[1]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;c&#x2F;BaumgartnerRestoration" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;c&#x2F;BaumgartnerRestoration</a>')
