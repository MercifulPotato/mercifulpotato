It unequivocally is the fail-whale of AWS regions. Every service there seems to have abnormally high usage numbers and absurd amount of scale to deal with. As James Hamilton would like to remind us, at such scale, even rare events are frequent [0]. The curse of being the default region, I suppose?<p>us-east-2 (Ohio) and eu-west-1 (Dublin) are my go-to regions. Prices are the same and most new services (and new features) are almost always ready to go on launch days.<p>[0] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;perspectives.mvdirona.com&#x2F;2017&#x2F;04&#x2F;at-scale-rare-events-arent-rare&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;perspectives.mvdirona.com&#x2F;2017&#x2F;04&#x2F;at-scale-rare-even...</a>')
