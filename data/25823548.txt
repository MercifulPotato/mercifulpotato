Item(by='StanislavPetrov', descendants=None, kids=[25823607], score=None, time=1610988168, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25823290, text='Perhaps you could being by asking yourself why a &quot;central authority&quot; is inherently more wise and capable of deciding what people are allowed to think and say than individuals themselves?  Does the ability to seize power and become the central authority denote inherent wisdom or morality?  History is littered with examples of societies where communications were overseen by a central authority, from Nazi Germany to the Soviet Union.  The freedom to think and speak freely is dangerous - like all freedom.  But before you reject the concept of freedom you ought to consider what the alternative is.')
