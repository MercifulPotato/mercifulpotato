As a web developer and designer I&#x27;m always looking to simplify my messages with illustrations. According to Simon Thorpe et al [1], it takes people an average of 150ms for a picture to be processed and 100ms more to understand its meaning. This means we can convey our message much easier with some kind of visual system.<p>The process of finding and using these illustrations frustrates me. I&#x27;m never sure if I&#x27;m infringing copyrights or if I have to add confusing attributions to the artist or platform... I&#x27;ve found several interesting libraries but .svg format (where I can change the illustrations to fit my needs) is often paid.<p>So - as a designer I have a bunch of illustrations sitting on my hard drive - I decided to make ilustra [2] this weekend to give them away for free and help solve this problem.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.researchgate.net&#x2F;publication&#x2F;14567385_Speed_of_Processing_in_the_Human_Visual_System" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.researchgate.net&#x2F;publication&#x2F;14567385_Speed_of_P...</a><p>[2] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;ilustra.io" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;ilustra.io</a>')
