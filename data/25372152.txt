Most coffee in the world is traded against the Coffee C future benchmark for instance: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.theice.com&#x2F;products&#x2F;15&#x2F;Coffee-C-Futures" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.theice.com&#x2F;products&#x2F;15&#x2F;Coffee-C-Futures</a><p>Giving producers and consumers a way to get a standardized contract at a given point ahead in time usually makes the price of the underlying commodity much more stable.<p>It has been argued that the way our society operates is based on the concept of futures contracts. It all began with the Dutch tulip futures in the 17th century that stabilized flower prices and made them a viable business: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Tulip_mania" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Tulip_mania</a>')
