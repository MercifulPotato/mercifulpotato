She uses plenty of sugar, along with a few spices.<p>I think it&#x27;s also, though, that we pick apples ourselves in local orchards, looking for good ripe ones and processing them within a week or three of picking.<p>That means we can pick varieties almost exclusively based on their flavor profile. We don&#x27;t have to worry about how well they survive in transit or sitting in grocery stores.<p>The big processing operators do have to worry about variables like that, as well as what types they can keep in supply year round for consistency of flavor.<p>I imagine that cuts down on the varieties they can realistically use.')
