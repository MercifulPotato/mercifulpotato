Item(by='aerosmile', descendants=None, kids=[25632774], score=None, time=1609721841, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25626673, text='My point is that we have to differentiate between bad employees and the overall effectiveness of ads.<p>The latter is all about incrementality. When you adjust for it, you get the true effectiveness of ads. In some instances, the incrementality will be significant, in others it won&#x27;t. It looks like that in Uber&#x27;s case, the incrementality was low. Btw, if you&#x27;re not familiar with this term, please look it up - there&#x27;s a ton of literature on it: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.adroll.com&#x2F;blog&#x2F;marketing-analytics&#x2F;beginners-guide-to-incrementality" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.adroll.com&#x2F;blog&#x2F;marketing-analytics&#x2F;beginners-gu...</a><p>Which brings me to the point about bad employees - marketers who are not adjusting their performance measurement based on incrementality should be fired (and it certainly looks like this was not being done at Uber at that time).')
