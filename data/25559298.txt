Item(by='matthewdgreen', descendants=None, kids=[25566113], score=None, time=1609164958, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25558576, text='That is indeed a good question, and the answer (like many chemicals) is “extremely toxic at high concentrations and nobody really knows the long-term effects at smaller ones because this would require enormous scientific investments that nobody will pay for.” But PFOA&#x2F;PFOS is different from other chemicals in that it’s extremely long-lived and so it doesn’t break down rapidly the way many other chemicals do. Putting something like this into the environment should demand the reverse of the evidentiary standard you’re describing: we should <i>know</i> exactly how safe it is before you dump it into the environment like DuPont did.')
