Item(by='ChrisMarshallNY', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605695556, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25133118, text='I managed a team for 25 years at a Japanese corporation. We had team members in many different timezones, and scheduling videoconferences was always a fraught process.<p>I write <i>a lot</i>. In fact “prolix” is not a bad word to use for my writing.<p>The biggest complaints I received were always about the lengths of my missives.<p>This was a legitimate complaint. It took a long time for people to translate my messages, and to translate the responses.<p>I learned to write emails with every sentence as a separate line, a clear abstract, and a clear conclusion&#x2F;question.<p>Bullets were important, as was careful wording. Fifty-cent words were OK, as long as they were accurate and relevant, but jargon was harmful.')
