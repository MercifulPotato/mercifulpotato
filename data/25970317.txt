Item(by='isbvhodnvemrwvn', descendants=None, kids=[25970648], score=None, time=1612023777, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25970258, text='From my understanding it&#x27;s mostly head-of-the-line blocking.<p>HTTP&#x2F;2 was introduced to solve head-of-the-line blocking on the application level (one HTTP&#x2F;1.1 connection could only handle one request at the time, barring poorly supported pipelining)<p>TCP itself also only allows one stream of data to flow at the time (in each direction) - in case of poor network quality you are going to lose some TCP segments, and if you fill in the receiver&#x27;s window, the transfer might halt until retransmission succeeds (this includes ALL streams within HTTP&#x2F;2). Using UDP allows you to circumvent that.')
