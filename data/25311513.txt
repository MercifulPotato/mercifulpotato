Item(by='ashtonkem', descendants=None, kids=[25311546], score=None, time=1607139634, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25311004, text='The big issue with the traditional car industry is how fragmented it is. OEMs, third party websites, and individual dealerships are not only on different tech stacks, they’re financially independent companies that by and large can’t be forced to work together. It’s not uncommon for OEMs to pay dealerships for data <i>through third party websites</i>, since they lack the ability to share certain types of data directly.<p>One of Tesla’s advantages is unquestionably their more centralized structure. They can make changes that other OEMs can’t.')
