However, it definitely provided my relatives a considerably better quality of life at a considerably cheaper cost than nearby apartments.<p>I know that after speaking to them about it, their only real regret was that there was no real equity in the home since they sell like vehicles -- depreciating assets. They bought it for $40k, sold it for $5k, and paid about $500&#x2F;mo in rent for the land.<p>They were forced to move when the land owner decided to get out of the mobile home tenancy business and sold their property to a developer.')
