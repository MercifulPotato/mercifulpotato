Item(by='smaddox', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611972388, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25964848, text='There&#x27;s also leverage&#x2F;margin (i.e. borrowing to buy stocks). With higher volatility, investors might be less willing to use leverage, resulting in less total demand, which results in lower prices.<p>When stocks drop, excess income and capitol drops, reducing demand in the wider market. Companies come under pressure to cut costs, and end up doing layoffs. This further lowers demand, resulting in a feedback loop.<p>This is all described by Minsky&#x27;s financial instability-hypothesis. And Steve Keen has built fully dynamic mathematical models that exhibit the process.')
