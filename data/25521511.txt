A public example of an apparent ZFS performance issue is <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;openzfs&#x2F;zfs&#x2F;issues&#x2F;9375" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;openzfs&#x2F;zfs&#x2F;issues&#x2F;9375</a>   Both are much more quirky than simpler filesystems like ext4.  Data integrity verification from checksumming makes it worth it though.<p>The ZFS vs. BTRFS choice, I think, depends more on whether you need specific features like offline deduplication or L2ARC &#x2F; SLOG cache devices.  And which one you&#x27;re more familiar with (can troubleshoot better).')
