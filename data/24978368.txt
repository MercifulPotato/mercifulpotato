My cheap as chips consumer laptop easily accumulates an uptime of a month or more before I decide a kernel update would be a good idea and I have to reboot. Also, the computers in use by professionals today _are_ specialized. As a web developer, I don&#x27;t need the expensive GPU(s) required by an AI researcher and thus my computer doesn&#x27;t have one. If I was doing video editing I might have a different storage solution than my current SSD-only setup, etc.<p>I think the downside of the demise of workstations is mainly a psychological one: we no longer have the ability to marvel at and dream about highly specialized computers offering a significantly different user experience than that of our ordinary home computers.
