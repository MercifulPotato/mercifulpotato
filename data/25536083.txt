Item(by='pdimitar', descendants=None, kids=[25536140, 25536520], score=None, time=1608899386, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25534766, text='I agree with the general sentiment but it has been brought to an extreme to the point that things in many dynamic languages are way too slow. Maybe some of the CS courses are to blame; people think &quot;hey, let me make my own language and an interpreter!&quot; -- and the rest is history.<p>IMO a chunk of the programming world is entering a mature stage where much more work is put in the runtime of a language on not on the language itself. End of the day, you are going to have a call from an executive asking why the hosting bills are ballooning. I&#x27;ve saved some of my customers literal [tens of] thousands of $$$ a month by migrating a critical chunk of their services from Ruby on Rails to Elixir&#x27;s Phoenix and, on one occasion, Rust&#x27;s Rocket.<p>Programmer ergonomy and happiness <i>are</i> important, don&#x27;t get me wrong. But IMO we are fixating way too much on them in some languages &#x2F; frameworks. Some pragmatism should be put back into the picture.')
