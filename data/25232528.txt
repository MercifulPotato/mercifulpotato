Item(by='colejohnson66', descendants=None, kids=[25233056], score=None, time=1606510830, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25232158, text='Not true. You actually can. Aliasing is about viewing the <i>same bits</i> as two different types (what `reinterpret_cast`, union-based type punning, and cast pointers do) `memcpy` <i>copies</i> the bits to a new location which doesn’t violate aliasing. I don’t have time to find the reference in the standard, but cppreference.com[0] says it’s ok:<p>&gt; Where strict aliasing prohibits examining the same memory as values of two different types, std::memcpy may be used to convert the values.<p>[0]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.cppreference.com&#x2F;w&#x2F;cpp&#x2F;string&#x2F;byte&#x2F;memcpy" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.cppreference.com&#x2F;w&#x2F;cpp&#x2F;string&#x2F;byte&#x2F;memcpy</a>')
