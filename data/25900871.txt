Item(by='jabl', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611563859, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25895589, text='Dual licensing requires copyright assignment, largely preventing a community of contributors forming around the software (nobody wants to provide free labor to somebody else&#x27;s get rich quick scheme), which is one of the big advantages of OSS in the first place.<p>In a way, dual licensing is essentially proprietary software, with a for free version that you hope will satisfy as few users as possible. It bit like the old shareware days.<p>Dual licensing also creates the perverse incentive of trying to scare your potential customers how horrible copyleft licensing is. So if you motivation is to increase the amount of FOSS through copyleft licensing, you&#x27;re shooting yourself in the foot.<p>See <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;sfconservancy.org&#x2F;blog&#x2F;2020&#x2F;jan&#x2F;06&#x2F;copyleft-equality&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;sfconservancy.org&#x2F;blog&#x2F;2020&#x2F;jan&#x2F;06&#x2F;copyleft-equality...</a>')
