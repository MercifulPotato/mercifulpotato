Simple exposure is one thing: most people aren&#x27;t going to <i>concoct</i> new conspiracy theories on their own, but they may decide to go in for them when they <i>see</i> them.<p>Social persuasion&#x2F;cohesion is another: we&#x27;re all influenced by the views of the people around us (like it or not). When you see half a dozen people in one of your peer groups talking about $WEIRD_THING, you&#x27;re naturally inclined to give it some time, and maybe some credence.<p>I don&#x27;t disagree about the deep problems with regulating it, though.')
