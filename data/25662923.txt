Item(by='atombender', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609969150, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25654750, text='I think you missed the real theme, which is death and resurrection, a recurring pattern throughout the book.<p>While Severian is groomed to be a torturer, for most of the book his job (and Severian repeatedly emphasizes that it&#x27;s just a job) is that of executioner, and he later holds the position of lictor for around a month. While Severian often recounts the procedures practiced in the guild tower, it&#x27;s not really torture that he is concerned about.<p>The themes of death and resurrection, however, are central to the plot, together with the water motif as a symbol of cleansing, from the moment Severian nearly drowns in the river (and is rescued by an undine) to the destruction of Urth.')
