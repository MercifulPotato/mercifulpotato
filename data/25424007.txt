Item(by='SpicyLemonZest', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607983407, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25422805, text='This Twitter thread is an overcorrection. I&#x27;ve seen people characterize Malthus&#x27;s position as &quot;Famine is an inevitable part of the natural order of things&quot; or &quot;The current global population is the maximum we can possibly support&quot;, and neither of those are true. But he definitely didn&#x27;t believe that &quot;there are no limits to resources or food production&quot;. He claimed that it was absolutely impossible to improve agricultural productivity more than linearly (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;books.google.com&#x2F;books?id=9-q_SSfE6skC&amp;pg=PA5#v=onepage&amp;q&amp;f=false" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;books.google.com&#x2F;books?id=9-q_SSfE6skC&amp;pg=PA5#v=onep...</a>, p.10), and questioned whether densely settled countries like Japan and China could ever achieve even twice their current productivity (p. 8).')
