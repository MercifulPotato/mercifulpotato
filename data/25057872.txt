However, sometimes I get tests for the logging messages &quot;for free&quot;. That happens when I break out some logic in its own function, and I want logging on what happened in that function (definitely not all the time, but is sometimes useful). Then the function can return its result, and a list of logging messages to output. When asserting the result, I can also assert on the logging messages.<p>It&#x27;s what I describe in &quot;Returning a message list&quot; in this post: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;henrikwarne.com&#x2F;2020&#x2F;07&#x2F;23&#x2F;good-logging&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;henrikwarne.com&#x2F;2020&#x2F;07&#x2F;23&#x2F;good-logging&#x2F;</a>')
