I can read 3-5 papers on a topic and the first chapters of each will give me enough to understand the gist of the topic, the status quo.<p>It&#x27;s a question of who you prioritize while writing a paper. Experts or the interested layman.<p>If you prioritize people who are already experts in the topic, you write little to no introduction, you get to the results quickly.<p>If you prioritize the &quot;curious, interested layman&quot; (or university students in their first years), a short introduction to a topic with references will provide enough information to understand the basics and the reader can continue reading the rest of the paper with enough context to understand why the topic is relevant.')
