A bit of googling drags up this account by a guy who seems to have actually read the paper:<p>&gt;In 1874 Dr. Syrski had announced the most recent solution. He had discovered a small lobed organ and described it as the testes of the eel. Carl Friedrich Claus, chief of the Institute for Comparative Anatomy in Vienna, assigned to his student Freud the task of checking Syrski&#x27;s observations. Freud dissected 400 eels, finding the Syrski organ in many of them. On microscopic examination he found the histological structure of the organ such that it could well be an immature form of the testes, though he found no definite evidence that this was the case.
