Item(by='blueblisters', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607027595, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25293898, text='&gt; If the upper management wanted to retract that paper then it seems reasonable to assume that preparing this paper did not produce any value to the company.<p>I don&#x27;t know about publishing standards at Google but, assuming they are uniform for all researchers, I am pretty sure Google would be unable to retain talent if researchers were asked to prove value to Google for every paper they publish. I think for researchers, this reinforces the oft-dismissed notion that you give up significant academic freedom in exchange for a fat paycheck when you join an industrial research lab.<p>This can be particularly damaging for Google because many of its research projects don&#x27;t have an immediate business impact, and it hires top researchers by offering an alternative to the slog of academia.')
