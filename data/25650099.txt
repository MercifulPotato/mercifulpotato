The waiting list was a year long through 2016, and in LA at least there was still a waiting list well into 2017. However, Nissan only ever made a few thousand Leafs a year and only made them available in select markets.<p><i>By comparison, the Model 3 went into production in 2017 and they built the 500,000th in March of 2020.</i><p>BMW, VW, Toyota, and Ford have all launched new models in significantly greater numbers over shorter timeframes and with fewer employees than Tesla. The Passat, for example, went from 50,000 cars made&#x2F;year (at the Chattanooga factory) to 250,000 cars made&#x2F;year in just over a year, with 1&#x2F;5 the employees of the Tesla line, minimal rework required...and here&#x27;s the real kicker: while making an <i>actual</i> profit per car under standard accounting practices (not Tesla&#x27;s imaginary profit per car that leaves out costs the other automakers include in unit economics).')
