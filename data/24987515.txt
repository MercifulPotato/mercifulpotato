Item(by='arethuza', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604481370, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24986506, text='Most modern weapons are fission-fusion-fission designs with the latter fission step using neutrons from the preceding fusion step. Most of the energy in most &quot;H-bomb&quot; designs comes from fission:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Thermonuclear_weapon" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Thermonuclear_weapon</a><p>A notable example where this wasn&#x27;t the case was the test of the Tsar Bomba where the final fission component was left out largely because of the enormous amount of fall-out this would create - reducing the yield from 100Mt to 50Mt. The resulting explosion did have a very high fusion component.')
