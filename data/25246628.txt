Item(by='sh1mmer', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606671938, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25242444, text='This is why writing scripts&#x2F;tools for ops work is helpful.<p>You can do `alias mv=&#x27;mv -n&#x27;` or similar but then you have to hope everyone is using the same shell prompt, etc.<p>Even if your tiny script is just:<p>```\n#!&#x2F;usr&#x2F;bin&#x2F;env bash\nmv -n recordings&#x2F;$1 public&#x2F;$1\n```<p>you’ve removed some of the human tendency towards occasional typos and written something move defensive.<p>In my experience as an SRE people set way to high a bar for what should be tooling or “automation”. As soon as you make something software then you can iterate on it, or not as it makes sense.<p>If you keep on typing one off commands then the humans need to be correct every time.')
