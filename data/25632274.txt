Item(by='lucideer', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609773208, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25630782, text='&gt; <i>For me, &quot;freedom&quot; doesn&#x27;t mean &quot;restricting what users can do.&quot;</i><p>&gt; <i>GPL restricts what users can do, and thus restricts freedom in my eyes.</i><p>A few sibling commenters have already rebutted, but to add to that, a way of looking at this is that all licences necessarily restrict users (with the possible&#x2F;arguable exception of things like CC0).<p>The difference with GPL vs MIT is that MIT <i>gives users the &quot;freedom&quot; to further restrict other users in future</i>, whereas GPL <i>restricts users from ever further restricting other users</i>')
