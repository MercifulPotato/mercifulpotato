Item(by='Stierlitz', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606506164, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25226464, text='This is a false premise. Human beings are a hierarchical animal and experience stress if uncertain of their position in the social order. Even in a professed leaderless organisation, the group will invariably form into an informal hierarchy, with those possessing more ‘social credit’ occupying positions of power. The rest spread out along the totem pole.<p>• ‘social credit’ being any of: good looks, intelligence, money, high-status relatives, a car ..')
