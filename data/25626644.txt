According to the Currency Act 1965 (section 16) coins are legal tender for payment of amounts which are limited as follows:<p>* not exceeding 20c if 1c and&#x2F;or 2c coins are offered (these coins have been withdrawn from circulation, but are still legal tender);<p>* not exceeding $5 if any combination of 5c, 10c, 20c and 50c coins are offered; and<p>* not exceeding 10 times the face value of the coin if $1 or $2 coins are offered.\n&quot;<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;banknotes.rba.gov.au&#x2F;legal&#x2F;legal-tender&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;banknotes.rba.gov.au&#x2F;legal&#x2F;legal-tender&#x2F;</a>')
