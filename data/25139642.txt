The signalling dynamic shifted, becoming both increasingly accessible, capable of defining increasingly finer social groups, <i>esspecially</i> those <i>not</i> associated with high-powwer or high-status groups (nobility, royalty, clergy, miltitary, artisans, merchants).<p>Fads-as-signalling shows up in many places if you look for it:  <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;old.reddit.com&#x2F;r&#x2F;dredmorbius&#x2F;comments&#x2F;62uroa&#x2F;clothing_music_diets_art_management_theory_fad_as&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;old.reddit.com&#x2F;r&#x2F;dredmorbius&#x2F;comments&#x2F;62uroa&#x2F;clothin...</a><p>Information is ... somewhat trickier.  Clothing is <i>manifest</i> and <i>overt</i>.  Information ... mostly isn&#x27;t.  (Memes, logos, jingles, musical hooks, and slogans being prime exceptions.)  Possession of specific information becomes a credible (expensive to obtain, hard to falsify) indicator of strong group identity --- whether you&#x27;re talking about membership within a religious sect, programming subspecialty, profession, banking cabal, political party, or neihbourhood gang.
