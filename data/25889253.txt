Item(by='icegreentea2', descendants=None, kids=[25890247], score=None, time=1611462576, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25889136, text='The default backend of pip is pypi. Pypi does not have a policy of removing previous releases (though package authors do have the ability to do this I believe). There are packages going back a really long time. You can find releases for Python 2.6 (EOL 2013) that were published in 2012 still on pypi (and accessible by pip).<p>In addition, paid extended support for Python 2.7 already exists from 3rd party vendors (I know of ActivePython) - Python 2 EOL just means you don&#x27;t get free updates and supports.')
