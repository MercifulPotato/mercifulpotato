Item(by='madamelic', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610733003, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25793597, text='Finding the person who can make hiring decisions or at least can advocate for you is so much easier than having to play the lottery of submitting a job application blindly.<p>It lets you present your value, show you can communicate well and that you really want to work there.<p>If you just drop a generic cover letter, that&#x27;s low-effort. If you spend the 10 - 15 minutes to write a good, succinct message on how you think you can help and that you are interested in the company, that&#x27;s huge in my opinion.<p>I think I have done it in total about 5 times and gotten hired twice from it which isn&#x27;t a bad success rate!<p>---<p>With all of that said, there is a certain size where this strategy stops being viable but I am not sure where.<p>As a company gets bigger, it would definitely become less likely to work in my opinion but inversely, I think the smaller they are, the better it will work.')
