Item(by='pmeunier', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608978074, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25541118, text='Applying a patch in Darcs usued to be in O(2^n), and now apparently O(n^2), where n is the size of history.<p>Applying a patch in Pijul is in O(p c log n), where p is the size of the patch and c the size of the largest &quot;deletion-insertion conflict&quot; p is involved in, where a &quot;deletion-insertion conflict&quot; is a situation where Alice deletes a block of text while Bob adds stuff in that same block.<p>Note that this is a rough bound, since all non-conflicting operations in a patch are in O(log n), except those involved in a &quot;deletion-insertion conflict&quot;, which are  in O(c log n).<p>So, Pijul is in fact faster than Git for merging (and rebasing). The only tradeoff at the moment is that going arbitrarily far back in history isn&#x27;t as fast as it could be (this will be fixed very soon).')
