I’m hard pressed to think of an exception to this conviction, not just on Apple’s App Store, but in any sphere of life — whether a harmless game...<p>There is a card game called &quot;Mao&quot; in which one of the rules is that you&#x27;re not supposed to explain the rules.  The base game is similar to Uno or Crazy 8s, and then, when you win a game, you get to define a new rule, which you name but don&#x27;t otherwise explain, and then start enforcing that rule (giving penalty cards when others break it).  Others are expected to guess the rule by observing its application.  <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Mao_(card_game)" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Mao_(card_game)</a>')
