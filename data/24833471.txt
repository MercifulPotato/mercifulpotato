Item(by='ohazi', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603161850, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24833174, text='Hashlife can theoretically &quot;cache&quot; a specific input and output, e.g. running an instruction on your simulated computer like &quot;add r0, r1, r2&quot; (when r0, r1, and r2 contain specific values that have already been used with this instruction, and assuming that any nearby internal state is also identical).<p>But this doesn&#x27;t let you magically mine bitcoin in fast-forward, because in order to mine bitcoin, you need to insert random bytes into your block and keep changing them until you get lucky and find a hash that starts with a certain number of zeroes. This effectively guarantees that you aren&#x27;t going to have cached computation snippets that are big enough to skip any meaningful amount of work.<p>At best, you might be able to fast-forward at the instruction level granularity, at which point you might as well be mining on your CPU.')
