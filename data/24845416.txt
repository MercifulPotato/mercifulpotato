Item(by='danielbarla', descendants=None, kids=[24845477, 24845440], score=None, time=1603263869, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24845376, text='To be more clear, the statement should have been that on average, they don&#x27;t.  As far as I am aware, this _is_ true, although quite surprising, and not a popular idea among investors.  The second part of the argument is interesting.  On the one hand, a la Warren Buffet&#x27;s analogy of thousands of professional coin flippers, it is obvious that a small number will do great, while the average will do average (or slightly worse, because of fees).  This does not imply that there are _zero_ people who can consistently and on average outsmart the markets, only that it seems to take more than the median full time, relatively highly trained professional to do so.')
