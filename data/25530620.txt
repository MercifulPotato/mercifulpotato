Okami.\nI&#x27;ve always been slightly frustrated by video games. Here we have the opportunity to create truly fantastical worlds with the only limits being what we can imagine and <i>so much</i> effort goes in to creating WW2 shooters or football games or whatever. When the first trailer was revealed for Okami at e3 in 2005, wow. That fake celestial language starts up, showing flowers blooming in the path of the sun god as she runs and then, at the press of a button, <i>the whole screen turns into parchment and you paint on it with a celestial brush to instantiate your nature god powers</i>. I almost cried. I tried not to get my hopes up too much because I thought there was no way for it to live up to my imagination. And then it did. Easily the greatest video game experience I have ever had. Windwaker and Hollowknight are close seconds.<p>2. Worm. Heavily hyped in my friend circles, I waited for it to finish.
