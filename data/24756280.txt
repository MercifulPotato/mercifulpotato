Item(by='jonahx', descendants=None, kids=[24756824], score=None, time=1602520722, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24756033, text='&gt; If petitioning your employer to fire you for something you said works, maybe you said something that your employer would fire you over?<p>Possibly, but there is another possibility which also occurs with alarming frequency:<p>The employer, feeling bullied and afraid of bad press, demonstrations, etc, makes a calculated risk vs reward decision: Is keeping this employee worth the (possibly existential) risk to my business?<p>That is, the employer may personally have zero problem with what you said, but fire you anyway because &quot;it&#x27;s just not worth it.&quot;  The decision to fire is the result of both cowardice by the employer and coercion by the petitioners, even if that coercion is not an explicit threat.')
