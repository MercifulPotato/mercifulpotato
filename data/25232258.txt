Item(by='qayxc', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606508588, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25231547, text='&gt; Remember: you can go any speed you can accelerate to, it is everything else that can&#x27;t go faster than light.<p>You cannot accelerate masses to the speed of light, because if General Relativity holds, you&#x27;d require infinite energy to do so.<p>In addition, every bit of radiation arriving from the direction of travel will be turned into high energy gamma radiation and cook you something fierce real quick; not to mention dust or tiny rocks that would release the energy equivalent of a thermonuclear explosion upon impact.<p>Since these effects alone will create a practical upper limit to your interstellar travel speed, there&#x27;s still a huge difference between FTL and reactionless drives. An FTL drive might get you to Proxima Centauri in a month, whereas even the most optimistic subluminal option will take at least a decade.')
