Item(by='zepearl', descendants=None, kids=[25015115], score=None, time=1604752385, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25014224, text='I remember when the big company I was working for started using T1s for databases (I guess caused by Oracle marketing &amp; change of licensing model) and asked us &quot;consumers&quot; to start using those systems.<p>I was skeptical so I downloaded some SPECint benchmark results ( <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.spec.org&#x2F;benchmarks.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.spec.org&#x2F;benchmarks.html</a> ) of T1s, Power and Xeon, compared them and thought &quot;mh, probably a bad idea&quot; and then I ended up having to invest quite some time to convince my management to keep using &quot;normal&quot; servers.<p>On the other hand, later, I had a bit of fun hearing stories from other colleagues telling me how slow those T1-machines were once they started running on them their normal DB-workloads =&gt; after months of everybody complaining about bad performance, everybody went back to normal servers =&gt; a lot of time &amp; money (&amp; nerves) spent for nothing.<p>Normal IT politics, I guess.')
