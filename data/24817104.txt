Item(by='ThePadawan', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603020949, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24813745, text='I used to work at an online shop that among other things, sold RAM.<p>Usually, any product sold comes with 2- or 5-year warranty if your lucky.<p>Some RAM manufacturers were so proud of their manufacturing quality, they instead offered 50-year warranties.<p>Now, the reasonable thing to do as a seller would have been to just limit that to something like 5 years, and then give the customer their money back if they had any issues after that.<p>However, someone actually programmed the system to use 50 years as the warranty duration, way in the 2040s.<p>I didn&#x27;t look further into the technical issues behind that (probably not many, since the required precision wasn&#x27;t seconds, just days), but that remains a great example of when not building a feature costs less than building it.')
