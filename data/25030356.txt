Item(by='leptons', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604881688, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25028486, text='In the US around the late 80&#x27;s we could buy a DTMF dialer from Radio Shack and change the oscillator crystal to a different value that would enable the device to produce the same tones as the $0.25 quarter sound that a payphone would make when you dropped a coin into it. That was a lot of phun.<p>&quot;war dialing&quot; was using a computer to repeatedly dial random passcodes on a PBX or similar system to try to make free calls. A commodore 64 was used quite a bit for this, dialing all night long you&#x27;d wake up to about 5 or 6 valid codes that you could then use for about a week to dial BBSs all over the planet for free.<p>There were plenty of other ways to get free phone calls. It was a really interesting part of the &#x27;underground&#x27; of computing back in the day.')
