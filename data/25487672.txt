Don&#x27;t forget that in many local governments gave monopoly grants to select ISPs. Then there&#x27;s the &quot;bootleggers and baptists&quot; [1] effect, where well-meaning regulation has a larger anti-competitive effect benefiting extant firms, e.g., requiring would-be ISPs to also serve cost-prohibitive rural areas.<p>Pretending that extant government regulation is the never the problem is just as bad as pretending it&#x27;s the only problem.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Bootleggers_and_Baptists" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Bootleggers_and_Baptists</a>')
