Item(by='phobosanomaly', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604616307, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24988981, text='Maybe agent-based modeling could be used to gain some interesting insights in the way you&#x27;re describing?<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Agent-based_model" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Agent-based_model</a><p>&quot;ABMs do not assume the economy can achieve equilibrium and &quot;representative agents&quot; are replaced by agents with diverse, dynamic, and interdependent behavior including herding. ABMs take a &quot;bottom-up&quot; approach and can generate extremely complex and volatile simulated economies. ABMs can represent unstable systems with crashes and booms that develop out of non-linear (disproportionate) responses to proportionally small changes.&quot;<p>We do see that to some extent in the United States, since the states do have a fair amount of leeway in making a lot of laws. Granted, nothing like what you&#x27;re talking about.<p>I can buy an AK-47 with the capacity to accept detachable magazines in Phoenix, but not in Los Angeles.<p>In Oregon, a minor can get an abortion without a parent present. In Texas, it can&#x27;t happen without a parent present.<p>In California I can walk into a store and buy an ounce of Nothern Lights cannabis, but in Alabama, everyone working in the dispensary would be put in prison on felony charges.<p>In Nevada I can legally visit a prostitute. In Arizona I cannot.<p>In California I can ride a motorcycle in-between lanes of traffic. In New York I cannot.')
