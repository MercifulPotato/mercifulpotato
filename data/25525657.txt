Underwriting based on health conditions (something that is currently banned in the US for health insurance, but allowed for life&#x2F;disability insurance) is all about the cost&#x2F;benefit of searching for data. If a lot of people have data with Google, it might make sense to pay to look at it.<p>Under US law at least, your health data isn&#x27;t inaccessible to companies. It just requires your consent for release. That consent is basically baked into the process under a checkbox for the terms of whatever you&#x27;re trying to buy.<p>That said, this kind of data is probably uninteresting. Diagnoses &amp; prescription drug fills are probably more indicative (and folks like <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.rxhistories.com&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.rxhistories.com&#x2F;</a> helpfully aggregate and standardize the data).')
