Item(by='intended', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609980358, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25665012, text='Modern conspiracy has nothing to do with facts, but entirely to do with the shape, rate and self forming nature of modern human communication networks.<p>I&#x27;ll argue, strongly, that right now - everything regarding Social Media misinformation is an epiphenomena of those factors.<p>To illustrate -<p>Misinformation based communication travels differently than &quot;Fact&quot; based information <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.pnas.org&#x2F;content&#x2F;113&#x2F;3&#x2F;554.full" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.pnas.org&#x2F;content&#x2F;113&#x2F;3&#x2F;554.full</a>.<p>People assign truth value based on heuristics, one of which is how many times information is repeated. If you self select a network of people who believe the same things and repeat it, with slight modifications - you can ensure people will believe conspriacies.<p>With the ability to self select nodes&#x2F;people who tend to be conspiracy inclined you will easily cross the number of people needed to make an idea seem socially valid.<p>The pace and size of our information networks naturally supports the evolution of highly viral content - not truthful content.')
