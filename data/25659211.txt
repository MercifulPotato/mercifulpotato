Item(by='jjk166', descendants=None, kids=[25678719], score=None, time=1609950783, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25658302, text='They&#x27;re similar but distinct concepts. Negligible senescence means after a point aging doesn&#x27;t negatively affect the organism. They don&#x27;t lose reproductive capability or experience functional decline. Imagine being 20 forever.<p>Biological immortality means after some point mortality rates don&#x27;t increase with age - you can still grow old, it just won&#x27;t directly kill you. Biologically immortal organisms don&#x27;t have a max lifespan, if they&#x27;re lucky they can just keep on going, but they may be dramatically more feeble than their younger counterparts.')
