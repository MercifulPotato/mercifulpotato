I hate this new supposed term &quot;minimoon&quot; -- it&#x27;s sensationalistic and misleading.<p>This object is 1 to 2 meters in diameter, was captured by Earth for just 2.7 years, and with an extremely irregular orbit.<p>By contrast, a moon requires a &quot;relatively stable orbit&quot; [1], which this clearly did <i>not</i> have.<p>Can we just call it what it actually is? It&#x27;s an <i>asteroid</i>. It revolves around the Sun, and it was very briefly a <i>temporary</i> satellite of the Earth.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Claimed_moons_of_Earth" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Claimed_moons_of_Earth</a>')
