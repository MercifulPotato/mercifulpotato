Item(by='fulafel', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606043255, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25172454, text='The idea in ECCs [1] is that you store some ancillary data that can be used to rectify a bounded amount of error introduced in the stored data. A straightforward virtual memory system implementation would be to have this information stored alongisde page tables and the OS would go through the pages and update their ECCs periodically (probably at a limited rate so as not to cause load spikes). The devil in the details would be the bookkeeping about when a page contents has been written to and the ECC is invalid (soft page faults?).<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Error_correction_code" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Error_correction_code</a>')
