My ideal &quot;let&#x27;s talk about names&quot; article would cover a lot more different societies and be a bit more universal in its outlook.  There&#x27;s a world of fun stuff with names in Arabic or Japanese, and how various states try to control what people name their kids.<p>(Saying this, I understand it&#x27;s a bit shallow to react primarily to an article&#x27;s title).<p>&gt;Moreover, at birth human infants are unable to appropriate names for themselves, consequently, another older person must confer a birth name.<p>There are groups that don&#x27;t name babies at or near birth (e.g. <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.scientificamerican.com&#x2F;article&#x2F;parents-in-a-remote-amazon-village-barely-talk-to-their-babies-mdash-and-the-kids-are-fine&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.scientificamerican.com&#x2F;article&#x2F;parents-in-a-remo...</a> leave it for a year, and I can&#x27;t find a citation but I think I recall some other groups pushing naming even further).<p>&gt;the nomen, which was a hereditary name (or surname) identifying a person as a member of a distinct gens, i.e., family, tribe, or clan, which constituted an extended Roman family, all members of which shared the same nomen and claimed descent from a common ancestor.<p>As an aside, if a roman slave was freed, they (at least in some periods) took the praenomen and nomen of their former master. (cf <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;latininscriptions.ashmus.ox.ac.uk&#x2F;resources&#x2F;resources&#x2F;AshLI-RomanNamesWORKSHEET3-FreedmenandFreedwomen.pdf" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;latininscriptions.ashmus.ox.ac.uk&#x2F;resources&#x2F;resource...</a> .  this article also has some details: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;carolashby.com&#x2F;roman-names&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;carolashby.com&#x2F;roman-names&#x2F;</a>).<p>Reading the article makes me really want someone to write a &quot;Naming Systems Very Different from Ours&quot; series of articles along the lines of the classic &quot;Legal Systems Very Different from Ours&quot; [ <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.daviddfriedman.com&#x2F;Legal%20Systems&#x2F;LegalSystemsContents.htm" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.daviddfriedman.com&#x2F;Legal%20Systems&#x2F;LegalSystemsCo...</a> ].')
