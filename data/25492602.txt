Item(by='millstone', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608532297, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25492321, text='This is an interesting point. Walking backwards through a UTF8 byte stream is easy: just look for the next non-continuation byte, and Rust really can do this, though the API is buried under `str.chars().rev()`.<p>C++ is structured around iterators that <i>point into</i>; this enables e.g. writing into the guts of a std::map but not the guts of a std::string if you care about Unicode, since the write might change the string&#x27;s byte length.')
