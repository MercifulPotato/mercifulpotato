You need to be a &quot;quick learner&quot; of the random sorts of skills you need to do your work.  As an actor you can outperform the person you become because your lines are written for you,  so you can put 100% of your effort into everything else.<p>I have a somewhat high IQ,  I&#x27;m good at hard math (I&#x27;m sure better than Sly Stallone) and abstract thought and my words&#x2F;minute rate of writing has been legendary at times,  but I struggle as an actor.<p>Stallone&#x27;s success had to do with the breakout success of his movie &quot;Rocky&quot;,  which he wrote.  That in turn had to do with convincing other people to take a chance on him and his script,  and that gets you into a whole interpersonal area where the role of IQ is really controversial.<p>If I wrote fiction I&#x27;d be dangerously tempted to be like<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Robert_L._Forward" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Robert_L._Forward</a><p>I am fascinated with how many &quot;Starship(s)&quot; full of ammonia it would take to fill the atmosphere of a small space colony,  what you do with the hydrogen and so forth.  Stallone is much better at relating to the average person than I am,  and that&#x27;s what makes him a &quot;genius&quot;.')
