It is a valid way to vote for a single candidate and not rank the rest. The ballot advises you not to do this, but it doesn&#x27;t explicitly invalidate your ballot. See slide #6:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.maine.gov&#x2F;sos&#x2F;cec&#x2F;elec&#x2F;upcoming&#x2F;pdf&#x2F;RCVMarkedBallots.Updated101420.pdf" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.maine.gov&#x2F;sos&#x2F;cec&#x2F;elec&#x2F;upcoming&#x2F;pdf&#x2F;RCVMarkedBal...</a><p>You can vote for a single candidate across all your choices&#x2F;rounds (across a single row), but voting for multiple candidates in the same choice <i>will</i> invalidate your ballot (down a single column). Multiples down a column would be like filling in all the circles for all candidates on a normal ballot, or even not filling any in. Same difference.')
