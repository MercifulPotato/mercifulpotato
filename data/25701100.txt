Item(by='lostcolony', descendants=None, kids=[25701643, 25701652], score=None, time=1610206192, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25700543, text='So there&#x27;s a nuance there you&#x27;re missing.<p>Newspaper publishing is opt in; that is, anything published they chose to publish.<p>Websites that allow third parties to post content on them is opt out; that is, anything published did so without initial moderation.<p>If a website operator posts their own statements, they can theoretically be found to be libelous. They can&#x27;t be held accountable for posts by other people. Newspapers potentially can (though I&#x27;ve never seen a court case where a newspaper was sued for something in the Opinion section), but they -chose- to publish that item.<p>Realistically websites should be thought of as a public bulletin board. Should you be able to sue the person who put up the bulletin board, for content that was posted to the bulletin board by other people?')
