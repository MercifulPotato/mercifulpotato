A society&#x27;s breeding mores have genetic as well as cultural effects. In fact the two are so closely intertwined I don&#x27;t believe they can be practicably separated.<p>[1] Degrees are counted by how many nodes in the tree have to be traversed upward to reach the nearest common direct ancestor. For example siblings are second degree since the two siblings share a common ancestor. An uncle and nephew would be 3rd degree, first cousins 4th degree, and so on.')
