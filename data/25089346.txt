Item(by='rektide', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605315644, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25084372, text='Ursala Franklin identified two main forms of technology,<p>* Holistic technology, which leaves humans in control but provides enhancements &amp; tools to let the person do more than they could have. Back then she mainly identified this with craftspeople, but now I think a lot of creatives are in this realm too, folks using Photoshop&#x2F;Gimp&#x2F;Glimpse.<p>* Prescriptive Technology, which has an operating paradigm that bends the world about it to fit itself; the assembly line. Technology as asserting it&#x27;s own will in the world.<p>Mid-century thinking that increasingly reflects how technology relates to the world. I urge us all to reflect on what kinds of technology we want to be involved with. I think Bruce here is focusing on one particular side that has, alas, come to dominate, but it&#x27;s important to remember that there&#x27;s other forms for tech to take.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Ursula_Franklin#Holistic_and_prescriptive_technologies" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Ursula_Franklin#Holistic_and_p...</a>')
