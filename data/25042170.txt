Item(by='cmrdporcupine', descendants=None, kids=[25043116, 25042413, 25042777, 25042771, 25042791, 25042956, 25046576, 25042605, 25042964], score=None, time=1604974012, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25041716, text='In the Annals of St Bertin there&#x27;s mention of an attack by the Danes (Vikings) on a region of Francia in 859 AD, where the peasants formed an association to defend themselves and fought off the Vikings, and drove them off without the assistance of the Carolingian rulers.<p>This is reported by the Carolingians as brave but immoral, because Charlemagne had banned any such associations or militias, and military service was reserved for aristocrats only.<p>The response of the Frankish leadership was to come in and massacre the peasants who had defended their own land.
