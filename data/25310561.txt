Item(by='dragonwriter', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607130299, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25309879, text='&gt; equal representation, of sorts - the things that are being represented are the states, so it is fair, of sorts.<p>Equal representation of things that aren&#x27;t the individual citizens is <i>not</i> democracy, and is in fact opposed to democracy; and &quot;fair, of sorts&quot; is orthogonal to democracy.<p>Yes, democracy is a more convenient term and concept than many of those that might be accurately applied to the Senate, but its not even remotely accurate when applied to the Senate, which is <i>deliberately</i> not merely <i>non</i>-democratic, but actively <i>anti</i>-democratic.<p>If you want to argue that the design of the Senate is useful or good or fair in some way, go ahead and do that, but don&#x27;t expect to use &quot;democracy&quot; for that purpose without argument just because its a warm fuzzy word that is more <i>convenient</i> than any word with equally positive general reception that accurately applies to the Senate.')
