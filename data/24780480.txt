Item(by='dangus', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602703440, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24780129, text='The practice itself doesn’t really bother me, but I do find it to be a strange and questionable marketing technique.<p>This amount of money represents a 4% price difference at most.<p>That means that someone in Apple marketing decided that increasing customer confusion and reducing goodwill was worth a gain of 4% on <i>some but not all</i> of the phones they sold.<p>I get it - being operational is important for the success of the business. Considering every single detail from the packaging to the included accessories to the way the product is sold and priced is incredibly important. But it seems like Apple is getting into the dangerous territory of sacrificing customer goodwill over it. Buying an iPhone now feels like signing a contract for cable rather than something that’s fun and exciting.')
