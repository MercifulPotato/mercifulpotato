Item(by='lostlogin', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603665489, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24889244, text='New Zealander here - a lot of people are using Oxalic acid and glycerine on paper strips and are getting great results here - me included.<p>The paper is a plasterboard jointing paper and 3-5 layers are sewn together and soaked in a solution of about 40% Oxalic acid, 60% glycerine. Used like conventional mite strips they need replacing more often but seem to work really well.<p>Randy Oliver has been trialing other delivery methods in California.<p>My hives are the best they have ever been this season, with a very strong start.<p><a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;scientificbeekeeping.com&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;scientificbeekeeping.com&#x2F;</a>')
