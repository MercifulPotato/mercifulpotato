Item(by='ncmncm', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603171268, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24821398, text='Whenever I find a copy of Elements of Style, I throw it away. I have deep-sixed at least four copies in the past five years.<p>Geoff Pullum, at Edinburgh, calls it &quot;The Nasty Book&quot;, because it makes people needlessly insecure about their writing, and because its supposed rules are not followed by any great writer.<p>The most damning fact is that, when he produced the second edition after Strunk died, White made up a bunch more phony rules, and then went through Strunk&#x27;s original text and <i>doctored it</i> to obey them. Then, he failed to obey them in his own text--frequently on the same page where he was advocating them.<p>There is a certain amount of correct advice, but it is the same as elsewhere, e.g.: &quot;omit needless words&quot;. Did we need a book to tell us that?<p>But his victims love him.')
