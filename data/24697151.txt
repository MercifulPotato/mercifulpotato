Item(by='matthewdgreen', descendants=None, kids=[24697244], score=None, time=1601988788, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24696951, text='A general rule is that cryptographers never know what applications’ requirements are, unless we’re also developing those applications. The Ed paper took a very opinionated view on what the requirements should be: out in the real world, it turned out that they were often stronger. This doesn’t mean anyone is “wrong” per se, but it should perhaps be a lesson in humility for people who develop primitives.')
