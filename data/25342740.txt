Item(by='myself248', descendants=None, kids=[25345713], score=None, time=1607411702, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25340985, text='A friend of mine used to work tech support, and said that from her perspective, a lot of the effort was trying to counter-outsmart customers who thought they were too smart.<p>For instance: &quot;This might just be a loose cable, can you unplug each end of it, and plug it back in?&quot; invariably elicited an &quot;I&#x27;ve already done that&quot;, or a brief pause followed by &quot;okay, there, I did it, do you see it?&quot;. Lies, often.<p>But: &quot;Alright, I want you to try turning the cable around for me, yeah swap it end for end. Sometimes you get a connector that just doesn&#x27;t fit quite right, but it works the other way around and it&#x27;s faster than sending a tech to replace it&quot;, would often get a startled &quot;Oh! I hadn&#x27;t thought of that, one moment...&quot; and then the customer actually DOES unplug the thing, and what do you know, they click it in properly this time.')
