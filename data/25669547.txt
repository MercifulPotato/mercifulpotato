Item(by='kleiba', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610016291, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25664647, text='Defining functions and data structures is all that programming is, hence debugging is <i>exactly</i> redefining classes (and functions).<p>Your question about not needing to restart the runtime (&quot;how often do you have to do this?&quot;) seems to imply that you expect the common answer to be &quot;(almost) never&quot; -- that expectation is not surprising since most languages don&#x27;t even support this kind of debugging!<p>That&#x27;s exactly the point of the author: to demonstrate a different way of debugging that&#x27;s afforded by Lisp and to demonstrate that it can be incredibly powerful. It&#x27;s just something that is perhaps unfamiliar to programmers who are more used to other programming languages where the debugging facilities are different.')
