Item(by='amatic', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604376442, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24974463, text='The original article has the same error in the title. x^(-1&#x2F;2) is the reciprocal square root. The fault is with math notation where rasing x to a negative power, i.e. x^-a means 1&#x2F;(x^a); while inverse function F(x) is written F^-1(x). The inverse square root would be just square. Edit: I saw it called multiplicative inverse of the square root')
