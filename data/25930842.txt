Item(by='jacksnipe', descendants=None, kids=[25935814, 25935087], score=None, time=1611767901, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25930757, text='I&#x27;m generally sympathetic to the idea that financial derivatives are a great evil, and a huge part of what feels &quot;broken&quot; about the current system.<p>However, consider futures, which are very similar to options. Futures were, as I understand it, popularized in Chicago as a way for farmers to be able to get money for their crop right now, at a fixed price. In this way, they could pass the risk -- and a little bit of the profit -- off to people with more capital, who were better able to weather all possible outcomes.')
