Item(by='35fbe7d3d5b9', descendants=None, kids=[25911747, 25914445, 25913668], score=None, time=1611627748, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25911614, text='This, like most intersections of law and software, is <i>tricky</i>.<p>Let&#x27;s say you, zakki, release v1 of a program as the sole author.  The code is yours, and you release it under 2-BSD.  v1 will remain under 2-BSD forever; however, you are free to:<p>* sell the <i>same code</i> under a different license (including a closed-source commercial license)<p>* change the license of v2 to a completely different license – in this case, v1 will remain under 2-BSD in perpetuity but future releases are not.<p>This would be true for 2-BSD, GPL, whatever license you like.<p>---<p>This gets interesting when you are not the sole author.  In that case, you either:<p>+ were fastidious about requiring your contributors to assign copyright to you, in which case the above holds<p>- accepted all comers, in which case you have to go on a crusade to chase down those people to assign copyright OR to agree to your license change – Mozilla did this (painful) exercise in its past[1].<p>---<p>Now, if you were not the sole author but you actually reused or forked code, like OpnSense (based on FreeBSD), your options are different.  In that case, the underlying license cannot just &quot;go away&quot;: FreeBSD&#x27;s license carries through.  However, as it is a very permissive 2-BSD, there&#x27;s nothing to say OpnSense couldn&#x27;t relicense to a proprietary license per the above.<p>---<p>Hypothetically, if OpnSense were based on Linux instead, the underlying GPLv2 would be attached.  In that case, you could <i>not</i> relicense to a closed-source model because<p>&gt; You must cause any work that you distribute or publish, that in whole <i>or in part contains or is derived from the Program</i> or any part thereof, <i>to be licensed as a whole at no charge to all third parties under the terms of this License.</i><p>(emphasis mine).<p>Clear as mud? :)<p>[1]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www-archive.mozilla.org&#x2F;mpl&#x2F;relicensing-faq" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www-archive.mozilla.org&#x2F;mpl&#x2F;relicensing-faq</a>')
