Intel sent us an eval board that wouldn’t boot.  After various people screwed around with it I finally looked at the schematics and saw a pullup capacitor on the UART Vcc.  A glance at the board and indeed there was a cap between Vcc and the chip supply.  An easy workaround but how the hell did this pass?<p>So the board was booting up fine, you just couldn’t talk to it.  We excited the chip wasn’t going to survive and indeed, it didn’t.')
