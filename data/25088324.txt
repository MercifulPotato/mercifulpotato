Item(by='core-questions', descendants=None, kids=[25088408, 25088444], score=None, time=1605306578, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25088276, text='As a Canadian, you&#x27;re missing out on the other critical half of the &#x27;sorry&#x27;.<p>You step on my foot. I look at you, and say &quot;sorry&quot;, and pull my foot away. You abashedly also &quot;sorry&quot; and we move on.<p>Am I apologizing? Hardly! The full sentence is, &quot;Sorry, did you just actually step on my foot, what the hell?&quot; and your reply is &quot;Sorry, yes, I did do that, didn&#x27;t mean to, apologies&quot;. Then the problem is solved and we move on.<p>&gt; But in the US, I&#x27;ve been pounced on for a casual use of that word, as if I&#x27;d announced failure and that I owed something to the other party.<p>It is precisely the opposite in circumstances like the above!')
