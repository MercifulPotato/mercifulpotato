Item(by='topkai22', descendants=None, kids=[24848504], score=None, time=1603291864, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24847793, text='Surprisingly hard to find a simple explanation from FSF, but here is a &quot;proof by contradiction&quot; explaining why should use GPL instead of the  LGPL- <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.gnu.org&#x2F;licenses&#x2F;why-not-lgpl.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.gnu.org&#x2F;licenses&#x2F;why-not-lgpl.html</a><p>Basically, the LGPL exists because the GPL requires any binaries linking to GPLed code to inherit the GPL.<p>My understanding is that properitary programs can utilize &#x2F; incorporate GPL binaries via interprocess communication (pipes, TCP, shared files,  etc), but if they cannot do intra process communication without potentially violating the GPL.<p>So it depends on how your define &quot;build&quot; here :)<p>Regardless, because my role involves creating and distributing potentially proprietary software, I generally handle GPL projects with caution. I&#x27;ll happily use Linux, but I don&#x27;t look at the sources. If I see a GPL license on github, I&#x27;ll generally move on. If I see something is AGPL licensed, I&#x27;ll move on very quickly.')
