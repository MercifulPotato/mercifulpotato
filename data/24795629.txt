Item(by='gilleain', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602804806, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24795112, text='Presumably (am not an expert in this!) because the barrier to entry in a field of knowledge was lower in the past. As in, at one point you could read all there was to know about a subject.<p>Also, there was more &#x27;low hanging fruit&#x27; in the past. No one is impressed now if you reinvent the calculus. So it used to be easier to have major discoveries in multiple fields.<p>Finally, the whole idea of different areas of knowledge is relatively modern. The &#x27;sciences&#x27; used to include astrology, for example. Even the distinction between biblical studies and observation of the natural world was vague at some point.')
