Item(by='dmurray', descendants=None, kids=[25265070], score=None, time=1606829984, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25264555, text='It might seem like they should have more redundancy - one cable broke, then a second, and just a week later the whole thing falls apart - but for structures held in place by tension it doesn&#x27;t work like that.<p>A bicycle wheel has redundant spokes: you could design a wheel with 66% of the typical number of spokes and it would work OK. But if you do break a spoke, the wheel will start deforming straight away. If you can&#x27;t replace the spoke, one piece of advice is to loosen the spoke directly opposite the broken one to even out the distribution of force.')
