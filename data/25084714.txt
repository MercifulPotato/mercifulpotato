Look, I know how explosive all this is and I don&#x27;t need to ban someone for having strong feelings. But we need to hold the container in a certain way.<p>It&#x27;s fine to say that another comment is false, especially if you then add corrective information. It&#x27;s fine to say that it&#x27;s misleading, if you show how and (again) add corrective info. But to say that another user is lying crosses a clear semantic line: it imputes malicious intent, i.e. not just that they&#x27;re wrong but that they know the truth and are intending to deceive. That we don&#x27;t allow. Surely it&#x27;s not hard to see why.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;newsguidelines.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;newsguidelines.html</a>')
