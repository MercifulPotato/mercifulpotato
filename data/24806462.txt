Item(by='ohazi', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602895011, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24806408, text='&gt; control-intensive, and memory-intensive<p>The ideal &quot;accelerator&quot; for these kinds of jobs is a CPU with a big cache.<p>Video encoding has well defined control loops and data paths that don&#x27;t arbitrarily interfere with each other, so it&#x27;s a good candidate for custom hardware.<p>That is, you have a high bandwidth, highly parallel fast-path between framebuffer memory and functional units that compute FFTs and do motion vector operations, and a control plane that looks at a small handful variables in order to decide which data plane operations to schedule and how to glue together the final result.<p>To run JS, you need a pile of functional units and lots of memory, and data for every operation needs to be able to come from &#x2F; go to anywhere in memory. That&#x27;s... just a general purpose computer.')
