Item(by='carapace', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607020830, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25288557, text='I found this out by accident.<p>I took some woodchips that had been used as bedding in a chicken coup (so it had feathers and poop in it), put down a layer about four inches thick, then covered it in a rich mulch that had lots of mycelium in it already[1], and left it alone for about six weeks.  The mycelium went all through there and converted the woodchips to a chalky almost asbestos-like form.<p>I had another experience with this same mycelium mulch[1] where it literally sealed off an area of sandy soil and routed water somewhere: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=15059654" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=15059654</a><p>[1] Kellogg&#x27;s GROMULCH 2-in-1, it&#x27;s my secret weapon.')
