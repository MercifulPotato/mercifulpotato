Item(by='shiftingleft', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610901185, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25811996, text='The Great Filter thesis is that there has to be one or more <i>very</i> low probability events in the Drake Equation.<p>However, as per my other comments, you can update the distributions of your parameters in different ways, such that none of the events in the Drake equation have a particularly low probability.<p>Then the Great Filter disappears and there&#x27;s no specific or few set of events to point to - it&#x27;s just that the probability of all events succeeding in combination is extremely low.')
