Item(by='gurkendoktor', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604785475, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25019271, text='I agree for hard science, but I see more wishful thinking about society on the &quot;left&quot;. A lot of the rhetoric about social justice implicitly hinges on the Blank Slate theory, the assumption that all (groups of) people would, in a vacuum, have exactly the same statistical outcomes. Therefore, all deviations from the average must automatically be a sign of systemic discrimination. When you look at studies, the situation is rarely as clear-cut.<p>The same is true with the &quot;diversity is strength&quot; mantra, where everyone seems to cite that one feel-good McKinsey study as if that told the full story.<p>This kind of naivety is not as bad as burning the planet, but at the end of the day, it&#x27;s still people looking at studies and saying &quot;can&#x27;t be true&quot;.')
