Item(by='CryptoPunk', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608180018, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25445092, text='Up until 2015, price talk was heavily discouraged in &#x2F;r&#x2F;Bitcoin.<p>It was all about adoption - new merchants accepting it, new people spending it, new use-cases enabled by its peer-to-peer electronic cash and immutable and permissionless record-keeping functionality, etc.<p>Everything changed after the &#x27;purge&#x27;, where the head moderator took a harsh stance against members trying to build consensus for a hard fork that would help realize Bitcoin&#x27;s potential as a global peer-to-peer electronic cash, and started banning people left and right. The Bitcoin subreddit turned into a full time price pumping forum after that.<p>Ironically, Bitcoin saw more rapid price growth before 2015, when the primary topic of discussion was consciously steered away from price, than after.')
