Item(by='TheMblabla', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609540170, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25607411, text='I don&#x27;t have numbers but generally, compared to smaller turbines, a larger blade length means a faster blade tip to maintain the same rotational frequency (the end of the blade will be going faster around the turbines circumference). This creates more noise, and noise is a HUGE limiting factor, so they increase the turbine&#x27;s torque to get more power from slower rotations. That means a larger, more complex, and more expensive generator in upfront and maintenance cost.')
