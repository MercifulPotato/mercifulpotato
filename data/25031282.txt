The Senate and the electoral college give enough of an advantage to small states that, even though no one or two small states are liable to flip an election by themselves, they are still able to have a voice in federal policy by coalition-building.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=25031351" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=25031351</a>')
