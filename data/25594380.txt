Fanatics don’t create, but they contribute energy (time, money, adulation, organization, analysis) to support the creators.</i><p>&gt; <i>Creators and fanatics are both geeks.2 They totally love the New Thing, they’re fascinated with all its esoteric ins and outs, and they spend all available time either doing it or talking about it.</i><p>&gt; <i>If the scene is sufficiently geeky, it remains a strictly geek thing; a weird hobby, not a subculture.</i><p>&gt; <i>If the scene is unusually exciting, and the New Thing can be appreciated without having to get utterly geeky about details, it draws mops.3 Mops are fans, but not rabid fans like the fanatics. They show up to have a good time, and contribute as little as they reasonably can in exchange.</i><p>* <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;meaningness.com&#x2F;geeks-mops-sociopaths" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;meaningness.com&#x2F;geeks-mops-sociopaths</a><p>* 2019: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=21963626" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=21963626</a><p>* 2018: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=17433487" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=17433487</a><p>* 2015: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=9632751" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=9632751</a>')
