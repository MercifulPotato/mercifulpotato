Item(by='tannhaeuser', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607338324, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25330036, text='OTOH, there&#x27;s the hypothesis that people who&#x27;re in it primarily to advance their programming language or other tool are bound to never advance the state of the art, or create something original really. I think this phenomenon is cast well in terms of Keirsey temperaments [1] used to this date by partnership agencies. In particular, the different characterization of The Engineer vs The Operator (or The Architect and the Artisan) comes to mind.<p>There are obviously other psychological and sociological phenomenae associated with adolescence, such as peer group pressure [2] and self-righteousness [3], at play as well.<p>It&#x27;s nothing new; the &quot;100% Pure Rust&quot; rhetoric is strikingly similar to 90s&#x2F;00s Java developer attitudes.<p>[1]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.m.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Keirsey_Temperament_Sorter" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.m.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Keirsey_Temperament_Sorter</a><p>[2]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.m.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Peer_pressure" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.m.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Peer_pressure</a><p>[3]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.m.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Self-righteousness" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.m.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Self-righteousness</a>')
