Item(by='29athrowaway', descendants=None, kids=[25491370], score=None, time=1608497109, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25488887, text='Gnome 3 was a big departure from what users were used to in Gnome 2. In Gnome 2 you could configure panels by simply dragging and dropping elements. In Gnome 3, this customization is achieved by installing extensions or Gnome shell themes. Installing extensions can be easy, but it will never be as easy as dragging and dropping elements with your mouse.<p>For many years, when you used XFCE and then went back to Gnome 3, you could perceive how much slower Gnome 3 was. Now, performance has gotten much better to the point Gnome 3 is my default desktop again.<p>You can tell there are users are still disgusted with the Gnome 3 UI. That&#x27;s why the MATE and Cinnamon projects exist.')
