Item(by='jzwinck', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611268141, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25864614, text='This is not how Intel sees it. They describe hyperthreading as a single core having two (or more, but usually two) sets of architectural state which basically means registers. It&#x27;s not two cores, it is one core that can switch between two different instruction pointers. They share almost everything else apart from the APIC.')
