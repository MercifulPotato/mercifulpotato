Item(by='avar', descendants=None, kids=[24998084], score=None, time=1604566699, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24995288, text='Livestock breeding isn&#x27;t inherently unsustainable. E.g. you can have closed-off poultry or pork farms with greenhouse-fed animals, all powered by wind or solar, with sewage processing to ensure there&#x27;s no external pollution. This isn&#x27;t sci-fi, it exists today in e.g. the denser parts of western Europe.<p>Yes it&#x27;s costlier than beef from the developing world raised on cut-down rainforest land, but growing lab is a lot more expensive.<p>You can argue that it&#x27;s inefficient, sure, but so is a vegan eating an avacado instead of white rice. Things can be relatively inefficient and still be sustainable.')
