I have a hard time coming up with important examples. Germany 1870-1932 had many parties with substantial representation, but how civil was it, particularly after 1918? Mark Twain&#x27;s essay &quot;Stirring Times in Austria&quot; gives a picture of a multi-party parliament in a nervous breakdown. George Dangerfield&#x27;s <i>The Strange Death of Liberal England</i> shows the UK ca. 1910 with two large parties, Liberal and Conservative, and two unignorable smaller ones, Labor and the Irish caucus; it was not pretty.<p>At this point, does any major European country have more than two viable parties or coalitions? Will the German Greens ever get a Cabinet seat outside a coalition with the Social Democrats? Will the UK Liberal Democrats ever get a Cabinet seat except in coalition?')
