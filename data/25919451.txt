Mostly because of the underlying hardware and ecosystem. Simple things were simple, and complex things were complicated but possible.<p>Today everything is complex and complicated for little reason.<p>(Compare e.g. the elegance of Amiga Message and signal ports to  named pipes &#x2F; shared memory &#x2F; tcp &#x2F; whatever. Of course it can be emulated - but the fact it’s standard on the Amiga means every program uses it and it makes a huge difference in interoperability. Similarly, IFF was good enough; there weren’t billions of framing formats on the Amiga, because there was an acceptable standard from day 1)')
