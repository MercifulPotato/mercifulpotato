The dominant perspective on writing in academia teaches people to do exactly what you are saying. This is what is taught in undergraduate English classes. The unconventional wisdom is to Write Like You Speak. So it&#x27;s more about unlearning popular things like &quot;bureaucratese&quot; and corporate speak. Poetry and literature aside, most of us are better off writing in plain English if we want to do well professionally.<p>The Plain English Campaign lays it out well:<p>&quot;The main advantages of plain English are: it is faster to write; it is faster to read; and  you get your message across more often, more easily and in a friendlier way.&quot;<p><a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.plainenglish.co.uk&#x2F;how-to-write-in-plain-english.html" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.plainenglish.co.uk&#x2F;how-to-write-in-plain-english....</a>')
