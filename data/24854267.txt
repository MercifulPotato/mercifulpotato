Item(by='yencabulator', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603332706, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24853366, text='You still have a critical section; the cacheline is in an exclusive mode during the change. LL&#x2F;SC operates on top the cache sharing protocols, just like everything else. Your wikipedia link even refers to that: &quot;Typically, CPUs track the load-linked address at a cache-line or other granularity [...]&quot;. MESI and friends <i>are that mechanism</i>.<p>What lock-free means in this case is that an <i>OS level thread freezing doesn&#x27;t prevent global progress</i>. That&#x27;s true on the abstraction level of typical programming, but grandparent is very much correct:<p>&gt; Really, &quot;lock free&quot; programming is just locking critical sections with lower level hardware primitives.')
