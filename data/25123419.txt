You can also use this to just uncommit locally if you made a mistake, too.<p>&gt;git reset --soft HEAD^<p>That preserves your work, but it uncommits it. You can now make changes, re-add files, and recommit. If you want to destroy the last commit instead, replace --soft with --hard.<p>&gt;git push origin +mybranch<p>The plus sign forces an overwrite of the remote branch.<p>I&#x27;ve used this technique countless times, for years.
