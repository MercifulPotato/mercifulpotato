Item(by='leetcrew', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608652794, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25502920, text='I don&#x27;t think this is a good tactic when you have an audience that is even halfway awake. when I see this happen on a topic I&#x27;m not very familiar with, my first reaction is to think it was an incisive question that OP realized they did not have a good response to.<p>I find that there is usually a fairly small number of &quot;gotcha&quot; questions that come up over and over for a particular topic. the &quot;trans people in competitive sports&quot; question in this thread is a good example. what makes these types of questions rhetorically effective is that they&#x27;re usually at least half-rooted in truth. in my experience it&#x27;s  more effective to recognize the likely gotchas and have a response ready, or better yet, preempt it entirely. at the very least, you should explain why you&#x27;re not answering the question, lest it appear that <i>you</i> are the one arguing in bad faith. you have to keep in mind that you&#x27;re not just trying to convince your interlocutor but also your audience.')
