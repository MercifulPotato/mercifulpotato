Or failing that, employing violence directly themselves as many large monopolies did in the 20s and 30s.<p>As an aside, I see this as the fundamental point of departure in language between a Marxian socialist and a libertarian-capitalist, they mean two different things by the &quot;state&quot;: libertarians believe the state is a parasite on the market and a corrupter and destroyer of the free market. Marxists believe that the state is created _by_ the market for itself (&quot;The executive of the modern state is nothing but a committee for managing the common affairs of the whole bourgeoisie.&quot;) Both see the other as hopelessly naive.')
