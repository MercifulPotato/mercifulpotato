Item(by='throwaway201103', descendants=None, kids=[25446163], score=None, time=1608141170, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25443997, text='&gt; Companies don&#x27;t do things to be nice.<p>They do if it gives them a market advantage. If Goodyear can market tires that are more &quot;sustainable&quot; or &quot;eco-friendly&quot; than their rivals that will resonate with consumers who focus on such things (many of them, particularly millenials and younger).<p>We saw this with trans-fats in food. They aren&#x27;t prohibited by the FDA, but most producers have eliminated them and have &quot;Zero Trans Fats&quot; emblazoned across the package labeling, because it sells better. Seeing this effect also with high-fructose corn syrup.')
