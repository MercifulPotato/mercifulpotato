Item(by='alibrarydweller', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609708614, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25624258, text='I&#x27;m a fan of the podcast but one argument they cited seemed to have a pretty glaring error - they looked at the case where eBay was comparing incremental gain on search ads over no ads. It&#x27;s methodologically hairy because eBay is a very major player with significant brand recognition.<p>&quot;When Tadelis was working for eBay, the company was in the practice of buying brand-keyword ads. Which meant that if you did an online search for “eBay,” the top result — before all the organic-search results — was a paid ad for eBay.&quot;<p>This doesn&#x27;t show that advertising doesn&#x27;t work per se, it shows that eBay didn&#x27;t hire a competent ad buyer. Whether or not you can prove the efficacy of advertising as a whole, this is not a valid approach.')
