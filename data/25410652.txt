Brave has local access to information about you; websites do not. Even if you were to give over all of your data to a website (why would you?), you now have to worry about that site sharing it, losing it, selling it, etc. With Brave, the data is on your device; cursory network scans confirm that it doesn&#x27;t flow outward either (see <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.zdnet.com&#x2F;article&#x2F;brave-deemed-most-private-browser-in-terms-of-phoning-home&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.zdnet.com&#x2F;article&#x2F;brave-deemed-most-private-brow...</a>). It&#x27;s much better to do this as a feature locally than as a third-party service remotely.')
