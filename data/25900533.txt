Item(by='hanslub42', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611561073, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25892024, text='For Plato mathematics was real, but not only that, the stuff we see around us was not.<p>In the Republic he therefore complains about mathematicians &quot;... always talking in a narrow and ridiculous manner, of &quot;squaring&quot; and &quot;extending&quot; and &quot;applying&quot; and the like - they confuse the ways of geometry with those of daily life; whereas knowledge is the real object of the whole science.&quot;<p>I find it amazing that Plato wrote this 2000 years before it was taken up again (quite radically) by Brouwer&#x27;s intuitionism, where &quot;squaring&quot;, &quot;extending&quot;, &quot;applying&quot; are the <i>only</i> way of doing mathematics (and proof by contradiction, one of Plato&#x27;s favourite devices, is not admissible)<p>The article&#x27;s conclusion &quot;Just about everything [except basic number theory]  in mathematics is determined by the society in which you live&quot; is rather disappointing, and not at all contradicts the reality of mathematics, as those societies  might just have made different discovery journeys in Plato&#x27;s realm of eternal mathematical truth.')
