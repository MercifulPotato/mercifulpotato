Item(by='csolorio', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609088281, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25552103, text='I&#x27;ve been reading Lords of Finance, which covers the early 1900s.  It&#x27;s eerie how the current situation reflects the pre Black Thursday boom.  Investments started to concentrate into a few stocks (then RCA and Montgomery Ward) as retail investors piled in.  Folks were buying on margin, credit was easy.  The difference now I think is the willingness of the Fed to reduce interest rates to support the stock market as well as a too big too fail ethos.  At the time, the Fed wanted to drive out speculators which exacerbated things.  I wonder how long the Fed will keep it up.')
