Item(by='marcinzm', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602112988, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24713147, text='The Oxford English Dictionary traced the use of the word &quot;startup&quot; for a budding business to a Forbes article in 1976 where it referred to the Silicon Valley electronics companies.<p>So, no, the term &quot;startup&quot; wasn&#x27;t co-opted but has meant a fast growing silicon valley type company for the last 50 years. Before that it was not used to refer to new businesses.<p>As a note, &quot;starting up&quot; is not the same as &quot;startup.&quot;')
