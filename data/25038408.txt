Item(by='troughway', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604948765, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25037917, text='That used to work back when more people were building things to scratch an itch and didn&#x27;t have a good sense of business and marketing.<p>Today, you can expect a product that launches to be extremely polished and have a very calculated marketing strategy from the original pitch, to some kind of an invitation list of early adopters, a lot of blogging time invested, and lastly, a sales team of some sort. A lot of them will have some kind of financial backing. And best of all, you won&#x27;t even know to what degree their image and branding is curated to sell one story (&quot;We&#x27;re a small shop&quot;) vs reality (&quot;We are very well connected with decision makers at important companies&quot;, etc).<p>Basically, it was taken from a fun side project and perfected to a business science on an industry-wide scale when the sharks smelled how much money was on the table.')
