Item(by='onecommentman', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609014051, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25545419, text='Not necessarily a refund, but some sort of compensation could be in order.  “A reboot has occurred.  For this inconvenience, we have credited the CC associated with your AppleID $2, (or $5 Applebucks or a free coffee).”  Design out the potential for gaming the system for infinite coffees.<p>If you impose a cost on a user, you compensate the user for that cost.  I think that is the basic idea behind tort law.  To keep lawyers and associated expense out of the system, do it voluntarily.<p>But if voluntary compensation of users is being ignored, a change in liability doctrine for software might be in order.  Start with the biggest players who can afford the legal costs to resolve the doctrinal issues.')
