Item(by='chmod600', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602138647, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24715856, text='Drugs are perceived as an assault on the idea of free will itself, which is fundamental to many &quot;red state&quot; politicial philosophies (including free market and religion).<p>Drugs challenge free will through addiction as well as influence on behavior (&quot;under the influence&quot;).<p>Marijuana just got swept up in that whole idea, and ended up polarizing like everything else.<p>But even the most hardcore libertarian would hesitate to say it should be legal for 14 year olds to get meth or fentanyl at the corner store.<p>There&#x27;s a big difference between illegal and &quot;war on drugs&quot;-illegal, though. Most small-government types would probably agree once you point out the kind of government apparatus that&#x27;s built up around the war on drugs.')
