Item(by='hibbelig', descendants=None, kids=[25342790], score=None, time=1607411671, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25342652, text='The cases where hanging indentation is used are plain function calls. The places where the other kind of indentation is used are similar to control structures in other languages. In C like languages you can tell by the curly braces that it’s a control structure (if, while,...). In Clojure they all use parentheses.<p>In Clojure you can define your own control structures, and the way to do that is using macros. That’s why the article talks about macros.<p>Does that help?')
