Item(by='nullc', descendants=None, kids=[25551422], score=None, time=1609080513, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25547448, text='I don&#x27;t often experience anxiety.<p>One day I came across a silly writing prompt thread on Reddit along the lines &quot;What would your mind was transported back to you as a 14-year old?&quot;...   Presumably anticipating lots of comments about buying Apple shares and Bitcoin.<p>I was struck by the sheer impossibility of retracing my footsteps to get back all the things I&#x27;ve liked about my life, the loss of loved ones who only exist as they are as a result of interactions with me I could never reproduce.<p>It&#x27;s just a silly hypothetical, but I find it crushing to even describe it in a comment.  The anxiety it produces is physical, distracting, disorienting, and reliably predictable.  It made the article even difficulty to skim.  Like the author writes, I find it easier to imagine different lives for other people.<p>I like what I&#x27;ve lived very much and the people in my life.<p>What If You Could Do It All Over?  -- I say: Thanks but no thanks. ... and please don&#x27;t mention the idea again!<p>:)')
