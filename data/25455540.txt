Item(by='reitanqild', descendants=None, kids=[25456281], score=None, time=1608213167, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25454142, text='Agree with DennisP but will add some more:<p>Since the Bible has already been mentioned, here is one key word to (mostly) put to rest this idea from a Christian perspective:<p>&quot;But someone will say, “You have faith, and I have works.” Show me your faith without your works, and I will show you my faith by my works. &quot; James 2.18.<p>I think from this (and the context) we can conclude that the Bible is very much in favour of putting in the actual work.<p>That said, a concept that I was introduced to when I was younger is that of &quot;dead works&quot;, a concept that is harder to explain in a technical text only forum, but which sounds very much like what you describe based on the explanations I received: &quot;how much more shall the blood of Christ, who through the eternal Spirit offered Himself without spot to God, cleanse your conscience from dead works to serve the living God?&quot; Hebrews 9.14.<p>Shortly summarized as far as I remember and understand &quot;dead works&quot; was explained to me as things you will do just because you feel you &quot;have to&quot; to please your conscience, not because of a drive inside you to do what is good and right.')
