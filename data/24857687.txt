Item(by='spijdar', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603372803, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24857606, text='It seems the problem here isn&#x27;t Youtube, though -- it&#x27;s the ad networks.<p>As great and beneficial as shifting to a p2p distribution model outside of Google is, I&#x27;m not sure how it defeats this &quot;censorship&quot;, as you go from &quot;Advertisers don&#x27;t want to pay for ad placements on x&#x2F;y&#x2F;z controversial topic and Google won&#x27;t promote me!&quot; to simply having no revenue model and no promotion.<p>You would need to start finding third party sponsors and ad networks, and some sort of promotion&#x2F;content discovery platform, where I suspect you&#x27;d run into the same problems -- the people with the money (ad networks) aren&#x27;t going to want to sponsor certain kinds of content.<p>I suspect the best way &quot;around&quot; this is to find sponsors and leverage models like Patreon and paypal donations to finance &quot;untouchable&quot; content. I think it encourages better content than adsense financed videos on top of that.')
