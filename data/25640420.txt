Wiktionary etymology:<p>From New Latin propāganda, short for Congregātiō dē Propagandā Fide, &quot;congregation for propagating the faith&quot;, a committee of cardinals established in 1622 by Gregory XV to supervise foreign missions, and properly the ablative feminine gerundive of Latin propāgō (“propagate”) (see English propagation). Modern political sense dates from World War I, not originally pejorative.<p>It basically means advertising or public relations, pejoratively.')
