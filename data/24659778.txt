Item(by='lordnacho', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1601622074, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24659696, text='There&#x27;s a lot of depth in it, but here&#x27;s my version for laymen. I was a derivatives trader in the financial markets and also did some work in crypto exchanges.<p>Imagine someone offers you a deal where instead of buying stocks, you agree with someone to pay you the difference between the price of a stock today and whenever you decide to exit the deal. PnL should look a lot like whatever the stock did over the period.<p>Add to this that of course when the trade starts, it&#x27;s worth about zero to either side. But there&#x27;s a chance you&#x27;re gonna lose money, so the exchange wants you to put up some money. Not the entire amount that the shares cost, because you probably won&#x27;t lose that much. And we can adjust the margin according to how the market moves.<p>So you can then get a much larger exposure than if you just bought the stock with the same amount of cash.<p>Now add to this a minor wrinkle, that is you don&#x27;t use the cash dollars to buy the bet, you actually use the stock. And if you win, you get more stock.')
