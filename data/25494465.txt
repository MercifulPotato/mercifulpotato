Item(by='ncmncm', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608555262, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25493324, text='Yes, wait-free is a more precise description of single-writer ring buffer performance.<p>Lock-free queues are not fixed-time, because operations on them involve negotiations on the bus to reclaim the cache line involved, often behind other queued operations.<p>With the ring buffer, the writer never gives up ownership of the head cache line, and the next one can be pre-fetched (speculatively) because the writes are walking memory sequentially. There is a little hiccup when you wrap around, so it&#x27;s not absolutely fixed-time.<p>I put my ring buffers on hugepages so I am not competing for page-map TLB cache.')
