Item(by='eru', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607412098, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25333230, text='A big search space is not a detriment to optimization.  It just keeps you from reaching provable optimality, but doesn&#x27;t keep you from saving on costs compared to doing nothing.<p>Same for the rest of what you describe: yes, a perfect system would be a lot of hassle, but making any effort at all is sure to yield benefits.<p>You don&#x27;t need to change the billing model necessarily.  The customers don&#x27;t have to know that you are doing this kind of optimization.<p>Of course, the more you can expose incentives to your customers, the more efficient you can get.<p>But even if your containers arrive in arbitrary order and you have to make your decisions on where to place them &#x27;online&#x27;, ie before the next container arrives; as long as you have any choice left at all, you can try your hand at optimization techniques.')
