Your country of origin, in particular, will play a huge role in how easy it&#x27;ll be to migrate to your country of choice.<p>I grew up in Malaysia, and is now living and working in Australia. My journey was a typical one - I studied at an Australian university, lucked out and got sponsored for a job here, and I leveraged that to obtain permanent residency.<p>Australia has a lenient skilled-independent visa (the 189 visa), which doesn&#x27;t require nomination, but does require certification. You can check online if you&#x27;re qualified for it: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;immi.homeaffairs.gov.au&#x2F;visas&#x2F;getting-a-visa&#x2F;visa-listing&#x2F;skilled-independent-189" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;immi.homeaffairs.gov.au&#x2F;visas&#x2F;getting-a-visa&#x2F;visa-li...</a><p>If there&#x27;s one piece of advice I can offer, it is to get proper certificates, preferably from an institution in your preferred country. Immigration departments look favourably on that.')
