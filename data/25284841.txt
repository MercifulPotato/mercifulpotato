Item(by='zepto', descendants=None, kids=[25284932], score=None, time=1606964679, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25284714, text='The inability of industry and academia (and the w3c) to create standards is what <i>created</i> the opportunity that enabled Apple to succeed.<p>If standards had been the solution, Apple wouldn’t have been able to differentiate themselves.<p>Turn signals are a great example of why this idea is dead on arrival.  They have been around for close to a century and the government mandated them 50 years ago.<p>If you want the US computing industry to move at that speed, the government will make that happen for you.<p>We just aren’t at the stage in computing where innovation is over and we can afford to force everyone to adhere to a government mandated design.')
