Item(by='ltratt', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603353496, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24852758, text='Alas, most parser generators don&#x27;t have very good error recovery (and some have such terrible error recovery that I think it&#x27;s worse than not having any!).<p>It turns out that this isn&#x27;t inevitable: there&#x27;s been a long strand of research on decent error recovery for LR parsers, at least, but it needed a bit of a refresh to be practical. If you&#x27;ll forgive the blatant self promotion, we tackled this problem in <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;soft-dev.org&#x2F;pubs&#x2F;html&#x2F;diekmann_tratt__dont_panic&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;soft-dev.org&#x2F;pubs&#x2F;html&#x2F;diekmann_tratt__dont_panic&#x2F;</a> which is implemented in our Rust parsing system <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;softdevteam&#x2F;grmtools&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;softdevteam&#x2F;grmtools&#x2F;</a>. It won&#x27;t beat the very best hand-written error recovery routines, but it&#x27;s often not far behind.')
