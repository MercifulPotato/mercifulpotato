Item(by='vidarh', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611734200, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25924895, text='Socialist thinking is about de facto freedom, not de jure freedom.<p>If you have the power <i>and</i> means to do whatever you want <i>then</i> the de jure limits to that power are irrelevant.<p>On the other hand, if you do not have the means to do as you want, then having de jure rights to do what you want is irrelevant.<p>In poverty, then, gaining means is as important as gaining rights, because they are two sides of the same coin.<p>Disagreement between different types of socialist ideologies  over how to balance that is part of why socialist political movements span the range from extreme anti-authoritarian to focusing on means, and the power that gives, at the cost of authoritarianism.<p>But in a post-scarcity society, the details of the distribution of power becomes less and less interesting, as long as everyone has enough power to do what they want to do. That de facto power is what matters.')
