The distinction is mostly architectural, in that the &#x27;horde&#x27; is composed of an undifferentiated mass of CPUs (currently 8, could probably be expanded up to 128 with a cache-based memory hierarchy, which would certainly feel more &#x27;horde-like&#x27;). The application processors can be accessed individually, whereas the horde can only be accessed as a group.')
