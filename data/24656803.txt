Item(by='sq_', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1601591001, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24652149, text='Getting into anecdotal evidence here, but, as a consumer, I&#x27;ve seen what happens to the hyper-cheap cables after a few weeks to months of use: they tend to fall apart or fail in fun and interesting ways.<p>On the other hand, I have never had a $10-15 Anker or some other brand cable fail on me without serious abuse. As a consumer, I&#x27;ll happily pay the extra few dollars for the expectation of durability, a warranty of some sort, and the knowledge that the cable should Just Work with most of my devices, even if I know that the price is way out of proportion to the materials involved.<p>Can&#x27;t speak for all other consumers, of course, but I would expect that many have figured the same thing out over the years of purchasing Micro USB&#x2F;Lightning&#x2F;30pin&#x2F;etc cables.')
