Item(by='PeterisP', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608081150, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25433594, text='&quot;Individuals can own and sell property&quot; is part of but not the whole concept of free market.<p>All the economic theory of free market (and all the advantages of it) rely on a few core assumptions, like liberty to trade and set prices, but also lots of buyers, lots of sellers, full competition, low barriers of entry and full information.<p>Just as it&#x27;s well known that a market that devolves into monopoly or oligopoly does not work like a free market anymore, or in the case of severely unequal bargaining power, the same applies in the case of information asymmetry, which also is well known to lead to a failure of free market.<p>So whether &quot;people are easily affected by suggestion&quot; <i>does</i> matter, because if that becomes the case and companies are widely using effective methods to do so, then the resulting economic structure of the competition is not like a free market.')
