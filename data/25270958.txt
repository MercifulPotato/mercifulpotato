Item(by='q3k', descendants=None, kids=[25271114], score=None, time=1606862123, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25270927, text='Check out LiveOverflow on YT. Maybe play some CTFs, but don&#x27;t do that super seriously, just enough to get you hooked on binary exploitation. They&#x27;re fun, especially if you find some teammates to cooperate with.<p>And then just, well, practice. A lot of practice. Mostly driven by curiosity about how things work - bugs will then just start to pop up and you are free to investigate whatever piques your interest. The more likely you are to just open up a debugger when a piece of software annoys you and try to binary patch it, the closer you are to being a security researcher :).<p>There&#x27;s not much books&#x2F;courses on this, low-level hacking is something that you kind of just learn as you go. But, for instance, if you never touched gdb&#x2F;lldb, or never looked at assembly code, or never wrote C - you should investigate that first as base skills.')
