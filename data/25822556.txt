Item(by='shiftingleft', descendants=None, kids=[25825669], score=None, time=1610983120, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25822488, text='Your note is very important, I think it would be good to give it more emphasis.<p>What works flawlessly, however, is:<p><pre><code>  In [1]: a = None\n  In [2]: b = 0\n  In [3]: (a is None) ^ (b is None)\n  Out[3]: True\n</code></pre>\nAlternatively, as suggested in another comment, you can use inequality as a replacement for XOR:<p><pre><code>  In [4]: (a is None) != (b is None)\n  Out[4]: True\n\n</code></pre>\nAnother error prone pattern is the following:<p><pre><code>  In [5]: a or b\n  Out[5]: 0\n</code></pre>\nWhich can behave differently depending on the order of elements&#x2F;values:<p><pre><code>  In [6]: b or a\n  Out[6]: None # should be 0\n</code></pre>\nSince b is zero, it doesn&#x27;t count.\nLess error prone, but also more verbose is:<p><pre><code>  In [10]: a if a is not None else b\n  Out[10]: 0</code></pre>')
