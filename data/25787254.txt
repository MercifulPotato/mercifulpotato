Item(by='spijdar', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610687584, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25786857, text='So, a few things. 4.2BSD was targeting VAX, which is most assuredly a 32 bit machine.<p>Further, the common denominator you&#x27;re seeing goes back much, much further than BSD itself. Behold! &quot;Version 1&quot; of UNIX, written in just 14 files of PDP-11 assembly. And if you look at u1.s, you&#x27;ll see the sysent routine which handles syscalls. The numbering should be familiar :-)<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;dspinellis&#x2F;unix-history-repo&#x2F;blob&#x2F;Research-V1-Snapshot-Development&#x2F;u1.s#L35" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;dspinellis&#x2F;unix-history-repo&#x2F;blob&#x2F;Researc...</a><p>If you go to 4.2BSD, you&#x27;ll see many of these syscalls labeled as &quot;old&quot;, e.g. &quot;old pause&quot;, &quot;old wait&quot;, &quot;old break&quot;.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;dspinellis&#x2F;unix-history-repo&#x2F;blob&#x2F;BSD-4_2-Snapshot-Development&#x2F;usr&#x2F;src&#x2F;sys&#x2F;sys&#x2F;syscalls.c#L6" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;dspinellis&#x2F;unix-history-repo&#x2F;blob&#x2F;BSD-4_2...</a><p>You can go a little further back to the original PDP7 assembly, but I don&#x27;t believe the &quot;kernel&quot; actually ran on a separate &quot;process&quot; at all, and the userland simply linked into kernel symbols and called &quot;read&quot;, &quot;readdir&quot; etc directly, hence why even later unix documentation tends to call these &quot;routines&quot; or &quot;library calls&quot;.')
