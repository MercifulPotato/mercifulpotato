Item(by='FabHK', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608142276, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25441581, text='Suboptimal. The &quot;NOT&quot; might be covered by something, and then what remains is &quot;THIS LEG&quot;. I would have written &quot;NO NO NO&quot; on one leg, and &quot;THIS THIS THIS&quot; on the other.<p>In aviation, standard phraseology is generally carefully designed such that (mosts) subsets of a phrase are distinct from the opposite phrase. For example, when ATC warns of traffic, you reply either &quot;traffic in sight&quot; or &quot;negative contact&quot;. When ATC hears only half of either, they still know what you meant.')
