Item(by='dragonwriter', descendants=None, kids=[25586481], score=None, time=1609361784, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25585317, text='No, repeating it with additional italics doesn&#x27;t make it less wrong.<p>Both sides may sometimes \nuse the same words to describe the problem, but they don&#x27;t agree on what the problem is; the words have different meanings when different people use them.<p>This is also true (and much easier to illustrate) with illegal immigration, where “both sides” agree that “illegal immigration” is a problem, but for one the problem is immigration outside of the current legal rules (or maybe even immigration generally), and for the other the problem is the current legal rules creating illegal status for certain immigrants who are not otherwise viewed by that side as undesirable.<p>Using the same words for a short description of the problem does not mean agreeing on what the problem is.')
