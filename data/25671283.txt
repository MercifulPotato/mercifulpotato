It needs to be proven that a statement was false or was reckless in deciding to publish the information without investigating whether it was accurate. (Initially established in New York Times v. Sullivan.)<p>You can of course sue for any reason but winning is another matter.<p>(It&#x27;s easier if the defendant is <i>not</i> a public figure, but still, in general, it&#x27;s relatively hard to win libel suits in the US.)')
