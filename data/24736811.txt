Item(by='tzs', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602306505, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24735817, text='Being pro gay rights has not really been reasonably mainstream in the US for quite a while now.<p>Overall, the majority has been pro gay rights since around 2004. By 2017 it was 70% for, 24% against. Breaking down by party in 2017, Democrats were 83% for, Republicans 54% for.<p>Women 73% for, men 66% for. White 73%, Hispanic 70%, Black 63%.<p>81% postgrad, 77% college grad, 66% some college, 64% high school or less.<p>83% 18-29, 79% 30-49, 65% 50-64, 58% 65+. See [1].<p>When it comes to same sex marriage, by 2019 61% overall approved. That&#x27;s been above 50% since 2013, and above the percentage opposed since 2011.<p>Republicans still aren&#x27;t majority in favor, at 44%. Democrats are at 75%.<p>By religion, 79% of the unaffiliated are in favor, 66% of white mainline protestants, 61% of Catholics, 44% of Black protestants, and only 29% of white evangelical protestants.<p>66% of women, 57% of men. 62% of whites, 58% of Hispanics, and 51% of Blacks. 74% of Millennials, 58% Gen-X, 51% Boomers, and 45% of the Silent Generation.
