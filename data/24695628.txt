Item(by='DougBTX', descendants=None, kids=[24695903], score=None, time=1601971532, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24695328, text='Bitwise or works at the level of bits, so the question is more like, in:<p><pre><code>    &gt;&gt;&gt; bin(0b011 | 0b010)\n    &#x27;0b11&#x27;\n</code></pre>\nDoes the leading 1 come from the first argument or the second argument? It isn&#x27;t really a sensible question for bits, since the 1s are indistinguishable. However the values in the hash table are distinguishable, so it&#x27;s a valid question.<p>Also, taking an example from another comment:<p><pre><code>    &gt;&gt;&gt; {0} | {False}\n    {0}\n    &gt;&gt;&gt; {False} | {0}\n    {False}\n</code></pre>\nThis is another example where the union operator is used, but here it picks the first value, rather than the second value.')
