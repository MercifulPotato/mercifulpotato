Item(by='geocrasher', descendants=None, kids=[25898349], score=None, time=1611539456, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25898045, text='If MS were to write a new bespoke OS that requires new everything and maintains no backward compatibility (that&#x27;s the point of a new OS, isn&#x27;t it?) and launch it to the world, the amount of people who&#x27;d rush out and buy it can be counted on one hand. It could be absolutely revolutionary, but it would lack one major thing:<p>Momentum.<p>Look at every OS that&#x27;s on the market right now. It solves a problem. Right now there are very few people who can&#x27;t pick an OS off the shelf that will solve their particular problem. Be it an RTOS or a fancy gui that your grandma is comfortable with, the software already exists.<p>I guess what I&#x27;m really trying to say is that unless you can upend the current OS landscape while maintaining compatibilty, then a new OS for the masses doesn&#x27;t solve a problem. It creates one.')
