Ability to change rules more rapidly and in a way that better suits local conditions.<p>2. Greater interntational cooperation and coordination.<p>The problem is that although 1. sounds like an unadulterated benefit it isn&#x27;t always. It&#x27;s more likely to result in rules that swing rapidly between approaches (as government changes especially in a system like the UK&#x27;s) and also probably more susceptible to regulatory capture.<p>So it&#x27;s not really abour &#x27;running better&#x27; - it&#x27;s about stability at the expense of a degree of responsiveness.')
