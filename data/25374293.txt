Item(by='Silhouette', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607612266, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25373816, text='That sort of clause is pretty much universally challenged by anyone with a bit of experience and credibility, too, at least here in the UK. And so it should be. That goes whether we&#x27;re talking about inventions (which normally means patents) or other intellectual property such as copyright (which appears to be what we&#x27;re talking about in OP&#x27;s situation).<p>&quot;In the course of your employment&quot; or similar words used to be a more reasonable standard that was widely considered fair to both sides for salaried employees. In other words, things you do on company time, using company resources and&#x2F;or in the company&#x27;s line of business go to the company, but they have no claim beyond that. I haven&#x27;t spoken to a lawyer about this recently, so if the issue might affect you, please check whether this is still correct and don&#x27;t just rely on my comment here.')
