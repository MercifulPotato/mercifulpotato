Item(by='compiler-guy', descendants=None, kids=[25269846], score=None, time=1606852562, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25267847, text='The paper he cites, Waldfogel&#x27;s &quot;The Deadweight Loss of Christmas&quot; omits one of the most valuable results of gift exchange. Namely, it only considers the intrinsic value of the object give, and <i>not</i> any goodwill or pleasant feelings that the mere act of giving and receiving itself provides.<p>In other words, by focusing purely on the material value of the object given, the paper systematically undervalues the benefits of the entire act itself.<p>Waldfogel himself noted this in a follow up.')
