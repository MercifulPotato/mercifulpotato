Item(by='sleavey', descendants=None, kids=[25144852], score=None, time=1605730550, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25137235, text='I can understand why the tech giants are getting involved in mapping. There are some really cool things being built with OSM and other data, like OpenRouteService [1] (Google Maps navigation for OSM), OpenTopoMap [2] (space shuttle topological data overlaid as contours on OSM), ORBIS [3] (Google Maps navigation for the Roman road network), and loads of detailed maps using OSM data directly but with a particular purpose like OpenRailwayMap [4] and OpenInfrastructureMap [5].<p>I&#x27;d go beyond what the article says about OSM being the atlas to Wikipedia&#x27;s encyclopedia: I&#x27;d say it&#x27;s becoming the spatial knowledge base equivalent to nothing seen before in history. This truly is an awesome, revolutionary tool that has shown its true potential. I look forward to the awesome things we&#x27;ve not even thought of being built with OSM data, and, I hope, all continuing to be free.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;maps.openrouteservice.org&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;maps.openrouteservice.org&#x2F;</a><p>[2] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;opentopomap.org&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;opentopomap.org&#x2F;</a><p>[3] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;orbis.stanford.edu&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;orbis.stanford.edu&#x2F;</a><p>[4] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.openrailwaymap.org&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.openrailwaymap.org&#x2F;</a><p>[5] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;openinframap.org&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;openinframap.org&#x2F;</a>')
