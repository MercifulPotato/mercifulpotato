Startups with too much venture capital are not a new thing.<p>The Teleharmonium was the first musical synthesizer. The approach was to have one tone generator for each note, and switchgear run from a keyboard. That&#x27;s a reasonable idea, and electric organs of the 1950s to 1970s worked that way.<p>But this was before amplifiers. They wanted to distribute the music over telephone wires to speakers in hotels, and needed power. So each tone generator was a sizable electrical generator. The whole installation was building-sized.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;i0.wp.com&#x2F;120years.net&#x2F;wordpress&#x2F;wp-content&#x2F;uploads&#x2F;2013&#x2F;09&#x2F;scientific-american-v96-n10-1907-03-09-1.png" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;i0.wp.com&#x2F;120years.net&#x2F;wordpress&#x2F;wp-content&#x2F;uploads&#x2F;...</a>')
