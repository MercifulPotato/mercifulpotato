Item(by='mlthoughts2018', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606222326, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25196950, text='&gt; “ Regarding recommender systems I see many companies trying neural nets and so many other fancy ML stuff for things that - in AB-tests are always outperformed by basic rules.”<p>I work on large scale recommender systems for an ecommerce company and in my career I’ve seen only the exact opposite.<p>Don’t get me wrong, sometimes simpler ML models, like clustering LSA vectors or nearest neighbors, work better than complex models like neural nets.<p>But I have never seen plain rule systems work better for any problem even remotely at scale. Rule systems give an illusory sense of control and understanding, yet are rife with complex interaction effects and edge cases that typically make them intractable to change.')
