Item(by='hardwaresofton', descendants=None, kids=[25354434, 25354538, 25355373, 25354951, 25355003], score=None, time=1607478634, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25346965, text='Granted IANAL, can someone explain how the AGPL prevents aaS providing of _unmodified_ AGPL-licensed software?<p>Reading the license text[0], the prominent section related to remote network interaction very clearly notes &quot;if you modify the Program&quot;, and the terms and conditions lay out that &quot;Mere interaction with a user through a computer network, with no transfer of a copy, is not conveying&quot;.<p>When people discuss not being able to offer AGPL-licensed programs as a service, are they exclusively talking about modifications?<p>Another point is the requirement to only provide source to your users, who might be bound by something else, like an EULA that prohibits them from using the AGPL software you give to them to run a competing service, for example.<p>I guess until this stuff gets tested in a court of law, everyone is reacting to the threat of litigation more than actual litigation but it feels like the threat to companies is overblown, except if they try to improve the software and not contribute back.<p>[0]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.gnu.org&#x2F;licenses&#x2F;agpl-3.0.en.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.gnu.org&#x2F;licenses&#x2F;agpl-3.0.en.html</a>')
