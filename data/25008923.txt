Item(by='crazygringo', descendants=None, kids=[25010338], score=None, time=1604680804, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25008351, text='Multiple people here are disagreeing and saying no, if everyone does what they can it <i>does</i> make a difference.<p>I think a better perspective is this: the <i>impact per time unit</i> of clean code is vanishingly tiny.<p>If you actually care about the environment, then it&#x27;s <i>orders of magnitude</i> more effective to write &quot;dirty&quot; code, and then use the free time it gives you to do things that actually make a difference: volunteering, lobbying, raising awareness, writing to representatives, working and donating the $ to environmental groups, etc.<p>The question isn&#x27;t about whether something is a net positive or not, it never has been. The question is, <i>is it the biggest positive you can achieve with your limited time&#x2F;resources</i>.<p>Which is where the author is right -- focusing on clean code (unless you&#x27;re Netflix-scale) is a massive time and energy suck away from action that can make a much larger difference.')
