Item(by='mostlystatic', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606337977, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25213630, text='Since Indie Hackers generally don&#x27;t take external funding, all the risk is concentrated on the founders. That makes it attractive to focus on established product categories with known demand.<p>Funded startups are in a better position to take risks and work on innovative products. You don&#x27;t know if you&#x27;re building the right thing, and even if you are your potential customers might not see the value right away. Funding allows founders to take a salary while trying to find a market.<p>I had lots innovative ideas I started working on my product. But in practice the few users I had were all asking for the same types of standard functionality. So I started trying to more directly compete with existing successful businesses.')
