Item(by='barmstrong', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605037591, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25047550, text='Here is the study <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.thelancet.com&#x2F;journals&#x2F;lanpsy&#x2F;article&#x2F;PIIS2215-0366(20)30462-4&#x2F;fulltext" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.thelancet.com&#x2F;journals&#x2F;lanpsy&#x2F;article&#x2F;PIIS2215-0...</a><p>The study has this line which caught my attention:<p>&gt; The incidence of any psychiatric diagnosis in the 14 to 90 days after COVID-19 diagnosis was 18·1% (95% CI 17·6–18·6), including 5·8% (5·2–6·4) that were a first diagnosis.<p>So only 5.8% of people got a first diagnosis, and the remainder of the 18% were people who had been previously diagnosed with one of these conditions.<p>This seems to contradict what is written in the linked article:<p>&gt; In the three months following testing positive for COVID-19, 1 in 5 survivors were recorded as having a first time diagnosis of anxiety, depression or insomnia.<p>So it seems like the linked article has a bit of hyperbole in it.')
