Advertising, especially web advertising, allows us to pay for services not with money but our attention. When the Economist polled people how much they would pay to forego search engines, the result was over $17,000 [1]. It&#x27;d be simplistic to say that this is what Google could charge in a subscription model, but the point is that the value is very high.<p>Is a world where poorer students who can&#x27;t afford google have to compete against wealthier students with google a better world than one with web ads? I think it&#x27;s easy to focus on the bad, the privacy issues and dark patterns that web ads incentivize, to the exclusion of the fact that it has expanded access to incredibly powerful services to people of all means (well, all means as long as they have access to the internet).<p>1. <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.economist.com&#x2F;graphic-detail&#x2F;2018&#x2F;04&#x2F;25&#x2F;how-much-would-you-pay-to-keep-using-google" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.economist.com&#x2F;graphic-detail&#x2F;2018&#x2F;04&#x2F;25&#x2F;how-much...</a>')
