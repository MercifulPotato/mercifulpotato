Item(by='fastball', descendants=None, kids=[25617331], score=None, time=1609626387, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25617098, text='Not really true. If two things are <i>strictly</i> correlative, that means there is an additional factor causing both.<p>If you eliminate all reasonable additional factors (by controlling variables), you can demonstrate causation. Arguing that there can be unknowable external factors behind everything is not very scientific.<p>Identifying causal relationships involving humans is difficult due to the excessively multivariate nature of all our interactions, and by extension how difficult it is to &quot;control&quot; humans (as opposed to water, or a wheel). That does not mean it is impossible to ever demonstrate causation.')
