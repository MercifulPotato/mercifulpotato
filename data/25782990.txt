If you look at a 4th Edition Wizard, they have at-will, encounter and daily powers, just like any other 4e class. Some of the spells feel like they haven&#x27;t changed at all from 3rd Edition.<p>Here&#x27;s 3.5th Edition magic missile: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.d20srd.org&#x2F;srd&#x2F;spells&#x2F;magicMissile.htm" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.d20srd.org&#x2F;srd&#x2F;spells&#x2F;magicMissile.htm</a><p>Here&#x27;s the same spell in 5th Edition: <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;5e.d20srd.org&#x2F;srd&#x2F;spells&#x2F;magicMissile.htm" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;5e.d20srd.org&#x2F;srd&#x2F;spells&#x2F;magicMissile.htm</a><p>Very similar. The 5th Edition version is a bit simpler to read&#x2F;understand, but frankly if you sit there at the table and think you&#x27;re casting the 3rd Edition magic missile you will get it right 8 times out of 10.<p>And then on page 20 of this quickstart rules, you can see the 4e magic missile, which is a very different spell altogether: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;issuu.com&#x2F;biggerstaffhosting&#x2F;docs&#x2F;quickstartrules" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;issuu.com&#x2F;biggerstaffhosting&#x2F;docs&#x2F;quickstartrules</a>')
