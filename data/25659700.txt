I&#x27;ve never used it but read a bit about it, here&#x27;s how you would use it:<p>- Install git-annex on your cloud<p>- Tell git-annex that you have a remote there<p>- Do your stuff on your local drive, push to the cloud when it&#x27;s done<p>- If some of the files are not needed locally, tell git-annex to drop them from your local drive. git-annex knows that there is another copy in a safe storage<p>- If you want to work on some files you don&#x27;t have, ask git-annex to transfer them to you.<p>The manual steps might be a bit tedious, so git-annex also has an assistant mode that is a good-enough copy of dropbox (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;git-annex.branchable.com&#x2F;assistant&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;git-annex.branchable.com&#x2F;assistant&#x2F;</a>). See the archival walkthrough, that seems to fit your use case (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;git-annex.branchable.com&#x2F;assistant&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;git-annex.branchable.com&#x2F;assistant&#x2F;</a>)<p>(I have more or less the same need, but haven&#x27;t committed to doing it just yet)')
