Item(by='HelloNurse', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605382667, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25093901, text='I recommend avoiding a role of &quot;Splunk Engineer&quot;, particularly in the long term, mainly because it&#x27;s a narrow role that is likely to become intolerably boring and limited quickly.<p>While Splunk is rather good, I don&#x27;t think there is enough depth and value in being a Splunk expert to sustain a whole career (like being an expert of SAP or C++ or some popular DBMS).<p>CTI is one of the good uses you can apply Splunk <i>and many more tools and methods</i> to; if you want to be a CTI expert you should transcend your current Splunk focus, or you&#x27;ll be the &quot;hero&quot; who keeps Splunk running despite budget problems and maintains the data sources and dashboards that someone actually important asks for.')
