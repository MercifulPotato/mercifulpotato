Item(by='fidelramos', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607990259, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25422267, text='From the article:<p>&gt; Each A2DP service, of possibly many, is designed to uni-directionally transfer an audio stream in up to 2 channel stereo, either to or from the Bluetooth host.<p>This matches what I has heard: Bluetooth devices with A2DP profile can either receive or send high-quality audio, but not both at the same time.<p>I don&#x27;t know if there are Bluetooth headsets that have separate A2DP devices for input and output sound, I have never heard about them, but I would be very interested in knowing of their existence.<p>Also from the article:<p>&gt; These systems often also implement Headset (HSP) or Hands-Free (HFP) profiles for telephone calls, which may be used separately.<p>This is the common case for Bluetooth headsets, the Bluetooth manager switches profile when the microphone is needed, but that greatly reduces audio quality.')
