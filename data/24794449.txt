Item(by='klyrs', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602798302, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24783780, text='Superconductors aren&#x27;t magic.  You can&#x27;t just stuff a few million amps into a tiny wire.  Just as there is a critical temperature, there&#x27;s also a critical current density.  While those two numbers are related, you&#x27;ll need quite a bit more than &quot;room temperature&quot; superconductors to carry currents like that.  And there&#x27;s a temperature&#x2F;current tradeoff: if the ambient temperature is close to the critical temperature, then a whisp of current can make it go normal.<p><a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;hyperphysics.phy-astr.gsu.edu&#x2F;hbase&#x2F;Solids&#x2F;scbc2.html" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;hyperphysics.phy-astr.gsu.edu&#x2F;hbase&#x2F;Solids&#x2F;scbc2.html</a>')
