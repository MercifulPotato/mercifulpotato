You also quickly start running into the Hidden Node[1] problem or the N^2 aspect of channel congestion(which is why LoRA has such low throughput).<p>Cell companies spend billions on this and even then you&#x27;ll still have gaps outside of the metro areas, and they&#x27;re using much more advanced modulations like CDMA and FDM&#x2F;TDM with a central coordinator.<p>I&#x27;ve talked with a fair number of people who maintain commercial repeaters in mountain areas and those can be incredibly difficult(combination of hard access in winter, large temperature swings and theft of solar panels&#x2F;batteries) so which it might be possible, my guess is the level of investment just doesn&#x27;t pencil out for the usage.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Hidden_node_problem" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Hidden_node_problem</a>')
