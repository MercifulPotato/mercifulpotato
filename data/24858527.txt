Item(by='whatshisface', descendants=None, kids=[24858664, 24858715, 24858901], score=None, time=1603377879, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24858484, text='That&#x27;s an interesting example of how partisan-seeming biases can develop inadvertently. There may be an objective reason why the ACLU doesn&#x27;t support armed demonstrations, but there&#x27;s only one side that likes to bring guns to their protests, so in practice this only cancels support for one side. Reminds me of the &quot;anyone is equally free to marry a person of the opposite gender&quot; response to gay people having the same rights.<p>The ACLU may or may not want to support armed demonstrations, but if they want &quot;docket diversity,&quot; they&#x27;d pragmatically have to loosen that rule, perhaps to &quot;we don&#x27;t support demonstrations where weapons were fired,&quot; or even, &quot;we defend demonstrators in all cases except when they admit to specifically trying to shoot someone,&quot; or something like that.')
