Item(by='ivan_ah', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609889862, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25650907, text='The Python package `shortuuid` makes working with UUIDs a little easier by encoding them as strings:\n<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;skorokithakis&#x2F;shortuuid#usage" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;skorokithakis&#x2F;shortuuid#usage</a>\n(uses base-57 encoding, with alphabet consisting of A-Za-z0-9 with potentially confusing symbols skipped)<p>The string representation is what you show to users, but under the hood it&#x27;s still a UUID and compatible&#x2F;interoperable with any other system that needs UUID-shaped identifiers.<p>The coolest part is youcan even truncate the string encodings to get shorter IDs, which correspond to UUIDs with lots of leading zeros.')
