Item(by='BurningFrog', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603847876, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24912148, text='Your productivity, as measured by how much value you bring to the employer, does influence your salary, by setting a ceiling for it.<p>Concretely, if your work adds $300k&#x2F;year to company revenue, it will at most pay you that much.<p>Of course, it prefers to pay you less, and to get close to that number it&#x27;s best to be part of a big enough labor market that you can get job offers from several employers.<p>Here&#x27;s the thing: In a mainly remote programmer labor market, that we seem to be entering, all employers in the entire US are in your local labor market, so that should work out fine.<p>What I just described <i>is</i> &quot;supply and demand&quot;, with a bit of detail.')
