Item(by='Centigonal', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609136068, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25556578, text='When I was doing my physics undergrad, my friends and I would always joke about how some professors (and some papers!) would use &quot;it trivially follows...&quot; in the same way. When an approximation or step in a proof was particularly hairy or weird, that phrase kept cropping up -- and why not? It handwaves away the step while simultaneously discouraging any pesky questions (if it&#x27;s trivial, surely only a weaker student would ask about it).<p>Honorable mentions are: &quot;hence&quot; and &quot;the remainder is left as an exercise for the reader.&quot;')
