Item(by='ceejayoz', descendants=None, kids=[24817075], score=None, time=1602977861, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24812652, text='<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.nature.com&#x2F;articles&#x2F;d41586-020-02598-6" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.nature.com&#x2F;articles&#x2F;d41586-020-02598-6</a><p>&gt; Symptoms might take a long time to fade; a study posted on the preprint server medRxiv in August followed up on people who had been hospitalized, and found that even a month after being discharged, more than 70% were reporting shortness of breath and 13.5% were still using oxygen at home.<p>&gt; One study of 143 people with COVID-19 discharged from a hospital in Rome found that 53% had reported fatigue and 43% had shortness of breath an average of 2 months after their symptoms started. A study of patients in China showed that 25% had abnormal lung function after 3 months, and that 16% were still fatigued.')
