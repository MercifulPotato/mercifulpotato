Item(by='M2Ys4U', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608081107, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25436068, text='It will depend on the law.<p>Tax law favours a designated location for residency, for example.<p>The GDPR doesn&#x27;t even operate on the level of residency (at least for data subjects). You&#x27;re covered by the GDPR when you&#x27;re physically in an EU member state[0] <i>as regards your activity in that member state</i> or if the data controller&#x2F;processor is established in the EU. The UK GDPR will be the same, mutatis mutandis.<p>[0] Or somewhere where EU law applies by virtue of international law, like an embassy, an EU-flagged ship, an area of Antarctica claimed by an EU member state etc. etc.')
