I can choose between at least two providers in my apartment in Brooklyn,  NY.<p>But somebody in upstate suburbia  cannot, and they are lucky if their only provider is not utterly bad.<p>Density is key here. Suburbia is not dense enough, and is finicky enough about digging or running wires, to make building a competing network lucrative.  And the US has a ton of low-density suburban population, even in formally urban agglomerations like LA or Houston.<p>One solution is to live in a proper city, but proper cities are not very numerous in US, and living there is more expensive.')
