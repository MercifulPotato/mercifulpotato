Item(by='wruza', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603216307, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24839889, text='<i>If you’re a native speaker, the comma goes where you’d naturally have a brief pause in speech.</i><p>Some speech styles use pause after &quot;but&quot;. You can hear it from news reporters and on tv shows in general, when actors read partial sentences from paper or screen. It is not exclusive to english, and it is a common mistake to use punctuation with respect to own&#x2F;technical intonations and delays instead of correct ones.<p>&quot;X but, Y&quot; likely means &quot;X, but... Y&quot; here, i.e. the first pause is much less pronounced than the second.')
