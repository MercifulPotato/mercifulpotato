“<i>Why do we have a government instead of just having a workers union?</i>”.<p>This is actually the political project of anarcho-syndicalists [1]: workers could self-organize the production and the collective life through democratic federalist instances.\nThe idea has been theorized since at least 1906 in the Charter of Amiens [2], an historical text of syndicalism [3]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.marxists.org&#x2F;history&#x2F;france&#x2F;cgt&#x2F;charter-amiens.htm" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.marxists.org&#x2F;history&#x2F;france&#x2F;cgt&#x2F;charter-amiens.h...</a> where we can read “<i>the union, today a resistance group will be, in the future, a group for production and redistribution, the basis of social reorganization.</i>”.<p>At least in France, the biggest workers union (CGT) is still organized in a way that would allow this, by having both geographical and per-industry-branch instances (sorry I lack the proper words to describe this in English).<p>It is also what the Spanish CNT did during and after the Spnish Revolution of 1936 [4,5].<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Anarcho-syndicalism" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Anarcho-syndicalism</a><p>[2] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Charter_of_Amiens" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Charter_of_Amiens</a><p>[3] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Syndicalism" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Syndicalism</a><p>[4] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Spanish_Revolution_of_1936" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Spanish_Revolution_of_1936</a><p>[5] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Revolutionary_Catalonia" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Revolutionary_Catalonia</a>')
