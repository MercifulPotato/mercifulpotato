Item(by='ElFitz', descendants=None, kids=[25685457], score=None, time=1610112139, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25682124, text='Depends on the type of work you do and what the company pays you for. It is the means (your time)? Or the outcome?<p>Had quite a few clients and employers asking and haggling over trying to get it both ways.<p>If you consider a creative job where you only care about the output, then the more creative it is, the less (above an undetermined threshold) time spent working and output are correlated.<p>The means (again, time spent) not being an accurate predictor of the outcome, they don’t matter much, and there is no reason to tie the salary to them.<p>If you consider a non-creative (factory &#x2F; fruits picking) or vaguely creative (low-level factory-style coding, data input) job, then the less creative it is, the more time spent working and output are correlated (up to a physical &#x2F; mental exhaustion threshold).<p>Here, the means are a good predictor of the outcome, hence the salary being tied to the number of hours spent working.<p>It really depends ¯\\_(ツ)_&#x2F;¯')
