A country is governed by its leaders <i>for</i> its people. The leaders have a responsibility to the people to act on their behalf in their benefit.<p>Companies are managed by an executive team on behalf of, and for the benefit of, the shareholders. Not the employees.<p>Shareholders can, and do, criticise management and demand they do things differently&#x2F;better, because management is ultimately responsible to them. Likewise the people can, and do, criticse governments because the government is responsible to them.<p>Management are not responsible to employees. Though it makes sense to not annoy them too much. But if it&#x27;s a question of shareholder or employee satisfaction, then legally there&#x27;s no choice.')
