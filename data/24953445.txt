Item(by='_greim_', descendants=None, kids=[24953819, 24953721, 24954016], score=None, time=1604165741, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24951024, text='By now it should be obvious—and this is purely descriptive of what I see playing out, not prescriptive—that self-driving tech will have to clear a higher bar than just <i>statistically better</i> in order to become mainstream.<p>Not only because anecdotes carry more weight than statistics in the media—as this story illustrates—but because people are much less afraid of bad things happening, as long as they&#x27;re in control when it happens.<p>Also, I suspect everything associated with fuel-burning cars is by now irrevocably linked with cultural ideals of individualism and freedom. Resistance to EVs and self-driving tech will continually materialize, as if out of thin air, regardless of how well documented the benefits are. It&#x27;s going to be an uphill battle for the foreseeable future.')
