Item(by='theptip', descendants=None, kids=[25856597], score=None, time=1611210219, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25855695, text='I think it&#x27;s an open question; economics papers are being written in 2021 on the subject (see <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.dropbox.com&#x2F;s&#x2F;oplls6utgf7z6ih&#x2F;Pennington_JMP.pdf?dl=0" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.dropbox.com&#x2F;s&#x2F;oplls6utgf7z6ih&#x2F;Pennington_JMP.pdf...</a> for example from the article).<p>One confounding factor here is that it&#x27;s quite hard to draw anecdotal conclusions, because it is impossible to test the counterfactual; the YIMBY would make the case that rents would have gone up even more in Portland if the fishtanks were not built.<p>And the effect is quite hard to measure; that paper I linked says &quot;I find that monthly rents fall by $22.77 - $43.18 relative to trend, or roughly 1.2 - 2.3%, for people living within 500m of a new project&quot; -- this is probably not noticeable by you or me with anecdotal observations, but if the study is correct, and we committed to lots of new buildings, then it could make a noticeable difference in aggregate after lots of new projects were completed.')
