Item(by='PeterisP', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602495757, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24750886, text='It&#x27;s worth noting that in current conditions the assumptions may be unwarranted.<p>First, while storage <i>used</i> to be orders of magnitude slower than memory, not SSD storage is just a single order of magnitude slower;<p>Second, in many domains now it&#x27;s often practical to ensure that your data set can fit in memory. For example, if your system is for storing financial transactions (which is a prime market for Oracle), then your enterprise has to be quite large to get a terabyte of transactions and you can put a terabyte (or much more) of RAM in a database system if you choose to.')
