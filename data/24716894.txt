This is how I explain it.<p>1&#x2F;3 of the time, you guess correctly on your initial guess. If you switch, you&#x27;d be wrong.<p>2&#x2F;3 of the time, you guess incorrectly on your initial guess. If you switch, you&#x27;re right.<p>So when switching, the expecting outcome 1&#x2F;3 of the time is 0, but the expected outcome 2&#x2F;3 of the time 1. (1&#x2F;3)<i>0 + (2&#x2F;3)</i>1 = 2&#x2F;3')
