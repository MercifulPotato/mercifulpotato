Item(by='bobthepanda', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605287352, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25084016, text='To add onto the other point about American apps stealing tip: the problem is that DoorDash and some other apps were <i>deducting</i> tips from base pay for deliveries, so that even if you tipped the driver would be paid the same amount. <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.theverge.com&#x2F;2019&#x2F;7&#x2F;22&#x2F;20703434&#x2F;delivery-app-tip-pay-theft-doordash-amazon-flex-instacart" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.theverge.com&#x2F;2019&#x2F;7&#x2F;22&#x2F;20703434&#x2F;delivery-app-tip...</a><p>This is part of the reason why there&#x27;s this whole struggle to define what exactly the gig-economy worker contract is supposed to look like, because they&#x27;re not paid hourly and it&#x27;s often net negative for full-timers, who do exist.<p>Also to clarify a point made in the article; this is <i>not</i> the equivalent to a tipped wage. With a tip wage you already know what your base pay is up front, and tips are additive; what DoorDash was doing was deducting tip from some set base pay. Fortunately I live in a state that does not differentiate tipped and regular wage.')
