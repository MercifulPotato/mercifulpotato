Item(by='lumost', descendants=None, kids=[25871841], score=None, time=1611324274, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25867693, text='My 2 cents.<p>Compute and storage capabilities keep growing rapidly, if one structures their data well, uses a reasonable query processor, and some form of underlying columnar storage then computing calculations on TBs of data can be accomplished in seconds for low costs.<p>Being able to recompute the world from scratch is a p0 requirement for most analytic workloads, as otherwise migrations and un-forseen computation changes due to new product requirements and other activities become painful.<p>This leaves differential techniques in an awkward spot where to be effective they need to<p>1) Operate on vast quantities of data or sufficiently complex calculations such that optimization of compute is a concern to the end-user.<p>2) Operate in a computational environment that is sufficiently constrained such that all present and future changes to the computation can be reasonably accounted for.<p>3) Be transparent enough that engineers don&#x27;t feel that they are duplicating logic.<p>It&#x27;s hard to think of applications that would meet this criteria outside of intelligent caching of computation within DB engines.')
