Item(by='deergomoo', descendants=None, kids=[24745897], score=None, time=1602425054, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24745788, text='This was quite a popular technique to achieve fancy video effects on low powered hardware.<p>A good example is the various parallax and scrolling effects in the intro cinematic to Link’s Awakening on the Game Boy&#x2F;Color, achieved by changing various hardware scroll registers in the horizontal blanking interval (time between one line being finished and the other starting). The main limitation is that you can only do horizontal effects, not vertical.<p>I’m wondering if the article you’re thinking of was the Wired piece on the Atari 2600? That famously ran all of its game logic in the H- and V-blank intervals.')
