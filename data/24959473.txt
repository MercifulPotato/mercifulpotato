Item(by='rswail', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604238479, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24959431, text='Increased government spending now means greater national debt. Governments get money by printing it. They get revenue by taxation. They can borrow by issuing debt instruments.<p>A recovering economy and greater economic activity leads to greater tax revenue without necessarily <i>increasing</i> taxation rates.<p>This is a natural &quot;budget stabilizer&quot; effect that happens during recessions and expansions.<p>Debt funding is currently at historically low, almost zero&#x2F;negative rates. Australia&#x27;s debt-to-GDP is relatively low (42% prior to the current recession), our debt ratings are AAA.')
