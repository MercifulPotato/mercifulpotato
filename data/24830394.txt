Item(by='to11mtm', descendants=None, kids=[24831179], score=None, time=1603138103, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24829440, text='&gt; Corrections welcome...<p>You&#x27;re cool.<p>Most think ML[0] did it first, and dare I say did it right first. It gets rid of entire categories of boilerplate (interfaces come to mind) because types are fully inferred. In other words, if `funpost(request)` uses .User, .Body, .Time, from request, the only requirement is that the &#x27;request&#x27; object has those properties with correct types further down the chain in usage. Other languages can do this but not typically to the degree in ML&#x27;s type system (known as Hindley-Milner [1]). There are times it can&#x27;t quite figure it out but it is, again, less frequent than in others.<p>[0] - <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;ML_(programming_language)" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;ML_(programming_language)</a>\n[1] - <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Hindley%E2%80%93Milner_type_system" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Hindley%E2%80%93Milner_type_sy...</a>')
