Item(by='caffeine', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610453002, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25736025, text='This article sort of skipped over the actual &quot;gamma&quot; part.<p>When the banks buy stocks to hedge their calls, they push the price up.  Why does that create a feedback loop?<p>Because the price going up makes each call more risky, which means they need to buy <i>more stock</i> for each call on their books (including the ones they already hedged).<p>That effect, the relationship between the price of the underlying and how much you hedge, is &quot;gamma&quot;.')
