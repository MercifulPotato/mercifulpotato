Item(by='def_true_false', descendants=None, kids=[24980666], score=None, time=1604417208, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24977603, text='Price controls will only result in low or no supply. If a country limits prices of, say, respirators, it will work for a moment. But then retailers will sell their inventory and, unable to charge more, they will be outbid by those in countries without price controls.\nThen people who really need the goods, e.g. the immunocompromised, can&#x27;t get them, even if they are willing to pay the market price.<p>Here is an interesting podcast episode about this phenomenon in an area hit by a hurricane: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.econtalk.org&#x2F;munger-on-price-gouging&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.econtalk.org&#x2F;munger-on-price-gouging&#x2F;</a><p>This is also what happened in a number of European countries due to the pandemic.')
