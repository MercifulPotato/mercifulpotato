Item(by='fractionalhare', descendants=None, kids=[25828340, 25829916, 25828244], score=None, time=1611009767, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25827546, text='That&#x27;s not necessarily true, it depends. For example risk parity is common knowledge but it still beats the market. You don&#x27;t really need any secret sauce to use it effectively. You could do it, personally, and you would probably do well.<p>However if your strategies are well known people typically won&#x27;t pay you much (if anything) to manage their money, because a bunch of shops will be offering comparable results with the same thing.<p>Continuing with risk parity: there are walkthroughs of how this works with code and math available online: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cryptm.org&#x2F;posts&#x2F;2020&#x2F;08&#x2F;01&#x2F;parity.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cryptm.org&#x2F;posts&#x2F;2020&#x2F;08&#x2F;01&#x2F;parity.html</a><p>Note the alpha, beta, volatility and Sharpe measures comparing a straightforward risk parity strategy to SPY.<p>It&#x27;s not controversial to anyone in the actual industry that you can beat the market on a risk-adjusted basis. Very often the techniques for doing that are well known and can be levered up to safely beat SPY on a total basis with less overall risk. What&#x27;s truly difficult (and secret) is beating the market by several standard deviations.')
