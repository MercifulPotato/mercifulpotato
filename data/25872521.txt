Item(by='wpietri', descendants=None, kids=[25873779], score=None, time=1611329841, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25871980, text='Eric Ries, the guy who coined the term, has 2 categories: pivots and leaps. The analogy he used is basketball footwork. If you keep one foot planted, it&#x27;s a pivot.  Reviewing the article:<p>1 -&gt; 2: Pivot, as it&#x27;s still in sales.<p>2 -&gt; 3: Pivot, as they kept the sales focus and &quot;predictive analytics&quot;.<p>3 -&gt; 4: Seems like more of a leap.<p>4 -&gt; 5: Pivot, as it&#x27;s still developer surveys.<p>5 -&gt; 6: Leap.<p>Both are fine, honestly. But one should favor pivots over leaps, as you have less to re-do. A leap is a desperation ploy.')
