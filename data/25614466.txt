Item(by='Jugurtha', descendants=None, kids=[25615680], score=None, time=1609608849, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25614175, text='&gt;<i>Well meaning no doubt, but relationship quality just doesn&#x27;t lend itself to quantisation like that.</i><p>Granted, but sometimes you can find proxies and variables that increase the likelihood of success if you hit them consistently. For example, what&#x27;s &quot;successfully land an aircraft&quot;? It&#x27;s a sequence of hitting certain parameters within certain time windows that, when you do that, result in a smooth, successful, landing. Successful landing is a &quot;lagging indicator&quot;.<p>There are many things, even in &quot;artistic performance&quot;, that are a sequence of consistently hitting a target within a certain tolerance, at a specific time, etc.<p>In the workplace, you may have the problem of having a quality relationship with your colleagues or &quot;reports&quot;. You may not be able to &quot;quantify&quot; that easily, but you can have regular one on ones in a relaxed enough setting that lead to candid conversations that unearth problems early enough that you can effectively solve them. The relationship quality is a lagging indicator, if you will, of what has been done upstream.<p>What do you think?')
