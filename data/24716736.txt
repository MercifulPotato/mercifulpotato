Item(by='seibelj', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602141775, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24716289, text='The Norwegian EV incentives:<p>No purchase&#x2F;import taxes (1990-)\nExemption from 25% VAT on purchase (2001-)\nNo annual road tax (1996-)\nNo charges on toll roads or ferries (1997- 2017).\nMaximum 50% of the total amount on ferry fares for electric vehicles (2018-)\nMaximum 50% of the total amount on toll roads (2019)\nFree municipal parking (1999- 2017)\nParking fee for EVs was introduced locally with an upper limit of a maximum 50% of the full price (2018-)\nAccess to bus lanes (2005-).\nNew rules allow local authorities to limit the access to only include EVs that carry one or more passengers (2016)\n50 % reduced company car tax (2000-2018).\nCompany car tax reduction reduced to 40% (2018-)\nExemption from 25% VAT on leasing (2015)\nFiscal compensation for the scrapping of fossil vans when converting to a zero-emission van (2018)\nAllowing holders of driver licence class B to drive electric vans class C1 (light lorries) up to 4250 kg (2019)<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;elbil.no&#x2F;english&#x2F;norwegian-ev-policy&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;elbil.no&#x2F;english&#x2F;norwegian-ev-policy&#x2F;</a><p>The fact EVs are successful is great! But don’t pretend it isn’t because taxpayers subsidize their  success.')
