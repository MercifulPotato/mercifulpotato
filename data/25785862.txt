Originally it could return larger amounts of arbitrary data, which was used to effectively store external data on-chain.   It was later reduced down to only allow 40 bytes, which would be enough for a hash, but not enough to store larger amounts of state.<p>This was what Vitalik Buterin and others were using for things like scripting and colored coins, and Vitalik cited (concerns that devs were threatening to constrain it &#x2F; remove it as a major reason he started Ethereum.<p>---<p>Vitalik about OP_RETURN (wish I had link w&#x2F; more context) - <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;twitter.com&#x2F;vitalikbuterin&#x2F;status&#x2F;929808394487320577" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;twitter.com&#x2F;vitalikbuterin&#x2F;status&#x2F;929808394487320577</a>')
