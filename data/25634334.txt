Item(by='srean', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609780227, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25632308, text='They do serve some level of balance against the &#x27;frog of the well&#x27; [alt. king of the hill] effect.<p>Smart kids often come away thinking they are the smartest and that they don&#x27;t need to learn or understand anything else, thanks to our cheer leaders. This can lead to cognitive and behavioral fallacies, expertise in some narrow field gives themselves the idea that they are smart and qualified in domains outside of the scope of their expertise. Meeting other smart people, exposure to domains outside of one&#x27;s own scope of expertise tempers that to a degree.<p>In retrospect the most valuable thing about my college&#x2F;university experience was meeting people from different backgrounds (financial, social, educational). High school was too homogeneous.')
