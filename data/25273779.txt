Item(by='pigscantfly', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606890608, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25273695, text='I would check out Michael Genesereth&#x27;s course on general game playing [1] -- I don&#x27;t remember if we specifically implemented a chess engine, but it&#x27;s more or less exactly what you&#x27;re looking for. Once you&#x27;ve mastered that, I&#x27;d move on to Mykel Kochenderfer&#x27;s series on decision making under uncertainty [2], which extends to more state of the art approaches to the problem (ie. MCTS + deep Q learning + better encoders, which is basically what got us AlphaGo, AlphaStar, Atari agents, etc.). Once you&#x27;ve got that down (or before), you should start reading whitepapers.<p>Genesereth and Kochenderfer also have books on the subject; Kochenderfer&#x27;s is extremely good (I haven&#x27;t read all of Genesereth&#x27;s).<p>[1] <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;ggp.stanford.edu&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;ggp.stanford.edu&#x2F;</a><p>[2] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;web.stanford.edu&#x2F;class&#x2F;aa228&#x2F;cgi-bin&#x2F;wp&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;web.stanford.edu&#x2F;class&#x2F;aa228&#x2F;cgi-bin&#x2F;wp&#x2F;</a> and <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;web.stanford.edu&#x2F;class&#x2F;aa229&#x2F;cgi-bin&#x2F;wp&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;web.stanford.edu&#x2F;class&#x2F;aa229&#x2F;cgi-bin&#x2F;wp&#x2F;</a>')
