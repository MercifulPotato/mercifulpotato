Item(by='shadowgovt', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603739560, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24896650, text='We used to sign off on planning discussions in a team I was in with the phrase &quot;Unless, of course, none of that happens because I quit this job and embrace the life of a humble dairy farmer.&quot;<p>The joke was twofold: first was the things the author discusses here, but second was enough of us had come from farms to know how little doing that would actually improve things. Schedule drudgery is nothing like the work of getting up at the crack of dawn to tend a farm. Pivots and rescheduling is nothing like the uncertainty of losing two-thirds of your crops to a bad season or half your cattle to a disease outbreak. if you hate corporate bureaucracy, try dealing with the EPA or the farm subsidy programs.')
