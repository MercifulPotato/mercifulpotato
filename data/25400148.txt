Item(by='detaro', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607796320, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25399352, text='If code had instrumentation for every variable someone ever looked at with a debugger it would be unreadable because it&#x27;d be buried under instrumentation code, configuration to only enable instrumentation when needed, ... That&#x27;s not to say instrumentation&#x2F;logging&#x2F;... aren&#x27;t useful tools that can be more appropriate, but &quot;never use a debugger&quot; seems like a misguided dogma that seems surprisingly common. Part of the strength of a debugger is being able to look at all the things to find the ones worth a closer look, without having to add instrumentation or logging to every single one.<p>&gt; <i>by adding [...] comments, [...] etc</i><p>If you need a debugger, those are not there or not good enough, and you can only meaningfully add them <i>after</i> you understand what&#x27;s happening.<p>(EDIT: there is also a wide variety in what people count as a &quot;debugger&quot; further muddying the debate)')
