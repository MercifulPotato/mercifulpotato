Item(by='danaris', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611932894, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25957825, text='I think it&#x27;s about the difference between having your goal be &quot;growth&quot; and having your goal be &quot;(grow to) have X customers and Y employees&quot; or &quot;(grow to) have $X profit per year&quot;.<p>In the latter case, growth is a means to an end—a specific goal that is (at least potentially) achievable, and can yield a sustainable, profitable steady-state for the foreseeable future. In the former case, growth is seen as an end in itself—or, to the extent that it&#x27;s a means, the end is simply &quot;more profit, forever&quot;.')
