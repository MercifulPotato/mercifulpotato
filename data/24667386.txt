Item(by='evanb', descendants=None, kids=[24674248], score=None, time=1601675521, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24666848, text='Chemical bonds are on the scale of eV---the cost to liberate an electron from a hydrogen atom is 13.6eV, for example.  So the best you can hope for, in a chemical reaction, is a change in mass of the scale of 10s of eV &#x2F; c^2.<p>In comparison, a single proton weighs 938 MeV = 9.38e+8 eV.  So the fraction of the mass that is converted into energy is on the scale of 1e-7.')
