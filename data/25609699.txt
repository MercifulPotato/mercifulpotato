Item(by='mattkrause', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609554391, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25607208, text='And in a similar vein...<p>Douglas Prasher did a lot of the foundational work on GFP (2010 Nobel Prize) was driving a courtesy shuttle at a Toyota dealership in 2008 because he ran out of funding.<p>Barry Marshall and Robin Warren had a really tough time convincing people that H. pylori caused stomachs ulcers (but won the 2005 Nobel Prize).<p>Number theory was famously nothing more than an intellectual curiosity, but makes stuff like this website possible. Microbial opsins were also a weird intellectual backwater before becoming vital to neuroscience. Paul Lauterbur famously quipped that &quot;you could write the entire history of science in the last 50 years in terms of papers rejected by Science or Nature.&quot;<p>We are <i>really bad</i> at predicting the future impact of projects and people, and it&#x27;s important to recognize that as a counterweight to schemes that try to select for &quot;excellence.&quot; Things that seem laughable at the time can also be the basis for future breakthroughs, so take stupid things like Rand Pauls&#x27; ranting about silly-sounding projects with a mountain of salt.')
