Item(by='timoth3y', descendants=None, kids=[25168529, 25168282, 25167612], score=None, time=1605922457, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25148942, text='I started my first tech startup in Japan in 1998 and have built several since them.<p>For the last 20 years there has been a steady stream of articles on how Japanese web design is &quot;broken&quot;, or &quot;behind&quot;, or &quot;cluttered&quot;.<p>There is also a steady steam of UI experts arriving from the US telling Japanese companies they need to modernize their look and feel.<p>The thing is, e-commerce sites like Rakuten are already heavily A&#x2F;B tested and optimized. That graphic experience is common because it is most effective. Any analysis that does not focus on actual, observed user behavior is simply navel-gazing.<p>That said, the ubiquity of iPhone and Android UI is causing a shift in Japanese UI&#x2F;UX design. People are becoming used to that as the primary interface.')
