The ability to determine distance and velocity allows the trajectory and dynamics of the instrument to be computed with high precision.<p>To see one application of this information, look for discussions of the &quot;Pioneer Anomaly&quot;. That particular effect is now understood, but had it held up to detailed systematic-effect analysis, it would have affected our understanding of gravitation on solar-system scales.<p>When one enters entirely new territory, the understanding derived from single reliable measurements can be quite substantial.')
