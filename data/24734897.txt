Item(by='znpy', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602281140, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24731750, text='Uh, it&#x27;s nice to see the name Actian come out for once.<p>My previous employer used Versant by Actian (now called Actian NoSQL) heavily.<p>It&#x27;s much more of a NoSQL database: it&#x27;s a real object oriented database. You don&#x27;t store tuples, you store objects. You don&#x27;t make queries with selection and projection, you make a cut of a graph of objects.<p>It&#x27;s insanely fast, multithreaded, has very good tooling, scales vertically very well, can do online schema evolution (class definition evolution, really).<p>Sadly it&#x27;s almost impossible to scale horizontally (I&#x27;d be glad to be proven wrong).<p>It&#x27;s basically what the industry needs to avoid the object-relational mismatch: an object oriented database.<p>But everybody only learns SQL...')
