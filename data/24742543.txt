Item(by='staticassertion', descendants=None, kids=[24743095], score=None, time=1602370856, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24742196, text='Saying &quot;all calories are the same&quot; ignores a few really important things.<p>1) It ignores the processes involved in burning fats, sugars, and proteins, which can heavily impact where your body prioritizes getting its energy (or storing it).<p>2) It ignores other health impacts of &#x27;calories&#x27; such as blood glucose levels, which absolutely will depend on what it is you&#x27;re eating and not just the calorie count. Sugars, fats, and carbs are also processed in different parts of your body - so you might be following a &quot;calories in calories out&quot; and giving yourself non-alcoholic fatty liver disease.<p>3) It conflates calories, as measured via completely artificial process, with human digestion.')
