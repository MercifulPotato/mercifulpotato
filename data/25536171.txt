Item(by='caf', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608900806, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25535902, text='When additional government spending doesn&#x27;t lead to inflation, one obvious reason is that it is utilising what would otherwise be excess unused&#x2F;idle capacity in the real economy.<p>It&#x27;s only when the spending exceeds the amount of otherwise idle capacity that inflation occurs, as too much money chases too little real production.<p>It&#x27;s evident that the economic shocks this year have idled a great deal of economic capacity.')
