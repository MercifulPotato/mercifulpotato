Item(by='tptacek', descendants=None, kids=[25885763, 25884222], score=None, time=1611421714, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25882499, text='A peeve of mine: pretty much all &quot;made outside the US&quot; messaging is sketchy, because one of the most important differences between stuff run in the US and stuff run outside the US is that NSA doesn&#x27;t need special permission to hack stuff run outside the US --- in fact, hacking stuff outside of the US is basically their whole charter.<p>I&#x27;m not saying you should purposefully select US technology in order to avoid surveillance, just that &quot;hosted or made in Switzerland&quot; messaging in privacy tools isn&#x27;t very meaningful, and takes advantage of an emotional reaction in prospective customers, not a rational one.')
