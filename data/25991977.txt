Item(by='35fbe7d3d5b9', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1612205457, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25991094, text='Careful here: while this may be true of <i>some</i> NoSQL stores that I&#x27;ve not used, this is a tarpit for the wide column datastores I&#x27;m familiar with (Dynamo, Cassandra, and others).<p>It&#x27;s very easy to get going quickly and find out that you&#x27;ve tripped over antipatterns, and now your database is &quot;a database, a Ruby app, and 30ms of latency on every call&quot;.  It&#x27;s easy to think &quot;I&#x27;ll model this later&quot; and end up hitting the database five times in a row to answer a question.<p>With these systems, up front modeling of your data model and access patterns is <i>essential</i> if the trade off you are trying to make (far less functionality for smooth performance at ridiculous scale) will ever make sense.')
