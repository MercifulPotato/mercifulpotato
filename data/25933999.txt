Item(by='megameter', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611781841, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25933800, text='A decent rule of thumb I sometimes apply is:<p>1. What I have could be formalized into a state machine.\n2. That state machine needs to be reused and re-entered.\n3. I want to apply inputs to the state machine with method calls.<p>Of course you can overapply the thought and end up with an enterprise architecture - that&#x27;s why YAGNI is important. But when a section builds up a bit of conceptual redundancy there&#x27;s usually a state machine that can be pulled out of it in OO form.<p>And I would certainly do it that way in Python, as well as other languages.')
