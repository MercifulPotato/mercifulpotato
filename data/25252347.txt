Item(by='gumby', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606731276, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25251222, text='And fixed-volume currencies are inherently deflationary (e.g. gold, Bitcoin, etc).  Actually gold is worse; back in the gold standard days economies suffered deflationary squeezes bunctuated by boom&#x2F;bust as new goldfield strikes occurred and the supply suddenly shot up (California, Yukon, Victoria, etc just to look at the mid-late 1800s).<p>You end up with a liquidity trap in the amount of money doesn’t increase&#x2F;decrease roughly in proportion to the size of economic activity.  Fortunately “roughly” as it’s hard to measure.')
