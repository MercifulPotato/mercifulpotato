Item(by='cblconfederate', descendants=None, kids=[24808281], score=None, time=1602917524, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24806195, text='<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;gdpr.eu&#x2F;gdpr-consent-requirements&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;gdpr.eu&#x2F;gdpr-consent-requirements&#x2F;</a><p>&gt; “Freely given” consent essentially means you have not cornered the data subject into agreeing to you using their data. For one thing, that means you cannot require consent to data processing as a condition of using the service. They need to be able to say no. According to Recital 42, “Consent should not be regarded as freely given if the data subject has no genuine or free choice or is unable to refuse or withdraw consent without detriment.”<p>&gt; 4. When assessing whether consent is freely given, utmost account shall be taken of whether, inter alia, the performance of a contract, including the provision of a service, is conditional on consent to the processing of personal data that is not necessary for the performance of that contract.')
