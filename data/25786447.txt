Item(by='DoreenMichele', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610679515, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25784744, text='One of the things you see is that companies that do in-person networking end up trapped where their network is. Moving can kill the company.<p>If you don&#x27;t want to be running a company in the SFBA, establishing your network remotely is probably the way to go. It won&#x27;t break if you decide to run it from someplace other than the SFBA at the end of the school session.<p>I&#x27;ve done a lot online for years. This has helped make my life portable and that&#x27;s helped me solve problems that should have been &quot;impossible&quot; to solve.<p>The pandemic helped set the standard that we can do a lot of things remotely that there used to be a lot of resistance to doing remotely. In the future, a remote network may be more valuable than an in person network. That idea just hasn&#x27;t yet caught on, but it&#x27;s possibly the wave of the future.')
