Item(by='theamk', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611805243, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25935548, text='We use python extensively, and I think python&#x27;s &quot;access control by convention&quot; is pretty great.<p>The reason for access control is not to protect from malicious programmers -- it is protect from programmers who don&#x27;t know better and from honest mistakes.<p>So all you need is a simple rule (&quot;don&#x27;t access private methods&quot;) and now everyone knows which fields are public (and thus are OK to use) and which fields can disappear any moment.<p>For extra enforcement, you can add pylint to your CI so it flags all the protected accesses. But really, a good team culture is often enough.')
