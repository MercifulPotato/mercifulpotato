This study would at most imply high incidence of unconscious racial biases among the population.<p>It could also be explained by employer preference for people who are more integrated in the cultural mainstream, which people with &quot;black&quot; names, or Chinese names, are less likely to be.<p>&gt;But to sweepingly declare there&#x27;s absolutely no evidence isn&#x27;t so much a claim of fact as a declaration of willful ignorance<p>To claim that there racism against black Americans is endemic, with the strongest evidence you were able to find being the incidence of bias with respect to job applicant names, shows a basic lack of regard for the truth.<p>Literally, your logic could be used to argue that &quot;racism against white Americans is endemic&quot; because there are studies like this one that shows police are 25 times more likely to shoot an unarmed white male than an unarmed black male:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.ncjrs.gov&#x2F;App&#x2F;Publications&#x2F;abstract.aspx?ID=267470" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.ncjrs.gov&#x2F;App&#x2F;Publications&#x2F;abstract.aspx?ID=2674...</a>')
