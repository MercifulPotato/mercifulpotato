A video talking about Russian interference via targeted facebook ads would be fine.<p>On the other hand, a video claiming Russian agents infiltrated thousands of voting centers with sleeper agents should probably not get through the filter.<p>Though as with any content filters, there will be edge cases, false positives, false negatives, etc. that will all pose a problem.<p>This is the fundamental problem of common user spaces on the web these days: a failure to impose standards will often result in a toxic environment. Yet attempting to impose standards is something of an arms-race game of whack-a-mole.<p>I think HN only manages the balance somewhat decently because the users themselves are also highly interested in productive conversation and mostly downvote -&gt; dead comments that are likely to provoke flaming instead of discourse.')
