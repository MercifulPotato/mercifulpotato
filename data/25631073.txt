Item(by='j1elo', descendants=None, kids=[25632168, 25631809], score=None, time=1609767254, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25630782, text='Realize how I oriented my question around how it might affect building an OSS community.<p>A MIT or Apache license <i>might</i> detract contributors, because essentially these licenses say &quot;your contribution is OSS today, but it might end up resulting as unintended free labor that helps build a commercial product of which you won&#x27;t be part of&quot;.<p>However some see GPL licenses as a guarantee that external contributions are <i>actually</i> contributions to the overall Open-Source community as a whole, because they cannot be turned into other non-OSS product without explicit permission of all people who built it.<p>So yes, copyleft is <i>more</i> restrictive than non-copyleft. But only in the subtle sense of ensuring that <i>no further restrictions will get added</i> by any third party, ever. Which is a strong promise that should encourage contributions.<p>Ultimately, my objective is to learn if this theory matches the practice.')
