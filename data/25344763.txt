Item(by='geofft', descendants=None, kids=[25344917, 25345065], score=None, time=1607434383, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25329201, text='&gt; <i>Have the old divisions between musical styles – a source of surprisingly bitter contention in the last decades of the 20th century – now healed? Do people now accept your chosen path of tonal harmony, melody, repetitive structures?</i><p>&gt; <i>No no no no no. Those divisions never healed. Those people just died! The people who don’t want you don’t change their minds. You outlive them, if you’re lucky. They’re all dead now, the older guys. The battle was never won or lost. The army just went away. What can I tell you? Isn’t that the truth? It’s biology. Nothing more than that. They’ve just gone away… and we carry on playing.</i><p>This seems true in so many things: politics, engineering approaches, even theology at times. Keep doing what you think is worthwhile, but don&#x27;t expect to change the minds of the old guard: you &quot;win&quot; by having the newer generation accept your approach.')
