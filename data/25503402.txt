Item(by='nl', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608616855, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25497808, text='Scientific consensus is <i>rarely</i> wrong.<p>There have been 3 major cases in the 20th century that I&#x27;m aware of where well-established theories turned out to be wrong.<p>1) Plate tectonics (dismissed until 1962)<p>2) The Bohr model of the atom, confirmed by the colours of light absorbed and emitted by ionised helium - which turned out to be wrong<p>3) The Sommerfeld extension of Bohr&#x27;s model.<p>Both (2) and (3) were thought to be correct because they gave predictions correct to 4 decimal places. This turned out to be a co-incidence(!!) and their theories turned out to be wrong and replaced by Dirac&#x27;s model.<p>Given the amount of science done in the 20th century, I think that&#x27;s a pretty good record.')
