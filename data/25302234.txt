Item(by='m000', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607090262, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25298574, text='&gt; If I&#x27;d had the opportunity to read-up on it, I would have been fine.<p>This highlights the paradox of Hackerrank-style tests. They would make sense in an age where the overhead to refresh your knowledge about something took time and effort: Find a relevant <i>paper</i> book, read it, perhaps type-out the examples. But in that age. where they would make sense, we didn&#x27;t have the technology to make them available to the masses.<p>Today, where refreshing your existing knowledge only takes a couple of minutes searching online, these tests actually do a bad job in telling apart the code-monkey from the expert who happens to be a bit rusty on a specific topic.')
