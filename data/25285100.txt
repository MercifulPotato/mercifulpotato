Item(by='4cao', descendants=None, kids=[25285177, 25286893], score=None, time=1606966940, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25284990, text='In spoken Mandarin Chinese you can normally derive the meaning from context but in Classical Chinese (or in fact anything written more formally) it&#x27;s not necessarily so straightforward.<p>There is a poem that illustrates it, where each syllable is pronounced exactly the same, &#x2F;shi&#x2F; just with different tones: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Lion-Eating_Poet_in_the_Stone_Den" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Lion-Eating_Poet_in_the_Stone_...</a> (施氏食獅史). It goes like that:<p><pre><code>    « Shī Shì shí shī shǐ »\n    Shíshì shīshì Shī Shì, shì shī, shì shí shí shī.\n    Shì shíshí shì shì shì shī.\n    Shí shí, shì shí shī shì shì.\n    [...]\n</code></pre>\nGood luck trying to figure out the meaning without the &quot;written adornments.&quot;')
