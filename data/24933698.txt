Doing it with Postgres allows for way more flexibility (the data could be local or remote) in this case, and the data will be <i>at home</i> in Postgres, with all the stability, features, operational knowledge (and also bugs&#x2F;warts of course) that come with Postgres.<p>You may never get 100% of the performance you&#x27;d get from a purpose-built database that doesn&#x27;t make the choices Postgres makes but the idea of getting 80&#x2F;90% of the way there, with only one thing to maintain is very exciting to me.<p>[0]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.postgresql.org&#x2F;docs&#x2F;current&#x2F;contrib-dblink-function.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.postgresql.org&#x2F;docs&#x2F;current&#x2F;contrib-dblink-funct...</a><p>[1]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.postgresql.org&#x2F;docs&#x2F;current&#x2F;postgres-fdw.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.postgresql.org&#x2F;docs&#x2F;current&#x2F;postgres-fdw.html</a>')
