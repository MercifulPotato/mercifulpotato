Item(by='wcoenen', descendants=None, kids=[25599031], score=None, time=1609459449, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25598783, text='When the government issues bonds, they sell them for commercial bank money, the same type of money in your bank account.<p>When the central bank later buys those bonds from banks (they never buy them directly), something different happens: the bonds are exchanged for &quot;bank reserves&quot;, a different type of digital money that only exists as numbers in the central bank&#x27;s database. This &quot;money&quot; cannot be used to buy groceries.')
