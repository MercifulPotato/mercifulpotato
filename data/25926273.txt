Item(by='carpdiem', descendants=None, kids=[25926342, 25926295, 25926355], score=None, time=1611741595, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25926236, text='Forests are not perpetual carbon sinks, as trees eventually die and then decompose, releasing their carbon back into the atmosphere.<p>At best, a young and growing forest can be a temporary carbon sink, until it reaches maturity (and steady state).<p>Alternatively, a forest might support continual carbon sinking if and only if the recently dead and&#x2F;or mature trees were somehow harvested and treated&#x2F;stored in such a way that their carbon would not decompose and re-enter the rest of the biosphere.')
