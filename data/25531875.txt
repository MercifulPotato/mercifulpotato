Item(by='pansa2', descendants=None, kids=[25535009], score=None, time=1608847418, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25530855, text='&gt; <i>Would a more rigorously-typed alternate-universe Python have been as successful?</i><p>One of the reasons Python became popular was its reputation as “executable pseudocode”. Rigorous typing would detract from that.<p>Guido and the authors of Mypy clearly believe that now the language is popular, it can afford to add explicit types and become less like pseudocode. But I don’t think it would have <i>become</i> as popular had it taken this direction from the beginning.')
