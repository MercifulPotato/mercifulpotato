Item(by='__d', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602793837, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24783651, text='IMO, all connectors have issues, some more, some less.<p>USB-A has proven remarkably good, despite the &quot;insert 3-times&quot; problem, and it&#x27;s now fairly large size.<p>Lightning is ok -- I have found that cables lose their &quot;snap in&quot; ness after a while, the arcing limits the connector lifetime, and I&#x27;ve snapped the tongue off a few (cheap) cables.<p>USB-B Micro has been pretty good for me, despite the &quot;insert 5 times&quot; problem.<p>USB-C has advantages of &quot;insert-once&quot;, and smaller size, but (in my one-off anecdata) <i>can</i> have issues with losing its snug-ness over time.  I thought it was notable, since it was with a MacBook -- so not something badly engineered, or cheap, or whatever.<p>I guess my view is that it&#x27;s not a panacea, and arguably, on a par with things it&#x27;s replacing.')
