Item(by='PaulHoule', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602256686, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24730572, text='There is &quot;BECCS&quot; which is bio-energy with carbon capture and storage.<p>An Ethanol factory in Decatur, IL captures waste CO2 from the fermentation process,  purifies it,  compresses it to about 1500 psi and pumps it into a saline aquifer.  If you burned the bio fuel and captured the CO2 by<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Chemical_looping_combustion" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Chemical_looping_combustion</a><p>or another method you could get CO2 and make methane by<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Sabatier_reaction" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Sabatier_reaction</a><p>then it is no longer storage,  but it is keeping the carbon in short-term circulation between the atmosphere and the biosphere.')
