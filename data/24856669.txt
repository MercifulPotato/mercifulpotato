Item(by='Red_Leaves_Flyy', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603363852, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24855406, text='A John or Chad is a colloquialism for certain types of men. I&#x27;ve met countless men named both and never heard them complain about sharing a name with these slang terms.<p>There are countless other examples in English and other languages. Amazon coopted Alexa. Apple, siri. I&#x27;ve met more than one person named Isis.<p>Unfortunately language is still a rough tool. We name things from mathematical proofs, to sea slugs, or even groups of people with certain identifiable characteristics, arbitrarily and sometimes capriciously.<p>Ultimately those effected find a way to adapt to an environment that&#x27;s coopted one of their identifiers under a new definition in the collective lexicon. Trying to fight it usually just makes things worse.')
