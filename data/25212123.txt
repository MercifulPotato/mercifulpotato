Item(by='cmrdporcupine', descendants=None, kids=[25212755, 25213126], score=None, time=1606327357, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25211927, text='I&#x27;m surprised you noticed the aggression before 6 months. It&#x27;s actually hard to even identify roosters as roosters in the first few months. They don&#x27;t develop their physical traits fully for some time.<p>Re: birds and rape, wait til you see ducks...<p>I should note that there are roosters and then there are roosters. We had a really amazing one, a well bred barred rock who was a real gentleman. They have an important role in the flock, warning about and fending off predators. He died after sustaining wounds from diverting a fox over 100 feet away from his hens. Tussled with the fox from the coop all the way down to the road, distracting it from his hens, then hid under a passing car until we could rescue him. Miss that guy.<p>But it is important that the hen to rooster ratio is right, or they get competitive and mean.')
