Item(by='gshubert17', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607047296, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25285778, text='Back in the 1970s I used a slide rule when 2-3 place accuracy was enough; or 5-place or 7-place log tables and a pocket mechanical adding machine (rather than by hand) when more accuracy was required.<p>I found a 2-minute video of the sort of pocket adding machine I used,\n<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=ryST18JJ7VU" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=ryST18JJ7VU</a>\n(The device is actually easier to use than shown. Using the colors next to the digits: if silver, pull the stylus down; if red, pull up and over the curve at the top of the column to the next digit. No extra or wasted motions are needed.)<p>Five places were usually enough, but seven places were needed for some astronomical calculations such as eclipses. The $7 I paid for the log tables in the late 1960s would purchase about $50 today; and the adding machine was probably a couple of dollars back then.')
