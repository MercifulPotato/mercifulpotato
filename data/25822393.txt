Item(by='nickelpro', descendants=None, kids=[25822622], score=None, time=1610982045, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25822291, text='It&#x27;s not overhead, it&#x27;s about dependency breaking. 32-bit xors on a single register are universally recognized as a zeroing idiom, which means the CPU doesn&#x27;t have to wait for the results of previous operations in order to set the value of the applicable register to zero.<p>In modern CPUs zero&#x27;ing idioms aren&#x27;t even executed, they only get as far as the register allocater. The register allocater will allocate a zero&#x27;d physical register for the architectural register that had the idiom applied to it and the job is done.')
