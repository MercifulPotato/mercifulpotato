Item(by='fest', descendants=None, kids=[25153201], score=None, time=1605788842, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25148002, text='I don&#x27;t see it as lowering the cost of entry much (i.e. FPGA chip with reasonable number of gates for Linux is on the order of 50EUR&#x2F;pc, and general purpose CPU performance is not even remotely close to, let&#x27;s say Allwinner or Rockchip ARM SoC), but as something that either opens up new possibilities as a whole (for example, a trusted computing platform, where you can actually verify that no backdoors exist- see <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.bunniestudios.com&#x2F;blog&#x2F;?p=5971" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.bunniestudios.com&#x2F;blog&#x2F;?p=5971</a> ) or something that provides unprecedented flexibility in the future proofness of embedded designs: for example when you realize that you need another UART in a product that&#x27;s been shipping for 5 years, and the MCU family you have been using just does not have the part you need.<p>I am particularly excited about the hardware flexibility- it seems a very good platform for scientific&#x2F;engineering instrumentation (LiteX actually seems to be conceived  because of this niche).')
