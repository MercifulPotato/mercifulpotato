We didn’t take the time to analyze why our theory was wrong. We didn’t question the experiment’s validity, which would have led us to realize our patch was wrong early on.<p>I&#x27;ve read dozens of postmortems where &quot;someone did something  to &#x27;prove&#x27; that X wasn&#x27;t the cause, but after ruling everything else out the team eventually realised that X was in fact the cause.&quot;, with &#x27;X&#x27; being everything from &quot;this patch can&#x27;t have caused that behavior&quot; to &quot;I know it&#x27;s not the kernel update because how could that have changed anything?&quot;<p>In the face of a crisis, if you are in charge you really have to take all the &quot;facts&quot; you are presented with as being contingent on the level of panic that the presenter is feeling -- being a great engineer is more or less orthogonal to calm under pressure.')
