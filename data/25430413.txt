Item(by='arooaroo', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608041402, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25423712, text='I recall (albeit vaguely) reading an interesting book &quot;Renegades of the Empire: How Three Software Warriors Started a Revolution Behind the Walls of Fortress&quot; by Michael Drummond.<p>My recollection of the genesis of DirectX was in fact a skunkworks project create their vision of the next generation web-browsing experience. I think it was demoed and they had begun touting to developers&#x2F;publishers with work with, but was axed.<p>I think it had a 3D aspect to it -- perhaps all 3d, I can&#x27;t recall. But my memory was that they needed a significant 3D library, and they didn&#x27;t want to go down the OpenGL (I think it was fundamentally political and personality disagreements with themselves and the maintainer(s) of the OpenGL implementation within MS) They convinced their boss to acquire a company that had a 3d engine product and hacked together a more comprehensive library, that ultimately became DirectX.')
