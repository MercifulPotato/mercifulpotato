Item(by='balls187', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609525853, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25604724, text='&gt; I&#x27;m not a fan of whiteboard coding challenges, even though they got me my first &quot;real&quot; Bay Area tech job.<p>This is the fundamental part that is broken in my experience.<p>They are often challenges&#x2F;coding puzzles, when rather white board exercises should focus on collaboration, and solving problems with code.<p>Having a &quot;gotcha&quot; that shows rudimentary CS understanding is fine (such as understanding why the obvious O(N^2) is a bad solution). But having a gotcha that requires some crazy leap of faith guess (or having seen the problem before) is not.<p>I admit, my fondness for a candidate increases with the level of complexity of coding problem they can solve; however needing hints on the harder problems doesn&#x27;t mean they are not qualified.')
