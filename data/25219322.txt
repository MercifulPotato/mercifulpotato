It will be a disruptive change like we saw with steam engines taking over, the ICE or electric power transmission.<p>Second comes self-driving cars. Not because they unlock the potential to stare at your smartphone so that you can give away more of your personal data to the Internet advertisment industry. But it would result in a turmoil in an endless list of supply chains living from the car industry. With self-driving cars, actually owning a car becomes a luxury only a few will be able to afford. The insurance system or fuel supply system will experience dramatic changes. This Schumpeterian destruction will unlock something. I don&#x27;t know if it will be growth. The past told us that high cultures also quickly disapeared from time to time.')
