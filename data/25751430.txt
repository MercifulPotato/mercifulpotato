Item(by='pwinnski', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610477053, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25749817, text='The lies in this case start at the top, and are clearly intentionally untrue.<p>That people farther away from the origin point come to believe these untrue things does not make the original lies anything other than lies.<p>On a small scale, it may be true that countering a mistaken belief with facts and reason is helpful--though a lot of research suggests that&#x27;s not the case even one at a time--but on the large scale, lies must be called out as lies, and letting them stand leads only to repetition and widespread belief.<p>Had there been a better job done of calling out and suppressing the lies at the stage they were still only lies, it&#x27;s possible we would have much less mistaken belief.<p>Also, the idea that most people are suffering from &quot;mistaken belief&quot; rather than being aware they are lies strikes me as naive, but that&#x27;s a matter of opinion.')
