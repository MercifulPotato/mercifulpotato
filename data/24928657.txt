Item(by='moksly', descendants=None, kids=[24928944], score=None, time=1603963826, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24926417, text='A lot of these comes down to structuring law and bureaucracy to be digitizable. In Denmark every cadastre is throughly mapped and logged, and have been for a long long time. As a result we have a national addressregistry with a public API. This way your digital solutions will never really get an address wrong.<p>I think you could build something similar with OpenStreetMaps if your bureaucracy sucks, but it’s a massive undertaking of course.<p>What surprises me the most is how countries that don’t do this, keeps track of all the other GIS related things. Like how much electricity or plumbing a cadastre will need. Or perhaps more important, taxes and property taxes.')
