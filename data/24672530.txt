Item(by='andrewjl', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1601737896, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24664408, text='I don&#x27;t think it&#x27;s necessarily false equivalence to say that if you assume a group of states can be bound in the same [legal, financial and political] ways that a single state is.<p>I get that it&#x27;s a near-identical variant of the argument you&#x27;re making, but you have an unstated assumption that effective supra-national political (or otherwise) systems cannot exist. The Five Eyes is an example to the contrary. ASEAN is another in a more economic vein. These examples shown it&#x27;s possible to achieve a balance between respecting national sovereignty and avoiding inter-state armed conflict. We could probably use more of them these days, global trends notwithstanding.')
