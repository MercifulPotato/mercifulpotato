Item(by='mikelevins', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609801499, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25621727, text='Oh, I forgot to mention that one of the cool features of Macintosh Common Lisp was its interactive assembler. You could write  &quot;LAP&quot; (&quot;Lisp Assembly Program&quot;) code in the repl, just like you could write Lisp code.<p>Around the time that MacFrames morphed into SK8, Apple hired an arcade-game programmer named Dave Vronay to work on the SK8 graphics subsystem. When I first met him, he was over the moon about MCL and how great it was to write assembly code in a repl and run it <i>right now</i>.')
