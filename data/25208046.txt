Item(by='yorwba', descendants=None, kids=[25208321, 25208467], score=None, time=1606302863, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25207835, text='That&#x27;s right, but it&#x27;s not necessary to aggregate small farms into large ones to maximize machine utilization. It&#x27;s also possible to separate the machine-owning part from the other aspects of farming and aggregate only that, by forming an association of farmers who share equipment. (Shared ownership of the means of production, so to speak.) In German, that&#x27;s called a Maschinenring. <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;de.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Maschinenring" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;de.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Maschinenring</a> Not sure whether the concept is unpopular in the English-speaking world, or just not considered noteworthy enough to warrant its own Wikipedia article.')
