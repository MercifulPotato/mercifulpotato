Item(by='Baeocystin', descendants=None, kids=[25273194], score=None, time=1606860034, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25267893, text='I work with small businesses, and 90% of the time, they are better served by running a few VMs in a 1U Xeon-powered rackmount Synology or the like.<p>Does it have a lot of compute power?  No.<p>Is it dirt-simple to admin, and have <i>enough</i> horsepower to do the simple things like run a QuickBooks server on a Windows VM, Fileshare on another, test on a third, etc that is all they usually need?  Absolutely!<p>It isn&#x27;t uncommon at all that I make my initial IT inventory at a new client&#x27;s business and find that they are spending hundreds to thousands&#x2F;month unnecessarily in overblown costs, and AWS is a primary driver of that.  A few kilobucks in to owned hardware, and the problem is essentially solved for a few years, and paid for in a few months.  Everybody wins.')
