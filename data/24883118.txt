Item(by='DoreenMichele', descendants=None, kids=[24883543], score=None, time=1603588405, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24883090, text='Historically, Middle Eastern cities were built on plateaus above the desert floor and streets were oriented such that prevailing winds cooled them. That plus other design decisions helped keep streets at tolerable temperatures during the day, even when it was above 100 degrees Fahrenheit out on the desert floor.<p>We have forgotten how to do that kind of thing, but that doesn&#x27;t mean it cannot be done. There is a lot we can do to mitigate urban heat island effect and use design to make even our outdoor public spaces pedestrian-friendly in terms of local weather.')
