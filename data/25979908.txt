Item(by='remram', descendants=None, kids=[25980698], score=None, time=1612110399, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25978185, text='I&#x27;m surprised they are not identifying the service in the token. A lot of services are scanning GitHub for committed tokens, and being able to tell if that token is a fake&#x2F;testing one or a real one for a specific company would make those scans more useful.<p>For example, SendGrid tokens start with &quot;SG&quot;, Amazon tokens with &quot;AKIA&quot;, etc. Why not build that into the URL scheme?<p>This scheme will have services stick to well-known unformatted prefixes in the token itself, and prevent small actors from being notified by GitHub and other security scanners.<p>See also <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=25016838" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=25016838</a>')
