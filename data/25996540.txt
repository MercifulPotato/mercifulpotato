When I started seeing my current doctor, they tried scare mongering by claiming stimulants can cause Parkinsons. There is a study to back this up [1,2], but I was skeptical of it since they didn&#x27;t seem to control for the fact that someone with ADHD but not on stimulants could either have less intense ADHD or perhaps have some other confounding condition.<p>The study authors actually mention:<p>&quot;The association of ADHD patients prescribed psychostimulants with higher risk of diseases of the basal ganglia and cerebellum may reflect a more severe ADHD phenotype rather than a direct association between prescribed stimulant use and basal ganglia or cerebellum disorders.&quot;[2]<p>It&#x27;s somewhat encouraging to see that this seems to be true and increased chance of Parkinson&#x27;s is likely due to the severity of ADHD and not due to medication which helps people with ADHD be functional.<p>[1]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;healthcare.utah.edu&#x2F;publicaffairs&#x2F;news&#x2F;2018&#x2F;09&#x2F;adhd-parkinsons.php" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;healthcare.utah.edu&#x2F;publicaffairs&#x2F;news&#x2F;2018&#x2F;09&#x2F;adhd-...</a>\n[2]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov&#x2F;30209407&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov&#x2F;30209407&#x2F;</a>')
