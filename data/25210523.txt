DSLs are a concept, not an implementation.    As implemented they can vary from chained procedure calls to actual sub languages with lexers and parsers (and I tend to consider the latter to be &#x27;proper&#x27; DSLs, but that&#x27;s just my view).<p>To have a &#x27;proper&#x27; DSL I reckon you need two things, and understanding that a thing can and should be broken out into its own sublanguage, and the ability to do so.    The first takes a certain kind of nouse, or common sense.    The latter requires knowing how to construct a parser properly and some knowledge of language design.<p>Knowing how to write a parser is not particularly complex but as the industry is driven by requirements more of knowing &#x27;big data&#x27; frameworks rather than stuff that is often more useful, well, that&#x27;s what you get, and that includes people who try to parse XML with regular expressions (check out this classic answer &lt;<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;stackoverflow.com&#x2F;questions&#x2F;1732348&#x2F;regex-match-open-tags-except-xhtml-self-contained-tags&#x2F;1732454#1732454" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;stackoverflow.com&#x2F;questions&#x2F;1732348&#x2F;regex-match-open...</a>&gt; Edit: if you haven&#x27;t seen this check it out cos it&#x27;s brilliant).<p>I think this reflects the fundamental problem in software development of the market&#x27;s not knowing what&#x27;s actually needed to solve real business problems.<p>++++<p>Edit, some reading material<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.amazon.co.uk&#x2F;Language-Implementation-Patterns-Domain-Specific-Programming&#x2F;dp&#x2F;193435645X&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.amazon.co.uk&#x2F;Language-Implementation-Patterns-Do...</a><p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.amazon.co.uk&#x2F;Definitive-ANTLR-Reference-Domain-Specific-Programmers&#x2F;dp&#x2F;0978739256" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.amazon.co.uk&#x2F;Definitive-ANTLR-Reference-Domain-S...</a><p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.amazon.co.uk&#x2F;yacc-Nutshell-Handbook-Doug-Brown&#x2F;dp&#x2F;1565920007" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.amazon.co.uk&#x2F;yacc-Nutshell-Handbook-Doug-Brown&#x2F;d...</a><p>They&#x27;re all worth investing the time in.')
