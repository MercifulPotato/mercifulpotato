Item(by='thayne', descendants=None, kids=[24701793, 24701419], score=None, time=1602012004, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24700628, text='&gt;  The final, net prices paid for insulins are likely to be significantly lower than manufacturer prices in the U.S. because rebates and other discounts often drive down the price paid by individuals in the U.S.<p>I suspect this gives a hint to one contributing factor. Suppliers probably list much higher prices so that they can negotiate lower prices with insurance companies without losing profit. But then the consumer needs to use an &quot;in-network&quot; supplier to get those reduced prices, which removes competition and drives prices up even further.')
