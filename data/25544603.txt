Tagging a getter with &quot;:lvalue&quot; will make it assignable, even usable with the increment operator:<p><pre><code>    pirl @&gt; sub hats : lvalue { my ($self) = @_; $self-&gt;{hats}; }\n    @var = ();\n    pirl @&gt; $obj = bless {}\n    @var = (\n             bless( {}, &#x27;Shell::Perl::sandbox&#x27; )\n           );\n    pirl @&gt; $obj-&gt;hats\n    @var = (\n             undef\n           );\n    pirl @&gt; $obj\n    @var = (\n             bless( {}, &#x27;Shell::Perl::sandbox&#x27; )\n           );\n    pirl @&gt; $obj-&gt;hats = 1\n    @var = (\n             1\n           );\n    pirl @&gt; $obj\n    @var = (\n             bless( {\n                      &#x27;hats&#x27; =&gt; 1\n                    }, &#x27;Shell::Perl::sandbox&#x27; )\n           );\n    pirl @&gt; $obj-&gt;hats\n    @var = (\n             1\n           );\n    pirl @&gt; $obj-&gt;hats++\n    @var = (\n             1\n           );\n    pirl @&gt; $obj-&gt;hats\n    @var = (\n             2\n           );\n    pirl @&gt;</code></pre>')
