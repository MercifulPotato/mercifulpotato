Item(by='npunt', descendants=None, kids=[25838060], score=None, time=1611080544, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25835864, text='Ben missed an important part of the geopolitical difference between TSMC and Intel: Taiwan is much more invested in TSMC&#x27;s success than America is in Intel&#x27;s.<p>Taiwan&#x27;s share of the semiconductor industry is 66% and TSMC is the leader of that industry. Semiconductors helps keep Taiwan from China&#x27;s encroachment because it buys them protection from allies like the US and Europe, whose economies heavily rely on them.<p>To Taiwan, semiconductor leadership is an existential question. To America, semiconductors are just business.<p>This means Taiwan is also likely to do more politically to keep TSMC competitive, much like Korea with Samsung.')
