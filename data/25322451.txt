Item(by='Jach', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607248978, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25321822, text='Regarding 4, I&#x27;d suggest reading <i>Object-Oriented Programming: The CLOS Perspective</i> first. It also has a great cover (same illustrator as AMOP), though it&#x27;s not evident from Amazon&#x27;s page last time I checked. This book is really a big collection of papers from several authors. It covers the topics of Lisp&#x27;s OOP system and some of its history, its unique features (especially some comparison chapters with C++, Smalltalk, Eiffel), its meta-object protocol (and why you&#x27;d want it), some production reports from the field on its use in helping application development, and some implementation details to ward off &quot;surely this is sooo much slower than a plain old function call?&quot;')
