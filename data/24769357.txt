Say that the reserve ratios requirement is 10%, which it currently is.  Then for every dollar it has in reserves, the bank may create $10 in loans.  The bank reserves don&#x27;t themselves circulate as money, but they let the bank create more credit, which <i>does</i> circulate as money.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.investopedia.com&#x2F;terms&#x2F;r&#x2F;reserveratio.asp" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.investopedia.com&#x2F;terms&#x2F;r&#x2F;reserveratio.asp</a>')
