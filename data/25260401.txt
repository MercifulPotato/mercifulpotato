Item(by='nostrademons', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606778954, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25259447, text='It doesn&#x27;t have to be affordable to the working class, it just has to be affordable enough for a few engineers who saved up a bit in the last boom to take a ~5-year sabbatical.  Then fear of falling into the struggling working class motivates them to keep inventing, while a desire to replace all the working-class employees who can no longer afford to live in the city gives them a ready-made market.<p>There&#x27;s no guarantee that the working class as we know it today (i.e. those who perform repetitive tasks with their hands) will continue to exist.  Most of the Bay Area&#x27;s history has been about automating and replacing progressively higher-level portions of the economy with machines.')
