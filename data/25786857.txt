Item(by='derefr', descendants=None, kids=[25787254], score=None, time=1610683139, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25786662, text='&gt; Saying that &quot;Linux [is] based on Unix&quot; is not really accurate either.<p>The context of the author&#x27;s statement was syscall ABIs. And Linux&#x27;s original (x86) syscall ABI <i>is</i> based on [a snapshot of] the syscall ABI from a Unix (4.2BSD, I think.)<p><i>Loosely</i> based, mind you — 4.2BSD wasn&#x27;t targeting a 32-bit architecture, let alone x86, so the registers et al weren&#x27;t the same. But the syscall numbers match up, and the number and order of registers used have direct parallels.<p>Compare and contrast:<p>• FreeBSD ABI (direct descendant of 4.2BSD): <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;freebsd&#x2F;freebsd-src&#x2F;blob&#x2F;master&#x2F;sys&#x2F;kern&#x2F;syscalls.master" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;freebsd&#x2F;freebsd-src&#x2F;blob&#x2F;master&#x2F;sys&#x2F;kern&#x2F;...</a><p>• Linux x86 ABI: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;chromium.googlesource.com&#x2F;chromiumos&#x2F;docs&#x2F;+&#x2F;master&#x2F;constants&#x2F;syscalls.md#x86-32_bit" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;chromium.googlesource.com&#x2F;chromiumos&#x2F;docs&#x2F;+&#x2F;master&#x2F;c...</a><p>Everything lines up until you get to the OS-proprietary stuff.')
