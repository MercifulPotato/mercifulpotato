Item(by='nickik', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607815374, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25402051, text='The terminology is very confusing. Most &#x27;solid state&#x27; batteries are lithium-ion batteries, specially all the commercial ones you hear about as being the next big thing.<p>In those batteries rather then having a graphite with some silicone mixed in, the anode is simply pure lithium metal and you do not have liquid electrolyte in the whole battery.<p>However some solid state batteries still have a liquid electrolyte on the cathode side.<p>Your phone is most likely using standard, 622-NCM (Nickel 60%, Cobalt 20%, Aluminum 20%) Cathode with graphite anode.')
