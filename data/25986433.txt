Item(by='zaroth', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1612164441, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25984249, text='What MF Global did was totally different;<p>&gt; The story states that a London clearinghouse forced MF Global to pay roughly $300 million to back some of <i>its bond holdings during its last week.</i><p>There&#x27;s a mountain of difference between sending customer money to purchase customer&#x27;s own securities, versus raiding customer accounts to pay a margin call against your own bond holdings.<p>You cannot raid customer funds to pay for proprietary (broker&#x27;s own) activity. Of course using customer own funds to pay for customer&#x27;s own orders is not restricted. There&#x27;s nothing to collateralize for a fully cash funded share purchase. Collateral can only possibly be an issue if the customer is trading on margin.')
