Item(by='sokoloff', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605277270, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25082291, text='I&#x27;m saying that if there is not enough demand for that work to support a wage that <i>will</i> provide a living wage on 40 hours&#x2F;week, that you can&#x27;t legislate up enough excess demand to make the equation solve the way you might like it to.<p>In most (maybe all) markets, there is not enough consumer demand at the prices that would be required to support a living wage for everyone for whom flexible ride-share driving is the most lucrative work readily available. Now, iff that&#x27;s true, what should the legislature do? Prevent that work from being done at all? Ration the supply (medallions or companies refusing to let drivers choose when&#x2F;if to work)? The people said no, <i>including a lot of current ride-share drivers</i>.')
