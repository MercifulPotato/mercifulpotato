Item(by='chaboud', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608937059, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25520009, text='Funny enough, Aggregability is NP-Hard, so comprehensive consideration of the larger set is not actually practical&#x2F;possible.<p>Here&#x27;s the proof:\n<a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cs.utep.edu&#x2F;vladik&#x2F;2006&#x2F;tr06-29.pdf" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cs.utep.edu&#x2F;vladik&#x2F;2006&#x2F;tr06-29.pdf</a><p>Perhaps there are mechanisms around performance under external test, auditing, or safe harbor due to documented good-faith, but it is <i>not possible</i> to guarantee correctness for the full set from a subset.<p>Similarly, some approaches will plainly fail to perform well with subgroups of people due to sensing technologies.  That may appear as model bias, but it&#x27;s really a matter of physics and available entropy.<p>For the time being, mechanisms that use machine inference as an aid rather than a guide (leaving the judgment to the human in the loop) are probably the best we can hope for.')
