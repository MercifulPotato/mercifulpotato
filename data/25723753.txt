Item(by='Jtsummers', descendants=None, kids=[25727027], score=None, time=1610333378, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25722540, text='Somehow I missed this comment. But as michaelcampbell wrote, in Ada you&#x27;d select the range that made sense for your application.<p>So if your application makes sense with 0-based (time series in your example) then you can do it:<p><pre><code>  Cash_Flows : array (0..max_time) of Double;\n</code></pre>\nIt may even make sense to go negative for some things. For instance:<p><pre><code>  Object_at_Altitude : array (-100..1000) of Object;\n</code></pre>\nOr characters:<p><pre><code>  Selected_Answers : array (range &#x27;a&#x27;..&#x27;z&#x27;) of Boolean;\n</code></pre>\nOr a type that you&#x27;ve created:<p><pre><code>  type Color is Red, Green, Blue, Yellow, Orange;\n  Histogram_of_Colors : array (Color&#x27;Range) of Natural;\n</code></pre>\nBut a particularly nice thing is that arrays in Ada, unlike C, don&#x27;t lose their size, so you can always do:<p><pre><code>  for I in Cash_Flows&#x27;Range loop\n    Some_Function(Cash_Flows(I));\n  end loop;\n</code></pre>\nSo really the index never even has to be directly touched or known after creation.')
