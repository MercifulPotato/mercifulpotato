Item(by='keiferski', descendants=None, kids=[25629664], score=None, time=1609746504, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25626389, text='A lot of this results from an underlying consumerism, I think. The notion that you can buy anything, whether that be coolness, meaning, social standing, or artistic skill. You can be anything you want, and you only need to buy something to get there. Hence the hipster concept, which is fundamentally about <i>representation</i> and not <i>being.</i> The life that Kerouac lived, for example, would make most contemporary upper-middle-class hipsters shudder in horror.<p>I recommend an old anthology for similar stories to this one called <i>On Bohemia: The Code of the Self-Exiled.</i>')
