Item(by='alexmingoia', descendants=None, kids=[25581108, 25590604], score=None, time=1609330381, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25580838, text='Semantic web doesn’t assume people would label everything the same. You can relate two resources (you call them tags) as “the same thing” :) It’s just another relationship.<p>RDF models facts&#x2F;knowledge as subject-predicate-object statements called triples. Each part of the triple is called a resource and has a URL.<p>You wouldn’t bookmark pages, you would state facts about them in RDF using a vocabulary of resources.<p>A hypothetical semantic note taking app would let you insert many facts (RDF triples) about something you find relevant into your own knowledge graph, and the facts you insert would be constructed from your chosen vocabularies (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.w3.org&#x2F;standards&#x2F;semanticweb&#x2F;ontology" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.w3.org&#x2F;standards&#x2F;semanticweb&#x2F;ontology</a>).<p>Roam&#x2F;bookmarks&#x2F;hypertext is the Stone Age compared to RDF knowledge graphs. <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.m.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Resource_Description_Framework" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.m.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Resource_Description_Framewo...</a>')
