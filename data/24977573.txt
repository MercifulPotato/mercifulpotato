For quantitative feedback tools like hotjar.com&#x27;s surveys can be quite effective (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.hotjar.com&#x2F;tour&#x2F;#polls" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.hotjar.com&#x2F;tour&#x2F;#polls</a>).<p>For qualitative data such as user interviews&#x2F;calls the solution for most teams is either to spend a lot of time on find users to talk to and set up calls or to pay companies like usertesting.com for access to their panels.<p>These tools don&#x27;t allow you to get quick (and for most teams affordable) access to your own users for user interviews though, which is something that was enough of a pain point within my own team that we&#x27;ve built a tool exactly for that (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;tryribbon.com" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;tryribbon.com</a>).<p>The Mom Test is also a great read on how to get useful user feedback in general (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;amzn.to&#x2F;383bo92" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;amzn.to&#x2F;383bo92</a>).')
