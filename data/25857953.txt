Item(by='QuesnayJr', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611229939, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25855873, text='People like heroic narratives in intellectual history, but very little of what&#x27;s in Adam Smith is new and unique to Smith.  I picked my username semi-jokingly to draw attention to Francois Quesnay, who was a significant predecessor to Smith.  But Quesnay isn&#x27;t even unique -- you can point to any number of competitors.  This is well-understood by actual historians of economic thought.<p>The significance of Smith is that he&#x27;s where everybody learned these ideas from, not because the ideas are necessarily new with him.  &quot;Wealth of Nations&quot; as much of an act of synthesis as it is an act of creation.')
