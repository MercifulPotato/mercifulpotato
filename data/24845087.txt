Item(by='nitrogen', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603259140, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24844110, text='This conflating of purchasing with trusting is harmful.  It&#x27;s an ongoing trend I&#x27;ve seen with large tech companies, with arguments of the form &quot;You accept a tiny X, therefore your rejection of the giant Y is invalid.&quot;<p>We buy things from companies we don&#x27;t implicitly trust all the time, because we can isolate and verify those things.<p>I don&#x27;t always trust the supermarket to sell me non-moldy produce, but I can look at the produce and see whether it&#x27;s moldy.<p>I don&#x27;t trust oil companies not to destroy the environment, but if they sell me bad fuel it will be very clear.<p>I don&#x27;t trust OS makers, but I can run firewalls and network sniffers to verify that the OS is behaving reasonably, and isolate it when it isn&#x27;t.  Until I can&#x27;t.')
