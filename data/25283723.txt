Item(by='chii', descendants=None, kids=[25297889], score=None, time=1606955232, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25283678, text='&gt; If you buy a share of a publicly-traded company you are not &quot;investing&quot; in the company<p>no you are - because it&#x27;s a chain of capital investment that is linked all the way to IPO (or private investment pre-IPO).<p>You purchase a share publicly from _someone_ who has purchased it from someone else. This chain of purchases leads all the way back to the initial share offering.<p>Just because your dollar doesn&#x27;t go directly to the company doesn&#x27;t mean this form of funding structure isn&#x27;t &quot;contributing capital&quot;. Your public share purchase allows an existing share holder to exit - without which they would not have purchased the initial share in the first place.')
