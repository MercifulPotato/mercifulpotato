That&#x27;s a big win on modern systems.<p>In reality it&#x27;s a bigger effect since electric signals travel slower than light. And the effect is yet bigger because off-package memory must be accessed through buses with several levels of synchronization and buffer and gate delays.<p>Apple gets away with &quot;unified memory&quot; in the M1 because having the memory on-package means a good deal of the bus and sync and contention logic becomes unnecessary. So everything that touches RAM gets a lot faster. And almost everything touches RAM.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.theregister.com&#x2F;2020&#x2F;11&#x2F;19&#x2F;apple_m1_high_bandwidth_memory_performance&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.theregister.com&#x2F;2020&#x2F;11&#x2F;19&#x2F;apple_m1_high_bandwid...</a><p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.macworld.com&#x2F;article&#x2F;3597569&#x2F;m1-macs-memory-isnt-what-it-used-to-be.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.macworld.com&#x2F;article&#x2F;3597569&#x2F;m1-macs-memory-isnt...</a>')
