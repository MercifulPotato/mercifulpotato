Item(by='ssivark', descendants=None, kids=[24780257], score=None, time=1602691130, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24772499, text='I agree with the core thesis, and have had multiple discussions&#x2F;debates about the importance of decentralization for promoting robustness through diversity (avoiding monocultures).<p>A common complaint against federated structures (whenever I’m involved in this debate) is the claim that it’s much harder to fight spam &amp; malicious actors than centralized systems operating at scale (which can afford to devote resources to this problem).<p>I find that claim quite unconvincing, and believe that the problem is manufactured by the existence of centralized systems. In decentralized systems, effects of malicious action (be it spam or misinformation of any kind) are localized and controlled — and cannot scale to have large impact (thus also providing some disincentives for malicious action).')
