Item(by='skissane', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611951084, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25956313, text='&gt; If you look into the screening tools, you&#x27;ll realize many of the questions are actually about sensory processing, not just human interaction.<p>A person doesn&#x27;t have to have abnormal &quot;sensory processing&quot; to have ASD, even though a lot of people have convinced themselves that&#x27;s what ASD is all about. Abnormal sensory responses is DSM-5 criteria B(4). You need at least two of B(1)-(4) to be diagnosed with ASD, so a person can meet the diagnostic criteria without abnormal sensory responses, if they have at least two of criteria B(1)-B(3) (along with criteria A,C,D,E).')
