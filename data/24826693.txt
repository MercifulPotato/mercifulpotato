Same story as why the x4 NVMe drive worked when attached to the x1 slot.<p>As far as the physical you can either get a riser or just Dremel the plastic back stop off (though you&#x27;d still need to figure out physical support for a larger device). Many short PCIe slots come with an open back out of the box just for this purpose, I&#x27;m surprised the Pi (for all it&#x27;s hacking purpose) doesn&#x27;t. Power is also going to be another thing, PCIe is supposed to provide up to 75 Watts. There are solutions for this as well, particularly if you went with a &quot;bitcoin mining&quot; type riser in the earlier step.<p>Plenty of people have done GPUs on <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.pine64.org&#x2F;rockpro64&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.pine64.org&#x2F;rockpro64&#x2F;</a>')
