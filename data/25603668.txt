Item(by='Mediterraneo10', descendants=None, kids=[25605770, 25604195], score=None, time=1609513237, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25601749, text='The seemingly archaic quality of modern Icelandic is largely a myth. Over the centuries Icelandic absorbed a huge amount of Danish loanwords. That modern Icelandic has similar vocabulary to the sagas, is because the 19th-century language reformers called for <i>restoring</i> old words. It wasn&#x27;t because Icelandic had kept them the whole time.<p>Modern Icelandic pronunciation, too, is vastly different from Old Norse, in spite of the similar orthography.<p>The one place where Icelandic has a legitimate claim to being archaic, is in the morphology.')
