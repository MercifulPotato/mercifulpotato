Which greatly hinders adoption, I believe. I would expect some kind of plugin system. Naturally, there are attempts to fix that: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;un-def&#x2F;dl-plus" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;un-def&#x2F;dl-plus</a> is one example. As a bonus that would help greatly with recent youtube-dl sort of situations and RIAA would have barely made a splash.<p>Plenty of other features immediately come to mind as well: universal media support, proper parallelism, GUI, desktop integrations, proxies support, anti-captcha... The punchline is: all of this and much more you could find in jdownloader 10 years ago. But somehow youtube-dl won that race. How did that happen?')
