It was supposed to be used, at the user&#x27;s discretion, to help with interoperability problems.  RFC7231 specifically refers to user-agent masquerading by the user as a useful practice.  It explicitly discourages using this header as a means of supposed user identification, e.g., fingerprinting.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;tools.ietf.org&#x2F;html&#x2F;rfc7231#section-5.5.3" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;tools.ietf.org&#x2F;html&#x2F;rfc7231#section-5.5.3</a>')
