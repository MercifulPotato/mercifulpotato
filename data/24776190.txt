Item(by='snowwrestler', descendants=None, kids=[24776230], score=None, time=1602681697, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24774762, text='There&#x27;s a lot of judging in this comment that makes me uncomfortable (&quot;think of them as a different species&quot; has a very nasty history in human society).<p>But what you&#x27;re talking about is essentially the innovator&#x27;s dilemma. Following the available data can lead to short-term optimizations, but also getting caught in a local minima. The silver lining is that this creates room for new competitors who can skip around that neighborhood in the business model and find a new kind of success.<p>So my question is the same whenever this sort of criticism comes up: if the current companies are screwing up so badly, who are the innovators that will go around those mistakes and out-compete them?')
