Item(by='marcan_42', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608093183, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25436740, text='Ex-Googler here.<p>Yes, apps are highly distributed. Yes, roll-outs are staggered and controlled.<p>But some things are necessarily global. Things like your Google account are global (what went down the other day). Of course you can (and Google does) design such a system such that it&#x27;s distributed and tolerant of any given piece failing. But it&#x27;s still <i>one</i> system. And so, if something goes wrong in a new and exciting way... It might just happen to hit the service globally.<p>When things go down, it&#x27;s because something <i>weird</i> happened. You don&#x27;t hear about all the times the regular process prevented downtime... because things don&#x27;t go down.')
