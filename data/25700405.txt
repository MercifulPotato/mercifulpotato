Item(by='mtlynch', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610200852, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25699361, text='&gt;<i>But listing books on how to do something that&#x27;s been discussed, and I don&#x27;t think I&#x27;m exaggerating here, millions of times, doesn&#x27;t seem like you&#x27;re &quot;helping developers find indie books.&quot;</i><p>Why shouldn&#x27;t there be indie books about subjects that are discussed frequently?<p>If something has been discussed millions of times, it means that the good information is scattered across several different sources and a lay person has no way of distinguishing between good and bad advice.<p>I&#x27;ve happily paid for indie books in the past few years from authors I trust because I want to read their perspective and information that they&#x27;ve curated.<p>Two I&#x27;ll mention in particular:<p><i>Hello Web App</i> by Tracy Osborn - This is a book about web design, which is discussed in millions of places, but Tracy&#x27;s book gathers together high-quality information in a concise book.<p><i>Starting &amp; Sustaining</i> by Garrett Dimon - This is about running a small SaaS app. Another topic discussed in tons of places online, but Garrett is telling it from the context of his personal experience running a successful SaaS app for several years.')
