Item(by='kllrnohj', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609306964, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25569695, text='You can write your own std::function, too, nor is it the only STL type that can take a capturing lambda (std::packaged_task for example).<p>A capturing lambda is just a class with an operator(). It&#x27;s complicated to do what std::function does, but fully possible.<p>In fact, custom std::function replacements have <i>better</i> lambda support than std::function itself. Such as unique_function in <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;Naios&#x2F;function2" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;Naios&#x2F;function2</a> which can handle non-copyable lambdas.')
