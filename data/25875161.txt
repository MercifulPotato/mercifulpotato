Item(by='core-questions', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611343324, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25874179, text='&gt; I believe Winamp became so popular because it had such a unique UI<p>Well, yes and no. Remember, Windows Media player used to be this:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.windows-media-player.com&#x2F;wp-content&#x2F;uploads&#x2F;2019&#x2F;12&#x2F;Media-Player-3.png" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.windows-media-player.com&#x2F;wp-content&#x2F;uploads&#x2F;2019...</a><p>and without system-installed codecs, which were hard to come by for something like MP3 files, it was basically useless; you could play MIDI files through your shitty OPL-3 FM synth and that&#x27;s about it.<p>Winamp came with a remarkable set of capabilities:\n- A bunch of useful codecs out of the box\n- An extensible plugin system, with input, output, general, and visualization plugins\n- Skinnable UI<p>The secret sauce was the extensibility, really. You could kill hours just tweaking your Winamp, installing crazy audio effect plugins, and so forth. It was really a new breed of application for most users who were used to really business-like apps.')
