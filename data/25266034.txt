Item(by='avery42', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606836935, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25265253, text='Like other commenters said, it&#x27;s just Linux so you can do pretty much anything.<p>For Qt, the current stack seems to be Kirigami, which is KDE&#x27;s library for convergent apps. There&#x27;s also the Mauikit framework, which is built on top of Kirigami (I don&#x27;t know enough to know the exact differences).<p>For Gtk, it&#x27;s libhandy, which adds a few widgets for convergent apps.<p>It looks like Flutter doesn&#x27;t support ARM yet, I found this after a quick search: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;flutter&#x2F;flutter&#x2F;issues&#x2F;60678" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;flutter&#x2F;flutter&#x2F;issues&#x2F;60678</a><p>There&#x27;s also a few Electron&#x2F;Webkit-based apps (especially for UBPorts), which is a nice temporary solution to get an app on the device while there isn&#x27;t yet a native equivalent.')
