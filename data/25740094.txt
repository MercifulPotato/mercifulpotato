Item(by='Spivak', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610409440, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25739845, text='I feel like the title is misleading to make it seem like social media platforms are the bad guys here. The article is actually about threatening loss of Section 230 protections to compel sites that host user-generated content to censor speech the government would not allowed to censor under the constitution.<p>It’s an interesting argument but it seems like it’s not a good characterization of what’s happening unless we’re specifically talking about deplatforming Parler which is decidedly not “big tech.” The gov’t is allowed to compel companies to censor speech that is not protected by the 1st amendment and those companies, not burdened by the legal 1st amendment, are allowed to comply by casting a broader net than the gov’t would legally be allowed to.')
