Most cats seem to develop a sort of pidgin[1] language in collaboration with you to communicate how they feel or what they want. It&#x27;s different for each cat, but is usually some combination of vocalization tones, body postures&#x2F;movements, eye contact, or even the location in the house they choose to do those things from.<p>They can be surprisingly great communicators.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Pidgin" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Pidgin</a>')
