If you let it stagnate, the hidden knowledge on how and - more importantly - WHY it does things gets lost.<p>If you write it once and fire all the programmers, every update is a huge undertaking with immense risks. But if you keep reiterating and refactoring it slowly over time, the code stays fresh and the knowledge isn&#x27;t lost.<p>You might change the language stack for the whole product during the iterations, maybe just use a fresh stack for a part of it. Maybe keep the old tech, but make it more readable and easier to integrate with other systems.')
