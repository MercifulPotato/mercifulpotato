Item(by='squeaky-clean', descendants=None, kids=[25988452], score=None, time=1612180340, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25986564, text='Investing your excess income as you get it isn&#x27;t really Dollar Cost Averaging the way firms talk about it. It&#x27;s called DCA on forums and such, but it&#x27;s really just investing your excess lump sum each time as soon as you get it.<p>Lump Sum vs DCA comparisons are usually made in the context that you have your lump sum now. Not that you&#x27;re accumulating a lump sum and trying to time the market for when to invest it. The page is titled &quot;How to invest a lump sum of money&quot; after all.<p>&gt; Suppose you received a windfall. Someone gave you a gift or you inherited a lot of money. Maybe you hit the lottery jackpot or got a huge bonus.<p>&gt; Here&#x27;s the question you face: Should you invest it all right away or in smaller increments over time, a strategy known as dollar-cost averaging?')
