Hints don&#x27;t count. Dogwhistles don&#x27;t count. <i>Impassioned political speech doesn&#x27;t count.</i><p>The fire in a theatre quote that everyone seems to know is even from an overturned case. (<i>Schenk v US</i>)<p>The <i>Brandenburg</i> case that overturned <i>Schenk</i> held:<p>If a threat is not immediate<p>OR<p>If it&#x27;s non-specific&#x2F; non-literal<p>Then it&#x27;s not criminal incitement, because it&#x27;s protected free speech.<p>Interpretations of &quot;what Trump meant&quot; don&#x27;t count, even if you believe with all your heart that he meant something other than the literal words spoken.')
