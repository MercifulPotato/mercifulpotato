Item(by='SAI_Peregrinus', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1601756546, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24673096, text='&gt; - Like the author I don&#x27;t know much about this topic, but for hashing BCrypt is pretty well known and appears well respected (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;BcryptNet&#x2F;bcrypt.net" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;BcryptNet&#x2F;bcrypt.net</a>)<p>bcrypt is a &quot;Password hashing function&quot;, not a cryptographic hash. bcrypt has tunable performance, it&#x27;s not particularly fast to start but can be made extremely slow (to thwart brute-forcing of weak passwords). It also takes multiple inputs (passphrase, salt, parameters) vs 1 parameter for a cryptographic hash (data to hash). For a cryptographic hash you probably want SHA2, Blake2, or Blake3.')
