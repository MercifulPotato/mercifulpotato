Item(by='superzadeh', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609334776, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25581409, text='TLDR; \n- it&#x27;s not fixed -&gt; but can vary over time with new jobs, new teams\n- it&#x27;s based on the measurement of what matters to you (your norms), not WHO you are acc. to a personality test \n- it&#x27;s also more recent research<p>16 personalities is a typical personality test, based on a trait-based approach: Myers-Briggs (originally based on Jung&#x27;s theory). The Stanford model we use is based on the work of Prof. Charles O&#x27;Reilly, who was studying team and company culture and how that relates to performance and satisfaction at work.<p>So the model we used in the product basically measures more &quot;what&#x27;s important to you at work&quot; and what your priorities are rather than &quot;who you are&quot; based on a pesonality model.<p>That also means that your mindset can change over the course of even just a few weeks, while personality is something more stable.')
