Item(by='perl4ever', descendants=None, kids=[25830444], score=None, time=1611028700, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25828956, text='It&#x27;s interesting that (I believe),<p>- bupropion is an antidepressant and an antismoking drug.<p>- naltrexone is an anti-opiate and anti-alcohol drug.<p>- bupropion&#x2F;naltrexone together is a weight loss drug.<p>After trying bupropion as an antidepressant, I would really like to try adding naltrexone, but my prescriber didn&#x27;t take me seriously, as I have neither an alcohol nor opiate nor weight problem.<p>I&#x27;m fascinated with the idea that when you try to increase a substance in your brain, it &quot;fights back&quot;, you get tolerance, the receptors get dulled, and so on, but if you <i>suppress</i> a receptor, then <i>more</i> is produced, which works better in the long run.')
