Item(by='crazygringo', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602343238, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24735242, text='Well I guess it depends on making a distinction between what consumers <i>think</i> they want, versus what they actually do.<p>Yes, people say they don&#x27;t want recommendations, because 95% of them are irrelevant.<p>But then the 5% (or 2% or 0.5%) turn out to be <i>super</i>-relevant, and they find new people to follow that they love, and learn about things they love, and the experience in the end turns out to be a net positive.<p>Their <i>actions</i> show that it&#x27;s valuable in the end. Otherwise the feature wouldn&#x27;t exist at all. Recommendations aren&#x27;t advertisements, sites don&#x27;t make money off them -- sites use them because people genuinely find things that lead them to use the sites more.')
