Item(by='autotech', descendants=None, kids=[25413815, 25413789, 25413894], score=None, time=1607921612, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25397628, text='Former dealer technician here, the Haynes books give most DIYers just enough rope to hang themselves. The wiring diagrams are never complete and a lot of info is missing outright. I guess it&#x27;s good enough for a casual person looking to maintain their own vehicle, but it is far from comprehensive. It was always a red flag when I would spot a Haynes book in a car I pulled. Sure to be a cleanup job after someone got in too deep. Haynes&#x27; choice to stop printing books is just another example of the shift away from printed materials. In the automotive industry, information aggregators like Alldata, Motor, Mitchell stopped doing printed service manuals many years ago. They even discontinued DVD service manuals. (Finally)')
