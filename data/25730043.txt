Item(by='tyingq', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610378429, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25728861, text='<i>&quot;Android mandates 4kb page sizes&quot;</i><p>I think the language they used is misleading. It&#x27;s more likely Android has introduced some bugs from assuming page sizes are always 4kb.<p><i>&quot;Whilst Ubuntu and other standard distros don&#x27;t care?&quot;</i><p>Many of the various different architectures Linux runs on have page sizes that aren&#x27;t 4kb.  It&#x27;s typically defined in a constant called &quot;PAGE_SHIFT&quot; in the arch&#x2F;&lt;ARCH&gt;&#x2F;include&#x2F;asm&#x2F;page.h file for the architecture.<p>For example, PAGE_SHIFT is 13 for OpenRisc: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;torvalds&#x2F;linux&#x2F;blob&#x2F;fcadab740480e0e0e9fa9bd272acd409884d431a&#x2F;arch&#x2F;openrisc&#x2F;include&#x2F;asm&#x2F;page.h#L21" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;torvalds&#x2F;linux&#x2F;blob&#x2F;fcadab740480e0e0e9fa9...</a><p>A quick way to convert that to the page size:<p><pre><code>  $ echo &quot;2^13&quot; | bc\n  8192</code></pre>')
