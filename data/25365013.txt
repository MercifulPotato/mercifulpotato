Item(by='_puk', descendants=None, kids=[25365404], score=None, time=1607548105, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25364719, text='And just to build on that thought.<p>(IMO) The only people who really care about open source are us, the philanthropic builders of said software.<p>If we shout down those in our community who question the status quo, by simply stating &quot;it&#x27;s not open source&quot;, then we lose those people.<p>Given the hard stance we see on open source, it&#x27;s very seldom that the reply to that is &quot;but why can&#x27;t it be&quot;.<p>An analogy that springs to mind is jslint (bear with me here..). A great idea that had true belief at heart, but was just too strict for mass adoption.<p>JSLint started a wave of enforcement that was needed, yet the far more flexible jshint (and subsequent eslint) won out in the long run.<p>I&#x27;m fully onboard with truly open source, and think there will always be a place for it. But feel that being all or nothing suffocates the discussion.')
