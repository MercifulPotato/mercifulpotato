Item(by='enriquto', descendants=None, kids=[24869757], score=None, time=1603451951, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24868236, text='Newton was just incredible.  The man polished his own lenses for his experiments.  This, although there were already professional lens makers in England at the time, and they were better than him.  Today, these antics would be dismissed as &quot;bad practice&quot;, &quot;reinventing the wheel&quot;, &quot;losing two weeks rewriting a stupid lens&quot; or some other corporate bullshit.  But his great insights on the nature of light came from the very act of polishing a glass, using finer and finer sand until it becomes transparent when the grain of the sand is half the wavelength of the light.')
