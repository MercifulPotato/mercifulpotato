Item(by='michaelt', descendants=None, kids=[24656569, 24655829], score=None, time=1601581129, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24655084, text='<i>&gt; Why is &quot;no color&quot; more accurate?</i><p>If that person is wearing an air force uniform and you colourise it to look more like an army uniform, or a different country&#x27;s uniform, you&#x27;ve made it less accurate as it looks like they&#x27;re in a different branch of the military.<p>If you&#x27;ve got a photo of someone whose hair was white from age, and you colourise it as blonde, you&#x27;ve made them look younger and maybe given them a hair colour they never had.<p>If you&#x27;ve got a photo of a famous scientist or war hero who had a Mediterranean complexion and you colourise them with more of a pale complexion, you&#x27;ve made it less accurate as it looks like they&#x27;re a different race.<p>(Of course, these sorts of accessibility-accuracy trade-offs  aren&#x27;t unique to colourising photos - they also apply to everything from translations of historical documents, through illustrations and selecting what content makes it into books, to questionably accurate history facts shared on social media)')
