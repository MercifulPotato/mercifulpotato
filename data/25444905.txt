Item(by='vladb38b', descendants=None, kids=[25445005], score=None, time=1608138039, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25444795, text='With n multiple tests you are much more likely to approach the mean of positive results, which for an actually positive patient is (1-p1)<i>n and for an actually negative patient is p2</i>n, where p1 and p2 are false result probabilities. If p1, p2 &lt;&lt; 1 then the means are sufficiently apart that you can compute the probability of getting your result (whatever that is) under the actual positive&#x2F;negative scenario and one probability will be significantly higher than the other.')
