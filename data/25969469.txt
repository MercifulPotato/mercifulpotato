Item(by='CobraKing', descendants=None, kids=[25969571], score=None, time=1612017986, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25969297, text='all things considered (i.e. equal performance on an interview&#x2F;test&#x2F;etc.) it makes better economic sense to hire more women and URMs. A cynic would say that&#x27;s because it&#x27;s more acceptable to pay less. But I would argue that someone who has built a successful career despite the headwinds of racism&#x2F;sexism&#x2F;implicit biases is going to be tougher and more resilient in the face of unexpected challenges than someone, who for reasons outside their control, has gone through life on an &#x27;easier difficulty level&#x27;.<p>And in fields where innovation matters, more diverse teams are more creative because they explore a larger space of ideas (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.pnas.org&#x2F;content&#x2F;117&#x2F;17&#x2F;9284" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.pnas.org&#x2F;content&#x2F;117&#x2F;17&#x2F;9284</a>).<p>To give another reason, a team that fits into a narrow demographic is unlikely to make products that appeal broadly or to other demographics. This creates market opportunities for more diverse teams. For instance, the Spanx founder was able to create a billion dollar company BECAUSE her inventions were dismissed by male-dominated teams in the garment industry.')
