Item(by='AlotOfReading', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610701570, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25786277, text='There&#x27;s a lot to unpack here and like any complex system, there&#x27;s a lot of history behind why things are a certain way. All of the following are likewise extreme simplifications of a messy reality.<p>1. Historically the reservation system evolved as a way to control and corral indigenous peoples. It continues to continues to exist today largely because natives want it to exist and there aren&#x27;t any better solutions. It gives them the ability to form their own governments, own their own land, and have their own quasi-sovereignty. They&#x27;re (nominally, not always practically) free to leave reservations whenever they like and many do.<p>2. The US had a couple centuries where the official policy on native peoples was &quot;integration&quot;. These years are widely regarded as constituting something called a &quot;cultural genocide&quot;. Suggesting it as a policy comes across... badly.<p>3. Pretty much everyone involved recognizes that logic has very little to do with the whole affair. It&#x27;s a complicated mess with a lot of sensitive areas, and more competing interests than you can possibly imagine.')
