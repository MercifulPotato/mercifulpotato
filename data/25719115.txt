Item(by='PaulDavisThe1st', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610309452, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25718981, text='Well, here&#x27;s the thing.<p>If you agree that the required ability is &quot;use my portable cell-connected computing device to exchange messages with others on their similar devices&quot;, then absolutely, I agree that there&#x27;s a duopoly there (I&#x27;m ignoring the possibility to use alternative app stores on Android, because it doesn&#x27;t seem particularly central).<p>But if you instead think the required ability is &quot;be able to exchange messages with others in more or less realtime&quot;, then the duopoly in the portable cell-connected computing device world is of much less importance.<p>For years, because I don&#x27;t have a cell phone, I was excluded from communications taking place via SMS (there were a few 1-way linux-accessible gateways, but they didn&#x27;t really change anything there). But I didn&#x27;t feel that my ability to communicate with others was really particularly impaired, and eventually Telegram (and others came along) that I could use from a desktop, and I was more or less back at parity for &quot;real time communication with others&quot;.<p>So whether or not the android&#x2F;ios duopoly matters in this sort of instance depends on precisely what functionality it is that you think people have a &quot;right&quot; to ...')
