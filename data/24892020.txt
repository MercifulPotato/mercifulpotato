Item(by='mikepurvis', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603676624, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24878342, text='&gt; Python offer many replacements for things that in Bash you need to call another process.<p>True, you&#x27;re never going to pipe something to grep in Python when you can just capture the output and use the inbuilt regex capability.<p>That said, it can be surprising how much worse performance-wise Python can be than shelling out to external utilities. Consider the simple case of downloading and extracting a tarball. I found that check_call(&quot;curl path&#x2F;to&#x2F;thing.tar | tar -x&quot;, shell=True) was <i>significantly</i> faster than anything I could figure out how to do with requests streaming, httpx (async), and the inbuilt tarfile module.')
