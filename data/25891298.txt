Item(by='sam_goody', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611489085, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25887223, text='There have been previous articles claiming that the expected cycle in most communities is that users will install panels + batteries first, for savings (of cash or of the environment ;).<p>At some point, as enough leave the grid, the utility company will install massive environmentally friendly  power sources (panels&#x2F;wind&#x2F;etc). At that point, it is not only cheaper to get green electric from the utility, it is more green (due to scale).<p>Battery packs like these have interest for a house, but they are also interesting for a city - like the Tesla packs in Australia et al.<p>If on the large scale they make sense, they should eventually leapfrog home usage, at which point there will be much less need for home packs and lithium.')
