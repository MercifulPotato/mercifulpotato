Item(by='KMag', descendants=None, kids=[25698829, 25696471], score=None, time=1610162395, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25695507, text='Porting code from SPARC to x86 is much easier than the other way around.  Most x86 instructions allow unaligned reads&#x2F;writes, whereas SPARC will SIGBUS on unaligned access (at least on the processors from the late 1990s).   The SPARC memory model is also more lax than x86, so x86 hides more concurrency bugs than x86 does.')
