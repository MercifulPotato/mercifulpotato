Item(by='Someone', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602428683, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24745897, text='If so, “sprite location could be calculated right up until the moment the first row was drawn to the screen” is a tad optimistic.<p>In theory, you could, but on the Atari 2600, during screen drawing, the CPU cycle budget for each line was 76 cycles (see <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cdn.hackaday.io&#x2F;files&#x2F;1646277043401568&#x2F;Atari_2600_Programming_for_Newbies_Revised_Edition.pdf" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cdn.hackaday.io&#x2F;files&#x2F;1646277043401568&#x2F;Atari_2600_Pr...</a>). Rounding up, that’s 40 instructions, and if the current line is different from the previous one, you’d have to update memory from that same budget.<p>Because of that, reading of user input and updating of player&#x2F;object positions typically&#x2F;always was done during the vertical blank interval.')
