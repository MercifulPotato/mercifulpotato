Item(by='jvns', descendants=None, kids=[25488134], score=None, time=1608485947, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25487866, text='my understanding of US stock options for Canadians (and really for anyone who can&#x27;t afford to exercise their options) is:<p>- having ISOs is bad, you don&#x27;t get any of the tax advantages that Americans get and it means that they expire 90 days after you leave the company if you don&#x27;t exercise them<p>- having NSOs that expire after 10 years is good, they can still be risky to exercise (same as with ISOs), but that&#x27;s not too bad because you can delay exercising for 10 years after you get them, and hopefully they&#x27;ll become liquid (or worthless) before then<p>- it&#x27;s possible for a company to switch employees&#x27; options from ISOs to NSOs (for example Pinterest did this <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;fortune.com&#x2F;2015&#x2F;03&#x2F;23&#x2F;pinterest-employee-taxes&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;fortune.com&#x2F;2015&#x2F;03&#x2F;23&#x2F;pinterest-employee-taxes&#x2F;</a>), so it might be worth putting pressure on your employer to offer that change if you&#x27;re in the bad (ISO) situation')
