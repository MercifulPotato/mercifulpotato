Item(by='jwalton', descendants=None, kids=[25402927], score=None, time=1607813729, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25400272, text='I built something like this a while ago, but mine is not nearly as nice.  I etched an interlaced finger pattern on a big copper clad board, then put a tented piece of metal overtop of that, then some lexan sheets with a graphic inbetween them on top, so when you step on the arrow, it squishes the metal plate on to the circuit board and closes the circuit.  It works remarkably well.  And then the whole thing is powered by an Arduino that pretends to be a keyboard: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;jwalton&#x2F;arduino-dancepad" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;jwalton&#x2F;arduino-dancepad</a>')
