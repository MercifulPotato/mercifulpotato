Item(by='qppo', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604000471, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24933972, text='When I was in college Fortinet pitched a capstone project for an automated doorman that tracked passersby, delivery people, residents, etc using cameras posted at doors.<p>We asked why they couldn&#x27;t use other biometrics which were much more established at the time (eg fingerprint scanners at the doorway), they said because other biometrics required consent. They wanted to track people without their knowing or agreement.<p>When asked about the ethics they said something along the lines of &quot;this is what our customers are asking for.&quot;<p>Point being you don&#x27;t need a privacy policy when you don&#x27;t have a reasonable expectation of privacy in the first place. They can take pictures of you, store them in a database, sell them if they want. You don&#x27;t have a right to be forgotten. And there are people that don&#x27;t see anything wrong with that.')
