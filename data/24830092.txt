Item(by='gamblor956', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603136543, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24829960, text='No, a preexisting attorney-client relationship isn&#x27;t always required. Offering advice pertaining to the application of laws or regulations to a specific person&#x27;s facts is generally considered offering legal advice.<p>Some states regulate this very heavily, others don&#x27;t care as much. Generally, California tends to regulate this pretty heavily for lawyers (for purposes of creating an attorney-client relationships that can subject them to malpractice claims) but otherwise not at all for non-lawyers unless they specifically hold themselves out to be in the business of providing legal advice (very common with immigration issues). So, offering random advice on the internet wouldn&#x27;t be an issue for a non-lawyer (but could be one for a practicing lawyer if they get too specific).')
