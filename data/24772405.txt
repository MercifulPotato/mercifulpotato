Item(by='peter303', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602640703, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24771354, text='Before there was the internet, many UNIX computers communicated by calling each other on telephone modems a few times a day. Among the exchanged texts were email and the discussion blog called usenet.<p>In the early there were only a few dozen to few hundred computers, mainly in universities and computer companies. You list all the networked computers in a single file called &#x2F;etc&#x2F;hosts.<p>In the late 1980s universal protocols and funding from Senator Al Gores 1991 Information Superhighway legislation allowed the computers anywhere in the world to interconnect.<p>My usenet email address from 1976 still works.')
