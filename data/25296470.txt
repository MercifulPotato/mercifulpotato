Item(by='dragonwriter', descendants=None, kids=[25296485], score=None, time=1607035986, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25295432, text='&gt; The H-1 is not the H-1B (in the same way that user ID 1030 in your database is not user ID 103) which is probably where the confusion is stemming from.<p>The H-1B is, under the statute, one of the subcategories within the H-1, and is covered by the same rules except to the extent they are explicitly distinguished. The H-1B1, another subcategory, <i>is</i> explicitly not included in the carve-out of the H-1 from the rule generally prohibiting immigration intent for nonimmigrant visas, which, so the H-1B1 is not, while other H-1s including the H-1B are, dual-intent visas.)<p>H-1 nonimmigrant visas are the set of visas governed by 8 USC Sec. 1101(a)(15)(h)(i), which included the H-1A [now defunct, formerly Sec 1101(a)(15)(H)(i)(a)], and includes the H-1B [Sec 1101(a)(15)(H)(i)(b)], the H-1B1 [Sec 1101(a)(15)(H)(i)(b1)], and the H-1C [Sec 1101(a)(15)(H)(i)(c)].<p>The relationship is not the same as ID 1030 to ID 103.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.law.cornell.edu&#x2F;uscode&#x2F;text&#x2F;8&#x2F;1101#a_15_H_i" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.law.cornell.edu&#x2F;uscode&#x2F;text&#x2F;8&#x2F;1101#a_15_H_i</a>')
