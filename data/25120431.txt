Item(by='TylerE', descendants=None, kids=[25120533], score=None, time=1605589439, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25120244, text='In languages with pattern matching (which I assume Elm has, although I&#x27;ve never used it personally, it&#x27;s very natural.<p>A portion of a binary tree walk in pseudo-code might look something like<p><pre><code>    match (s.left){ with\n      Some x -&gt; recurse(s.left)\n      Nothing -&gt; pass\n    }\n</code></pre>\nBasically the difference is that it forces you to deal both branches (In a typical pattern match, you either need to explicitly handle all subtypes, or have some sort of catch-all&#x2F;default case) and it makes sure you can&#x27;t call the &quot;do something&quot; branch when you actually have nothing.')
