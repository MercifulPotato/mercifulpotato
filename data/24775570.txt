Item(by='cywick', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602676978, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24775450, text='One might say that the original fault was to include a battery that was somewhat too small, so that after a bit of normal wear, the battery was no longer able to provide the voltage required for demanding situations (e.g., launching the Camera app).<p>Fortunately, they learned from that mistake and started to make iPhone batteries significantly more powerful. As a nice side effect, even a two-year old iPhone XR, for instance, still has excellent battery life today.')
