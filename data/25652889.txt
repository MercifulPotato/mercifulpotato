Item(by='AnimalMuppet', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609890182, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25652742, text='Getting deeper into a language you already know has value.  On the other hand, learning a new way of thinking about programming <i>also</i> has value.  The ideal might be to be T-shaped:  deep in one (or a few) languages, but broad at a shallow level.<p>In this view, you don&#x27;t have to spend much effort to keep up the skill over time, since you only knew it at a shallow level to begin with.<p>If you&#x27;re worried about too many esoteric languages on your resume, <i>don&#x27;t put them on your resume</i>.  You are under no obligation to state <i>everything</i> you know on there...<p>Is it a distraction?  Maybe.  Should you make it a serious project?  Perhaps not.  But there&#x27;s nothing wrong with stopping and smelling the roses (and the rare languages) every now and then.')
