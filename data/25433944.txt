Item(by='PaulDavisThe1st', descendants=None, kids=[25436100], score=None, time=1608060232, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25430084, text='&gt; People should be allowed to make their own choices in a free society.<p>When the rest of the &quot;free society&quot; has to bear the costs of certain choices, it doesn&#x27;t seem so illegitimate to argue that it has some interest in constraining those choices.<p>And there of course is the endless contradiction of being a social creature: on the one hand, being part of a society creates possibilities that were not there before, but also leads to constraints that were also not there before.<p>Endless political philosophers across centuries have grappled with how best to resolve this paradox, with no definitive answer at this point.')
