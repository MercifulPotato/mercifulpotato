Item(by='mschuster91', descendants=None, kids=[25825773], score=None, time=1610990565, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25823900, text='&gt; I am interested to understand what makes the &quot;raw&quot; costs so wildly different.<p>In the US, hospitals lose a <i>lot</i> of money on patients who don&#x27;t pay at all or pay pennies, and in order to not make a loss at the end of the year they charge those who can pay more money.<p>Also, insurances have an incentive in having hospitals set high &quot;sticker prices&quot; because then they can claim &quot;higher savings&quot; for their members.<p>Contrast to that, in Germany as long as a patient has any insurance (and 99.9999% of Germans do) the hospitals and doctors will get their services paid (so no need to overcharge for financial reasons), and both the mandatory insurance scheme and the private insurance companies pay fixed, government-regulated fees (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;de.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Einheitlicher_Bewertungsma%C3%9Fstab" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;de.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Einheitlicher_Bewertungsma%C3%...</a> for the government insurance, <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;de.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Geb%C3%BChrenordnung_f%C3%BCr_%C3%84rzte" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;de.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Geb%C3%BChrenordnung_f%C3%BCr_...</a> for the private insurance system).')
