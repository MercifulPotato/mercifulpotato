Item(by='clairity', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602784534, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24791035, text='having watched the media landscape over the past two decades, the market for real journalism is apparently small and shrinking. there&#x27;s not enough such money available to support it at its desired size.<p>&quot;real news&quot; loses to social media because it can&#x27;t compete on the novelty dopamine feedback loop (otherwise known as popularity): being the first to bring up new info or make a novel comment about it, getting a dopamine hit (esteem), then going back to the trough for more. old journalism is comparatively too slow and sparse at this.<p>so traditional news, seeing the writing on the wall, decided to join the fray instead of being crowded out. this won&#x27;t change until good journalism can compete for (enough) attention without being subsumed by the social novelty feedback loop.<p>it&#x27;s not so much about value or even actualizing journalists&#x27; activist stances (as others seem to be arguing), but the competitive dynamics of the market they&#x27;re in.')
