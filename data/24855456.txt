Zero cases in the country will never be realistic while we have people flying in, goods arriving on airplanes and ships.<p>We&#x27;ve learned not to freak out about cases that are in managed isolation, where new arrivals into the country are housed for 14 days until any Covid infections reveals itself.<p>We&#x27;re slowly learning that we need to be always checking in to businesses so that contract tracing is easy for the inevitable infections that slip through port workers or are late-blooming after quarantine ends. (Miraculously, no wider community infection from the latter)')
