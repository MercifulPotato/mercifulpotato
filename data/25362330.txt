This is not a trivial question as democracy, which is in essence a non-violent system for transition of power, relies on a nearly unanimous acceptance of the process and the results.<p>So, for introspection, and I know it is not a perfect analogy, but imagine you are at a scientific presentation, and you&#x27;re gut feeling tells you there is a flaw in the arguments presented. So you pose a question but instead of receiving an answer that might lay to rest your misgivings, the speaker just says &quot;That question is not allowed. Next.&quot;. How likely is it that that response changed your mind in favor of the theories presented?')
