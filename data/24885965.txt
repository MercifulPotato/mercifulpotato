Item(by='chrismorgan', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603630834, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24885922, text='It depends on the environment.<p>If you’re dealing with a web app registering itself as a protocol handler, the scheme is the highest precision you get: navigator.registerProtocolHandler(&quot;payto&quot;, &quot;<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;bank.example&#x2F;payto-handler&#x2F;%s" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;bank.example&#x2F;payto-handler&#x2F;%s</a>&quot;). So there, you only get the protocol level of granularity. You might therefore set up bank.example and bitcoin.example, and now when you click on a payto: link, the browser will say “do you want to open this link with bank.example or bitcoin.example?”<p>On mobile platforms, I’m not certain, but you may be able to have the bank app only register for payto:&#x2F;&#x2F;iban&#x2F; (and any other payment authorities it supports) and the Bitcoin app only register for payto:&#x2F;&#x2F;bitcoin&#x2F;.')
