Item(by='blix', descendants=None, kids=[25461628], score=None, time=1608241560, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25460588, text='Pork belly contains important macronutrients like protein and fat, which is part of why it tastes good. Despite that, I know I can stop eating it when choose to. Pork belly itself is not a problem; the problem is unhealthy eating habits. Thankfully, habits can be learned, unlearned, and modified.<p>&quot;X in moderation&quot; is pretty good advice for most X. It just turns out many people are quite bad at moderation, prefering instead to &quot;shove X into their mouth&quot; or otherwise consume in ways that promote overindulgence. Usually, these habits deserve scrutiny more than X. It possible to choose to eat in a different way.<p>Seriously, read that book. If you&#x27;ve studied &quot;serious diets,&quot; you should know their medium- and long-term success rates are absolutely dismal. Luckily it is not the only way. Working with the hard-to-modify aspects of food psychology is easier, more sustainable, more enjoyable, and more effective than fighting against them.')
