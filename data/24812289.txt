Item(by='Tainnor', descendants=None, kids=[24812700], score=None, time=1602961310, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24811306, text='There have been studies, and I know this also anecdotally from native speakers, that e.g. &quot;business English&quot;, at least in non-English countries, becomes its own, more restrictive variant of English because of the high percentage of non-native speakers. I know that some such native speakers have said that they feel their English got worse after living abroad for a while.<p>Of course, overall, this is a very slow process. People don&#x27;t stop applying the rules or simplify vocabulary over night or even within the span of a couple of years, but there is growing evidence in general that the more speakers a language has, the more it tends to simplify structurally, e.g. <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;royalsocietypublishing.org&#x2F;doi&#x2F;10.1098&#x2F;rsos.181274" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;royalsocietypublishing.org&#x2F;doi&#x2F;10.1098&#x2F;rsos.181274</a>')
