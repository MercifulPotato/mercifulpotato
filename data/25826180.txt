Item(by='SKILNER', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611001460, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25825244, text='Not only do we not know how to predict how long a software project will take, we don&#x27;t even know how to predict what the end product will look like.<p>So who are we kidding?<p>Another way to look at it: take a small one-person project and assign it to three different developers. You may get wildly different results. How could you have predicted those differences in advance? Let alone apply that type of prediction across a large team.<p>About a dozen years ago I gave a presentation to the Silicon Valley Software Process Improvement Network (does it still exist?)  My presentation: &quot;Unsolved Problems of Software Maintenance.&quot;  You think predicting greenfield development is difficult? Try predicting maintenance work, where figuring out what to do can be more than half the work.')
