Item(by='ergothus', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605799508, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25143405, text='Re: comparing to jQuery.<p>A lot of the ease and benefits depends on your style.<p>If your code pattern was to have variables holding your state and rendering html based on that, you will have an easy transition to React ( and likely didn&#x27;t suffer horrible pains with jQuery).<p>If your code style is to update the DOM to reflect your state, and when you need to know something about state, you read the DOM, then React will feel odd and complex (and you likely have had jQuery projects work great until they hit a level of complexity and then they suddenly become brittle and hard to change)<p>This is why I prefer React - the best practices if React are just the best practices for functions.  (That and I don&#x27;t like the pseudo js syntax of Vue with the mysterious scoping)')
