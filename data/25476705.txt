Item(by='chiefalchemist', descendants=None, kids=[25476722], score=None, time=1608376605, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25476266, text='Companies do this, but I&#x27;m not so sure smart is the right adjective. Better positioned, or risk adverse might be better description.<p>Effectively, this argues for seeding a new market (e.g., IBM and enterprise OSS). Makes sense. But what&#x27;s to stop _anyone_ else from getting into that same business? And doing it better? Maybe you create an initial advantage, but for how long? Game consoles come to mind.<p>It&#x27;s an effective strategy. But you have to be spot on in the execution, and always be looking over your shoulder. Wouldn&#x27;t a Zero to One &#x2F; Blue Ocean mindset be smarter? Yes, more difficult. But none the less the far bigger win.')
