Item(by='shagie', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609021553, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25546285, text='One company that I worked at used a NetLi (now part of Akamai).  The approach that they used (IIRC) had multiple data centers closer to the end users and then a server in each client&#x27;s datacenter.  The server in the client data center and the servers in the edge data centers would establish a pre-negotiated connection that had its TCP parameters optimized for its traffic... and then serve compressed traffic over that connection.<p>It was pretty impressive back in the days of CGI and JSP - before the presentation was pushed entirely out to the browser and everything was rendered on the server.')
