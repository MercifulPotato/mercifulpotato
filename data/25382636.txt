Item(by='donw', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607664454, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25381985, text='&gt; Yeah probably true, I didn&#x27;t post a source because I thought it was common knowledge which in retrospect I&#x27;m not sure why I thought that.<p>There&#x27;s a name for this phenomenon! It&#x27;s a cognitive bias called the &quot;Curse of Knowledge&quot;: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Curse_of_knowledge" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Curse_of_knowledge</a><p>In a nutshell, we instinctively assume others know what we know. This was probably true when we lived in small tribes on the Serengeti, but not so much in the global information age.<p>It takes practice, but you can grow the habit of always providing a little extra context without sounding patronizing, as well, at least in-person, a better sense for when people are following the conversation.<p>What helped me grow this skill was <i>lots</i> of pair-programming, because that daily pair rotation forces you to be really good at context-sharing on the quick.')
