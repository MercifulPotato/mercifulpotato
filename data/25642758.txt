Item(by='Pfhreak', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609827926, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25642703, text='That&#x27;s entirely because of the way our procurement process works. We have separate capital and maintenance procurement processes. Typically, we splurge on a high capital budget (the cost to build housing) and then skimp or cut out the maintenance budget. Or worse, we expect the housing to be self supporting.<p>Public housing done right is distributed, low rise apartments rented below market rate and not expected to be self supporting.<p>As a tax payer, it&#x27;s so frustrating to see us build good things and then totally neglect to maintain them.<p>I will say that high rise public housing in the US introduces complexity because it necessitates a degree of service infrastructure that&#x27;s more costly than more, lower buildings.')
