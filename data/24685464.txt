Item(by='buran77', descendants=None, kids=[24686089], score=None, time=1601883909, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24685300, text='I&#x27;m sure that in certain contexts you might even throw in a totally bogus, invented term and a human would be perfectly able to match this with the assumption that you&#x27;re referring to &quot;a group&quot; just from context. Tell someone you saw a &quot;rowlow of ravens all flying in unison&quot; and they&#x27;ll just assume you used a neologism, perhaps with some doubt or uncertainly in the back of the mind.<p>The weird thing about the &quot;murder of crows&quot; or a &quot;parliament of rooks&quot; is the use of such common words, especially slightly shocking ones like &quot;murder&quot;. This raises more eyebrows and feels more like a joke for the unaware.<p>A &quot;wisdom of wombats&quot;, a &quot;lamentation of swans&quot;... An &quot;ambush of tigers&quot; would certainly be interpreted as the act of ambushing.<p>Look at the list here [0] and you&#x27;ll find it very random.<p>[0] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;List_of_animal_names" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;List_of_animal_names</a>')
