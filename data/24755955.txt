Item(by='Nextgrid', descendants=None, kids=[24756969], score=None, time=1602519156, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24755775, text='To be clear, I am absolutely not arguing that malware authors should get impunity - we have other laws such as the CFAA that should be used to punish this activity.<p>I am also not saying that software developers should automatically be liable for bugs or security vulnerabilities in their products.<p>My concern is that such a precedent would then allow software developers to sue on brand damage grounds even in non-malicious cases such as merely documenting an exploit or publishing a proof of concept (designed to test the vulnerability on your own infrastructure), in which case even &quot;mens rea&quot; would not apply since technically documentation&#x2F;PoC code is <i>intended</i> to let others know about the vulnerability (so they can protect themselves, but that does technically damage the brand, although rightfully in this case).')
