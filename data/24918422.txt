Item(by='mannykannot', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603891402, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24916497, text='Considerable vagueness is to be expected in any field so far from an explanation, and the situation is even worse in the dualist camp: to the best of my knowledge, they have never been able to offer one definite, <i>affirmative</i> claim about how the alleged non-physical aspects of the mind work. All they do is weave tendentious arguments against there ever being a physical explanation of the mind.<p>Emergent phenomena are pervasive in complex systems, and this stands as an effective response to naive dualist misconceptions such as that materialism requires that a quale must be identical with a single physical thing.<p>Even in airplanes, behavioral traits such as stability emerge from the interaction of several physical features.')
