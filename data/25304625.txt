Item(by='ethbr0', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607100906, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25303821, text='The relative trajectories of Java and JavaScript over the years are fascinating.<p>Java became a language that you can toss a 100-developers onto a project for, and incrementally grind out something that has a mostly-consistent architecture. Aka the perfect IBM Services language.<p>JavaScript became a language you can toss up a SPA in a day from boilerplate glueing together frameworks and only writing the use-specific bits. Aka the perfect web developer language.<p>As other commenter said, bad developers are probably a consequence of demand &gt; supply for any language.')
