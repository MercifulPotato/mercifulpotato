Item(by='lukifer', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606593228, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25239631, text='To be clear: I was neither advocating for or against lockdowns. I&#x27;m advocating thoughtfulness of trade-offs, informed by (but not limited to) data; as opposed to rigid ideology, or knee-jerk gut reactions. And in particular: to recognize that even if one believes that the data of a specific real-world circumstance clearly favors one end of the spectrum, there are also circumstances where the data can break the other way. Therefore we need to be tolerant and open-minded to good-faith skepticism in either direction.<p>The fact that a wrong answer can be fatal makes it all the <i>more</i> important to avoid what Daniel Schmachtenberger refers to as &quot;limbic highjacking&quot;, or more colloquially, &quot;being triggered&quot; (which includes both fear of death, and fear of losing autonomy).<p>&gt; ends justifying the means... the trend of a growing security state<p>It&#x27;s also worth remembering that these are not mutually exclusive: that circumstances exist where the ends <i>do</i> justify the means, yet are <i>also</i> used as a cynical power grab with additional negative externalities. Again, the only way to know the difference is dispassionate discernment (what Julia Galef refers to as &quot;scout mindset&quot; rather than &quot;soldier mindset&quot; [0]).<p>[0] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=3MYEtQ5Zdn8" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=3MYEtQ5Zdn8</a>')
