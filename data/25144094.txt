I had straight answers for each of them.<p>But if I worked in a bank, I wouldn&#x27;t use an ML behavioral security tool to stop bad guys, I&#x27;d use it to identify, model, and detect top traders so I could double down on their bets. Seriously, if the model actually worked, why would you waste it catching hackers? Arguably, if the underlying tech is not useful for that, it&#x27;s not useful for anything.<p>What it comes down to is, &quot;the confusion matrix is the product.&quot; The mistake ML tech companies seem to make is thinking that their product applies to a given vertical (banking, health, govt, etc) , but in fact the balance of that confusion matrix applies to companies whose growth stage defines their needs, imo.')
