That people should learn programming one specific way.<p>I think this idea is often well-intentioned. For JavaScript, it has been &quot;you don&#x27;t need jquery&quot; for a long time. And you probably don&#x27;t need jquery, but I generally think you should feel free to use tools that help you accomplish your goals.<p>I agree that learning concurrency primitives in c++ is useful and rewarding, but pedagogically I don&#x27;t endorse any particular entry point to parallelism.<p>That said, once you&#x27;ve found that you do need concurrency for your task, yes, absolutely learn the fundamentals. They&#x27;re called fundamentals for a reason.')
