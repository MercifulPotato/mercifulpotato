Item(by='Robotbeat', descendants=None, kids=[24714146, 24714107], score=None, time=1602103769, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24712360, text='NASA has solved physics, though:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.nasa.gov&#x2F;X59" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.nasa.gov&#x2F;X59</a><p><i>The Low-boom Flight Demonstration mission has two goals: 1) design and build a piloted, large-scale supersonic X-plane with technology that reduces the loudness of a sonic boom to that of a gentle thump; and 2) fly the X-plane over select U.S. communities to gather data on human responses to the low-boom flights and deliver that data set to U.S. and international regulators.<p>Using this data, new sound-based rules regarding supersonic flight over land can be written and adopted, which would open the doors to new commercial cargo and passenger markets to provide faster-than-sound air travel.</i>')
