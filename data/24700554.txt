Item(by='Diederich', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602006967, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24693195, text='Curious thing.  The Chattahoochee River divides the US states of Alabama and Georgia, the former being on Central time and the latter being on Eastern time.  There&#x27;s a city in Georgia called Columbus, and it&#x27;s right up against the river, and so is adjacent to Alabama and Central time.<p>Years ago we visited my wife&#x27;s family in Columbus with some frequency, once we visited the Wal-Mart supercenter in Phenix City (yes, it&#x27;s spelled way wrong) Alabama, which is right on the eastern border of Alabama, also next to the river.  Since I worked in Wal-Mart Information Systems at the time, I had quite a bit of access and noted that the store&#x27;s systems were on Eastern time, even though the entire state of Alabama is on Central.<p>Sure enough: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Phenix_City,_Alabama" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Phenix_City,_Alabama</a> &quot;Phenix City lies immediately west across the Chattahoochee River from the much larger Columbus, Georgia and observes Eastern Time on a de facto basis (in contrast to the rest of Alabama, which observes Central Time) due to Phenix City&#x27;s strong economic ties to Columbus.&quot;<p>The Olson Database is amazing; I&#x27;ve non-trivially interacted with it a number of times in my career.<p>Even so, the final, on the ground authority is the people who use and are affected by time zones.')
