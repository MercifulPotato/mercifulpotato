Item(by='abdullahkhalids', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603374112, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24857459, text='I agree that we need to discuss how inalienable is the right to privacy, especially digital privacy.<p>But note that you can not sell your organs, because that will (and does on the black market) quickly create highly coercive contracts between individuals, or worse organ robbery. You can, however, donate your organs.<p>I would see a similar scheme for your digital data. That, most of it cannot be sold [1]. It can be donated by your positive assent, but the receiver (businesses and such) are highly regulated to ensure they don&#x27;t exploit you or society using that data.<p>[1] I concede that there might be certain types of data that could be sold, though I cannot think of an example.')
