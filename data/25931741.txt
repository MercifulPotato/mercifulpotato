Item(by='Youden', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611771563, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25930757, text='So many ways:<p>- If you own a stock and want to reduce the downside risk, you can buy a put option to ensure you&#x27;ll always be able to sell your stock for a reasonable price, even if there&#x27;s a financial crash.<p>- If you have the ability to produce some product (e.g. grain) but need some extra money (e.g. to buy seed or fix a tractor) or just want to lock in a sale price for your product, you can sell a futures contract.<p>- If you want need some product at some point in the future (e.g. fuel for your jets) and want to lock in the price, you can buy a futures contract.<p>Fundamentally, derivatives allow you to trade risk. You can pay somebody to absorb some risk for you or you can accept some risk in exchange for money.')
