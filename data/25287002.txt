Either have good relations with your end users or use QA with properly outfit machines.</i><p>It doesn’t have to be either&#x2F;or, it can be both. Developers absolutely should be <i>using</i> their own software frequently, seeing and feeling how well it performs; trying it out on a lower-spec machine is always a useful exercise.<p>That doesn’t mean you can’t also have dedicated QA. Developers shouldn’t have to be <i>in charge</i> of QA, but they should have some involvement.')
