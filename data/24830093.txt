Item(by='flubert', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603136549, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24824142, text='&gt;But what about the people who will be rejected because of their genes?<p>That is illegal in the U.S., with the Genetic Information Nondiscrimination Act (GINA)<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.genome.gov&#x2F;about-genomics&#x2F;policy-issues&#x2F;Genetic-Discrimination" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.genome.gov&#x2F;about-genomics&#x2F;policy-issues&#x2F;Genetic-...</a><p>&gt;GINA prohibits health insurers from discrimination based on the genetic information of enrollees. Specifically, health insurers may not use genetic information to determine if someone is eligible for insurance or to make coverage, underwriting or premium-setting decisions. Furthermore, health insurers may not request or require individuals or their family members to undergo genetic testing or to provide genetic information.')
