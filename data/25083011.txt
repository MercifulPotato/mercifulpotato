The study compares questionnaire results of healthy controls (HC) to people with a history of major depressive disorder (MDD):<p>&gt; Cross-sectional analysis of 84,860 participants showed that in both MDD and HCs, physical activity, healthy diet, and optimal sleep duration were associated with less frequency of depressed mood (all p &lt; 0.001; ORs 0.62 to 0.94), whereas screen time and also tobacco smoking were associated with higher frequency of depressed mood (both p &lt; 0.0001; ORs 1.09 to 1.36).<p>This is correlation; it does not measure whether lifestyle interventions improve depressed mood.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;bmcmedicine.biomedcentral.com&#x2F;articles&#x2F;10.1186&#x2F;s12916-020-01813-5" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;bmcmedicine.biomedcentral.com&#x2F;articles&#x2F;10.1186&#x2F;s1291...</a>')
