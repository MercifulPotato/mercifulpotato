Item(by='BearOso', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605306063, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25086039, text='As a Linux user, it unfortunately doesn&#x27;t have a definitive anything.<p>Say you want to write a GUI app.<p>On Windows, you&#x27;ve got Win32. It&#x27;s old, but it&#x27;ll still work and look new.<p>MacOS you have Cocoa.<p>Linux, you have Xlib. Wait, that doesn&#x27;t provide enough. We have to use a toolkit, like Gtk or Qt. Which one if we want to look integrated? How do we integrate 3D APIs?<p>Then there&#x27;s Wayland. You want to use that instead because X is deprecated. But there&#x27;s no extensive API--it&#x27;s basically only a way of putting rectangles on the screen. You have nothing to get you started unless you stick hard to Gtk, Qt, or do really rudimentary graphics, and cross-compatibility is really ugly.')
