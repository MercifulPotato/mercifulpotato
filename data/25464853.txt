Item(by='Someone', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608278098, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25462835, text='On IBM punched cards, the letters needn’t look very different, as you could always look at the punches (digits have only one punch; also look at the patterns for letters A-I, J-R, and S-Z)<p>And yes, people learned that. Because the primary output of a puncher were the holes, not the printed text, maintenance of the ‘print’ part wasn’t always done well (ink smear or devices that ran out of ink), resulting in print output didn’t look remotely as clean as that manual shows.')
