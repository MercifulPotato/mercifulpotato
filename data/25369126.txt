That seems like a perfectly acceptable reason for an API to exist, and a reasonable tradeoff.<p>Follow up with big angry warnings about extensions that use the less-safe APIs, and let users choose.  As opposed to now, where all permissions are quite calmly displayed.  &quot;Half of all malicious extensions use this API, we strongly advise you to look for an alternative&quot;[1] is very different than &quot;x wants permission to modify requests&quot;.<p>The failings of the current permissions system doesn&#x27;t mean users <i>are incapable of deciding</i>.  It means the current system doesn&#x27;t work for users.  Either it&#x27;s unsolvable for the majority (unlikely IMO), or it&#x27;s not matching what users need to know to decide.  I thoroughly support making better APIs (declarativeNetRequest is plenty useful, and makes decent tradeoffs), but that doesn&#x27;t have to be at the cost of other things, nor is some &quot;make everything safe&quot; dream the only option.<p>[1]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;blog.chromium.org&#x2F;2019&#x2F;06&#x2F;web-request-and-declarative-net-request.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;blog.chromium.org&#x2F;2019&#x2F;06&#x2F;web-request-and-declarativ...</a>')
