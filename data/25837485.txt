Item(by='twoodfin', descendants=None, kids=[25837583], score=None, time=1611083718, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25836398, text='This discussion reminds me of the Alloy[1] language and the associated analyzer.<p>The analyzer transforms an Alloy model and some formal statement about it (e.g. &quot;a caller can never connect to callee without the callee&#x27;s consent to that caller connecting&quot;) into a giant SAT problem, which it feeds to a SAT solver looking for counterexamples, which it can map back to a series of valid state transitions in the original model.<p>Very cool stuff, at least 20 years ago when I was playing with it.<p>[1] <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;alloytools.org&#x2F;about.html" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;alloytools.org&#x2F;about.html</a>')
