Item(by='blueblisters', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606055362, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25176230, text='Looks like they made the classic Streisand effect mistake - attempts to clamp down on critical voices brought more attention to them. For a startup that ramped up and exited so quickly, they don&#x27;t seem particularly smart.<p>Notwithstanding the marketing, I don&#x27;t think the product <i>itself</i> is egregious. Their primary sell is teaching logic (&quot;coding&quot;) to kids through 1:1 tutoring sessions.  Many Indian upper&#x2F;middle class households already send their kids to in-person tutors at a very young age, and looks like Whitehat Jr found a niche that could cater to that market during the pandemic.<p>1:1 tutoring can be highly effective, creating a 2-sigma improvement in student performance (<a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;web.mit.edu&#x2F;5.95&#x2F;readings&#x2F;bloom-two-sigma.pdf" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;web.mit.edu&#x2F;5.95&#x2F;readings&#x2F;bloom-two-sigma.pdf</a>). Of course, Whitehat Jr likely doesn&#x27;t follow specific structured pedagogy to get this advantage, and maybe the target age group is too young to get any benefits.')
