Item(by='mumblemumble', descendants=None, kids=[25958021], score=None, time=1611927768, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25956421, text='So, first off, a disclaimer: I&#x27;m a partisan of classical TDD.<p>With that out of the way, I would recommend using a test double in exactly one kind of situation: When it&#x27;s genuinely awkward to use the real thing. Concrete examples include talking to a third-party API, testing for error conditions that are difficult or impossible to reliably organically produce, and when the real dependency hasn&#x27;t actually been implemented yet.<p>As for the different kinds of test doubles, Fowler has a concise overview at <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;martinfowler.com&#x2F;bliki&#x2F;TestDouble.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;martinfowler.com&#x2F;bliki&#x2F;TestDouble.html</a> that is just about the clearest explanation I&#x27;ve seen of the different kinds and what they&#x27;re used for.')
