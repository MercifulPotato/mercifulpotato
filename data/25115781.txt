Item(by='RichardCA', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605554255, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25113448, text='The galactic center roughly aligns with the constellation Sagittarius, which is considered a southern constellation.<p>If you try to do milky way astrophotography in the northern hemisphere you quickly find out you are limited to the late spring &#x2F; early summer, because you want to be seeing the southern sky at night. Most of the year the sun is too close to Sagittarius during daylight hours, making viewing impossible.')
