Item(by='mattbee', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606942683, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25276264, text='In the UK they already add VAT to their commission fees, which is what Airbnb is &quot;selling&quot; e.g. if you pay £100 for a stay in a UK-based short let, Airbnb&#x27;s fee on that might be £15, of which £12.50 is their fee, and £3 is 20% VAT.  £85 is held in trust for the host, and passed on direct.<p>So Airbnb&#x27;s income on that transaction is £12.50, not £100.<p>Hosts do sell their properties on multiple platforms, and their income determines whether they have to register for VAT.  In Canada it&#x27;s apparently $30,000 per quarter, in the UK it&#x27;s about £85,000 per year.  So lots of Airbnb hosts <i>don&#x27;t</i> currently have to pay VAT.')
