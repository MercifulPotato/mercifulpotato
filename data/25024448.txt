It was called OLE (Object Linking and Embedding). You could embed a photoshop document live in your word document and if you clicked the embedded document the photoshop UI for editing would appear.<p>It turned out to be absolutely horrible. Maybe it was before it&#x27;s time with computers having only a few meg of memory and being slower than today but it seemed more of an issue because things like UI, when you click the embedded document, what should happen? How much of photoshop&#x27;s ui should be shown vs Word&#x27;s (the outer app). It was also a nightmare because unless every user had the exact same apps on the exact same version nothing worked.<p>Your embedded photo uses filter XYZ but that&#x27;s only in version 7+ of whatever app edited it, etc...<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Object_Linking_and_Embedding" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Object_Linking_and_Embedding</a>')
