Item(by='PaulDavisThe1st', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610041794, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25673985, text='Here&#x27;s the thing.<p>If you have an expectation that every event in history is accompanied by the instigators writing&#x2F;saying &quot;Let&#x27;s all go do an X&quot; (where X might be &quot;coup&quot; or &quot;revolution&quot; or whatever), you&#x27;re going to be disappointed.<p>Most often, it&#x27;s only historical hindsight that forms the narrative in which &quot;X said Y which led to Z&quot;.<p>If you&#x27;re somewhat sympathetic towards Trump, then likely only utterly unambiguous language - &quot;So listen up everyone, I want you, in fact I order you, to go to the Capitol, get inside, prevent the certification from taking place, by any means necessary, including violence&quot; - would convince you that he had incited a coup (and perhaps not even that).<p>Out in the real world, few people speak in such unambiguous ways even if their goals are clear. That&#x27;s why we often need trials to establish whether or not certain language had intent A or intent B, because the language used was not unambiguous (often deliberately so).')
