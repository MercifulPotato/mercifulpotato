Item(by='oftenwrong', descendants=None, kids=[25123564], score=None, time=1605618248, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25121416, text='I have often thought about what a truly intuitive, simple, easy, user-friendly VCS would look like. Git is fine for programmers, but what about non-programmers, or student-programmers? Would it be possible to make a VCS that my mother could use without sacrificing too much power?<p>One thing I would give up easily is decentralisation - a centralised model is more straightforward, and, for better or for worse, most people use Git in a centralised fashion.<p>Conflict resolution is one of the hardest version control tasks for beginners. Maybe it could be made easier by making the VCS AST-aware for more legible diffs, or by automating conflict resolution altogether with CRDTs or OT.<p>There were some interesting, very different takes on version control envisioned for darklang:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;blog.darklang.com&#x2F;how-dark-deploys-code-in-50ms&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;blog.darklang.com&#x2F;how-dark-deploys-code-in-50ms&#x2F;</a>')
