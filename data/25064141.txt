That could be the case, although if they were taking a substantial loss it&#x27;s unlikely they would hold on for as long as they have.<p>YouTube also has the benefit of having monopoly power over its market - to their credit, no one else, ever, has had a platform where an independent video creator could start with a handful of viewers and build up to millions. But since they have all of the creators already, and starting a competitor is extremely capital-intensive, there aren&#x27;t any competitors at anything near their scale. For the most part the usual way to find other YouTube creators is YouTube, via their recommendation algorithm. There is a significant lock-in effect.<p>Since they have monopoly power, they should be able to find ways to monetize their platform, even if potentially contentious.
