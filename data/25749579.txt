Item(by='endominus', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610470398, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25748150, text='&gt; What has been censored effectively?<p>&gt;As far as I&#x27;ve been aware, legal censorship has never worked.<p>If you hold &quot;worked&quot; to the impossible standard of &quot;completely eradicated its target&quot; then no, just as basically no policy or initiative has ever &quot;worked.&quot; Hell, polio still exists![0] By the much more reasonable standard of &quot;suppressed the dissemination and popularization of its target&quot; then there are a plethora of examples:<p>- Libel and slander laws successfully limit outright lies reported by the media.<p>- Various governments censor and punish criticism of themselves or specific officials.<p>- Copyright law successfully limits propagation of media.<p>- Certain content - typically sexual or graphically violent - cannot legally be broadcast.<p>- You are legally barred from lying about, for example, the ingredients of a product you produce or its efficacy.<p>- State secrets are heavily censored, and those who circumvent this censorship are heavily punished.<p>Not to mention individual cases of censorship, such as whether a man may publish his own autobiography.[1] That particular case was eventually decided in his favor, but there was no guarantee of that.<p>[0] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.npr.org&#x2F;sections&#x2F;goatsandsoda&#x2F;2020&#x2F;10&#x2F;30&#x2F;929080692&#x2F;the-campaign-to-wipe-out-polio-was-going-really-well-until-it-wasnt" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.npr.org&#x2F;sections&#x2F;goatsandsoda&#x2F;2020&#x2F;10&#x2F;30&#x2F;9290806...</a><p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.theguardian.com&#x2F;music&#x2F;2015&#x2F;may&#x2F;20&#x2F;concert-pianist-james-rhodes-wins-right-to-publish-autobiography" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.theguardian.com&#x2F;music&#x2F;2015&#x2F;may&#x2F;20&#x2F;concert-pianis...</a>')
