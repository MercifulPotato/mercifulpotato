Item(by='maxerickson', descendants=None, kids=[24969274, 24969506], score=None, time=1604331093, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24969085, text='<i>Does the term &quot;herd immunity&quot; make sense for a seasonal virus at all?</i><p>Yes. For instance, one of the benefits of widespread flu vaccination is that it slows the rate that the virus spreads at. Herd immunity is just the threshold where enough people have immunity that the spread diminishes. That we don&#x27;t necessarily reach the threshold doesn&#x27;t make the lesser effect useless.')
