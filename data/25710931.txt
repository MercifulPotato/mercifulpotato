Item(by='ashton314', descendants=None, kids=[25713985, 25712444, 25711517, 25713099, 25711449], score=None, time=1610257575, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25708099, text='The tone is perhaps a <i>tad</i> melodramatic, but that keeps it fun to read!<p>I think there’s a question worth asking here that I don’t thing gets asked enough: what are the decisions we want to leave to humans? What decisions do we want to leave to <i>individual</i> humans?<p>This article reminded me of Neil Postman’s <i>Technopoly: The Surrender of Culture to Technology</i>. I’ve written my thoughts on it here.[^1] His primary thesis is that all technology has a trade-off and an agenda of how it wants to be used, and more importantly, we are often blind to that trade-off and agenda.<p>For simple technologies, like hammers and spoons, the danger is negligible. For more powerful technologies like writing, telegraphy, and statistics, the dangers are much harder to see.<p>I highly recommend <i>Technopoly</i> if this article struck a cord with you.<p>[^1]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;lambdaland.org&#x2F;posts&#x2F;2020-08-16-book-review-technopoly-v2&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;lambdaland.org&#x2F;posts&#x2F;2020-08-16-book-review-technopo...</a>')
