Item(by='musicale', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610952153, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25809409, text='&gt; It&#x27;s about having paid for an iOS app and thinking that you now have the right to run it on a completely different type of device, and also presumably on many more devices (given how Apple allows installs for any device hooked to a given Apple ID).<p>You might think that with the &quot;App Store&quot; you were, you know, buying the App. You might think you could run it however you like, or even sell it to someone else if you wanted to (e.g. first sale doctrine.)<p>But of course the App Store isn&#x27;t actually a &quot;store&quot; that sells &quot;apps&quot; at all rather but a &quot;limited licensing service&quot; enforced by Apple&#x27;s DRM. You&#x27;re getting a limited license to use the App, and if you use it on an Apple device then Apple&#x27;s DRM will usually impose specific limits on how you can use it.<p>I like the App Store, but it&#x27;s poorly named. I guess &quot;App Limited License Store&quot; wasn&#x27;t catchy enough.')
