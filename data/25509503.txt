Selling all your movies for $1 a pop may kill most piracy, but it will kill your movie studio too. Selling streams for $500 per person will price most people out, regardless of whether piracy exists or not. Piracy hurts profits regardless of pricing. iTunes did not make up for the death of CDs at all. Digital sales have never even made up a third of what physical sales used to be: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.riaa.com&#x2F;u-s-sales-database&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.riaa.com&#x2F;u-s-sales-database&#x2F;</a><p>Personally, I don&#x27;t think consumers have a right to tell creators, &quot;You have to sell that to me for $x or I will take it without paying.&quot; Companies certainly aren&#x27;t good, but they&#x27;re not typically stupid either. They&#x27;re not going to price things to a point where people can&#x27;t afford them. I think pirates just lie to themselves a lot to justify their actions.')
