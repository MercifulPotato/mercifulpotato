We&#x27;re lucky that they align with environmental concerns in this case.<p>Additionally, right to repair isn&#x27;t really about <i>individual</i> freedoms. Most people aren&#x27;t going to fix products that require complex repairs. The right to repair is, first and foremost, about allowing people to exercise that right <i>through delegation</i> to specialists. Unencumbered repairability allows for local markets of repair shops to form, that can offer repairs at reasonable price points.')
