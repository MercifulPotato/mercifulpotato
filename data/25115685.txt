Item(by='onlyrealcuzzo', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605553802, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25112987, text='For the most beginner of beginners - I would say that the best advice is to control the center of the board, play aggressively &#x2F; make an attacking move whenever possible, and <i></i>DON&#x27;T<i></i> be afraid to trade pieces.<p>If you don&#x27;t have a move that attacks your opponent and forces them to play defense, develop your least active piece.<p>Also, learn the King&#x27;s Indian opening. You can use it as black or white, and it&#x27;s one of the easiest openings. I also think it&#x27;s a good opening against beginners, because they make a lot of mistakes against this. So, 1) you&#x27;ll be able to beat beginners - and winning is always more fun than losing; and 2) you&#x27;ll learn to watch for your opponents to make mistakes - and that will help you learn to avoid making common mistakes.<p>[1] Controlling the center of the board and making attacking moves - <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=g5jTMqdYXWM&amp;t=1100s" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=g5jTMqdYXWM&amp;t=1100s</a><p>[2] King&#x27;s Indian opening - <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=kK0cq6UBt1Y" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=kK0cq6UBt1Y</a><p>These two videos got me from ~700 to ~1000 in about a week. And they got me to the point where Chess was actually fun instead of a being a frustrating minefield I tried to navigate until I inevitably made a blunder and self-imploded.')
