This data is almost irrelevant to majority of their audience trying to deduce something out of it to help their buying decisions.<p>In addition to usage patterns, there&#x27;s a lot more that contributes to mean time between failure of hard drives; like operating temperature (ambient, fluctuations, max), relative humidity, vibration and alike.<p>There&#x27;s also well researched and published literature around this.<p>From Microsoft 2016: Finds relative humidity to be the dominant factor for disk failure\n<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.usenix.org&#x2F;system&#x2F;files&#x2F;conference&#x2F;fast16&#x2F;fast16-papers-manousakis.pdf" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.usenix.org&#x2F;system&#x2F;files&#x2F;conference&#x2F;fast16&#x2F;fast16...</a><p>From UToronto 2012: Finds no correlation with DRAM failures, nor high temp and disk failure but concludes temperature variability affects disk reliability more than maximum operating temperature\n<a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cs.toronto.edu&#x2F;~bianca&#x2F;papers&#x2F;temperature_cam.pdf" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cs.toronto.edu&#x2F;~bianca&#x2F;papers&#x2F;temperature_cam.pdf</a><p>From Google 2007: cannot find strong correlation between failure rate and temperature or utilization.\n<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;static.googleusercontent.com&#x2F;media&#x2F;research.google.com&#x2F;en&#x2F;&#x2F;archive&#x2F;disk_failures.pdf" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;static.googleusercontent.com&#x2F;media&#x2F;research.google.c...</a><p>From Microsoft 2013: focuses on operating temperature being the driving factor of failure, more than load.\n<a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cs.virginia.edu&#x2F;~gurumurthi&#x2F;papers&#x2F;acmtos13.pdf" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cs.virginia.edu&#x2F;~gurumurthi&#x2F;papers&#x2F;acmtos13.pdf</a>')
