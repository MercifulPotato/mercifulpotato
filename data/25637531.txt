Item(by='ascagnel_', descendants=None, kids=[25639328], score=None, time=1609792688, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25637047, text='I disagree on the Electoral College -- it&#x27;s a system designed so that votes aren&#x27;t equal in value.  If you live in California, your presidential vote is worth less than a vote in Wyoming[0].  Similarly, the cap on the size of the House of Representatives also means that larger states will generally see less representation per capita than smaller states.  (Note: I&#x27;m leaving the Senate out, given that it&#x27;s designed to assign two votes per state without regard to population).<p>A referendum is great at giving legislators or executives a priority -- my home state of New Jersey has been working on marijuana legalization for years, but it&#x27;s largely stalled in the statehouse.  The 2020 election featured a ballot question on legalization, and the overwhelming &quot;yes&quot; (more than 2&#x2F;3 of the electorate supports the move) helped kick off another round of legislation (that, in a sadly predictable pattern, seems to be stuck once again).<p>[0] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.axios.com&#x2F;electoral-college-by-vote-per-capita-420912ee-30be-4101-8dfb-9de5e7ebcdd4.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.axios.com&#x2F;electoral-college-by-vote-per-capita-4...</a>')
