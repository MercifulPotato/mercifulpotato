Similar to the Wallace-Wells book, but from a different perspective.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;smile.amazon.com&#x2F;How-Prepare-Climate-Change-Practical&#x2F;dp&#x2F;1982134518&#x2F;ref=pd_cart_vw_crc_1_2&#x2F;138-5985187-2859747?_encoding=UTF8&amp;pd_rd_i=1982134518&amp;pd_rd_r=1a514c87-ffe2-4dd3-9a61-42b3601b9c32&amp;pd_rd_w=C1ulG&amp;pd_rd_wg=bVXml&amp;pf_rd_p=01004c92-8f40-4f1a-bee8-08cb36dccac2&amp;pf_rd_r=DD7EX6XRNNSPH4V92GMK&amp;psc=1&amp;refRID=DD7EX6XRNNSPH4V92GMK" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;smile.amazon.com&#x2F;How-Prepare-Climate-Change-Practica...</a><p>Cormack McCarthy&#x27;s <i>The Road</i> is a disturbing, post-apocalyptic novel about a father and son&#x27;s fight to survive when life on earth dies off from the bottom up.  It is set a bit further in the future than the others, at a point where the die off of humans is happening. Collapse and death is mostly low tech.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;smile.amazon.com&#x2F;Road-Cormac-McCarthy&#x2F;dp&#x2F;0307387895&#x2F;ref=sr_1_2?dchild=1&amp;keywords=the+road&amp;qid=1612113251&amp;s=books&amp;sr=1-2" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;smile.amazon.com&#x2F;Road-Cormac-McCarthy&#x2F;dp&#x2F;0307387895&#x2F;...</a><p>It does seem like we are living in a utopian sci fi novel these days (pandemic aside).  We have ignored the warnings of pending collapse until there is no longer time to correct course or even adapt.  So it&#x27;s likely we wail be living in environmental chaos for the next 20 years and then slip into extinction over the next 10 as we experience first hand the last great die-off.')
