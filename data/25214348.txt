There&#x27;s always a bug or two in it.<p>0-terminated strings not only have proven to be a rich source of bugs, they&#x27;re remarkably inefficient as well [1]. Doing better was a major focus of the initial design of D.<p>[1] This is because of constantly scanning to get the length (which also necessitates reloading the string contents into the memory cache), and having to make copies of strings instead of just slicing them.')
