Item(by='jabl', descendants=None, kids=[24661671], score=None, time=1601632536, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24660507, text='&gt; The shape of the future is people are stopping feeding the GPL commons in favour of permissive licenses that the $$$ wants <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;resources.whitesourcesoftware.com&#x2F;blog-whitesource&#x2F;o" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;resources.whitesourcesoftware.com&#x2F;blog-whitesource&#x2F;o</a>... shows over half of projects in 2019 are MIT &#x2F;Apache vs GPL(s), and a steady decline in even GPL3 usage.<p>Yes, that&#x27;s how it looks. In the long term, I think that will turn out to be a Faustian bargain for users and hackers.<p>It will be the world the commercial software devs want; a large pool of permissively licensed software that they can pick and choose from to assemble $$$ products (with a number of useful idiots and sometimes even businesses contributing to maintaining the underlying pieces), without having to worry about contributing anything back.<p>But the dream of a Free Software computing experience, where you can inspect and modify every part of the software you run, will have died.')
