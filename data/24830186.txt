We have many more connections and computation power reserved for the fovea than for our peripheral vision.<p>With that said, I&#x27;m pretty sure that our perception of noise is not as dissimilar as you suggest. By doing the simple experiment of freezing on a static noise frame and asking participants to tell me whether a pixel was black or white, I&#x27;d expect most people to get it right.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cns.nyu.edu&#x2F;csh&#x2F;csh04&#x2F;Articles&#x2F;Rieke1998.pdf" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cns.nyu.edu&#x2F;csh&#x2F;csh04&#x2F;Articles&#x2F;Rieke1998.pdf</a>')
