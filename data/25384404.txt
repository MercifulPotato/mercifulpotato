Item(by='cesarb', descendants=None, kids=[25385079], score=None, time=1607684777, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25383844, text='According to Wikipedia (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;X86-64#History" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;X86-64#History</a>), AMD&#x27;s x86-64 was announced in 1999 and the full specification was released in 2000. Assuming patents last for 20 years, they should have all expired by now. For SIMD, this includes everything up to SSE2; most software which uses any of the newer SIMD extensions should have a fallback to SSE2, since older CPUs don&#x27;t have these newer extensions.')
