Item(by='EvanAnderson', descendants=None, kids=[25174524, 25175630], score=None, time=1606005767, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25173544, text='It&#x27;s a more plausible scenario, to me, that browsers will become more strict w&#x2F; CA verification. Non-technical users will be permitted easy access to resources secured by third-party-signed certificates. They&#x27;ll be forced to plumb into the confusing depths of their browser &#x2F; OS to make changes that would allow access to resources protected with untrusted certificates (assuming they&#x27;re even allowed to make such changes, which I think will also eventually be taken away).<p>Nobody in the industry wants anything locally-hosted by end users to work well. Locally-hosted means not &quot;cloud&quot;-hosted. That means no sweet, sweet recurring subscription revenue stream or centrally-stored user data to &quot;monetize&quot;.')
