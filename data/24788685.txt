Item(by='jlokier', descendants=None, kids=[24789758, 24788770], score=None, time=1602770129, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24787073, text='We can&#x27;t discuss human rights in absolute worldwide terms while looking at immigration systems, because there&#x27;s no universally agreed set of human rights to discuss, as far as I know.<p>But we can discuss violations of what it is widely regarded should be universal human rights.<p>Here&#x27;s a list of some that I consider should be universal human rights, which are often violated by immigration systems:<p>- The right of young children to be with their parents<p>- The right of spouses to be together and start a family<p>- The right to get married<p>- The right to legal due process, including appeals and representation<p>- The right to work for essentials for survival such as food, shelter and medicine<p>- The right to enter private transactions for things like shelter<p>- The right to access the prevailing currency system (basic banking in the modern world)<p>- The right to leave an abusive job, without enduring serious harm caused by the state if you do<p>- The right to leave an abusive relationship, without enduring serious harm caused by the state if you do<p>- The right to stay in the country where you were born, raised and have never known anywhere else<p>- The right to vote in the country where you were born and raised<p>- The right to protection and safety when talking to the police about a crime where you are a victim or witness')
