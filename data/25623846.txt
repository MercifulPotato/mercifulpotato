Item(by='onetom', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609699014, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25623151, text='You can get quite close to &quot;AST editing&quot;-like experience if you just use the expand&#x2F;shrink selection feature of a JetBrains IDE. It supports most mainstream languages.<p>You just select a syntactic unit with opt-up&#x2F;down, then u either type over it, copy&#x2F;cut&#x2F;past or press left or right to go to the beginning or the end of the selection, hence using the selection as an intermediate step achieving AST-level navigation.<p>And of course you can teach Emacs to behave similarly, using the Expand Region (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;wikemacs.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Expand_region" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;wikemacs.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Expand_region</a>) + some hacks, but I agree, that smartparens also solves this problem quite well.<p>UPDATE: also use avy-jump for emacs or acejump for intellij: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;plugins.jetbrains.com&#x2F;plugin&#x2F;7086-acejump" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;plugins.jetbrains.com&#x2F;plugin&#x2F;7086-acejump</a>\nthese in combination with expand&#x2F;shrink-region operations are a significant productivity boost, which is easy to learn and teach.')
