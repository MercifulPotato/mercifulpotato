Item(by='derbOac', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606584256, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25238207, text='Vitanyi -- one of the authors cited in the linked piece -- has a paper where he and his colleagues show that even though ideal compression isn&#x27;t knowable&#x2F;computable, you can show that two encodings approach the ideal differentially with some computable probability (I&#x27;m being vague and handwavy because it&#x27;s been awhile since I&#x27;ve read this literature carefully). That is, if you have two encodings A and B, you can compute some probability that A is closer to ideal than B.<p>It&#x27;s kind of a Kolmogorov&#x2F;algorithmic complexity analogue of a p-value.<p>I think this literature&#x2F;inferential paradigm in general is far less known than it should be.')
