Item(by='asdfman123', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603205567, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24837520, text='I had a similar experience with Encarta at a similar age -- in the US, in perhaps in a far more privileged environment -- but it hit me the same way.<p>Encarta was great because it curated and presented information intentionally. Everything was in one place. You didn&#x27;t get lost in rabbit holes or overwhelmed by information overload. You didn&#x27;t get distracted by Instagram.<p>It&#x27;s so obvious that it barely needs to be said, but giving a kid a well-crafted learning experience is better than just throwing them into the deep end of the internet.<p>I remember in elementary school some student asked why race cars make that distinct zooming noise as they race past. The teacher didn&#x27;t know, but I was able to go to the front of the class and explain the Doppler effect based on an interactive lesson I had watched on MS Encarta.')
