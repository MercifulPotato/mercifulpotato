Item(by='drothlis', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603826233, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24909261, text='&gt; Ninja looks like it tracks dependencies roughly automatically, whereas dependencies are manual in Make<p>Ninja has built-in support for the &quot;.d&quot; files generated by gcc&#x27;s &quot;-M&quot; family of flags, but you still have to make your gcc command-line actually pass the right flags, and you have to tell Ninja the name of the &quot;.d&quot; file. See <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;lwn.net&#x2F;Articles&#x2F;706404&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;lwn.net&#x2F;Articles&#x2F;706404&#x2F;</a><p>Another neat feature of Ninja is that it&#x27;s much better at &quot;pruning&quot; downstream targets if you rebuilt something (because of an out-of-date timestamp) but then it turned out to be bit-for-bit identical as it was before. In this case, downstream targets that depend on this intermediate target don&#x27;t need to be rebuilt.')
