Item(by='MattJ100', descendants=None, kids=[25584591, 25584508], score=None, time=1609349093, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25583087, text='You hint at this being a downside, but (speaking as a Prosody developer) this is one of the things that makes Jitsi extremely powerful and flexible.<p>For example, Jitsi delegates authentication of users to Prosody. Prosody already has a bunch of authentication backends ( <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;modules.prosody.im&#x2F;type_auth" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;modules.prosody.im&#x2F;type_auth</a> ) and Jitsi benefits from these without needing to reinvent the wheel.<p>I&#x27;ve seen the Jitsi+Prosody combo integrated into many kinds of platforms. People also implement things like custom access control, logging, notifications and provisioning at this layer. These things would generally be harder to customize in a monolithic off-the-shelf system.<p>Meanwhile a simple self-hosted setup is still easy enough to pull off in a spare afternoon ( <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;jitsi.github.io&#x2F;handbook&#x2F;docs&#x2F;devops-guide&#x2F;devops-guide-start" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;jitsi.github.io&#x2F;handbook&#x2F;docs&#x2F;devops-guide&#x2F;devops-gu...</a> ).')
