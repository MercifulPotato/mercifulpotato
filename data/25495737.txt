Item(by='lumost', descendants=None, kids=[25496774, 25496450, 25496901], score=None, time=1608564302, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25495621, text='I think you overestimate how much negotiating power an individual worker has when deciding the price for their labor.<p>Anecdotally, in the Software field there is a lot of &quot;price anchoring&quot; where a large employer decides that a software engineer makes ~125k, and both smaller&#x2F;peer employers decide that a software engineer makes 125k +&#x2F;- 10%.<p>From past experience the base &quot;going rate&quot; in a given market doesn&#x27;t seem to change all that much unless a large employer decides to change the going rate because a higher <i>or</i> lower price point better suits their business - other companies will set their salaries to the baseline.  Big Tech has recently been dragging wages up across the board by both hiring in volume, and paying more than everyone else.<p>I&#x27;d be curious if anyone has a formal study on price anchoring in wage negotiations.')
