A lot of software engineers who grew up as hobby coders before doing it professionally seem to have a hard time going to the documentation, and will start refactoring stuff to figure out how it works.<p>Sure, it&#x27;s possible to approach code in that way, but to me it&#x27;s similar to never developing taste. The defining characteristics of the software (the documentation) is for many intents and purposes simply illegible to them, and they&#x27;re basically saying &quot;I&#x27;ll know the entry &#x2F; extensibility point &#x2F; bug when I see it.&quot;<p>I interview for this in part by asking the interviewee what the most interesting new features are about recent versions of X language.')
