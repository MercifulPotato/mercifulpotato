Item(by='ev1', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606866884, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25271629, text='Do you sell it as a free version and a paid version as two separate packages&#x2F;apps, or do you activate an in app purchase?<p>From my experience dealing with the general public (I used to build some games that also exported to Android) the latter seemingly gets treated as a gross ripoff scam and the former is acceptable to people - it&#x27;s a giant buy button with the price on it. We got better results when the &quot;premium version!&quot; upsell just opened the premium version in the app store. Worked great for one time purchases.<p>This seems like a common practice, for example the non-abusive third party reddit client (the first-party one is an abomination of spam, invasive tracking, spam push notifications, and incredibly terrible dark patterns) app:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;play.google.com&#x2F;store&#x2F;apps&#x2F;details?id=com.andrewshu.android.reddit" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;play.google.com&#x2F;store&#x2F;apps&#x2F;details?id=com.andrewshu....</a> vs <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;play.google.com&#x2F;store&#x2F;apps&#x2F;details?id=com.andrewshu.android.redditdonation" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;play.google.com&#x2F;store&#x2F;apps&#x2F;details?id=com.andrewshu....</a>')
