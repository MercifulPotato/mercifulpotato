Item(by='megameter', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604721574, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25007697, text='Study.<p>What is most common, and least helpful, in societal issues, is to look to an authority for a quick fix, presuming that protest and action from above cures all ills. But how did they get that authority?<p>Well, at some point they most likely researched a topic to the point where they could craft a convincing argument.<p>But that isn&#x27;t where the matter should rest. When you want better, you have to set a benchmark for better.<p>And so studies are the tool - deep, rich ones, crafted around the specific scenario. This lets you convince the public and authorities, one at a time, that a certain course of action has merit. Often, officials respond well to requests to study a topic: It builds a path to legitimize action. But you have to hold your own standards high too, and not fall into partisan ways of thinking.<p>Sometimes you can approach with a broad message, other times you should focus on developing a small circle of people. It is not a uniform thing.')
