Item(by='audunw', descendants=None, kids=[25416240, 25415570], score=None, time=1607940998, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25411422, text='&gt; Zig is a lot more complex than C, but I think most of the language complexity came from filling in gaps rather than tacking things on.<p>I think it&#x27;s important to keep in mind that with C you might end up using a bunch of extenions, complex pre-processor macros or pragma magic to achieve essential things.<p>C in itself is relatively simple, but using it in practice can end up becoming quite complex.<p>Zig ends up being simpler in some ways by having less magic and special ways of achieving things. &quot;printf&quot; is very magical in most C compilers, but the equivalent in Zig is nothing particularly special from either the language or the compilers side.<p>I think the only thing that truly adds complexity without being strictly necessary, is the async stuff. But you can argue that async IO is becoming a really essential thing for systems programming, and using it without explicit language suppport is a nightmare.')
