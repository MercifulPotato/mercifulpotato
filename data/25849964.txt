Item(by='jgraettinger1', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611167122, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25848542, text='We&#x27;re building a tool, Estuary Flow, which seeks to be an end-to-end realization of practical, configuration driven, and scale-out flow-based programming -- with an important twist.<p>The central concept is a &quot;collection&quot;, an append-only set of schematized documents, which can be <i>captured</i> and <i>materialized</i> into other systems (e.x. pub&#x2F;sub, S3 buckets, etc). &quot;Derivations&quot; are collections defined in terms of source collections, and stateful transformations&#x2F;joins&#x2F;aggregations which are applied to them.<p>A key twist is that collections are simultaneously a batch dataset (backed by cloud-storage) and also a real-time stream.\nThey unify the current dichotomy of &quot;historical&quot; vs &quot;streaming&quot; data into a single addressed concept. Declare a new derivation, and it automatically back-fills over history right from S3, then seamlessly transitions to live data.<p>If this sounds interesting, check out our docs [0]. We&#x27;re early, but love feedback!<p>[0] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;estuary.readthedocs.io&#x2F;en&#x2F;latest&#x2F;README.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;estuary.readthedocs.io&#x2F;en&#x2F;latest&#x2F;README.html</a>')
