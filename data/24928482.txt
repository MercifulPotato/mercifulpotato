Item(by='PeterisP', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603961709, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24926667, text='Most of current best practices for backups, redundancy and business continuity are intended for the risks of random disasters. Malicious attacks are substantially different.<p>There are many organizations which are doing regular secure backups, but are doing so in a way that can be sabotaged once a skilled attacker gains domain admin privileges, and sabotaging backups is one of key things that the attackers are doing after they are in the network and before triggering the ransom encryption. We&#x27;re not talking about a virus randomly spreading, in such high-ransom targeted attacks the preparation before triggering a ransom is done manually by skilled teams going on from one target to another.')
