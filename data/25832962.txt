Item(by='simias', descendants=None, kids=[25833187], score=None, time=1611060581, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25832915, text='Sometimes less is more. Having access to more features sometimes leads devs to bloat their applications and make them subjectively less usable.<p>A pathological example for me would be gvim. I tried using it for a while but I kept running into weird performance issues and general lagginess. Meanwhile from a terminal it Just Works. So I don&#x27;t bother with it anymore, I always run Vim from a terminal and I never looked back.<p>In general I find that the terminal imposes two generally good constraints to application makers: everything must exist within a single window and mouse interaction is very limited (and not supported everywhere) so it&#x27;s important that everything must be achievable with keyboard inputs.<p>These constraints disincentivize pathological mouse-driven &quot;click click&quot; pop up dialog fests which are the bane of my existence.')
