Item(by='jacques_chester', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602693989, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24773295, text='Adding slightly to it: they use Brier scores to say how &quot;good&quot; someone is at prediction. Because these predictions require a probability of confidence, you can punish those who were confidently wrong or reward those who were confidently right.<p>So if I predict &quot;aliens will land on or before December 1st&quot; with 100% and no aliens appear, then my Brier score is 1 (the worst). If I say 0% confidence, then my score is 0 (the best).<p>There are dozens and dozens of ways of scoring prediction ability, as it happens: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cawcr.gov.au&#x2F;projects&#x2F;verification&#x2F;verif_web_page.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cawcr.gov.au&#x2F;projects&#x2F;verification&#x2F;verif_web_pag...</a>')
