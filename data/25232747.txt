Item(by='martinald', descendants=None, kids=[25232965, 25238571, 25233095, 25233183], score=None, time=1606512626, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25229946, text='This isn&#x27;t a big deal. The European parliament can only suggest laws to the comission, it has no real power to enact them. Unfortunately the US media doesn&#x27;t report this part of it and often sees these votes as legally binding (another example was the recentish vote on USB-C charging).<p>I think the best was to explain the EU parliament to US readers is that the parliament only has veto control, much like the US president (if anything, less, since the president can issue executive orders, which the EP can&#x27;t). It&#x27;s basically inverted.')
