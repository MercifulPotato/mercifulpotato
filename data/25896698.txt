Item(by='BlargMcLarg', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611526309, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25895240, text='I do think corps are part of the problem, but not necessarily due to control. Cities are self-reinforcing growth due to the inherent chicken-and-egg problem regarding jobs and companies. Companies don&#x27;t want to move to the boonies, not enough workers. Workers don&#x27;t want to move to the boonies, not enough jobs. And everyone wants to be close to universities, because networks, while universities aren&#x27;t willing to spend resources to move. This was already the case before telecommunication became as advanced as it did in the recent 15 years.<p>WFH might help break the cycle to some degree, but the majority people will still be in cities (by virtue of, well, being a city), which creates incentives for younger generations to go there (dating + outgoing life), even for those who would rather settle outside the city once they find a partner.')
