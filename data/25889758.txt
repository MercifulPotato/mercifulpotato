Blindly following them makes you an idiot.<p>Very true. &quot;Best practice&quot; are most often just &quot;common practices&quot; - how we&#x27;ve done things here forever.<p>That&#x27;s great for standardization. It gets annoying when each team member does things their own way.<p>But it&#x27;s a stretch to call them best. The research into what is truly best, A&#x2F;B tests of various methods, or building prototypes of new experimental frameworks is rarely scheduled in sprint plans.
