Item(by='simo7', descendants=None, kids=[25480434], score=None, time=1608376367, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25475856, text='The problem with your analogy to English is that you&#x27;re comparing the wrong things.<p>A function is a &quot;thing&quot;, you should rather compare it to an entity in the English language.<p>Why do we say <i>washing machine</i> instead of <i>a home appliance powered by electricity used to wash laundry through the use of centrifugal force</i>?<p>When naming things you need to balance descriptiveness with conciseness, a name is not a definition.<p>As a rule of thumb, avoid specifying things that can be easily guessed, especially if they are right there in your function signature (!):<p><pre><code>     def find_xy_coordinate_of_dogs_cats_and_baboons_in_picture(picture: Picture) -&gt; List[XYCoordinates]:\n</code></pre>\nI&#x27;d refactor it to:<p><pre><code>    def find_animals_in_pic(picture: Picture) -&gt; List[XYCoordinates]:</code></pre>')
