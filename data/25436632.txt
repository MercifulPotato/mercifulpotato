Item(by='vel0city', descendants=None, kids=[25440779], score=None, time=1608073742, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25435784, text='The two main tricks I&#x27;d recommend to not take potato-quality pictures are lighting and framing&#x2F;composing. Asking yourself a few questions about the lighting situation (which way is the light coming from? Is my foreground darker than my background? should I take the shot from the other direction, if possible?) will help a good bit to start.<p>For a quick idea on composing, think about the rule of thirds and the golden ratio<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Rule_of_thirds" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Rule_of_thirds</a>\n<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.apogeephoto.com&#x2F;how-to-use-the-golden-ratio-to-improve-your-photography&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.apogeephoto.com&#x2F;how-to-use-the-golden-ratio-to-i...</a><p>Just these two concepts will get you started taking much better photos than most unskilled photographers, and at least I find it doesn&#x27;t take me too much time to give a second or two thought to them. I&#x27;d encourage you to turn on a rule of thirds layer on your camera, it&#x27;ll start to make it really easy to do some quick composition.')
