Item(by='signal11', descendants=None, kids=[25397637, 25397796], score=None, time=1607769200, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25396828, text='The thing with English grammar is that it&#x27;s very malleable. And exception-ridden. In this particular case, the specificity of what you&#x27;re describing is important as well.<p>&gt; absolutely have to be in this order: opinion-size-age-shape-colour-origin-material-purpose Noun. So you can have a lovely little old rectangular green French silver whittling knife<p>For instance, &quot;An expensive, peaty fifteen year old Scotch&quot; is a potential counter-example, because [fifteen year old scotch] is the specific item you&#x27;re talking about.<p>&quot;Barman, a peaty large|double Scotch for me please&quot; would also be a counterexample, I think, because the key here is that you want a [large|double Scotch]. Large and Double are both size descriptors for a bar drink in different parts of the world.<p>And I&#x27;m not seeing why &quot;I received a hand-painted wooden Native-American jewellery box as a present&quot; would somehow be wrong, if I wanted to emphasize the origin, over &quot;I received a hand-painted Native-American wooden jewellery box as a present&quot;.')
