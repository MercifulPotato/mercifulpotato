Item(by='imtringued', descendants=None, kids=[24953601], score=None, time=1604141352, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24949610, text='It also has to be near a reliable water source so that it can be cooled. The bigger the power plant the more geographically constrained it is. France faces low river flow rates in the summer because of climate chance and this forces them to take some of the nuclear plants offline. Otherwise the waste heat would make it impossible for aquatic life to live near those power plants. Ever wondered why someone make an &quot;obvious&quot; mistake such as building Fukushima directly near the tsunami prone coast? Because the ocean is a reliable heat sink.')
