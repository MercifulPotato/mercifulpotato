Item(by='d_burfoot', descendants=None, kids=[25646253], score=None, time=1609859100, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25644580, text='There are many problems with mainstream ideas about inflation, but one key problem is that it does not take into account the disastrous decline in the quality of public services that Americans have experienced over the last couple of decades. How do you measure the inflationary impact of the fact that in many cities, the public schools have declined in quality so much that people are faced with the choice of either moving to the suburbs, or paying for private school? What about the impact of the transformation of the built landscape from nice walkable towns to disgusting exurban sprawl?')
