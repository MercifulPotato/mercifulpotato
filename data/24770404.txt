Item(by='PeterisP', descendants=None, kids=[24771168], score=None, time=1602623802, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24769801, text='Usually, a big part of attribution is the reuse of specific TTPs (tactics&#x2F;tools&#x2F;procedures), malware components, and components of C&amp;C infrastructure - sophisticated attacks are tool-heavy, the tools are custom and generally not distributed much (even within the same country - e.g. it appears that in Russia the military intelligence and foreign intelligence units don&#x27;t share much of their tooling&#x2F;infrastructure with each other for some political reasons), you don&#x27;t&#x2F;can&#x27;t recreate all that code from scratch for every single attack, so there&#x27;s inevitably reuse that allows you to link some attacks to others.  Since there are some earlier attacks where we can link particular actions not only to a country, but to specific people in specific organizations, usually due to some mistake on their behalf, it&#x27;s sometimes possible to also determine the attribution of new atacks with reasonable certainty.')
