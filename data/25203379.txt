Item(by='egypturnash', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606254839, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25199193, text='If that &quot;certain timespan&quot; was the eighties then what you are seeing is more that they used the <i>exact same</i> colors... which were designed to be seen on yellowish newsprint that sucked in a ton of ink, instead of bright white glossy paper that all the ink sits right on top of.<p>Over time people mostly quit doing that. Once it became reasonably easy and affordable to deal with a multi-layer 300dpi (or better, it&#x27;s always nice to be able to have your minor mistakes vanish when it&#x27;s shrunk to print size) file, and for that file to get turned into four CMYK plates, comics colors started to look a <i>lot</i> better than when everything had to be planned in watercolors over a xerox, then turned into those four CMYK plates by hand.')
