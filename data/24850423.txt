It&#x27;s more like, here we go again.<p>Ideas from Lisp have been creeping into &quot;mainstream&quot; programming languages for decades. As the article points out, Common Lisp introduced multiple dispatch over 40 years ago and now it&#x27;s finding its way into Julia. There&#x27;s lots more in Lisp that can still find its way into a modern programming language.<p>I&#x27;m particularly interested in the inclusion of a LispSyntax  for Julia that might allow Lisp macros to be written at the LispSyntax level that can then be used at the surface Julia level.')
