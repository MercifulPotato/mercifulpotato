Item(by='wpietri', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609650277, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25614498, text='I think the common element in both cases is building clarity and focus.<p>I don&#x27;t do an OKR process, but I have congruent ways of limiting how much personal change I&#x27;m tackling at once. If I try to a bunch of vague and broad things, I don&#x27;t succeed. If I pick some clear, specific, time-bound actions, I do much better.<p>And I think it&#x27;s fine that OKRs leave out the hills. In setting them, we think about the hills and possible routes. When we&#x27;re working toward them, we develop a lot of understanding of where we are and our current specifics. If that in the moment demands creating more explicit or formal, that&#x27;s fine. The OKRs are just there to create the context.')
