Item(by='zetazzed', descendants=None, kids=[25071994, 25071827, 25071674, 25073775, 25072392], score=None, time=1605199134, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25070224, text='I think people are mis-conceiving L3 as &quot;the car might yell &#x27;grab the wheel at any moment.&#x27;&quot; At least for L3 systems I&#x27;ve worked on, the model was - there will be exception cases we don&#x27;t know how to navigate, but we have a plan to safely transfer control to the driver in them. Examples would include:\n * a human construction worker or police officer is directing traffic, so safely slow to a halt or near halt and ask the driver to follow directions\n * the route takes the driver from a supported road (large, well-mapped) to an unsupported road - tell the driver they have to take the exit, and if they don&#x27;t then you&#x27;ll miss the exit and stay on the larger, supported road\n * broken traffic light - tell the user and prepare to treat as red light unless they override<p>These are not sudden dangerous handoffs, but they do mean that a driver can&#x27;t be drunk or asleep when behind the wheel.')
