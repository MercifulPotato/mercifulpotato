Item(by='severino', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605085211, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25054010, text='You can use &quot;apt-cache policy&quot;, for example. This way you can see the current installed version of a package in your system, and also if there&#x27;s a newer version you can install (the candidate).<p><pre><code>  $ apt-cache policy accountsservice\n  accountsservice:\n    Installed: 0.6.40-2ubuntu11.6\n    Candidate: 0.6.40-2ubuntu11.6\n</code></pre>\nIf the installed version reported by the utility is the version that Ubuntu states the problem has been fixed for your release, then presumably your system is safe (for this bug).')
