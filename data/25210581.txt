Item(by='HarryHirsch', descendants=None, kids=[25210890], score=None, time=1606320008, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25209654, text='The difference is that the Romans had no way of telling if their theory was right, it was based on guesses. Plato considered whether matter was continuous or discontinuous 400 years earlier and he settled on discontinuous, and the properties of the &quot;atoms&quot; as he called them were what gave macroscopic matter its properties. But the Greeks had no way of detecting atoms.<p>But when the microscope was discovered in the 17th century people looked at microorganisms and found that the grosser the sample the more microorganisms you could find. That was material to work with, and the germ theory followed in due course.')
