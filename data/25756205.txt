I’m not remotely confused, but it would seem that you are.<p>Without section 230, companies can carry whatever content they like, but <i>they’re legally liable</i> for it.<p>A common carrier is not responsible for the content they carry, as long as they carry it for customers <i>without discrimination</i>.<p>In <i>exchange</i> for serving the public good, common carriers are granted the privilege of immunity.<p>Section 230, however, eliminates that exchange — a government grant of privileged immunity, in exchange for nothing — while <i>also</i> having editorial control over what they publish.')
