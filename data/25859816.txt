Item(by='IggleSniggle', descendants=None, kids=[25861912, 25860048], score=None, time=1611242805, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25849714, text='I get what you&#x27;re saying. However, I think this is nice a compromise. The closed-source client is pushes out continued development of the bridges for a little longer than it otherwise would. The client only matters insomuch as it has good bridges, so the bridges must naturally come first.<p>In the longer term, <i>if</i> a project like this succeeds, then <i>all</i> messaging clients become defined by their feature set rather than their vendor lock-in. If Facebook Messenger wants to compete with Beeper, then it better be able to connect to Hangouts, etc.<p>I welcome a player that pushes chat clients as a whole towards interoperability and commoditization, and am happy to (as a side effect) support a proprietary client that helps drive additional platform support over time (eg, I don&#x27;t see Airbnb on the list of Beeper messaging bridges yet, etc).')
