Item(by='sradman', descendants=None, kids=[25631176, 25631134], score=None, time=1609764895, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25629801, text='In my mind, what distinguishes the Snowden case from the Assange&#x2F;Manning cases is the distinction between whistleblowing [1] and a fishing expedition [2]:<p>&gt; A fishing expedition is an informal, pejorative term for a non-specific search for information, especially incriminating information. It is most frequently organized by policing authorities.<p>The question is whether it is lawful for any group to mine a corpus of private documents searching for a crime or misdeed. Individuals or small activist organizations now have this ability and it is not exactly the same as a whistleblower leaking documents associated with a known crime.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Whistleblower" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Whistleblower</a><p>[2] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Fishing_expedition" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Fishing_expedition</a>')
