Item(by='nybble41', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603730786, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24895083, text='If the standard for what counts as &quot;copyright law&quot; is &quot;what we would nowadays refer to [as] copyright&quot; then it is of course trivially true that all &quot;copyright&quot; statutes, past, present, or future, share the same purpose as modern copyright. However, more generally the term &quot;copyright&quot; refers to &quot;the right by law to be the entity which determines who may publish, copy and distribute a piece of writing, music, picture or other work of authorship&quot;[0], and for that the Licensing of the Press Act qualifies.<p>The Statute of Anne was intended as a modernized replacement for the LPA; it represented an evolution in the concept of copyright away from its origins as a means of censorship and toward a more lenient and democratized commercial model.<p>[0] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wiktionary.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;copyright#Noun" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wiktionary.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;copyright#Noun</a>')
