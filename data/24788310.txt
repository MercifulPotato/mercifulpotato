Shakespeare is always funniest in his tragedies. The comedies have almost universally aged badly, but the kind of humor he puts in the tragedies holds up much better.<p>A piece of advice I was given very early on is to find the funny parts of the tragedies, and that has served me well. Hamlet in particular is very clever about wordplay -- far better than nearly all of Shakespeare&#x27;s actual clowns (though the clown role in Hamlet, as the gravediggers, is among his very best comic writing).')
