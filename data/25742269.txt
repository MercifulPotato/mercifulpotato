Item(by='throwaway5415', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610425550, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25730188, text='For future reference, &#x27;xartutil --erase-all&#x27; in Terminal in macOS Recovery erases all data in the Secure Enclave, including the keys used for disk encryption. In that way, it can be considered equivalent to a secure erase command on a non-Apple SSD, except Apple can be trusted to have implemented it properly unlike normal SSD vendors who have been caught implementing it as a NOP.<p>Also, in my experience, Apple does not require returns to have a working OS installation. You can erase and return in that state.')
