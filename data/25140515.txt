Item(by='clairity', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605724545, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25137562, text='&gt; &quot;The Public Health Services Act is almost entirely an exercise of Congressional power under the Commerce Clause.&quot;<p>some quick research reveals that the federal quarantine powers of the PHSA are largely restricted to state and national borders. so that&#x27;s indeed based (partly) in the commerce clause, protecting trade across borders from destabilizing forces. but the 5th &amp; 14th amendments (due process and equal protection) also contribute, as does the necessary and proper clause (allowing the fed to use unenumerated powers to carry out its charter, and only its charter, when necessary and proper).<p>but a true national quarantine, where people are confined to homes or communities, and other proposed national measures (like a mask mandate) would require broader powers that go beyond the commerce clause, and that was the (unstated) common understanding from which i was arguing from before.<p>i still find this use of the commerce clause troubling, as it&#x27;s just another stepping stone in the consolidation of power away from states to a future autocrat. the commerce clause is meant to curtail human-induced distortions on interborder trade, not pandemics.')
