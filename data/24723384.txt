Banning politics in the workplace has no bearing on this.<p>Other political issues may very well be important to people, and they&#x27;re free to act on them in their own time. Your co-workers are a captive audience, and it&#x27;s not appropriate to abuse that relationship for political activism. People who want to be politically active can do so on their own time. I would not take issue with, say, a Mormon co-worker who goes door to door on weekends and takes PTO to go on missionary trips. I <i>would</i> however, take issue with proselytizing to co-workers. I regard politics the same way.')
