In many states you do not need to prove it to vote. Some states allow a normal vote and some states make them provisional. Still, no concrete identification is required when voting in most states. The voter is trusted to be who they say they are under possible penalty. This only applies to in person voting. Almost all states allowing mail in voting do not require notarization.<p>With electronic voting you have a huge barrier in that not everyone has access to the means to vote electronically. You disenfranchise a pool of voters.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;ballotpedia.org&#x2F;Voter_identification_laws_by_state" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;ballotpedia.org&#x2F;Voter_identification_laws_by_state</a>')
