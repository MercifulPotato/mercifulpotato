Item(by='benlivengood', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603608041, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24882819, text='What I&#x27;ve seen over the last ~25 years is that general purpose computing is a double-edged sword for normal people.  The most straightforward reasons for most of the walled gardens are malware, adware, spyware, and ransomware.<p>Android probably comes the closest to a free market for software that gives normal users some measure of control over their own devices.  It requires TPM and secure boot so that there&#x27;s a viable option for normal people who accidentally install a bad APK.  Normal people can&#x27;t flash EEPROMS by hand to remove rootkits.<p>I&#x27;d love if general computing was pushed as one of the most important things for people to know.  I still think that the level of expertise necessary to maintain a malware-free computing experience is beyond the reach of most people.  Computer and network security are hard.')
