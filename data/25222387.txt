Item(by='hinkley', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606415207, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25221689, text='The warp is connected to the machine, sure. But I think it’s a waste of time to set up a pattern to be as long as the loom is wide. You get no cloth at all until you’ve done all of the fiddly bits with getting the warp how you want it.<p>I believe that if you’re going to weave anything, professionally, you’ll pick the loom that’s the width of the shortest dimension of your design, for purely practical reasons. I briefly worked as a mechanic in college, and what separates the pros from dilettantes like me is speed. I’ve heard similar tales from other skilled laborers. “portrait vs landscape” I understood to be very much a speed issue with looms.')
