I just want competition.<p>The problem is that when the market is <i>already</i> consolidated, you need one to get the other.<p>Suppose you want to compete with the major phone platforms. Your biggest problem is apps. No apps, no users; no users, no app developers.<p>A way to fix this is to create a cross-platform app development framework. Developers would love that -- write your app once and it runs on both the existing major platforms <i>and</i> any new ones? Great!<p>Except that the incumbents block it. You can&#x27;t create a competing app store for the existing platforms, which means you can&#x27;t leverage that into a competing platform.<p>But without competition there, they can also do this:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=24304275" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=24304275</a><p>So there goes your competition for Twitter and Facebook.<p>Constrain them from limiting competing app stores. Works a lot better than doing nothing.')
