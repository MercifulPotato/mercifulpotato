Item(by='natfriedman', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609949780, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25651466, text='We have a really excellent policy regarding government takedowns: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;docs.github.com&#x2F;en&#x2F;free-pro-team@latest&#x2F;github&#x2F;site-policy&#x2F;github-government-takedown-policy" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;docs.github.com&#x2F;en&#x2F;free-pro-team@latest&#x2F;github&#x2F;site-...</a><p>Among other things, the policy requires that governments that want content removed from GitHub issue a lawful request to us, which we then push to a public repo:\n<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;github&#x2F;gov-takedowns" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;github&#x2F;gov-takedowns</a><p>So you are able to see all of the government takedown requests that we have processed, there. You&#x27;ll notice that there are only 3 directories in that repo: Russia, China, and Spain. When we do (reluctantly) take down content at the request of a government, we try to limit the takedown only to viewers in the country that made the request, rather than doing a global takedown.')
