Gandhi (note corrected spelling) certainly had his flaws.<p>Meanwhile, Gandhi&#x27;s views on the value of science were not as well known and he is often mis-reported as &quot;anti-science,&quot;. because he often harshly criticized animal testing and weapons development. He in fact admired the scientific method and its search for the truth.<p>What he was against was imposing imported western technology on a poor and illiterate populace without actually &quot;teaching them to fish&quot;. His contemporaries like Jawaharlal Nehru wanted rapid industrialization to catch up with the West. Gandhi wanted the vast masses in the countryside to develop a scientific outlook and become self-sufficient, not just a supplier of raw materials for the educated urban elite.<p>His views on science are analyzed in a long article by Prof. Shambu Prasad:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.mkgandhi.org&#x2F;articles&#x2F;views_on_sci.htm" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.mkgandhi.org&#x2F;articles&#x2F;views_on_sci.htm</a><p>A 2019 NPR interview with famous historian Ramachandra Guha about Gandhi&#x27;s flaws and his evolving views on racism is worth reading.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.npr.org&#x2F;2019&#x2F;10&#x2F;02&#x2F;766083651&#x2F;gandhi-is-deeply-revered-but-his-attitudes-on-race-and-sex-are-under-scrutiny" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.npr.org&#x2F;2019&#x2F;10&#x2F;02&#x2F;766083651&#x2F;gandhi-is-deeply-re...</a>')
