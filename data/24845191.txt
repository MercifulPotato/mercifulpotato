Item(by='nostrademons', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603260742, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24845138, text='In 2009 we standardized on this form for all Google Search JS for-each loops, because we were literally counting bytes on the SRP:<p><pre><code>  for(var i=0,e;e=a[i++];){ ... }\n</code></pre>\nCould result in problems if the array contained falsey values, but we just didn&#x27;t do that.<p>Nowadays, like I mentioned above, I&#x27;d just do<p><pre><code>  arr.foreach((elem, i) =&gt; { ... });\n</code></pre>\nWhich last time I checked was significantly slower than the for-loop, but I&#x27;ve learned my lesson about trying to optimize for browser quirks that may disappear in a year or two. :-)')
