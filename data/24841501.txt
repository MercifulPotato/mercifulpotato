Item(by='PeterisP', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603222506, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24835241, text='The thing is, if you look at the stats for questions of real  support, the vast majority of those questions are things that did not ever need &quot;real support&quot; - no matter how much or little support you&#x27;re offering, half or more of the questions they get can be answered even by a &quot;bot&quot; that simply enters your query in a search engine and returns the first answer.<p>So there is always the temptation to save costs of your support with a pre-filter, so that your support can talk to people who actually have a question or problem that needs to be talked about.<p>It&#x27;s the equivalent of &quot;have you tried turning it off and on again?&quot;. This question <i>must</i> be asked, because it actually resolves a substantial portion of the issues - but it does seem quite wasteful to employ people to whom 30% of the calls ends with asking that question. Or, for other scenarios, reading verbatim one of the top answers from your FAQ page.')
