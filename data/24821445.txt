Item(by='paulpauper', descendants=None, kids=[24821503, 24821641, 24822338], score=None, time=1603058232, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24821415, text='&gt;I heard an interesting question at one point: &quot;how come, when you throw a ball up on Earth, the parabola is so strongly curved? Spacetime is nearly flat, so how can a straight line become such a steep parabola?&quot;<p>Air resistance, wind, and horizonal acceleration. Over long vertical distances, these perturbations in the x-axis cause an arc.  Nothing to do with general relativity.')
