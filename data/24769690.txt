Item(by='yorwba', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602619307, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24767074, text='The English and Chinese Wikipedia articles about the dialect will usually have at least some useful information e.g. about the phonology and maybe links to other resources.<p>You can also try Wiktionary: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wiktionary.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Wiktionary:About_Chinese#About_specific_lects" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wiktionary.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Wiktionary:About_Chinese#Abou...</a><p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;xiaoxue.iis.sinica.edu.tw&#x2F;ccr&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;xiaoxue.iis.sinica.edu.tw&#x2F;ccr&#x2F;</a> has IPA pronunciations for single characters across many different dialects.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;forvo.com&#x2F;languages-codes&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;forvo.com&#x2F;languages-codes&#x2F;</a> may have some recorded vocabulary.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;laubonghaudoi&#x2F;Chinese_Rime" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;laubonghaudoi&#x2F;Chinese_Rime</a> has input methods for many dialects that can also be abused as a dictionary (associating romanization with Chinese characters you can look up).<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;xefjord.wixsite.com&#x2F;xefscompletelangs&#x2F;courses#comp-kb0gr11d1" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;xefjord.wixsite.com&#x2F;xefscompletelangs&#x2F;courses#comp-k...</a> may have flashcards if you&#x27;re lucky.<p>You&#x27;ll probably also want to make your own flashcards. <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;ppwwyyxx&#x2F;wechat-dump" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;ppwwyyxx&#x2F;wechat-dump</a> is helpful for getting voice messages out of WeChat.<p>If you want to practice by passive listening, you can try <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;phonemica.net&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;phonemica.net&#x2F;</a> , searching on <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;youku.com&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;youku.com&#x2F;</a> or check whether there&#x27;s a local TV station that has programs in dialect.<p>Reading material will be almost impossible to find, but maybe there&#x27;s a bible translation from the 19th century or something.<p>Also check whether there&#x27;s a local language preservation group.<p>Finally, <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;zhongguoyuyan.cn&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;zhongguoyuyan.cn&#x2F;</a> is supposed make a lot of material available to the public soonish (right now I get a certificate error in Firefox, but you can see the landing page in a less strict browser).')
