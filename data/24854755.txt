I don&#x27;t know if there are mental ergonomics that apply universally, or even ones that generalise well. For me, I recognise that I think well when I&#x27;m physically engaged in some other non-cognitive task, like walking, running, showering, etc.<p>Another key factor is eliminating the risk of interruptions. I know when I&#x27;m at my desk I&#x27;m likely to get interrupted, but times and spaces that are interruption free seem to encourage the deepest thought.')
