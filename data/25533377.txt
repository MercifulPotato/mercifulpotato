Item(by='rossdavidh', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608859740, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25530238, text='Visa&#x2F;MC have several advantages over a federal regulator:<p>- they have a more regular feedback mechanism (if someone&#x27;s db gets leaked, they get more fraudulent charges from that company&#x27;s customers, so they can figure out whose security was bad, and this happens often)<p>- they have a more credible enforcement mechanism: they can and will turn off your ability to process CC transactions, while a federal regulator in many cases faces a lot of political pushback if they try to actually shut down a medical facility<p>- the intended (and actual) purpose of PCI was to reduce the number of operators actually storing CC data; if a federal regulator tried to make things onerous enough to force most medical facilities to not store medical data, there would be enormous pushback and they would be forced by Congress to back down')
