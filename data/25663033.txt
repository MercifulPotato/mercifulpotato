Item(by='smoyer', descendants=None, kids=[25663053], score=None, time=1609969635, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25643940, text='That&#x27;s an interesting question and I&#x27;m going to give you a somewhat snarky but truthful answer:  Software mastery is exhibited by the code that I wrote today - and oh how horrible it is to look at what I wrote six months ago.<p>Through-out my career, which is now over four decades if you include the software I wrote as a teenager (before I got paid to do it), I&#x27;ve continuously found a better, more elegant (which can almost always be read simpler or clearer) way to tell the computer what to do.<p>So my non-snarky view-point is that there&#x27;s no such thing as mastery but rather that it&#x27;s the proverbial journey.  There is however a baseline at the other end ... if it&#x27;s not functional and maintainable, there&#x27;s a definite lack of mastery.')
