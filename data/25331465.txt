Item(by='BoorishBears', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607343849, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25330456, text='I&#x27;ve had this argument a <i>ton</i> with people used to new high level languages that make null safety &quot;too easy&quot; like:<p><pre><code>  next?.doThing()\n</code></pre>\nWhere doThing is never called if next is null.<p>Languages that do this use &quot;scary&quot; operators to crash on null:<p><pre><code>  next!!.doThing()\n</code></pre>\nAnd it&#x27;s drilled into people&#x27;s heads the latter is a Bad Thing (tm)<p>-<p>You really need to consider context in null handling.<p>Imagine an app that alerts a nurse when the patients heartbeat is out of range.<p>In an application where the UI context might have been closed out, it&#x27;s common to see<p><pre><code>  someUiContext?.showAlert()\n</code></pre>\nBut what actually happens if the context is gone?<p>It&#x27;s better to crash and have part of your startup procedure be communicating that a crash occurred, and the doctor should check that something went wrong, than silently continuing.<p>-<p>The problem is when you tell people this, the kneejerk reaction is always &quot;are you saying we intentionally add crashes&quot;!<p>Because safe null handling was specifically added to avoid the situation where that crashes...<p>But I think the pendulum has swung too far at this point')
