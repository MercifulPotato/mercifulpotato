Item(by='andor', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607442806, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25325627, text='I recommend searching  for &quot;vitamin c training adaptation&quot; on Google Scholar. You&#x27;ll get a few results plus a list of citations for each result (click &quot;cited by&quot;). The citations help to put each paper into context.<p>The general idea is that high doses of antioxidants influence cellular pathways that are usually triggered by the presence of reactive oxygen species. The vitamin C reacts with the ROS before the body can build up a response.<p>I&#x27;m not a coach or nutritionist, I can&#x27;t give a good recommendation. I think the trend goes towards supplementing as little as possible and instead eating enough fruit and whole foods. The body is extremely complex (check out <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;biochemical-pathways.com&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;biochemical-pathways.com&#x2F;</a>), supplementing with high dosage might have consequences we don&#x27;t understand yet.<p>Personally I stopped taking vitamin C a while ago and have not noticed a difference.<p>&quot;Dylan Johnson&quot; is a coach who gives science-based recommendations, he has a video on Youtube about vitamin C.')
