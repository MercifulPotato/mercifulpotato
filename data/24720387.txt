Here is a (1599!) plea to Englishmen to cease wastefully killing each other in  duels (esp. with Italian mores&#x2F;swords&#x2F;styles) b&#x2F;c those swords&#x2F;styles are unsuited to generalized warfare (and thus provide no useful training for the common defense) and also are inferior to other more common weapons&#x2F;styles, esp. the short staff, in single combat:<p><i>&quot;Now for the vantage of the short staff against the sword and buckler, sword &amp; target, two handed sword, single sword, sword and dagger, or rapier and poniard, there is no great question ... the short staff has the vantage against the battle axe, black bill, or halberd: the short staff has the advantage, by reason of the nimbleness and length: he will strike and thrust freely, and in better and swifter time than can the battle axe, black bill, or halberd, and by reason of his judgement, distance and time, fight safe. And this resolve upon, the short staff is the best weapon against all manner of weapons, the forest bill excepted...Also the short staff has advantage against two swords and daggers, or two rapiers, poniards and gauntlets(19)...</i><p>From George Silver&#x27;s <i>Paradoxes of Defense</i>:<p><a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.pbm.com&#x2F;~lindahl&#x2F;paradoxes.html" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.pbm.com&#x2F;~lindahl&#x2F;paradoxes.html</a><p>wherein Silver analyzes the interaction of various weapons  in great detail, underscores the advantages of the short staff in most situations and in doing so presages Theodore Roosevelt:<p>&quot;I have always been fond of the West African proverb:  <i>&#x27;Speak softly and carry a big stick: you will go far!&#x27;</i>&quot;<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.phrases.org.uk&#x2F;meanings&#x2F;speak-softly-and-carry-a-big-stick.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.phrases.org.uk&#x2F;meanings&#x2F;speak-softly-and-carry-a...</a>')
