Item(by='Arkdy', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607636490, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25377695, text='Part of AMP (1) is a web component library grounded in the extensible web manifesto (2).<p>The plan was that the browser vendors give developers deeper access so that they could test new elements&#x2F;standards through web components and polyfills.<p>The AMP component library is one example of this, and stories are one of their forms. So basically if you use their tags to structure your content, it creates a ig&#x2F;sc style Web Story.<p>The structure that they have is (3):\nStory -&gt; Pages -&gt; Layers -&gt; Elements<p>Each page represents a tappable screen, \nLayers occupy the full screen, stack and have layout defaults like fill or lower-third and\nElements can be regular html elements or other AMP components.<p>I think that it&#x27;s a neat way of organizing pages of information and made a clojure wrapper around it here: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;rainbow-bamboo&#x2F;sargam" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;rainbow-bamboo&#x2F;sargam</a><p>1. <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;amp.dev&#x2F;about&#x2F;mission-and-vision&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;amp.dev&#x2F;about&#x2F;mission-and-vision&#x2F;</a>\n2. <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;extensibleweb&#x2F;manifesto&#x2F;blob&#x2F;master&#x2F;README.md" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;extensibleweb&#x2F;manifesto&#x2F;blob&#x2F;master&#x2F;READM...</a>\n3. <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;amp.dev&#x2F;documentation&#x2F;guides-and-tutorials&#x2F;start&#x2F;visual_story&#x2F;parts_of_story&#x2F;?format=stories" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;amp.dev&#x2F;documentation&#x2F;guides-and-tutorials&#x2F;start&#x2F;vis...</a>')
