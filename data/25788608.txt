Item(by='bipson', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610701589, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25788299, text='Only considering that:<p>- some recipes can be <i>very</i> finicky regarding ratios,<p>- e.g. different flour types can vary quite drastically in density<p>- you can e.g. easily reduce the volume of a flour batch by at least 15% by pouring differently (or simply bumping the container a few times)<p>going by volume alone still works for most recipes and is quite frankly as easy as it gets.<p>Going by weight alone also is not entirely scientific though (e.g. moisture of raw ingredients is usually not exact&#x2F;controlled). And flour can behave differently, based on batch or type, even if you get the weight perfectly right.<p>So most bakers have their recipes &quot;dialed in&quot;, i.e. they have a feel for the right texture, how the dough behaves, how to adjust resting times, etc.')
