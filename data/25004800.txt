Item(by='ncmncm', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604635732, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24996872, text='Right, conversion of iron to rust yielding heat is 100% efficient, less only the work needed to move the rust out of the way.<p>If you wanted to convert that heat back to electricity, there would be losses, but nowhere near as much as some people are saying. 1800 degrees minus ambient is a very big delta T. You are not bound to a closed cycle, so Carnot does not apply. So it is likely 80+% would be achievable, maybe over 65% round trip.')
