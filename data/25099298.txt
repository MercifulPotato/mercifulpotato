Item(by='jv22222', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605428500, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25098304, text='The main reason why it isn&#x27;t immeasurably harder for an indie founder is they can target market segments that are just not interesting to market leaders who already have a tight&#x2F;clear idea of who their target customer is.<p>Additionally, as an indie founder you essentially have an invisibility cloak because you won&#x27;t even be on the radar of the market leaders.<p>These two reasons are why someone like Less Annoying CRM can build a $1m&#x2F;year business and Intuit couldn&#x27;t even give a tiny care about it.<p>You can even study the market leaders and tackle things they are not interested in fixing - but that some customers have asked for on forums and such.<p>Of course, the indie founder invisibility cloak effect diminishes the smaller the market is, or the larger market share you take.<p>It&#x27;s pretty hard to go un-noticed when you enter a market that just has 1,000  customers.<p>Likewise, it&#x27;s pretty hard to go un-noticed when you start getting 1%+ of a larger market. But that&#x27;s ok, because if you do get that big, you most likely know what you&#x27;re doing.')
