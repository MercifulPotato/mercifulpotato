Item(by='pjc50', descendants=None, kids=[25488849], score=None, time=1608491177, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25487435, text='I&#x27;d love to see a deep analysis of this, but I think a confluence of a few elements:<p>- fixed instruction width, small instruction set =&gt; simple decode<p>- instructions wide enough to load many constants into registers in immediate mode<p>- comparatively large register file (15 general + PC) compared to the 80386 (effectively 8 + PC, plus the segment registers) meant reduced need to fill&#x2F;spill intermediate values, a tradeoff which gets better over time as the power consumption of bus doesn&#x27;t benefit from Moore&#x27;s law since it&#x27;s off chip<p>- any-to-any register instructions saves space and time shuffling values within the CPU (again, a hassle on the x86 series)<p>- conditional execution bits were a nice pipelining assistance trick, not clear how widely used they were as they weren&#x27;t included in Thumb<p>- not having the 8086&#x27;s ridiculous segment system')
