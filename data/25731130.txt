Item(by='silluk', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610382343, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25728241, text='To me it&#x27;s not the trivial cases like this that make type inference useful. It&#x27;s when you get longer types like `Arc&lt;Mutex&lt;HashMap&lt;String, String&gt;&gt;&gt;`. Granted, that could be solved with a `type` declaration (or `typedef` in C++) but it&#x27;s still convenient to be able to say: `let mut x = Arc::new(Mutex::new(HashMap::new()));` and let the compiler figure out the rest based on usage.')
