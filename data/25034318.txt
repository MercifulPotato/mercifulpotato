Item(by='muspimerol', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604926995, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25033680, text='I definitely understand the desire to avoid a build-time tool. Especially Gatsby is way harder than it should be to get up and running. But for me, portability and data integrity trumps ease-of-publishing. If you want to move your blog to a new system in 5 years, you&#x27;ll have to migrate a bunch of HTML. If you had the posts in markdown, chances are it would be a plug-and-play with the new system.<p>That&#x27;s the reason I pointed out the &lt;span&gt; usage - your source of truth for your data is HTML, but in this case it&#x27;s already getting mixed up with design-related oddities.<p>Not bashing your choice for your blog, I think it&#x27;s reasonable. I just wonder if HTML is the most resilient way to store blog data. It&#x27;s something I considered a lot when redoing my blog last year.')
