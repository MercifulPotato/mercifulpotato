Item(by='PaulHoule', descendants=None, kids=[24908079, 24908207], score=None, time=1603810211, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24907777, text='To remind people:  Yann LeCun is an engineering superstar who was working on neural networks at Bell labs long before they were cool.<p>Those handwritten digits that are a scourge today (e.g. &#x27;would any of these methods work on a different set of symbols?&#x27; is unasked) came from a competition to develop a commercial zip code reader for the U.S. Postal Service post back in the day of the Apple Newton.  He won it!<p>I was a bagman for text classification data in the early 2000&#x27;s and his reviews of the results you got using methods of the time (Naive Bayes, Rocchio, Perceptron, SVM) showed a depth of thought and attention to detail which helped me pick and choose tools to make classifiers with fairly predictable performance and development paths.<p>GPT-3 on the other hand does a good job of spouting nonsense like Peter Thiel and that has something to do with it&#x27;s emotional appeal.  People make fun of it and laugh at the mistakes it makes like those videos where somebody kicks down one of those Boston Robotics dogs:  it&#x27;s just good enough to be an object for those sort of feelings.')
