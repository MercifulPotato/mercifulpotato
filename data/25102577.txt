Item(by='fuzzfactor', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605459315, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25089497, text='&gt;The fact that hazard rather than ability plays so large a role is not alone or even predominantly owing to the &#x27;human, all too human&#x27; factors, which naturally occur in the process of academic selection as in any other selection. It would be unfair to hold the personal inferiority of faculty members or educational ministries responsible for the fact that so many mediocrities undoubtedly play an eminent role at the universities. The predominance of mediocrity is rather due to the laws of human co-operation, especially of the co-operation of several bodies, and, in this case, co-operation of the faculties who recommend and of the ministries of education.<p>from Science as a Vocation, by Max Weber (1918) (american.edu)<p>Bureaucracy costs more than money, how much bureaucracy would you like?')
