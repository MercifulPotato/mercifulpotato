Item(by='daanx', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606070279, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25179225, text='Just to add to this: Koka can (obviously :-)) not always determine if a function will terminate or not so it generally adds a `div` effect whenever there is the possibility of infinite recursion.<p>However, since most data types in Koka are inductive, any recursion over such inductive data types are still inferred to be always terminating.<p>In practice, it looks like about 70% of a typical program can usually be `total`, with 20% being `pure` (which is exceptions + divergence as in Haskell), and the final 10% being arbitrary side effects in the `io` effect.')
