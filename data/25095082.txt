Item(by='PragmaticPulp', descendants=None, kids=[25095518, 25095720], score=None, time=1605383614, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25094940, text='I worked at a company founded by an ex-Apple CEO. He tried this technique because Steve Jobs did it. The problem was that it doesn’t work at all unless you truly have 2 or 3 entire teams of fully competent people to pull it off. It can work at Apple because they’re absolutely massive and they only focus on a very small number of products.<p>In our case, making two teams work independently on the same  problem just created a situation where each team had half as many resources.<p>Given Intel’s history of paying median rather than top wages and losing their best employees to competitors, they didn’t even have one full department capable of pulling this off, let alone 2 or 3.')
