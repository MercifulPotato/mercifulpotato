Item(by='dragonwriter', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609092497, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25552079, text='&gt; The way I interpret DKE is that low level people don’t (yet) know what they don’t know.<p>That&#x27;s obviously a part of it, but if that was all of it, you would expect accuracy of assessment of relative ability to consistently get better with relative ability.<p>But, in fact, the result is basically “everyone seems themselves as closer to the 70th percentile than they actually are”.<p>Everyone focusses on the low end of DKE to use it as a low-brow dismissal, but the fact that people at the top of the distribution tend to <i>underestimate</i> their relative ability is also interesting.<p>&gt; imposters DO exist (that’s kind of why we use interviews when hiring people).<p>Yes, the presence of imposters in hiring and hiring-policy-setting positions is why we use assessment tools that don&#x27;t work very well to evaluate candidates.')
