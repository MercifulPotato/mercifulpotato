Item(by='Nullabillity', descendants=None, kids=[24778366, 24781630], score=None, time=1602692286, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24778272, text='Docker uses iptables for port forwarding, and those rules typically end up ahead of the rules inserted by your firewall (firewalld&#x2F;ufw&#x2F;manual scripts&#x2F;whatever).<p>It&#x27;s not so much that they explicitly open a firewall rule, as that they take a networking path that isn&#x27;t really covered by traditional firewalls.<p>Another way of viewing it is that Docker &quot;is&quot; your firewall for your container workloads, and that adding a port-forward is equivalent to adding a firewall rule. Of course, that doesn&#x27;t change that public-by-default is a bad default.')
