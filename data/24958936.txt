Item(by='adimitrov', descendants=None, kids=[24959024, 24959491, 24960249, 24960728], score=None, time=1604232946, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24958882, text='As they say, those who seek to combat traffic jams by building more roads, would also attempt to lose weight by undoing their belts.<p>It&#x27;s not different with phones. Your 2 year old flagship phone does not have a laggy UI because it&#x27;s slow. It&#x27;s because people code for today&#x27;s phone. And as long as enough people keep buying new phones, the software market will not optimize apps, since it&#x27;s cheaper not to. And as long as software needs ever more resources, people will continue to buy new phones.<p>It&#x27;s recursive. But it&#x27;s not pure: the side effects are resource depletion, electronic waste, labour exploitation, increasing energy demand.<p>We won&#x27;t escape this vicious cycle by perpetuating that very behaviour. And so maybe we can all do something about it, however little. Maybe by accepting a tiny bit of UI lag here and there.')
