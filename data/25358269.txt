Item(by='simonebrunozzi', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607520955, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25342265, text='&gt; Simplifying, we could say that the CTO role is closer to architecture and code; whereas the VPE would be in charge of processes and management.<p>Hmm, not sure I fully agree with this separation between CTO and VPE (Engineering).<p>At large companies, the CTO is more of a public-facing technical person (e.g. Amazon&#x27;s Werner Vogels), who doesn&#x27;t necessarily have a team under him&#x2F;her; or, if it does, it&#x27;s a team of other CTOs (e.g. Google&#x27;s Will Grannis, or VMware&#x27;s Chris Wolf), as part of an &quot;office of the CTO&quot;.<p>My sense is that, for startups, the CTO role is usually interchangeable with VP of Engineering; but most people prefer the former because it almost implies &quot;founder&quot; too.<p>It&#x27;s a pity that there&#x27;s confusion around these roles, especially if applied to different type of companies, or stage of companies (early stage vs late stage vs public).')
