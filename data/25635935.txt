The next halving is May 7, 2024.<p>The amount of new coins mined will continue to decrease until 2140. You might want to read about bitcoin&#x27;s stock to flow: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;medium.com&#x2F;@100trillionUSD&#x2F;modeling-bitcoins-value-with-scarcity-91fa0fc03e25" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;medium.com&#x2F;@100trillionUSD&#x2F;modeling-bitcoins-value-w...</a><p><i>Sure, the code says that it will stop when 21 million are mined, but there&#x27;s no real guarantee that the code won&#x27;t change before we reach that point.</i><p>There&#x27;s over $500 billion (and growing) in market capitalization that is based on the limit being 21 million bitcoin and not ever changing. This was already litigated by the bitcoin community.<p><i>Or, the blockchain might fork (again) and now there are double the coins.</i><p>Again, this isn&#x27;t how it works.<p>There could be dozens of forks; that doesn&#x27;t matter. Only the longest chain with the most work on it matters. All of the hashing power would continue on the one true bitcoin chain while the forks languish.<p>Bitcoin is open source; anyone can download the source code, make a few changes and launch a new chain. But it would get virtually zero support.<p>You may not be aware of the number of bitcoin forks that have been attempted and failed… Bitcoin Cash, Bitcoin XT… you might want to do some reading: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.exodus.io&#x2F;blog&#x2F;bitcoin-fork&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.exodus.io&#x2F;blog&#x2F;bitcoin-fork&#x2F;</a>')
