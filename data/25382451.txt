Item(by='cjfd', descendants=None, kids=[25382487], score=None, time=1607662467, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25382301, text='If a computation goes through N states before completing the universe in which it takes place has to have at least N possible states. One theory (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Bekenstein_bound" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Bekenstein_bound</a>) would state that the possible number of bits of information in a universe with radius R is the surface of the sphere 4 * pi * R^2 expressed in Planck units. This gives a limit to how many states this universe can have.')
