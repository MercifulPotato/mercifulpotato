Item(by='AnimalMuppet', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605547450, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25108140, text='If the time to give a more accurate estimate takes more time than the actual work, you aren&#x27;t dealing with an estimate longer than three weeks.  If the estimate is less than a day, it&#x27;s not worth getting more precise.<p>To your first point:  Yes, that happens sometimes.  When it does, your estimates can be wrong.  (Hey, they&#x27;re <i>estimates</i> - they&#x27;re not prophecies.)  If that happens very often, though, you might add a fudge factor for &quot;that kind of thing&quot;.  Maybe something like &quot;unknown surprises crop up most of the time, and when they do, they take about 20% of the effort, so we&#x27;ll make our best estimate, then add 20%&quot;.  That won&#x27;t be perfect either - sometimes it will be 40%, and sometimes 0.  But, you know, <i>estimates</i>...')
