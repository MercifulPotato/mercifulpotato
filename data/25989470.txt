Item(by='jfk13', descendants=None, kids=[25996110], score=None, time=1612193327, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25989202, text='This reflects the fact that PDF uses the UTF-16BE encoding form for Unicode text, not UTF-8.<p>One oddity is that the PDF spec&#x27;s description of the &quot;Text String Type&quot;, e.g. at p.158 in <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.adobe.com&#x2F;content&#x2F;dam&#x2F;acom&#x2F;en&#x2F;devnet&#x2F;pdf&#x2F;pdf_reference_archive&#x2F;pdf_reference_1-7.pdf" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.adobe.com&#x2F;content&#x2F;dam&#x2F;acom&#x2F;en&#x2F;devnet&#x2F;pdf&#x2F;pdf_ref...</a>, appears to say there should be a leading BOM (U+FEFF) in the string here, but this doesn&#x27;t seem to be the case in reality. Indeed, adding one may cause issues for at least some PDF readers, according to discussion at <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;tesseract-ocr&#x2F;tesseract&#x2F;issues&#x2F;1150" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;tesseract-ocr&#x2F;tesseract&#x2F;issues&#x2F;1150</a>.<p>But anyhow, in short: that&#x27;s simply UTF-16BE text being represented as a series of bytes. It&#x27;s nothing to do with any kind of &quot;security hack&quot;, and the null bytes are not &quot;bad characters&quot;, they&#x27;re the high byte of each UTF-16 code unit.')
