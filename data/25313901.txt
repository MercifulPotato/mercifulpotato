Item(by='cepth', descendants=None, kids=[25314165], score=None, time=1607169494, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25313830, text='In the United States, liberal and leftist are distinct terms.<p>Even someone like Ben Shapiro recognizes a difference between liberals and leftists (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;twitter.com&#x2F;benshapiro&#x2F;status&#x2F;966081078166421504" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;twitter.com&#x2F;benshapiro&#x2F;status&#x2F;966081078166421504</a>).<p>Silicon Valley types would hardly be described as leftists. Numerous studies have been done on the attitudes of Silicon Valley founders and execs (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.vox.com&#x2F;2015&#x2F;9&#x2F;29&#x2F;9411117&#x2F;silicon-valley-politics-charts" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.vox.com&#x2F;2015&#x2F;9&#x2F;29&#x2F;9411117&#x2F;silicon-valley-politic...</a>). The distinctions are dramatic.<p>We see that on average, tech founders are less likely to support vs. even Democrats generally (not just progressives):<p>* Banning the Keystone XL pipeline (60% vs 78%)<p>* The individual healthcare mandate (59% vs 70%)<p>* Labor unions being good (29% vs 73%)<p>This is to say, the average Silicon Valley type, particularly the C-suite exec or founder, tends not to be on the left wing of the Democratic party.<p>During the 2020 Democratic primary, even the Silicon Valley billionaires who are openly Democratic-leaning donated to candidates who were not to the left of the field (i.e. Elizabeth Warren and Bernie Sanders) (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cnbc.com&#x2F;2019&#x2F;08&#x2F;13&#x2F;2020-democratic-presidential-candidates-donations-from-tech-execs.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cnbc.com&#x2F;2019&#x2F;08&#x2F;13&#x2F;2020-democratic-presidential...</a>):<p>* Eric Schmidt -&gt; Cory Booker and Joe Biden<p>* Reed Hastings -&gt; Pete Buttigieg<p>* Marc Benioff -&gt; Cory Booker, Kamala Harris, and Jay Inslee<p>* Reid Hoffman -&gt; Cory Booker, Kirsten Gillibrand, Amy Klobuchar<p>* Jack Dorsey -&gt; Andrew Yang, Tulsi Gabbard<p>* Ben Silbermann -&gt; Pete Buttigieg<p>I&#x27;m engaging with you in good faith, and because I was intrigued that in a previous comment you mentioned that you live in Spain (though who&#x27;s to say you&#x27;re not a US ex-pat). But calling US tech companies &quot;leftist&quot; is a stretch at best.')
