Item(by='bhupy', descendants=None, kids=[24671795], score=None, time=1601660285, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24664554, text='That&#x27;s correct, but also not particularly relevant, as there&#x27;s no society on the planet where low income salaries experience a higher <i>absolute</i> increase than higher income salaries.<p>The fundamental point is: there&#x27;s a narrative that lower income salaries 1) don&#x27;t increase at all, and 2) increase at a slower rate than higher income salaries, and both are false.<p>It&#x27;s also important to remember that the increase in salaries at the lower income levels, do NOT necessarily represent a single earner&#x27;s pay history. Those numbers show us the snapshot-in-time income numbers for everyone <i>currently at that salary level</i>, but does not account for people actually moving income levels as they progress in their career. The data isn&#x27;t &quot;here&#x27;s the life of Joe over the last 5 years&quot;, it&#x27;s &quot;here&#x27;s the life of people without college degrees that are X years old with Y years of experience with X &amp; Y held constant over 5 years&quot;.')
