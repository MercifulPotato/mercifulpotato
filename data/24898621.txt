Item(by='danpalmer', descendants=None, kids=[24898771], score=None, time=1603733058, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24898356, text='To expand on this...<p>Engineers often love to say you can&#x27;t do this because regular expressions parse regular languages, and HTML is context-sensitive, not regular, and therefore it&#x27;s impossible to parse.<p>What they often miss is that the language actually being scraped may only be regular. If you want to parse a page to see if it has the word Banana on it, then your language may defined as .<i>?Banana.</i>?, and that&#x27;s regular, it doesn&#x27;t matter that it&#x27;s HTML. This even applies to questions like &quot;does this contain &lt;element&gt; in the &lt;head&gt;?&quot;, or &quot;is there a table in the body&quot;.<p>HTML is not regular, but you&#x27;re not implementing a browser, you&#x27;re implementing the language of what you&#x27;re scraping, and that may well be regular.')
