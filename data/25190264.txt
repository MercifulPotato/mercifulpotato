Item(by='drewda', descendants=None, kids=[25190349], score=None, time=1606157915, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25188558, text='This blog post and the associated startup&#x2F;investment are, I think, identifying a real issue but not acknowledging the underlying structural issues: most independent creators probably do need to sell a range of complementary products and services to make ends meet. At the same time, this is still no guarantee that all their offerings will add up to a sufficient living. Just because some independent newsletter writers are able to do so does not necessarily mean that this model will work for the majority.<p>I think it&#x27;s fascinating to read about how this model has worked and has not worked for artists, starting with musicians and now extending across many different artist mediums.<p>William Deresciewicz&#x27;s &quot;Death of the Artist&quot; book interviews 100+ &quot;successful&quot; artists on their experiences selling through digital platforms. It&#x27;s particularly interesting to read about how the digital sales channels affect the type of art and the topics they chose to pursue. It isn&#x27;t necessarily &quot;dumbing down&quot; art, but it does change the art itself.<p>The book started with this article: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.theatlantic.com&#x2F;magazine&#x2F;archive&#x2F;2015&#x2F;01&#x2F;the-death-of-the-artist-and-the-birth-of-the-creative-entrepreneur&#x2F;383497&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.theatlantic.com&#x2F;magazine&#x2F;archive&#x2F;2015&#x2F;01&#x2F;the-dea...</a>')
