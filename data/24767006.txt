Item(by='interestica', descendants=None, kids=[24767661, 24767367], score=None, time=1602605999, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24765798, text='Not mentioned in the article, Hangul (the Korean alphabet) was created in 1443 by the King. It was meant to aid literacy because of the preexisting incompatibility of Chinese characters and the Korean spoken language. It&#x27;s actually surprisingly easy to learn - you could do it in a really short time. But, because the symbols (24 of them) represent sound only, you won&#x27;t usually know what you&#x27;re saying.<p>Also, it was designed with a certain concept in mind: &quot;The letters for the five basic consonants reflect the shape of the speech organs used to pronounce them, and they are systematically modified to indicate phonetic features&quot;<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.m.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Hangul" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.m.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Hangul</a>')
