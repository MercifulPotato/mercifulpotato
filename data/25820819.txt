Item(by='Kliment', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610970809, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25817509, text='Engineering things for predictable failure is often necessary when the alternative is a worse, dangerous failure further down the line. The classical example of this is the fuse - it&#x27;s a device designed to fail first in order to prevent other, more destructive failures. I know that&#x27;s not the kind of engineered failure point you&#x27;re thinking of, but it&#x27;s important to understand that things like this may actually have good reasons.')
