Item(by='firethief', descendants=None, kids=[25184108], score=None, time=1606073061, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25174421, text='Addition is O(log n), so the naive version is O(n log n).<p>Gauss would have used a pre-[1960](<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Karatsuba_algorithm" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Karatsuba_algorithm</a>) multiplication algorithm, with complexity at best O((log n) ^ 2).<p>Using an algorithm discovered in 2019 (cf. Harvey and van der Hoeven), it&#x27;s possible to multiply in O(log n * log log n), if you have a lot of time. This is presumably optimal.')
