However, that policy also says &quot;If one of the assets claiming a video has missing ownership information, the default policy action is Track (owner missing).&quot; which &quot;Allows video to be viewable on YouTube and tracks viewership, but does not serve ads against it.&quot;[1]<p>An alternative might just be to add some content at the end of the video which is not advertiser-friendly, which would demonetise the video while not annoying the viewers too much.<p>[0] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;support.google.com&#x2F;youtube&#x2F;answer&#x2F;6300781?hl=en" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;support.google.com&#x2F;youtube&#x2F;answer&#x2F;6300781?hl=en</a><p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;support.google.com&#x2F;youtube&#x2F;answer&#x2F;6085494?hl=en" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;support.google.com&#x2F;youtube&#x2F;answer&#x2F;6085494?hl=en</a>')
