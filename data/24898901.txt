Item(by='Swizec', descendants=None, kids=[24908942], score=None, time=1603734509, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24898377, text='The thrust of my argument is that there’s more of everything in a bigger city. As such you have more options and more competition.<p>The competition means that a lot of places scraping by in smaller cities can’t make it in a bigger city. In theory that means median quality is higher.<p>And even a 3-star restaurant won’t survive in a place that doesn’t get enough well-to-do visitors to sustain it.<p>For a more casual perspective, SF has 126 restaurants on the Michelin list (not all hve stars). My entire home country has 52.')
