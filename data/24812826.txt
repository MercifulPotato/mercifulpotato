Item(by='tsimionescu', descendants=None, kids=[24812955], score=None, time=1602965294, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24812601, text='Many more phonetic writing systems re-write loanwords to match local pronunciation, at least once the words are established enough.<p>For example, in Romanian, recent borrows do typically preserve the original spelling (e.g. English &#x27;computer&#x27;,&#x27;mall&#x27; -&gt; Romanian &#x27;computer&#x27;, &#x27;mall&#x27;; plausible Romanian phonetic spelling &#x27;compiutÄƒr&#x27;,&#x27;mol&#x27;). But older loan-words that have become established take on a phonetic spelling (e.g. French &#x27;bureau&#x27; -&gt; Romanian &#x27;birou&#x27;; English &#x27;interview&#x27;, &#x27;tramway&#x27;, &#x27;jam&#x27; -&gt; Romanian &#x27;interviu&#x27;, &#x27;tramvai&#x27;, &#x27;gem&#x27;).<p>So it is possible to absorb large amounts of loan-words into a language and completely disregard the orthography of the original language. I don&#x27;t think you lose much by doing that, in fact.')
