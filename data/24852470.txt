Item(by='kybernetikos', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603316099, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24848081, text='I think it&#x27;s because people like to laugh at silly economists for thinking that markets are efficient.<p>Of course economists know that markets are not perfectly efficient, but what they tend to believe is that markets <i>tend towards</i> efficiency, and markets that are big enough and free enough will correct trivial inefficiencies relatively quickly.<p>This is why the advice is generally that returns in easy to access markets are very closely related to the risk you took on.  If you made better than market returns, it&#x27;s probably because (whether intentionally or not) you took on more risk than the market as a whole.  This doesn&#x27;t hold true for those with an information edge, but more people believe they have such a thing than actually have it.')
