Item(by='notatoad', descendants=None, kids=[24832740], score=None, time=1603145942, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24830569, text='you&#x27;re right that using biometric data for authentication is bad, but that&#x27;s not what&#x27;s happening here.  faceid&#x2F;touchid stores a private key on your device, and uses your biometrics to unlock that private key.  it&#x27;s the same idea as using a yubikey or something where you have to press the button on the 2fa dongle to prove it&#x27;s physically in your posession and unlock the private key stored within it, but it goes beyond just pressing a button by doing some biometric identification.<p>the concern isn&#x27;t what if the biometric data is leaked, because the biometrics are only used to allow local access to your phone.  the concern is what if the private keys stored in your phone are leaked.  and the secure enclave makes that as close to impossible as any other existing solution.')
