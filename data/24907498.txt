Item(by='CivBase', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603807393, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24902926, text='&gt; There has never been an instance where I have not wanted backwards compatibility<p>There have been plenty of times where I have wanted to break backwards compatibility.  Breaking backwards compatibility is always a cost-benefit analysis.  I&#x27;ll absolutely take a USB Type-C port on a new phone even if it means it wont be compatible with my existing Micro USB cables.  At the same time, I do not value the tiny bit of extra thinness and waterproofing afforded by removing the 3.5mm headphone jack over the utility of the jack.<p>Breaking backwards compatibility is not inherently good or bad.  It&#x27;s a difficult decision which must be considered carefully.  Sometimes it&#x27;s great.  Sometimes it&#x27;s terrible.  Sometimes it&#x27;s a mixed bag.')
