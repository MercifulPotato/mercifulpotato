Item(by='jedimastert', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608861522, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25529676, text='Long-time self-taught improviser here[0]. 5 years is, I think, a reasonable time frame if you&#x27;re passionate. My suggestion would be to go through a beginner&#x27;s course, then as soon as you can start copying and analyzing. Find things like licks and styles and little bits here and there that you like, then try to learn them by ear.<p>I know the &quot;music is a language&quot; troupe is tired, but it really does help me explain. It&#x27;s sort of like muscle memory, but with a musical vocabulary.<p>Look up basic Chord theory, then look around youtube for theory videos on the kind of music you like. After a while, you remove thinking from the equation, and then it gets kinda hard to explain in a comment<p>[0]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;youtube.com&#x2F;user&#x2F;jedimastert0810" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;youtube.com&#x2F;user&#x2F;jedimastert0810</a>')
