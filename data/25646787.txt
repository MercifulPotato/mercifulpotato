If I make a basket full of housing, healthcare, college education, and fresh produce, I can show you that inflation is out of control.<p>If you look at the &quot;price changes&quot; chart, prices for the red line items have gone up 4-6% per year over 20 years.  While it&#x27;s nice that you can buy a 55&quot; flatscreen for the price of a 15&quot; crt from 1998, TVs (and cars, furniture, clothing) are occasional purchases and can be cost-controlled by choosing cheaper options.  Unlike healthcare&#x2F;insurance, where you&#x27;re stuck with paying for what you can get.')
