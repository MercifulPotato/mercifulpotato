Item(by='mfontani', descendants=None, kids=[24662609], score=None, time=1601645974, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24661126, text='I run apps like that inside a docker container, which is a little bit better, I think:<p>- security library updates update with the base OS, which I update religiously and images are rebuilt (via &quot;make&quot;) whenever the base updates, too<p>- the apps are effectively &quot;somewhat&quot; sandboxed<p>- I only bind-mount the directories I want the app to have access to (i.e. -v &quot;$HOME&#x2F;Pictures:&#x2F;home&#x2F;user&#x2F;Pictures&quot;, -v &quot;$HOME&#x2F;.config&#x2F;appname:&#x2F;home&#x2F;user&#x2F;.config&#x2F;appname&quot; etc)<p>While this isn&#x27;t ideal for graphical apps due to needing sharing the X11 socket for things to work well, which comes with its own type of problems...<p>... at least no app can change _my_ bashrc, simply because it can&#x27;t even see it, nevermind edit it.<p>Going one step further, some bind mounts can also be mounted &quot;:ro&quot; to ensure the app cannot change the contents.')
