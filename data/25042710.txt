Item(by='kory', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604980827, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25041361, text='I can&#x27;t help but feel that this article is overlooking luck as a factor.<p>Nathan&#x27;s journey is certainly one of hard work and continuous learning. He earned his success. But he also took a large risk. This is sort of acknowledged in &quot;income comes down between ladders&quot;, but is never explicitly stated.<p>There is a wealth of evidence online that shows a successful career at a high paying software company will, on average, build more wealth than entrepreneurship. That said, the wealth upside to entrepreneurship is not bounded unlike total compensation.<p>Nathan also started a software company at the beginning of what has arguably been the best decade for software. The most effective way to build wealth is to catch a wealth wave at the perfect time--not too early, but just as exponential growth begins to kick in. &#x27;09 was that time for software--especially software as a service. The tools were there but businesses had not yet captured their potential.')
