Item(by='hakfoo', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608776973, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25514930, text='Was I the only one who shed a tear when XFCE stopped having the CDE-alike panel design?<p>I wonder if it&#x27;s sort of a signal of the evolution of the free-software desktop experience.<p>A lot of the &#x27;90s&#x2F;&#x27;00s paradigms were pretty strongly &quot;we&#x27;re trying to replicate the experience of a lustworthy expensive system&quot; -- see WindowMaker&#x2F;AfterStep to look like a NeXT, FVWM to look like Motif back when that was expensive and proprietary, IceWM offering OS&#x2F;2 style themes, and how everyone went to make workalike themes when the QNX Photon &quot;full desktop on a 1.44M floppy&quot; demo disc dropped.<p>Now, they just have their own styles, but at the same time, they seem less uniquely identifiable.')
