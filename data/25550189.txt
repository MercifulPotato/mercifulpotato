Item(by='kybernetikos', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609065825, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25547867, text='Part of the problem is that there is so <i>much</i> to do that you tend to let the AI do almost all of it, and then get incredibly frustrated when it sends your key resource gathering units through known dangerous territory.<p>To really do this kind of gameplay well, you&#x27;d need to have the ability to task military units to guard supply train units, and for them to have the kind of intelligence &#x2F; user interface that doesn&#x27;t just have them defenselessly wandering around an opponents base.<p>It&#x27;s been interesting to read the <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;acoup.blog&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;acoup.blog&#x2F;</a> take on the importance of supply chains and logistics in warfare, and how to organise a properly defended supply chain. I agree that it&#x27;s an area that could be addressed interestingly, but it&#x27;d be a game made out of the bits that other games deliberately abstracted away because they were hard to do well, so I think it&#x27;d be a real challenge.  Even fiction tends to abstract away from the details of these things because it&#x27;s so hard to make interesting.')
