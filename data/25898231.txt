The students were significantly easier to tap into and often cheaper if compensation was involved.<p>With that said, if there&#x27;s an IRB process for any sort of human subject work, it&#x27;s always skirting along the ethically grey area to do this. So, not only is your data biased towards a specific subpopulation (concentrated age groups, often concentrated socioeconomic statuses, etc.), faculty are often on the verge of unethical conduct with students.<p>98% is pretty high even for the cheapest of faculty I interacted with.')
