Item(by='Majromax', descendants=None, kids=[25702431], score=None, time=1610210111, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25701021, text='But the NYT can carry liability for letters to the editor published in its dead tree format -- see <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.rcfp.org&#x2F;supreme-court-will-not-hear-letter-editor-libel-case&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.rcfp.org&#x2F;supreme-court-will-not-hear-letter-edit...</a> as an example of a local newspaper being held liable for letter-to-the-editor-published defamation.<p>The CDA draws a bright line between content &quot;authored&quot; by a firm and content &quot;made available.&quot; In practice, that line is fuzzy.<p>As a hypothetical example, Twitter probably <i>should</i> face liability if it took a random tweet (say) accusing Bezos of pedophilia and made an editorial decision to promote that tweet to all its users, but it could still plausibly claim that it was just making the content available.<p>It&#x27;s a complicated topic, and I don&#x27;t know where the best balance lies.')
