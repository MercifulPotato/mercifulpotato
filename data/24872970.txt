Item(by='Frondo', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603481559, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24867761, text='I don&#x27;t want to get into an &quot;all languages are the same &#x2F; all languages have quirks&quot; kind of thing, but the idea that PHP is weird, different, and in a sense inscrutable, <i>and this has an impact on its use and performance</i>, just doesn&#x27;t bear out.<p>Yes, it has quirks -- so does C, so does C#, so does JS, so does lua, so does tcl, so does every assembly language I&#x27;ve ever worked with.<p>So what? Are those preventing people from writing good code? Are those preventing people from shipping? Could it be improved? Has it already been improved? No, no, yes, and yes.<p>The biggest reason PHP&#x27;s gotten so much scrutiny, to where people write these kinds of posts, is its ubiquity. When JS moved from being that simple, silly language built into the browser into a widely used one, we started seeing slide deck gags about the inscrutability of its comparison operators, yet JS seldom attracts this level of derision.')
