Item(by='JdeBP', descendants=None, kids=[24899162, 24896571, 24895327], score=None, time=1603705804, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24892924, text='In several European countries until 2003 or so, this was indeed the case.<p>For example:  Making a local temporary copy in a cache or on a hard disc counted (obviously) as <i>making a copy</i> under the U.K.&#x27;s 1988 <i>Copyright, Designs, and Patents Act</i>, which had no exception for computer caches and temporary copies, and which pre-dated the invention of the WWW (albeit not of UUCP).  Doing so thus infringed upon copyright.<p>* <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;legislation.gov.uk&#x2F;ukpga&#x2F;1988&#x2F;48&#x2F;contents&#x2F;enacted" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;legislation.gov.uk&#x2F;ukpga&#x2F;1988&#x2F;48&#x2F;contents&#x2F;enacted</a><p>The European Union recognized this defect in the law and came up with Directive 2001&#x2F;29&#x2F;EC, which explicitly carved out in article 5 an exception to copyright for &quot;temporary acts of reproduction&quot;.<p>* <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;eur-lex.europa.eu&#x2F;legal-content&#x2F;EN&#x2F;TXT&#x2F;HTML&#x2F;?uri=CELEX:32001L0029&amp;from=EN" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;eur-lex.europa.eu&#x2F;legal-content&#x2F;EN&#x2F;TXT&#x2F;HTML&#x2F;?uri=CEL...</a><p>The U.K. implemented this Directive (Directives having to be implemented by legislation in member states of the E.U.) via a statutory instrument, the 2003 <i>Copyright and Related Rights Regulations</i>, which amended the 1988 Act to carve out this exception in U.K. law.<p>* <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;legislation.gov.uk&#x2F;uksi&#x2F;2003&#x2F;2498&#x2F;note" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;legislation.gov.uk&#x2F;uksi&#x2F;2003&#x2F;2498&#x2F;note</a><p>* <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;legislation.gov.uk&#x2F;ukpga&#x2F;1988&#x2F;48&#x2F;section&#x2F;28A" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;legislation.gov.uk&#x2F;ukpga&#x2F;1988&#x2F;48&#x2F;section&#x2F;28A</a><p>Browsing the WWW with caching proxies and caching WWW browsers was technically a violation of copyright in U.K. law for about 11 years.  And the U.K. wasn&#x27;t alone in this.  Several other E.U. member states also had to tweak their copyright legislation because it didn&#x27;t carve out this exception for cached WWW browsing.<p>But the RIAA&#x27;s objection to youtube-dl is not the making of temporary copies for legitimate browsing and viewing purposes.  It is the circumvention of YouTube&#x27;s mechanisms in order to make <i>permanent</i> copies that aren&#x27;t being used as an integral part of WWW browsing.  The RIAA is not challenging the making of temporary copies.  It is challenging the circumvention of access control mechanisms.<p>It is asserting that youtube-dl circumvents the &quot;YouTube rolling cipher&quot; mechanism, whatever that is (the referenced explanation being in German and apparently gibberish), and so is a tool for circumventing technological access control measures.  So whether you can legally use WWW browsers is not the point at issue.  It is whether youtube-dl is a tool that enables access to YouTube contents by circumventing a &quot;cipher&quot;.<p>* <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;openjur.de&#x2F;u&#x2F;2194436.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;openjur.de&#x2F;u&#x2F;2194436.html</a><p>Ironically, this same consideration applies to tools like<p>* Real Player Downloader (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.real.com&#x2F;uk&#x2F;realplayer" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.real.com&#x2F;uk&#x2F;realplayer</a> );<p>* Media Human&#x27;s YouTubeDownloader (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;mediahuman.com&#x2F;howto&#x2F;download-vevo-video.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;mediahuman.com&#x2F;howto&#x2F;download-vevo-video.html</a> );<p>* Ace Thinker&#x27;s Video Keeper Lite (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;acethinker.com&#x2F;video-keeper-lite" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;acethinker.com&#x2F;video-keeper-lite</a> );<p>and many others.  Interestingly, furthermore, the RIAA appears to assert that the &quot;rolling cipher&quot; applies to all YouTube content, however copyright licensed.')
