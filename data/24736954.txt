Item(by='ithkuil', descendants=None, kids=[24737288, 24737318], score=None, time=1602309633, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24736872, text='&gt; But obviously it&#x27;s for original RISC i.e. ARM.<p>Not sure if you actually mean that, but to me it sounds like you&#x27;re implying that ARM is the &quot;original RISC&quot;.<p>The first RISC processor was called RISC-I and came out of Berkeley research in 1981.<p>Around the same time came out the MIPS cpu, from Standford university, by a group who shortly after founded a company that by 1985 commercialized the first RISC CPU, the R2000.<p>ARM, SPARC and PA-RISC came out just about the same time.<p>The ideas were brewing for some time, but I think it&#x27;s safe to say historically that the name &quot;RISC&quot; was not invented by ARM and that the R in ARM references a pre-existing term that has been developed at Berkeley.')
