Item(by='TheOtherHobbes', descendants=None, kids=[24813150, 24814928, 24813175], score=None, time=1602966562, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24812598, text='This is trivially disproven by the excess mortality figures from earlier in the year.<p>By the start of the Summer the UK had around 60,000 excess deaths <i>above the five year median</i> - which included at least one fairly severe flu season.<p>Later in the Summer when lockdown was still in place and&#x2F;or infection rates were still very controlled, the number of excess deaths dipped slightly below the median - as you would expect it to, given that people weren&#x27;t commuting and there were far fewer road accidents.<p>The figures also disprove the usual talking point that other deaths had increased dramatically because hospital care and chronic medical attention were hard to access. There were certainly some extra deaths, but <i>not on the scale of COVID itself.</i><p>Unless you&#x27;re going to claim that some other lethal illness was stalking the land and no one had noticed, COVID is the only remotely plausible explanation for those excess deaths.')
