Item(by='hannibalhorn', descendants=None, kids=[24864922], score=None, time=1603372569, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24854622, text='I have a Unifi USG, which has a third party wireguard addon, though of course OpenWRT could work just fine.  You setup a wireguard tunnel normally, but disable the default routing (on a Unifi, set route-allowed-ip false), and then explicitly add a route for 10.124.0.0&#x2F;16 through the wireguard interface.  The mullvad servers page[1] has socks 5 proxy addresses for all of their servers - assign one to a firefox container and you&#x27;re all set.  No reason you couldn&#x27;t have a container for each geographic region you want.<p>1. <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;mullvad.net&#x2F;en&#x2F;servers&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;mullvad.net&#x2F;en&#x2F;servers&#x2F;</a><p>On a USG the config looks like this:<p><pre><code>    wireguard wg0 {\n        address ***&#x2F;32\n        mtu 1420\n        peer *** {\n            allowed-ips 0.0.0.0&#x2F;0\n            endpoint ***:51820\n        }\n        private-key ***\n        route-allowed-ips false\n        up-command &quot;ip route add 10.64.0.1&#x2F;32 dev wg0 &amp;&amp; ip route add 10.124.0.0&#x2F;16 dev wg0&quot;\n    }</code></pre>')
