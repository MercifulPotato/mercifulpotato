This is compliant HTML:<p><pre><code>    &lt;!DOCTYPE html&gt; &lt;title&gt;something&lt;&#x2F;title&gt; hello\n</code></pre>\nBut that&#x27;s just notation thing, implicit element will be automatically opened&#x2F;closed, all rules still apply. Thus<p><pre><code>    &lt;!DOCTYPE html&gt; hello &lt;title&gt;something&lt;&#x2F;title&gt;\n</code></pre>\nis not valid, because &#x27;hello&#x27; implicitly opens &lt;body&gt;, and &lt;title&gt; is not allowed to be there. Of course all of these would probably work in practice, because there&#x27;s no junk that browser wouldn&#x27;t happily accept. Discussed quine also violates standard, because it misses doctype and places &lt;style&gt; tags in body.')
