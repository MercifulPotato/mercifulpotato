Item(by='jka', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1601649531, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24662477, text='I&#x27;ll explain why I was wondering about disposable income in a little more detail:<p>In some dense cities in particular, renting (typical for young people prior to property purchase) can be very expensive.<p>My sense is that this creates a large transfer of wealth from professionals to property owners on a monthly basis.<p>Across San Francisco or New York, for example, what percentage of the paycheck sums paid by employers is redirected to their landlords?<p>If that percentage were to rise it would become more difficult for young people to become property owners since it&#x27;d be challenging for them to put savings aside.<p>With that context provided: disposable income is an attempt to quantify how effectively people could save towards property purchase - regardless of whether they decide to take that route.<p>(and to some extent it&#x27;s also a &#x27;guard metric&#x27; to detect communities that rent-seek to unsustainable levels)<p>Edit: &#x27;tech companies&#x27; -&gt; &#x27;employers&#x27; in third para, and remove assumption of high salary.  This doesn&#x27;t just affect tech..')
