Item(by='zmmmmm', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605992274, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25171123, text='Another caveat depending on your jurisdiction: where I live, claiming part of your home as a work place excludes it from qualifying as you &quot;residence&quot; for tax purposes and can mean you get hit with a raft of other complications.<p>For my case, I claimed 1 room of my house as a home office for 10 years, and then when we sold the house my accountant told me we had to pay a huge sum of money in tax because there is a capital gains tax exemption for your primary residence but 25% of the house now did not qualify for that. I paid something like 5 times as much in capital gains tax as I saved from those minor tax writeoffs over the years.<p>Also - make sure to check insurance. Costs could be higher or you might even not be covered at all.')
