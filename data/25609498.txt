Item(by='1e-9', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609552779, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25606769, text='This reminds me of Bertha Benz who made the first long-distance automobile trip in 1888.  Bertha wanted to convince her husband Karl that his invention had great potential and should be marketed.  So, early one morning, without telling anyone, she loaded up her two teenaged sons and set off over 65 miles of wagon tracks between Mannheim and Pforzheim.  Through ingenuity and determination, she overcame numerous obstacles to complete the illegal trip.  Fascinating woman.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Bertha_Benz" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Bertha_Benz</a>')
