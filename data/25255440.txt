Item(by='ftio', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606753479, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25239360, text='Tony&#x27;s a genius, but it&#x27;s important not to cargo cult this.<p>&quot;Culture-first&quot; worked at Zappos because of the specific culture they chose to pursue, namely an intensely customer-centric one. At that point, their culture is not merely <i>culture</i> but also <i>strategy</i>: we will win in this market by rejecting short-term optimization (charging for returns, spending less on customer support agents) in favor of long-term thinking that will produce customer loyalty and thus long-term success.<p>I know, I know. <i>Culture eats strategy for breakfast.</i> But the reality is that if you want to build a successful business that isn&#x27;t a daily soul-sucking grind (strategy only) or a fluffy wannabe &#x27;startup&#x27; flapping in the wind (culture only), you need both.')
