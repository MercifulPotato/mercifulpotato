Item(by='jswrenn', descendants=None, kids=[25424598], score=None, time=1607971362, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25421233, text='Yes, the pointer issues Ralf raises has implications about what operations Rust can make safe. An example Ralf posed to the safe transmute working group: a transmutation from a reference (i.e., a pointer with provenance) to one without (e.g., a &quot;raw&quot; pointer) or to a `usize` number cannot be safe. (Rust <i>can</i> safely provide these conversions via the `as` keyword, just not via the more general framework of transmutations).<p>More broadly: Rust <i>is</i> very sensitive to LLVM bugs that inadvertently generate UB. The longstanding issues with `noalias` optimizations is the prime example of this: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;stackoverflow.com&#x2F;a&#x2F;57259339&#x2F;7501301" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;stackoverflow.com&#x2F;a&#x2F;57259339&#x2F;7501301</a>')
