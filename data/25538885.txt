Item(by='dgb23', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608929001, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25538066, text='As a designer who can code you get a job as a Designer who can code.<p>I&#x27;ve been mentoring a Designer and friend over the last year, and I&#x27;m also teaching coding for designers since a short while.<p>The benefits are quite tangible: As a programmer, you still structure the application, set up the tooling, build the abstractions and the logic, but you&#x27;re now freed from fiddling with pixels, because the designer has already built his prototypes with implementation in mind, can communicate intent, compromises and solutions for edge cases, and they can also implement a lot of things by themselves, sometimes with guidance. Git is not rocket science either if you give them a clear workflow and resolve issues that are beyond what you&#x27;d expect.')
