Item(by='cpeterso', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607980249, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25423136, text='I like your description of this being a local maxima problem. The UX won&#x27;t escape this maxima with incremental user testing. Users also push back on any changes to existing products&#x27; UI. Sometimes the best option is to create a new sibling product with a new UX.<p>&gt; Apple didn&#x27;t used to suffer from this problem, because Steve Jobs didn&#x27;t care about data if something bothered him.<p>In his book &quot;Creative Selection: Inside Apple&#x27;s Design Process During the Golden Age of Steve Jobs&quot;, author Ken Kocienda (an early engineer on Safari and the iPhone) claims Apple doesn&#x27;t do <i>any</i> user testing to make data-driven product design decisions. Apple designers and engineers would create many prototypes and demo them up the VP reporting chain until the best prototypes got to Steve.')
