Item(by='WalterBright', descendants=None, kids=[25847667, 25847157, 25847213, 25847541, 25850192, 25847180, 25847797, 25847193, 25851278, 25845942], score=None, time=1611133015, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25843040, text='It&#x27;s important to note the reason the President has the power to pardon anyone for any reason is it&#x27;s one of the checks and balances, the Constitution is full of them. It&#x27;s meant as a check to blunt overzealous prosecutions.<p>Note that the President does <i>not</i> have the corresponding power to convict anyone.<p>An interesting set of pardons was when Andrew Johnson pardoned all the rebels in the Confederacy, including Jefferson Davis.<p>As far as I know, the only Confederate officers convicted and hanged were the ones running the POW camps. The crime was the brutal way they ran them, not rebellion.')
