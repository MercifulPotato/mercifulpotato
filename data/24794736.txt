Item(by='jariel', descendants=None, kids=[24794822], score=None, time=1602799703, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24794236, text='So this is an interesting point and shouldn&#x27;t be dismissed, it may be at the heart of irrational beliefs.<p>People can &#x27;come to believe&#x27; what benefits them.<p>If an idea is truly exciting or engaging, or makes people feel special, or part of a &#x27;group&#x27; esp. of &#x27;special people&#x27; - it&#x27;s just more likely we believe those things as &#x27;factual&#x27;.<p>So in a way - though they would &#x27;pass a lie detector test&#x27; and &#x27;truly believe in a flat earth&#x27; - it&#x27;s predicate upon all those things - it&#x27;s an <i>impassioned</i> or <i>subjective</i> kind of belief.<p>They would act almost perfectly rationally if you asked them about some completely mundane subject like rocks rolling down a hill.<p>Our egos tend to believe it when we&#x27;re told we are good, and tend to be dismissive when we are told we&#x27;re bad at something, that right there is evidence we&#x27;re not very objective about taking in data to begin with.')
