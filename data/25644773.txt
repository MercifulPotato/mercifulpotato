Item(by='londons_explore', descendants=None, kids=[25644960], score=None, time=1609849240, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25644408, text='2FA should be bypassable after some longish lockout period.<p>For example, someone has lost their password, email access, phone number, and 2FA app.   Make them wait a month to regain account access.<p>If any time during that month, the account is used or logged into, cancel the takeover request.  During the month, every day send an email to all points of contact on the account letting them know what will happen.<p>It&#x27;s a trade-off of the harm of unauthorized access to a dormant account Vs blocking someone from accessing their data (that is probably not backed up, and probably took considerable effort to create).<p>Have an account-level setting to disable such a process, for the people who might be offline for extended periods.')
