Item(by='cmpb', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611326753, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25868644, text='&gt;An unusually high number of reports of late breeding and captive rearing near the coast—primarily on tropical milkweed (Asclepias curassavica)—may also suggest there are higher numbers of non-migrating monarchs than usual which can negatively impact the population.<p>Just a friendly reminder to milkweed enthusiasts to always use your local native milkweed instead of the exotics. They might attract more butterflies, but they&#x27;re bad for the population because they spend more time later in the year on the exotics instead of migrating back for overwintering.')
