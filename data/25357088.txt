Item(by='MaxBarraclough', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607508744, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25356569, text='&gt; If you make a page of X bytes, Y number of resources of Z bytes, you can pretty easily calculate load time ranges for a variety of network conditions.<p>To expand on Cthulhu_&#x27;s point: I agree with the gist here but I think this is a bit of a simplification. From the user&#x27;s point of view, fast downloading is only part of the picture. There&#x27;s also the matter of render time, the dreaded <i>flash of unstyled content</i>, and related irritations such as banners and popups that appear after the page has seemingly finished rendering. (Wikipedia&#x27;s current fund-raising drive commits this sin, perhaps deliberately.) All of these things contribute to a sense of the page &#x27;getting in your way&#x27;. <i>edit</i> Another one for the list: distracting autoplaying videos. The Ars Technica site does this, for instance.<p>Sites like Pinboard and HackerNews are compact in terms of avoiding bloated JavaScript, but importantly they also stay out of your way in terms of UX.')
