Item(by='GianFabien', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609833060, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25640893, text='I can relate to your optimistic view. I was there once. By all means do the PhD if it is in an area that you are passionate about. You should remember that getting tenure is extremely difficult, so a career in academia is a very long shot. Like many others, I have found that having a PhD has had a negative impact upon my career.<p>So if you do decide to go ahead. Agree with your partner under what circumstances you will unconditionally abandon the programme.  Without that you run the risk of falling into the sunk cost fallacy. A part-time PhD will easily take 10 years of your life.  Your kids might be graduating from high school and daddy is still in school!<p>You might want to take a look at: <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;phdcomics.com" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;phdcomics.com</a> - it is truer to life than it might appear.')
