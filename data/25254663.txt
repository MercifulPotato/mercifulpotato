GCC, Perl, and Python meant never purchasing a compiler.<p>And purchasing, well, it&#x27;s the lesser of evils these days. Purchasing at least gave you vitually cart blanche access to software on your computer with time limits imposed only by processing architectures.<p>Now, in contrast, everything is moving towards subscriptions. 1Password. Microsoft Office. Photoshop. Sublime Text (professional). Apple Music (or Spotify, et.al.). Apple TV (or Netflix, et.al.). Novlr (a word processor).<p>All expect me to shell out anything from $1 to upwards of $50 a month in perpetuity for the right to borrow their software. And that&#x27;s not even mentioning microtransactions, for things like alternate brushes in PS.')
