Item(by='OliverJones', descendants=None, kids=[25651759], score=None, time=1609883029, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25650551, text='The grid-nerd term for rapid-response capacity delivery is &quot;frequency control ancillary services.&quot; It&#x27;s a real problem in times of heavy load. If a local grid&#x27;s frequency drops below nominal (60Hz US, 50Hz elsewhere) the regional grid has to disconnect. So without rapid-release stored energy a power generating org has to fire up extra capacity and keep it running to cover transients.<p>Old missile silos might be interesting. But most of them are far from population centers.<p>Pumped storage is still great given the right geography. Northfield Mountain has been running flawlessly for almost half a century, storing about 9 GwH. <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Northfield_Mountain_(hydroelectricity_facility)" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Northfield_Mountain_(hydroelec...</a>  But it takes about ten minutes to spool up. And the fish and boaters in the nearby Connecticut River definitely notice it.')
