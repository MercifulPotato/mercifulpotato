Item(by='remram', descendants=None, kids=[24655387, 24654382, 24654162, 24660748], score=None, time=1601574778, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24652733, text='A problem with most commands is that they usually don&#x27;t need extremely-unfortunate typos or russian-roulette-enabled shells to turn into something wrong: they are usually wrong <i>in passing</i>, while you&#x27;re typing them. I always wondered if something could be done against the effects of an accidental premature return. Some of us have cats...<p>`sudo rm -rf &#x2F;tmp&#x2F;workdir` starts with `sudo rm -rf &#x2F;`<p>`git reset libs` starts with `git reset` which will wipe your staging area<p>`docker-compose rm -f db` starts with `docker-compose rm -f` which will remove all stopped containers (and their logs)<p>Most commands just tend to do something very broad with no arguments, and the fact that paths start with bigger folders and go down to files doesn&#x27;t help either. No GUI tool would ask you to confirm before you type in the path, the way shell commands tend to.')
