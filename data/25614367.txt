You can also read it as:<p>&quot;A country&#x27;s segments (e.g. states) could have borders between them, but that doesn&#x27;t mean that each has borders with each other.&quot;<p>Another commenter explained what I meant well, but here&#x27;s my explanation:<p>The US might have a border with Canada, but doesn&#x27;t mean that Colorado also has a border with Canada.<p>Similarly, Alaska and Hawaii might be part of the US, and thus their borders are the US borders too, but that doesn&#x27;t mean e.g. Hawaii borders Nebraska.<p>Now borders might be on land or on water, but they&#x27;re still borders.
