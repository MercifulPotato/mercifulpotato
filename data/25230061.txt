Item(by='chriswarbo', descendants=None, kids=[25231749], score=None, time=1606494800, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25229344, text='I would absolutely love to see such a thing (e.g. VPRI&#x27;s &#x27;frank&#x27; system), but it&#x27;s quite a different approach than Scheme, and I wouldn&#x27;t want to see PLT&#x2F;Racket take such a drastic course change while there&#x27;s still so much interesting work to be done in the Scheme world (e.g. Kernel&#x27;s F-expressions are powerful, but hard to optimise; work on reflective towers and multi-stage programming is pushing languages beyond the traditional AOT&#x2F;JIT dichotomy; and so on).<p>From my understanding, Piumarta&#x27;s main trick with COLA is fast dynamic dispatch through a uniform interface (putting pointers to vtables at offset -1), and implementing vtables via interface (so they can be replaced). It&#x27;s a cool approach, but it&#x27;s also a case of &#x27;everything can be solved by adding a layer of indirection&#x27;; and I&#x27;ve not sat down and thought through how these ideas might apply to different paradigms, and what might be unique or common to them all.')
