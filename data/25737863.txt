Item(by='robocat', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610402049, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25729552, text='Hourly rates are not appropriate.<p>* experts are paid for applying their knowledge, not their time[1][2]<p>* A “fair” time based system should also pay for unsuccessful searches e.g. the previous month unsuccessfully searching for a bug in Chrome.<p>* if person A spends 1 hour finding bug X, and person B spends 1000 hours finding exactly the same bug X, then it is a fallacy that you could pick a fair hourly rate.<p>Also I’ll mention that you don’t get paid according to how much damage a bug can cause. 1: usually the damages occur to a third party (e.g. users of Microsoft Windows, not Microsoft). 2: imagine you find ten bugs that could wipe out the business Acme - you can’t get paid 10x Acme’s value (not even just 1x Acme’s profits.)<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;quoteinvestigator.com&#x2F;2018&#x2F;01&#x2F;14&#x2F;time-art&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;quoteinvestigator.com&#x2F;2018&#x2F;01&#x2F;14&#x2F;time-art&#x2F;</a><p>[2] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.snopes.com&#x2F;fact-check&#x2F;know-where-man&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.snopes.com&#x2F;fact-check&#x2F;know-where-man&#x2F;</a>')
