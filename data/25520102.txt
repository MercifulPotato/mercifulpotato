Some institutions will bias in favor of supporting high-visibility research over &quot;meat and potatoes&quot; base-line research (such as reproduction of other experimenters&#x27; results, or chasing down un-sexy problems of elaboration or exploration of an information space). Their incentives are to chase grant money and get published, even if the published results aren&#x27;t thorough or rigorous but will capture column inches (leaving someone else to do the un-sexy work of verifying the claims from a single experiment run).<p>It leaves gaps in the robustness of human knowledge, a bit of a Swiss-cheese at the edges that isn&#x27;t necessary, but for the way we reward people for cutting the edge instead of filling the back.')
