Item(by='hn_throwaway_99', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602031006, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24703929, text='As someone who has worked with lots of remote teams, I&#x27;ll give you a simple answers: timezones matter. A lot. Especially for relatively early stage companies where tight collaboration on new products is key. I have actually refused, and will continue to refuse, work that requires me to collaborate with someone 11.5 hours different. It sucks for everyone involved.<p>Years ago I had a job when I worked noon-5 and then 10pm-1am to overlap with colleagues in India. At first I thought &quot;Hey, I&#x27;m a night owl, this will be great.&quot; I grew to hate it pretty quickly, and I&#x27;ll never do it again.<p>Contrarily, I&#x27;ve worked with colleagues in Brazil who were only 2 or 3 hours off from me, and it worked out great.')
