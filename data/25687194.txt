Item(by='throwaway894345', descendants=None, kids=[25688831], score=None, time=1610127132, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25674868, text='Right, but if an individual spreads misinformation, child pornography, piracy, threats, harassment, defamation, etc they are liable. Social networks are uniquely not responsible for the content that they curate--they enjoy the best of both worlds, and they oughtn&#x27;t. If their curation is &quot;speech&quot; they should be legally accountable to for their speech like the rest of us are. There&#x27;s precedent here with telecoms--they&#x27;re free from scrutiny because they&#x27;re just dumb pipes.<p>This is one conceivable kind of legislation that doesn&#x27;t run afoul of the first amendment. Another approach might be to require social networks to interoperate so consumers can choose between different networks. I should be able to leave Twitter without leaving my <i>network</i>. In this way, Twitter (et al) loses a lot of its power and thus its ability to threaten democracy.<p>There&#x27;s more than one way to skin a cat.')
