But if I were to make an educated guess, I would say they used NOC (National Occupation Code) for determining what does or does not require a degree. NOC divides all occupations into 4 skill levels, from A (occupations that usually require university education) to D (occupations that usually do not require even a secondary school diploma). Most probably, they used the same framework and tallied the number of people in engineering, other A level occupations, and everyone else. Finance and consulting are definitely A level.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;noc.esdc.gc.ca&#x2F;Structure&#x2F;NocProfile&#x2F;36841eebd34e457e9ed5a16fda9145e9?objectid=uSzBscPUpnD%2FtIHmYZbYdC7VIbH9efXmkHNhOllqcTPi90qqDDAgavVV1ws4JDVW4p50puJD2UaF7d8B6YVfSXbb2pWkDSIFR%2FcMldBT6Rs%3D" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;noc.esdc.gc.ca&#x2F;Structure&#x2F;NocProfile&#x2F;36841eebd34e457e...</a><p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;noc.esdc.gc.ca&#x2F;Structure&#x2F;NocProfile&#x2F;f9c47da9dd2644f399e41adcc0635961?objectid=hTVs7PvQQWa2ZOTc%2B2HYbbREtRxtS7KPIZw16ilnuu4ktymb2AKZjmbap%2FFvDdmT" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;noc.esdc.gc.ca&#x2F;Structure&#x2F;NocProfile&#x2F;f9c47da9dd2644f3...</a>')
