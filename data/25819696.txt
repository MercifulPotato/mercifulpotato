Item(by='redis_mlc', descendants=None, kids=[25821813], score=None, time=1610960264, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25819641, text='A reverse proxy waits for a request from another server and responds to the calling server. You can think of a reverse proxy as the &quot;deep backend&quot; in a web stack.<p>One of the first and most powerful reverse proxies was mod_perl running under httpd. Typically 5 or 6 mod_perl instances can handle requests from 300 normal httpd children.<p>So when somebody says &quot;forward proxy&quot; they usually mean &quot;not a reverse proxy.&quot;')
