Item(by='Myrmornis', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606734628, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25252536, text='In the inner expectation there’s only one choice: you’re conditioning on Y so it’s clear that X is the random variable whose expectation is being taken.<p>In the outer expectation, there’s only one choice: X no longer exists as a potential random variable (as if it were a local variable in the inner expectation) so the outer expectation must be over Y.<p>I’m not saying that you’re wrong that those subscripts could be used (they often are) but the meaning of the expression is  clear after a little while working with expectations.')
