First of all, you don&#x27;t get tenure right away, so your first few years are brutal -- 80 hour weeks. You typically get &quot;start up&quot; funding, which eventually runs out. Then you have to come up with your own money. The basic salary is 9 months. Your summer salary comes out of your own grants. Also, if you stop making research progress, your promotions and pay raises evaporate, and it becomes difficult to promote yourself by job hopping.<p>Think of it like you&#x27;re a shopkeeper at the mall, and someone is paying your rent for you, which is nice, but you have to figure out how to actually make money.')
