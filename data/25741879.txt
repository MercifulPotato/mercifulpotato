Item(by='Alupis', descendants=None, kids=[25741948], score=None, time=1610422041, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25741844, text='More explicitly, you own the shape within a certain, limited in scope, industry (sometimes in broader industry).<p>Most trademarks that contain everyday words must explicitly state no ownership claim of such words, but instead claim ownership of the shape of the word (and sometimes color) within a specific context, ie. &quot;Apple&quot;.<p>For most trademarks, this usually means shape of the logo is what is protected, not the word used inside the logo.<p>So while you could create a lawn mowing service called &quot;Apple&quot; without any legal issue, you cannot use the exact shape of the letters (ie, font) in the Apple logo.')
