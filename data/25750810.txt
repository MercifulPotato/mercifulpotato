Item(by='dchichkov', descendants=None, kids=[25752700, 25751945], score=None, time=1610474949, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25748920, text='As per Wikipedia: &quot;A majority of developed democracies have laws that restrict hate speech, including Australia, Denmark, France, Germany, India, South Africa, Sweden, New Zealand, and the United Kingdom.&quot;<p>The Unites States is not in that list. Hence it is more vulnerable to problems associated with allowing hate speech (i.e. incitement of violence by foreign-state actors, etc.).<p>Companies that operate on the global markets tend to operate with the standards that are acceptable globally.  In particular: &quot;On 31 May 2016, Facebook, Google, Microsoft, and Twitter, jointly agreed to a European Union code of conduct obligating them to review &quot;[the] majority of valid notifications for removal of illegal hate speech&quot; posted on their services within 24 hours.&quot;<p>So in that particular case, influence of these companies might be bringing United States closer to best practices adopted in the majority of developed democracies.')
