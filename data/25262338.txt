Item(by='sova', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606797369, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25262264, text='Pay-per-click implies a surveillance network to track authentic clicks.  What if search engines were privacy-first?  What if a search engine had &quot;billboard time&quot; for advertising on search queries with a bidding system, rather than PPC?  No user data would need to be stored because it doesn&#x27;t matter how many times one person clicks the same ad.  Is privacy important to users in 2020?  People treat privacy like access to their credit card -- they are not that concerned.  The problem is that there are no chargebacks in privacy land, once the info is out there, it&#x27;s out there.  Would you use a search provider that provided just-as-good results if they could guarantee you would not be tagged and tracked relentlessly?')
