Item(by='wegs', descendants=None, kids=[25756214, 25756219, 25759132, 25756720, 25756479, 25758066], score=None, time=1610497392, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25755911, text='It&#x27;s an analogy. None are perfect. But people need livelihoods to live. It&#x27;s not okay or fair to destroy a business arbitrarily and capriciously.<p>My lesson here is that the Internet is essential infrastructure, and once companies reach a certain size (Google, Facebook, AWS), they should be regulated similarly to public utilities.<p>An electric company shouldn&#x27;t be able to cut off electricity to a crack house or brothel unless law enforcement first asks for it. Law enforcement should have due process appropriate to the situation; a terrorist attack should be cut off right now. A company making pirated copies of Windows should have their day in court. Your propaganda-believing gun-toting drunk crazy uncle should probably keep their electricity (which isn&#x27;t to say he shouldn&#x27;t be handled somehow, but perhaps committed to a medical facility).<p>I don&#x27;t feel good or bad about them being taken off-line, since I don&#x27;t actually know what they hosted. I do feel bad about that decision being made by corporations behind closed doors.')
