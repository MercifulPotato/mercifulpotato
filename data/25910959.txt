In particular, the machine that the voter makes their choice on prints out a paper ballot marked with that choice, which the voter then turns in to be counted on a different machine.<p>The paper ballots can then be used for risk limiting hand audit afterwards to verify that the machine count was accurate, or in the case of a close race a full hand recount.')
