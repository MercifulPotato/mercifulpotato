The numeric features are well thought out and generally work the way you&#x27;d expect. The same with syntax. Swift Playgrounds are a REPL with ability to visualize what your program is doing to build on intuition.<p>If you&#x27;re keen on using higher level language features such as generics, you can make use of them easily. Otherwise they&#x27;ll stay out of your way. In terms of progressive disclosure of complexity, it is the best language I&#x27;ve used. And I&#x27;ve used many.<p>There are also many[1][2][3][4][5] open source projects to peruse. Even if you&#x27;re not on Mac, there is Linux and Windows support, though I don&#x27;t have direct experience with them.<p>The language itself is now fairly stable with a fixed ABI, relatively fast, and is open source so you can modify it if that&#x27;s something you&#x27;re interested in down the road. Despite the commentary, it&#x27;s not an Apple-only language, though when you&#x27;re outside the Apple ecosystem things are more bottom-up and hence the experience can be inconsistent at times.<p>I credit Swift with revitalizing my own interest in mathematics. It made re-learning the stuff I&#x27;d forgot from college fun and interactive.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;apple&#x2F;swift-numerics" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;apple&#x2F;swift-numerics</a><p>[2] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;phlegmaticprogrammer&#x2F;LANumerics" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;phlegmaticprogrammer&#x2F;LANumerics</a><p>[3] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;apple&#x2F;swift-algorithms" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;apple&#x2F;swift-algorithms</a><p>[4] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;stsievert&#x2F;swix" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;stsievert&#x2F;swix</a><p>[5] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;AlexanderTar&#x2F;LASwift" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;AlexanderTar&#x2F;LASwift</a>')
