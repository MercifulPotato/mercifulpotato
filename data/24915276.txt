However, when I send the almost-complete draft to a professional copy editor, it was indeed a pain to add the comments. Either handwritten+scanned, as acrobat comments, or word comments, they had to be manually input into the markdown file.<p>- Everything else worked relatively better. It was a bit tedious to type loooong pandoc commands &quot;pandoc --filter=... etc etc&quot; so I recently coded pandocmk [1] to make my life easier. It&#x27;s not super well documented (but it&#x27;s a quite short script, so readable), but the idea is that you type the command line options as metadata at the top.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;sergiocorreia&#x2F;pandocmk" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;sergiocorreia&#x2F;pandocmk</a>')
