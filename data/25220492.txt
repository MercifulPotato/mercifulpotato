Item(by='kortex', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606402457, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25220241, text='I&#x27;ll bite, as I&#x27;ve long had this same question.<p>So the short answer is, it looks up the hash in the hidden services directory (iirc it&#x27;s a distributed hash table of sorts): <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;tor.stackexchange.com&#x2F;questions&#x2F;711&#x2F;onion-domains-dns-lookup" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;tor.stackexchange.com&#x2F;questions&#x2F;711&#x2F;onion-domains-dn...</a><p>But what the heck is the hidden service directory?<p>This explains it better than I can at the moment: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;2019.www.torproject.org&#x2F;docs&#x2F;onion-services" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;2019.www.torproject.org&#x2F;docs&#x2F;onion-services</a><p>As I understand it, Bob&#x27;s tor software sets up entry points on the tor network, which don&#x27;t know where Bob is, but know how to get to a circuit which can get to Bob, like regular Tor. Bob signs those entry points and publishes to the DHT. Alice&#x27;s client asks the DHT for the entry points to the .onion hash. The entry points can then route Alice&#x27;s traffic to Bob.<p>I hope someone can further unpack this :)')
