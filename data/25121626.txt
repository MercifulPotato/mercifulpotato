Item(by='BossingAround', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605604842, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25121560, text='&gt;  First, I don&#x27;t get why diversity is given passing mention when there&#x27;s nothing related specific to diversity or gender politics in the solid idea discussed by the article.<p>If you can get promoted for many things in multiple ways, that means diverse promotion system, which means you&#x27;ll get more diverse seniors and more diverse leadership.<p>If your goal is to have a wide range of skilled people from various backgrounds in senior places, relaxing rules around promotions is definitely a way to get closer to that goal.<p>If you have a strict promotion criteria, you might end up promoting very similar people.<p>An extreme example: Only people from Ivy League schools can get to senior and above.<p>Consequence: you&#x27;ll end up with very heterogeneous leadership 5-10 years of such a policy (most likely even leadership that formed friendships while in university).')
