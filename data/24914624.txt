Item(by='rdw', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603854204, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24914062, text='This is a good take.  To add to it a bit, there&#x27;s another perspective that makes no-code solutions useful: team decoupling.  You know Conway&#x27;s Law about how the software is structured like the organization that produces it?  No-code is that, but it&#x27;s giving &quot;coding power&quot; to parts of the team that didn&#x27;t have them before.  E.g. a design team can accomplish a reasonable number of funnel tweaks using an A&#x2F;B testing tool, and it means they get their job done faster (= more effectively), and the engineering team can focus on other problems.  It&#x27;s not a panacea and there&#x27;s definitely risks, but I&#x27;ve seen it be helpful in this way in many contexts.')
