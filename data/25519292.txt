Item(by='cat199', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608741078, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25518122, text='they are different animals .. &#x27;right tool for the right job&#x27;<p>hardware raid has the hw complexity &#x2F; pickyness you mention, but also is safer in crashes for checksumming raid levels (e.g. 4,5,6) since the stripe checksum happens from controller-&gt;disk in hardware rather than os-&gt;controller-&gt;disk in software, so you&#x27;re less likely to lose a stripe due to OS issues (all else - like bugs - being equal)<p>also, IO wise, any mirrored raid is going to require N*mirrors of IO on the system bus, so you&#x27;re more likely to saturate it<p>that said, with faster tech (NVMe), individual drives can easily saturate a single card, so its &#x27;worth&#x27; paying the multiple IO pentalty multiplexed onto multiple individual lanes since the card is more likely to be the bottleneck than the bus<p>also, hw raid controllers even in jbod mode might be needed to get enough device fanout, though you&#x27;re not using the controller for raid in that case')
