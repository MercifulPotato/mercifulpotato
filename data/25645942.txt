Item(by='BlueTemplar', descendants=None, kids=[25651426], score=None, time=1609857561, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25645255, text='I&#x27;m not sure what you mean by &quot;physically accurate&quot; ?<p>No color reproduction is going to be physically accurate, except by accident, since color is an (average) human qualia, not a physical observable.<p>And especially because whatever the way that the colors are going to be reproduced, there&#x27;s no guarantee that they will correspond to the same light spectrum than the original, since there&#x27;s an infinity of spectra corresponding to the same impression for a specific color.<p>And if you want to do perceptually accurate averaging, you&#x27;re going to have to work in a perceptually uniform color space ! Which (even linear) sRGB isn&#x27;t.<p>All that said, it&#x27;s perfectly possible that linear sRGB is just good enough for most use cases of averaging, even despite not being perceptually uniform. Especially in OP&#x27;s example with just 2 shades : black and white.')
