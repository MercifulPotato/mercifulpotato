Item(by='Jeema101', descendants=None, kids=[25553665], score=None, time=1609089797, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25551122, text='The takeway IMO is that the reason their transition was so seamless is because they took the time to do it &#x27;the right way&#x27; - that is: set up the new system in parallel, duplicating the reads and writes, and verifying that things looked good every step of the way.<p>That gives you not only a long window to test and validate, but also the option to rollback if things suddenly don&#x27;t look so good with the new system.  It seems like people sometimes get impatient and want to do a &#x27;big switchover&#x27; with insufficient production testing and no real rollback plan, but in my experience that&#x27;s almost never a good idea.')
