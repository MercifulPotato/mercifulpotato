Item(by='eru', descendants=None, kids=[25372235, 25372343], score=None, time=1607596053, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25371958, text='Your comment is somewhat of a non sequitur.<p>First, if you look hard enough, you can see traces of the hand of government in almost anything on earth, including any market.<p>In practice, government meddling is a matter of degree and kind.  The dose makes the poison.  There are some markets that are free enough of interference that we can fruitfully analyze them as &#x27;free&#x27; markets.<p>Second, what do you mean by &#x27;try to game the market&#x27;?  In general, markets are sustainable in the long run without government interference (and usually actually more stable this way).<p>Interactions in markets often behave like the famous Prisoner&#x27;s Dilemma.  So, yes, for a single interaction it&#x27;s profitable to cheat the other guy, but repeated interactions tend very strongly towards cooperation.')
