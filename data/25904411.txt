Item(by='downandout', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611589202, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25903049, text='In 1997, I had a meeting with Netscape executives about a similar technology I had created out of concern for the privacy implications of cookies.  I called it LAD (local advertising decision).  The server would send a script down to the browser saying “if the user meets X criteria, show this ad, else show Y” and so on.  It could use browser history, installed software, and other factors for targeting.<p>At the time I was laughed out of the room.  Turns out I was just 24 years too early.')
