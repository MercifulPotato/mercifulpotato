Item(by='thought_alarm', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611360844, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25877543, text='Objective-C was adopted by NeXT Computer in the late 1980s for their app development framework.<p>The modern version of Objective-C, the one that&#x27;s still in use today, was developed by NeXT and Sun and was called OpenStep.  The first OpenStep specification was published in 1994.<p>OpenStep API implementations were created for NeXTSTEP OS, Solaris, and Windows NT, running on Motorola 68040, Intel, PA-RISC, and SPARC (and later PPC) platforms.<p>Sun would switch gears to Java, Apple would buy NeXT, and OpenStep would become Cocoa.')
