Item(by='philosopher1234', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603264309, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24845416, text='Is warren buffet himself not an example of someone who has been able to consistently generate alpha?<p>I think this question is hard to comprehend when you blow it up to this massive scale. Heres a simpler (and I claim) equivalent problem.<p>Imagine you&#x27;re in a room with a friend. You&#x27;re both tasked with estimating the price of a company. You both read through the companies 10Ks and whatever other information you can dig up. You read a bit more carefully than your friend, and detect an issue in the company&#x27;s finances. Now you have alpha, and can develop a better estimate.<p>The market is this same scenario, but there are millions of folks playing the same game. That means its harder to find that undiscovered blip of insight, but that doesn&#x27;t mean it doesn&#x27;t lie in wait for the diligent. Just as there are still original thoughts, there are still original insights, and possessing them means, for a moment, you can beat the market.')
