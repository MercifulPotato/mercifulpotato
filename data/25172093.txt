Item(by='Foxboron', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605986765, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25171785, text='I&#x27;ll start off with a hyperbole: We don&#x27;t have any of that.<p>But that isn&#x27;t really true. Arch historically has always been a DIY distribution with an equally DIY contribution structure. Our leaders has been BDFLs for close to two decades until the process was formalized and we held our first project leader election this year.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;wiki.archlinux.org&#x2F;index.php&#x2F;DeveloperWiki:Project_Leader" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;wiki.archlinux.org&#x2F;index.php&#x2F;DeveloperWiki:Project_L...</a><p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.archlinux.org&#x2F;news&#x2F;the-future-of-the-arch-linux-project-leader&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.archlinux.org&#x2F;news&#x2F;the-future-of-the-arch-linux-...</a><p>There isn&#x27;t any RFC process, but some consensus making on the mailing list and who wants to work on stuff.<p>The only other formalized structure is the Trusted Users which are elected in a formalized process.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;aur.archlinux.org&#x2F;trusted-user&#x2F;TUbylaws.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;aur.archlinux.org&#x2F;trusted-user&#x2F;TUbylaws.html</a><p>There is probably a lot of bad things with a less formalized process, but it allows Arch to move fairly rapidly and decide things without a lot of internal politics.')
