Item(by='ksaj', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609901576, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25647299, text='Apparently tungsten is what forgers use to (fraudulently) pad gold bars. Its mass is pretty close, so it takes something similar to a ground penetrating radar to detect that it is fake, unless you drill or cut it.<p>I guess a side effect is that you instinctively know what the weight of that size of a piece of gold feels like. A piece of gilded lead or iron the same size won&#x27;t weigh in enough to convince you. But a piece of gilded tungsten? Not so obvious, apparently.')
