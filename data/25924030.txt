Item(by='hprotagonist', descendants=None, kids=[25924117, 25925018, 25924706, 25924412], score=None, time=1611719525, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25922975, text='The perennial impostor syndrome problem is not so much worrying about asking questions, it&#x27;s the lurking fear that asking questions means you don&#x27;t already <i>know</i> and are therefore <i>bad</i>.<p>At least for myself, this manifestation is not &quot;oh i don&#x27;t want to be a bother&quot;, it&#x27;s &quot;if i ask questions {my peers will sneer at the idiot, my boss will take note that i&#x27;m disposable ...}&quot;.<p>This failure mode has, at many times over my academic career, been really vocally reinforced by my peers at the time.  It&#x27;s extremely damaging, really stupid groupthink that usually winds up with a silent classroom of people too cowed or proud to admit that they, the student, don&#x27;t already know the material that they&#x27;re here to learn.  And it produces engineers who have to consciously force themselves to not do that, in industry. Like me.')
