Item(by='IfOnlyYouKnew', descendants=None, kids=[25089428], score=None, time=1605313093, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25088018, text='The point of apologising isn&#x27;t to avoid consequences. It&#x27;s intended to mitigate three sorts of damages: that inflicted on whoever suffered because of you, the damage society experiences any time its rules are broken, and the damage you have done to yourself by breaking some moral standard you believe in.<p>To that end, an apology that allows s&#x2F;o to &quot;get out&quot; as in &quot;saw no further consequences&quot; isn&#x27;t necessarily what&#x27;s being claimed, because it still needs to come with (some) costs, even if they are mostly symbolic.<p>And, yes, of course there are people apologising for all sorts of behaviour that&#x27;s being criticised every day. A Google News search has about  five dozen examples from just the last day or two[1]. If apologies are never beneficial, I doubt they would be used that often.<p>Apologies are also a central factor in the most formalised system of &quot;being cancelled&quot; we have, the criminal justice system.<p>As to &quot;getting out&quot; of &quot;being cancelled&quot; by &quot;mobs&quot; I wouldn&#x27;t know since I already have trouble identifying what that&#x27;s supposed to mean.<p>[1]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.google.com&#x2F;search?q=apologized&amp;client=safari&amp;rls=en&amp;tbm=nws&amp;sxsrf=ALeKk015YH3KfbTXXzdxGIp7ude9dVVZ9Q:1605312453030&amp;ei=xR-vX9mWAYKMrwSH5LegAw&amp;start=50&amp;sa=N&amp;ved=0ahUKEwiZjZD03oDtAhUCxosKHQfyDTQQ8NMDCM0D&amp;biw=1415&amp;bih=739&amp;dpr=1" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.google.com&#x2F;search?q=apologized&amp;client=safari&amp;rls...</a>')
