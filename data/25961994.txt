[0]<p>&gt; You have the right to remain silent and refuse to answer questions.<p>&gt; Anything you say may be used against you in a court of law.\nYou have the right to consult an attorney before speaking to the police and to have an attorney present during questioning now or in the future.<p>&gt; If you cannot afford an attorney, one will be appointed for you before any questioning if you wish.<p>&gt; If you decide to answer questions now without an attorney present, you will still have the right to stop answering at any time until you talk to an attorney.<p>&gt; Knowing and understanding your rights as I have explained them to you, are you willing to answer my questions without an attorney present?
