Item(by='jokethrowaway', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607545613, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25359897, text='Open source doesn&#x27;t work and I&#x27;m glad the world is starting to understand.\nIt promotes unfair compensation (or lack thereof).<p>If you have the skills to advance technology and no capital to invest to acquire other technical solutions you may need, you can get a job and use that money to pay what<p>Expecting free work from people rarely work, there are just not enough motivated individuals willing to work and maintain something for free.<p>Most open source I rely on is either backed by a giant company which uses it as a way to attract talents (and retain talents, by letting their employee work on their favourite project on company time) or it&#x27;s unmaintained. \nIf it&#x27;s unmaintained I either need to do the work myself or hire someone, which introduces a myriad of variables.\nI&#x27;d rather hire the author but often he&#x27;s busy with his real job and doesn&#x27;t think he can live off its project.<p>If it wasn&#x27;t for the OSS movement and the sheer amount of free work we would have a network of company buying OSS solutions and, most likely, more distributed, smaller companies that produce and resell proprietary software.')
