Item(by='beerandt', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607968022, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25413475, text='&gt;The SR-71 used a different jet fuel than normal, special engines, and likely required a lot of maintenance.<p>The bigger issue is refueling, which required an entire fleet of separate, dedicated tankers, so that fuel wasn&#x27;t accidently mixed with the regular jet fuel.<p>The special fuel was some sort of coal slurry, and would immediately do pretty bad things to normal jets. Additionally, the tankers needed special, segregated tanks that didn&#x27;t to their own fuel supply.<p>There was also a radioactive additive that could be used to reduce the radar cross section of the exhaust plume, although it&#x27;s not clear if it was ever used. This supposedly would have been much more of a maintenance issue for engine wear and maintenance.')
