The literal translation is &quot;without land.&quot; This past summer, during six weeks conducting research on a global health project in Bangladesh, I heard the term often -- in reference to me.<p>&gt; It felt strange, as I was born in Bangladesh and lived there until I was 3. To me, Bangladesh is where I can easily blend into a crowd. Bengali was the first language I learned. I visit often to see family. I feel a sense of ownership and belonging in Bangladesh that I have not always felt in America.<p>Citizenship isn’t particularly meaningful in Bangladesh. You can’t become Bangladeshi by acquiring citizenship.')
