Item(by='bcrl', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609811404, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25640693, text='AT&amp;T &#x2F; Bell was broken up in the 1980s into multiple entities.  Splitting local and long distance services resulted in significant competition for long distance, while local phone service remained &#x2F; remains heavily regulated in many markets.  Here in Canada I remember having to pay $0.34 per minute to Bell in the 1980s to call a friend that was a 30 km drive away. Overseas long distance was in the dollars per minute ballpark.<p>This wasn&#x27;t without some losses.  Bell Labs lost a lot of funding once the Bell monopoly was split up.  The amount of R&amp;D that the telecom giants funded over the years resulted in massive public benefits, creating the tech industry we all know and love.')
