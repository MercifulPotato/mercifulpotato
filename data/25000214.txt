Item(by='nickik', descendants=None, kids=[25000532], score=None, time=1604598245, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24999755, text='If you have a fleet of trucks, having battery swapping batteries also means you need to have more batteries then trucks. Meaning extra capital cost.<p>Secondly, battery swapping architecture of a vehicle makes it significantly heavier specially wants you consider battery packs or even cells with structural loads to make the vehicle lighter. For one personal luxury car that might be fine, but if you are operating 150+ trucks for 20 years, that adds up to a lot of missed payloads.<p>This is exactly why Tesla, dropped this idea. JB Struble just gave a nice analysis of this at a Standford talk.<p>Also, trucks, will spend some time on break and more time loading and unloading. During that time you can charge them or top them off.')
