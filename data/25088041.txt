Item(by='ratmice', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605304893, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25087296, text='Seems like it, but typeclasses are inherently anti-modular,\nthey provide a globally coherent unique instance.\nconsider the Ord typeclass giving a single ordering for a specific type. To reverse the order you need to create a new type with a new instance of ord which reverses it.<p>Where in a module system its perfectly fine to have multiple instances of Ord for a given type.  One gives global consistency where the other gives local consistency.')
