Item(by='dragonwriter', descendants=None, kids=[24855442], score=None, time=1603347470, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24854151, text='&gt; Significant whitespace seems like a good idea and looks great in small examples but it has some very serious downsides in real use.<p>In some contexts, sure, which is one reason why YAML has two presentational styles with equal semantic power, between which a suitable tool can convert mechanically, and only one of those two styles features whitespace sensitivity.<p>&gt; Being able to reliably reformat hundreds of lines of code with a single keypress is far more valuable than a few saved braces.<p>YAML gives you the ability to freely switch between a presentational style which provides the former and one which provides the latter, with no loss of semantic power, so it doesn&#x27;t really matter which is better, since you don&#x27;t have to choose between one and the other.')
