Item(by='marcus_holmes', descendants=None, kids=[25677691], score=None, time=1610047737, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25673221, text='Again, I get that this can be tricky.<p>But if you get it wrong, what happens? You have to refactor (if you didn&#x27;t get too far in), or start again if you did. No big deal - code is perfectly amenable to this.<p>But the political and social problems of &quot;we got it wrong, we&#x27;re going to have to start again from scratch&quot; are the real problems. Dealing with a marketing manager who has a product launch scheduled for the 2nd quarter and you&#x27;re about to tell them that you need to reschedule because you got the architecture wrong on the first try - that is a <i>business</i> problem not an <i>architectural</i> problem.<p>The tech problems get a lot easier if you have some kind of framework for dealing with the business problems.')
