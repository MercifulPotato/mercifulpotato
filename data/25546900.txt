Item(by='azalemeth', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609021386, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25539679, text='The physics department I did my undergrad at had a rule -- you could do your computing practicals in whatever language you liked, but they only officially &quot;supported&quot; a few (C, fortran, etc) -- if you wanted help in others, tough.<p>Naturally, this led to some people writing answers in less common languages, like, say, 4-bit assembly for a custom CPU, metafont, and a particularly memorable Ising model in postscript (just print it, and the number of pages is the number of iterations!). It wasn&#x27;t soon after the last one that a nice little laminated notice appeared saying &quot;please do not send fractals to this printer&quot;.')
