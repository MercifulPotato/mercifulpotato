Item(by='Barrin92', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606554906, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25236190, text='the vast majority of knowledge acquired by humanity isn&#x27;t even written down, on the web or anywhere else, because knowledge can be institutional, tacit, incorporated in organisations, embodied in cultural practises, families, even biological systems and so on. If your six year old learned to swim on wikipedia, do you throw her into a pool?<p>The kind of knowledge that facilitates economic development is of that sort, it&#x27;s institutional and tacit. In <i>How Asia works</i> Joe Studwell makes an interesting observation.  When South Korea and Taiwan had already taken off economically, significant parts of the population still could not read. The expansion of education lagged, not preceded economic growth.<p>Beaming markup text around the world is not all that it&#x27;s made out to be for real world development.')
