Item(by='sandworm101', descendants=None, kids=[25066762, 25064442], score=None, time=1605138386, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25057824, text='&gt;&gt;And, because so few new nuclear power stations are built, there are very few opportunities to learn from mistakes.<p>That is a horrible turn of phrase for nuclear power.  In the real world, having very few nuclear mistakes from which to learn is a good thing.  I&#x27;m all for nuclear power, but it doesn&#x27;t help the cause for the BBC to imply that building more plants will allow for more learning from <i>more mistakes</i>.<p>A better wording: The economies of scale will allow lessons learned during construction of early units to be quickly integrated during production of subsequent units.')
