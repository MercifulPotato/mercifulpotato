Item(by='alasdair_', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602557223, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24759485, text='There is also the random negative event problem.<p>Not owning a car and taking uber everywhere seemed okay when living in a dense city, until covid happened and Uber all but ceased operations for a while.<p>The small condo in the city was a great choice when you went out most nights and worked at the office but it sucked terribly when circumstances changed and everyone was home all the time.<p>The main blockers for me are transaction costs - sure I can rent a car when I want to drive to the mountains but the amount of extra time and effort required means I’d probably not bother.<p>To be clear: it <i>can</i> work - I lived in central London and never bothered to own a car but, well, I don’t now because even something trivial like carrying around three car seats every time I wanted to take an uber would be so cumbersome I’d never do it.')
