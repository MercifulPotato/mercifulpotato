Item(by='HenryBemis', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608934754, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25535472, text='In most decent frameworks (NIST, COBIT, PCI-DSS) the changing of default passwords, removal of (unecessary) default accounts, and similar controls is a MUST. The network admin who doesn&#x27;t do that the minute they add a new device on their network should lose their job. The companies who have IT Sec, and IT Auditors who don&#x27;t check for this should also lose their jobs (or they should all get educated and keep their jobs).<p>These are basic stuff, a newbie IT should know these things.<p>I will also assume that (large) organizations test the updates, and have an action plan in place (i.e. apply fix&#x2F;patch&#x2F;update XYZ, study what it does, read the documentation, make the future-state-config, deploy that config, validate the config). I know, simple words, we &#x27;all&#x27; (in the profession) know this but when you need to patch x1000, and the boss is barking.......')
