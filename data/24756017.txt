Item(by='Scoundreller', descendants=None, kids=[24756220], score=None, time=1602519429, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24755834, text='While I could see steer-by-wire driven by lowered production costs (easier to share parts between LHD and RHD models), I could also see it indirectly driven by weight savings to meet fuel economy regulations.<p>No pinion shaft required. No pinion shaft cutout through the firewall means the body can be made with less steel.<p>Belt-driven pumps are moving to electric-drive for efficiency, but when you&#x27;re switching from hydraulic assist to motor-assist, making the whole thing &#x27;by-wire&#x27; and motor driven reduces complexity.')
