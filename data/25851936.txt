Item(by='brundolf', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611176500, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25850087, text='I think the broader idea of increasing political accountability through data tracking&#x2F;visualization&#x2F;notification could be a powerful tool for improving democracy<p>In the past I&#x27;ve thought about doing something similar at a local level, for county commissioners and the like. Local government - due to most people not paying attention to it - is known for having some of the highest corruption of any level. Imagine shining a light on it with data (maybe partly automated?), identifying conflicts of interest, sources of egregious waste, and elected officials who don&#x27;t hold up their campaign promises. Most of this data is made public already, it&#x27;s just on hard-to-find webpages and in hard-to-parse formats (and not aggregated).<p>One could do the legwork to find it, scrape it, organize it, and then publish both the raw data and various analyses in a digestible format, and that might have a real impact. Imagine signing up to get a push notification whenever your councilman votes on an issue')
