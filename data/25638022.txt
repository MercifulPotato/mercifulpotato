Item(by='Someone', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609794345, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25637462, text='Takeaway alcohol sales run a real risk of alcohol consumption  on the streets in the neighborhood of alcohol sellers. That, in turn, can easily lead to loose interpretation of social distancing rules.<p>The argument for allowing elite sports to go on typically is to give the population some distraction during lockdown (it’s a bit <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Bread_and_circuses" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Bread_and_circuses</a>)<p>As to golf: if you allow that, why wouldn’t a group of four people be allowed to kick a football around? Tennis be allowed?<p>Also, part of lockdown is the prevention of people mixing over longer distances. A local outburst of COVID-19 is bad, but if the infected move home over tens of kilometers (as, I guess, is quite normal for golf), you’re asking for a much bigger problem.<p>Having said that, it is safe to assume the government is far from an expert on what are the risky things to do. Nobody is.')
