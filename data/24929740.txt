With a Code of Conduct you&#x27;re (ideally) told up front what you can and cannot do. This reduces ambiguity and arbitrary decision making.<p>I can also tell you that a Code of Conduct isn&#x27;t a change to how I personally govern the projects and communities I&#x27;m in charge of. Instead, it&#x27;s about writing down what I would already remove people for anyway.')
