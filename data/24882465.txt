Item(by='PaulHoule', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603579156, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24882054, text='I do not like the &quot;Generations&quot; style of narrative because it is wrong in so many ways.  (Particularly I&#x27;ve seen marketers fail because it led them segment the wrong ways -- you want customers who love your brand and to say it is for people under 32 and not over 33 is just dumb.)<p>Some trends (such as &quot;environmental awareness&quot;) wash across society over time:  in this case we have an increasing number of &quot;fish&quot; on &quot;fishbowl Earth&quot; who are affected by the waste products of the other &quot;fish&quot;.<p>That kind of trend has this pattern<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Diffusion_of_innovations" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Diffusion_of_innovations</a><p>and the way it turns out is that 10% of the boomers did something,  and 20% of Gen X did it,  40% of Y,  80% of Z. If you look at the heroes of the boomers,  such as<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Jim_Morrison" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Jim_Morrison</a>\n<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Abbie_Hoffman" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Abbie_Hoffman</a>\n<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Gail_Sheehy" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Gail_Sheehy</a><p>they were &quot;early adopters&quot; who were born before the baby boom.<p>My mother-in-law has leased a Toyota Corolla or similar vehicle and has no attachment to large vehicles,  fossil fuels,  etc.  She&#x27;s on a &quot;fixed income&quot; and is motivated by (i) total cost of ownership and (ii) trouble-free motoring.  She would switch to electric if it won on these grounds.  Getting her to switch brands from Toyota might be tough.<p>My son is an auto enthusiast (wands an Oldsmobile 98),  but many people his age will want an efficient reliable car like my mother in law.')
