Item(by='analog31', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608342110, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25474032, text='A couple of things. Once you leave, you&#x27;re not going back. So it&#x27;s a profound decision with a big unknown on the other side. Your publication output drops to zero. You won&#x27;t have time to develop your own program. You won&#x27;t keep up with the literature. (Maybe you&#x27;re not doing those things already, in which case you <i>should</i> try to jump ship).<p>Maybe you&#x27;re an academic spouse, in which case you&#x27;re probably living in an area that&#x27;s already crowded with like minded PhDs.<p>Maybe it&#x27;s not really all that much better on the outside. Depending on your field of course. Even as a grad student, I noticed that acceptance into grad school doesn&#x27;t necessarily select for the traits that are needed in a research or R&amp;D leadership role, such as being outgoing, self confident, and competitive.<p>Disclosure: Physics PhD, graduated early 90&#x27;s, went straight to industry, never looked back.')
