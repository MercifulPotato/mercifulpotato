Item(by='pacbard', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610550401, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25761353, text='To build upon this answer a little bit, people want to go back in person not for the educational reasons but for social reasons. While you can learn in an online format and (with enough work&#x2F;investment) make the experience similar to in-person classes, you cannot replace the social aspects of college with an online environment.<p>US colleges have been moving from a learning experience towards a social experience build on to of learning (see, for example, <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.nber.org&#x2F;papers&#x2F;w18745" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.nber.org&#x2F;papers&#x2F;w18745</a>).<p>My university was in person last fall but classes mostly online, students still socialized (e.g., partied) even if health recommendations were strongly recommend against it.')
