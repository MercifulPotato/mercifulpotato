Item(by='NovemberWhiskey', descendants=None, kids=[25984897], score=None, time=1612122003, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25981026, text='The whole premise of client asset segregation is that failure of the broker should not put the assets of a client in jeopardy. This isn&#x27;t a technical detail of &quot;the regulations&quot;, it&#x27;s the entire foundational premise.<p>The trade is not &quot;done&quot; until it settles. As discussed throughout, that&#x27;s T+2. Prior to settlement, the trade can fail (e.g. your counterparty turns out not to have the money&#x2F;stock to fund their side of the trade).<p>If you really want to read the detail, it&#x27;s called Rule 15c3-3.')
