But why would you choose to go into theater or journalism—rather than law, medicine or finance—if you are a talented child of ambitious but not well off immigrants?<p>A really weird point. There&#x27;s plenty of concern about under-representation of black people in law, and medicine, and finance.<p>In law there is a lot of effort spent getting more black lawyers and judges into the system, and we&#x27;ve recently seen the experience of Alexandra Wilson who was mistaken for a defendant at least 3 times in one day in her place of work. <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.lawgazette.co.uk&#x2F;news&#x2F;investigation-launched-after-black-barrister-mistaken-for-defendant&#x2F;5105752.article" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.lawgazette.co.uk&#x2F;news&#x2F;investigation-launched-aft...</a><p>In healthcare we have NHSWRES (NHS Workforce Race Equality Standard) who are trying to increase representation in NHS exec and non-exec teams.<p>&gt; In a country in which black people make up only three percent of the population, for example, six percent of junior doctors are black.<p>They got this information from here: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.ethnicity-facts-figures.service.gov.uk&#x2F;workforce-and-business&#x2F;workforce-diversity&#x2F;nhs-workforce&#x2F;latest#by-ethnicity" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.ethnicity-facts-figures.service.gov.uk&#x2F;workforce...</a><p>If we&#x27;re looking at racism we don&#x27;t want to compare junior doctors with journalists. We want to compare junior doctors to consultants and senior leaders. We also want to compare the different levels of junior doctors to each other to see if the ratio of black:white doctors changes.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.ethnicity-facts-figures.service.gov.uk&#x2F;workforce-and-business&#x2F;workforce-diversity&#x2F;nhs-workforce&#x2F;latest" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.ethnicity-facts-figures.service.gov.uk&#x2F;workforce...</a><p>&gt; a higher percentage of junior doctors were from the Black, Chinese, Mixed and Other groups than senior doctors<p>We can see that 6.2% of junior doctors are black, but only 3.5% of consultants are black. Where did all those juniors go?')
