Item(by='derefr', descendants=None, kids=[25934607, 25936448], score=None, time=1611782036, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25932362, text='It&#x27;s not about &quot;backing a commercial website&quot;; it&#x27;s about selling a solution for your own customers to use, where WordPress might be a component of that offered solution, customized into being something else (e.g. a control panel.)<p>There are concerns that doing so, in the context of a monorepo, might infect your entire codebase — including the components that have nothing to do with the control-panel, and so never touch the Wordpress stuff — with GPL.<p>Of course, you can architect code to get around that, e.g. building your shared architecture that the control-panel uses as a library, and then pulling that lib in from your isolated Wordpress control-panel codebase. But enterprises don&#x27;t want to be forced into (possibly sub-optimal) architectures just because one component legally requires it. They&#x27;d rather just not use that component, so that their architecture can be totally determined by what&#x27;s best for their development.')
