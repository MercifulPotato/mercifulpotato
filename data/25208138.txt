Item(by='AntiImperialist', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606303725, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25207991, text='Regardless of what certain individuals want, ignoring inevitable outcomes don&#x27;t fare well for them.<p>For example, like Yuval Noah Harari mentions in Sapiens, agriculture was not necessarily good for individuals. But since it allowed long term storage of food (in the form of grains), it allowed for stable habitats which grew into cities and nation states. There were still tribal people for a long time (there still are) who didn&#x27;t adopt the technology but they were increasingly at the mercy of those on the other side.<p>We&#x27;ve seen the same with other technologies, like powerful weapons in the last centuries.<p>I think, in the new information age, we&#x27;re headed to a similar trajectory with this new set of propaganda methods and defenses. Those who don&#x27;t incorporate these new technologies into their systems will be at the mercy of those who do.')
