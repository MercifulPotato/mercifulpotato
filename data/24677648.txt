Item(by='pydry', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1601805053, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24677392, text='&gt;basically YAML, &quot;but better&quot;<p>That was my intention behind this, too:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;crdoconnor&#x2F;strictyaml&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;crdoconnor&#x2F;strictyaml&#x2F;</a><p>The general structure of YAML is fine I think but its feature set grew a little bit out of control.<p>The &quot;cleanliness&quot; of the format leads to one of its inherent weaknesses - syntax can&#x27;t be used to encode type information so you either need a schema to encode type information (strictyaml approach) or have magic conversions (yaml approach) or to assume strings (strictyaml w&#x2F;o schema&#x2F;nestedtext).<p>The interesting thing I discovered about schemas building this is that it kind of pays to make them extensible and build them in a turing complete language. Schema validation done using a non-turing complete language (e.g. jsonschema) allows for cross language usage but it ends up being a kind of blunt object.')
