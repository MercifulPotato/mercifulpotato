Item(by='Der_Einzige', descendants=None, kids=[25144707, 25144387, 25144391], score=None, time=1605743176, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25144229, text='Its impossible to not be fallacious in any meaningful discourse that goes on long enough, and that&#x27;s okay.<p>Every single invocation of an expert is an argument from authority.<p>Every single invocation of logic like &quot;it is more like nature therefor it is more good&quot; is a naturalistic fallacy.<p>Do we even need to get to other ones like &quot;appeal to emotion&quot;?<p>Maybe we should instead recognize that &quot;logic&quot; does not necessarily describe reality (given that there are an infinite number of possible logics based on which axioms you start with), and that being worried about and trying to avoid &quot;logical fallacies&quot; in our reasoning does not help us see reality more closely to how it is.')
