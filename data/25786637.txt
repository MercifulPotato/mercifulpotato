Item(by='Karunamon', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610681172, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25786036, text='They can&#x27;t even enforce their own existing rules against things that are absolutely banned already - look at Facebook&#x27;s controversies.<p>Put plainly, I do not believe it is possible for a social network to moderate hard or fast enough at Facebook&#x2F;Twitter scale to reject section 230 immunity. Even if they took the step of pre-moderating all content before it appears on the site, there is simply too much content coming in for that to be a realistic option (and not lose a ton of users due to the delay putting people off).<p>&gt;<i>It’s also not clear to me why we’d expect a private company to have to answer to you or me or anybody else about decisions they make.</i><p>They answer to society at the end of the day, which can express its desires via the legal system.')
