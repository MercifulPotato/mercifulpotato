Item(by='lacker', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604686059, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25008587, text='I think the term &quot;technical debt&quot; is a really good analogy. Regular financial debt isn&#x27;t something to be avoided at all costs. Often, it&#x27;s the right move to take on some debt in order to quickly achieve an important goal. Taking out a loan for a house or a college education can be a good investment that pays off. But at the same time, if you completely ignore your financial debt, you&#x27;re going to find yourself overwhelmed by your debt payments.<p>The key is to be aware of your technical debt, and be aware that you&#x27;re making a tradeoff, and that the right answer often falls between the two extremes.')
