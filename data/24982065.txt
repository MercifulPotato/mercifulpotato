Item(by='Karawebnetwork', descendants=None, kids=[24982190], score=None, time=1604426125, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24981516, text='Having wore that title before, it means &quot;developer who is able to build a JavaScript application and bring it to production&quot;.<p>For me, this included doing UX&#x2F;UI, the design, implementing web front-end (HTML, CSS, making sure it&#x27;s responsive and optimized), the front-end business logic (Angular, React, Vue), setting up the server (typically the &quot;back-end&quot;, either node.js or a more traditional approach like PHP), persisting to a database (like using MongoDB), managing APIs, etc.<p>I am not a fan of the title &quot;full-stack&quot; (to me, that&#x27;s just what being a developer is) but you need to wear it in order to get the right job offers and salary.<p>For the longest time, front-end meant &quot;HTML+CSS and a pinch of jQuery&quot;. So we need a title to break away from that trend.')
