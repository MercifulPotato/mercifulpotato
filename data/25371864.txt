Item(by='yrro', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607594069, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25358642, text='Last time I checked, nothing was actually confined by AppArmor out of the box (IIRC I was looking at the output of ps -eZ and found that AppArmor wasn&#x27;t actaully protecting anything...)<p>Specifically in RHEL&#x2F;CentOS&#x2F;Fedora I like that everything in the base system is reasonably well confined out of the box - including random container images that users insist on downloading&#x2F;running. I don&#x27;t know if AppArmor is even capable of doing this:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;access.redhat.com&#x2F;documentation&#x2F;en-us&#x2F;red_hat_enterprise_linux&#x2F;7&#x2F;html&#x2F;selinux_users_and_administrators_guide&#x2F;chap-security-enhanced_linux-svirt" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;access.redhat.com&#x2F;documentation&#x2F;en-us&#x2F;red_hat_enterp...</a><p>i.e., if I &#x27;docker run --rm -it debian:unstable sleep 300&#x27; twice, then:<p>$ pgrep sleep | xargs ps -Z\nLABEL                               PID TTY      STAT   TIME COMMAND\nsystem_u:system_r:svirt_lxc_net_t:s0:c563,c603 1595289 pts&#x2F;0 S+   0:00 sleep 123\nsystem_u:system_r:svirt_lxc_net_t:s0:c25,c163 1595325 pts&#x2F;0 S+   0:00 sleep 345<p>Both containers are confined by the svirt_lxc_net_t domain, but since they have different labels, they aren&#x27;t able to interfere with each other, or the host system, even if the process inside the container is running as uid 0.')
