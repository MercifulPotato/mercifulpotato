Item(by='samatman', descendants=None, kids=[25422467], score=None, time=1607975523, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25419740, text='Excellent article.<p>It seems to me (and this is somewhat off-the-cuff) that compilers have another option: integers <i>which are cast from pointers</i> have provenance.<p>That is, provenance is a taint: you can&#x27;t clean it off through casting. So casting from pointer means the user can do integer-things like addition, but it means the compiler can&#x27;t do integer-things like constant folding.')
