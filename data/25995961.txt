Item(by='PragmaticPulp', descendants=None, kids=[25996059, 25996549], score=None, time=1612225118, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25995823, text='As always, the only real answer is to consult with your doctor. Taking breaks from medication isn&#x27;t a terrible idea, especially if you hate it. The real problem is the people who associate taking the pill with getting work done, to the point that they forget how to accomplish anything without first reaching for the medication. The key is to break that connection before it becomes a craving for medication.<p>&gt; I don&#x27;t like being on stimulants though.<p>It&#x27;s very possible that you&#x27;re on too high of a dose, or that you&#x27;d do better on an alternative medication. There are at least 4 different classes of ADHD medication, 2 of which aren&#x27;t traditional stimulants. Guanfacine is remarkably effective, for example, but it&#x27;s nowhere near as popular because it&#x27;s much less enjoyable than stimulants during the ramp-up phase.<p>&gt; The only time I feel the need to abuse them<p>Yikes. If you ever find yourself feeling the &quot;need to abuse&quot; a medication, I strongly recommend you keep an eye on those tendencies. That&#x27;s the kind of warning sign than can spiral into addiction if you ever find yourself burdened by some unfortunate life circumstances (the trigger).')
