Item(by='gvb', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603803044, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24906687, text='The performance of soft cores are significantly lower than hard cores.<p>Xilinx already has a RISC soft core in their MicroBlaze architecture so they don&#x27;t have a pressing need for a low power, reasonable performance RISC soft core. Ref: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;MicroBlaze" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;MicroBlaze</a><p>AMD has high performance CPUs being fabbed by TSMC (same foundry as Xilinx), so (theoretically) AMD CPUs can be grafted onto the Xilinx FPGA as a hard core.<p>With AMD and the MicroBlaze, they have the high performance and low power processor spectrum covered with no need for 3rd party licensing costs.')
