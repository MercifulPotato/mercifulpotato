Item(by='coldtea', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611787202, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25934747, text='&gt;<i>Shifts in the Overton window do not come about simply with the suggestion of radical policy proposals</i><p>If those proposes are frequent enough, and adopted widely, they do.<p>&gt;<i>Change only comes in the face of a tidal wave of evidence and an energized advocacy strategy.</i><p>People could not care less about &quot;evidence&quot; (unless it supports their side), and an &quot;energized advocacy strategy&quot; is another way to describe what the author tried to deny, the &quot;overton window shifting&quot; by advocacy.<p>The only myth here, is that it doesn&#x27;t have to happen by radical advocacy (to get people to &quot;meet in the middle&quot;). It can just as well happen by very slowly gradually moving towards one or the other direction regarding some topic. So, you could just add tons of +0.1 proposals and get to move to +10 eventually, you don&#x27;t need to propose +50 in the hopes you&#x27;ll get to +10.')
