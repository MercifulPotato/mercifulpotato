Item(by='speeder', descendants=None, kids=[24980259, 24980463, 24987023, 24980788], score=None, time=1604414910, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24979079, text='Another thing that bugs me: many countries are taking &quot;tech improvements&quot; in inflation.<p>For example: suppose a TV costs 2x now than what it did 10 years ago, but it is also twice the size, for many countries this means 0% of inflation.<p>Problem is, you can&#x27;t buy the old stuff.<p>For example, are cars now much faster, safer, etc... than cars of 1950?<p>Yes.<p>But, if you are buying your first car, you can&#x27;t buy one from 1950, you either buy a car, or you don&#x27;t, you either spend the 2020 price for the car, or you don&#x27;t, thus this &quot;correction&quot; for technology development is bogus.<p>Or a personal anecdote:<p>My phone broke.<p>A phone now is ludicrously more expensive than it was years ago, but doesn&#x27;t count in inflation because it also became a computer, that can do a ton of things.<p>Problem is, I don&#x27;t need a computer, I need a phone, I want to do phone calls! But nobody sells those here, they only sell computers that happen to make calls, for the price of a computer.')
