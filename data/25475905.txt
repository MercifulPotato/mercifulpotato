Item(by='tsimionescu', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608364650, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25472642, text='The article on superdense coding that started this mini thread [0] talks about a protocol for sending 2 bits of information as 1 qubit, and recovering the 2 bits on the receiver side.<p>More specifically, it seems that actually there are 2 shared qubits - one half of an entangled pair ahead of time; and one additional qubit when the message is decided. While 0 information can be transmitted using the entangled pair, entangled qubit + 1 qubit leads to 2 classical bits of information.<p>[0] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Superdense_coding" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Superdense_coding</a>')
