If it&#x27;s a monstrosity (for example, this: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;onezero.medium.com&#x2F;how-to-destroy-surveillance-capitalism-8135e6744d59" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;onezero.medium.com&#x2F;how-to-destroy-surveillance-capit...</a>), it doesn&#x27;t get parsed correctly.<p>As for Twitter, you can bookmark a link to the tweet, but it won&#x27;t parse a Twitter thread for example. Same applies for thread compilers (like <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;threadreaderapp.com&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;threadreaderapp.com&#x2F;</a>).<p>Probably the biggest annoyance with Pocket is that you can highlight stuff, but you can&#x27;t export your highlights. It has an API, but it doesn&#x27;t support highlights. Contacting them about that resulted in something like &quot;we&#x27;re a small team and have no API expansion updates we&#x27;re willing to share right now&quot; (paraphrasing). They also protect their sign-in form with Google&#x27;s Captcha, making it non-trivial to scrape that info. The best option I found (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;karlicoss&#x2F;pockexport" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;karlicoss&#x2F;pockexport</a>) requires fiddling with browser&#x27;s dev tools to get the consumer_key, but I couldn&#x27;t be bothered to get that to work.<p>Memex (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;getmemex.com&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;getmemex.com&#x2F;</a>) just gives you a browser extension that allows you to highlight nearly every text on desktop, but becomes completely useless on phones (they&#x27;re too locked down for something like that to work).')
