Item(by='ogogmad', descendants=None, kids=[25472231, 25472217], score=None, time=1608326408, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25472169, text='I thought Grassmann algebra consisted of only scalars, vectors, bivectors, etc. but <i>not</i> their linear combinations. So a mixed scalar plus vector is not an element of a Grassmann algebra. An alternative name is <i>exterior algebra</i>. You get Clifford algebra &#x2F; Geometric Algebra (two names for the same thing) by taking closure under sums and considering a more general product.<p>[edit: It appears I&#x27;m right: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Exterior_algebra" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Exterior_algebra</a>]')
