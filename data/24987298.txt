Item(by='stevekemp', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604478595, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24981771, text='I did create a a couple of simple actions which abstract this a little, but I agree it is best when everything is in the repository and self-contained.<p>So I have a &quot;run tests&quot; action which just executes .github&#x2F;run-tests, and it is up to the project-owner to write &quot;make test&quot; in that script, or whatever else they prefer.<p>It keeps things well-defined, and portable, but avoids the need to have project-specific workflows.  I like being able to keep the same .yaml files in all my projects.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;skx&#x2F;github-action-tester" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;skx&#x2F;github-action-tester</a><p>Similar thing for building and uploading artifacts, run &quot;.github&#x2F;build&quot; and specify the glob-pattern for your artifacts:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;skx&#x2F;github-action-build" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;skx&#x2F;github-action-build</a>')
