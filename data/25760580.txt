Item(by='howerj', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610535978, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25760063, text='It should also be noted that double in this context has nothing to do with\nfloating point numbers, Forth implementations often do not\nhave the functions (called &quot;words&quot;) to manipulate FP numbers. Instead I\nbelieve it refers to the double-number word set, words like &quot;d+&quot;, &quot;d-&quot;. See\n&lt;<a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;lars.nocrew.org&#x2F;forth2012&#x2F;double.html" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;lars.nocrew.org&#x2F;forth2012&#x2F;double.html</a>&gt;.<p>Forth often uses different terminology, or slightly odd terminology, for\nmore common computer science terms because of how the language evolved\nindependently from universities and research labs. For example &quot;meta-compiler&quot;\nis used when &quot;cross-compiler&quot; would be more appropriate, instead of functions\nForth has &quot;words&quot; which are combined into a &quot;dictionary&quot;, because the\nterm &quot;word&quot; is already used &quot;cell&quot; is used instead of &quot;word&quot; when referring\nto a machines word-width.<p>Edit: Grammar.')
