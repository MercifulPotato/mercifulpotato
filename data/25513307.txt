Item(by='simonblack', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608685425, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25511241, text='I used UNIX all through the 1990s, first with AT&amp;T UNIX, then Novell&#x27;s then Solaris (both SPARC and x86).<p>From time to time I&#x27;d look at Linux, and was always astonished at how fast it was developing, though it was always a &#x27;second OS&#x27; that I&#x27;d put on laptops or whatever.<p>Around 1997-2000, it was not much different from the Solaris I was using, but I remained with Solaris as my main OS.<p>In 2001, I had a problem with Solaris and my new x86 desktop machine, so I put Linux on it. I haven&#x27;t used any other OS but Linux as my everyday OS since then.<p>I have wandered around the distros, however. Mandrake, Slackware, Debian, Mint were the main ones. If I had my druthers it would be Slackware combined with the Debian&#x2F;Mint apt software package system.')
