That&#x27;s a symbol conflict, dating back to TOPS-10 that used forward slash for option separators[1]. \\ was picked instead as it was shift-free as well, and directly opposite &#x2F; on IBM Model F keyboards[2].<p>2. All those platforms have supported mutually compatible formats for as long as that has been relevant (that is, after some standardization on network hardware). That local variants stems from different OS&#x27;s having different features that need support. E.g., Windows&#x27; complex ACLs vs. POSIX permissions (support for ACLs on UNIX-like platforms came later).<p>3. Office for Mac was a product developed by an entirely different team, having no relation to the regular office products. It&#x27;s natural it was not identical. However, the solution here is not to force Microsoft to make 1:1 products for all platforms. It&#x27;s to let alternatives grow: No one needs nor wants Microsoft Remote Desktop for Linux. We have FreeRDP, Remmina, and others. Don&#x27;t monopolize computing.<p>4.
