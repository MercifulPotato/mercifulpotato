The most used approach are lots of macros in C++ to completely modify the language. One popular alternative is <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cpeditor.org&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cpeditor.org&#x2F;</a>.<p>For example, look at how Benjamin Qi does it:\n<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;codeforces.com&#x2F;contest&#x2F;1446&#x2F;submission&#x2F;98447974" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;codeforces.com&#x2F;contest&#x2F;1446&#x2F;submission&#x2F;98447974</a><p>The actual code is at the bottom. Here is a sample of a submission to a problem harder than your usual AoC second part:<p><pre><code>  int main() {\n   setIO(); re(N,M);\n   str A,B; re(A,B);\n   F0R(i,N+2) F0R(j,M+2) mx[i][j] = -MOD;\n   int ans = 0;\n   R0F(i,N) R0F(j,M) {\n    ckmax(mx[i][j],mx[i+1][j]);\n    ckmax(mx[i][j],mx[i][j+1]);\n    if (A[i] == B[j]) {\n     dp[i][j] = 2;\n     ckmax(dp[i][j],mx[i+1][j+1]+4+i+j);\n     ckmax(mx[i][j],dp[i][j]-i-j);\n     ckmax(ans,dp[i][j]);\n    }\n   }\n   ps(ans);\n  }</code></pre>')
