Item(by='gamblor956', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603393962, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24861473, text='It used to be a no-brainer to go overseas.<p>But the Double Irish (and similar structuring) is no longer permitted and would be treated as an illegal tax scheme today. (It&#x27;s grandfathered in for companies already using the structure.)<p>Generally, between BEAT, GILTI, global transfer pricing policies, and the rise of nationalist economic policies, it&#x27;s now riskier and more expensive to switch to a global corporate structure than to just keep everything in the US.<p>I used to do a lot of outbound structuring work, and now (post TCJA) almost everything is inbound, compliance, or restructuring existing foreign operations to more tax-friendly foreign jurisdictions (i.e., moving out of China to Vietname, etc.)')
