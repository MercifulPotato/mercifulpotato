Sub-retinal injections are actually done through the inside of the eye.<p>Your eyeball is filled with vitreous humour which although technically a fluid is really like a thick layer of gelatine, surgical instruments can&#x27;t navigate that.<p>Before a needle is able to reach that retinal area, the surgeon must perform what is known as a Vitrectomy which is basically a chop and suction of that gelatine in order to have a good visual and be able to accurately manipulate surgical instruments.<p>Source: worked in embedded software for ophthalmic surgery equipment.')
