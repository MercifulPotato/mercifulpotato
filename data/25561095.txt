Item(by='refoundable', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609176701, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25560251, text='I agree that discovery is far from a straight-forward process, and that many of the cavalier ways in which it happened in decades past would be criminally prosecuted and beyond the pale today.<p>But I don&#x27;t think that there was ever much low-hanging fruit to begin with. Discoveries always look &quot;obvious&quot; ex post and almost never ex ante.<p>I find it best to think of &quot;low-hanging fruit&quot; as a euphemism that polite people have agreed to use in order to excuse the massive failure that has taken place and stop curious people like Scannell from asking too many questions.')
