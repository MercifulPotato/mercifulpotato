Item(by='oasisbob', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609218595, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25566876, text='Another school of thought is that plants aren&#x27;t generally damaged by casual overwatering, but that many houseplants aren&#x27;t thriving because of poor-draining soil mixtures or being left with wet feet.<p>If you fix the poor soil, you don&#x27;t need to worry as much about overwatering because they aren&#x27;t suffocating every time they&#x27;re watered, creating a perched water table in the container.<p>Peat-heavy mixes also have a nasty habit of going hydrophobic when they dry completely - it can be a difficult problem to fix.<p>A bonzai grower called Tapla wrote a fantastic series of posts on the Gardenweb forums about the importance of drainage in container soils. I&#x27;d encourage anyone interested in this stuff to check them out - they&#x27;ve been instrumental in keeping my indoor trees alive.')
