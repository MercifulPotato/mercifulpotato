Item(by='colechristensen', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608711682, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25511084, text='There can be a sizable gap between complying with regulations to ensure <i>X</i> and trying to do <i>X</i>.<p>lock picking lawyer had a great video on that topic recently <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;youtu.be&#x2F;DykUXXcJGIw" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;youtu.be&#x2F;DykUXXcJGIw</a><p>Writing regulations is hard to achieve the intended effect. Complying with regulators frequently involves a mindset of checking boxes instead of trying to accomplish something.<p>If, however, an investment bank essentially buys a large chunk of your company, if only temporarily, they have a real motivation to figure out if it is legitimate, and there are real consequences if you screw up, not just legislated consequences but real financial bag holding ones.<p>You have to think of the everything-is-a-game mindset to help you evaluate situations.  The game of being a regulator is different than the game of being an investor.  There is value in both.')
