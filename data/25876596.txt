Rather, it&#x27;s an argument against the common misconception that kids have a natural technical proficiency with computers because they grew up with them.<p>The author makes his point pretty clear here:<p>&gt; Not really knowing how to use a computer is deemed acceptable if you&#x27;re twenty-five or over. It&#x27;s something that some people are even perversely proud of, but the prevailing wisdom is that all under eighteens are technical wizards, and this is simply not true.<p>He then offers some suggestions for how to help kids become more technically proficient with computers because he thinks it&#x27;s a useful skill to have.')
