Item(by='rayiner', descendants=None, kids=[25182237], score=None, time=1606086053, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25181863, text='My understanding is that Alicia Garza and the other founders of the BLM organization coined the phrase itself: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.history.com&#x2F;this-day-in-history&#x2F;blacklivesmatter-hashtag-first-appears-facebook-sparking-a-movement" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.history.com&#x2F;this-day-in-history&#x2F;blacklivesmatter...</a><p>&gt; Outraged and saddened after the acquittal of George Zimmerman, the Florida man who killed a Black teenager in 2012, Oakland, California resident Alicia Garza posts a message on Facebook on July 13, 2013. Her post contains the phrase &quot;Black lives matter,&quot; which soon becomes a rallying cry and a movement throughout the United States and around the world.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;library.law.howard.edu&#x2F;civilrightshistory&#x2F;BLM" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;library.law.howard.edu&#x2F;civilrightshistory&#x2F;BLM</a><p>&gt; In 2013, three female Black organizers — Alicia Garza, Patrisse Cullors, and Opal Tometi — created a Black-centered political will and movement building project called Black Lives Matter.  Black Lives Matter began with a social media hashtag, #BlackLivesMatter, after the acquittal of George Zimmerman in the shooting death of Trayvon Martin back in 2012.<p>&gt; According to the Black Lives Matter website they were &quot;founded in 2013 in response to the acquittal of Trayvon Martin’s murderer.  Black Lives Matter Foundation, Inc is a global organization in the US, UK, and Canada, whose mission is to eradicate white supremacy and build local power to intervene in violence inflicted on Black communities by the state and vigilantes.')
