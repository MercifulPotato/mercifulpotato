Item(by='incrudible', descendants=None, kids=[25352226, 25352562, 25349241], score=None, time=1607450758, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25344640, text='Personality traits that are not advantageous in <i>some</i> way are unlikely to be maintained over many generations of evolution.<p>Personality traits that are <i>universally</i> advantageous would not be discernible as traits, because they would become universal to everyone.<p>Therefore, all personality traits must also be disadvantageous in some way.<p>So-called &quot;dark trait&quot; behaviors obviously confer advantages at least in the <i>short term</i>. In the long run, we&#x27;re all dead, but early success compounds, like interest.<p>Some of the luckiest psychopaths will end up at the very top, while some of the unluckiest will end up six feet under.<p>This is difficult to accurately capture in statistics, but the expected gain from &quot;dark triad&quot; traits across the whole population is probably zero.')
