Then you have all the apps and content (iOS and macOS) you already paid for.<p>The difference in business model between G, Apple (or other tech behemoths) are very superficial. Yes, to google and apple operations they are very different, but to a consumer: it&#x27;s the same. Both try to become essential, all encompassing locking you in and increasing the cost of switching. All brands try to do this. But it&#x27;s much easier for me to pick a new brand toothpaste after I&#x27;m done with previous one than for me to move app ecosystems, specially when those apps ecosystems are all locked down as all non-libre app ecosystems are.<p>You may &quot;feel&quot; like you&#x27;re an Apple product because you think you can just buy less Apple products or not at all. That&#x27;s until you consider the consequences of losing access to third-party content&#x2F;products you purchase.')
