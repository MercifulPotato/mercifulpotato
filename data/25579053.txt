Item(by='yellowapple', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609308435, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25565148, text='My Pixelbook seems to hit most of those points (though unfortunately not quite all):<p>- Ships with CoreBoot<p>- Much nicer fit&#x2F;finish than the average non-Apple laptop (all metal, aside from the rubber palm rest)<p>- Display wasn&#x27;t defective :)<p>- There&#x27;s a logo, alas<p>- Comparing to my work Macbook Pro, I&#x27;d say viewing angle and brightness are both on par, and the screen is indeed glossy<p>- I don&#x27;t know if the trackpad is actually glass, but it does feel nicer nicer than pretty much any other trackpad I&#x27;ve used (to the point where I don&#x27;t miss having a nub like on my usual Latitudes&#x2F;Precisions and ThinkPads)<p>- No crazy vertical integration hardware-wise, but it being one of the main targets for Fuchsia is kinda cool, I guess<p>- It has both touch and screen-flipping, but it&#x27;s easy enough to just... not use them (and indeed, most of the time I don&#x27;t)<p>- I can&#x27;t speak to the support aspect (since I&#x27;ve never had to put in a support request, and typically prefer fixing things myself anyway), but Google unfortunately has a, um, reputation...<p>I&#x27;ve been pretty happy with it; it&#x27;s a nice solid machine, and while I ain&#x27;t a fan of Chrome OS at all, the Crostini support makes it at least usable for my purposes (and the first-class Android app support is a nice bonus).<p>The main downside for me is that Chrome OS seems to be the only option for it that doesn&#x27;t have some major drawbacks like missing audio support or suspend&#x2F;resume issues.')
