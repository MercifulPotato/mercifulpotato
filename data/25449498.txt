Item(by='_hyn3', descendants=None, kids=[25450327, 25449625], score=None, time=1608156809, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25447805, text='<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;zerotier.com" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;zerotier.com</a> does a very nice job of NAT &quot;bouncing&quot;, free (hosted) for up to 50 hosts, or you can run your own.<p>You end up with another &quot;zt&quot; interface, so you can apply firewall rules to that interface, separate from your main NIC(s), kinda like assigning your own VPC but for your laptop, etc. It  only takes a few seconds to install the agent and join your private network, and it seems to reconnect quite nicely as well; if your network is private, you can force all new attempts to connect to be approved (where you can check the mac address first).
