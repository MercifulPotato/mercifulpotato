Item(by='dehrmann', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607622201, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25376006, text='I remember interviewing someone around 50 who interviewed well, we hired, and did good work. I think part of why he interviewed well was he knew we&#x27;d ask whiteboard coding questions, so he studied for them.<p>Some amount of what you hear about age discrimination is older candidates are less familiar with CS fundamentals because you don&#x27;t use them all that often on the job, whereas fresh-out-of-school candidates still remember them.<p>This isn&#x27;t to say the typical software engineering interview is effective or ineffective, just that preparing for what you know you&#x27;ll be tested on helps a lot.')
