Item(by='dragonwriter', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602957214, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24808361, text='&gt; So if the government is stepping in here and moving the liability from the platform to the content creator as a favor to the company, shouldn&#x27;t the company be required to not be allowed to selectively allow posts (assuming it is legal content and related).<p>That&#x27;s what the law was before 230, except that it essentially applied to <i>illegal</i> content, as well, because of the company tried to actively moderate even for just illegal content then it became liable for all content (it had to be 100% right if it tried to moderate illegal content.)<p>Being liable for only known illegal content is distributor, not publisher, liability, which is what it superficially looks like 230 does on its face (courts have applied 230 to provide no liability, not even distributor liability; expressly imposing distributor-style liability would be a modest reform.)')
