Item(by='austincheney', descendants=None, kids=[25662584], score=None, time=1609959732, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25659044, text='This isn&#x27;t a limitation of PeerJS or event WebRTC.  This is a limitation imposed by IPv4 implementations that include NAT and PAT.  These services in the middle are there to create tunnels as a mutually available broker for all concerned end points.  Even after the connection is established additional third party services are still required for authentication, identity management (trust&#x2F;certificates&#x2F;keys), and possibly codec resolution.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;developer.mozilla.org&#x2F;en-US&#x2F;docs&#x2F;Web&#x2F;API&#x2F;WebRTC_API#protocols" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;developer.mozilla.org&#x2F;en-US&#x2F;docs&#x2F;Web&#x2F;API&#x2F;WebRTC_API#...</a><p>If all parties were IPv6 first they would still need the secondary services mentioned above, but they could likely broker a direct peer-to-peer connection without a separate service provider.')
