It is not immediately clear that Khan Academy and YouTube pass the muster where I live. Even if they did, for me there is a larger non-starter.<p>I believe schooling is not best achieved by placing kids at a computer all day and that there is a clear expectation that homeschooled children be schooled and supervised at home by an adult. Secondly, I think my kids get too much screen time as it is. Finally, with the end in sight for ACT&#x2F;SAT style standardized testing, I am not sure how universities would view or evaluate a &quot;do it yourself via YouTube&quot; high school education.<p>Even if the content is great, I&#x27;m not going to risk these unknowns. They might however make great supplements.')
