Item(by='gregw2', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609871173, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25648186, text='The author casts this in moral terms which is useful in grabbing attention (moral competence vs moral incompetence!), but I&#x27;m not sure how those two labels and concepts as he describes them are any different than the neither-moral-nor-immoral standard management practice of trying to focus oneself and others on outcomes rather than effort.<p>I am not sure &quot;moral&quot; is truly the right word for what the author is discussing... morality is not just about the outcome, it&#x27;s about what you are aiming for.  If you aim for the wrong thing, that is a moral failure, moral incompetence if you will. But that is not what the author is talking about.  True, it is good to be effective and obtain a desired outcome, particularly one that you or others think is good, but I&#x27;d point out that this &quot;moral competence&quot; and what another early poster mentioned, &quot;effective altruism&quot;, are not that dissimilar from earlier concepts and discussions of ... &quot;wisdom&quot;.')
