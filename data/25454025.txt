Item(by='CobrastanJorji', descendants=None, kids=[25455895, 25454549], score=None, time=1608197605, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25453805, text='Ethics tie directly to the quality of research. Good acting is good acting regardless of whether the actor is acting morally, but research can be manipulated by an unethical actor in myriad ways. It&#x27;s frankly quite disturbing how much trust there is in the research process and how little that trust is deserved.<p>There&#x27;s a broad spectrum of potential bad behavior impacting the results, ranging from straight up faking results on one end to p-hacking to just failing to publish relevant negative results or undesired results. An agenda can affect research in many, many ways, and it&#x27;s absolutely relevant to whether you should trust a paper.<p>It shouldn&#x27;t be that way. Not needing trust is rather the point of the scientific process. But it is.')
