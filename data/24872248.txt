Item(by='ironmagma', descendants=None, kids=[24873518, 24872418], score=None, time=1603476952, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24872078, text='It&#x27;s also a bit of an abuse of the term. Overengineering in engineering (mechanical, civil and other similar disciplines) means building a component to withstand more than the absolute minimum. So, a bridge you&#x27;d want to be overengineered by a factor of 2 or 3 at least, whereas airplanes are hardly overengineered (lower factor of safety).')
