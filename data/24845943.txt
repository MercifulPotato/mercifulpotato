Item(by='BigJono', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603269927, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24845554, text='There&#x27;s a big difference between HTTP, Apache, and ORMs. People not understanding why they&#x27;re different is what sinks companies all the time.<p>Using any abstraction is a gamble that it doesn&#x27;t leak before you have the resources to fix it.<p>HTTP is a terrible comparison because in almost every domain you will either make millions or (far more likely) fail for other reasons before you need to understand how TCP&#x2F;IP works.<p>I bet there&#x27;s loads of companies that have died a slow death, releasing everything a month later than they want to, because the back-end developers aren&#x27;t comfortable with whatever the stack of the day is and they were too busy learning yesterday&#x27;s stack of the day instead of the intricacies of the language they&#x27;re working with, or the standard library.')
