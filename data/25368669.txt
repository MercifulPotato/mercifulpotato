Item(by='mercurialshark', descendants=None, kids=[25368763], score=None, time=1607565863, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25368493, text='It&#x27;s not just the ruling that was problematic, but Oliver Wendell Holmes&#x27; often referenced grotesque metaphor.<p>Holmes&#x27; used the metaphor to equate criticism of the government during wartime to be so dangerous for society, specifically speaking against the draft during WWI, that it was akin to yelling fire in a crowded theatre.<p>Clearly, criticism of the government, especially regarding matters of war and public safety, are protected by the first amendment. Not only was the metaphor used to justify an unconstitutional ruling, but it also furthered gross misperceptions regarding limits on protected speech that are still referenced today.')
