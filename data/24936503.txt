Item(by='PeterisP', descendants=None, kids=[24938153], score=None, time=1604008324, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24935643, text='I believe that vegans have made a strong point that humans can live reasonably well without any animal-derived foods and other materials, so a mass extinction of animals would not destroy our way of life.<p>The same goes for pollinators - while there are many commercially important plants that rely on pollinators (e.g. almonds as one of main exports of California), the staple crops that feed humanity - wheat, rice, corn, potatoes - do not require pollinators and would be viable even if all insects died.<p>So even the horrible ecological disaster scenarios threaten human wellbeing and comfort, but they don&#x27;t really threaten the existence of humanity; fucking up our ecosystem would be a great cultural loss but industrialized farming could still march on.')
