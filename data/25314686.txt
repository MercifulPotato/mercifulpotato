I was very skeptical at first, but within 3 weeks I was being kept honest by this new manager on our daily calls. I didn&#x27;t realize that we&#x27;ve never had a &quot;good&quot; one and how amazing it might be.<p>I feel like the process for building your software is more important than the software itself. I.e. How can you worry about building cars when the factory that produces them is burning down, or experiencing a work stoppage because no one ever thought to develop a maintenance schedule for a particularly tricky piece of equipment?<p>The more I look at this whole enterprise of software engineering as a virtual factory floor, the easier it gets to draw analogies between developers and project managers regarding how we should manage work.')
