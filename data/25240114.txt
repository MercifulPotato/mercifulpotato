Item(by='atombender', descendants=None, kids=[25240555, 25240539], score=None, time=1606594191, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25236235, text='Does K6 deal with the coordinated omission problem?<p>Gil Tene (Azul Systems) has argued convincingly [1] (slides [2]) that monitoring tools get latency measurement wrong because sudden spikes aren&#x27;t represented correctly in timings because of averaging and the wrong use of percentiles.<p>He argues that percentiles simply aren&#x27;t useful, because, statistically, most requests will experience &gt;= 99.99-percentile response times. All percentiles lie; the only truly useful and realistic number is, in fact, the 100th percentile.<p>He also argues that the most revealing way to show latency is with a complete histogram.<p>I haven&#x27;t used K6 yet, but I&#x27;ve been looking for a good load-testing tool, and intend to try it out.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=lJ8ydIuPFeU" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=lJ8ydIuPFeU</a><p>[2] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.azul.com&#x2F;files&#x2F;HowNotToMeasureLatency_LLSummit_NYC_12Nov2013.pdf" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.azul.com&#x2F;files&#x2F;HowNotToMeasureLatency_LLSummit_N...</a>')
