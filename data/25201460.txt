Item(by='goatinaboat', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606243101, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25199921, text='<i>I once saw something similar where a co-worker on a newly formed team wanted to keep design documents in a git repo. Those types of documents can have dozens of small changes, or in this case commits, each day. It was also painful to read and edit. Luckily our manager stopped the practice early before it took root.</i><p>There&#x27;s a thing now called GitOps, where you manage your infrastructure by tweaking things in a Git repo then Puppet&#x2F;Ansible&#x2F;Terraform&#x2F;Saltstack&#x2F;whatever runs them automatically. Easy way to get &quot;number of commits&quot; way, way up in a plausible way.<p>I have a colleague who creates a Jira for every tiny thing that for most people would be just a routine part of their job. He literally spends more time fooling around in Jira than he does actually doing his real job. But he looks great in the stats...')
