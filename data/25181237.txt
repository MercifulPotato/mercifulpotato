Item(by='crazygringo', descendants=None, kids=[25181627, 25181321, 25181884, 25181555, 25181316, 25181640], score=None, time=1606080217, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25180180, text='&gt; <i>True black tends to look pretty unnatural, so start with a really dark grey</i><p>I wish this myth would die. #000 is already dark gray on any normal real-life screen anyone uses because of ambient light, halos, etc. It&#x27;s <i>nowhere</i> near &quot;true black&quot;.<p>There <i>is</i> a good reason to avoid pure black or pure white in an interface, but it&#x27;s nothing to do with &quot;naturalness&quot; or even legibility.<p>It&#x27;s simply so that <i>images</i> or <i>content</i> which <i>do</i> include the full brightness gamut from #000 to #FFF &quot;pop&quot; in comparison. You want the darkest shadows and brightest highlights to catch your attention, and not have them compete with browser or interface chrome.<p>An interface is generally intended to &quot;recede&quot; next to actual content. But if your interface is star of the show? Use pure black and pure white. Go crazy. It&#x27;s been used to great effect before.')
