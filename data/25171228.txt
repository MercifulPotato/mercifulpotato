Item(by='jlokier', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605980856, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25169896, text='Quite a few databases run on raw partitions.  Often the &quot;big, old&quot; ones.  Oracle, DB2, Sybase come to mind.<p>To improve performance, many OSes also provided kernel extensions to provide a DB-specific alternative block-device API for faster &quot;raw&quot; I&#x2F;O and something like &quot;async&quot; I&#x2F;O, rather than going through the regular unix block layer.  These extensions were probably only ever used by databases.')
