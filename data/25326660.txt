Item(by='janekm', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607289005, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25325525, text='Radio receivers (and the LNA component of such) are incredibly sensitive... that&#x27;s what allows all the radio systems we use daily to work at all.\nIf a signal is strong enough to have a physiological effect on the human body it is many many orders of magnitude stronger than can be measured with off-the-shelf instruments (i.e. an antenna and spectrum analyser).<p>Even if you make an effort to concentrate the effect in a specific spot (e.g. phased array antennas), you can only achieve a moderate reduction (e.g. 1-2 orders of magnitude) outside the central lobe. It will be easily detectable outside the effective area.')
