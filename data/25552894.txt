Item(by='rsync', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609092391, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25552800, text='This is <i>not</i> intended to solve your use-case - in fact, what I am about to write is primitive and childish ... however, I have done it myself and seen others do it:<p>You can replace a command like this in your crontab:<p><pre><code>  rsync blah blah\n</code></pre>\nwith a command like this:<p><pre><code>  rsync blah blah ; rsync blah blah ; rsync blah blah\n</code></pre>\nSee what I am doing there ?  I sometimes deploy this hack with very spotty WAN connections but the laptop sleep&#x2F;wake is basically the same thing ... you wake up the laptop and the running command bombs out and the next one starts.<p>The reason this &quot;works&quot; is because rsync picks up right where it left off on a broken transfer.  I believe restic and borg have similar behavior ...')
