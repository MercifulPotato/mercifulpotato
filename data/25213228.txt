Item(by='romanoderoma', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606332578, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25210307, text='As someone who grew up in the country where my family (that was kinda poor when I was born) bred the animals we ate, I remember that businesses selling eggs also sold male chicks to the local farmers and only kept the females to make eggs<p>We had a few chickens who layed eggs and we ate the rest of them<p>My grandparents bought male chicks from said businesses because the ones hatching from the eggs were not enough to sustain a quite large family<p>You had to go there to buy them, so I don&#x27;t think it was very costly<p>The logistics of killing the males and shipping the meat to companies making animal food is not more convenient (IMO) unless animal food is more profitable than human food,  I don&#x27;t know about it, but I hope it&#x27;s not the case and it&#x27;s not the main motivation behind it.<p>The point of not killing the male chicks I imagine is to show kindness towards animals, selling them (or selling the male eggs) to someone who could make food out of it, I think should be considered equally good, if not more.')
