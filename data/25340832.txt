Item(by='musicale', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607393869, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25332332, text='If you have a gas range or oven (which I wish I did since they are basically banned in new construction) definitely turn the hood fan on <i>high</i> before turning on the gas and leave the fan on until after you turn it off. Otherwise your kitchen will fill up with methane, nitrogen oxides, carbon monoxide, and other pollutants. (Some when you turn the burner on, some during operation, and some when you turn it off.)<p>&quot;the investigators estimated that, during a typical winter week, 1.7 million Californians could be exposed to CO levels that exceed standards for ambient air, and 12 million could be exposed to excessive NO2 levels, if they do not use venting range hoods during cooking.&quot;<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;ehp.niehs.nih.gov&#x2F;doi&#x2F;10.1289&#x2F;ehp.122-A27" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;ehp.niehs.nih.gov&#x2F;doi&#x2F;10.1289&#x2F;ehp.122-A27</a><p>Also pilot lights are apparently responsible for about 30% of methane emissions from older gas appliances. So ventilate your house or switch to electric ignition.')
