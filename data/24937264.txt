Item(by='gpanders', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604013580, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24936992, text='mutt can probably do something like this with its send-hook [1] feature. Another alternative is to send emails as multipart&#x2F;alternative with a plaintext part and an HTML part. That way, recipients who use an HTML email client will see the HTML and plaintext readers will see plaintext.<p>This is what I do at work. My work uses Outlook which does not support format=flowed [2], meaning my plaintext emails would look weird to colleagues who use the standard Outlook settings. Outlook will also default to responding in plaintext to a plaintext email. To avoid forcing my preferences on my colleagues, I now use an automatic filter [3] to convert my plaintext emails into HTML using a Markdown parser. My email client then sends both parts: the HTML and plaintext part as a multipart&#x2F;alternative email.<p>[1]: <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.mutt.org&#x2F;doc&#x2F;manual&#x2F;#send-hook" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.mutt.org&#x2F;doc&#x2F;manual&#x2F;#send-hook</a><p>[2]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;joeclark.org&#x2F;ffaq.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;joeclark.org&#x2F;ffaq.html</a><p>[3]: <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.mutt.org&#x2F;doc&#x2F;manual&#x2F;#send-multipart-alternative-filter" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.mutt.org&#x2F;doc&#x2F;manual&#x2F;#send-multipart-alternative-f...</a>')
