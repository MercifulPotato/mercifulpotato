Item(by='smabie', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603772661, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24904283, text='Increased volatility of the underlying always increases option prices.<p>Moreover, a higher standard deviation decreases the compounded returns of a log normal distribution, not decreases it. This is called volatility drag, and is closesly related to the AM-GM inequality: the geometric and arithmetic means of a series are only equal if all terms are identical. If this is not true, an arithmetic average will always be greater.')
