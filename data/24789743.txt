Item(by='martythemaniak', descendants=None, kids=[24790988], score=None, time=1602776433, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24789379, text='Most people are pragmatic. Simple, axiomatic principles can be very intellectually appealing (probably more so amongst the STEM-crowd than the general population), if they don&#x27;t produce good outcomes, they will and should be discarded.<p>In our field we constantly see languages with stronger intellectual foundations (Haskell, Lisp, Scala, Perl6) constantly lose out market share to pragmatic, practical, messy and oftentimes ugly languages&#x2F;libraries&#x2F;tookits etc. We often knowingly choose the latter set knowing full well what their downsides are, because we still come out ahead.<p>If you&#x27;re wondering why someone would support Facebook&#x2F;Twitter&#x2F;YouTube&#x27;s recent moves, it really is that simple. Their foundations don&#x27;t have to be perfect, or even particularly good or fair. They just have to be better the deluge of toxic sludge that&#x27;s currently overwhelming these systems. That is to say, there is no answer to the Paradox of Tolerance and you don&#x27;t need an answer. If you feel people are taking advantage of you, you will (and should) crack down, and if you crack down too much eventually people will (and should) rebel.')
