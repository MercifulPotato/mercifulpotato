But what you have there is just rent plain and simple, not rent-seeking.<p>Rent-seeking is when a third party uses force to claim  profit from an agreement they&#x27;re not actually party to.<p>For example, you rent a business property. You pay the landlord and agree to their terms. Next week the mob shows up and demand protection money to &quot;keep you from harm&quot;. Implying that harm will surely come to you unless you pay. That&#x27;s rent-seeking.<p>The app store analogy fits rather nicely I think, but it depends on your viewpoint.')
