Item(by='floatrock', descendants=None, kids=[24852971], score=None, time=1603318239, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24852169, text='Yeah, it&#x27;s unclear whether those 5 and 7MW figures quoted are electricity production or just heat energy.<p>Not to imply that &quot;just heat energy&quot; is useless -- <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;District_heating" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;District_heating</a> is the term for using waste industrial heat to heat up homes in the surrounding areas. More common in Europe and Canada than the US I believe.<p>Cogeneration is when the concept is used specifically by electricity plants. It&#x27;s used mostly in places that have need to generate their own electricity and heating like college and hospital campuses -- by harvesting the waste heat for another localized purpose, it becomes cheaper than relying solely on the grid.')
