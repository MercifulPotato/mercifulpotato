I prefer to use native binary gRPC because:<p>1) REST verb&#x2F;code mapping is too arbitrary for my taste, I prefer explicit verbs in RPC method names and error codes explicitly designed for RPC [2] and ones that will not be accidentally thrown by your HTTP proxy thereby confusing your client<p>2) REST stream multiplexing over a minimum amount of TCP connections is difficult to do, I trust the gRPC authors to have done their homework better than the average HTTP library. In addition, you can multiplex multiple gRPC bidirectional streams over a single TCP connection, which is something you can&#x27;t do over plain HTTP without resorting to websockets.<p>[1] - <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cloud.google.com&#x2F;endpoints&#x2F;docs&#x2F;grpc&#x2F;transcoding" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cloud.google.com&#x2F;endpoints&#x2F;docs&#x2F;grpc&#x2F;transcoding</a><p>[2] - <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;grpc&#x2F;grpc&#x2F;blob&#x2F;master&#x2F;doc&#x2F;statuscodes.md" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;grpc&#x2F;grpc&#x2F;blob&#x2F;master&#x2F;doc&#x2F;statuscodes.md</a>')
