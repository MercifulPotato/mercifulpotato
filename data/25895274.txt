Item(by='franga2000', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611518379, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25893855, text='Here&#x27;s their website [0], but they don&#x27;t have an English version.<p>Basically, they have a handful of workshops around the country where people can drop off computers and other related hardware, but they also work with companies who donate write-off hardware (there&#x27;s a tax incentive). Anything they can piece together into working systems gets sent out to people who request it (these days a lot also went to schools who distributed it further) and anything that&#x27;s unfixable or too old gets sold to a recycling company and partially funds the operation.<p>They usually ship the computers with their own Linux distro [1] and they also provide free tech support to people that receive their computers. I think they&#x27;ve given away over 4000 computers since 2011 (keep in mind the population of Slovenia is 2M, so that&#x27;s pretty significant) and provided at least half of the estimated required computers for schoolchildren when the first lockdown went into effect.<p>P.S.: if anyone is interested in starting something similar in their own country, the software they use to manage everything is open-source [2] and they&#x27;ve said in the past that they&#x27;d love to help people set up similar projects in other countries, so do get in touch<p>[0] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;racunalniki.duh-casa.si" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;racunalniki.duh-casa.si</a>\n[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;sites.google.com&#x2F;site&#x2F;chaletoslinux&#x2F;home" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;sites.google.com&#x2F;site&#x2F;chaletoslinux&#x2F;home</a>\n[2] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;gitlab.com&#x2F;franga2000&#x2F;erso" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;gitlab.com&#x2F;franga2000&#x2F;erso</a>')
