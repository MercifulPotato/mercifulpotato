Item(by='rektide', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605908022, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25165154, text='I don&#x27;t have much information or much of an explanation.<p>But it seems to me like there was a big push a couple years ago to integrate everything with G+. Someone collected the political capital inside the company to get this to be a mandate for the company, a direction for everyone to head.<p>Well, it really wasn&#x27;t that bad, but no the gangbusters success Google hoped for (not sure how anything with the flimsiest tiniest Read-Only api like that would ever get anywhere?).<p>Since then it seems like there&#x27;s a corporation, but mostly a lot of different teams marching to different drummers. It&#x27;s pretty common in corporate world that managers &amp; mini-executives &amp; executives push for huge home run hits, within their fief, then leverage that smashing success to push for even bigger agendas. There&#x27;s little incentive to maintain &amp; care for a product well: everything is about metrics, &amp; the existing user base&#x27;s retention is usually not all that influenced by the love &amp; care of a carefully thoughtfully evolved product. Trying to work across teams, to create something that works consistently across products, is even harder, as the organizational complexity of bringing more &amp; more teams in keeps increasing.<p>Organizational complexity grows. Teams seek autonomy &amp; wins. Products get focus. Ecosystems &amp; long-burning initiatives are much much much much harder to sheppard forward. Little of this is unique to Google.')
