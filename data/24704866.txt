In moving to better-paid, senior, more specialist roles a kind of pervasive apathy sets in as less and less time spent could be linked to the real world in any kind of depth.<p>It&#x27;s possible to shake the feeling somewhat if the work tempo is high and you can tie it to some overall notion of societal benefit. I also suspect it&#x27;s responsible for the manifestation of desires to move back to the land, work with one&#x27;s hands, and generally check out from contribution to society and the world at large. I suppose that instinct often gets channeled into day dreams of an eventual retirement.<p>Similar commentary from Marx on Abstract Labour.')
