But two caveats:<p>(1) Adding stocks to one&#x27;s portfolio makes it easier to succeed with a 4% withdrawal rate in very low interest-rate environments:\n<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;thepoorswiss.com&#x2F;updated-trinity-study&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;thepoorswiss.com&#x2F;updated-trinity-study&#x2F;</a><p>(2) Life expectancy. People who retire at a young age, should use a lower withdrawal rate. But a person age 75, expecting to die before age 100, could have a withdrawal rate of roughly 4% (reciprocal of 100-75) in a zero-interest-rate environment.')
