Item(by='TheOperator', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603822116, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24909734, text='I can buy the argument that they could require special treatment but I&#x27;m much more sceptical that social media specifically resembles a public utility.<p>If I decide Facebook and all it&#x27;s products are trash I can pretty trivially not use any of them.  If I dislike all social media I can choose not to engage with it at all.  I would have a much harder time going without electricity or water.<p>Social media feels somewhere between a public commons and print media, and it&#x27;s acting more and more like print media all the time.  I&#x27;m seeing this election the banning of certain sources like the New York Post and the editorialization of what people say with friendly links to approved &quot;non-partisan fact checkers&quot; who are essentially opinion columnists that cite more sources.<p>Netflix is about as close to a public utility as the local movie theatre, which is to say, nowhere close.')
