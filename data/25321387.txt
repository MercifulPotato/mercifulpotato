Item(by='ChuckMcM', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607233183, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25319539, text='Funny DEC trivia, their &quot;personal computer&quot; ran a variant of RSX-11 called &quot;POS&quot;. The joke on campus &quot;It runs POS, and it is.&quot; It had some ungodly co-processor to run DOS or CP&#x2F;M or something too. Mostly I&#x27;ve pushed it out of my memory.<p>Second, the book &quot;Computer Engineering: A DEC View of System Design&quot; is a treasure trove of information about early DEC machines and computer architecture in general. I have a copy that I got to get Gordon Bell autograph at a Computer History event. If you&#x27;re interested in system architecture I can easily recommend it.<p>Finally, Ken Olsen&#x27;s inability to see how &quot;personal&quot; computers would change the world is pretty legendary. It is right up there with the folks who believed it would never be possible for someone to make a business out of flying people places.')
