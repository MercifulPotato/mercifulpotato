Item(by='azornathogron', descendants=None, kids=[25869286], score=None, time=1611266150, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25861297, text='One of the design criteria for Unicode was to guarantee lossless round-trip conversion through Unicode for a bunch of encodings that Unicode superceded. If one of those encodings contained both a K and a K-for-Kelvin, then Unicode also had to allocate two codepoints for it. I don&#x27;t know if that&#x27;s the case for these characters in particular, but it is one possible explanation.<p>Supporting round-trip conversion may sound pointless now that Unicode is ubiquitous, but it was probably necessary to make gradual adoption practical.<p>Ref: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.m.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Unicode_compatibility_characters" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.m.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Unicode_compatibility_charac...</a>')
