Item(by='mhh__', descendants=None, kids=[25671347], score=None, time=1609986179, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25664935, text='RISC-V was originally a teaching ISA but because of it&#x27;s licence and open design process it&#x27;s increasingly being adopted by industry.<p>There are other open ISAs available but they are generally bigger targets (POWER is open now but it&#x27;s a result of decades of IBM-work so it&#x27;s not as initially simple as RISC-V).<p>Also one thing that even on HN seems to be missed - <i>RISC-V IS THE ISA</i> not the silicon. All it means is that you don&#x27;t have to pay the designer of the ISA any royalties to use it a la Arm, the CPU itself is most likely still a black box.')
