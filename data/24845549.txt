Item(by='seer', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603265365, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24845483, text='The biggest “aha” moment for me was when I started working with graphql.<p>The tooling would statically analyze your queries, and produce types for exactly what you were requesting.<p>Then you would just have a bunch of raw queries laying around, but you’d be confident the data retuned is the right data at compile time.<p>That made just writing raw queries a lot more simple and feasible than building abstractions on top of them.<p>Now TS and sql doesn’t really have a robust lib for that I think, last time I checked it was only <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;adelsz&#x2F;pgtyped" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;adelsz&#x2F;pgtyped</a>\nBut haven’t looked at how far it has progressed.')
