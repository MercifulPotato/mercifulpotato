Item(by='giantrobot', descendants=None, kids=[25814985, 25814119], score=None, time=1610911845, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25813239, text='Congress and the Nixon administration trapped NASA in LEO. There was extensive planning inside NASA for applications of Saturn-Apollo technologies [0]. Two of the ambitious ones were a manned Venus flyby [1] and a permanent Lunar base [2]. After the Apollo I fire Congress lost its appetite for space and slashed NASA&#x27;s budget [3]. The Nixon administration went all-in on the Space Shuttle which ate up all of NASA&#x27;s manned spaceflight budget. Skylab and the Apollo-Soyuz Test Project were the only AAP projects to survive and only at the cost of Apollo missions 18-20.<p>The Saturn-Apollo program had a super-heavy lift rocket (Saturn V), a man-rated heavy lift rocket (Saturn IB), a Lunar trajectory capable command and service module, a lunar lander, and a workable wet workshop space station (Skylab).<p>Congress tossed all of that away. The Space Shuttle program wasn&#x27;t a <i>bad</i> idea, a heavy lift vehicle that provided a shirt sleeve environment but could support EVA is actually an awesome thing for LEO. But Congress scrapping Saturn-Apollo trapped NASA in LEO because the Shuttle was only capable of LEO. It&#x27;s not a platform that can support manned Moon missions let alone any more distant bodies.<p>[0] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.m.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Apollo_Applications_Program" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.m.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Apollo_Applications_Program</a><p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.m.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Manned_Venus_flyby" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.m.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Manned_Venus_flyby</a><p>[2] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.wired.com&#x2F;2013&#x2F;11&#x2F;moonbase-apollo-1968&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.wired.com&#x2F;2013&#x2F;11&#x2F;moonbase-apollo-1968&#x2F;</a><p>[3] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.wired.com&#x2F;2012&#x2F;08&#x2F;before-the-fire&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.wired.com&#x2F;2012&#x2F;08&#x2F;before-the-fire&#x2F;</a>')
