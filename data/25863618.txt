Item(by='benlivengood', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611260089, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25862013, text='&gt; Basically, it seems like the terminal state of this is a gigantic freeloader problem where everyone wants the network to be secure but nobody is incentivized to do it.<p>It&#x27;ll be in the interest of exchanges and payment processors to prevent double-spend at least, and they have an incentive for the blockchain to be trustworthy vs. running their own attacks and could adjust their own fees to maintain &gt;51% control of miners across the lot of them.<p>There&#x27;s also a fallback recovery mechanism; for any attacker willing to invest $X in a 51% attack the remaining users only have to invest an additional $X*.04 to regain 51% control and make a hard fork that most miners would switch to.  My guess is that faced with evidence of a double-spend most miners would switch to the hard fork to avoid losing the block rewards the attacker&#x27;s chain stole from them.  This relies on rapid detection of the attack and availability of a hard-fork client.')
