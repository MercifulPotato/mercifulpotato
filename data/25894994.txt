What&#x27;s the difference?<p>Having &quot;accents fall off&quot; has gotten people murdered [0]. Accents aren&#x27;t things peppered in for effect, they turn letters into different letters, spelling different words. Analogously, imagine that a bunch of software accidentally turned every &quot;d&quot; into a &quot;c&quot; because some committee halfway around the world decided &quot;d&quot; should be composed of the &quot;c&quot; and &quot;|&quot; glyphs. That&#x27;s the kind of text corruption that regularly happens in other languages when dealing with text at the code point layer.<p>[0] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;languagelog.ldc.upenn.edu&#x2F;nll&#x2F;?p=73" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;languagelog.ldc.upenn.edu&#x2F;nll&#x2F;?p=73</a> . Note that this is Turkish, which has the &quot;dotted i&quot; problem, meaning that this was more than likely a .toupper() gone wrong rather than a truncation issue.')
