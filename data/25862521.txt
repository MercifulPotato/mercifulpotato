Item(by='tanelpoder', descendants=None, kids=[25862962, 25863641], score=None, time=1611255169, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25862264, text='Also worth noting that scalability != efficiency. With enough NVMe drives, a single server can do <i>millions of IOPS</i> and scan data at over 100 GB&#x2F;s. A single PCIe 4.0 x4 SSD on my machine can do large I&#x2F;Os at 6.8 GB&#x2F;s rate, so 16 of them (with 4 x quad SSD adapter cards) in a 2-socket EPYC machine can do over 100 GB&#x2F;s.<p>You may need clusters, duplicated systems, replication, etc for resiliency reasons of course, but a single modern machine with lots of memory channels per CPU and PCIe 4.0 can achieve ridiculous throughput...<p>edit: Here&#x27;s an example of doing 11M IOPS with 10x Samsung Pro 980 PCIe 4.0 SSDs (it&#x27;s from an upcoming blog entry):<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;twitter.com&#x2F;TanelPoder&#x2F;status&#x2F;1352329243070504964" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;twitter.com&#x2F;TanelPoder&#x2F;status&#x2F;1352329243070504964</a>')
