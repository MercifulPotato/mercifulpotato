Item(by='CamperBob2', descendants=None, kids=[25810645], score=None, time=1610847324, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25806428, text='It comes down to a simple, enduring principle: <i>people who don&#x27;t use something shouldn&#x27;t have sole authority over its design.</i><p>So many of the complaints about the dominance of seemingly-incompetent UX professionals really boil down to failure to consume one&#x27;s own dog food.  The UX pros are not incompetent at UX design, but they <i>are</i> unfamiliar with the tasks that the program will ultimately be used for, and that&#x27;s even worse.<p>This phenomenon is more widespread than most people realize, even here.  If you buy a new Porsche 911 today, you&#x27;ll find that the garage-door opener button has been moved to a touchscreen menu.  People who take a bus to work are now designing Porsches, in other words.  It&#x27;s only going to get worse from here.')
