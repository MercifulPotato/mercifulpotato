Essential in that link simply indicates, as I read it, that the specific way the text of an image logo is rendered is essential to it&#x27;s function. In other words that you can&#x27;t simply replace the WASI in the image with the text &quot;WASI.&quot; Essential does not mean that is must meet some requirements or be readable. In fact the contrast requirement your presentation cites is explicit in that it doesn&#x27;t:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.w3.org&#x2F;TR&#x2F;UNDERSTANDING-WCAG20&#x2F;visual-audio-contrast-contrast.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.w3.org&#x2F;TR&#x2F;UNDERSTANDING-WCAG20&#x2F;visual-audio-cont...</a><p>&gt;Logotypes: Text that is part of a logo or brand name has no minimum contrast requirement.')
