Item(by='geomark', descendants=None, kids=[25262289], score=None, time=1606792340, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25261669, text='Asia is a big place. In some densely populated areas in Asia  most people live in bubbles, moving from air conditioned condos to taxis&#x2F;subways to offices and then malls. They spend as little time as possible in the open due to pollution, weather and a cultural aversion to tanning. As a result, vitamin D deficiency is very common.<p>Some examples for Thailand are included in this paper[1]. One study said this: &quot;Soontrapa et al. [15] evaluated vitamin D status in a younger group of premenopausal women found the prevalence of vitamin D insufficiency to be 77.8%, which was as high as the rate found in elderly Thai women living in nursing homes.&quot;<p>There is lots of sun in Thailand, but people avoid it as much as possible, staying indoors and applying lots of sunscreen if they must go out.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov&#x2F;pmc&#x2F;articles&#x2F;PMC5685050&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov&#x2F;pmc&#x2F;articles&#x2F;PMC5685050&#x2F;</a>')
