In the end, you can deduce anything to being political.<p>Or this definition by Alex P. Schmid from 1988:<p>&gt; &quot;Terrorism is an anxiety-inspiring method of repeated violent action, employed by (semi-)clandestine individual, group, or state actors, for idiosyncratic, criminal, or political reasons, whereby—in contrast to assassination—the direct targets of violence are not the main targets. The immediate human victims of violence are generally chosen randomly (targets of opportunity) or selectively (representative or symbolic targets) from a target population, and serve as message generators. Threat- and violence-based communication processes between terrorist (organization), (imperiled) victims, and main targets are used to manipulate the main target (audience(s), turning it into a target of terror, a target of demands, or a target of attention, depending on whether intimidation, coercion, or propaganda is primarily sought&quot;.<p>Source and more scholar definitions see [2].<p>For in-depth criteria I can recommend Alex P. Schmid&#x27;s &quot;Revised Academic Consensus Definition of Terrorism&quot; from 2011 [3] as it is what scholars at Leiden University use.<p>Regarding the criterium is it always political, see #9:<p>&gt; 9.
