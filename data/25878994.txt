It is not Zuckerberg&#x27;s personal home, it is a place open to the public to conduct business. If Facebook is going to set conditions that some members of the public are allowed and not others, it should be clear what those conditions are and when they are and are not met.<p>That said, I do agree that people <i>should</i> flee these places where they&#x27;re at the mercy of whatever algorithm or ideological fad that takes over the current employees or whatever. But insofar as they aren&#x27;t we should regulate it the same way we regulate (in the US) hotels, restaurants, and other public-facing businesses to ensure that the whole public has a fair chance to use them.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Public_accommodations_in_the_United_States" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Public_accommodations_in_the_U...</a>')
