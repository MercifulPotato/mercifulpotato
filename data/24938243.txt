Item(by='syrrim', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604022347, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24929726, text='I noticed a while ago that the powers of 1001 encode the successive rows of\npascal&#x27;s triangle&#x2F;the binomial coefficients. This is not so surprising, since\nwe are effectively taking powers of the polynomial (1+x), but replacing x with \n1000 (clearly it works the same if we use any other power of ten instead). I\nwonder if we can find a relationship to that here. We might start by looking at\nthis relation:<p><pre><code>  1&#x2F;(1-x) = 1 + x + x^2 + x^3 + ...\n</code></pre>\nHopefully this will yield an operator x whose succesive powers are the\nfibonacci numbers. Take .01&#x2F;(1-x) = 1&#x2F;89, then x = 0.11. Actually, the powers of\nx, just like 1001 above, will yield rows of pascal&#x27;s triangle. So the taylor\nexpansion above tells us that F(k) = Σ(n=0..k-1) B(n, k), in other words that\neach fibonacci number is the sum of a diagonal of pascal&#x27;s triangle (like here:\n<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cdn1.byjus.com&#x2F;wp-content&#x2F;uploads&#x2F;2018&#x2F;11&#x2F;maths&#x2F;2016&#x2F;08&#x2F;01092801&#x2F;PascalTriangleFibonacci.png" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;cdn1.byjus.com&#x2F;wp-content&#x2F;uploads&#x2F;2018&#x2F;11&#x2F;maths&#x2F;2016...</a>)<p>More generally, we can compute numbers with decimal expansions of the fibonacci\nnumbers with 10^-2n &#x2F; (1 - 10^-n - 10^-2n). Notice that this is just the\nz-transform of the recurrence relation of the fibonacci series, with 10^n\nreplacing z:<p><pre><code>  Z(f(n)) = Z(f(n-1) + f(n-2) + δ(n-2)) (δ is the kronecker delta)\n     F(z) = z^-2 &#x2F; (1 - z^-1 - z^-2)\n</code></pre>\nThe taylor expansion of this expression has coefficients equal to the terms of\nthe fibonacci sequence - which makes sense, because that&#x27;s the definition of\nthe z-transform. We can, with a little rearranging, get an explicit formula\nfor the fibonacci sequence from it too:<p><pre><code>  Take φ± = (1 ± √5)&#x2F;2\n  Then F(z) = z^2&#x2F;(z - φ+)&#x2F;(z - φ-)\n            = ( φ+ z&#x2F;(z - φ+) - φ- z&#x2F;(z - φ-) )&#x2F;√5 (by partial fraction decomposition)\n  Z^-1(F(z)) = f(n) = (φ+^(n+1) - φ-^(n+1))&#x2F;√5</code></pre>')
