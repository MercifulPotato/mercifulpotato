Item(by='valuearb', descendants=None, kids=[25319894], score=None, time=1607212828, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25319439, text='The reason for giving capital gains a preferred long term rate is inflation. If you invest in business for 10 years and double your money, you&#x27;ve made a 7% annual return. But if inflation averaged 7% during that time you actually made zero economic gains.<p>A better way to restore progressively to capital gains is to index them for inflation, and then you can tax them at ordinary income rates.')
