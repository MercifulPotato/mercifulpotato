Item(by='opportune', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606542243, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25235370, text='I&#x27;ve worked way over 9-5 for ~months a few times and I usually feel very unhappy with my decision for months afterwards.<p>If you are just an engineer, there is no point. You are just burning yourself out to make someone else&#x27;s dream come true, or get your boss promoted, or get your director their bonus. Maybe you will get promoted, or a better rating. Even when I&#x27;ve seen that outcome I was still unhappy with it.<p>Any company will always have an endless backlog of work and burning it down faster doesn&#x27;t do anything. IMO you should only do it when financial or career incentives are very well aligned.<p>I don&#x27;t know if I&#x27;ve ever met another person who is truly (not for resume&#x2F;career&#x2F;money purposes) &quot;passionate&quot; about the kind of software people get paid to write. Most software is really boring. Even for software that isn&#x27;t, it looks boring once you&#x27;ve seen how it works.')
