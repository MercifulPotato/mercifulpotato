Item(by='Naac', descendants=None, kids=[24725200], score=None, time=1602198265, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24716218, text='To really understand <i>how</i> ed was useful, it&#x27;s eye-opening to see exactly what life was like before video terminals.<p>Essentially, think of using your laptop, but instead of looking at a monitor you&#x27;re reading the output from a printer. So if you type `ls`, you see the directory listing being literally printed out on a dot-matrix printer. Naturally the only way of text editing is with a line EDitor, which lets you edit text one command at a time.<p>When trying to use ed on a video terminal, it obviously looks wasteful. But that&#x27;s because it was designed before video terminals were a thing. Kind of like using a modern day graphics card to play pong.<p>here is a video illustrating using a teletype:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=2XLZ4Z8LpEE" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=2XLZ4Z8LpEE</a>')
