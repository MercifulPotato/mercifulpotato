That same driver will not have had any tuition or assessment on their ability to judge whether a car with advanced assistive piloting* is acting sensibly.<p>In the case of some event requiring manual intervention, I&#x27;d be surprised if the driver&#x27;s focus would be significantly worse than a similar event while actively driving. Not only that but the judgement, knowledge, and experience of being able to identify such events in a timely manner.<p>I&#x27;m genuinely shocked that this feature is allowed on public roads. It&#x27;s one thing a car owner consenting to being a trial participant, but pedestrians and other road users have no such opportunity to provide or deny consent and are at equal or greater risk if things go awry.<p>*I refuse to use Tesla&#x27;s irresponsible &#x27;self-driving&#x27; marketing nonsense')
