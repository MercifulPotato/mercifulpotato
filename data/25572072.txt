Item(by='TameAntelope', descendants=None, kids=[25574896, 25572413], score=None, time=1609262220, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25571848, text='I can&#x27;t stop thinking about the concept of &quot;policy-based innovation&quot; a new startup could build off of to disrupt an industry, but I feel like it violates one of the primary tenets of Starting a Startup because a big player can theoretically &quot;just change their policy&quot; to eat your lunch.<p>For example (given enough capital), is there genuinely an opportunity to build a YouTube-like platform that simply doesn&#x27;t have the policies YouTube has (e.g. shares revenue more fairly, has a more-sane copyright law enforcement, explains their moderation actions)?<p>The idea here is that while it&#x27;s true, in theory, that YouTube could make those policy changes and your YouTube alternative would then be pointless, the reality is they never will, even if it begins to cost them.<p>I really wish I could talk to someone more about this, or if there was targeted reading, but I haven&#x27;t really found much, because I think most folks dismiss this as a viable strategy.')
