Item(by='andreareina', descendants=None, kids=[25169312], score=None, time=1605960419, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25169089, text='I think the point is that given the size of the error bars, that much precision is not an accurate representation of the confidence we should have in the result. 25.68% might be the <i>observed</i> change, but it&#x27;s not one we should have much confidence of being accurate, not to four significant figures. Glibly put, it&#x27;s false precision.<p>No-one I&#x27;ve seen arguing for fewer significant digits says that a probability of 0.999 should be rounded to 1.
