Item(by='abathur', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611016953, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25826766, text='Writing-advice is tricky.<p>The people who need it <i>most</i> are on the far side of a chasm they can&#x27;t bridge without learning to read their own writing.<p>Doing so takes a mix of interest&#x2F;dedication and practice that I suspect are hard to <i>hack</i>. The closest you can get, I think, is to spend some time breaking down, analyzing, critiquing, and &quot;fixing&quot; things others have written. You may also fare better, in calendar time, if you like writing and reading as ends.<p>If someone has learned to read themselves, you can give them all sorts of great advice. If they haven&#x27;t, the best you can do is give straightforward &quot;rules&quot; they&#x27;ll have some hope of spotting and fixing in their own work.<p><i>Edits:</i><p>- Yes--I think this holds for programming.<p>- Taking a newswriting class is my stock recommendation for ~working people who ask me &quot;how write?&quot;')
