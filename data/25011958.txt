It looks like all it does is convert your json blob into a bunch of assignment statements that have the full path from root to node, and the ability to parse that back into an object.  Not sure why I didn&#x27;t think of that intermediate form being way more greppable.  Kudos to the author.<p>Just as an example, this just took me about a minute to get the data I wanted, whereas I probably spent a half an hour on it yesterday with jq:<p><pre><code>  curl -s https:&#x2F;&#x2F;static01.nyt.com&#x2F;elections-assets&#x2F;2020&#x2F;data&#x2F;api&#x2F;2020-11-03&#x2F;national-map-page&#x2F;national&#x2F;president.json | gron | grep -E &#x27;races.*(leader_margin_votes|leader_margin_name_display|state_name)&#x27; | grep -vE &#x27;townships|counties&#x27; | gron -ungron</code></pre>')
