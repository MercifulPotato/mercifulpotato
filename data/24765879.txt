When there is a heavy rain, it used to be sewage would pour into the Willamette river.<p>However, for the past 10 years the city has been installing rain gardens that contain water storage devices that allow flash rain to be collected like a capacitor and then slowly drain into the ground after. [1]<p>These have had a big impact, not only in reducing or eliminated sewage spills into the river, but also reducing the amount of storm water being processed by sewage treatment plants.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.portlandoregon.gov&#x2F;bes&#x2F;78921" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.portlandoregon.gov&#x2F;bes&#x2F;78921</a>')
