Item(by='Tainnor', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605909415, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25165478, text='&gt; I think it&#x27;s easy to hold this opinion when the views silenced are not your own.<p>It&#x27;s also really easy to be a &quot;free speech&quot; absolutist[1] when you&#x27;re not personally being persecuted.<p>As others have already mentioned ITT, there is a legal argument to be made that most opinions can be expressed in some form, but there is neither a legal argument to be made that they can be expressed through a particular medium, nor is there a moral argument to be made that every opinion is equally worthy of defense.<p>It&#x27;s not about the question whether Google censoring LGBT content would be technically illegal, or violate &quot;freedom of speech&quot; - I think neither of these are necessarily true - but it would be morally wrong[2] and thus worthy of outrage.<p>[1] By that I mean people who think that &quot;freedom of speech&quot; means that you should have a right to speak your mind in every context without any repercussions. Freedom of speech is important, but so is protection from persecution, or the trustworthiness of reputable sources etc.<p>[2] The only way in which it wouldn&#x27;t be morally wrong is if they decided e.g. to ban all political content altogether or something similar.')
