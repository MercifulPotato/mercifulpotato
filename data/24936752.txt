In Canada the PIPEDA [1] covers this federally, though the law is written such that it is a backstop, and provinces are free to enact their own &quot;substantially similar&quot; (or stricter) laws if they choose. For example, in Alberta the PIPA [2] covers these issues.<p>And as the article states, both federal and provincial privacy commissioners found CF in violation of those laws.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.priv.gc.ca&#x2F;en&#x2F;privacy-topics&#x2F;privacy-laws-in-canada&#x2F;the-personal-information-protection-and-electronic-documents-act-pipeda&#x2F;pipeda_brief&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.priv.gc.ca&#x2F;en&#x2F;privacy-topics&#x2F;privacy-laws-in-can...</a><p>[2] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.alberta.ca&#x2F;personal-information-protection-act-overview.aspx" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.alberta.ca&#x2F;personal-information-protection-act-o...</a>')
