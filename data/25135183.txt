Item(by='stephc_int13', descendants=None, kids=[25136105], score=None, time=1605695274, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25133620, text='I&#x27;ve been writing code for more than twenty years now and I had the opportunity to try many different styles.<p>It is, unfortunately, very easy to write difficult to read code, especially with good intentions and principles.<p>In practice, I&#x27;ve found that the most important principle is Locality, that is avoiding nested indirections and unecessary abstractions.<p>I completely agree with the author of the article here, the simple and dumb recipe with constants local values is both easy to read and easy to maintain.<p>It might seems like duplication but the complexity has to live somewhere and it is more manageable when it is not scattered.')
