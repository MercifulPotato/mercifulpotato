Item(by='adrian_b', descendants=None, kids=[25092876], score=None, time=1605355170, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25089305, text='The GeekBench tests have revealed that the clock frequency of the M1 cores is 3.2 GHz, both in MB Air and in MB Pro.<p>Therefore M1 succeeds to have a slightly higher single-thread performance (between 3% and 8%) than Intel Tiger Lake and AMD Zen 3 at only 2&#x2F;3 of their clock frequency.<p>The speed advantage of M1 in single-thread is not high enough to be noticeable in practice, but what counts is that reaching the same performance at high IPC and low frequency instead of low IPC and high frequency results in having the same performance as the competitors at a much lower power consumption.<p>This is the real achievement of Apple and not the ridiculous claims about M1 being 3 times faster than obsolete and slow older products.')
