Item(by='jrockway', descendants=None, kids=[25972168], score=None, time=1612035042, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25971873, text='Nothing is stopping you from starting your own CA and distributing your own web browser that trusts the certificates.  What underlying transport protocol is used doesn&#x27;t change the complexity of removing corporate influence from the Internet.  There is one root DNS namespace.  Certificates prove that the stream your user agent receives from example.com is the example.com that is pointed to by the DNS system.  That&#x27;s it.  No part of the Internet requires DNS or trusted-by-default CAs.  It&#x27;s just a convenience.<p>Before widespread TLS adoption, your ISP could (and did!) inject ads into arbitrary websites.  Now they can&#x27;t.  That&#x27;s a good thing.  It is actually amazing how little power your ISP has to tamper with your network traffic -- all they can do it shut it off completely.')
