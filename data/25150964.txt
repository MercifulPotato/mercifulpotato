Item(by='gioele', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605802602, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25149291, text='That is just a coincidence (decimal ending in &quot;5&quot;), plus the fact that `.to_d` does some cleanup preprocessing.<p>Counterexample:<p><pre><code>  BigDecimal(&quot;1.5999999999999996&quot;) == 1.5999999999999996.to_d\n  =&gt; false\n</code></pre>\nThe most ergonomic way to initialize decimal numbers is IMO<p><pre><code>  &quot;123.45&quot;.to_d\n</code></pre>\n(Note the quotes around the number. `.to_d` is being called on a string.)<p>BigDecimal&#x27;s only lossless API is through strings. It would be nice to have native support for decimals at the parser level:<p><pre><code>  0d123.45 =&gt; BigDecimal(&quot;123.45&quot;)</code></pre>')
