Item(by='formerly_proven', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602451351, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24745831, text='Most better monitors and everything targeted at office use does not use PWM for backlight brightness control.<p>LCDs have poor blacks because they&#x27;re a filter put in front of a lamp, so to render black, they have to try and block as much light as possible, but our visual perception is logarithmic and adaptive, making them look quite bad. The workaround is to add bias light behind the monitor; it quite literally provides a bias for the eye to keep it inside a certain adaption range, which reduces the perceived poorness of dark tones. (It also is more ergonomic.).')
