Item(by='tannhaeuser', descendants=None, kids=[24868080], score=None, time=1603448881, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24867524, text='Backward-compat and syntax doesn&#x27;t have to be a problem. The `&lt;?php ...&gt;` processing instruction can stay as it is, only to be augmented with a new opt-in mechanism based on entity expansion (the fully context-aware templating mechanism built-in to SGML&#x2F;XML). Eg roughly<p><pre><code>    &lt;title&gt;bla&lt;&#x2F;&gt;\n    &lt;?php let x =\n      &quot;&lt;p&gt;whatever&lt;&#x2F;p&gt;&quot;?&gt;\n    &amp;x\n    &lt;div class=&quot;debug&quot;&gt;\n     &lt;![ RCDATA [\n       &amp;x\n     ]]&gt;\n    &lt;&#x2F;div&gt;\n</code></pre>\nwhere x is bound to markup checked against permissible elements in the first context (eg rejecting &lt;script&gt;) and escaped into &amp;lt;p&gt;whatever&amp;lt;p&gt; in the second. Rejecting script elements in user or other dynamic content as well as escaping comes for free with regular SGML parsing.')
