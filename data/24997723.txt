Item(by='mistermann', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604580158, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24993422, text='I sometimes wonder if there is an unseen fundamental problem (or many problems) hiding somewhere that we don&#x27;t realize exists, and as a result we end up attributing the cause of our problems to what may often be red herrings.<p>After all, many far more reasonable approaches (incremental improvements, <i>at least</i>) to many things are usually fairly obvious, and I suspect most people wouldn&#x27;t <i>really</i> be opposed to it (if one was to sit down and have a serious conversation about it that truly addresses their nuanced concerns)...and yet, decade after decade we seem to make little forward progress (or at least, &quot;not enough&quot; - see: BLM), while a collective <i>feeling</i> of confident, &quot;righteous&quot; frustration and rage with those &quot;on&quot; &quot;the other side&quot; (on both sides) builds.<p>The whole thing seems rather paradoxical, which to me is <i>suggestive</i> that we may indeed be missing something important in our system analysis.')
