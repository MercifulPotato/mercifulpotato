Write a blank in an equation to mean &quot;the thing that fills in this blank&quot;, ex. [_+1=2]=1. So<p><pre><code>  x^y = [x^y = _]\n  z^(1&#x2F;y) = [_^y = z]\n  log_x(z) = [x^_ = z]\n</code></pre>\nThe first four identities from the post are<p><pre><code>  [x^_ = x^y] = y\n  x^[x^_ = z] = z\n  [_^y = x^y] = x\n  [_^y = z]^y = z\n</code></pre>\nThe next two are (the nested [] confirm these are more complicated)<p><pre><code>  [[_^y = z]^_ = z] = y\n  [_^[x^_ = z] = z] = x\n</code></pre>\nGeneralizing<p><pre><code>  [f(x) = _] = f(x)\n  [f(_) = f(x)] = x\n  f([f(_) = x]) = x\n  [f(_, [f(x, _) = y]) = y] = x</code></pre>')
