Item(by='aidenn0', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605917519, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25165261, text='Many of the &quot;true RISC&quot; cpus that could dispatch 1 ALU instruction per clock cycle had instructions for accelerating division; it would do one round of division and could be called in a loop to implement a full divide.<p>Note that dividing by a constant does not need a divide instruction, so it&#x27;s only when dividing by a variable that division is needed.  Even on CISC machines of the time division instructions were so slow that programmers would go out of their way to avoid using them.')
