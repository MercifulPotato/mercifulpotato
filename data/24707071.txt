Item(by='dzudndlf', descendants=None, kids=[24707249], score=None, time=1602069073, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24706917, text='Although “pomelo” in Spanish translates (typically) to “grapefruit”, “pomelo” in English refers to another fruit, that is like a grapefruit.<p>Turns out English is surprisingly rich when it comes to describing citrus. The citrus family  hybridizes very easily, so every planted seed will give you a “new species” with a unique cultivar. English appears to have assigned more unique names to citrus than the other two languages I know (Italian and Spanish)')
