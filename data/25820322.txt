Item(by='remus', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610966055, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25814972, text='I don&#x27;t think it is that odd, it&#x27;s just a case of costs, scale and who you&#x27;re designing for.<p>A TOPS500 computer is gonna set you back a few million $, so you&#x27;re only going to have one of them and it&#x27;s economic to employ a team of well qualified people to write software and interface relatively directly with the hardware.<p>Now that same computing power can be purchased by ~anyone and most of those people are not specialists and have essentially no ability to write software for the platform, so (from a design perspective) it&#x27;s a waste of time designing an interface that allows them to do so.<p>If your goal is to have a small powerful platform aimed at people who want to run software on it directly that&#x27;s completely doable. Just see the raspberry pi and the raft of similar devices now available.')
