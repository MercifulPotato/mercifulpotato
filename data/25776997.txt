Item(by='mytailorisrich', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610636189, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25776125, text='Up&#x2F;downvote mechanisms always end up as agree&#x2F;disagree votes.<p>Moderation is not the same. It is not about agreeing but curating content that is not acceptable (off-topic, illegal, insulting).<p>Article quote:\n&quot;<i>In decentralised social media, I believe that ultimately it should be the users themselves who decide what is acceptable or not</i>&quot;<p>In my view that is only workable if that means users define the rules because, as said above, I think &#x27;voting&#x27; on individual piece of content always leads to echo chambers and to censoring dissenting views.<p>Of course this may be fine if within an online community focus on one topic or interest, but probably not if you want to foster open discussions and a plurality of views and opinions.<p>We can observe this right here on HN. On submissions that are prone to trigger strong opinions downvotes and flagging explode.')
