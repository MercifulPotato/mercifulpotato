Item(by='dragonwriter', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604191367, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24956348, text='&gt; Why a self argument on every function?<p>A self argument is on <i>methods</i> because then, from the inside, methods are identical to functions, rather than functions with additional implicit magic.<p>Also because it&#x27;s a function member of the class with exactly that argument pattern; calling instance.method(...) is just syntax sugar for ClassOfInstance.method(instance, ...).<p>This also makes Python handling of unbound methods a lot cleaner than, say, Ruby.')
