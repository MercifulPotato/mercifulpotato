Item(by='AnthonyMouse', descendants=None, kids=[25653931], score=None, time=1609896302, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25652088, text='&gt; The reason the ISM bands look so &quot;hot&quot; or busy on a spectrum analyzer is exactly because they&#x27;re a &quot;free for all.&quot; It&#x27;s a classic tragedy of the commons: everyone keeps &quot;shouting&quot; at max volume to be heard and there&#x27;s no coordination between operators and users of different services.<p>This would be a lot more believable if the unlicensed spectrum wasn&#x27;t already under low regulatory limits on power levels.<p>You can always come up with some kind of system that will extract a little more efficiency out of things, but the reason most things use maximum power is that &quot;maximum power&quot; is already low enough that you can barely provide decent coverage from an access point on one side of your apartment to a device on the other.<p>The reason the unlicensed spectrum is busy it that it&#x27;s <i>busy</i>. Because there isn&#x27;t enough of it.<p>And the reason licensed spectrum isn&#x27;t as busy is that incumbents buy more spectrum than they strictly need because it&#x27;s a mechanism of excluding competitors.')
