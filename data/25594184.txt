Item(by='londons_explore', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609432645, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25581276, text='&gt; Google, and other companies are welcome to use OpenStreetMap Data as long as they meet their obligations in the OpenStreetMap License.<p>But they do not make wide use of OSM data, because one of the terms of the license is attribution, of which there is none on any Google product I have seen.<p>I would guess they are big supporters of OSM as a hiring thing - by supporting an opensource project, you will get young engineers interested in mapping, some of which will then go work on Google Maps.<p>It also helps any antitrust cases if Google isn&#x27;t the only game in town, which was the case for many years.')
