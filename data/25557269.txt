Item(by='jlj', descendants=None, kids=[25557417], score=None, time=1609137867, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25556726, text='It is common for software development expenses to be capitalized and expensed over time (depreciated).<p>A lot of what shows up as intangibles is from M&amp;A activity. If the acquirer paid more than book value for a company, then all of the remainder goes into intangibles (goodwill).<p>The write-up argues that non-physical assets (software) show up as intangables (balance sheet) then argues that they are expenses (income statement). It doesn&#x27;t make sense.<p>I don&#x27;t follow what the article is arguing, and it seems to draw big and likely incorrect conclusions from loosely related accounting terms and data points.')
