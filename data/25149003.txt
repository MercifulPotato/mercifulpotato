Someone probably told me that every cell in my body has the same DNA. But no one shook me by the shoulders, saying how crazy that was.<p>The key thought of the article comes right at the top. The distance between the astonishing nature of reality and the dry tedious way it&#x27;s taught to people is present in all subjects, but seems greatest in biology. What seems to always be missing is what Chomsky calls a &#x27;willingness to be puzzled&#x27;.<p>In fact I think this ability to be puzzled or amazed is there to begin with, but is beaten out of us by the educational system.')
