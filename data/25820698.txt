Item(by='robenkleene', descendants=None, kids=[25822340, 25821120], score=None, time=1610969733, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25819486, text='I&#x27;d argue that the Mac developer community was hollowed out by mandatory Sandboxing in the Mac App Store, introduced in 2011, not by the iPhone (2007). Sketch, arguably the most successful third-party Mac-only app since OS X, was released in 2010, several years after the iPhone, but almost immediately before mandatory sandboxing.<p>I believe the reason sandboxing destroyed the Mac software ecosystem is because it&#x27;s too restrictive to support the kinds of productivity utilities that have historically thrived on the platform. And those apps are too small to handle promotion without Apple&#x27;s help, Apple&#x27;s help now being reserved for apps in the Mac App Store, which are by extension sandboxed.')
