Item(by='DanBC', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604931623, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25034899, text='The full title is &quot;Suicide in England since the COVID-19 pandemic -early figures from real-time surveillance&quot;, but that doesn&#x27;t fit the 80 character limit.<p>This report is from NCISH (the National confidential inquiry into suicide and self harm) which is a national organisation with responsibility for research into suicide.<p>They issue 4 conclusions:<p>&gt; (1) We have found no evidence of the large national rise in suicide post-lockdown that many feared;<p>&gt; (2) There  appears  to  have  been  no rise  in  suicide  post-lockdown,  at least  in  these  areas.  The  higher figures  in  2020  should  be  seen  in  the  context  of a rising  national  rate  and  maturing  real-time surveillance systems;<p>&gt; (3) There  are  several important caveats.  These  are  early  figures  and  could  change  over  time  or  with the inclusion of more areas. We cannot rule out higher rates in some local areas or population sub-groups, especially as the effect of COVID-19 itself has varied between communities;<p>&gt; (4) In particular, it is too soon to examine  the full long-term impact of economic adversity on mental health and suicide')
