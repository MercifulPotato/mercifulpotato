Item(by='spijdar', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606145925, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25187418, text='Intel Macs have never had typical BIOSes or standards compliant UEFI, to say nothing of their device drivers.<p>Without Apple providing a HAL FLL for the windows executive and device drivers along with a special bootloader shim (the marketing guys call this package &quot;Bootcamp&quot;), Windows would not work on Intel Macs. Microsoft or some third party would have to reverse engineer the device drivers, and they&#x27;re definitely not making that investment.<p>Not to mention more basically that Apple has to sign the NT kernel for it to load on Macs without disabling the equivalent of SecureBoot.')
