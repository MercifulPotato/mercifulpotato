Item(by='dagw', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603359366, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24855798, text='<i>Did anyone ever take Microsoft&#x27;s &quot;Microsoft Certified Application Developer&quot; or &quot;Microsoft Certified Solutions Architect&quot; titles seriously?</i><p>Most developers generally didn&#x27;t, but certainly back in the early 2000s I heard lots of managers and other people responsible for hiring being impressed by it. To be blunt, if you where a mediocre developer with a mediocre resume, it was for a while a pretty efficient way to stand out among other mediocre developers.<p>Microsoft also used to offer some nice goodies and discounts to Microsoft Certified Professionals that could make it worth having, financially, if you where a freelance developer or worked for a smaller company.')
