There exist cryptographic schemes where tokens can be exchanged peer-to-peer in a way where the issuing bank can&#x27;t identify participants <i>until</i> tokens are double spent. Double spends can then be charged against the account of the double spender when the recipient of a transaction deposits the tokens. These schemes are far more efficient, natural progression to our current fiat monetary system than Bitcoin or any of the other &quot;crypto currencies&quot;.<p>The only reason we don&#x27;t have this tech today is there are no incentives for our governments or banks to maintain the  anonymity of cash.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;archive.org&#x2F;details&#x2F;CryptographyOfAnonymousElectronicCash" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;archive.org&#x2F;details&#x2F;CryptographyOfAnonymousElectroni...</a>')
