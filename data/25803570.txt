Item(by='vaduz', descendants=None, kids=[25804081], score=None, time=1610817186, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25803503, text='First distrbuted computing fallacy is &quot;Network is reliable&quot;.<p>Detecting if you can deliver a packet in advance is not exactly a solved problem - network detection APIs at best inform you if the PHY&#x2F;MAC layer for a given connection is up, not if any given packet you generate can be routed out - and fail to detect a number of rather common edge cases (all traffic being routed over VPN on WiFi, without mobile fallback, for instance, or mobile connection <i>appearing</i> to be up, but not transferring data) - therefore ultimately the solution is to try, and try again, with proper backoff to prevent overwhelming your own infrastructure.<p>In this case the latter appears to have been missing.')
