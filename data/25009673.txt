Item(by='lcall', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604685701, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25005567, text='FWIW here is how I replaced scp commands in my scripts with sftp.  (If there is a better way of course I am interested.)<p>OLD:<p>scp $FILE &quot;$USER_AT_HOST:&#x2F;home&#x2F;username&#x2F;dir1&#x2F;&quot;<p>NEW (a <i>single</i> embedded newline or Enter right after the word &quot;progress&quot; seemed required, though not correctly displayed here, to enable a visual progress indicator; maybe some kind of \\n could work instead but didn&#x27;t immediately for me and this was easy enough in the end):<p>echo &quot;progress<p>put $FILE&quot;|sftp -f -p -N -b - &quot;$USER_AT_HOST:&#x2F;home&#x2F;username&#x2F;dir1&#x2F;&quot;<p>OR (single line, without progress indicator):<p>echo &quot;put $FILE&quot;|nice sftp -f -p -N -b - &quot;$USER_AT_HOST:&#x2F;home&#x2F;username&#x2F;dir1&#x2F;&quot;<p>Edit:  I suppose this could be carefully put in a script called &quot;scp&quot; that takes parameters, or aliased, something like others here have done with rsync options (^F here for the word &quot;progress&quot; to see some).')
