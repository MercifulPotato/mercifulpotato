It asserts most definitely that it is not.  The story was about how the claim went viral.  The story was introduced in 1968 by the New York Times picking up on the NEJM article.<p>It&#x27;s an amusing tale, but rather unsettling how easily fooled we can be. A number of tv news programs like 20&#x2F;20 ran cover stories about it.  To hear it was all bunk is a sobering reminder to take what we read with a grain of salt.  Even much of the hysteria surrounding coronavirus and the like.<p>I don&#x27;t have an answer to this, but these publications take a serious long term credibility hit with stories like these.
