You can create seperate profiles to use one for direct access and one only for your VPN connection. I would even recommend using different browser profiles for specific tasks. Like &quot;shopping&quot; for example.<p>Create Firefox Profile:\n  firefox -ProfileManager<p>Use Firefox Profile:\n  firefox --no-remote -private -P profile-name<p>Create &amp; Use Chrome Profile:\n  chromium --incognito --user-data-dir=location&#x2F;profile-name<p>On top of seperate browser profiles (at least in your VPN profiles), you need to do browser hardening and use something like uBlock&#x2F;uMatrix to block everything by default. [1] I do not recommend using regular Google Chrome for VPN at all. Use something like Ungoogled-Chrome. [2] or similar security browser instead.<p>Also it is highly recommented if you use Linux&#x2F;Unix OS, to properly firewall your VPN connection. So for example if your VPN connection suddenly stops working, it will not leak out any data. There is many tutorials on how to do this.
