Item(by='mattmcknight', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606663045, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25245443, text='Whatever got us the Internet was worth it. To generalize, it&#x27;s fine to have a variety of components, as long as the interface between them is roughly standardized, and the innovation can come in the components. That we can have a Playstation and an iPhone use the same network is a good thing, and they can do whatever they want with the rest of the stack.<p>A computer pre-internet felt a lot more like an island. I had an Amiga, but I wasn&#x27;t aware of 90% of the stuff that was out there, and could barely afford to buy a compiler.')
