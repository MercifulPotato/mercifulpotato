Item(by='aksss', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610649803, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25779568, text='“Fight like hell” is very common language in politics.<p>A lesson <i>all</i> politicians need to take to heart is that there are people running around in our population who take everything <i>very</i> literally and <i>very</i> seriously. The hyperbole of political rhetoric needs to adapt to the deinstitutionalization that is modern society. But an accounting of politicians using this kind of hyperbole would show no favor to either party - in fact IME more often than not it’s the party not in power at any given moment that uses the most impassioned rhetoric.<p>We can scold Trump for using playing with matches in a dry forest, but let’s not pretend this is uniquely inciting language. That standard would convict politicians of all stripes.')
