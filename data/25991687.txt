Item(by='cratermoon', descendants=None, kids=[25991982], score=None, time=1612204269, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25990518, text='There&#x27;s a Middle Way.<p>- drop what you are doing and focus on the person. In a face-to-face situation, body language matters.<p>- figure out if it&#x27;s something you can do quickly. Because you&#x27;ve already lost your focus on whatever you were doing, it&#x27;s efficient to just go ahead and knock out small tasks.<p>- if you can&#x27;t do it quickly, either because it&#x27;s a lot of work or it takes time to explain what the problem is, you must negotiate and schedule the task for later, or have a meeting.  Interpersonal skills are important: be sure the person goes away feeling like you care as much about their problem as they do.<p>- follow up with your scheduling.<p>Yes, frequent interruptions are bad for focus. Part of becoming the sort of person people feel comfortable going to is to help people know when it&#x27;s OK to interrupt you and when they should wait for another time, or communicate in a way that is less disruptive. There will always be that sort of person who doesn&#x27;t &quot;get&quot; it, and will interrupt you when they want to.')
