Item(by='belval', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604672470, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25007517, text='The Wikipedia article seem to give weight to actually cutting hands for not filling quota, but I guess you could argue that his &quot;system&quot; was not how he actually acted.<p>&quot;Columbus&#x27;s forced labour system was described by his son Ferdinand: &quot;In the Cibao, where the gold mines were, every person of fourteen years of age or upward was to pay a large hawk&#x27;s bell of gold dust; all others were each to pay twenty-five pounds of cotton. Whenever an Indian delivered his tribute, he was to receive a brass or copper token which he must wear about his neck as proof that he had made his payment; any Indian found without such a token was to be punished.&quot; The monarchs, who suggested the tokens, called for a light punishment, but any Indian found without a copper token had their hands cut off, which was a likely death sentence.[118] Since there was no abundance of gold on the island, the natives had no chance of meeting Columbus&#x27; quota and thousands are reported to have committed suicide.&quot;<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Christopher_Columbus" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Christopher_Columbus</a>')
