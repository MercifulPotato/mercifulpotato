Item(by='Zak', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603251535, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24834701, text='I don&#x27;t find these ideas really contrast as much as they initially appear. Curtis advocates optimizing for some absolute best when shopping for stuff (which as a hobbyist product reviewer I find a bit absurd; few products are one-dimensional enough for that). Marlinspike talks about the value of unplanned life experiences.<p>There&#x27;s a middle way for a lot of product categories: find an enthusiast community and get their recommendation for a low-cost and beginner-friendly product. It&#x27;s often listed in their FAQ. The gap between enthusiast-grade and mass-market products in some categories is huge, while the gap in price may not be.')
