Item(by='TomVDB', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604510390, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24990195, text='The RTL-SDR is only an RF front-end that brings down the desired RF signal to a baseband frequency, followed by an quadrature AD converter, followed by a USB interface.<p>All the DSP operations are already done by the Raspberry Pi.<p><a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;aaronscher.com&#x2F;wireless_com_SDR&#x2F;rtl_sdr_info.html" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;aaronscher.com&#x2F;wireless_com_SDR&#x2F;rtl_sdr_info.html</a><p>In the diagram above, it shows “DSP” but that’s just two basic multiplications and a low pass filter. It doesn’t do any heavy lifting.<p>If you want to get rid of the RTL-SDR, you’d need that analog RF block and the AD converter inside the Pi. That doesn’t make a lot of sense of a general purpose SOC.')
