Item(by='AnIdiotOnTheNet', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602598325, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24765616, text='Usually when we say something &quot;cratered&quot; we mean that it fell rapidly, as one might imagine a meteor would fall from space and impact the earth with enough force to produce a crater.<p>It doesn&#x27;t get used very often in my experience.<p>As mentioned in another post, &quot;crater up to&quot; is a bizarre formation of words. &quot;Rents fall by up to 31%&quot; is perfectly understandable phrasing, but replacing &quot;fall&quot; with &quot;crater&quot; makes it sound very strange.')
