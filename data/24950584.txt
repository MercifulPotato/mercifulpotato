Item(by='pron', descendants=None, kids=[24953642, 24952380], score=None, time=1604143815, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24947797, text='Java is at a stage where it&#x27;s clear it will continue to be a popular platform and language for decades to come. It can afford to do &quot;the right thing&quot; because the question it&#x27;s facing is not how to attract more developers now, but how to avoid mistakes its numerous developers will pay for, and build solid foundations for many years to come. It&#x27;s a common difference between young and established languages, and, naturally, Java made its own fair share of design mistakes when it was young.<p>Of course, there are technical differences that make certain design choices easier or harder in different languages. As aardvark179 mentions above, Java doesn&#x27;t have pointers into the stack, and intermediate FFI frames are extremely rare (also due to the platform&#x27;s established and large ecosystem).<p>(I&#x27;m the technical lead for OpenJDK&#x27;s Project Loom)')
