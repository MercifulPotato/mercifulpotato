Item(by='jerf', descendants=None, kids=[25907577, 25906982, 25906487], score=None, time=1611597510, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25906273, text='Coronavirus will naturally evolve into the attraction basin for viruses of its type, which is the common cold: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.sacbee.com&#x2F;news&#x2F;coronavirus&#x2F;article248455480.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.sacbee.com&#x2F;news&#x2F;coronavirus&#x2F;article248455480.htm...</a><p>It&#x27;s already well on its way, from what I can gather.<p>One frustrating aspect of the reporting for me is people talking about &quot;more infectious&quot; variants without describing whether or not they are more or less <i>dangerous</i>. In general, the virus putting more work into being infectious is <i>good news</i>; it generally comes at the cost of being less dangerous in other ways as it hyper-optimizes into replication rather than anything else. We should expect and want to hear about &quot;more infectious&quot; variants, which double as natural vaccines to some extent against other less infectious, more dangerous strains still using the same external protiens.')
