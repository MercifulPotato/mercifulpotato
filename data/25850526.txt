Item(by='jkaptur', descendants=None, kids=[25850896, 25850864, 25851213], score=None, time=1611169698, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25850234, text='The binary distinction between &quot;cost centers&quot; and &quot;profit centers&quot; has always seemed arbitrary to me (especially since, as an engineer, I&#x27;ve been in both without my work being substantially different).<p>To be frank, it seems like an organizational way to say &quot;we don&#x27;t find this work to be valuable or interesting, and we&#x27;d like to do the bare minimum of it - in fact, we&#x27;d like to unleash smart people to explore new frontiers of just how minimal the bare minimum could possibly be.&quot;<p>It seems like this leads to incredibly predictable problems: brain drain, demoralized workers, the bare minimum being aimed for and not actually being achieved, etc.<p>I feel like a better organization has no &quot;cost centers&quot; - every single role at the company contributes to the mission and to the bottom line. If they didn&#x27;t, that position wouldn&#x27;t exist.<p>What am I missing?')
