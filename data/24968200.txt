Item(by='ido', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604325827, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24968113, text='The market does not on itself account for externalities: it can be better for the individual to drive a car even if it&#x27;s better for all the road&#x27;s users collectively if as many people as possible took the bus or rode a bike (less congestion).<p>Likewise your personal air pollution doesn&#x27;t make a perceptible difference but if everyone is driving cleaner vehicles it benefits everyone collectively with cleaner air (this was very noticable when e.g. Delhi required auto-rickshaws to convert to natural gas).')
