Item(by='spijdar', descendants=None, kids=[25897064], score=None, time=1611523300, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25894717, text='What&#x27;s more, there were 64 bit <i>laptops</i> in the mid 90s. Not particularly mobile ones, but if you could tolerate a 7 pound behemoth, you could get a 200 MHz 64 bit SPARC processor with 512MB of RAM in &#x27;96&#x2F;97. The AlphaBook with an Alpha processor that was announced in &#x27;95 comes even earlier. <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.vaxbarn.com&#x2F;index.php&#x2F;other-bits&#x2F;555-tadpole-alphabook1" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.vaxbarn.com&#x2F;index.php&#x2F;other-bits&#x2F;555-tadpole-alp...</a><p>One thing that always strikes me about workstations and (especially?) these laptops is that, while the CPUs themselves weren&#x27;t always much faster than competing Intel ones, they always seemed to have much better I&#x2F;O options. The SCSI drives and controllers seemed to get much better throughput with lower CPU utilization, which pays dividends to how the overall system performs.<p>A well designed workstation is more than just the sum of its parts. While these workstations&#x2F;servers were arguably overpriced and it&#x27;s not surprising they failed to the cheaper Wintel machines, they just seemed more well rounded...')
