HTTPS is a prime example of this. It effectively &quot;locks&quot; the data channel or pipe from the source (FB&#x2F;Instagram&#x2F;Google servers) to your viewing device (screen, and in a lot of cases, locked-down web-browser) making it effectively a dumb terminal. If MITM-type techologies were allowed to exist <i>safely</i> and controlled by the user, we could be seeing a proliferation of an ecosystem geared around breaking and integrating-with the walled-gardens. I&#x27;d go out on a limb and say that HTTPS is one of <i>the biggest</i> hurdles we face when it comes to keeping the web open. With it firmly in-place, we are forced to <i>beg</i> for &quot;scraps&quot; in terms of these walled-gardens giving us API&#x2F;Integration points.<p>Edit. Grammar.')
