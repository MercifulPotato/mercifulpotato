Item(by='moufestaphio', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609350700, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25583437, text='Chinese characters (In Japan they referred to as Kanji),\nare ideographic rather than phonetic.<p>This means they represent an idea (or concept?) instead of a sound. \nThe meaning is completely divorced from the pronunciation.\nIn Japanese each character usually has at least two &#x27;readings&#x27; for pronunciation onyomi (chinese-origin reading) and kunyomi (Japanese reading).<p>But often have even more than that, and specifically with peoples names the readings some times feel completely arbitrary.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Kanji#Readings" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Kanji#Readings</a><p>Basically, there are multiple ways to get the same &#x27;sound&#x27; from different kanji, or get a different &#x27;sound&#x27; the same kanji.')
