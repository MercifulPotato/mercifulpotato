Item(by='Arnavion', descendants=None, kids=[25224119], score=None, time=1606423424, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25223291, text='The underlying principle of submitting a job to be done and letting the kernel do it (submitting a read() and waiting for it to complete), as opposed to the original model of waiting for the kernel when it&#x27;s ready to let you do the job (waiting for an fd to become readable so you can call read() on it) is the same as the completion-based model of Windows async I&#x2F;O (and I think BSD&#x27;s kqueue too?) as opposed to the readiness-based model of epoll.<p>The part of where this submission and completion information involves a ring buffer mapped to kernel space is unique to Linux, I believe.')
