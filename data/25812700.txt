Item(by='granzymes', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610904663, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25812563, text='Here is what appeared on the ballot, courtesy of the California Attorney General:<p>Exempts App-Based Transportation and Delivery Companies from Providing Employee Benefits to Certain Drivers. Initiative Statute.<p>* Classifies drivers for app-based transportation (rideshare) and delivery companies as “independent contractors,” not “employees,” unless company: sets drivers’ hours, requires acceptance of specific ride and delivery requests, or restricts working for other companies.<p>* Independent contractors are not covered by various state employment laws—including minimum wage, overtime, unemployment insurance, and workers’ compensation.<p>* Instead, independent-contractor drivers would be entitled to other compensation—including minimum earnings, healthcare subsidies, and vehicle insurance.<p>* Restricts certain local regulation of app-based drivers.<p>* Criminalizes impersonation of drivers.')
