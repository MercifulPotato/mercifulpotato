Item(by='fasterthanlime', descendants=None, kids=[25616071], score=None, time=1609611925, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25614528, text='I&#x27;d argue the exact opposite. Languages that <i>don&#x27;t</i> have a concept of ownership, and a borrow checker, and don&#x27;t explicitly say if they want ownership, a reference, or a mutable reference, force you to keep all of these details in your head.<p>Here, if I have a `&amp;T` and I try to call a function that has a `&amp;mut T`, the compiler will tell me that&#x27;s not gonna work - and then I can pick whether I want <i>my</i> function to take a `&amp;mut T`, or if I want to make a clone and modify <i>that</i>, etc.<p>There&#x27;s a learning curve, it&#x27;s a set of habits to adopt, but once you embrace it it&#x27;s really hard to go back to languages that don&#x27;t have it! (See the rest of the comments for testimonials)')
