Item(by='tempestn', descendants=None, kids=[25667179], score=None, time=1609989616, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25666802, text='I&#x27;d argue that the problem with this analogy is that a bird flying overhead is something we know is possible and indeed common. According to all scientific knowledge and evidence, resurrection is not possible. So absent significant evidence, it&#x27;s not logical to believe that this happened. We also know that religion has historically provided numerous benefits to societies, allowing for trust, cooperation, and trade in societies that predate effective government. So it is logical to assume that such belief systems would develop, and that societies that developed them would prevail over those that didn&#x27;t, even in the absence of supernatural evidence.<p>Given that religion contradicts what we know about the physical world, and that its existence is explained without reliance on the supernatural, it&#x27;s not logical to believe its supernatural claims.')
