Item(by='imgabe', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603173026, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24833789, text='Something can&#x27;t simultaneously have inelastic demand and also be a luxury good. Those things are kind of opposite.<p>Demand for taxis has dropped a lot because of Uber. Uber also gets passengers who would have otherwise not made the trip, taken a bus, or driven drunk. If Uber is no longer an option they&#x27;ll go back to those options,not to taxis.<p>&gt; The only difference will be in the price of rides - for what is largely a luxury good.<p>The price of rides makes a big difference and hugely effects demand. I don&#x27;t know why you&#x27;re treating it like it doesn&#x27;t matter. Lower prices open the service up to more people. Indoor plumbing was once a luxury good. Generally progress means turning luxuries into commodities, not the opposite.')
