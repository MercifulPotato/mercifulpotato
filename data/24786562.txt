In my second year of med school (1971) half the day was spent in pathology lecture&#x2F;lab. I developed swelling in my neck lymph nodes and went to student health. In the waiting room I reviewed my life to that point, knowing without any doubt that I had Hodgkin&#x27;s lymphoma. The doctor took my history, examined me, and ordered a Monospot test. It was positive. I started my second life that day.')
