Item(by='johncolanduoni', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611917954, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25955787, text='The major architectures &amp; ABIs are all capable of producing and loading fully position independent code. The performance impact varies; for x86_64 and aarch64 instruction pointer relative addressing reduces the number of relocations that are needed by quite a lot.<p>Languages that support subclassing and inheritance don’t represent any special hazard to relocation; ultimately these are built out of data and function symbols that need to be supported even if you’re only compiling C.')
