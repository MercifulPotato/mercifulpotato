Item(by='bitshiftfaced', descendants=None, kids=[25970560], score=None, time=1612022325, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25968432, text='&gt; For example, using &quot;appeal to authority&quot;, it is logically true that just because a group of Fields Medalists think your proof is wrong, does not necessarily mean that your proof is wrong. But in practice, there is a very high chance you are in the wrong.<p>The proof is wrong not because the status of those Field Medalists but instead because of the content of their argument. That&#x27;s all that the appeal to authority is saying.<p>I&#x27;d agree that it&#x27;s a mistake to relate the existence of a logical fallacies to the probability of a statement being correct. Instead, it only gives information about whether the reasoning of the statement is on solid ground.')
