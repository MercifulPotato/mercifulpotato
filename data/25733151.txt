Item(by='quotemstr', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610388779, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25732739, text='It&#x27;s not that the is-ought gap is unpopular: it&#x27;s that the gap doesn&#x27;t even make it into the debate. We&#x27;re dealing with an epistemic regime in which it&#x27;s acceptable to engineer desired &quot;ought&quot; outcomes by controlling who gets to make statements about what &quot;is&quot;. That is, is no &quot;is&quot; independent from power dynamics, oppressor and oppressed. &quot;Is&quot;, in this model, is just another form of narrative that serves the powerful.<p>In this world, you should expect downvotes for arguing that science informs us about objective reality, because people of a certain mindset see scientific results that contradict their worldview as just another form of oppressive discourse intended to subjugate the powerless. To them, science is just one of many equal and arbitrary forms of rhetoric and power gaming.')
