Item(by='philipkglass', descendants=None, kids=[24815222], score=None, time=1602961958, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24811980, text='He&#x27;s referring to beryllium-10, which is mostly generated by cosmic ray interactions with nitrogen and oxygen in the atmosphere. The Be-10 subsequently washes out of the atmosphere with precipitation and tends to bind in surface soils. It has a half life of 1.39 million years so it is useful for dating various geological phenomena.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Beryllium-10" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Beryllium-10</a><p>You can see how this beryllium isotope was used in the original paper &quot;Climate controls on erosion in tectonically active landscapes&quot; (linked by sradman in comment <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=24812181" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=24812181</a>) within the Materials and Methods section of the paper.')
