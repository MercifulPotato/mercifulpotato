Item(by='tomjakubowski', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606243227, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25201193, text='Short, incomplete list of reasons I prefer Rust, speaking from about five years experience with C++:<p>* Real pattern matching and sum types. std::variant doesn&#x27;t cut the mustard<p>* Traits and derivable impls, rather than obscure operator overloads with fussy cv-qualified signatures<p>* No iterator invalidation bugs (among the worst C++ issues to debug, IME); no inheritance or need to care about &quot;virtual destructors&quot;; no &quot;object slicing&quot;; no crazy interactions between overloads and templates which require an IDE to determine which function you are actually calling<p>* Way better compiler error messages than clang or gcc<p>Each of those &quot;no&quot;s is something I&#x27;ve spent days if not weeks of my C++ career debugging.')
