The average bank robbery is a quiet affair conducted with a slipped note and the implication of a gun.<p>I interviewed a family friend who had then recently retired from chasing bank robbers in the FBI for a school project a decade and change ago. The biggest take-a-way was his assertion that, at least at that time, anyone could get away with robbing a bank once. It&#x27;s not getting away with the crime itself that&#x27;s hard: it&#x27;s not leaving a pattern on the next one.')
