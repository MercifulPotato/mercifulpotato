Item(by='yodelshady', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602672849, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24774411, text='I&#x27;m a bit sceptical - not rubbishing, but sceptical - of the castle walls analogy.<p>If an attacker gets through your walls (network access), why do you think you&#x27;d stop them at the keep?<p>I think castles have something of an answer to that question, because physical static defence is just more constrained than computational static defence - the ratio of effort of building a wall : effort to breach is poor compared to even complex passwords.<p>Your REAL physical defence is(are?) your soldiers, and the wall mainly aids their co-ordination: small attacks aren&#x27;t worth attention, big attacks are SLOW AND VISIBLE. Does infosec have a similar wall to that? Certainly, when attackers breached castle walls via backdoors, it was normally game over, keep or no.')
