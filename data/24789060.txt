Item(by='analog31', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602772604, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24786721, text='In addition to what&#x27;s been mentioned, printing has much tighter tolerance requirements, e.g., to distinguish between &quot;h&quot; and &quot;b&quot; at a small font size. It actually has to be a very good spatial representation of the design. And how cheap printers actually accomplish this with crude bits of plastic and stamped metal is pretty amazing.<p>On the other hand, 3d printed objects may only need to satisfy overall mechanical needs, or be suggestive of the shape that they model, to serve a purpose. Most 3d printing that I&#x27;ve seen needs a bit of hand tooling at the end, to really be useful.')
