Item(by='TameAntelope', descendants=None, kids=[25443265, 25446435], score=None, time=1608129915, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25441529, text='I try very hard (and still often fail) to put myself in the shoes of people who are meaningfully not like me, and one thing that&#x27;s come from that is understanding how motivation doesn&#x27;t always track equally for everyone (and trying to come to terms with the fact that this might be okay).<p>Finding things that give enjoyment and deliberately distract from the things going on in a person&#x27;s life has potentially immense value, depending on what those things going on in their life are.<p>The implicit agreement made here is that both parties know what you&#x27;re saying, and both parties agree to not talk about it -- for the seller, because it&#x27;s an ethical quandary, for the buyer because they don&#x27;t want to have to confront the things in their life they&#x27;re trying to ignore.<p>It doesn&#x27;t always have to be so sinister of course (maybe I just want to relax and am otherwise keeping my responsibilities), but it can be sinister, which I think is what you&#x27;re getting at.  My argument is that both parties know this, and it may be the whole point.')
