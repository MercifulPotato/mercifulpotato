The responsibilities of retail employees have grown vastly in the last 50 years. There&#x27;s more product, with a greater diversity of handling requirements, which need to be stocked across larger stores later in the day (as closing times slowly approach midnight or later). That&#x27;s for supermarkets (which are increasingly part of larger big box stores). If you work checkout at a place that sells anything with a microprocessor, be prepared for weeks of onboarding as you learn enough about your products to actually sell to and support customers, across a stupidly wide assortment that used to be spread across several departments.<p>Let&#x27;s not get started on discussing the interpersonal communication skills needed to survive Thanksgiving&#x2F;Black Friday.<p>And for all this, you&#x27;re getting paid less than Joe W. Cash Register got way back when.')
