Item(by='ludocode', descendants=None, kids=[24862053, 24865603], score=None, time=1603376698, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24857983, text='Google doesn&#x27;t just use the search term. It also uses your search and browsing history. This is why if you&#x27;re a programmer, you&#x27;re more likely to get results from StackOverflow because your search is more likely to be about programming. It&#x27;s one reason why people can get better search results from Google than, say, DuckDuckGo.<p>If you&#x27;ve been watching lots of conspiracy YouTube videos and reading lots of conspiracy blogs, Google is more likely to give you conspiracy search results because it thinks that&#x27;s what you want to see. This is called a filter bubble [1], and it&#x27;s how social media as well as search amplify questionable content.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Filter_bubble" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Filter_bubble</a>')
