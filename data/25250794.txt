Item(by='throwaway0a5e', descendants=None, kids=[25251120], score=None, time=1606711241, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25250491, text='West coast cities homelessness problems are indicative of nothing more than the specific economic and political realities in those cities.  It is folly to extrapolate that to larger macroeconomic conditions.<p>While some specific cities may have their heads up their butts with regard to public policy resulting in more visible homeless the living conditions of the &quot;almost homeless&quot; and working poor have very much improved since the early 1970s (as far back as my knowledge goes).<p>You can say what you want about the blue collar middle class (off-shoring and student debt have hit them hard over the past decades) but things have very much been improving up for the poor as a whole even though some poor people are unlucky enough to live in backwards places where things have gotten worse locally.')
