Part of what has been amazing about the web is that the target remains the same. There is the DOM. Everyone is trying different ways to build &amp; update the DOM.<p>Agreed that there are better alternatives than a lot of what is out there. We seem to be in a mass consolidation, focusing around a couple very popular systems. I am glad to see folks like Github presenting some of the better alternatives, such as their Catalyst tools[1] which speed up (developer-wise &amp; page-wise (via &quot;Actions&quot;) both) &amp; give some patterns for building WebComponents.<p>The web has been unimaginably stable a platform for building things, has retained it&#x27;s spirit while allowing hundreds of different architectures for how things get built. Yes, we can make a mess of our architectures. Yes, humanity can over-consume resources. But we can also, often, do it right, and we can learn &amp; evolve, as we have done so, over the past 30 years we&#x27;ve had with the web.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.github.io&#x2F;catalyst&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.github.io&#x2F;catalyst&#x2F;</a>')
