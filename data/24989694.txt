Item(by='wenc', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604502719, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24988098, text='I read the (quite comprehensive) wiki article [1] to get some perspectives.<p>I think we can all agree that excessive light is undesirable, but there&#x27;s some subjectivity in determining what constitutes &quot;excessive&quot; lighting. I grew up in a highly urbanized environment (I confess I&#x27;m not a nature person), and lighting was very much desirable for safety and for promoting after-hours activity and therefore has a bearing on the economy as well. In northern parts of the world, days get shorter in the winter so artificial lighting can help alleviate a feeling of gloom for some. Some people are uplifted by Christmas lights or by the warm orange glow of a Parisian street lamp, for instance.<p>It sounds like one of those things where different groups need to work together toward a negotiated compromise -- there might not exist an ironclad objective answer as to what&#x27;s best.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Light_pollution" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Light_pollution</a>')
