Item(by='cxcorp', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609940948, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25656580, text='It was pretty easy to get up and running with Docker Compose:<p><pre><code>  version: &#x27;3&#x27;\n\n  services:\n      mysql:\n          image: mysql:8\n          environment:\n              MYSQL_ROOT_PASSWORD: root\n              MYSQL_DATABASE: matomo\n      matomo:\n          image: matomo:4\n          ports:\n              - 4000:80\n</code></pre>\nThis lets me in at localhost:4000 and I just enter &quot;mysql&quot; as the DB host, &quot;root&quot; as the username &amp; password and &quot;matomo&quot; as the database name, and it&#x27;s basically done.<p>Of course, I probably have to point it out or someone else will, that it&#x27;s a bad idea to be using the MySQL root user, instead of creating a user with the rights that Matomo needs: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;matomo.org&#x2F;faq&#x2F;how-to-install&#x2F;faq_23484&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;matomo.org&#x2F;faq&#x2F;how-to-install&#x2F;faq_23484&#x2F;</a>')
