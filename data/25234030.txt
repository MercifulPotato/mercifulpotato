Item(by='generalizations', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606524297, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25232924, text='What I&#x27;ve run into as well is free software, where the source is available but the process of building it into something usable would take &quot;months&quot; of work, and the internal toolchain (old software versions, patched packages, etc) is only vaguely documented.<p>Effectively, it&#x27;s a &#x27;moat&#x27; around the business, and allows the business to get the best of both worlds: association with the open source community, and effectively proprietary software.<p>The actual business plan can then treat the software as an exclusive asset (not in a technical, legal, sense, but <i>de facto</i> for sure).<p>I&#x27;m not saying it&#x27;s a bad model; it&#x27;s probably one of the few that can provide a reliable income, and it gives outside developers a &#x27;fighting chance&#x27; at accessing the value of the software.')
