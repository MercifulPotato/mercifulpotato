That they should is a favorite fallacy of schoolteachers. The written and spoken versions of a language are slightly different dialects; what works on the page is often less than optimal to the ear, and the tricks and colloquialisms of conversation are out of place in most written contexts.<p>I shudder to think what this electronic nanny would make of the prose of Nabokov or Poe.
