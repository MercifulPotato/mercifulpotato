Item(by='prepend', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1612186016, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25987854, text='Thanks for the correction, I didn’t know DCA was meant for lump sums. That makes it really dumb, I think, as it assumes down market. This whole time I thought DCA was a reflection of constantly investing chunks over time as money comes available, a good idea I think.<p>The Wikipedia article [0] has quite a few links to studies showing this is worse than lump sum.<p>I’m trying to think of any scenarios where this would be smart, but they all seem to assume market knowledge and just adjusting a lump sum payment. Even if you “knew” it was a down market and didn’t know when it would pick up it is bad because you would miss the biggest upswings at the bottom of the bear.<p>I think this is just one of those platitudes that retail advisors give people because it sounds good, but actually isn’t.<p>[0] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Dollar_cost_averaging" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Dollar_cost_averaging</a>')
