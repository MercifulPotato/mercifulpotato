Item(by='vanusa', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1601921272, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24687589, text='The point is: having another fundamental class of postures available - in this case &quot;lying&quot; (in addition to sitting and standing) - increases our flexibility (and potential for respite) by approximately 33 percent.<p>For people suffering from severe RSI -- especially those for whom essentially <i>any</i> amount of sitting in one class (say &quot;sitting&quot;), even in difference positions within that class -- becomes unbearable for more than an hour or so -- then this 33 percent can be of significant impact.<p>Also - for some people standing just doesn&#x27;t work (for more than very short intervals).  So in fact the lying option provides significantly more respite potential (50 percent).')
