Item(by='atombender', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606977930, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25283829, text='One of the fascinating observations in Carlota Perez&#x27;s &quot;Technological Revolutions and Financial Capital: The Dynamics of Bubbles and Golden Ages&quot; [1] is that outdated technological industries continued to provide superior returns even after they&#x27;d been supplanted. You&#x27;d have more money today if you&#x27;d invested in Exxon in the mid 1970s than if you&#x27;d put the money in IBM, even though Exxon has slipped from the top 10 to something like 40th place in the S&amp;P. Meanwhile, railroads peaked at 60% of the US stock market around 1860, shrinking to less 2%, yet continued to beat the market for the next 120 years.
