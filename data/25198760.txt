Item(by='tga', descendants=None, kids=[25199056], score=None, time=1606228646, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25198590, text='This is the great part about Zigbee&#x2F;BLE&#x2F;433MHz devices, they do _not_ go on your wifi (and the internet), but instead talk to a dedicated gateway. As long as you find a way to talk to them, you can mix and match sensors from the shadiest of vendors.<p>You can ignore the gateways offered by each manufacturer and instead go with something (more) open like zigbee2mqtt or deConz.<p>Zigbee:\n<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.zigbee2mqtt.io" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.zigbee2mqtt.io</a>\n<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.phoscon.de&#x2F;en&#x2F;conbee2&#x2F;software" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.phoscon.de&#x2F;en&#x2F;conbee2&#x2F;software</a><p>BLE:\nRPi or ESP32 \nrunning <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;esphome.io" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;esphome.io</a><p>433MHz:\n<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;sonoff.tech&#x2F;product&#x2F;accessories&#x2F;433-rf-bridge" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;sonoff.tech&#x2F;product&#x2F;accessories&#x2F;433-rf-bridge</a>\nrunning <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;esphome.io" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;esphome.io</a> or <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;tasmota.github.io" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;tasmota.github.io</a><p>Of course, this way you lose the (more or less) polished apps that come with each system and need to set up something like Home Assistant or openHAB for control and automation.')
