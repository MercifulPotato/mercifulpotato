Item(by='jacquesm', descendants=None, kids=[25529294], score=None, time=1608828845, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25529039, text='So, the takeaways for me are:<p>- Teachers can not give you motivation but they can sure kill it.<p>- An app that keeps people motivated is better than nothing at all.<p>- Horses for courses (not everybody is the same, and equally affluent, what works for you may not work for someone else) and if people are on a budget and they find a way that can make them enjoy what they are doing without breaking the bank on teachers and piano tuners then that&#x27;s a benefit.<p>- With what is available online + a $400 digital piano you can get surprisingly far if you are self motivated, and occasionally spending an hour or two with a teacher (or simply a better pianist) will stretch your abilities further.<p>- if you feel that you are not comfortable or maybe even are injuring yourself then stop whatever you are doing and ask an expert.<p>- If you have a goal and&#x2F;or want to be able to perform and value your time over your cash then a good teacher is the way to go but beware, not all teachers are good.<p>- if you can afford it, want it and you can find a good teacher definitely go for that.<p>- An acoustic piano is not a &#x27;must&#x27; to be able to learn and practice, in fact, a digital has some advantages (the ability to practice with headphones). Silent pianos exist but are usually quite pricey.<p>Finally, I&#x27;d like to plug <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;forum.pianoworld.com&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;forum.pianoworld.com&#x2F;</a> which is an excellent resource for people interested in playing and teaching piano.')
