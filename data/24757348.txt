Item(by='haswell', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602525656, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24756471, text='I am, without question, an introvert. But I also miss some of the things you describe in your comment.<p>If you very generically categorize introversion&#x2F;extroversion as (I realize this is an oversimplification and doesn&#x27;t take all factors into account):<p>- Interacting with people consumes energy<p>- Interacting with people is fuel<p>Missing that interaction as an introvert doesn&#x27;t seem to make sense at first.<p>But for myself, I&#x27;ve realized that it&#x27;s a bit more nuanced than that. It&#x27;s not so much that <i>all</i> interaction saps energy. Certain types of interaction does.<p>Put me in a conference room all day with a group of colleagues to plan the next release, and I&#x27;ll be utterly exhausted by the end.<p>Put me in a crowded street festival, and I&#x27;ll feel energized and alive.<p>Make me talk with people at the crowded street festival, and I&#x27;ll be exhausted by the end.<p>All of this to say, not all interactions are created equal, and I think it&#x27;s fair to say that even introverts like&#x2F;need to be around people, but the kinds of interactions an introvert is asked to participate in will mean the difference between energy&#x2F;exhaustion.')
