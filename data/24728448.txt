Item(by='seanhunter', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602238368, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24716218, text='Everyone who&#x27;s serious about unix should learn a bit of ed.  Ed + a here doc is really useful for some types of batch edits that you can&#x27;t do using sed or perl -pi -e.  Say you want to delete the line before the second line which starts &quot;---&quot; in the yaml frontmatter of 100 markdown files or swap the second and third line or whatever.  Ed is your go-to.<p><pre><code>  find somedir -name &quot;*.md&quot; | while read f ; do\n    ed $f &lt;&lt;__DONE\n    ...whatever magic you need\n    w\n    __DONE\n  done\n</code></pre>\nI&#x27;ve done this kind of thing in shellscripts so many times.  Recently it was in recovering my  personal website by dumping out the wayback machine through lynx and retro-converting the text to markdown.')
