Item(by='nullc', descendants=None, kids=[25497917], score=None, time=1608571888, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25495692, text='If you divide the CEO compensation increase by the number of employees in the company you&#x27;ll see that it itself is not particularly relevant in employee wages.<p>For example Tim Cook earns 133M&#x2F;yr which is $976 per employee. ... and this probably massively overstates the figure due to contractors.<p>Or Sundar Pichai with $86M&#x2F;yr which is $676 per employee (again... not counting contractors).<p>Obviously it&#x27;s more if you include more executives, but the number of top executive companies is basically a constant and at large companies it still ends up being not very large per employee.<p>This isn&#x27;t to say that it isn&#x27;t a concern but I don&#x27;t see how to justify the belief that the executive compensation at large companies is a major factor in the overall wage market.')
