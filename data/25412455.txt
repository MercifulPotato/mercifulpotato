Item(by='jen729w', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607909588, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25411581, text='(I&#x27;m the eponymous &#x27;Johnny&#x27;.)<p>Symlinks can work, and I&#x27;ve used them.<p>My advice to people is usually, though, that your brain will sort it out for you. And it really does.<p>The canonical example is &quot;home insurance&quot;. Say you have a category of &quot;home&quot; and a category elsewhere of &quot;insurance&quot;. Perfectly possible.<p>Which one does it go in? Well, you designed this system. Your brain came up with it. I&#x27;ve found that in nearly all cases, <i>you just know</i>.<p>I see where this may fall down in a shared environment (work), which is why I advocate (in one of the many unwritten posts on my as-yet-to-exist blog) for the role of a &#x27;librarian&#x27; at work. We seem to think that 20 people can work on a project together and all just magically decide where everything goes. That has been shown again and again to be a fallacy that we believe search will help us with. My argument -- from experience -- is that it does not, and that we need to go back to a world where we organise things, and that people are responsible for this organisation.')
