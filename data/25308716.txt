Item(by='IvyMike', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607119171, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25308204, text='&gt; ...nor are they so numerous that iterating over them is computationally difficult<p>That&#x27;s not correct; for a given bound x, the expected number of primes is x&#x2F;ln(x).<p>For example, if you need to choose a prime of 100 digits, there are something like 10^96 of them; you can find a random one using probabilistic methods, do your cryptography, and know that your attacker would have to try 10^96 primes to break it.  Since they can&#x27;t really do this, this is not the weak point of your cryptosystem.<p>If you chose a Mersenne prime, there are only 51 of them.  Your attacker would have to try less than 10^2 of them.')
