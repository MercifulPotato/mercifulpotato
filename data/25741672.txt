Item(by='codebje', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610420157, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25741551, text='There&#x27;s something like a 256 count limit on total websockets, and 30 per domain, in Chromium.<p>A malicious website could open up 256 websockets and as many HTTP connections as the browser allows, and that might be enough to swamp cheaper NATs.<p>See <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;bugs.chromium.org&#x2F;p&#x2F;chromium&#x2F;issues&#x2F;detail?id=12066" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;bugs.chromium.org&#x2F;p&#x2F;chromium&#x2F;issues&#x2F;detail?id=12066</a> for some 2009 discussion about people having troubles using the web when background tabs held connections open for polling. That wasn&#x27;t a NAT issue, but it does highlight that a decade or two ago we all thought we only needed to manage tens of connections for a host to be online but that rapidly spiralled into hundreds.')
