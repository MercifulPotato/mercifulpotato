Item(by='thristian', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611318769, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25870964, text='As a rule of thumb, a table that grows over time probably has a &quot;creation time&quot; field, and a query that touches one row probably touches other rows created around the same time (like a weekly report). Since rows are probably stored on disk in chronological order, you can index the &quot;creation time&quot; field and do a good job, but if your primary key is already roughly chronological you may be able to get away with one fewer index and keep inserts&#x2F;updates cheaper.')
