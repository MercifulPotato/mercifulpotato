Item(by='zdragnar', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607522687, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25358102, text='Small quibble: <i>All</i> rights precede the constitution, as the government is not the source of rights (they are natural or God-given, take your pick).<p>The constitution and subsequent amendments merely outline which rights the government may violate as a matter of necessity to run, and certain rights which it may not violate at all.<p>As you say, privacy is not mentioned; it does exist as a right but has no explicit protection except for the conditions in the 4th and 5th.<p>Of course, the federal government&#x27;s role has grown so much, and the interstate clause abuse has basically expanded it&#x27;s power to encompass anything and everything, it is not unreasonable to think that the first 10 amendments are the only rights we have anymore :&#x2F;')
