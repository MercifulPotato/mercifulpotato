Item(by='busterarm', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608140050, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25445236, text='I think of it maybe a little differently.<p>CentOS was acquired as a marketing tool to sell support contracts.<p>RedHat was acquired by IBM because selling support contracts no longer works as an open source software business model. It was a rescue operation (on both sides), really.<p>In a twisted way, it&#x27;s a success story for OSS. The software is so good and its main users so competent that the support contract has moved from being the value to the value-add. (aside)<p>Unfortunately, this is the same thing driving the adoption of all of these new split licenses, which I don&#x27;t think is a positive direction.<p>(aside): This is actually the second demonstration of this.  The first was when Rackspace got rid of its world-class support organization in a race to compete on costs.')
