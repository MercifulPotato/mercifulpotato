Item(by='lindner', descendants=None, kids=[25740230, 25741448, 25739155], score=None, time=1610398910, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25733200, text='So many thoughts about OpenSocial and the reference implementation, Shindig.  I have it thank for my time at hi5, LinkedIn and then Google.<p>Some little known facts about OpenSocial<p>- Hangouts Apps (remember those?) were based on OpenSocial containers.<p>- OpenSocial powered the LinkedIn Apps Platform and Labs for a number of years.  The team built Rails and Node apps and deployed on Joyent.<p>- Eric Schmidt gave a pep talk to the working group pre-launch and mentioned about how open always wins in the end...<p>- MySpace was concerned about the attack surface of 3p apps running in iframes.  They toyed with the idea of requiring a webkit browser plugin to run apps (!).  It did lead to Caja* as a project.  <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Caja_project" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Caja_project</a><p>- The work on OpenSocial led in small part to the Activity Streams spec which led to ActivityPub and thus the latest Fediverse protocols.  I like to think of OpenSocial as dead, but a good organ donor.<p>Fun times...')
