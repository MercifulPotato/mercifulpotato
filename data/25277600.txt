Item(by='kazinator', descendants=None, kids=[25278782], score=None, time=1606924941, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25276227, text='Common Lisp has pretty clear, straightforward rules about inlining.<p>File compilation is allowed to automatically inline function calls that are in the same compilation unit as their target.<p>This follows from the reasoning that material which is compiled together will be loaded together, and those functions will be redefined together.<p>The details are in 3.2.2.3 Semantic Constraints, bullets 3-5.<p>In any case where this inlining would be a problem, you must use <i>declaim</i> globally or <i>declare</i> locally to request <i>notinline</i>.<p>Likewise, you can use an <i>inline</i> declaration to encourage inlining.')
