Item(by='safog', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608152453, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25448124, text='My other problem with N95 fitting argument (as I&#x27;ve seen it made during the early days of the pandemic) is that there is a level that is &quot;good enough&quot; for most people.<p>Yes, if you want absolute certainty in a highly infectious environment like the hospital, when you&#x27;re doing bronchoscopies or taking care of COVID patients, you&#x27;d want to get the fit absolutely perfect (especially if it takes key resources like Nurses &#x2F; docs out of commission for several weeks).<p>But if you&#x27;re just worried about everyday scenarios like sharing an elevator in my apartment building or going to a grocery store where people still seem to turn up with out masks (or worse, pretend bandanas are masks) or get on public transit, then getting some protection is good enough.')
