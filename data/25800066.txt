Item(by='ericbarrett', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610771622, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25799815, text='The ability to trivially cross-compile in Go, and get a single-file executable artifact, is often mentioned but still not appreciated enough. This is still a multi-hour project, minimum, for most languages, especially if you are introducing a second platform to a well-established project.  In Go, it‘s a compiler flag.<p>I think between http, encoding&#x2F;json, and the io libraries, it’s also very simple and straightforward to do most of the things a typical command-line program does, in a cleaner way than shell or Python (both have their place) and with less fuss than Rust or C++.')
