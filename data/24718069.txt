English surname prefixes like &quot;Mac&quot;, &quot;Mc&quot;, &quot;Fitz&quot;, &quot;O&quot; [1] are generally attached to the name that follows (sometimes with the second part capitalised, sometimes not), so there is an old and widespread convention being followed here.  Nowadays it&#x27;s easier to search for or use as a username too.<p>Yann LeCun spells his own name like that: <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;yann.lecun.com&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;yann.lecun.com&#x2F;</a><p>[1] These are actually Celtic originally: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Celtic_onomastics#Surnames" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Celtic_onomastics#Surnames</a>')
