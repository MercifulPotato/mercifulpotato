Item(by='danaliv', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611957713, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25962792, text='<i>&gt; For me, and I think the majority, memory is where you keep what your brain thinks that it will use again.</i><p>There&#x27;s some pedagogical theory around this. (I learned this through the FAA&#x27;s instructor training.) Sort of common sense stuff, but it&#x27;s helpful for structuring learning. Basically you&#x27;ll remember things that you use a lot, have used recently, experienced intensely (e.g. actually used rather than just read about), and had fun learning. In addition, the first way you learn to do something tends to stick.<p>Cite: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Principles_of_learning" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Principles_of_learning</a>')
