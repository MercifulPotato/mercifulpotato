Item(by='pull_my_finger', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610781939, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25797386, text='Having a set of &quot;blessed&quot; libraries has definitely been discussed on the mailing list. Also there are plenty of &quot;distributions&quot; like Lua for Windows[1], LuaPower[2], or runtimes like Luvit[3] that add features ootb.<p>That said - the Lua ecosystem in 2021 is not the same as it was in 2005 or whenever people compare it to. There is a healthy amount of Libraries and choices of libraries for solving most tasks on Luarocks. And I would suggest you produce a better end project by selecting which libraries are included in your project that uses Lua for scripting. You can even further remove your choice of the libraries that _are_ included ootb.<p>[1] - <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;rjpcomputing&#x2F;luaforwindows" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;rjpcomputing&#x2F;luaforwindows</a><p>[2] - <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;luapower.com&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;luapower.com&#x2F;</a><p>[3] - <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;luvit.io&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;luvit.io&#x2F;</a>')
