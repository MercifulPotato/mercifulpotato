Item(by='thecedarprince', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602455132, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24749217, text='So, to give an example:<p>In manim, one could do something like this:<p><pre><code>  from manimlib.imports import *\n\n  class ReplacementTransformExhibit(Scene):\n      def construct(self):\n          square = Square().shift(LEFT)\n          circle = Circle().shift(RIGHT)\n          self.play(ReplacementTransform(square, circle))\n</code></pre>\nTo have a square morph to a circle. Currently in Javis, one could do something like this:<p><pre><code>  using Javis\n  \n  astar(args...) = star(O, 50)\n  acirc(args...) = circle(Point(100,100), 50)\n  \n  function ground(args...)\n      background(&quot;white&quot;)\n      sethue(&quot;black&quot;)\n  end\n\n  \n  video = Video(500, 500)\n  javis(video, [\n      Action(1:100, ground),\n      Action(1:50, morph(astar, acirc)),\n      Action(51:100, morph(acirc, astar))\n  ], creategif=true, tempdirectory=&quot;images&quot;,\n      pathname=&quot;star2circle.gif&quot;, deletetemp=true)\n</code></pre>\nAs you can see, Javis is, at this stage, a little more verbose. However, for v0.3.0 we are moving away from Action syntax to something more generic and user friendly (this shift based on user feedback).')
