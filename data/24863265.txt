If you&#x27;re in that pool, it&#x27;s good for you, because it would probably be cheaper. But fewer people would be insured, so it&#x27;s overall bad, right?<p>Am I&#x27;m missing some part of your argument?<p>The idea of making it universally deductible&#x2F;subsidized for individuals seems to be the path that we&#x27;re on right now.<p>Case 1: If you purchase health insurance on the exchange, and are very poor, you can get fully-subsidized plans. (Not to mention if you qualify for medicare).<p>Case 2: The most plausible proposals of &quot;Medicare for All&quot; are basically expanding the pool of people who qualify for zero-premium medicare advantage plans. This keeps insurance individualized and operated by private companies to maintain quality improvements driven by capitalistic-competition, although the government is the ultimate payer.<p>Case 3: the government (yes, even the current administration) has taken several steps to encourage employers to subsidize the costs of employees purchasing insurance individually, rather than as a group [see <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.takecommandhealth.com&#x2F;ichra-guide" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.takecommandhealth.com&#x2F;ichra-guide</a>].')
