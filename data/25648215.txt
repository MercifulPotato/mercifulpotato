Since then, Danish homeowners have enjoyed continuous slides in borrowing costs.<p>This is like a Canadian getting a 3.0% loan in USD to buy a house, then having the Canadian government fix the Canadian dollar such that it appreciate 3.0% pa against the USD.  It&#x27;s effectively a 0% loan, but due to obscure technicalities.<p>It seems, the lender still earns interest, it&#x27;s just that the interest is covered by the variance in exchange rates between currencies.  So your DKK1000 mortgage payment is worth €1030 after the first year, €1091 the next, etc.')
