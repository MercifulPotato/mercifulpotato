Item(by='soylentcola', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607037252, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25294197, text='That was sort of my takeaway as well.  I use desktop computers for most of my more demanding work.  Laptops are great and (like smartphones, tablets, etc) any improvement in their capabilities is to be welcomed--but there&#x27;s a difference between a headline that reads &quot;New laptop is the fastest computer ever!&quot; and one that reads &quot;New laptop faster than all previous laptops!&quot;<p>I don&#x27;t do the most intensive tasks on thin, battery-powered computers for a reason.  I try to do those on the box that&#x27;s always plugged into mains power with heatsinks and fans as needed, plenty of ports to plug things in, and a big monitor.  A more performant portable computer is awesome, but until I can spend a similar amount to make my workstation smaller without losing capability, I&#x27;ll likely keep using it for heavy lifting alongside a cheaper, less powerful laptop or tablet for lighter work or remoting in.')
