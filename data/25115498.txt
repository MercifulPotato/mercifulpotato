Denmark&#x27;s earlier, national debit card system is called DanKort.  Nowadays, almost all DanKort cards are issued through Visa, so they are &quot;Visa DanKort&quot; -- within Denmark, there&#x27;s the option to bypass some sort of fee for a lower cost of processing.<p>Presumably the value &quot;visadankort&quot; is there to distinguish these cards.  However, they can be processed exactly as normal Visa cards.<p>This is not unique to Denmark, there are a list of card prefixes like this (see 4571 for DanKort[1]), but perhaps the different processing fees in unusual.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.stevemorse.org&#x2F;ssn&#x2F;List_of_Bank_Identification_Numbers.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.stevemorse.org&#x2F;ssn&#x2F;List_of_Bank_Identification_N...</a>')
