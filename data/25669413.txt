It had pros &amp; cons, and trying to weigh them up overall seems a bit of a distraction. I&#x27;m glad I don&#x27;t live under it, although perhaps those who did were also glad not to live under earlier systems. But we don&#x27;t get to choose when to be born,  so we are not obliged to reach a yes&#x2F;no vote.<p>Right, the experience of black people was pretty distinct, 20s-60s-now, and those are really interesting stories. And there&#x27;s a fractal of smaller group stories, too. But I think the story arc from hyper-partisan 19th C news-politics to Walter Cronkite to Twitter can be understood (to a large degree) before zooming in that far. Although once you do, then indeed, the present tendency to focus on smaller groups&#x27; stories does look like part of the same Cronkite -&gt; Twitter arc.')
