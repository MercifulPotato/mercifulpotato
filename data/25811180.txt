Platforms will backup and restore key material from authenticators\n2. Users will figure out how to use those backups (including if they e.g. switch from Mac to Windows)<p>they will have to provide some fallback. (Realistically, that means &quot;for the foreseeable future.&quot;)<p>This relegates WebAuthn, unfortunately, to a &quot;neat trick&quot;--eBay&#x27;s &quot;passwordless option&quot;--and not a security guarantee. (Phishers can always downgrade the user to passwords or similar.)<p>What&#x27;s needed in the interregnum is probably a way to make that fallback option more secure by, at minimum, conveying to users that it&#x27;s not _normal_--so that phishing attempts targeting that phishable backup are less likely to succeed.<p>Unfortunately this requires a lot of good UX design and unilateral action on part of the relying parties, which I think is tough.')
