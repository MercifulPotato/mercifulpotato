They think the existing laws are just fine and oppose the idea that additional regulation would be meaningful in compelling companies to do the right thing. Instead, they worked with other activist organizations to create The Santa Clara Principles. [0]<p>The chief problem is that enforcement will always be unequal, especially when the majority of people in positions of authority come from one political culture. The EFF thinks it&#x27;s just fine to deal with this retroactively, but basically, that&#x27;s saying it&#x27;s okay to sacrifice a few to save the many. I&#x27;m okay with saying that <i>if</i> you&#x27;re okay with making the consequences for sacrificing those few huge. eg: If Facebook disables someones account because they misunderstood some context because they don&#x27;t have people working there that can properly frame it, then Facebook owes them substantial damages.<p>[0] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;santaclaraprinciples.org&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;santaclaraprinciples.org&#x2F;</a>')
