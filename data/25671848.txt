Item(by='mikewarot', descendants=None, kids=[25675084], score=None, time=1610031987, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25663107, text='The problem isn&#x27;t the computers, it is <i>changing requirements</i>.<p>If you want to edit text files, do some spreadsheets, maybe send and receive messages, an IBM PC will do the job. Just be sure to have a modem, Qmodem, and the numbers of your favorite BBSs.<p>With an IBM PC, MS-DOS 6.21, Turbo Pascal 7, Qmodem, Edwin, pkZIP, and a stack of floppies for backup, and you could stay in business for a very, very long time, <i>if</i> you don&#x27;t expect more each year.<p>Look at what people are managing to fit into a boot sector... BASIC, Games, Demos, etc.  We&#x27;re wasting most of the bounty of Moore&#x27;s law. Not all of it, of course...the ability to stream video from anywhere on the planet for $50&#x2F;month is something I only dreamed of back in the 1990s.<p>We have amazing computers now, but those old machines, like the Model T, can still do their original task, if you invest the time and effort to maintain them.')
