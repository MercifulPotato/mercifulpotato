Item(by='peter_d_sherman', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610483327, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25747659, text='First off, I like Glenn Greenwald very much as a journalist.<p>Remember that he and Laura Poitras helped Edward Snowden disclose to the world what he did, in 2013.<p>In other words, as a journalist, he does, or should command a huge amount of respect from the HN community.<p>He has my respect.<p>Now, with that as a background, let&#x27;s talk about this article.<p>It&#x27;s an important article, and yes, broadly speaking, the claims that are made are true.<p>But the problem I have with this article (and not with Glenn Greenwald personally, who again, is a great journalist!) is that it&#x27;s a little bit too broad...<p>To give you an understanding of this, let&#x27;s say that in the future, I ran an online service with an App Store, like Apple or Google.<p>OK, so now, for whatever reason, the Parler App is removed from the App Store that I run.<p>But, to tell me (and the newsreader) that it was an act of Tech Tyranny, of Monopolistic Force -- is not good enough.<p>You see, I believe in several things:<p>1) Strong Logging<p>2) Chain Of Command<p>3) Chain Of Custody<p>But most importantly, the &quot;5 Whys&quot;:<p>4) <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Five_whys" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Five_whys</a><p>In other words, I&#x27;d like (as a member of the public) an <i>&quot;organizational backtrace&quot;</i>, starting with the engineer who physically removed the App from the app store, moving from there to his manager that ordered it, and moving from there up levels of management.<p>In other words, <i>WHO</i> ordered <i>WHAT</i>, <i>WHEN</i>, and <i>WHY</i>.<p>In other words, don&#x27;t start with the highest level effect, the silencing of free speech, start with the lowest level effect -- the physical removal of the App from the App Store by the engineer that did it.<p>From that point, use the <i>5 WHYS</i> to work backward, something like, &quot;OK, this engineer did this because he was ordered to by his manager&quot;, so WHY did the manager do it?, &quot;Because he was ordered to by his manager&quot;, etc.<p>But now the question arises -- <i>Who was at the highest level of management at that company that gave that order?</i><p>And now we ask WHY again... so we need to talk to him, and find out exactly <i>WHY</i> (what socio-political-moral-ethic-legal-or-whatever pressure was applied to him, and how?)<p>See, answer all of those questions, and <i>THEN</i> you have the true story!<p>But all this being said, I do love Glenn Greenwald!')
