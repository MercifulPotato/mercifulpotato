Here in California, from looking at the states graphs it seems that about 90% of our renewables are being generated in a 12 hour window[0] (a window which unfortunately ends at the time when demand starts to build up to its peak). If you want to even out 50GWh of renewables across 24 hours, you&#x27;d be spending billions of dollars on storage and you&#x27;d <i>still</i> be burning fossil fuels on cloudy and&#x2F;or low-wind days. Nuclear reduces storage needs, as well as the bursty-ness of renewables.
