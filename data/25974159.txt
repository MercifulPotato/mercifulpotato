Item(by='megous', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1612049579, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25970207, text='On normal Linux, if your device has a proper PMIC with a driver that allows this, you can just change the max charging voltage to something like 4.1V or less via sysfs (&#x2F;sys&#x2F;class&#x2F;power_supply&#x2F;*) on each boot. You can do the same for maximum charging current, to avoid so called &quot;fast charging&quot;, and charge at 0.2C for example. And that&#x27;s it.')
