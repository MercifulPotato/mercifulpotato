Item(by='jacknews', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609208470, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25559571, text='Post-capitalism, post-market economies.<p>I think at this point it&#x27;s clear that market economies are good at getting people out of bed, and for innovating, finding new markets, optimizing for efficiency, etc, and generating &#x27;wealth&#x27;.<p>But they are perhaps not good at creating the right kind of wealth, spreading it fairly, fostering healthy societies, respecting the environment, optimizing for resilience, sustainability, etc.<p>It isn&#x27;t really fixed by what we do now; tacking on taxes by government - which is always seen as &#x27;lazy bureaucrats stealing from wealth creators&#x27;, etc.<p>We should be researching, and testing alternatives, built into the very fabric of how economies are structured, along with ideas like Georgeist land taxes, universal basic equity&#x2F;dividend etc.')
