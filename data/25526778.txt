Item(by='fxtentacle', descendants=None, kids=[25527042, 25527983], score=None, time=1608811096, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25525726, text='This is an interesting one, because they optimize for an untypical goal: &quot;If the scale&#x2F;exposure of colors are changed, the perceptual coordinates should just be scaled by a factor&quot;<p>For most computer vision applications, that is the opposite of what you want. When you analyze a video stream, it is quite common for frames to have different brightness due to things like aliasing between the flicker of LEDs in the room and the camera shutter. That&#x27;s why CV needs a color space where colors remain close to each other, no matter the frame brightness.')
