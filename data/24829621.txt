Item(by='eikenberry', descendants=None, kids=[24848777], score=None, time=1603133744, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24829083, text='Just because it is present, doesn&#x27;t mean its the default or used internally by things that call shell scripts.<p>For example... Not that long ago I started a new job where I took over as maintainer of a project with a fairly extensive Makefile, including targets for release. I cleaned it up some but missed a couple bashisms. The original author wrote it on a Mac, on which &#x2F;bin&#x2F;sh is bash, and used bashisms. I took over on Linux, which has &#x2F;bin&#x2F;sh as dash (POSIX). So while the author used it fine, it broke for me in subtle ways that I didn&#x27;t notice until I made my first, broken release (error was in formatting of md5 signature file).<p>The problem with bashisms is that people don&#x27;t think through if their current use case is an appropriate one.')
