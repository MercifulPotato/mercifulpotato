Item(by='jokethrowaway', descendants=None, kids=[25582047], score=None, time=1609338562, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25581417, text='I think the core assumption of this essay is that innovation can be had only with startups.<p>This is something I personally believed as well in the past, probably because of my experience with highly hierarchical companies and middle management.<p>This idea changed after hearing how teams work independently from each other at Amazon.<p>I&#x27;m not sure if this is really the case at Amazon, but I believe that if a company is able to organise itself so that you essentially have an internal market of tiny startups instead of teams (so they have freedom to pursue their products, they can fail and they can be hugely successful) you can replicate most of the startup benefits.<p>I&#x27;ve been trying to propose this model in the companies I&#x27;ve worked at - but generally there is always some Napoleone on top who vetoes risky bets or force teams to work on non productive endeavours.<p>You can get approved some practices that give more freedom to individual contributors, but important decisions always need to be directly or indirectly approved on the top.<p>I&#x27;d be curious to know what M$ did internally')
