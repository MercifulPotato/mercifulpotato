The under-performing Chinese rural schools are at ~17, and they say that urban Chinese schools are at ~12. 17 is roughly typical student teacher ratio in US public schools, regardless of status. In the US a ratio of 12 or less is common in elite private schools.<p>I was expecting that the CAL program they implemented was what is known as an &quot;Inverted Classroom&quot;. It&#x27;s a learning system where students view recorded (or live) lectures by master teachers as &quot;homework&quot;, and then use classroom time with a regular teacher to practice&#x2F;drill&#x2F;explore what they&#x27;ve learned-- not unlike a college recitation session with a TA.<p>But that was not the case in the Chinese CAL study. Instead...<p>&gt; In our context, however, remote learning happened in the classroom under the direct supervision of local teachers, therefore limiting distractions and procrastination.')
