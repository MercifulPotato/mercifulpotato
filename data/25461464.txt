I was about to respond and say the opposite :)<p>My (personal) unbound jail has 95 files in it.  The reason it has <i>so many</i> files is because it is simultaneously my &#x27;nsd&#x27; jail.<p>Which is to say, I actually point <i>two different</i> &#x27;jail&#x27; commands at the same directory - each with a different IP and command line:<p><pre><code>  &#x2F;usr&#x2F;sbin&#x2F;jail &#x2F;jails&#x2F;dns ns1 1.2.3.4 &#x2F;nsd&#x2F;nsd -c &#x2F;nsd&#x2F;nsd.conf</code></pre>\n&#x2F;usr&#x2F;sbin&#x2F;jail &#x2F;jails&#x2F;dns dns 1.2.3.5 &#x2F;unbound&#x2F;unbound -c &#x2F;unbound&#x2F;unbound.conf<p>So &#x2F;jails&#x2F;dns is a holder for two different dns servers and they run, jailed, from the same root.  It&#x27;s a little unorthodox but in this case I find it to be elegant.')
