Item(by='NovemberWhiskey', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602958312, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24811677, text='&gt;It amazes me that in 2020, we still struggle to implement a simple system where someone can reliably and permanently transfer money to someone else as the default, expected behaviour of the financial system.<p>It&#x27;s called cash, maybe you&#x27;ve heard of it?<p>If you want an electronic system that&#x27;s exactly like putting cash in an envelope and handing it to someone, Zelle etc. represent that system. A wire transfer is basically the same, only with greater formality, complexity and cost.<p>By and large, consumers don&#x27;t actually want that. They like protections such as being able to chargeback if services they&#x27;ve paid for don&#x27;t get delivered; goods are defective or counterfeit and so on. Yes, this can make life hard for merchants: on the other hand, they are free not to accept credit cards. Most do, because it&#x27;s a price of doing business.')
