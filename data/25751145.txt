Item(by='mancerayder', descendants=None, kids=[25751501, 25751383, 25751431, 25751421], score=None, time=1610475929, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25750059, text='One is adapt to a world where information (and lack thereof) is weaponized. Get out your skepticism and find trusted sources of information.  There is an enormous amount of partisan-oriented misrepresentations and under-reported stuff, on BOTH sides of the political spectrum.<p>As Chomsky pointed out in Manufacturing Consent, modern-day propaganda isn&#x27;t about lying or faking data, that&#x27;s  pre-War stuff.  Post WWII we discovered that a much better way to manipulate public opinion is to focus on some data and not focusing on other data.  Omitting that which doesn&#x27;t match narrative, and headlining the stuff that matches narrative.<p>Believe it or not, it helps to stabilize you.  As Taibbi points out, the news media has turned itself into market segments, where a news story is decided upon, &quot;Will my market want to hear this&quot; and NOT &quot;People should know this.&quot;    Therefore it&#x27;s intended as outrage and anger by design.  Once you know this, you can take a step back.<p>That&#x27;s the psychological preparation.<p>Several people below wrote &quot;community&quot; and &quot;social group.&quot;  For those of us in cities and not small towns &#x2F; farming communities, that translates to &quot;make some friends&quot; which isn&#x27;t really that useful advice, I&#x27;m afraid.')
