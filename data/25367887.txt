(\n<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.marketplacepulse.com&#x2F;articles&#x2F;amazons-retail-business-is-now-just-50-of-the-companys-revenue" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.marketplacepulse.com&#x2F;articles&#x2F;amazons-retail-bus...</a>)<p>And a portion of the products that can be purchased from &quot;Amazon.com&quot; (the seller) are JIT orders that Amazon places with the supplier. This is why some products on Amazon are perpetually never &quot;in stock&quot; until a future date on the store listing page; Amazon has made the decision based on sales data to only order those products on demand. In such cases, the supplier will <i>usually</i> ship to Amazon for transshipment to the customer, but depending on the cost of logistics, Amazon may have them ship directly to the customer.')
