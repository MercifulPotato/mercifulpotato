Item(by='bzbarsky', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605073430, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25049103, text='It depends on the state.  For example, Massachussetts has semi-open primaries.  If you&#x27;re registered R and show up at the primary, you get an R ballot.  If you&#x27;re registered D, you get a D ballot.  If you&#x27;re registered &quot;Unaffiliated&quot;, they ask you which ballot you&#x27;d like.  That would be something like 55% of MA voters, last I checked.<p>Looking at <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Open_primaries_in_the_United_States#States_with_an_open_presidential_primary" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Open_primaries_in_the_United_S...</a> it looks like 17 states have completely open primaries.  4 more are like Massachusetts.  3 more have open or semi-open primaries on the Democratic side only.')
