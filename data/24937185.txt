Item(by='lhorie', descendants=None, kids=[24941599, 24937531, 24937229], score=None, time=1604012941, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24936973, text='My take is that the claim that &quot;shoppers were made aware of the activity through decals&quot; fails on multiple levels: whether said decals were actually there, whether they would be capable of capturing nuances of scope if they were (e.g. there&#x27;s different expectations between a mall entrance, the Shoppers entrance and the LCBO entrance), whether people noticed the alleged decals if they were there, whether they could be reasonably expect to understand it (e.g. non-english speakers), whether people could infer that legalese existed if they did understand it, whether they were capable of understanding the actual terms (e.g. tech illiterates), whether it is reasonable to expect that people would consciously agree to whatever terms are laid out in the legalese, whether all people captured by face recognition necessarily were actually aware of the terms, etc. It just doesn&#x27;t stick on so many levels.')
