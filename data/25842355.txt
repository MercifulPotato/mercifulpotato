Item(by='gabereiser', descendants=None, kids=[25843126], score=None, time=1611114342, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25841270, text='&gt;<i>When building a project using IL2CPP, Unity converts IL code from scripts and assemblies to C++, before creating a native binary file (.exe, apk, .xap, for example) for your chosen platform. Some of the uses for IL2CPP include increasing the performance, security, and platform compatibility of your Unity projects.</i><p>Mono runtime will load your assembly and bind it with all the pinvoke stuff and JIT.<p>IL2cpp will take the IL code from your assemblies and convert it to C++ when making the binary, AOT.<p>IL2cpp is the preferred way to make sure your game supports the most platforms.')
