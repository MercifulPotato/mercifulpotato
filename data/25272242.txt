Item(by='dTal', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606872202, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25266170, text='It seems to me like it&#x27;s just vaguely guessing the question, in a manner of a Google search:<p>&quot;When did the Simpson&#x27;s first...&quot; and it leaps ahead, ignoring the nonsense.<p>&quot;Who flew the first airplane...&quot;, again.<p>I tested the theory with &quot;How big is the Death Star&#x27;s toilet&quot;, and sure enough it promotes a result on the diameter of the Death Star. So, part of this phenomenon is finding queries that are very close to &quot;common&quot; searches, with nonsense attached that&#x27;s so completely zany that it doesn&#x27;t push the statistical needle in any particular direction - and it latches on to the &quot;common&quot; search.<p>edit: I succeeded again with &quot;When did doctor who invent fish fingers&quot;, which proudly returns &quot;2010&quot; - which is probably merely the most likely answer to any question involving &quot;when&quot;, &quot;doctor who&quot; and &quot;fish fingers&quot;.')
