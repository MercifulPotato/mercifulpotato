The classic phrase was the strangely gendered &quot;to each his own [choice]&quot; and the &quot;proper&quot; phrase is probably &quot;to each <i>their</i> own [choice]&quot;. &quot;To each is own&quot; implies something else, but I&#x27;m not entirely sure what. &quot;To each is own[er] [of choices]&quot; perhaps?<p>I&#x27;ve seen it like two or three times in recent weeks and find it fascinating. (Sorry, not trying single you out, or derail the conversation too much, but armchair linguistics is a hobby of many on HN and somewhat curious if others have spotted it.)<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Mondegreen" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Mondegreen</a>')
