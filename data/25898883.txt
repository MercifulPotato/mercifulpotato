Reading https:&#x2F;&#x2F;developers.google.com&#x2F;web&#x2F;updates&#x2F;2015&#x2F;03&#x2F;push-notifications-on-the-open-web I realized that even though there&#x27;s a standard API, each browser uses a proprietary &quot;push service&quot; to which my own server sends messages first, before they are delivered to the user&#x27;s browser: https:&#x2F;&#x2F;developers.google.com&#x2F;web&#x2F;fundamentals&#x2F;push-notifications&#x2F;how-push-works<p>In particular, the last link says: &quot;Each browser can use any push service they want, it&#x27;s something developers have no control over.&quot;. Given the recent news of Google&#x2F;Apple deplatoforming Parler, and the recent push to deplatform Telegram (e.g. https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.vox.com&#x2F;recode&#x2F;22238755&#x2F;telegram-messaging-social-media-extremists ), is it wise to rely on Google&#x27;s push service long term?
