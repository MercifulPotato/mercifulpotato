Item(by='dqpb', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610723947, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25788645, text='For anyone else who is unfamiliar with “monitor” in this context, found this on Wikipedia:<p>&gt; Among audio engineers, the term monitor implies that the speaker is designed to produce relatively flat (linear) phase and frequency responses. In other words, it exhibits minimal emphasis or de-emphasis of particular frequencies, the loudspeaker gives an accurate reproduction of the tonal qualities of the source audio (&quot;uncolored&quot; or &quot;transparent&quot; are synonyms), and there will be no relative phase shift of particular frequencies—meaning no distortion in sound-stage perspective for stereo recordings.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.m.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Studio_monitor" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.m.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Studio_monitor</a>')
