Item(by='crazygringo', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604979191, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25041457, text='Until recently, the hunt was broadly successful (maybe not every single day, but certainly every week).<p>Hunter-gatherers are the only type of group for which there are no anthropologically&#x2F;historically documented cases of starvation. In contrast to pastoralists and agriculturalists, which have suffered starvation regularly.<p>Hunter-gatherers across nearly all of the globe always had a diet of meat and plants. It is absolutely false that it was plant-based &quot;most of the time&quot;. Eating meat has absolutely <i>not</i> always been an unaffordable luxury -- that&#x27;s a complete myth. Meat as a &quot;luxury&quot; was a totally new phenomenon that arose only with agriculture.<p>For the vast majority of human history, meat (or fish) has been plentiful and broadly consumed.')
