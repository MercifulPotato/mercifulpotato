Item(by='Closi', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604911350, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25026844, text='&gt; Sending kids to school has to be the stupidest idea during this pandemic.<p>It&#x27;s only the stupidest idea if you see it as &#x27;low or medium cost&#x27; - if you see it as a high cost then you can understand that there is a strategic trade-off to be made (kids staying at home would clearly have a high benefit for controlling the pandemic, so that is the trade off).<p>A generation of students losing a year of high-quality education could represent a huge long-term cost, particularly considering that a lot of Europe&#x27;s business is knowledge-work, and the impact education has on quality of life &#x2F; socioeconomic factors.<p>Adding to that, kids at home blocks some adults who can work from working, and there is a multiplier effect.<p>I&#x27;m not saying it&#x27;s the right decision, but to claim that it&#x27;s &quot;the stupidest decision&quot; makes it sound like it&#x27;s a no-brainer when I&#x27;m not sure it is.')
