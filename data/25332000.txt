It&#x27;s OK to check some values at specific points (like when a value is passed from user space), but in general it&#x27;s bad practice to check it at every single function. You trust in your program flow.<p>Imagine if at every function down a complex stack you go with:<p><pre><code>    if (!ptr1 || !*ptr1 || *ptr1 &gt; 5 || param1 &lt; 0 || param2)\n        &#x2F;* etc.... *&#x2F;\n    {\n        return NULL;\n    }\n</code></pre>\n(used arbitrary names and values).')
