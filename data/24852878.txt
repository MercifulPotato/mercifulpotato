Item(by='tptacek', descendants=None, kids=[24853072], score=None, time=1603318837, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24852081, text='There&#x27;s not much to know! &quot;XDP&quot; is really just the Linux term of art for &quot;BPF running directly off the network driver&quot;. Your BPF program --- ordinarily, just a C program you compiled with clang --- is given a struct with pointers to the beginning and end of a packet, and your program can return OK, DROP, or REDIRECT, in addition to modifying the packet.<p>The XDP project itself has a pretty excellent tutorial:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;xdp-project&#x2F;xdp-tutorial" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;xdp-project&#x2F;xdp-tutorial</a>')
