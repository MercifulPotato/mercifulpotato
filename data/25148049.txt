Item(by='TeMPOraL', descendants=None, kids=[25149518], score=None, time=1605781671, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25146924, text='&gt; <i>Producing a whole new limb or whatever is way more complicated that we&#x27;d like it to be.</i><p>It isn&#x27;t <i>that</i> much more complicated, though. Because as it turns out, you don&#x27;t always have to code for that limb from scratch. You just have to flip a switch.<p>The key phrase here is &quot;evolutionary developmental biology&quot;. We&#x27;ve learned that complex life has a lot of Lego-life quality - a lot of DNA codes for signals that enable or disable creation of whole organs at various locations, stages and cadences.<p>A TL;DR in four minutes by A Capella Science: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=ydqReeTV_vk" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=ydqReeTV_vk</a>.<p>A nice example that video gives is that snakes have DNA coding for legs, it&#x27;s just turned off. It can be turned on, and a snake will grow legs.')
