Item(by='Jedd', descendants=None, kids=[24842627], score=None, time=1603229508, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24839848, text='I&#x27;ve got an &#x27;even worse&#x27; problem - a box of about 20 different 8mm (or similar) reel-to-reel tapes, containing family videos from perhaps as far back as the 1960s, but certainly much from the early 1970s.<p>The tape tins themselves don&#x27;t reveal anything about the media type or actual content.<p>Apparently there are some cheap (~A$500) devices that let you convert these directly to digital, but reviews and blogs suggest highly variable results, probably based on how well the tapes have been stored.<p>Paying someone to convert them is <i>hideously</i> expensive, however, and is generally charged on a per-tape processed rather than viable output basis -- which is why most people stump up for a &#x27;single use&#x27; device and spend the time.  On the upside, at least there&#x27;s no audio track for these things.')
