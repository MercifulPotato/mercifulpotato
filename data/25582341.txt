Item(by='vidarh', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609341450, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25581240, text='Microsoft became successful because everyone <i>wanted</i> BASIC, and they were the main vendor able to quickly provide compact versions for the main CPUs in use.<p>The idea they &quot;pushed it&quot; onto the home micro scene is nonsense - Microsoft was small enough to be pushed around by much larger vendors at the time, and were cheap.<p>Jack Tramiel at Commodore infamously received an offer for $3 per unit, and supposedly countered with a $25k offer for a perpetual license (they did end up negotiating a new agreement when releasing the C128, as the license only applied to versions of BASIC already released at the time it was entered into, but by then they&#x27;d shipped <i>millions</i> of units).')
