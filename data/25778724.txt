I find it interesting to read Khamenei&#x27;s statements, even though I find his regime repugnant for killing gays, jailing dissidents, stifling women&#x27;s rights, and threatening Israel. It&#x27;s important that we be able to see what he says.  It doesn&#x27;t mean we&#x27;re glorifying him or legitimizing him, and those elements in the West or elsewhere who may agree with him will get it from alternative sources anyway.<p>Likewise, it&#x27;s important to see what violent elements in our society are saying, even if we disagree with them and find them reprehensible.<p>You can&#x27;t stop ideas from flowing; you can slow them down temporarily, by banning them from the mainstream marketplace of idea exchange.  But ideas still have a way of leaking through.<p>If someone is advocating assassination of the President on social media, they should be reported to the FBI.  Simply deleting their posting accomplishes nothing.<p>These media platforms mistakenly think that they are arbiters of ideas and information, when in reality they&#x27;re just glorified bulletin boards.  It should not be up to them to decide <i>on others&#x27; behalf</i> what is appropriate to be seen and what is not.<p>Just my opinions.')
