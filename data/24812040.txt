Item(by='kwhitefoot', descendants=None, kids=[24812193, 24812203], score=None, time=1602959225, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24811739, text='No, Twain was satirising the whole ridiculous idea that trying to regularise the spelling of English is a good idea.<p>English has homonyms and homophones in part because it is always open to taking words from other languages.  In fact &quot;We don&#x27;t just borrow words; on occasion, English has pursued other languages down alleyways to beat them unconscious and rifle their pockets for new vocabulary.&quot;<p>Any attempt to force English to be strictly &#x27;pronounced as written&#x27; will fail in the future even if it succeeds in the present because someone will import a word from another language that sounds like an existing word or invent a new one unaware that a word of the same pronunciation already exists or just attach a new meaning to an existing word.<p>See <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;wiki.c2.com&#x2F;?PurityOfEnglish" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;wiki.c2.com&#x2F;?PurityOfEnglish</a> and <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;library.conlang.org&#x2F;blog&#x2F;?p=116" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;library.conlang.org&#x2F;blog&#x2F;?p=116</a>')
