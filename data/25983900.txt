Item(by='derefr', descendants=None, kids=[25984100, 25984066], score=None, time=1612137143, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25983860, text='&gt; Giving direct access to your database is fast but leads to very strong data coupling.<p>Or you could just give &quot;direct&quot; access to (a DB role that only has been granted SELECT on) a single schema within your database, entirely consisting of views. Then you&#x27;ve got a stable data-access API that you can iterate your data model behind, with nothing more than your DB itself.<p>(And you don&#x27;t need to put much thought into setting it up, if you don&#x27;t want to; your stable schema-API-layer can just start off as a bunch of views with 1:1 relationships to your real tables, which each just &quot;SELECT * FROM their_underlying_table&quot;.)')
