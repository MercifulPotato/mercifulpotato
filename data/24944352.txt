It&#x27;s not that hard. You have official counters and each candidate can appoint scrutineers to observe the count[0]. Scrutineers can challenge how a ballot has been counted: &quot;I think that&#x27;s actually a 7, not a 1&quot;. If the count supervisor agrees, it&#x27;s changed. If not, then not. If it&#x27;s ruled impossible to distinguish, the ballot is considered informal and removed from the count entirely.<p>[0] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.abc.net.au&#x2F;cm&#x2F;rimage&#x2F;7570634-3x2-xlarge.jpg?v=6" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.abc.net.au&#x2F;cm&#x2F;rimage&#x2F;7570634-3x2-xlarge.jpg?v=6</a>')
