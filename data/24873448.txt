Item(by='dragontamer', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603483684, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24873254, text='&gt; It&#x27;s not something that an IBM committee would have come up with but it fits the needs of an application.<p>Items can be order-dependent in XML. So the &quot;id=1&quot; thing is still superfluous. See xs:sequence. (In contrast: xs:all declares something to be order independent).<p>If you were doing XML for your own apps, I think xs:sequence items simplify parsing grossly.<p>Ex: if you have Address, maybe you always want &quot;Street&quot;, then &quot;Zip Code&quot;, then &quot;State&quot;.<p><pre><code>    &lt;Address&gt;\n    &lt;Street&gt; 1234 Blah Street &lt;&#x2F;Street&gt;\n    &lt;City&gt; Springfield &lt;&#x2F;City&gt;\n    &lt;State&gt; Whatever &lt;&#x2F;State&gt;\n    &lt;Zip&gt; 55555 &lt;&#x2F;Zip&gt;\n    &lt;&#x2F;Address&gt;\n</code></pre>\nBy declaring that Street -&gt; City -&gt; State -&gt; Zip must happen in that particular order, you can grossly simplify the parser&#x27;s job when reading such data in. (Or writing it out).<p>--------<p>If there are multiple People at this address, you could (and probably should) just list them out in order.<p><pre><code>    &lt;Address&gt;\n    &lt;Street&gt; 1234 Blah Street &lt;&#x2F;Street&gt;\n    &lt;City&gt; Springfield &lt;&#x2F;City&gt;\n    &lt;State&gt; Whatever &lt;&#x2F;State&gt;\n    &lt;Zip&gt; 55555 &lt;&#x2F;Zip&gt;\n\n    &lt;People&gt;\n      &lt;p&gt; Marge &lt;&#x2F;p&gt;\n      &lt;p&gt; Homer &lt;&#x2F;p&gt;\n      &lt;p&gt; Lisa &lt;&#x2F;p&gt;\n      &lt;p&gt; Bart &lt;&#x2F;p&gt;\n      &lt;p&gt; Maggie &lt;&#x2F;p&gt;\n    &lt;&#x2F;People&gt;\n    &lt;&#x2F;Address&gt;</code></pre>')
