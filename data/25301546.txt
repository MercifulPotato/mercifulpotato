Item(by='dragonshed', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607085138, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25300716, text='On multiple projects I&#x27;ve used a tool [1] to closely match a built-in font to a web font in order to present content faster and avoid avoid FOUC (Flash of Unstyled Content&#x2F;Text).  In one case, I replaced webfonts entirely with native platform fonts, purely for speed.<p>With this approach you can be more practical with your goals.  If the design cannot be compromised, you can still get to initial paint quickly and let the WOFF files load in as needed.<p>Edit: If anyone is looking for more information about this, the post [2] from this tool&#x27;s author from 2016 does a good job of discussing the tradeoffs.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;notwaldorf&#x2F;font-style-matcher" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;notwaldorf&#x2F;font-style-matcher</a><p>[2] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;meowni.ca&#x2F;posts&#x2F;web-fonts&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;meowni.ca&#x2F;posts&#x2F;web-fonts&#x2F;</a>')
