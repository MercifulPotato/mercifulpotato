Item(by='rpedela', descendants=None, kids=[25498482], score=None, time=1608577836, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25498120, text='&quot;Here is why the consensus is _______&quot; is not argument by authority, correct.<p>&quot;The ________ theory&#x2F;hypothesis is fringe, the consensus is ________&quot; is argument by authority.<p>See the difference? Phrasing matters in this case. All too often it is phrased the second way. I don&#x27;t expect someone to literally enumerate all the evidence for or against something, but there is a middle ground between that and just saying &quot;well consensus is ______&quot;.')
