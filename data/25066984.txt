Item(by='stevekemp', descendants=None, kids=[25068024, 25067446], score=None, time=1605161448, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25065560, text='I know we were gifted one, and it didn&#x27;t ever get used.<p>When the baby was brought home he shared the bedroom with the pair of us, and it was immediately obvious if he was crying.<p>Later when he moved to sleeping in his own room we could still easily hear the noise of crying, as the room was immediately adjacent to our own, and we left the doors open.<p>I remember we setup a routine at some point where one of us would sleep in his room with him, leaving the other one free to sleep through the whole night.<p>Maybe if you live in a mansion, and you can&#x27;t hear a baby crying from the other wing it might be necessary.  But most of the parents I know have always been close enough to hear crying just with their ears.  (Although I know there certainly are days, especially early on, where you&#x27;ll hear but be half-asleep and not really do anything!)')
