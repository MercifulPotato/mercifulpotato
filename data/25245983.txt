Item(by='Retric', descendants=None, kids=[25267573], score=None, time=1606666258, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25245599, text='Logic unfortunately tends to fail in the real world.  Even seemingly concrete ideas like life and death get tricky when dealing with frozen sperm and viruses.  Edge cases abound <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Chimera_(genetics)" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Chimera_(genetics)</a> People really want stuff like Bayesian statistics to work.  But it’s also fails for similar reasons as it depends on accurately updating a model.<p>The world is filled with models that are simple enough to use and accurate enough to be useful, sadly that doesn’t make them correct.<p>Censorship seems so simple on the surface, but like everything else the implementation rarely matches the theory.  I would argue for disclaimers as a much better option, even if they also fail.')
