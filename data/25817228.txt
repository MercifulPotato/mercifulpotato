Item(by='skykooler', descendants=None, kids=[25819226, 25817260, 25817306], score=None, time=1610933455, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25816516, text='A minor point: you can&#x27;t really measure distances by the speed of light via Bluetooth, the protocol is too coarse-grained. Bluetooth distance measurements are usually done via signal strength. However, time-of-flight distance measurements can be done on an extension of wifi called 802.11v - this is how the Apple Watch proximity unlock works, for example.')
