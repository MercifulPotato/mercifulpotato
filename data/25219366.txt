Item(by='scatters', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606393582, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25218600, text='Accessing other hardware isn&#x27;t latency-sensitive, though; that&#x27;s usually disk and the internal network, for logging, telemetry and control. Typically a trading application will keep a thread running on the shared core and send messages between that and its dedicated core thread using lock-free queues, or simple atomics. As you say, writing for bare metal is a lot of hassle and it&#x27;s unnecessary for the parts of a trading application that don&#x27;t need to be super fast; using a commodity OS and isolating resources gives the best of both worlds.')
