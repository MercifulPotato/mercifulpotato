Item(by='natdempk', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1601932688, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24691798, text='I wrote a bit about Orleans here (and also Akka if you scroll up a bit): <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;dist-prog-book.com&#x2F;chapter&#x2F;3&#x2F;message-passing.html#orleans" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;dist-prog-book.com&#x2F;chapter&#x2F;3&#x2F;message-passing.html#orl...</a><p>At the end of the day, I think Orleans and Akka are conceptually different types&#x2F;notions of what an actor framework is.<p>&gt; Orleans uses a different notion of identity than other actor systems. In other systems an “actor” might refer to a behavior and instances of that actor might refer to identities that the actor represents like individual users. In Orleans, an actor represents that persistent identity, and the actual instantiations are in fact reconcilable copies of that identity.')
