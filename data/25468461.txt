Item(by='Enginerrrd', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608308023, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25468096, text='Strange, because chess <i>definitely</i> has elements of risk calculation based on your uncertainty.  Without a computer, you cannot calculate all the lines ahead, so many decisions come down to trade-offs involving uncertainty.  Colloquially, this usually is called &quot;strategy&quot;, which involve decisions that are much longer term than calculated tactics, and therefore involve a great deal of uncertainty.<p>Tactics still dominate, but strategy leads to tactics.<p>You&#x27;re often left making decisions on the basis of, well, maybe if I do this I&#x27;ll be able to poke my rook through later, but maybe they&#x27;ll win counterattacking the file I&#x27;m leaving.')
