I think it’s hard to design a study and measure.<p>And I think there’s a deontological vs. utilitarian aspect as I think if applicants learned I was randomly picking 1 of 30 pretty goods it would discourage high quality applicants.<p>For me, I think the additional 100 hours interviewing helps and is worth it.<p>I’d be interested in hearing of hiring managers and orgs who just hire randomly from minimally qualified applicants. I hear from lots of applicants who claim they are minimally qualified that this is a successful strategy. But, naturally, they are a bit biased and not very useful for deciding how to hire people.')
