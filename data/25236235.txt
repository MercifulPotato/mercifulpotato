Although k6 gives you a lot of flexibility to decide what your pass&#x2F;fail criteria are with its custom metrics ( <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;k6.io&#x2F;docs&#x2F;using-k6&#x2F;metrics#custom-metrics" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;k6.io&#x2F;docs&#x2F;using-k6&#x2F;metrics#custom-metrics</a> ), checks (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;k6.io&#x2F;docs&#x2F;using-k6&#x2F;checks" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;k6.io&#x2F;docs&#x2F;using-k6&#x2F;checks</a>) and thresholds ( <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;k6.io&#x2F;docs&#x2F;using-k6&#x2F;thresholds" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;k6.io&#x2F;docs&#x2F;using-k6&#x2F;thresholds</a> ).<p>See the example in <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;loadimpact&#x2F;k6&#x2F;#checks-and-thresholds" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;loadimpact&#x2F;k6&#x2F;#checks-and-thresholds</a>. If, at the end of the test run, some of these rules (encoded as `thresholds`) were unsatisfied, k6 will exit with a non-zero exit code (and thus fail your CI check):\n- The 95th percentile of all HTTP request durations should be be less than 500ms\n- The 99th percentile of all HTTP request durations that were tagged with `staticAsset:yes` should be less than 250ms\n- The failure rate of the checks should be less than 1%, although if it&#x27;s more than 5% the test will abort immediately.')
