Item(by='brg', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605592061, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25110667, text='I think one answer is a new, lighter weight libel law that is a civil offense.<p>At the moment, current libel and defamation laws in the US have too high a test to overcome.  In particular, the public figure can be stretched to cover online profiles.  The tests of negligence and malice are also too strict.<p>Strict liability for untrue or derogatory statements posted on social media platforms could make the poser liable for 5k to 10k fines.  This would go a long way in making discourse more factual and stemming hyperbole.')
