Item(by='jffhn', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602370515, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24740798, text='&gt;Everyone who has taken a class on complex systems is aware of the phenomenon of emergence.<p>Reminds me of some conference (lost the URL) of Robert Laughlin, where he said (if I recall correctly) that rigidity (e.g. of a steel bar) was an emergent property, to which someone in the audience objected that sure enough it should be deducible from quantum mechanics, which he dismissed.<p>That would mean that emergence can occur even in simple systems (regularly packed atoms), and for quite basic properties.')
