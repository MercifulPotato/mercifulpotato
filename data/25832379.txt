Item(by='GordonS', descendants=None, kids=[25832584, 25832651], score=None, time=1611055754, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25826835, text='&gt; I&#x27;ll just prescribe what I normally do, since I&#x27;m comfortable with it<p>This is actually something that drives me absolutely nuts about doctors in the UK (I presume they are the same elsewhere) - <i>inertia</i>.<p>It&#x27;s like doctors leave medical school with &quot;best practices&quot; about what they should prescribe - like they are glorified, human decision trees - and then across their 40-year career, they <i>never</i> read a paper, <i>never</i> read any new guidance, and general never <i>change</i>.<p>Inertia seems to be a particular problem in the NHS, where doctors have a set list they are willing to prescribe. Why? Because it&#x27;s what they&#x27;ve prescribed previously, so they are &quot;comfortable&quot; with it. You can see there is a bit of a &quot;chicken and egg problem&quot; with other medications.')
