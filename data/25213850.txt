Item(by='mindslight', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606335744, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25213689, text='50k attempts is tiny, like a 3 character password or a single account run through &#x2F;usr&#x2F;share&#x2F;dict&#x2F;words.<p>If you&#x27;re talking about preventing logins in case your own userdb has been leaked, that&#x27;s madness. Force users to change their passwords.<p>If you&#x27;re worried about password reuse between other sites that have leaked, then the real answer is to generate a username or secondary password for each user that they won&#x27;t be able to share between sites. In fact with the prevalence of password managers, generating users&#x27; passwords for them might just be the better approach these days. And just fall back to email auth every time if they don&#x27;t want to store it.<p>Duct taping your broken system by throwing up an annoyance for every user from a non-residential IP is not the way.')
