Item(by='throwbackThurs', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605079545, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25055309, text='In the United States, Reform Judaism rejects the concept that any rules or rituals should be considered necessary for conversion to Judaism. In the late 19th century, the Central Conference of American Rabbis, the official body of American Reform rabbis, formally resolved to permit the admission of converts &quot;without any initiatory rite, ceremony, or observance whatsoever&quot;. (CCAR Yearbook 3 (1893), 73–95; American Reform Responsa (ARR), no. 68, at 236–237.)')
