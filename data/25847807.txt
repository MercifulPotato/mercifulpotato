Not my call, as I don&#x27;t employ any.<p>I&#x27;d say Agile methodologies have done well in exposing the key factors for successful delivery, and they seem not very amenable to generalisation:\n- creative endeavours such as software creation are fraught with estimation difficulties (contrast, say, with migrating VMs from on-prem to cloud)\n- whoever prioritises &#x2F; deprioritises features will have a large say in the accuracy\n- whatever technologies imposed&#x2F;chosen will have a reasonable say in the accuracy\n- whatever architecture decided on will have a reasonable say in the accuracy\n- unforeseen requirements that may necessitate architecture &#x2F; tech &#x2F; people changes have a large say in the accuracy\n- individuals&#x27; skills in a team will have a very large say in the accuracy\n- how long a team has worked together &#x2F; in an architecture &#x2F; with a technology will have a very large say in the accuracy<p>We know all this. What can we learn? Genuine question.')
