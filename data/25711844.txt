It&#x27;s a reminder to <i>sometimes</i> think about the process itself. It can lead to discoveries that can improve the entire process.<p>The &#x27;erroneous truism&#x27; is like the saying &quot;Don&#x27;t judge a book by its cover.&quot; The meaning of the saying isn&#x27;t that you must ignore the cover and judge every book by its content. Picking out a book to read from the library would be impossible. We still <i>need</i> to make decisions based on heuristics. The saying is simply a reminder that sometimes a poor outward appearance can have a gem hidden underneath.')
