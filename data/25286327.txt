Item(by='heipei', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606979065, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25286218, text='I was the only one using Linux (read: sub-par HW acceleration coupled with less-beefy HW) with Chrome with a user-facing web-app at a previous employer. I noticed that the UI was janky on scroll, and when I opened DevTools&#x2F;Rendering&#x2F;Paint Flashing I realised everything was being repainted on scroll. The culprit ultimately was a JS library deep down in the frontend that had this unintended side-behaviour. None of the UI devs ever noticed it because on their 15&quot; MBPs on OS X everything ran &quot;smooth enough&quot;.')
