It&#x27;s a very, very small core of contributors.<p>Toward your example, the 161 &quot;active&quot; users[2] (meaning any number of edits in the last 30 days) as of today on English UESP combined for 5,974 edits in that span. Of those edits, 4,779 were from 13 users. 3,039 — more than half of the last month&#x27;s activity — were from the top 5 users.<p>On the non-English UESPs? No active users in Portuguese, one in Italian, none in Arabic.[3][4][5]<p>From my experience at least, it&#x27;s because it&#x27;s work — fun work, at times, but still work. Translating MDN content is also work. Doing _any_ documentation of _anything_ is work. Hell, I burned out on fiction documentation work before I burned out on paid work.<p>Some people do enjoy it!
