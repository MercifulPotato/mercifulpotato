Item(by='z2', descendants=None, kids=[25284068, 25281430, 25283839], score=None, time=1606937372, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25280187, text='They probably asked (conceptually) for a &quot;fapiao&quot;, which is a very formal invoice in China but required for reimbursement [0] and is a government-acknowledged piece of paper declaring that a sales transaction really happened. Asking for one there would require the restaurant to log revenue live with the tax bureau. I discovered this by mistakenly asking for one in China, thinking it was just a receipt. I was led to the office of the restaurant, where they printed it onto half an A4 paper with carbon copies, e-verification serial number, and QR code. There are even more fascinating patterns that emerge from the incentives here--business don&#x27;t like declaring revenue, so these sometimes have scratch-to-win lottery tickets built in, so to encourage everyone to request one (thus preventing tax evasion) [1].<p>The less formal option is a &quot;shouju&quot; but doesn&#x27;t work for official business reimbursement.<p>0: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.thebeijinger.com&#x2F;blog&#x2F;2019&#x2F;08&#x2F;29&#x2F;what-is-a-fapiao-and-when-do-i-need-one" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.thebeijinger.com&#x2F;blog&#x2F;2019&#x2F;08&#x2F;29&#x2F;what-is-a-fapia...</a><p>1: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.globalfromasia.com&#x2F;fapiao&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.globalfromasia.com&#x2F;fapiao&#x2F;</a>')
