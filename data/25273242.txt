Item(by='analog31', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606883071, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25269590, text='Indeed, PCB&#x27;s achieved a high level of standardization and data transfer long before other processes came along such as CNC machining. It&#x27;s been more than a couple decades since I ordered my first boards from a board house, but after they received my Gerber&#x27;s, I heard nothing from them until boards came back.<p>In contrast, even today, sending any level of drawing to a machine shop at the time would have immediately resulted in a phone call to talk about exactly how I wanted something made, and why it couldn&#x27;t really be made that way. While there are &quot;quick turn&quot; shops that can work directly from files, it&#x27;s definitely a more recent development.<p>Even though &quot;Gerbers&quot; were a deeply primitive format, they got the job done.<p>At my workplace, one of the old-timers showed me the automated wire-wrapping machine, long since retired, on its way to the scrap yard.')
