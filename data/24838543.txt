Item(by='hcarvalhoalves', descendants=None, kids=[24839967], score=None, time=1603208001, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24838064, text='I&#x27;ve found one way to conquer fear and develop perseverance is to just (gradually) go thru enough hard things and learn to be okay w&#x2F; discomfort.<p>I&#x27;ve never been as active as I wanted in my young years due to weak health, and recently started engaging in outdoor activities I always wanted to do (long-distance hiking, climbing, scuba diving), mostly because I don&#x27;t enjoy hitting the gym and looking at a wall, but I didn&#x27;t anticipate the benefit it would have in my psyche.<p>Now, when faced w&#x2F; hardships of life or random sources of stressors, I can relate back to some past experience and think &quot;hey, I did that crazy thing, of course I can handle this other thing here&quot;. I don&#x27;t know if this psychological phenomena has a name, I would only describe as &quot;developing thick skin&quot;. I think it also happens naturally to individuals who had a hard upbringing and are hard working nonetheless. Maybe there&#x27;s some idea to explore here.')
