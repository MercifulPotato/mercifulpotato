Item(by='AnthonyMouse', descendants=None, kids=[24920916], score=None, time=1603898254, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24919674, text='Cryptographic signing is itself almost totally useless, and possibly worse than useless if people trust it when they shouldn&#x27;t, because the signing keys by definition have to be in the device. Then you have to keep every model of every device secure against key extraction, which, let&#x27;s face it, isn&#x27;t happening; or the attacker will be able to sign their fake with the keys from whichever device has a vulnerability. And the vulnerability itself need not even be published.')
