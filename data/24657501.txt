Item(by='karaterobot', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1601597410, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24649770, text='I suspect this article is trying to problematize the issue more than describing an existing, widespread controversy among historians.<p>It lumps digital restoration, upscaling, frame interpolation, and colorization together, but all of these techniques are different, and some are more controversial than others.<p>For example, it&#x27;s hard to think of an argument against using AI to remove imperfections from an image, if you are not troubled by having a human do the same thing. In both cases, information is being added where it had been lost. There&#x27;s no way around that.<p>Colorization is a different thing altogether. But, pretending that historians view noise removal and colorization as equally problematic is misleading.')
