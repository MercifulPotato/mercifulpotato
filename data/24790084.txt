Item(by='pimterry', descendants=None, kids=[24790389], score=None, time=1602777923, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24789095, text='Interestingly, Spain has already started on regulating to handle this. An english-language writeup: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;english.elpais.com&#x2F;economy_and_business&#x2F;2020-06-26&#x2F;flexible-schedules-and-covered-expenses-spain-drafts-bill-protecting-the-rights-of-remote-workers.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;english.elpais.com&#x2F;economy_and_business&#x2F;2020-06-26&#x2F;f...</a><p>In short, for remote working employees (any employee that spends &gt;30% of their time working outside an employer-designated office&#x2F;client site&#x2F;etc over 3+ months), all employers must have a written contract with the employee, that must:<p>* Define the tools &amp; workspace necessary to provide a sufficient &amp; safe workspace, for which the employer must cover any costs<p>* Define working hours (with some requirements on flexibility)<p>* Be voluntary, in both directions (employers can&#x27;t fire people who don&#x27;t want to change to remote work, employees aren&#x27;t entitled to remote work if employers don&#x27;t agree)<p>* Detail how, if at all, the employee&#x27;s productivity might be monitored, and do so with reasonable regard to privacy and ability to disconnect from work<p>* Ensure equal treatment of on-site &amp; remote employees, including pay, job stability, promotions, etc.<p>There&#x27;s some caveats in there for force majeure, such that this doesn&#x27;t immediately apply for the unavoidable remote setups created by COVID, but will apply for ongoing remote work where that continues in future.')
