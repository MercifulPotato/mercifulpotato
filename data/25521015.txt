Item(by='CobrastanJorji', descendants=None, kids=[25521522, 25521410], score=None, time=1608751016, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25520414, text='I&#x27;ve had this discussion during interviews as a disarming technique. Candidates will sometimes express nervousness at the beginning and say stuff like &quot;I&#x27;m terrible at interviews&quot; or &quot;it&#x27;s been a while since I&#x27;ve had to do this kind of programming,&quot; and I try to respond by making it clear that I understand that the whiteboard coding has very little in common with the actual job and that I&#x27;m not judging them, so when I dutifully proceed to ask them to invert a linked list, they hopefully don&#x27;t feel as judged when they approach the problem. Seems to help at least a bit. Mind you, I&#x27;d prefer NOT asking those questions, but I&#x27;m not empowered to make that change at my workplace and I&#x27;m honestly not sure what I&#x27;d replace it with if I were.')
