Item(by='augustk', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603965904, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24921796, text='I guess a verb phrase comes up naturally when you are eager to implement a new function and think of all the steps required to calculate the result. However, a noun phrase is a more proper abstraction since the implementation may change to simply return a cached value. (see also the Uniform Access Principle).<p>Anyway, here is my naming strategy:<p>1. Boolean pure function<p>Use an <i>adjective</i> phrase where the adjective is the last word, for instance <i>UrlValid</i> or <i>DefinitionFound</i>.<p>2. Non-boolean pure function<p>Use a <i>noun</i> phrase where the noun is the last word, for instance <i>CurrentDefinition</i> or <i>DefinitionUrl</i>.<p>3. Non-pure &quot;function&quot;<p>Use a <i>verb</i> phrase where the verb is the first word, for instance <i>PrintError</i> or <i>ReadInput</i>.')
