Item(by='pontus', descendants=None, kids=[25660977], score=None, time=1609958684, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25659949, text='While the Schrodinger equation is linear, that doesn&#x27;t mean that the time evolution of various derived quantities are also linear. For example, the expectation value of position follows a nonlinear equation of motion even though the schrodinger equation is linear.<p>This is known as Ehrenfest&#x27;s theorem:\n<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.m.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Ehrenfest_theorem" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.m.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Ehrenfest_theorem</a><p>In other words, nonlinear time evolution is natural in quantum mechanics for quantities other than the wave function and does not require collapse.')
