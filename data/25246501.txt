Item(by='crazygringo', descendants=None, kids=[25246645], score=None, time=1606670833, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25243260, text='Pop-up notifications from background processes only really became necessary once people started having a lot of internet-connected programs that needed to notify you of incoming things. Back when the only thing was your e-mail inbox, you didn&#x27;t need a separate notification service.<p>Obviously then it was then extended for things like a long process completing, etc.<p>But back in 1990 or 1995, there&#x27;s wasn&#x27;t a need for something like Growl. Your e-mail inbox and the occasional beep and modal dialog did the job just fine.<p>To counter your &quot;most things are obvious in retrospect&quot; philosophy, you might be interested in the &quot;multiple discovery&quot; viewpoint [1] which says precisely the opposite -- that, extended to design, essentially says that the need for a solution becomes obvious to people at about the same time, and that people will solve it in similar ways because they&#x27;re facing the same constraints.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Multiple_discovery" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Multiple_discovery</a>')
