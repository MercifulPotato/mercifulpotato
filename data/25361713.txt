Item(by='powersnail', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607537748, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25349214, text='Confucius also has a similar remark:<p>&quot;Studying* without thinking leads to incomprehension; thinking without studying leads to peril.&quot;<p>However, I&#x27;d like to offer a counter-argument here.<p>There different ways of reading. Some are passive. Some are active. Reading with a critical mind is anything but detrimental to creativity.<p>Notably, great authors are often voracious readers, and reading is what they all agreed to be the foundations of their craft. It didn&#x27;t hinder their creativity. The reason is two-folds: 1) they read critically; 2) they wrote.<p>The true way to foster creativity, is to create. Unless one read so much that no time is left to do anything else, reading isn&#x27;t the enemy of creating.<p>And a further note on vicariously living one&#x27;s life. I don&#x27;t really see what&#x27;s wrong with that. A person gets one life only, and it may be a fortunate one, a challenging one, a boring one, or one that&#x27;s an absolute dismay. Drudging through the day and fantasizing through the night is hardly the worst form of life, in my opinion.<p>* &quot;Studying&quot; here refers to the intake of information.')
