So whoever comes in with ARM better be prepared for lots of competitions and low margins in a cutthroat environment. That&#x27;s not a very attractive market proposition, especially since the x86 makers won&#x27;t just take this lying down.<p>I think we&#x27;ll be in a weird place soon.<p>Apple will be doing Apple things and increasing its market share but never taking over (maybe it will reach 20-25%? who knows).<p>Many server environments will switch to ARM, especially the big cloud providers that can make their own chips.<p>Qualcomm will push some more desktop class SOCs but I doubt they&#x27;ll reach Apple&#x27;s performance levels so they risk having their lunch eaten, especially by AMD.<p>So it&#x27;s possible that the PC desktop&#x2F;laptop market might be &quot;stuck&quot; with x86 for many more decades.<p>There is also a scenario where Intel or AMD come up with some brilliant designs and catch up completely or well enough to Apple in which case this will all have been a tempest in a teacup.')
