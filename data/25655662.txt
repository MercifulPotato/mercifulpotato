Item(by='simiones', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609920918, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25649897, text='I think the GP understood this, but their point was that it is a false assumption that introducing the uncertainty principle in a classical simulation would simplify the computation.<p>That is, if we think that the universe is a simulation running on a classical computer, then quantum effects such as the uncertainty principle CAN&#x27;T be a &quot;shortcut&quot; to make the simulation easier - as in fact they make the simulation literally exponentially harder. If we assume that the universe is a simulation running on a quantum computer, then quantum effects are fundamental anyway.<p>Of course, everyone understands that this is a simple joke. Still, it&#x27;s interesting that it goes completely against our intuitions as computer simulation designers.')
