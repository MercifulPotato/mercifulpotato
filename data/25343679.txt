Item(by='memsom', descendants=None, kids=[25344577], score=None, time=1607423211, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25331309, text='I think Danish has a more complicated system. 1 - 20 is similar to most Germanic languages, by extension Indo European in general. But, then it gets weirder the closer you get to 100:<p>20 - Tyve (Swedish tjugo, Norwegian: tjue) &#x2F;\n30 - Tredive (Swedish: trettio, Norwegian: tretti) &#x2F;\n40 - Fyrre (Swedish: fyrtio, Norwegian: førti) &#x2F;\n50 - Halvtreds (Swedish: femtio, Norwegian: femti) &#x2F;\n60 - tres (Swedish: sextio, Norwegian: seksti) &#x2F;\n70 - halvfjerds (Swedish: sjuttio, Norwegian: sytti) &#x2F;\n80 - firs (Swedish: åttio, Norwegian: åtti) &#x2F;\n90 - halvfems (Swedish: nittio, Norwegian: nitti) &#x2F;\n100 - hundrede (Swedish: hundra, Norwegian: hundre)<p>If I understand correctly, the numbers are contractions of longer words... for example, 60 is tres, sixtieth is tresindstyvende, which means something like &quot;three lots of twenty&quot;-th. Weird? Yeah. But no idea what halvfjerds means... it&#x27;s making my brain hurt trying to work it out.')
