But S takes 3, K takes 2, and I takes 1. This isn&#x27;t strictly correct because S&#x2F;K&#x2F;I isn&#x27;t a function, but rather a chain of lambdas with no free variables (and we get the number of &quot;parameters the function takes&quot; by counting the lambda symbols). See §1 in [1]. In your specific case,<p><pre><code>    S 11 12 = 12\n    K 11 12 13 = 11 13 (12 13)\n    I 11 = 11\n</code></pre>\nKeep in mind that the literals &quot;11,&quot; &quot;12,&quot; and &quot;13&quot; in λ-Calculus aren&#x27;t <i>numbers</i>.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;personal.utdallas.edu&#x2F;~gupta&#x2F;courses&#x2F;apl&#x2F;lambda.pdf" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;personal.utdallas.edu&#x2F;~gupta&#x2F;courses&#x2F;apl&#x2F;lambda.pdf</a>')
