Item(by='zanecodes', descendants=None, kids=[24843879], score=None, time=1603223952, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24841503, text='Not to mention that the design constraints of a reaction wheel and a drive wheel are almost polar opposites: a reaction wheel needs to have the largest possible moment of inertia, to maximize the change in angular momentum for a given mass and torque; meanwhile, a drive wheel should have the smallest possible moment of inertia, to maximize the change in angular speed for a given mass and torque, particularly since you typically want to minimize unsprung mass (in fairness, the demoed robot didn&#x27;t have any suspension to worry about).')
