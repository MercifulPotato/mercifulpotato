Item(by='mturmon', descendants=None, kids=[25062681], score=None, time=1605123709, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25061829, text='One of the usual examples given in reaction to this question is the puzzle of Euclid&#x27;s 5th postulate, the parallel-lines postulate.  People tried for centuries to prove that it could be deduced from the other axioms.  Turned out, it was independent of the others.<p>A proof system could identify cases where you need hidden assumptions in order to make proofs.  In this case, you would get a null result from the oracle, which would be valuable in itself.<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Parallel_postulate" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Parallel_postulate</a>')
