Item(by='dehrmann', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609700514, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25623709, text='(guessing you mean apostrophes)<p>It&#x27;s because they have two different uses (three if you count nested quotes, but those aren&#x27;t common and are pretty easy to figure out), contractions and possession, and they seemingly collide on words like &quot;its&quot; where you&#x27;d think it could mean either.<p>Not sure if you&#x27;ve already learned this (or if it helps), but English used to be declined, and its pronouns still are, e.g. they&#x2F;their&#x2F;them. That&#x27;s why &quot;its&quot; isn&#x27;t contracted; the possessive marker is already in the word.')
