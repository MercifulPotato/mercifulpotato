Item(by='dragonwriter', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604371330, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24975365, text='&gt; Monoliths can do transactionally consistent stuff.<p>So can microservices, both internal to the service (via simple transactions) and between services (via distributed transactions, e.g., 2PC), though a good service design minimizes the need for the latter and is mostly guided by consistency boundaries as to where to draw service boundaries.)<p>(It&#x27;s true that there are naive microservices architectures pursued that are essentially normalized relational designs with a one-service-per-table rule, but that&#x27;s just bad—and usually cargo-cult—design.')
