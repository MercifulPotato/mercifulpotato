Any way to get a stack trace can be a rudimentary profiler, just keep looking at stack traces until you see what comes up most commonly. If you&#x27;ve got good tools, you can get a map of the code you want to read first. Here is an old MySQL executing some queries: <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.brendangregg.com&#x2F;FlameGraphs&#x2F;cpuflamegraphs.html#FlameGraph" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.brendangregg.com&#x2F;FlameGraphs&#x2F;cpuflamegraphs.html#...</a><p>A debugger can also help. You can step through code as it runs. You can also often identify which code is associated with a feature, by putting breakpoints in likely places (dispatch functions!) and triggering the feature.')
