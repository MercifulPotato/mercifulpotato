Item(by='Zak', descendants=None, kids=[24947101], score=None, time=1604090926, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24946239, text='&gt; <i>Rewarding honest voting means that people have perfect information and act rationally</i><p>Democracy itself implies that we expect voters to be informed and rational. Since we know that isn&#x27;t the case, perhaps the important business of ruling countries should be left up to those who are very well educated and who possess a certain... <i>nobility</i>.<p>What I like about Condorcet methods is the Condorcet criterion itself. I have yet to find a better definition of a candidate being most preferred by the voters than that the candidate would beat every other candidate in a two-person race. The later-no-harm criterion (that giving a positive rating to a less favored candidate cannot hurt a more favored candidate) is also valuable and STAR doesn&#x27;t satisfy it.<p>STAR does seem easy to count though, and without doing much research, likely to elect the Condorcet winner most of the time.')
