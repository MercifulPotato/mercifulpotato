Item(by='babesh', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603467189, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24869077, text='The title is disingenuous.<p>The article is based on <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.equality-of-opportunity.org&#x2F;assets&#x2F;documents&#x2F;inventors_summary.pdf" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.equality-of-opportunity.org&#x2F;assets&#x2F;documents&#x2F;inve...</a> which states that “Children at the top of their 3rd grade math class are much more likely to become inventors, but only if they come from high-income families (Figure 2)”.<p>The chart does show high performing kids producing more inventions irrespective of class but with the rich kids producing at approximately double the rate. There seems to be a clear pattern of increasing invention with increasing scores for poor kids but not so for rich kids till you get to the top performing kids.<p>The secret to success appears to be a combination of talent and opportunity in approximately equal measures. The article also points out that opportunities are not equal.<p>Sounds like the classical nature or nurture question with the answer being both. You aren’t getting very far without talent.')
