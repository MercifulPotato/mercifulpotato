Item(by='klodolph', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603948388, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24927414, text='Sometimes the tar files are just archives that get extracted at &#x2F;, which is a fairly primitive but functional way to distribute packages.<p>For a description of the directories, see: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Filesystem_Hierarchy_Standard" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Filesystem_Hierarchy_Standard</a><p>Note that these directories do not actually have many hard and fast rules... while you can&#x27;t change &#x2F;proc, you can argue all you like over whether something goes in &#x2F;bin or &#x2F;sbin, or argue whether it should go in &#x2F;usr. There are a ton of differences in the details.')
