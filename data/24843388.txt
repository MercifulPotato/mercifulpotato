Item(by='twoodfin', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603236924, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24843264, text='Modern processors speed up large copies with SIMD instructions like AVX.<p>The Linux kernel chooses not to use SIMD because the cost of copying &amp; saving the relevant processor state (registers, in this case) would be borne by every syscall and consume precious CPU cache.<p>This would be true for any similar copying mechanism that spanned multiple cycles; the state—however represented—must be preserved.')
