Item(by='dwohnitmok', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606970048, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25285177, text='Amusingly that link actually undersells how English orthography fails to capture English pronunciation and even its final sentence has many pronunciation &quot;mistakes&quot; by its own standard.<p>&gt; Fainali, xen, aafte sam 20 iers ov orxogrefkl riform, wi wud hev a lojikl, kohirnt speling in ius xrewawt xe Ingliy-spiking werld.<p>The first &quot;a&quot; in &quot;Fainali&quot; is not pronounced the same as the second &quot;a&quot; in &quot;Fainali&quot; (e.g. finally does not rhyme with rally and rawly, the correspondence between the latter two depending on your own regional accent). The &quot;x&quot; in &quot;xen&quot; and &quot;xe&quot; is not the same sound as the &quot;x&quot; in &quot;xrewawt&quot; or in &quot;orxogrefkl.&quot; The &quot;o&quot; in &quot;lojikl&quot; is not the same &quot;o&quot; as in &quot;kohirnt.&quot; The &quot;e&quot; in &quot;xe&quot; is not the same &quot;e&quot; as in &quot;xen&quot; and is not the same &quot;e&quot; as in &quot;orxogrefkl.&quot; And more.<p>And that&#x27;s not even touching stuff that has heavy regional variation such as the &quot;e&quot; in &quot;aafte&quot; and the &quot;i&quot; in &quot;kohirnt.&quot;')
