Casual players who believe that they are good persist in the illusion because the element of chance obscures their deficits. At its heart, backgammonâ€™s cruelty resides in the dramatic volatility of the dice. Even a player who builds flawless structures on the board can lose to a novice. One notable match lasted five days, with both players getting up only for bathroom breaks.
