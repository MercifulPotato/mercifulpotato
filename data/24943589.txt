Item(by='keb_', descendants=None, kids=[24943662], score=None, time=1604073604, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24942037, text='If you&#x27;re a techie, you are likely capable enough to learn and use a static-site gen and host your blog on Github Pages, Vercel, Surge, Netlify, etc. If you&#x27;re a techie, you probably also care <i>enough</i> to not inundate your readers with a crapton of intrusive trackers and weird browser hacks&#x2F;overrides that Medium employs.<p>If you don&#x27;t want to use an SSG, or if you&#x27;re not a techie, you can use or self-host: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;write.as&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;write.as&#x2F;</a>, <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;bearblog.dev&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;bearblog.dev&#x2F;</a>, <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;rwtxt.com&#x2F;public" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;rwtxt.com&#x2F;public</a>, <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;the-federation.info&#x2F;plume" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;the-federation.info&#x2F;plume</a><p>The downside of the above options is that they are nowhere near as prominent and thus don&#x27;t provide the same level of discoverability as Medium -- in which case, if you&#x27;re really hurting for cash and are trying to make a career of writing, maybe it is in <i>your</i> best interest to stick to platforms like Medium, despite how intrusive and reader-unfriendly they are.<p>EDIT: I am hesitant to recommend <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;dev.to&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;dev.to&#x2F;</a> because I have this sinking feeling that it will eventually become like Medium. Also, semi-related, but Dev.to uses endless scroll, which I think is very bad for readers (also it makes it impossible to click links in the footer).')
