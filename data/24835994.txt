Item(by='dmurray', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603191848, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24835758, text='Correct: Raspberry Pi don&#x27;t have the absolute right to prevent you from having websites with &quot;raspberry-pi&quot; in the domain name. If you treat this document as <i>legal advice</i> from RPi, it was not written in good faith.<p>If you treat it as a <i>licensing agreement</i> or a <i>contract</i> instead, it would be plausible to include clauses like this. &quot;You may do A, B and C, but you may not do D&quot; = &quot;by entering into this agreement you acquire the rights to do A, B and C in return for foregoing the right to do D&quot;. That would be perfectly fine.<p>I don&#x27;t see anything in the document that grants you rights you didn&#x27;t already have (to use RPi&#x27;s IP only in accordance with fair use). Additionally, it describes itself as &quot;guidelines&quot; and never as a contract or an agreement. So, bad faith it is.<p>Here&#x27;s a substantial review of the treatment of &quot;gripe sites&quot; in US law [0]. In general, the legal situation has been that if your site is non-commercial, then your use of the trademark, including in a domain name, is protected free speech.<p>[0] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.sgrlaw.com&#x2F;ttl-articles&#x2F;858&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.sgrlaw.com&#x2F;ttl-articles&#x2F;858&#x2F;</a>')
