Aren&#x27;t they equally useful as market messages?<p>If I am willing to tie up some money holding an instrument afloat at overvalued prices longer than you are willing to remain in your short position, haven&#x27;t we ultimately, together in our conflict, created a useful message about the underlying asset?<p>Maybe I think Company G is worth $20 but not $40, but if you are selling it short at $5, and I&#x27;m prepared to be illiquid for a while, don&#x27;t I send the correct market signal by squeezing you out of your short?')
