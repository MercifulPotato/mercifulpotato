Burrito monads are the prime example of what <i>not</i> to do, but my university course on category theory didn&#x27;t start with the lecturer throwing out definitions, it started with an explanation in plain English (well, it wasn&#x27;t English but you get the point :)) of what the theory is trying to accomplish. I&#x27;m completely behind the Dijkstrian viewpoint that computing is a radical novelty in itself, but I don&#x27;t see why trying to explain the purpose of what you are doing is bad.\nTo make a more low-level example, pretty much every explanation ever of the concept of R-module started with &#x27;we are trying to generalize the concept of a vector space to sets that have a ring structure but not a field structure&#x27;, not &#x27;here, have a bunch of axioms&#x27;.')
