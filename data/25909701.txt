Item(by='jefftk', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611612981, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25909548, text='If you want to prevent fingerprinting, you need to look at where the identifying bits are coming from. (ex: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;coveryourtracks.eff.org&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;coveryourtracks.eff.org&#x2F;</a>)  The IP address provides enough bits to uniquely identify many users, and when combined with just a few more bits, to identify almost anyone.<p>TOR is one solution here, which you could potentially also describe as &quot;adding forced MitM to every connection&quot;.  The proposals in <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;bslassey&#x2F;ip-blindness&#x2F;blob&#x2F;master&#x2F;near_path_nat.md" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;bslassey&#x2F;ip-blindness&#x2F;blob&#x2F;master&#x2F;near_pa...</a> and <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;bslassey&#x2F;ip-blindness&#x2F;blob&#x2F;master&#x2F;willful_ip_blindness.md" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;bslassey&#x2F;ip-blindness&#x2F;blob&#x2F;master&#x2F;willful...</a> have different tradeoffs than TOR, with the &quot;TOR is painfully slow&quot; problem being a big one.<p>If you have better ideas, though, I would be very interested in reading them!')
