Item(by='hardwaregeek', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608243618, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25461033, text='Infix operators are certainly more readable if you&#x27;re familiar with the operators.<p>I imagine the possible ways of handling non-nullable types (and algebraic types as a whole) as a spectrum:<p>* TS style explicit if branches: Easy for beginners, annoying to experts<p>* Rust style methods on Option&#x2F;Result&#x2F;etc: Slightly confusing for beginners, somewhat elegant for experts<p>* Haskell&#x2F;OCaml style infix operators: Confusing for beginners, elegant and easy for experts<p>Note that this is beginners <i>to functional programming</i>. Not necessarily beginners in programming as a whole. Plenty of smart people would get tripped up by a `&lt;$&gt;`')
