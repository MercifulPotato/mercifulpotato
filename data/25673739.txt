Item(by='bzbarsky', descendants=None, kids=[25674012], score=None, time=1610039233, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25673573, text='&gt; The only speech assured by the constitution is from your literal mouth<p>Even if you are going to narrowly interpret freedom of speech as &quot;constitutionally protected rights only&quot;, the US Constitution explicitly says:<p><pre><code>  Congress shall make no law ... abridging the freedom of\n  speech, or of the press; or the right of the people\n  peaceably to assemble, and to petition the Government for\n  a redress of grievances.\n</code></pre>\n&quot;of the press&quot; is not &quot;from your literal mouth&quot;, pretty much by definition.  So please, let&#x27;s not misrepresent what the constitutional right is about, even if we debate what &quot;of the press&quot; means in today&#x27;s technology and media environment.')
