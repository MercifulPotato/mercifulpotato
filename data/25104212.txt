Longer fibers can be extracted, oriented, and used to make oriented strand board, which is like particleboard but with more tensile strength. Here&#x27;s some research in Brazil on that.[1] Brazil being the world&#x27;s largest produce of sugar cane residue, they have plenty of raw material to work with.<p>Probably good for making IKEA-type furniture even more cheaply.<p>[1] <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;article.sapub.org&#x2F;pdf&#x2F;10.5923.j.ijme.20120204.04.pdf" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;article.sapub.org&#x2F;pdf&#x2F;10.5923.j.ijme.20120204.04.pdf</a>')
