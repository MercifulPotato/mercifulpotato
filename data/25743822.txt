Item(by='mlyle', descendants=None, kids=[25743847], score=None, time=1610439475, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25743742, text='It&#x27;s a MEMS microphone: silicon electromechanical device + amplifier in one package.<p>First, this generally means that when depowered, the output signal line is connected to ground by the ESD protection path.<p>Second, the actual mechanism which picks up the audio is generally variably capacitive-- a few picofarads-- in MEMS mics, and requires a pretty high bias (~15-200V) voltage to induce a usable signal for the in-package amplifier to be able to pick up the audio.<p>To not only have the in-package amplifier turned off, but also no significant bias voltage across the microphone electrodes, and somehow pick this up on the other side of the trace would be somewhat miraculous.')
