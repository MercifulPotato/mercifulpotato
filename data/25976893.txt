Item(by='brmgb', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1612077964, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25975919, text='&gt; Can anything tradeable with a fixed supply become a currency?<p>Yes, very much so (including something with unpredictable supply). Through history, diverse things were used as currency including shells, salt, silver and gold.<p>The commonly used definition of a currency was coined by Aristotle and is that it&#x27;s an asset fulfilling three requirements:<p>- being an intermediary of exchange;<p>- being a store of value;<p>- being a unit of account.<p>You might notice that most cryptocurrencies are not actually currency owning to the first point.')
