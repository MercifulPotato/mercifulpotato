Item(by='nitrogen', descendants=None, kids=[24913012], score=None, time=1603837470, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24912114, text='<i>How is any of this remotely related to gender studies?</i><p>The bizarreness of linking feminism to climate change notwithstanding, I think that in the interest of productive discussion on HN, an interesting point can be found within it.<p>A lot of the fuel for the opposition to both feminism and climate change comes from the way both are portrayed in the media.  When people are repeatedly brow-beaten with an idea, and anyone who dissents is ostracized and insulted, it causes onlookers to move <i>away</i> from the position that is being shoved in their faces.<p>These techniques are sometimes misapplied in both equal rights and climate change, which ultimately hurts both causes.  These techniques might strengthen an already committed base, but they polarize and antagonize everyone not already sold.<p>This comic [0], linked last week in a different thread [1], shows the same thing in visual form, and I think there&#x27;s merit to the concept that overly vociferous opposition to idea X actually <i>increases</i> support of idea X.<p>[0] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;pbfcomics.com&#x2F;comics&#x2F;deeply-held-beliefs&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;pbfcomics.com&#x2F;comics&#x2F;deeply-held-beliefs&#x2F;</a> -- I don&#x27;t know anything about the site hosting the comic, but I think the comic stands on its own<p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=24832003" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;news.ycombinator.com&#x2F;item?id=24832003</a>')
