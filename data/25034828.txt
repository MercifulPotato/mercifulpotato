Item(by='TomGullen', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604930243, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25034240, text='If a stocks price is $100, and tomorrow there will be a news event where there is a 50% chance it will be worth $50, and a 50% chance it will be worth $200, the trading value today should probably be around $125 (it&#x27;s expected value or EV, $200 + $50 &#x2F; 2).<p>When the defining event actually occurs, it will either move from $125 to $50, or $125 to $200.<p>So the news event in this example is priced in at $125, but there is still scope for big moves when certainty is realised.<p>Obviously in real life the values are never as certain.  In the above example if the stocks ever trades &lt; $125 before the event, you would make money in the long run.<p>EMH shouldn&#x27;t be taken seriously as the market actors are compromised of irrational humans.')
