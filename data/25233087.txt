Item(by='jdbernard', descendants=None, kids=[25242276], score=None, time=1606515779, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25225763, text='I didn&#x27;t down vote you, and your question deserves a serious reply because the same principle is broadly useful.<p>Communication is hard. People actively in the team probably have the context to understand the differences, but that implied context is what tribal knowledge is made of.<p>OP indicated that there were non-trivial &quot;non-linear&quot; differences going from 1 to 2  and -1 to -2. The use of numbers implies a relationship that doesn&#x27;t really exist. The use of accurate language makes the actual meaning immediately obvious without the need for implied context. For example, I had no idea that only privileged devs could give 2s. &quot;Accepted&quot; and &quot;Declined&quot; do imply a finality that more accurately mirrors the intended usage.<p>This may seem overly analytical for something that is easy to just explain, but this kind of thing build up in layers.')
