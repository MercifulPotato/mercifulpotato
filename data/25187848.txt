Item(by='alisonkisk', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606145927, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25185391, text='Computers benefit from different kinds of tricks.<p>The key difference is that computers don&#x27;t have any trouble adding&#x2F;subtracting arbitrary 8&#x2F;16&#x2F;32&#x2F;64 numbers, but human performance gets much worse when there&#x27;s more than 1 or 2 non-zero  or carrying. So while humans like to find ways to round things off, computers care more about minimizing the number of additions and multiplications, but don&#x27;t have much preference for the (small, constant) complexity of each operation. (Except multiplying&#x2F;dividing by powers of 10 -- both humans and computers prefer that)')
