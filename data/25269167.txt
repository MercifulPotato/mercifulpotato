Leaving aside that not everyone supports (or is subject to) GDPR, that doesn&#x27;t justify controlling <i>individuals</i>.<p>There are arguments for addressing the power asymmetry in mass data collection. Those arguments do not extend to individuals communicating with each other, and they <i>certainly</i> do not extend to forcing those individuals to use technological measures that reduce the amount of control those individuals have over their own devices.')
