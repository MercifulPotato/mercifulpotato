Item(by='rayiner', descendants=None, kids=[25614695], score=None, time=1609608924, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25614389, text='&gt; Medicaid is not &quot;free&quot; there are requirements, but assuming you qualify, there are hidden costs.<p>There are low, published co-pays, similar to Italy.<p>The example you&#x27;re quoting is for Medicaid nursing home care, a very expensive program with a distinct set of requirements: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.medicaidplanningassistance.org&#x2F;medicaid-look-back-period&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.medicaidplanningassistance.org&#x2F;medicaid-look-bac...</a><p>&gt; When a senior is applying for long-term care Medicaid, whether that is for services in one’s home, an assisted living residence, or a nursing home, there is an asset (resource) limit. In order to be eligible for Medicaid, one cannot have assets greater than the limit. Medicaid’s look-back period is meant to prevent Medicaid applicants from giving away assets or selling them under fair market value in an attempt to meet Medicaid’s asset limit.<p>Italy does not have universal long-term care for the elderly: <a href="http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.ancien-longtermcare.eu&#x2F;sites&#x2F;default&#x2F;files&#x2F;ENEPRI%20_ANCIEN_%20RR%20No%2080%20Italy%20edited%20final.pdf" rel="nofollow">http:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.ancien-longtermcare.eu&#x2F;sites&#x2F;default&#x2F;files&#x2F;ENEPRI...</a>. Like the U.S., it has some support for lower-income people who need long-term care.')
