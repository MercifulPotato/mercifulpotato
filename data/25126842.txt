Item(by='astrobe_', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605635159, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25124384, text='&gt; I&#x27;m unable to fathom the notion that if a computer doesn&#x27;t work the way people want, the answer is for people to adapt to the computer. The whole point of computers is to do things for people.<p>If you try to do that too hard you run into the task automation problem:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;xkcd.com&#x2F;1319&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;xkcd.com&#x2F;1319&#x2F;</a><p>In other words, &quot;make the computer do things for me&quot; as a diminishing returns problem. This is what you don&#x27;t get.<p>What works best is a user-tool co-evolution. If I&#x27;m not mistaken, this is one of the core ideas of the Agile manifesto.<p>“We become what we behold. We shape our tools, and thereafter our tools shape us.”\n  <i>Marshall McLuhan</i>')
