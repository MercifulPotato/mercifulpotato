Item(by='vidarh', descendants=None, kids=[24766970, 24769095], score=None, time=1602601210, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24765526, text='Knowing if you&#x27;re copying or passing references is essential to understanding most languages that supports both.<p>It&#x27;s scary how many people are unaware of it, though.<p>Not nearly as dramatic as the example above, but I once cut the time spent on page generation for a commercial CMS by 30% my first day in a new job by realising they did excessive new allocation of strings instead of concatenation (which will amortise the allocations by allocating more space than needed in most C++ implementations).<p>I find it quite disturbing, because, while I prefer to use Ruby these days, C++ documentation is <i>really</i> explicit about guarantees provided by both the language and the standard library with respect to things like memory behaviour and algorithmic complexity.<p>There&#x27;s if anything <i>less</i> going on under the hood with C++ that you&#x27;re not explicitly told about than in most modern languages.')
