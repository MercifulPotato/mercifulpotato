Each portrait simply lists the nationality or ethnicity and then the name of the person. Some are French Algerians, some black.<p>I wonder what the point was here. Was it &quot;our enemies are bringing black people to Europe to fight us?&quot; That could be. I found another contemporary book called &quot;Der Völkercircus unserer Feinde&quot; - &quot;Our Enemies&#x27; Circus of Races&quot;, by Otto Frobenius, an anthropologist of a fairly racist kind.<p>Or it may not have been so simple. Otto Stiehl [1], the author of this book, was an architect. In 1916 he was in charge of a prison camp for Tartars who had fought on the allied side.
