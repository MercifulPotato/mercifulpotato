This is almost never the case.<p>There is ultimately <i>some</i> tradeoff between repairability and size&#x2F;weight&#x2F;thermals due to physics. But companies compromise on repairability way below an actual physical limit.<p>Examples:<p>- To replace the batteries of a MBP Retina, you must disassemble the entire motherboard before freeing up the batteries [0]. A lot of people end up damaging the board in the process.<p>- The Surface Laptop isn&#x27;t even meant to be opened. If you open it, you can&#x27;t put it back together [1]. I wonder what &quot;physical tradeoff&quot; Microsoft made here that even Apple didn&#x27;t have to make in an iPhone?<p>- All MBPs after MBP Retina have the NVMe drive soldered on [2].
