Item(by='dehrmann', descendants=None, kids=[24732181], score=None, time=1602263630, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24731400, text='This is this same sort of group that would say &quot;trickle down economics doesn&#x27;t work.&quot; It&#x27;s funny they&#x27;d expect productivity gains to trickle down to wages.<p>Productivity is a <i>ceiling</i> on wages; once wages raise beyond a certain point, the company will take a loss, even after passing down the cost of labor to customers.<p>Wages not keeping up with productivity gains isn&#x27;t just on the low end. Seeing how well they tracked before 1970, there was an overall labor shortage. Around 1970, that changed, and there was more labor than the market needed, so wages were kept low enough that it&#x27;s cheaper to pay someone minimum wage to take your order than to install an order kiosk.')
