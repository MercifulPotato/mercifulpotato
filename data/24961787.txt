British soldier Peter Kemp fought on behalf of Franco’s Nationalists (i.e. the side with the fascists) and documented his experience in the autobiographical “Mine Were of Trouble.” Although he had sided with Franco’s quasi-fascists* in Spain, Kemp would later fight the Nazis as a British commando in WW2. After the war, he worked as a foreign correspondent for The Spectator.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Peter_Kemp_(writer)" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Peter_Kemp_(writer)</a><p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.amazon.com&#x2F;Mine-Were-Trouble-Nationalist-Account&#x2F;dp&#x2F;B08673MBF1" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.amazon.com&#x2F;Mine-Were-Trouble-Nationalist-Account...</a><p>*While Franco’s authoritarian conservative coalition include the fascist Falange, it also included monarchists, Catholics, and other social conservatives who Franco balanced against one another while in power.')
