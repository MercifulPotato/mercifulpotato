Item(by='AnimalMuppet', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602714347, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24780561, text='I could see a bit more realistically thinking of the 4004 as &quot;generation zero&quot; (yes, I know it was a pioneer, but I still have a hard time calling it even &quot;first generation&quot;).  8-bit micros are first generation (yes, I know, the 8080 was significantly better than the 8008, but on the larger scale, there wasn&#x27;t that much difference).  16-bit (Z80 and 8086) were second generation, 32 bit (386, 68020, 32000) were third, with 68000 being a grey area.  Maybe the 4th generation comes when floating point and memory management are on the same chip, and 5th generation when there&#x27;s more than one core.')
