Item(by='lucideer', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603453756, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24868031, text='I think a lot of the confusion around the consent banner stuff arises from the 2002 EU ePrivacy Directive (ePD)[0] which long predates GDPR.<p>ePD introduced the idea of the cookie consent banners we see today.<p>While it was enacted in 2002, ePD didn&#x27;t really start to come into broad legal force in many member states until ~2010ish (EU Directives are not like federal laws; instead they&#x27;re implemented &amp; enforced by individual member states separately).<p>GDPR&#x27;s focus on prior consent makes consent banners in their popular format largely useless, but when GDPR came along, the intent was that PD <i>should</i> have been replaced by the accompanying EU ePrivacy Regulation (ePR)[1] to clarify this. ePR has been delayed, so we&#x27;re in this ambiguous place.<p>[0] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Privacy_and_Electronic_Communications_Directive_2002" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Privacy_and_Electronic_Communi...</a><p>[1] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;EPrivacy_Regulation" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;EPrivacy_Regulation</a>')
