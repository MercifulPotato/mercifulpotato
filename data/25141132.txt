Item(by='philwelch', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605727172, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25140968, text='Farm subsidies generally serve a few purposes in the US:<p>1. Maintaining the political support of farmers<p>2. Maintaining surplus food production capacity in case of some contingency (e.g. war).<p>The same is true for many developed countries. The US government actually produced “government cheese” with surplus milk to help support dairy farmers, providing the cheese to schools and welfare recipients. The EU has also experienced subsidy-related supply surpluses, including a “butter mountain” and a “wine lake”.')
