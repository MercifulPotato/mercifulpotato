Item(by='rattray', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602004389, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24698562, text='The source you cite has the following headline: &quot;More than half of COVID-19 patients experience ongoing fatigue, study finds&quot;<p>So I&#x27;m not sure why that article gives you the impression that any more than 2% is implausible.<p>When I see a medical journal article with n~150 saying that 50% experience fatigue after ~70 days, and an app reports that self-reported &quot;symptoms&quot; (may not be asking about fatigue?) are below 2% after 90 days, my takeaway is that much more data is needed because it could be that &quot;a lot of people have long-lasting side-effects&quot;.<p>Think about if you (or a young healthy family member) tested positive and felt mostly fine for a week or two.<p>After reading both of the above, I&#x27;d be concerned that there&#x27;s a decent chance (1%-60%?) that you&#x27;d be worse off in some way for a long time, possibly the rest of your life.')
