Item(by='ben509', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603479339, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24872050, text='S-expressions are great once you&#x27;ve figured out functions, atoms, complex data types, etc. That&#x27;s how Amazon&#x27;s Ion[1] uses them: as a nice supplement to a serialization standard.<p>&gt; “CAR”&#x2F;“CDR” are not a part of the expression syntax<p>If you were implementing them for a serialization format, you&#x27;d leave out cons cells and car &#x2F; cdr.<p>But in late-binding LISPs, which is where they&#x27;re typically used, (a . b) is part of the syntax. It&#x27;s worth calling out for why you don&#x27;t want it: it&#x27;s badly typed since it could be a 2-tuple, or it could be a list depending on what &#x27;b&#x27; is.<p>[1]: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;amzn.github.io&#x2F;ion-docs&#x2F;docs&#x2F;spec.html#sexp" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;amzn.github.io&#x2F;ion-docs&#x2F;docs&#x2F;spec.html#sexp</a>')
