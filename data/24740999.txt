Item(by='masklinn', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602355897, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24739857, text='&gt; Do most (or any) languages in which people take this approach actually enforce handling of all cases?<p>In &quot;most functional languages&quot;, your result would be a <i>sum type</i> which requires dispatching explicitly between one case or the other, aka you wouldn&#x27;t have a pair of conditionals instead you&#x27;d have something along the lines<p><pre><code>    switch (customerOrError) {\n        case IsCustomer(customer):\n            Console.WriteLine(customer.ID);\n        case IsError(errorMessage):\n            Console.WriteLine(errorMessage);\n    }\n</code></pre>\nso from `customerOrError` you wouldn&#x27;t have any sort of direct access to the customer or error, you <i>must</i> go through a dispatching construct.<p>In OO languages lacking sum types this sort of constructs would generally be implemented through lambdas or some sort of visitor e.g.<p><pre><code>    customerOrError.then(\n        customer =&gt; { Console.WriteLine(customer.Id); },\n        onError: errorMessage =&gt; { Console.WriteLine(errorMessage) }\n    );</code></pre>')
