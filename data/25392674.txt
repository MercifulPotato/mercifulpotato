Item(by='bhuber', descendants=None, kids=[25392711], score=None, time=1607727276, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25389629, text='This isn&#x27;t at all universally true, it depends on the costs of failure.  If you&#x27;re programming a Mars rover, then sure, avoiding mistakes is paramount.  If you&#x27;re writing a one-off data analysis script that only has to work once and only has to be mostly accurate, spending a week coming up with the ideal specification and writing a full comprehensive test suite is usually much less productive than hacking at it for a few hours until it works.  Good software developers know when to make the appropriate safety tradeoffs given the goals and constraints of the task at hand.')
