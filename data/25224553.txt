Item(by='ben509', descendants=None, kids=[25226325], score=None, time=1606436953, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25223203, text='&gt; The support for datetimes is irrelevant if the protocol spec specifies how to serialize a date.<p>Consider JSON and how numbers are handled right now.<p>Per the spec, it&#x27;s an arbitrarily long decimal, and <i>some</i> libraries do deserialize it to a BigDecimal class.<p>But many libraries in a chain of processing will treat it as a double, often libraries outside your control.<p>So you can&#x27;t rely on them preserving any accuracy beyond what a double can handle.<p>Likewise, if you have a complex date-time representation, the spec can require all kinds of detail, but in practice, some of that data will be thrown out.<p>And worse, they won&#x27;t throw an error, they&#x27;ll truncate that data quietly.')
