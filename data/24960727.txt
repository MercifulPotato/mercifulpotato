The line already has been drawn, in law. SCOTUS has made the line <i>clear and present danger</i>[1]. That is, a threat made via speech has to be clear, credible and imminent else it is not considered a threat and hence not speech that may be interfered with legally, but if it is then it can be.<p>It&#x27;s distinctly possible you&#x27;ve read more Popper than I have but his words seem very clear here too[2]:<p>&gt; I do not imply, for instance, that we should always suppress the utterance of intolerant philosophies; as long as we can counter them by rational argument and keep them in check by public opinion<p>He goes on to contrast that with those who give up on speech and respond with violence (and it&#x27;s all within about a page).<p>So we have a clear line and there&#x27;s no need to wait for violence to actually occur before legal remedy can be sought, which would appear to me to fit with Popper&#x27;s statement and what you studied.<p>I completely agree with Popper on elections, and free speech fits the same mould, it&#x27;s primary function is to reduce violence. Without free speech the options for those who wish to change society are only violent.
