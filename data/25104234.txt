Item(by='TheOtherHobbes', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605470335, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25101903, text='Yes, it is. Also true for data. The problem is that common mode rejection is limited for unshielded pairs, so it works - but not perfectly.<p>POTS is legacy now, so I&#x27;m not sure how much of a consideration it is for modern builds.<p>There are safety issues with running network and power cables close to each other, but generally on small domestic systems crosstalk between data and power shouldn&#x27;t be a serious problem.<p>The exception is if you have a lot of hash on the power lines from USB chargers, unfiltered noisy motors, or white goods that generate both digital and analog noise.<p>Ferrites or other kinds of filtering can help. So can using shielded cat cable.')
