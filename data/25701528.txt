Item(by='starfallg', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610208766, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25700638, text='&gt;US does not do either, and any attempt to add security either during registration or on voting day is attributed to racism or classism or some other bigotry<p>The US already has a registration system. The information just need to be current and the system designed in such a way where a registered person can only vote once (which is not what campaigners are against).<p>Over here, the local council sends you a letter regularly for you to update your details in the electoral register. If you don&#x27;t respond then you are not registered to vote in upcoming elections.<p>They send you a poll card which tell you where to go vote. If you don&#x27;t have your poll card you can still vote by turning up at the correct polling station. You can&#x27;t vote at another station.<p>You have to provide your name and address and they will look you up on the electoral register. If the staff suspect you are fraudulent, they will call the police. After you are provided with a ballot, they cross you name out from the list so that it can&#x27;t be reused.<p>There are a small number of cases of error or fraud is very low as you need a lot of information on a large scale over a large area across many polling stations to do meaningful election fraud undetected.')
