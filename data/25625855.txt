Item(by='forrestthewoods', descendants=None, kids=[25626279], score=None, time=1609713179, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25625707, text='You’re stretching there imho.<p>L1 and L2 are per core. A cacheline is 64 bytes and per-core cache size is on the order of 32kb and 256kb for L1&#x2F;L2.<p>Reading data from L1 is on the order of 1 nanosecond (or less) and RAM on the order of 50 nanoseconds.<p>If you’re scanning an array and load a dozen cachelines that’s  almost certainly preferable to several cache-misses (and lines).<p>Memory access is very often an application’s bottleneck. The answer is almost always more arrays and fewer pointers.')
