And don&#x27;t get me wrong, I think you&#x27;re improving things.<p>It&#x27;s just that with something that advertises E2E encryption, the expectations are high. Unavoidably so. Doesn&#x27;t help that various snake-oil salesmen have tried to hijack the term too, because of its implied strength. So being upfront about the threat model and in particular what your service can <i>not</i> protect against is important.<p>It just happens that in this particular case, the not-in-threat-model-scope is also a thing of interesting value. Perhaps even more so if your service gets adopted by civil rights or other organising movements.')
