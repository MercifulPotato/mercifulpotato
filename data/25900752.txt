Item(by='bastawhiz', descendants=None, kids=[25900860, 25901055, 25901446], score=None, time=1611562938, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25899418, text='&gt; Crypto-as-money is still young. We have a lot to do. Do you remember Napster? Tether is kinda like Napster – it&#x27;s taking off, people love it, it&#x27;s a little sketchy, and it&#x27;s probably not the design that will last. We don&#x27;t want to make the equivalent of Kazaa – another blip in the history that ultimately doesn&#x27;t ultimately work out. The challenge is to build a platform that&#x27;s as robust as BitTorrent and as great to use as Spotify and Netflix.<p>For something on an about page, this copy draws some extremely frank comparisons.<p>&gt; If users all have to go through a KYC process, this significantly limits the viability of the token to permit free movement of money across borders.<p>KYC processes are, essentially, the law. Governments go out of their way to do this to cut down on financial crime. Regardless of whether you believe these laws are useful or just, I find it difficult to believe they&#x27;ll have any success trying to work around these laws. Governments (in general) do not like it when folks try to create loopholes: I wouldn&#x27;t bet on a project whose stated goal was to avoid complying with the law.')
