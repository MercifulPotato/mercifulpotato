Item(by='lhorie', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611359598, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25877460, text='IMHO, there&#x27;s a bit of an irony that I see with a lot of React projects: people often say that w&#x2F; vanilla JS, you end up reinventing wheels poorly, yet this is exactly what a lot of React projects I see look like: people that don&#x27;t really have framework-building experience attempting to cobble up something similar in scope to Angular or Ember by using React + a bunch of other libraries (often libraries they&#x27;ve never used before).<p>There&#x27;s certainly a case to be made about large teams being a problem of their own, but FWIW, a lot of projects _don&#x27;t_ have large frontend teams (I&#x27;d say 1 to 3 frontend devs is fairly typical). Standardization _across_ teams can definitely be a problem, but at some point one has to wonder if it isn&#x27;t more sensible to just have each teams develop separate apps that load in separate pages, or, you know, just use a more batteries-included, prescriptive framework.')
