Item(by='dimitrios1', descendants=None, kids=[25200547, 25199626], score=None, time=1606233476, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25199393, text='Not to pick on you here, but to me, this is case and point of the type of &quot;privacy rot&quot; we have allowed to settle in the psyche of American minds.<p>Imagine if it was discovered that Bell, or Motorolla, or Cisco installed taps on business phones in the 80s and 90s to see which &quot;productivity features&quot; were being used, or for &quot;reporting&quot; -- it would make national headlines and businesses would be up in arms.<p>To me this is the worst side effect of the modern internet we have built, where data is being mined and pumped into every orifice available: we&#x27;ve lost a sense of what our privacy is truly worth. Thankfully, this latest generation is starting to lash out against it.')
