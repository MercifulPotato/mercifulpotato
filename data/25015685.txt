Item(by='pgrote', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604763848, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25008554, text='How many people learned to type by typing in programs from magazines? I did.<p>By the time I took &quot;typing&quot; freshman year in high school I could type 80wpm. No issues. I received an F in typing and couldn&#x27;t understand why. When I asked the teacher I was told I was typing wrong and had to use home row. Even though my typing was fast with few mistakes, still received an F. The teacher couldn&#x27;t understand I taught myself to type using a Commodore 64. He maintained there is only one way to type and I explained he was wrong.<p>Parents and principal got involved. The negotiated result was a B in the class, I had to apologize to the teacher and had study hall for the rest of the semester.<p>Everything from Compute or Computes Gazette was awesome. You&#x27;d type it all in and sometimes it didn&#x27;t work ... you could wait until the next month when fixes were printed or you could debug and solve it yourself.<p>This started to end with the release of SpeedScript, which was the first MLX release from the magazine. Since it was machine language you didn&#x27;t enter the program lines, but numbers.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;SpeedScript" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;en.wikipedia.org&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;SpeedScript</a><p>Amazing times.')
