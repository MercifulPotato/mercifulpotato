Item(by='clairity', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610745561, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25794996, text='sure, a bridge provides net economic benefit, so is not simply a depreciating asset. it&#x27;s paying for broad benefits that accrue widely across society, rather than strictly to the government (externalized benefits). that doesn&#x27;t happen with cars. in fact, cars produce large negative externalities. there&#x27;s also fewer viable, affordable substitutes for bridges, whereas cars have multiple viable such alternatives, which makes debt often a bad choice for cars but not bridges.<p>but to the point of bonds, a lot of bond measures are not for net economic benefit, but rather political gain (school funding bonds, for example, tend to fail to improve educational outcomes despite the political rhetoric).')
