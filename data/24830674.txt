Item(by='nyghtly', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603139548, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24827295, text='In academic writing, the general rule is that whenever you introduce an acronym, you must first use the full version.<p>For example: You must always be mindful of privacy laws when handling personal health information (PHI). Remember, that you may only access PHI when necessary to do your job, and never to fulfill personal curiosity about a patient.<p>Unfortunately, this doesn&#x27;t work for a chat room because it&#x27;s not meant to be read from beginning to end. My personal rule is to never use acronyms, unless I can follow the above rule.<p>I&#x27;ve worked in many different industries, and learning acronyms has been a consistent and pointless barrier to achieving productivity. I don&#x27;t want to put that onto anyone else. Even worse, you run the risk of your reader ignoring the lesson entirely, simply because they don&#x27;t know what the acronym means.<p>In and of itself, &quot;PHI&quot; does not seem any more important than any other acronym. On the other hand, &quot;personal health information,&quot; has an obvious connotation of importance.')
