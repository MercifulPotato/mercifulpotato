Infantry would often talk to each other about how they were looking forward to &quot;buying a farm&quot; once the war was over and they went home to their families.<p>When the soldiers died in the trenches, as they often did, it was said that they had &quot;bought the farm&quot; as a euphemism. Usually it was better than saying how they actually died.')
