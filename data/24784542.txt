Item(by='hackerfromthefu', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602731935, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24777640, text='As always, thoughtful resources, especially provided gratis, are a wonderful contribution, and appreciated!<p>That said, I think there&#x27;s a fundamental problem with what determines a &#x27;good resumes&#x27;!<p>Resumes are generally assessed by a wide range of hirers and various layers or recruiters or hr in the middle - and the people who assess it they all have (very) different ideas of what is good!<p>I&#x27;ve seen this play out many times where person 1 says make it longer, person 2 says make it shorter, person 3 says not enough detail, person 4 says too much etc ..<p>So on balance I don&#x27;t think it&#x27;s possible to make an objectively perfect format of resume that will always be more successful than a different format for any given job application.<p>Other variables like number of applications, personal connection with the people you talk to etc are possibly going to be more valuable to focus efforts on in a job search, once your resume is &#x27;good enough&#x27;. Then expect to modify it to suit various people&#x27;s preference if you are really interested in a specific role.<p>My 2c ..')
