If I have a great new idea for a high-performance computer chip, which would also cause them to be twice the cost, it won&#x27;t get funded, because people will just settle for a higher performance chip in a few years at a lower cost.<p>In the case of medicine, as society gets wealthier, it is willing&#x2F;able to spend more on medicine.  The only time you say &quot;no thank you&quot; to an improvement in medicine in exchange for higher costs, is when you&#x27;re out of money.<p>There is no such thing as &quot;good enough&quot; medicine, since we all still die eventually.  Therefore, we pay more for medicine, including pharmaceutical R&amp;D, because we can, and we can never actually get as much as we want.')
