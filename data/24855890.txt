Item(by='taphangum', descendants=None, kids=[24875177], score=None, time=1603354457, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24854999, text='The key reason why you (and likely most developers) struggle with CSS is because we underestimate it.<p>In reality, it is as complicated, maybe more so, than any programming language or framework you will ever come across.<p>The best way to start to get better at it, is to gain an appreciation for it, as a &#x27;hard&#x27;, technical thing. Which it is.<p>You do this by re-framing how you see it.<p>Instead of seeing CSS as an annoying way to &#x27;style&#x27; pages, see it instead as a visual programming language for constructing visual guides (UI&#x27;s) for your user.<p>You don&#x27;t &#x27;style&#x27; pages, you &#x27;construct&#x27; and &#x27;architect&#x27; them. It sounds weird to use these words, but they have a massive effect in changing your perception of what you&#x27;re doing. And that has a massive effect on your impression and willingness to learn how to do it well.<p>Essentially, you can properly learn CSS by transforming it in your mind from a &#x27;styling&#x27; problem thing, to an &#x27;engineering&#x27; problem thing.<p>I&#x27;ve got a post on this coming up tomorrow, which I&#x27;ll post here.<p>If you want to get a shout when the full post on this is up, you can drop your email on the site homepage here: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;planflow.dev" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;planflow.dev</a>.
