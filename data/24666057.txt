Item(by='kube-system', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1601666177, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24665913, text='That goes contrary to basic tenets of law in most of the world.  People who are charged with a crime start with a presumption of innocence, it is the responsibility of the prosecution to demonstrate that they broke the law.  In the US, the standard required is &quot;beyond a reasonable doubt&quot;.<p>On the other hand, <i>civil penalties</i> (like fines) don&#x27;t have the same burden of proof, which is why you often see them in situations like these.  They are easier to prove.  They generally only require a &quot;preponderance of the evidence&quot; to prove.')
