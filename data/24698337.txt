Item(by='gizmo686', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1601996377, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24696346, text='In some sense, nothing particuarly interesting happens at the event horizon of a black hole. For a sufficiently large black hole, the space at the event horizon is essentially flat [0]. The time and space swapping weirdness is entirely an artifact of the coordinate system being used.<p>If you think of spacetime as a 4 dimensional geometry, there is no particularly reason to pick out any specific shape of the &quot;time&quot; coordinate. For a given interval in space-time, there is notion of &quot;proper time&quot; that is invariant to coordinate system transformations, so a given observer does have an &quot;ideal&quot; coordinate to use for time. The &quot;proper time&quot; of a given observer could be the space axis of another coordinate system.<p>Assuming you have enough background in math, what I think is a reasonably understandable explanation is here:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=yv2-KPagCQs&amp;feature=youtu.be&amp;list=PLpGHT1n4-mAvcXwzOIz3dHnGZaQP1LEib&amp;t=4090" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=yv2-KPagCQs&amp;feature=youtu.be...</a><p>Once he gets through explaining the hyperbolic coordinate sytstem, you can skip ahead to<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=yv2-KPagCQs&amp;feature=youtu.be&amp;list=PLpGHT1n4-mAvcXwzOIz3dHnGZaQP1LEib&amp;t=5247" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=yv2-KPagCQs&amp;feature=youtu.be...</a><p>Space is time line:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=yv2-KPagCQs&amp;feature=youtu.be&amp;list=PLpGHT1n4-mAvcXwzOIz3dHnGZaQP1LEib&amp;t=5478" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=yv2-KPagCQs&amp;feature=youtu.be...</a><p>A somewhat clarifying picture of it taking &quot;infinite&quot; time to cross the event horizon, without anything interesting happening near the event horizon:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=yv2-KPagCQs&amp;feature=youtu.be&amp;list=PLpGHT1n4-mAvcXwzOIz3dHnGZaQP1LEib&amp;t=5767" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=yv2-KPagCQs&amp;feature=youtu.be...</a><p>[0] As with any gravitational object, there are tidal forces.
