Item(by='dane-pgp', descendants=None, kids=[24778585], score=None, time=1602692456, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24773481, text='If federated nodes have to mutually verify each others&#x27; compliance, then every email server in the EU is breaking the law.<p>Presumably the reason email servers can operate legally is that the sender of each email is deemed to be the data controller or is otherwise giving explicit consent to their private data being sent to each recipient&#x27;s mail provider.<p>Social networking is roughly equivalent to the sending of emails BCCed to everyone in your address book.  In the case of something like Twitter-style microblogging, the GDPR is even less relevant since the user is deliberately trying to make information publicly available.')
