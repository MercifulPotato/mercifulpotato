Item(by='rhodozelia', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609479454, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25601024, text='It is odd that dams don’t incorporate a means for sediment to continue its path downstream.\nIf there were a channel that ran the length of the reservoir that was narrow enough it would\nKeep the velocity of the water high enough that the sediment wouldn’t settle out. It could then be run through the turbines, admittedly causing more wear, or allowed to settle behind the dam in an area with a sluice gate that could be used to flush sediment out. Probably much easier said than done so the problem has just been universally ignored.')
