Item(by='stickfigure', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606311401, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25206005, text='There seems to be a lot of confusion about what this is.<p>The author posted a survey of the <i>Thiel Question</i>, which asks &quot;Tell me one or more things almost nobody agrees with you on.&quot; The motivation of the question is a belief that to build something revolutionary, you need to be able to think outside the box. &quot;People would be willing to rent air mattresses in their living rooms like hotels&quot; is the canonical example (from 2008).<p>The problem is, most iconoclastic ideas tend to be... pretty outlandish. Also, people posting to anonymous web surveys (especially in the rationalist community) are probably tempted to exaggerate a little. So it&#x27;s comedic to survey a group of people, collect the responses, and put them together as a hypothetical political platform.<p>I found it hilarious.')
