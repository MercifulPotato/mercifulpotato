Item(by='toast0', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606858442, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25269963, text='I&#x27;m not super familiar with LISPs, but what the JIT is doing here is transforming bytecode into native code, which is what other JITs do.<p>What it&#x27;s not doing, but is commonly done in other JITs, is any sort of runtime profiling and chosing of which modules to transforming; all modules are transformed when loaded.<p>As described in the article, previous JIT attempts with BEAM did have that functionality, and they did meet the project goals; profiling cost too much, the compilation step was too expensive, and mixing modes between interpreted modules and native modules added too much complexity.<p>I haven&#x27;t looked at the code behind this, but from articles, I haven&#x27;t seen anything that would preclude running the bytecode to native code transformation ahead of time (or caching the just-in-time transformation for future use), but it&#x27;s not part of the implementation as of now.')
