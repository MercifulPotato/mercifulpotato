Item(by='Mountain_Skies', descendants=None, kids=[25553406], score=None, time=1609095218, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25553150, text='Might also be a side effect of the desire to live an urban lifestyle. Throughout my 20s and 30s I saw large numbers of fellow young urban workers cycle through a circuit of city centers, moving every two or three years. Seattle, DC, Austin, Portland, Chicago, Atlanta, and on they&#x27;d go. Dealing with purchasing and selling a home every couple of years is a huge hassle and unless you&#x27;re savvy about selecting the right area with increasing property values each time, transaction costs can end up eating up a good bit of wealth. It&#x27;s much easier to sign a lease and then decide to renew it or not depending on if you want to move on to the next metro that year.<p>Most of us are in our 40s now and regardless of family formation have found an area to settle down in. Perhaps Millennials will follow a similar pattern once they&#x27;ve been through the circuit of cities and found the one that fits them best.')
