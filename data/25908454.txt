Item(by='bswinnerton', descendants=None, kids=[25910109, 25920058], score=None, time=1611607045, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25908096, text='This is known as “GitHub Flow” (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;guides.github.com&#x2F;introduction&#x2F;flow&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;guides.github.com&#x2F;introduction&#x2F;flow&#x2F;</a>). I was pretty surprised by it when I first joined GitHub but I’ve grown to love it. It makes rolling back changes much faster than having to open up a revert branch, get it approved, and deploy it. When something goes sideways, just deploy master &#x2F; main, which is meant to always be in a safe state.')
