Item(by='harperlee', descendants=None, kids=[25552699], score=None, time=1609090688, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25552576, text='And of course even the best possible functions introduce barriers to reading the code, which is often very expensive.<p>They are both abstractions that substitute an explicit operation with just a name; and thus both require that you expand your vocabulary when holding the program context in your head. Abstract at the wrong level, and you increase difficulty; abstract at the correct one, and you lower it.<p>Where I do think the main problem with macros is, is in debugging - because I know no macro system that maintains metadata on the written code to help explain the higher (macro) conceptual level once a stacktrace &#x2F; condition &#x2F; segfault &#x2F; error arises.')
