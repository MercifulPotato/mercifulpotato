Item(by='JoeAltmaier', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1601933849, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24684462, text='&#x27;Vast&#x27; is a matter of scale. What seemed large when we were a manual-labor country of 100M people, and what seems large now that we&#x27;re an automated-production county of 450M, are quite different.<p>Michael Dell wrote in his memoir, that each time Dell Computer doubled in size, he had to completely redefine their processes.  Well, the USA has grown a lot. Trying to fit 50-100 year old processes into the modern economy is bound to fail, for lack of scale and imagination.<p>A public transit system is honestly cheap at the price. Kind of a enlightened-self-interest thing. It lowers the cost of working, lets people make more choices about where to live, and generally makes markets more efficient.')
