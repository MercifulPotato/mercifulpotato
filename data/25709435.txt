Item(by='woofcat', descendants=None, kids=[25709504], score=None, time=1610250171, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25708845, text='My problem sometimes with this line of reasoning is that some rights are more important than others, we&#x27;ve already decided that as a fact.<p>For instance &quot;Forcing them to host parler would be an egregious violation of their right to choose who they associate with.&quot; Is true, but we force companies not to discriminate based on Age, Sex, Religion, etc. So your right of who you can associate with isn&#x27;t iron clad.<p>We could just as easily add &quot;Freedom of Expression&quot; to the list of things you can not discriminate against, and suddenly the argument holds no water.<p>I&#x27;m not an American, however I sometimes enjoy the irony of America where one side of the political spectrum will be angry that a bakery is forced to bake a cake for a gay couple, but think it&#x27;s against the customers rights for AWS to not host content they don&#x27;t agree with.<p>Whilst the other thinks the baker should be forced to bake a cake against their will, and AWS is free to drop any business they feel like.')
