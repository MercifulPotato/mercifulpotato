Item(by='dfabulich', descendants=None, kids=[24677456, 24677864], score=None, time=1601801925, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24677221, text='Rudolf Carnap worked hard on formalizing ethics (and everything else) in the 20th century; the project was a total flop.<p>It turns out you can&#x27;t formalize anything that doesn&#x27;t have proofs -- in other words, it basically only works for what we&#x27;d call &quot;mathematics&quot; or &quot;pure logic&quot;. You can&#x27;t formalize literally anything else in philosophy, at all.<p>The regress argument has nothing to do with ethics in particular. It applies just as much to any non-proof area of study, including all of science, history, and even ordinary observations and recollections.')
