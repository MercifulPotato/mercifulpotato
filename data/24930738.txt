Item(by='magnio', descendants=None, kids=[24930848], score=None, time=1603981428, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24930433, text='For a linearly recursive sequence x_0, x_1, x_(n+2) = ax_(n+1) + bx_n, the general formula for the terms is<p>x_n = cα^n + dβ^n,<p>where α, β are the roots of the quadratic x²−ax−b; c, d are solutions to the system<p>c + d = x_0\ncα + dβ = x_1.<p>If the series Σ x_n⋅10^n converges then its value is<p>10c&#x2F;(10−α) + 10d&#x2F;(10−β)\n= ((100−10a)x_0 + 10x_1)&#x2F;(100 − 10a − b).<p>If a, b, x_0, x_1 are all rational then the above series converges to a rational number, too. This is the case for the Fibonacci sequence, with a=b=1, x_0=0 and x_1=1.')
