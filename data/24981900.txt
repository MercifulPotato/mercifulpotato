Item(by='ls65536', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604425287, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24981075, text='And if you know anyone who has paid for content that suddenly they had lost access to because of shut down DRM servers or intermittent internet connectivity, then you might be inclined to think a bit differently.<p>Ultimately I think there needs to be a balance here, and most importantly users ought to be made more aware of what they&#x27;re usually getting when purchasing DRM-protected content: a limited license to consume their paid-for content for a (possibly unspecified) period of time on a specific set of devices under certain circumstances that often lie well beyond their control.<p>I&#x27;d like to think that some kind of free market-based approach of consumers choosing to support the content creators who take a more balanced approach to DRM (or just avoid it entirely) would &quot;solve&quot; these problems, but I&#x27;m not too hopeful of this ever working out given the enormous knowledge asymmetry between content creators&#x2F;distributors and consumers about how stuff like this works.')
