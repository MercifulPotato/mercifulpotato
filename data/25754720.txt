Why the bias for seeing and archiving everything? The implicit model, which is right there in his title, is &quot;talk&quot;, which is not naturally archived.<p>I&#x27;ve built products just fine using verbal communication nearly exclusively. [1] We don&#x27;t need to create some sort of panoptic archive of every conversation ever had to get things done. If people miss things, they miss things. We can catch each other up on the parts that truly matter.<p>Forgetting and repetition can be negative, but they can also be highly positive. We don&#x27;t have to carry with us the weight of every word ever spoken about a project.<p>[1] The main exception being index cards with a few words on each. <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;williampietri.com&#x2F;writing&#x2F;2015&#x2F;the-big-board&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;williampietri.com&#x2F;writing&#x2F;2015&#x2F;the-big-board&#x2F;</a>')
