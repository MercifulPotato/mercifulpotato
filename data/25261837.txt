Item(by='tptacek', descendants=None, kids=[25262028], score=None, time=1606791975, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25261661, text='Lack of deniability is strictly a downside in a messaging protocol. Applications built on top of messaging protocols that want non-repudiability can provide it directly, using signatures. You almost never want non-repudiable messages between ordinary people.<p><i>Later</i><p>I also dispute that subkeys are reasonably looked at as a forward secrecy feature. Most uses of subkeys, and virtually all of the tooling, are about having a hardware root of trust tied to shorter-term software keys.')
