Item(by='ludocode', descendants=None, kids=[24898286], score=None, time=1603722763, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24895330, text='Minor nitpick, but inline and weak are not the same thing. Weak linkage is a property that is resolved by the dynamic linker (at runtime startup); inline linkage is a property that is resolved by the static linker (at compile-time linking.) GCC calls inline &quot;vague linkage&quot; and it only uses weak linkage if the platform does not have COMDAT support:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;gcc.gnu.org&#x2F;onlinedocs&#x2F;gcc&#x2F;Vague-Linkage.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;gcc.gnu.org&#x2F;onlinedocs&#x2F;gcc&#x2F;Vague-Linkage.html</a>')
