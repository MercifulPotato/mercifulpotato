Item(by='mlyle', descendants=None, kids=[25609797], score=None, time=1609554747, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25609714, text='It&#x27;s more like 5% of people don&#x27;t build as many or as effective of antibodies to the vaccine spike protein, and then when infected are petri dishes providing selective pressure for variants to mutate that escape these antibodies and become dominant within their bodies (and, in turn, are more readily transmitted to vaccinated people around them).<p>Some of the 5% may just be unlucky people who got bigger doses of the virus&#x2F;higher initial infectious dose, but the same logic applies.<p>It may be more than 5%, even; the vaccine likely does more to prevent symptomatic illness than infection.<p>It&#x27;s hard to estimate what the probability of this happening is.  It&#x27;s certainly more likely when there&#x27;s more illness circulating around-- e.g. if 1 million people with the vaccine become infected it&#x27;s worse than if it&#x27;s 10,000.')
