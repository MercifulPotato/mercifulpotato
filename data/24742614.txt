Item(by='serf', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602371809, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24742588, text='&gt;All fuel tanks have a vent valve to redirect vapours from the fuel. During a fillup the turbulent flow of the fuel causes quite a build-up of vapours that need to be redirected.<p>vented fuel tanks began in the 60s. Many older cars have absolutely no vent valve to speak of -- the vapor is dumped from the fuel cap, which acts as a vent; meaning the vent isn&#x27;t present during fill-up on this style of design.')
