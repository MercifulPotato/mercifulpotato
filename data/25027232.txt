Following an old custom, the body was flayed and a priest donned her skin in an ancient agricultural rite symbolising the renewal of life. The unsuspecting chieftain Achitometl, invited to participate in the concluding festivities, suddenly recognised the skin of his daughter on the body of the priest.<p>this was a traditional rite; the skin would then be placed in a clay vessel underneath a temple or shrine alongside many others')
