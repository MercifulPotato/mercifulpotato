Item(by='nl', descendants=None, kids=[25057867], score=None, time=1605060677, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25050723, text='Patents in chip processes are often electo-chemical in nature, which are &quot;obvious&quot; in the sense people know what they want to do, but the <i>exact</i> process for doing it takes years to develop and refine.<p>This is why chip &quot;yields&quot; change over time - the process is slowly tweaked (maybe the temperature get changed 0.001 degree or something) to improve yield.<p>Chip manufactures use a mix of patents and trade secrets to protect their advantages.')
