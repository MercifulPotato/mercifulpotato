I&#x27;m sure Hotwire may be very useful for a narrow use case. And I have deep respect for DHH and his contributions to the building the web. But like most recent web technology, Hotwire is another case of misaligned priorities.<p>I wrote another article Beyond Rails [0] recently. Some of the key points<p>- Rails became popular 10 years ago because it allowed us to quickly build MVPs with just 2 or 3 developers<p>- software development has gotten far more complex, often needlessly<p>- software development has become needlessly specialized<p>- organizations have become dependent on revenue models that support large teams<p>- startups need more people and money than ever before<p>- the technologies we choose to build web applications influence more than just software architecture, they shape the nature of society<p>- we need to build with web standards that make websites simpler and easier to build and maintain<p>Out of those 400 comments, did anyone say, stop this madness? What are the technologies that are going to get us back to building MVPs with 2-3 people? Because the original vision of the web focused on simplicity and empowerment.<p>[0] Beyond Rails: [<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;tutorials.yax.com&#x2F;articles&#x2F;beyond-rails&#x2F;index.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;tutorials.yax.com&#x2F;articles&#x2F;beyond-rails&#x2F;index.html</a>]')
