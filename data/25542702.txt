The natural gas infrastructure uses natural gas generators to run the pumps that pressurize the lines and the meters are 100% mechanical.  There&#x27;s no reliance on the electrical grid.  I lived through Northeast Blackout of 2003 (visible from space, the extent was so great) and Hurricane Sandy (power was out for a week) and in neither case was there an interruption to natural gas service.  In my 40+ years, I don&#x27;t ever recall the natural gas service ever going &quot;out&quot;.')
