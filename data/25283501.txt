Item(by='wpietri', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606953538, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25283276, text='Why take on a person who reads things closely, thinks about them carefully, values privacy, and has a sense of integrity?<p>There are a bunch of reasons. But the business one is that modern companies can&#x27;t concentrate power at the top if they want to be successful in the long term. Innovation and adaptation don&#x27;t happen because the SEVP of whatever has a brainstorm. They happen because people doing the work are intelligent, well informed, and care about the customers, their colleagues, and the company. If the most important characteristic in hiring is &quot;agree instantly with all authority figures&quot; that&#x27;s a recipe for organizational rigidity and stagnation.<p>Some of the best hires I&#x27;ve made have been &quot;difficult&quot;. Because some kinds of &quot;difficult&quot; are just taking values seriously.')
