Item(by='netcan', descendants=None, kids=[24728492], score=None, time=1602238053, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24727890, text='It&#x27;s kind of ironic that people educated in social science tend to have idealistic or just legible, few factor theories about something this complex. I mean complex in the strong sense.<p>Early theories were about <i>&quot;access to information unleashing a wave of democratisation.</i>&quot; Current theories are about foreign and domestic intelligence manipulating social media.<p>The internet is an era. Eras have a lot going on.<p>The way we should (IMO) be thinking about this stuff is as complex systems, where the mechanisms can&#x27;t really be understood to the point of predictiveness. Just like printing presses, mass literacy, radio and television broke political equilibriums... the internet breaks political equilibriums, for better or worse.<p>The internet is, OTOH, obviously structurally inclined to being an agent of chaos. Despite all the centralisation, the facebook, twitter, google and such use extremism like an exploitation film uses sex and violence. If something is dangerous, sexy and naked... people are going to look.')
