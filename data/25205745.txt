Item(by='Wingman4l7', descendants=None, kids=[25218613], score=None, time=1606275006, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25204316, text='Rainbow tables can still make sense for a salted hash if the salt can be predicted.  A current real-world example I just recently learned about: SSIDs <i>(network names)</i> are used to salt WPA-based WiFi network password hashes.  If the SSID is a default name <i>(examples: NETGEAR, LINKSYS, XFINITY, etc.)</i>, then you could use a rainbow table.')
