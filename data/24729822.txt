Item(by='kingkilr', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602251076, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24729791, text='Rust has a few interlocking behaviors that provide its memory safety, a few of the most important are:<p>- The borrow checker enforces mutable XOR shared references.<p>- The compiler does not allow use of local variables before they&#x27;re assigned to, requires structs to be completely initialized, etc..<p>- All the builtin datastructures perform bounds checks<p>- The compiler disallows deferencing raw pointers except in unsafe blocks.<p>There&#x27;s a lot of good things to be said about modern C++, particular smart pointers. However, it&#x27;s significantly less resilient to common mistakes than Rust is: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;alexgaynor.net&#x2F;2019&#x2F;apr&#x2F;21&#x2F;modern-c++-wont-save-us&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;alexgaynor.net&#x2F;2019&#x2F;apr&#x2F;21&#x2F;modern-c++-wont-save-us&#x2F;</a>')
