Item(by='commoner', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609867778, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25645792, text='The biggest difference is that &#x2F;e&#x2F; is one of the few OSes that has MicroG pre-installed, which allows apps to make use of some features of Google Play Services (e.g. push notifications, map widgets, and COVID-19 exposure notifications) through a FOSS client. MicroG lets users turn on&#x2F;off some of these features, and does not support Google&#x27;s APIs for ads or analytics.<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;microg.org" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;microg.org</a><p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;microg&#x2F;GmsCore&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Implementation-Status" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;github.com&#x2F;microg&#x2F;GmsCore&#x2F;wiki&#x2F;Implementation-Status</a><p>LineageOS does not support the mechanism (signature spoofing) that enables MicroG to replace Google Play Services. The developers of LineageOS consider signature spoofing to be a security risk:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;review.lineageos.org&#x2F;c&#x2F;LineageOS&#x2F;android_frameworks_base&#x2F;+&#x2F;195284&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;review.lineageos.org&#x2F;c&#x2F;LineageOS&#x2F;android_frameworks_...</a><p>It is up to users to select the OS that best meets their own privacy, security, and usability needs.')
