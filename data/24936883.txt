Item(by='young_unixer', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604010616, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24924925, text='In practice, censorship always comes with this bias:<p>Mainstream journalists and politicians can say all the lies they want, because they&#x27;re usually not considered lies by whoever is in charge of the medium (Twitter, Spotify?, the government?).<p>Fringe movements don&#x27;t have the popularity to pass off their lies as truth to the general population or the censor, they can only do it to their relatively small group of followers.<p>In the end, you end up with: popular lies are accepted, unpopular lies are censored.
