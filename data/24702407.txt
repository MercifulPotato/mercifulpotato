Item(by='NateEag', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602018933, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24701651, text='I&#x27;m not talking about widespread surveillance.<p>Decades ago, if they had evidence you were involved in crime, they could apply for a warrant to tap your phone, search your house, or read your mail.<p>That&#x27;s focused surveillance, not mass, and it&#x27;s under the oversight of a judge.<p>In a world of end-to-end encryption, they can&#x27;t realistically find useful evidence by doing those things any more.<p>Parking their van outside doesn&#x27;t help as much, either - you can conspire to commit crimes quite easily without ever leaving your house or having confederates come there, thanks to encrypted video chats.')
