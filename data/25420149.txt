Item(by='52-6F-62', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607966348, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25419097, text='Problem is, you could report the most well-sourced, well-worded article and some portion of the readership would only glean a few details, wrapped up in the context of their own understanding or existing outlook, and then repeated through their own interpretation.<p>There&#x27;s really nothing to be done to prevent this except to note how important it is to consider <i>all</i> of the associated information, and present its context.<p>You&#x27;ll never control how the information is processed by each receiving party, and nor should you.<p>It&#x27;s good to have this information—it&#x27;s good to let people know that there is always the potential for something similar, or worse, to arise.<p>Similar to knowing your entire house might be wiped out [by a major storm] someday if you move to Florida—which wouldn&#x27;t surprise anyone anymore. Repeated dispersal of the information will help prevent rampant hysteria... once a semblance of understanding and context is normalized.')
