Item(by='eru', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611727363, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25923094, text='The exciting thing is exactly that it lacks all those capabilities.  Thus the attack surface is smaller.<p>&gt; You could achieve the same goals with a statically linked program in any languages...<p>Typically statically linked programs still run under an operating system like Linux.  (And Linux might run on top of a hypervisor.)  Linux is a complicated beast, with lots of (undiscovered) vulnerabilities and bugs.<p>MirageOS cuts out Linux.<p>Basically, MirageOS treats the hypervisor as if it was an operating system.  It&#x27;s just an accident of history, that we call processes running on a hypervisor &#x27;virtual machines&#x27;, but fundamentally a hypervisor is just a special kind of OS.')
