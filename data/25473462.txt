Item(by='rsync', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608334616, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25473251, text='&quot;Question: Is physical therapy noninferior to early surgery with arthroscopic partial meniscectomy for improving knee function among patients with nonobstructive meniscal tears?&quot;<p>Vertical (deep, interior) meniscal tears can actually heal, since they have a blood supply, and the surgery for them is basically a coin flip - the results of which you only learn after a <i>nine month recovery period</i>.<p>You should absolutely be pursuing a rigorous course of PT to heal such a vertical meniscal tear.  You should specifically look into whether your adductors (inner leg) are weaker than, and being overpowered by, your abductors (outer leg) resulting in knee valgus.')
