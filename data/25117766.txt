Item(by='avianes', descendants=None, kids=[25118797], score=None, time=1605565067, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25107575, text='It seems to me that the correct term is &quot;Static Binary Translation&quot; (SBT) for what you call &quot;ahead of time binary translation&quot;.<p>And the correct term for &quot;JIT-based emulation&quot; is &quot;Dynamic Binary Translation&quot; (DBT).<p>At least these are the terms you should use if you want to find some literature on this subject.<p>We&#x27;re not talking about JIT or AOT compiler because it&#x27;s not really a compilation (compilation is translating to a lower level language).<p>I think a lot of people talk about JIT rather than DBT because the JIT term is better known, and there is confusion when Apple says they do &quot;Dynamic translation for JITs&quot;.\nWhich means that: they do DBT to handle applications that use JIT.<p>Edit: So Rossetta 2 does both: SBT and DBT.')
