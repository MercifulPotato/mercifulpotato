Item(by='AnHonestComment', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1603575972, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24879781, text='What...?<p>Every set {1&#x2F;n^k | k in N} for n in N is a basis for R over Q, in the sense that any r in R is a Sum[0, inf)[q_i * b_i].<p>It’s not a finite basis, but it’s definitely countable.<p>It’s not generally the case that a countable union of countable sets is countable — that only holds in the finite case, as we see with power series representations of real numbers.')
