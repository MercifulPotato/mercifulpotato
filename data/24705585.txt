Item(by='staticautomatic', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1602048917, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24703804, text='My point is simply this: As far as the primary sources go, we have, as of the 1000&#x27;s, an Anglo-Saxon word &quot;wicing&quot; that looks nearly identical to &quot;viking&quot; and would have been pronounced &quot;wy-ching.&quot; As of at least 100 years later, in what may have been an Icelandic dialect of Old Norse, we have a word &quot;vikingr&quot;, which would have been pronounced &quot;veek-een-grr&quot; with a trilled &quot;r&quot;. The only other thing we know is that Old Norse had a word &quot;vic&quot;. I don&#x27;t think we can rule out that the word &quot;viking&quot; was invented by the Norse, but based on the above information alone, I don&#x27;t think we can reasonably conclude that it must have been.')
