Item(by='jrandm', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611725874, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25924030, text='My favorite working situations are when asking &quot;stupid&quot; questions and&#x2F;or burning 6-12 hours on a subject is normalized and endorsed. I don&#x27;t expect to live long enough to transcend anecdote, but both are necessary to <i>learn</i> and that&#x27;s almost always important. I certainly had this in academic environments but they were somewhat cloistered or the educators expected them to be. In jobs like sysadmin&#x2F;programming 6-12 hours on a thing could be 1 day or 15.<p>The 30-90 minute rule is to force people to search for answers themselves. If you don&#x27;t know why that&#x27;s necessary, I encourage you to spend several hours answering questions on sites you browse for answers (SO, HN, reddit, IRC, twitter, discord, slack, zoom, signal, mailing lists, GH issues, bugzilla, phpbb, <i>anything</i>) -- it&#x27;ll take less than a week. People exist that don&#x27;t do basic reading or research and will expect you to do everything for them. You&#x27;ll get jaded.<p>The downside to this environment is a sort of pathological self-reliance. That&#x27;s when these rules become upper limits: to keep someone from chasing waterfalls ( <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=NoKufWbP_L4" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.youtube.com&#x2F;watch?v=NoKufWbP_L4</a> ). Collaboration always helps, some personal research is always needed... when to mix them varies.')
