Item(by='fractallyte', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606810377, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25257932, text='So they have &#x27;co-processors&#x27;, like the Amiga, but on a chip!<p>Recall, this is what made the Amiga such a groundbreaking computer back in the 80s and early 90s: separate co-processors that handled graphics, sound, DMA, IO,...<p>It worked (for a while) because the Amiga was an integrated system of hardware-and-OS - exactly like Apple. Unlike the MS-DOS and Windows PCs of the time, there was no need to support third-party hardware.<p>Together with a <i>really nice</i> OS, the platform encouraged creativity and gave rise to a generation of coders, artists, and musicians.<p>So everyone: now you know what it felt like when the Amiga first appeared on the home computing scene!')
