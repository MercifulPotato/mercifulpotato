Item(by='Youden', descendants=None, kids=[25979062, 25979056, 25978723, 25978536], score=None, time=1612098336, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25976473, text='I&#x27;m not trying to pick on you personally here but this comment, like many I see on HN, seems overly adversarial and could have made the same arguments in a less combatative way.<p>There&#x27;s a lot of emphasis on your own perspective: &quot;*I* don&#x27;t get&quot;, &quot;*I&#x27;ve* been doing that&quot;, &quot;Not once have *I* missed Docker&quot;, &quot;making *me* less productive&quot;, &quot;*my* personal experience*&quot;<p>And an assumption that it applies to others: &quot;Just use the same [...]&quot;, &quot;I feel OP would be better off [...]&quot;.<p>The comment could provoke a more productive, thoughtful discussion by focusing on understanding rather than on pushing particular views and practices.<p>For example:<p>- &quot;I don&#x27;t get why people use Docker&quot; -&gt; &quot;Could anybody share their reasoning for using Docker?&quot;<p>- &quot;You can run everything without Docker to remove an extra level of indirection&quot; -&gt; &quot;What benefits do you see in exchange for this extra level of indirection?&quot;<p>- &quot;Not once have I missed Docker.&quot; -&gt; &quot;I haven&#x27;t found much use for Docker and would like to understand those who have&quot;<p>- &quot;I have taken over [...] making me less productive&quot; -&gt; &quot;I&#x27;ve only encountered downsides in my own experience with Docker, could anybody share what the upsides may have been for others involved?&quot;<p>- &quot;Again in my personal experience, this simply isn&#x27;t an issue&quot; -&gt; &quot;I&#x27;d be interested to hear more about the benefits as I haven&#x27;t encountered issues before&quot;<p>- &quot;In short, I feel OP would be better off without Docker&quot; -&gt; &quot;I wonder if the OP really needs Docker, perhaps they could be more productive without it&quot;<p>If we all wrote like this (and I&#x27;m certainly not perfect myself), HN could be a much friendlier, more welcoming place.')
