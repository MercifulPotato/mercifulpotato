This might be one of (numerous) areas in business where success is not measured correctly and the methods used are never really validated.<p>Do human resources departments actually evaluate their hiring criteria and the tests they use in experimental designs, e.g. by hiring two sufficiently large groups A and B based on different interview methods and checking a year later how the groups performed relative to each other?<p>Bear in mind that there are plenty of people who believe that they&#x27;re good at doing something just because they have been doing it for a long time. Without an independent method for confirming such claims they are practically useless.')
