Item(by='inglor_cz', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610550255, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25762053, text='It seems that Gresham&#x27;s Law does not fully apply here. Otherwise all high quality journalists would disappear.<p>Gresham&#x27;s Law in financial case is underpinned by two things. First, it is in core interest of every market player to pay with low quality money only; but it is not in interest of every reader to consume or even pay for low quality content only. Second, Gresham&#x27;s Law only works when an outside authority forces you to accept bad money at the same value as good money; but there isn&#x27;t really any equivalent of fiat money in journalism. You do not have to click on clickbaity articles at the same rate as on quality ones.')
