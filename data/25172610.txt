Item(by='theevilsharpie', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605991391, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25168676, text='JEDEC specifications for DDR4 top out at DDR4-3200, and anything beyond that is vendor overclocking with whatever settings that particular vendor deems is suitable.<p>While there&#x27;s nothing technically preventing vendors from factory-overclocking ECC memory the same way, ECC&#x27;s (perceived) main audience is reliability-critical systems, and so it doesn&#x27;t make sense for vendors to market a feature that their target audience wouldn&#x27;t appreciate.<p>What you can do is get two 32 GB DDR4-3200 memory modules (example: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.crucial.com&#x2F;memory&#x2F;server-ddr4&#x2F;mta18asf4g72az-3g2b1" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.crucial.com&#x2F;memory&#x2F;server-ddr4&#x2F;mta18asf4g72az-3g...</a>), and then overclock them yourself to 3600 MT&#x2F;s. As with any overclock, your results may vary, but that&#x27;s also the case with factory overclocking as well.')
