Item(by='nicbou', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607161413, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25313008, text='The Ottoman Empire banned the use of the printing press for 270 years.<p>&quot;This opposition to the printing press had the obvious consequences for literacy, education, and economic success. In 1800 probably only 2 to 3 percent of the citizens of the Ottoman empire were literate, compared with 60 percent of the adult males and 40 percent of the adult females in England. In the Netherlands and Germany, literacy rates were even higher. The Ottoman lands lagged far behind the European countries with the lowest educational attainment in this period, such as Portugal, where probably only around 20 percent of adults could read and write.&quot;<p>(excerpt from Why Nations Fail)')
