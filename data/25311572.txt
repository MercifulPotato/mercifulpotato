Item(by='gfxgirl', descendants=None, kids=[25311807, 25319737, 25311683, 25311817, 25311761], score=None, time=1607140446, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25310779, text='&gt; Growing up, I kind of thought &quot;Most people are just nice.&quot; I had no means to figure out how much was natural charm, how much was me dressing well...<p>&gt; Being homeless taught me that, no, most people absolutely are not just nice.<p>I know this will get downvoted but AFAIK most people see homeless people themselves as &quot;not nice&quot; as in &quot;it&#x27;s not nice to foist your problems on the rest of us&quot;, &quot;it&#x27;s not nice not to take responsibility for yourself&quot;, &quot;it&#x27;s not nice for your to stink up places&quot;, &quot;it&#x27;s not nice for you to foist your crazy on us&quot;, etc...<p>In other words, most people are not nice to people they perceive as being not nice to others and so homeless are first perceived as the originator of being &quot;not nice&quot; and so get treated reciprocally.<p>I&#x27;m all for helping people who are down and out and help give them a 2nd chance but, for lack of a better way to state this, the homeless person needs to take responsibility and go take advantage of whatever programs are offered vs putting their problems on everyone else. It&#x27;s a very western thing to think society owes you just for existing.')
