Item(by='musicale', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609450735, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25593644, text='&gt; how do you know if it&#x27;s the graduate student&#x27;s fault or the experiment&#x27;s fault?<p>Usually by<p>- checking the original publications to make sure you are repeating the experiment correctly<p>- checking (and monitoring) the experimental setup to make sure it is doing what you think it is doing and you aren&#x27;t introducing errors<p>- checking the data analysis<p>- running &quot;sanity check&quot; experiments with the same setup to make sure it has no obvious flaws<p>- comparing with recent replication experiments by other researchers<p>- showing that the experiment and results are repeatable by multiple people in different organizations with their own lab setups<p>- consulting with the original authors (who may be helpful or unhelpful) if they are available<p>- comparing against other data sets<p>- comparing against results from analytical modeling or simulation<p>- looking for alternate explanations of the anomalous results and checking for whether they might be occurring<p>etc.<p>As others have noted, the same methods apply to sorting out conflicting experimental results regardless of who conducts the experiments.')
