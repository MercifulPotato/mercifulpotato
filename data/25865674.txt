Item(by='charlesdaniels', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611269463, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25863428, text='This is indeed an idea that has been coming up from time to time for over 25 years at least. I think the earliest publication in this space was Gockhale&#x27;s Terasys (DOI 10.1109&#x2F;2.375174).<p>It is a good idea in principle, but it&#x27;s never really taken off to my knowledge. Parallel programming is hard. Deeply embedded programming is hard. The languages we have are mostly bad at both.<p>If you want to search for more, the keyword is &quot;processor in memory&quot; or &quot;processing in memory&quot; (PIM). Also &quot;in memory computing&quot; is commonly used as well. A number of groups are working on this right now. The new buss is &quot;memristors&quot; (memristive computing). Whether or not any of it actually ends up working or being commercially viable outside of a lab remains to be seen.')
