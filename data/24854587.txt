Finding <i>eager</i> users with a <i>high-risk tolerance</i> is hard. But, when you do find them, it means that you have tapped into a problem that is frustrating and has no good existing solution.<p>The main insights you should try to get from these users are the following:<p>- what exactly is their &quot;Job to be Done&quot;? [0]<p>- what is their existing workflow? are you solving one thing but unintentionally creating problems elsewhere?<p>- does this person have the authority or decision-making power to commit to your offering?<p>[0] <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;hbr.org&#x2F;2016&#x2F;09&#x2F;know-your-customers-jobs-to-be-done" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;hbr.org&#x2F;2016&#x2F;09&#x2F;know-your-customers-jobs-to-be-done</a>')
