Item(by='wyattpeak', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1612143649, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25984425, text='I feel like the trouble with this line of thinking re large consumer-facing companies is that many of their loyalty relationships are in conflict with each other, so even when they&#x27;re acting in the interests of preserving trust with one group, they&#x27;re undermining the trust of another.<p>When Github restored youtube-dl, they increased user trust while undermining media industry trust. When they pulled popcorntime, they did the reverse.<p>In each case, they were presumably acting rationally to preserve their relationships as best as they could. But whether or not that benefits you as an individual is more or less a toss of a coin.')
