Item(by='Rury', descendants=None, kids=[24831283], score=None, time=1603140615, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24830120, text='The fact that it didn&#x27;t make software developers obsolete. Instead it created a whole new breed of &#x27;specialized&#x27; software developers - ones that knew Fortran.<p>This is the fundamental problem with &quot;they are going to automate our jobs!&quot; thing people seemingly fear. Sure automation may make old jobs obsolete, but it also spawns new jobs in their place.<p>Before the invention of cars - there were no auto mechanics.\nBefore the invention of telephones - there were no telephone operators.\nBefore the invention of the internet - there were no web developers.\nBefore ATMs - there were surely more teller jobs in existence. But with ATMs, someone is now needed to go around and stock these ATMs with money periodically, as well as someone to repair them occasionally - both of which can be viewed as a new kind of &quot;teller&quot;.<p>As so, software developers will never become obsolete. It&#x27;s just the way in which we execute our jobs, which may become obsolete...')
