Item(by='arderary', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607189713, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25315060, text='It seems like your specific use case could be solved (as you mentioned) by passing down a `className` prop, <i>exactly</i> like React. It&#x27;s described as a workaround in the RFC because the approach there relies on internal class names (and couples the internal component API to the parent), whereas you&#x27;d be passing in the specific class you&#x27;d be using. Something like this should work, without going too much against the grain of the framework:<p><pre><code>    &#x2F;&#x2F; Parent.svelte\n    &lt;script&gt;\n      import Child from &quot;.&#x2F;Child.svelte&quot;\n    &lt;&#x2F;script&gt;\n\n    &lt;style&gt;\n      .container :global(.child) {\n        color: red &#x2F;&#x2F; your styles here\n      }\n    &lt;&#x2F;style&gt;\n\n    &lt;div class=&quot;container&quot;&gt;\n      &lt;Child className=&quot;child&quot; &#x2F;&gt;\n    &lt;&#x2F;div&gt;\n\n    &#x2F;&#x2F; Child.svelte\n    &lt;script&gt;\n      export let className\n    &lt;&#x2F;script&gt;\n\n    &lt;h1 class={className}&gt;I am styled by my parent&lt;&#x2F;h1&gt;</code></pre>')
