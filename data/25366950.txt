Item(by='mfontani', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607556263, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25366201, text='The vast majority of what I guess is &quot;robotic, but javascript-running&quot; traffic identifies itself as the (headless) browser it most likely uses, i.e. Chrome.<p>When such crawlers use a distributed network of machines to crawl, there&#x27;s no easy way to identify them as such, other than _guess_ that they&#x27;re robotic in nature due to them coming from &quot;data centre&quot; like IPs (i.e. AWS, Azure, GCP, waht-have-you).<p>While such requests may well be users using those &quot;data centre&quot; services for, say, a personal VPN... the overall pattern is what gives them away.<p>Not all users look at a page and then start requesting (maybe from a separate IP, mind you, but still from the same provider yet with the same UA) each URL in that page in succession.<p>Crawlers who want to stay under the radar are much, <i>much</i> harder to detect as such nowadays.')
