Item(by='ferongr', descendants=None, kids=[25391603], score=None, time=1607712287, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25388837, text='Well, with a carbureted engine that has no mechanism to limit RPM, you&#x27;re probably not actually producing work at maximum RPM, but rather wasting mixture due to valve float. In essence, you&#x27;re not burning that mixture. If you did, the RPM would keep increasing until the entire motor disintegrated.<p>With electronic ignition, you usually have a way to limit max RPM by stopping ignition, so you&#x27;re not producing work (and heat) either way.')
