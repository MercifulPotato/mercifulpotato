Item(by='Wowfunhappy', descendants=None, kids=[25772714], score=None, time=1610601491, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25771929, text='No, and there never will be. It&#x27;s not the execution of 32bit code that&#x27;s the problem (you can trivially re-enable that with a kernel flag, if you want), but rather the removal of <i>every</i> 32bit library from the OS. Without those libraries, apps won&#x27;t run.<p>You can sometimes get away with copying a handful of individual frameworks from older versions of macOS to newer ones, (I recently got Mountain Lion&#x27;s QuickTime to work on Mavericks this way), but for <i>so many</i> libraries, many of which operate at a low level, it&#x27;s just not going to happen!<p>Mojave is still a perfectly fine OS for a while longer. And you can of course use virtual machines, although personally I&#x27;d probably opt for dual booting if it really came to that.')
