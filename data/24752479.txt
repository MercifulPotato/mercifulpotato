Item(by='dragonwriter', descendants=None, kids=[24752692, 24753277, 24752782], score=None, time=1602491349, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24751269, text='&gt; Today, three out of five people in U.S. jails have not been convicted of a crime.<p>While its not perfectly divided this way, it&#x27;s worth noting that the US has different things called “jails” and “prisons” and that the former are mostly for pretrial and short-sentence incarceration and mostly run by local governments, while the latter in state systems are almost exclusively for post-conviction felony sentences, while they are used for all purposes in the federal system.<p>This is important, because while the number of pre-trial confinements in the US is way too high, if you fail to understand that distinction statistics about the proportion of <i>jail</i> inmates that are on pre-trial confinement may be grossly misleading.<p>There is a graphic overview of the whole detention picture here: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.prisonpolicy.org&#x2F;reports&#x2F;pie2020.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.prisonpolicy.org&#x2F;reports&#x2F;pie2020.html</a>')
