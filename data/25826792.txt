Item(by='danShumway', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1611004975, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25825901, text='Philadelphia recently passed a law outright requiring stores to accept cash; they viewed requiring a bank account and credit card to be an accessibility problem for homeless residents. A couple of fast-food places I (pre-pandemic) used to frequent switched from requiring credit cards to accepting both afterwards, which was kind of nice.<p>There is reason in general to be concerned about the lack of good, private digital payment systems, but there are still plenty of cities where a pro-cash sentiment is strong, or at least a pro-&quot;cash should be an option&quot; sentiment. And I suspect (but can&#x27;t confirm) that rural areas might also bias towards more cash transactions as well.<p>The general trend away from cash is something to pay attention to, but depending on where you live, it may not be an emergency right now.')
