Item(by='not_knuth', descendants=5, kids=[25245376, 25246862, 25244635], score=1, time=1606650545, title='Ask HN: Should governments contribute to Open Source?', item_type='story', url=None, parent=None, text='Open source software is in many ways an accelerator of innovation and has, in my opinion, improved the lives of nearly every person on earth (directly or indirectly, for example: pretty much all software services I can think of rely heavily on OSS).<p>However, OSS relies on the good will of people and this can often lead to conflict with companies who are trying to turn a profit.<p>So my question is: Is it in the interest of governments to actively pay people to maintain OSS projects and remove this potential friction so that companies can rely on OSS as infrastructure?<p>This is a bit of a stab in the dark, so I&#x27;m interested in what HN has to say.')
