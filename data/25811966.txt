Item(by='mehagar', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610900454, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25809346, text='I feel a strong need to be accepted by other people, and one of the ways that I meet that need is by considering how my actions (&quot;taking the leap&quot;) might affect others. Sometimes, though, I place more value on others&#x27; needs than my own. This can lead to sadness that I&#x27;m not able to do what I want.<p>This author seems like he wants to overcome this fear of how others may respond, and do the thing anyway. I&#x27;m reminded of the stages of emotional liberation:<p>First stage: Emotional slavery: we see ourselves responsible for others’ feelings.<p>Second stage: “Obnoxious”: we feel angry; we no longer want to be responsible for others’ feelings.<p>Third stage: Emotional liberation: we take responsibility for our intentions and actions.<p>[1] - <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;about-nvc.tumblr.com&#x2F;post&#x2F;98642157391&#x2F;from-emotional-slavery-to-emotional-liberation-the" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;about-nvc.tumblr.com&#x2F;post&#x2F;98642157391&#x2F;from-emotional...</a>')
