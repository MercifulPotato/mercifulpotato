Item(by='baobabKoodaa', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606870022, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25271037, text='As far as messengers go, IRC is about as far from Slack as you can get. Two main differences:<p>1. You can&#x27;t send IRC messages to people who are offline (yes you can work around this with bouncers etc. but that&#x27;s beside the point). You can send Slack messages to people who are offline.<p>2. Slack is a proprietary, centralized system. Slack, the company, decides who is allowed to participate, and at what cost. IRC is a federated protocol, where different servers can be part of the same network, or part of a different network. You can set up a server or client by yourself if you want.')
