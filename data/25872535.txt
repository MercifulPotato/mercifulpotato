Item(by='whizzter', descendants=None, kids=[25873902], score=None, time=1611329937, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25872289, text='The semantics of Raft has a &quot;simple&quot; (compared to the harder to understand Paxos) forward motion of events that is supposed to guarantee that you won&#x27;t get into weird states regardless of if any particular node(s) goes down (I think it can surive (N&#x2F;2)-1 dead machines in a cluster of N).<p>Raft is based on having a leader decide what the next COMMIT is going to be, so B could never have X and Y at the same time (they could both be queued but other mechanisms could reject them).<p>Also data is not considered committed until more than half the cluster has acknowledged it (at which point the leader will know it and handle going forward), leader election also works in a similar way iirc.<p>As others mentioned, the visualization on <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;raft.github.io&#x2F;" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;raft.github.io&#x2F;</a> is really good (You can affect it to create commits and control downtime of machines)')
