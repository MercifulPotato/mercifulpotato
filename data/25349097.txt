Item(by='ceejayoz', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1607450816, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25348161, text='There will always be small numbers of cases of stupid coroners, clerical mistakes, etc. Finding one is not proof that there are hundreds of thousands.<p>We can rule out the &quot;they&#x27;re reporting everything as COVID and that&#x27;s making the numbers high&quot; hypothesis by looking at overall excess deaths - how many <i>more</i> deaths is the nation experiencing than is typical. A misdiagnosis won&#x27;t affect excess deaths, as it counts <i>all</i> deaths, regardless of cause. The CDC provides a page for this:<p><a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cdc.gov&#x2F;nchs&#x2F;nvss&#x2F;vsrr&#x2F;covid19&#x2F;excess_deaths.htm" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cdc.gov&#x2F;nchs&#x2F;nvss&#x2F;vsrr&#x2F;covid19&#x2F;excess_deaths.htm</a><p>As is readily visible in the chart, we&#x27;ve been substantially over the norm since late March.<p>(Switch the chart to New York City for an even more obvious example of how a spike in excess deaths shows up.
