Item(by='catlifeonmars', descendants=None, kids=[25322055, 25321228], score=None, time=1607222844, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25320033, text='Memory fragmentation is generally a problem for pets, not cattle. In 90% of use cases it’s nothing that frequently reaping processes won’t fix. This works regardless of language, as it pushes the job of garbage collection onto the os. Another nice feature of treating your processes like dirt is that you tend to get high fault tolerance and scalability as emergent properties.')
