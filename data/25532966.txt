E.g.:<p>&quot;Things are what they are. There is only one reality, namely the way things are.&quot;<p>That&#x27;s only true for the least interesting things: objects and their arrangements in space-time.<p>On that, the huge majority (besides maybe crazy people) agrees anyway, when they see them, or when they&#x27;re given evidence of them (e.g. a photo of a laptop on a desk. 99.999% of the people will agree there&#x27;s a laptop on a desk there).<p>For anything else, politics, values, claims and the veracity of claims, morality, motives, aesthetics, goals, etc., there is no single way things are, there are ways people perceive them, and there are different interets, worldviews, and ways of interpreting them.<p>What is the &quot;reality&quot; or &quot;non-reality&quot; of &quot;I&#x27;m willing to die for my country&quot;? Or of &quot;I find it OK to make money stealing&quot;? Or &quot;You should treat people well even if you are not forced to|?')
