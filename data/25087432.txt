Item(by='comex', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605301246, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25087128, text='From another perspective, they&#x27;re both performing the same operation on different types.  With what you&#x27;d call &quot;destination passing&quot;, like<p><pre><code>    char *buf = malloc(128);\n    snprintf(buf, 128, &quot;hello&quot;);\n</code></pre>\nthe function is accepting a pointer to an uninitialized array of char, and initializing the array of char.  With what you&#x27;d call an &quot;out parameter&quot;, like<p><pre><code>    char *buf;\n    asprintf(&amp;buf, &quot;hello&quot;);\n</code></pre>\nthe function is accepting a pointer to an uninitialized <i>pointer</i> to array of char, and initializing the pointer.  The caller still controls the allocation of storage for the pointer itself.')
