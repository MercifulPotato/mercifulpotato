Item(by='ncmncm', descendants=None, kids=[24685726, 24682918], score=None, time=1601845954, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24680146, text='The moment after two RNA molecules that copy other RNA molecules encounter one another, the seas are absolutely teeming with them. On the way, any other molecules that make it go faster, or stabilize the result, also get reproduced like crazy.<p>Nowhere in nature does any cell create a membrane <i>de novo</i>. So far as has been discovered, membranes are always produced by extending an existing membrane. So, arguably, life is that aboriginal membrane extending itself, and picking up helpful molecules on the way.')
