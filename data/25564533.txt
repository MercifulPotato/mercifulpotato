Item(by='aeternum', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609197775, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25561592, text='Yes, it seems pretty straightforward to reason about the consequences:<p>Money becomes a liability rather than an asset.  Individuals and entities will attempt to exchange money for real assets, triggering a rapid devaluation.  If that currency was widely used, it won&#x27;t be anymore, no one will want it.  Governments can and probably will try to force people to use it for a time but it won&#x27;t work long term.<p>Negative interest rates are basically just another way to print money.  This devaluation has not only happened many times throughout history, I believe it has happened to <i>every</i> reserve currency used in the past.')
