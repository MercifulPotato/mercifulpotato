But conflating threats with 1st amendment protected action is dumb.<p>And yes, Twitter, under the first amendment, is free to associate with whomever they want (or to refuse to associate with them)! Twitter isn&#x27;t compelling Meghan Murphy to do anything anymore than than twitter is, at this moment, compelling me to give them my wallet.<p>On the other hand, if you said &quot;Give me your wallet, or me and my 9 associates here will punch you in the face,&quot; you&#x27;d be committing a crime.<p>That&#x27;s the difference between being kicked off twitter and punching someone in the face. One of them is a crime. No matter how purely you uphold the principle of free speech, it doesn&#x27;t make sense to compare someone who doesn&#x27;t share those values to a criminal.')
