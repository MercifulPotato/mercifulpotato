Item(by='barumi', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1605038484, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25046087, text='&gt;  The author of &quot;GitFlow Considered Harmful&quot; put out a simpler workflow that achieves all the upsides of GitFlow with none of the downsides:<p>I see no relevant difference between that workflow and GitFlow, besides the rebranding of old concepts and some unexplained focus on merging options.<p>I&#x27;ve also revisited the author&#x27;s old rant from 2015 regarding gitflow and the only tangible criticism he had was the separation of the master&#x2F;mainline and development branches, which is extremely shortsighted and miopic as it fails to acknowledge the reality of non-CICD&#x2F;old-fashined software release lifecycles.<p>GitFlow has one main concept: using feature branches to do develop work, use release-specific branches to integrate changes, establish and enforce upstream&#x2F;downstream relationships between branches.
