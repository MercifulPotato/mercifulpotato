Item(by='samatman', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610073837, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25680242, text='I don&#x27;t see the actual difference between <i>an individual</i> choosing to drive slowly, eat vegan, and be cold at home, and that same individual choosing to spend extra for sequestration.  Assuming that the offsets are honest, admittedly a big if.<p>One is painful in the lifestyle, one in the wallet, and both are drops in the bucket compared to changes on a civilizational level.<p>Which isn&#x27;t to say you or anyone shouldn&#x27;t pursue your own sense of moral behavior, quite the opposite in fact.<p>I would even say that using a normal amount of, say, electricity, but offsetting it with carbon capture, has one advantage: using a subnormal amount makes power fractionally cheaper for others.<p>But by how much? Well... by about the amount of difference any one set of human actions makes.')
