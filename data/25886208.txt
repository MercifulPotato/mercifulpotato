Item(by='dvdkon', descendants=None, kids=[25886881], score=None, time=1611436847, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25885509, text='I think the demise of WYSIWYG GUI builders was partly just it &quot;going out of fashion&quot;, but also partly because of rising requirements for dynamic GUIs, meaning actually changing the layout programatically, but also things like arbitrary resizing and dynamically switching based on device type (&quot;responsive&quot; design).<p>All of the popular 90s RAD design tools dealt with resizing as an afterthought, which made them obsolete in many cases and probably contributed negatively to the reputation of the whole class of software. Today&#x27;s WYSISYG tools (I&#x27;d say as early as mid 2000s or even before) have certainly rectified these early mistakes, but I guess fashion moved on by then.')
