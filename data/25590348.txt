Item(by='AdieuToLogic', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1609398688, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25590106, text='&gt; A public health professional who does health policy and community outreach is a bureaucrat, not a scientist.<p>Who are epidemiologists?<p>When disease outbreaks or other threats emerge, epidemiologists are on the scene to investigate. Often called &quot;Disease Detectives&quot;, epidemiologists search for the cause of disease, identify people who are at risk, determine how to control or stop the spread or prevent it from happening again. Physicians, veterinarians, scientists, and other health professionals often train to be &quot;Disease Detectives&quot;.<p>source: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cdc.gov&#x2F;careerpaths&#x2F;k12teacherroadmap&#x2F;epidemiologists.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cdc.gov&#x2F;careerpaths&#x2F;k12teacherroadmap&#x2F;epidemiolo...</a><p>See also: Epidemic Intelligence Service (EIS) (<a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cdc.gov&#x2F;eis&#x2F;index.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cdc.gov&#x2F;eis&#x2F;index.html</a>)<p>What EIS Officers Do<p>EIS officers serve on the front lines of public health, protecting Americans and the global community, while training under the guidance of seasoned mentors. When disease outbreaks or other public health threats emerge, EIS officers investigate, identify the cause, rapidly implement control measures, and collect evidence to recommend preventive actions.<p>source: <a href="https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cdc.gov&#x2F;eis&#x2F;what-eis-officers-do&#x2F;index.html" rel="nofollow">https:&#x2F;&#x2F;www.cdc.gov&#x2F;eis&#x2F;what-eis-officers-do&#x2F;index.html</a>')
