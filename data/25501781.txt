Item(by='kortex', descendants=None, kids=[25502043, 25502679, 25502175], score=None, time=1608599667, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25501632, text='I don&#x27;t think GOPATH is poorly thought out at all. Dependency-environment-locating is a PITA. Off the top of my head, I can&#x27;t think of a single package management system that doesn&#x27;t use universal installs, FOO_PATH or &quot;giant local dump per project&quot;.<p>Universal:<p>- apt, yum, brew<p>Team PATH:<p>- GOPATH<p>- CMAKE_PREFIX_PATH<p>- PYTHONPATH (which Conda, virtualenv, etc modify)<p>- CARGO_HOME<p>Team redundant local blob:<p>- Node<p>- pipenv<p>Rust is probably the least-half-assed (most full-assed?) model, with both a sane user-wide default for cache (~&#x2F;.local&#x2F;cargo), a way to edit that default, and project location flexibility.<p>But I actually love the Go notation that I&#x27;ve opted to organize most of my code around the ~&#x2F;namespace&#x2F;src&#x2F;domain&#x2F;repo scheme. I never lose track of where a folder is :)')
