Item(by='Tomminn', descendants=None, kids=[25166284], score=None, time=1605905802, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25135418, text='The sin^n x notation is bad. I don&#x27;t blame the undergrads there.<p>So we learn the following fact:<p><pre><code>              sin^{-1} x = y \n  =&gt;                   x = sin y\n</code></pre>\nBeing enthusiastic new algebra students, we presume know this must work by applying sine to both sides:<p><pre><code>      sin^{1} sin^{-1} x = sin^{1} y\n  =&gt;          sin^{0}  x = sin y           here sin^{0} is zero applications of sine to x    \n  =&gt;                   x = sin y\n</code></pre>\nNoting we could start at line 2, and apply sin^{-1} to both side also, we have now learnt that:<p><pre><code>       sin^{a} sin^{b} x = sin^{a+b} x\n  if                   a = +&#x2F;- 1\n  and                  b = -&#x2F;+ 1.\n</code></pre>\nPresumably, if notation is at all sane, the rule applies to other values of a and b so:<p><pre><code>                 sin^2 x = sin sin x (? Surely!)\n</code></pre>\nRight? No.<p><pre><code>                sin^2 x  = (sin x)^2\n and           sin sin x = (has no other name) \n              \n</code></pre>\nNo wonder students get confused. The notation is trying it&#x27;s darnedest to confuse them.')
