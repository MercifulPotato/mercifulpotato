This is why anything shirt of general A.I.—which economically is automation that spills over from jobs one tries to automate to a significant portion of others—is not revolutionary.<p>So a declining working age population could shrink the economy, and this would be the orthodox economist position which doesn&#x27;t recognize a structural underconsumption problem. And with underconsumption I think it&#x27;s a not of a toss-up, but if the underconsumption is prevented, I don&#x27;t the the demographics will get in the way.<p>tl;dr lock in your UBI before the population peaks.')
