Item(by='pbhjpbhj', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1606059120, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25177387, text='Huh, until now I&#x27;ve only ever thought of &quot;number cruncher&quot; as a euphemism for &quot;processes numbers rapidly like a creature crunching through food&quot;.<p>But, now you say this ... early &#x27;computers&#x27; (eg adding machines) of course did have teeth (they used mechanical gears) and would literally be &#x27;number crunchers&#x27; and so would fit a description of &quot;crunches numbers with it&#x27;s teeth&quot; as a literal term.<p>Now, a number cruncher in UK English is used to refer to a person, like an accountant: I suppose in the past they crunched numbers using mathematical machines (analogue calculators).<p>Also, of note is that originally a &#x27;computer&#x27; was a job role of someone operating a mathematical machine.<p>Obviously then a &#x27;computer&#x27; became the machine, and it still &#x27;crunched numbers&#x27; except it did it silently using transistors.<p>It sounds like that textbook was not wrong but perhaps just needed explanation.')
