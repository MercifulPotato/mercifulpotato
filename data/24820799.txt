I can&#x27;t see why that definition wouldn&#x27;t apply.<p>FWIW all the supposed complex questions they ask are clear cut. Advertising on a website makes it commercial, using works in a presentation at work makes it commercial, etc., they&#x27;re all clearly commercial.<p>A harder question might be &quot;what if I&#x27;m not charging and am giving files away&quot; that becomes commercial if it substantially inhibits commercial activities, for example (according to copyright caselaw I&#x27;ve read).<p>But that doesn&#x27;t matter that much, was the guy making $10 a month from adsense using your work commercially (legally, yes): it doesn&#x27;t matter, why bother to sue them (actual damages is going to be tiny). NC is for the company advertising with your work, or ripping off your work, etc..<p>NC doesn&#x27;t seem that hard to define but in any situation where it would be worth you suing for tortuous infringement then it&#x27;s going to be very clear -- unless someone made a lot of money, or you lost a lot of money, then  ...')
