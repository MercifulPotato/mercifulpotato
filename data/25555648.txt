The professor said that due to an essentially oral history tradition, they talked about the Crusades as something that had happened relatively recently, like to their great-grandparents.<p>American parents will warn their children that if they misbehave, &quot;the boogeyman&quot; will come for them at night. I was told that the Middle Eastern analogue was &quot;Richard will come and get you&quot; — as in Richard the Lion-Hearted. (I have no idea if this is true or not… it sounded like a fun anecdote back in college, now it sounds slightly ridiculous to me, but who knows?)')
