Item(by='steveklabnik', descendants=None, kids=[24838254], score=None, time=1603201960, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24837140, text='Yes, it also can matter what you even mean by &quot;competitor.&quot; Some people take it to mean &quot;similarity in language features.&quot; Others can take it to mean &quot;would I choose it as a substitute.&quot; Some take it to mean &quot;would I choose it as a substitute for this specific domain or project.&quot;<p>I&#x27;ve seen this play out a number of times. &quot;Rust cannot compete with C, because C is too entrenched in embedded.&quot; &quot;I do embedded development in Rust, so it can.&quot; &quot;Well, I work with these chips that Rust can&#x27;t target yet, so it can&#x27;t, for me.&quot; None of these statements are incorrect, but they can lead to huge back-and-forths.<p>Words are hard.')
