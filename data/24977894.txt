Item(by='SideburnsOfDoom', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1604394946, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24971734, text='English definitely has two registers, that you might find in use in the same sentence. This becomes clear if you e.g. translate a scientific text into Dutch or German.<p>&quot;Carbon&quot; becomes &quot;Kohlenstoff&quot;, and &quot;metabolism&quot; becomes &quot;Stoffwechsel&quot; in German.<p>These might be literally translated back as &quot;Coal-stuff&quot; and &quot;stuff-change&quot; but that is an unfamiliar way to say it in English because it remains in the lower register, not derived academically from old Latin and Greek.')
