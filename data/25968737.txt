Item(by='tsimionescu', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1612012048, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25967918, text='There seems to be a major flaw in comparing the data between the two studies: the Pfizer paper is counting Covid19 cases (symptomatic cases with confirmed PCR for SARS-CoV-2), while the Maccabi paper seems to be counting SARS-CoV-2 infections overall, which would include asymptomatic cases.<p>They even say so explicitly:<p>&gt; Second, our case definition was positive SARS-CoV-2 PCR test irrespective to presenting symptoms that defined COVID-19 cases in the RCT. This might explain the discrepancy in results.<p>So, this new research is not really contradicting the Pfizer paper in any way - they are simply measuring different quantities.<p>Still, it&#x27;s nice to know that the vaccine also offers protection from infection, not just the disease.')
