Item(by='strenholme', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1608712161, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25515314, text='With “display: table”, “margin-left” and “margin-right” horizontally center the content.  For example:<p><pre><code>  &lt;style&gt;\n   .foo { \n     display: table; \n     margin-left: auto; \n     margin-right: auto; }\n  &lt;&#x2F;style&gt;\n  &lt;div class=foo&gt;\n  &lt;h1&gt;Hello, world!&lt;&#x2F;h1&gt;\n  &lt;&#x2F;div&gt;\n</code></pre>\nHere, it is <i>only</i> centered when “display: table” is present. e.g. this is <i>not</i> centered:<p><pre><code>  &lt;style&gt;\n   .foo { \n     &#x2F;* display: table; DISABLED *&#x2F; \n     margin-left: auto; \n     margin-right: auto; }\n  &lt;&#x2F;style&gt;\n  &lt;div class=foo&gt;\n  &lt;h1&gt;Hello, world!&lt;&#x2F;h1&gt;\n  &lt;&#x2F;div&gt;\n</code></pre>\nSimple “display: table” two-column layout:<p><pre><code>  &lt;style&gt;\n  .all {\n     display: table;\n     margin-left: auto;\n     margin-right: auto;\n  }\n  .sub {\n     display: table-row;\n  }\n  .left {\n     display: table-cell;\n  }\n  .right {\n     display: table-cell;\n  }\n  &lt;&#x2F;style&gt;\n  &lt;div class=all&gt;\n    &lt;div class=sub&gt;\n       &lt;div class=left&gt;\n          &lt;h1&gt;Hello, world!&lt;&#x2F;h1&gt;\n          Left side.\n       &lt;&#x2F;div&gt;\n       &lt;div class=right&gt;\n          &lt;h1&gt;Right bar&lt;&#x2F;h1&gt;\n          Right side.\n       &lt;&#x2F;div&gt;\n    &lt;&#x2F;div&gt;\n  &lt;&#x2F;div&gt;\n</code></pre>\nThere are ways to vertically center content, but since that wasn’t something CSS was designed to do (for the kinds of things where something vertically centered makes sense, we were supposed to be making web apps in Java), they are all clunky.')
