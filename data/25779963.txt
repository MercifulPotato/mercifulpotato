Item(by='rchaud', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1610648381, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=25779831, text='No, therapists make you do homework. The job involves more than lending a sympathetic ear and pocketing the cash.<p>Behavioural change doesn&#x27;t happen by just talking. Maybe that&#x27;s for session 1, but for session 2, you&#x27;ll have to do work to understand your feelings, what triggers them and how to set up your life and daily schedule to manage them. To do this you will likely be asked to keep a mood diary, and review the results during the sessions.<p>Lots of people quit therapy because it&#x27;s expensive and their employer may only cover the cost of say 10 sessions. But a lot of people quit well before that because they go into it thinking that they&#x27;ll &quot;let it all out&quot; and get instant results.')
