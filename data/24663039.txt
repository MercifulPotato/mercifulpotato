Item(by='colejohnson66', descendants=None, kids=None, score=None, time=1601649415, title=None, item_type='comment', url=None, parent=24661492, text='While the US does love exporting our&#x2F;their rule of law outside, the internet creates an interesting problem with jurisdiction. By <i>serving</i> customers in the US, have they subjected themselves to US law?[a] Many times before, the courts have found that an American company doing business in State X meant they could be sued there. But they’ve also found otherwise. It’s an interesting question that doesn’t seem to have a clear answer.<p>[a] When <i>visiting</i> another country, it’s very clear you’re subjecting yourself to their laws (I can’t visit France and expect only US laws will apply to me), but the internet (a global system) poses a problem.')
