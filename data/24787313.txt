It might start from good intentions, but eventually we&#x27;re shushing people that go against the herd without actually knowing they&#x27;re wrong.<p>In this case, it seems pretty obvious that there aren&#x27;t microchips in vaccines.  I&#x27;m not even sure what those chips would do, or how they&#x27;d work.<p>But what if some day the government really <i>did</i> do that, or something else that seems ridiculous?  We&#x27;ll be censoring anyone who tries to tell us otherwise.<p>And 1 step worse:  The government could <i>order</i> YouTube&#x2F;etc to do that censoring, and it&#x27;ll look like it&#x27;s just their normal thing.<p>The problem with censorship is that legit and valuable free speech will eventually be restricted.')
